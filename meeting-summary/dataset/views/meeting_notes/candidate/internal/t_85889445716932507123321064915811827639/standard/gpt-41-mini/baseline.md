### Overview
The Rental Housing Committee convened via video conference to address several key agenda items, including the approval of routine consent calendar items, a quasi-judicial appeal hearing, monthly status reporting, and a comprehensive community outreach communications plan. The appeal hearing focused on a landlord's challenge to a hearing officer's decision regarding rent concessions and the retroactive application of related regulations. Staff recommended upholding the hearing officer's decision, which the committee ultimately approved unanimously after deliberation and consideration of landlord and tenant presentations. The committee discussed the rationale behind a one-year rollback limitation on rent concessions predating September 1, 2022, acknowledging it as a compromise to balance tenant protections and landlord concerns. The monthly status report highlighted ongoing program activities, eviction prevention efforts, and market trends, with committee members seeking clarifications on property sales and data reporting enhancements. The community outreach presentation detailed extensive past efforts and ambitious plans to increase awareness and compliance through multilingual materials, targeted mailings, workshops, and digital improvements, with committee members expressing strong support and appreciation for staff dedication. Upcoming events and workshops were announced, emphasizing continued engagement with tenants, landlords, and vulnerable populations. Public comments underscored the importance of clear communication and timely information dissemination, particularly regarding mobile home rent stabilization. Overall, the meeting reflected the committee's commitment to balancing legal mandates, community needs, and effective program administration.

#### Appeal Hearing Procedures
The committee conducted a quasi-judicial hearing on an appeal regarding petition 21220016, involving a tenant's request for downward rent adjustment due to rent concessions and the landlord's appeal of the hearing officer's decision. The hearing followed established procedures including presentations by both parties, rebuttals, and committee deliberations. No ex parte communications were disclosed.
- Chair Haynes-Livesay outlined the hearing procedures and roles of appellant landlord and respondent tenant.
- Staff member Nas presented the role of the committee and the scope of review limited to appealed elements.
- No ex parte communications were disclosed by committee members.
#### Staff Presentation on Appeal
Staff presented the tentative appeal decision recommending the committee uphold the hearing officer's ruling that the tenant was entitled to a downward rent adjustment due to unlawful rent increases related to rent concessions. Staff explained the regulatory background, the rationale for a one-year rollback limitation for concessions before September 1, 2022, and advised against a de novo review due to sufficient existing evidence.
- Nas explained the appeal concerns the application of rent concession regulations effective September 1, 2022.
- Staff recommended affirming the hearing officer's decision and limiting rollback to one year prior to petition filing for concessions before that date.
- The hearing officer calculated the base rent accounting for a one-month concession in the second month of tenancy.
#### Appellant Landlord Presentation
The landlord appellant, Steve, argued that the retroactive application of the rent concession regulations is unfair and penalizes landlords for providing rent assistance. He contended that the effective date of the amendment should be September 1, 2022, not retroactive, and expressed concerns about the implications of the decision on landlord business operations and consistency with prior compliance.
- Steve stated the July 18, 2022 meeting did not clearly discuss retroactive application of the amendment.
- He compared retroactive enforcement to penalizing legal parking after the fact.
- He emphasized that rent was paid either by tenant or landlord and that retroactive changes limit landlords' ability to provide concessions.
#### Respondent Tenant Presentation
The tenant, Ron Dillon, supported the hearing officer's decision and explained that the rent concession was provided in the second month of tenancy, with rent paid monthly at $1545. He expressed understanding of the regulations and affirmed that the tenant followed the rules as laid out, supporting the tentative decision to stand.
- Ron Dillon confirmed the second month rent concession and monthly rent payments of $1545.
- He stated the base rent was understood as the amount paid during the initial 12 months.
- He supported the tentative decision and acknowledged landlords can make future decisions accordingly.
#### Rebuttals by Parties
Both parties provided brief rebuttals. The landlord acknowledged the decision was properly decided but disagreed with the retroactive application. The tenant had no additional rebuttal beyond prior statements.
- Steve acknowledged the decision was valid but felt it was written incorrectly regarding retroactivity.
- Ron Dillon had no further rebuttal comments.
#### Committee Questions and Discussion
Committee members asked staff about the rationale and discussion history for the one-year rollback limitation on rent concessions. Staff clarified that the limitation was a compromise to address landlord concerns and was discussed extensively in prior meetings, including a straw poll. Committee members expressed understanding of the compromise and the challenges of retroactive application.
- Committee member Greenawald inquired about the rationale for the one-year rollback limit.
- Staff explained it was a compromise to limit landlord financial impact from concessions given years ago.
- Vice Chair Ramos confirmed the one-year look back was a compromise to avoid harsher retroactive penalties.
#### Committee Deliberations and Comments
Committee members deliberated on the appeal, acknowledging the legal clarity of the decision while empathizing with landlord concerns about retroactivity. Members emphasized the importance of clear communication for future compliance and recognized the pandemic context affecting tenants. The motion to accept the tentative decision was made and passed unanimously.
- Committee member Greenawald expressed sympathy for landlord concerns about retroactivity but supported the decision.
- Committee member Rosas highlighted the hardships tenants faced during the pandemic and the importance of safety nets.
- Chair Haynes-Livesay explained the committee's efforts to mitigate financial impacts within legal constraints.
- Motion to accept the tentative decision was moved by Almond, seconded by Ramos, and passed unanimously.
