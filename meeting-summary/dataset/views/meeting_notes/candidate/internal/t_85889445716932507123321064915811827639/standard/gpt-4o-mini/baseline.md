### Overview
The Rental Housing Committee meeting covered several key topics, including the approval of the consent calendar, public comments, and an appeal hearing regarding a rent adjustment petition. The committee unanimously approved the consent calendar and heard from a member of the public expressing gratitude for staff support. The appeal hearing involved detailed presentations from both the appellant landlord and the respondent tenant, with staff recommending the acceptance of the tentative appeal decision. The committee deliberated and voted unanimously to uphold the decision. Additionally, staff provided a monthly status report highlighting community outreach efforts and announced upcoming workshops aimed at educating residents about housing resources. The meeting concluded with a positive outlook on the ongoing engagement with the community.

### Detailed Summary
#### Consent Calendar Approval
The committee reviewed the consent calendar, which included routine business items. All items were approved unanimously without any items being pulled for discussion, indicating a smooth consensus among committee members.
- Chair Haynes-Livesay noted that no items were pulled for discussion.
- Committee member Almond moved to approve the minutes from the previous meeting.
- Vice Chair Ramos seconded the motion, leading to a unanimous vote in favor.

#### Public Comments
During the public comment period, Teresa expressed gratitude for the staff's responsiveness regarding unit registration software. This highlights the importance of community engagement and the effectiveness of the committee's outreach efforts.
- Teresa thanked Anke and the staff for their assistance with the unit registration software.
- She noted that progress is being made, reflecting positively on the committee's efforts.

#### Appeal Hearing Process
The committee conducted an appeal hearing regarding a petition for a downward adjustment of rent. The process was outlined, emphasizing the quasi-judicial nature of the hearing and the roles of the appellant and respondent in presenting their cases.
- Chair Haynes-Livesay explained the procedures for the appeal hearing, including time limits for presentations.
- Staff member Nas provided a detailed overview of the tentative appeal decision and the rationale behind it.

#### Tentative Appeal Decision
Staff presented the tentative appeal decision, which recommended upholding the hearing officer's decision regarding the rent adjustment. The discussion focused on the application of regulations and the implications for both the tenant and landlord.
- Nas stated that the hearing officer's decision was based on substantial evidence and did not recommend a de novo review.
- The appellant landlord, Steve, expressed concerns about the retroactive application of the regulations.

#### Public Deliberation and Vote
The committee deliberated on the appeal and ultimately voted to accept the tentative decision. This decision reflects the committee's commitment to uphold the regulations while considering the concerns raised by the appellant.
- Committee member Greenawald acknowledged the complexities of the situation and the need for clarity in future regulations.
- The motion to accept the tentative decision passed unanimously, indicating strong support from the committee.

#### Monthly Status Report
Staff provided a monthly status report detailing inquiries, workshops, and community outreach efforts. The report highlighted the ongoing challenges and successes in engaging the community regarding housing issues.
- Staff reported 782 inquiries made through the end of October, with a significant portion being bilingual services.
- The report included data on workshops held and the number of attendees, showcasing the effectiveness of outreach efforts.

#### Community Outreach Plan Overview
The committee reviewed the community outreach plan for the fiscal year, focusing on strategies to improve communication and engagement with tenants and landlords. The plan aims to enhance understanding of tenant protections and compliance requirements.
- Staff outlined objectives for the outreach plan, including developing clear materials and increasing community engagement.
- Committee member Almond praised the depth and breadth of the outreach efforts, emphasizing the importance of effective communication.

#### Upcoming Events and Workshops
Staff announced upcoming events and workshops aimed at educating the community about housing and eviction resources. These events are part of the ongoing effort to connect with residents and provide necessary support.
- The Housing and Eviction Help Center will continue to operate with in-person and virtual options for community members.
- Upcoming workshops include a focus on mobile home rent stabilization, demonstrating the committee's commitment to diverse housing needs.
