### Overview
  The Rental Housing Committee convenes under Chair Haynes-Livesay with staff leads (Nas, Karen, Anky, Andrea) and committee members (Almond, Ramos, Greenawald, Rosas, et al.) to handle routine business, hear a quasi-judicial appeal, review program metrics, and approve an expanded communications plan. The consent calendar (minutes, AB 361 virtual meetings resolution, and quarterly expenditures) passes unanimously and a public commenter thanks staff for progress on the unit registration software. In a contested appeal (petition 21220016) about a one-month rent concession and the retroactive application of recently clarified concession rules, staff recommends affirming the hearing officer and advises against a de novo review; after oral arguments by the landlord appellant (Steve) and the tenant respondent (Ron Dillon), the committee unanimously adopts the tentative decision affirming the hearing officer and the staff-framed one-year lookback limit for concessions provided before September 1, 2022. Staff presents October program metrics (inquiries, workshops, eviction help center activity, mediations, petitions, and market indicators) and outlines a comprehensive FY22/23 outreach plan that adds targeted mailings, multilingual materials, portal improvements (bulk upload, public search), realtor outreach, and timelines for new petition filings; committee members commend staff, discuss measurement and mailer costs, and confirm ARPA funding and one budgeted outreach position to support implementation. The meeting concludes with schedules for the Help Center, weekly office hours, upcoming workshops (including mobile-home outreach), and continued community engagement events.

  #### Appeal hearing petition
  The committee hears an appeal of Hearing Officer Decision on petition 21220016 concerning a tenant's downward rent adjustment tied to a one-month rent concession provided in the tenant's second month. Staff (Nas) recommends against a de novo review and presents a tentative appeal decision that affirms the hearing officer's calculation of base rent and limited recovery rules; the landlord appellant challenges retroactive application of newly clarified concession regulations. After presentations and questions, the committee deliberates and unanimously adopts the tentative decision, which affirms the hearing officer and leaves the one-year lookback/limit in place for concessions provided before September 1, 2022.
- Staff (Nas) explains the RHC's review is limited to whether the appealed elements of the hearing officer's decision are supported by substantial evidence and recommends against a de novo review.
- Staff (Nas) summarizes that the hearing officer calculated a corrected base rent accounting for the one-month concession and found the tenant entitled to refunds for overpaid rent.
- Staff (Nas) clarifies the adopted regulation limits recovery for concessions provided before September 1, 2022 to a one-year lookback and that the regulation was discussed in prior meetings including July 18, 2022.
- Appellant landlord Steve argues retroactive application of the rent-concession clarification penalizes landlords, contends the effective date should be September 1, 2022, and requests the committee make the rule nonretroactive.
- Respondent tenant Ron Dillon states he moved in May 1, 2021, had the second month rent-free, and supports the hearing officer and tentative decisions on how base rent should be calculated.
- Committee member Greenawald asks staff for the rationale behind the one-year lookback and staff (Karen/Anky) explains it was a compromise discussed at prior meetings to limit landlord impacts.
- Vice Chair Ramos confirms he supported the one-year lookback as a compromise to avoid harsher retroactivity going further back.
- Committee member Almond moves to adopt the tentative decision and Chair Haynes-Livesay seconds the motion.
- Andrea conducts the roll call and reports the motion to accept the tentative decision passes unanimously.

  #### Communications plan overview
  Staff presents the fiscal year 2022/23 communications and outreach plan to increase awareness of tenant protections and program services through multilingual materials, targeted mailings, workshops, and portal improvements. Key planned actions include expanded monthly status report metrics, targeted landlord and new-owner packets, registration survey mailings for small property owners, public search and bulk upload portal features, and realtor-focused trainings; staff also notes ARPA funds for multicultural and language work and one budgeted outreach position that remains to be filled.
