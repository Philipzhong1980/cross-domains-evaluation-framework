### Overview
The briefing featured key participants including Ms. Jean-Pierre and Admiral Kirby, who addressed a range of pressing issues. The session began with condolences and calls for action following a tragic school shooting in Iowa, emphasizing the administration's push for stronger gun control laws. Admiral Kirby provided detailed intelligence on Russia's use of North Korean ballistic missiles in Ukraine and ongoing negotiations with Iran for missile acquisitions, highlighting the international efforts to sanction and hold Russia accountable. The discussion also covered the complexities of the Israel-Hamas conflict, with the U.S. supporting Israel's right to self-defense while urging minimization of civilian casualties and increased humanitarian aid in Gaza. Additional topics included the threat posed by ISIS-K, U.S. military actions in Iraq, and the critical need for Congress to approve supplemental funding to sustain aid to Ukraine. Ms. Jean-Pierre addressed domestic political dynamics, including challenges in congressional negotiations over border security and supplemental funding, President Biden's engagement with historians on democracy, the rollout of federal financial aid applications, and ongoing efforts to combat gun violence. The briefing underscored the administration's multifaceted approach to national security, foreign policy, and domestic priorities amid complex global and domestic challenges.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Iowa School Shooting Response
The briefing opened with a statement from Ms. Jean-Pierre addressing the tragic school shooting at Perry Middle and High School in Iowa. She emphasized the administration's condolences to the victims' families and praised first responders. The administration highlighted ongoing federal support for local investigations and reiterated the urgent need for stronger gun control measures to prevent future tragedies.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre expressed heartbreak over the Iowa school shooting and gratitude for first responders.
- She noted that senior White House staff are coordinating with state and local officials on the investigation.
- The administration called on Congress to enact universal background checks, assault weapons bans, safe firearm storage laws, and a national red flag law.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre stressed that students and teachers deserve safe schools free from the threat of gun violence.
#### Russia's Use of North Korean Ballistic Missiles
Admiral Kirby detailed new intelligence indicating that Russia has received ballistic missile launchers and missiles from North Korea, which have been used in attacks against Ukraine. This marks a significant escalation in North Korea's support for Russia's war efforts. The U.S. is responding with diplomatic efforts at the U.N., sanctions, and public disclosures to hold Russia accountable and to pressure its suppliers.
- Admiral Kirby confirmed Russia launched North Korean ballistic missiles into Ukraine on December 30, 2023, and January 2, 2024.
- The missiles have ranges of approximately 900 kilometers, posing a serious threat to Ukrainian civilian infrastructure.
- The U.S. assesses that North Korea seeks military assistance from Russia in return, including advanced technologies.
- The administration plans to raise these violations at the U.N. Security Council and impose sanctions on facilitators.
#### Russia's Missile Procurement from Iran
The briefing addressed Russia's ongoing negotiations with Iran to acquire close-range ballistic missiles. While no delivery has been confirmed, the U.S. is monitoring the situation closely. Iran has publicly displayed missile systems to Russian officials, indicating advancing talks. The U.S. is concerned about the security implications for the region if such transfers occur.
- Admiral Kirby noted that Russia has not yet received close-range ballistic missiles from Iran but negotiations are progressing.
- Iran's Revolutionary Guard Corps showcased missile systems to Russian Defense Minister Shoigu in September 2023.
- In December 2023, Iran deployed missile systems for display to a visiting Russian delegation.
- The U.S. is concerned about the potential regional security impact of these arms transfers.
#### Effectiveness of Sanctions on Russia's Military Production
In response to questions, Admiral Kirby explained that international sanctions and export controls have pressured Russia's defense industry, limiting its ability to fully sustain military production. Although Russia has increased some domestic production, it still relies on external sources like North Korea and Iran for munitions. The imported missile systems add to Russia's existing capabilities and enable continued attacks on Ukraine.
- Admiral Kirby stated that sanctions have had a detrimental effect on Russia's defense industrial capacity.
- Russia has increased domestic missile production but still depends on North Korean and Iranian supplies.
- The imported missile systems are additive rather than replacing existing Russian capabilities.
- Russia is weaponizing winter by targeting civilian infrastructure to break Ukrainian morale.
#### Israel-Hamas Conflict and Civilian Casualties
Admiral Kirby addressed questions about the ongoing Israeli military campaign against Hamas, emphasizing that the U.S. supports Israel's right to defend itself while urging efforts to minimize civilian casualties. He noted that Israel is targeting Hamas leadership and has taken steps to reduce harm to civilians, including opening humanitarian corridors. The U.S. continues diplomatic engagement to encourage compliance with international law.
- Admiral Kirby affirmed Israel's right and responsibility to target Hamas leadership within international law.
- He acknowledged civilian casualties and stressed the goal of zero civilian deaths.
- Israel has reduced reliance on airstrikes and opened humanitarian corridors in Gaza.
- The U.S. is actively engaging with Israeli officials to promote protection of innocent lives.
#### ISIS-K Threat Assessment
The briefing touched on the Islamic State Khorasan (ISIS-K) group's claim of responsibility for an attack in Iran. Admiral Kirby confirmed the claim is credible but cautioned against drawing broad conclusions about the group's strength from a single event. ISIS-K remains a viable terrorist threat primarily based in Afghanistan, posing ongoing regional security concerns.
- Admiral Kirby confirmed ISIS-K's public claim of the attack in Iran is credible.
- He stated it is difficult to assess ISIS-K's overall strength based on one incident.
- ISIS-K remains a viable terrorist threat headquartered in Afghanistan.
- The U.S. continues to monitor ISIS-K's activities in the region.
#### U.S. Support for Israel and Compliance with International Law
When questioned about assessments of Israel's adherence to humanitarian law, Admiral Kirby clarified that the U.S. has not conducted formal evaluations but maintains ongoing dialogue with Israeli counterparts. The U.S. expects Israel to operate within international law and has observed efforts to reduce civilian harm. The administration continues to monitor the situation closely.
- Admiral Kirby stated there is no formal U.S. assessment of Israel's compliance with international law.
- The U.S. expects Israel to conduct operations within the boundaries of humanitarian law.
- Dialogue with Israeli officials emphasizes minimizing civilian casualties.
- The U.S. has seen some Israeli efforts to reduce harm but encourages further measures.
#### U.S. Military Actions in Iraq and Regional Security
Admiral Kirby declined to comment on a specific strike in Baghdad that killed a pro-Iranian militia commander, referring inquiries to the Department of Defense. He reiterated the U.S. commitment to supporting Iraqi security forces against ISIS in an advisory role, respecting Iraqi sovereignty while protecting U.S. personnel.
- Admiral Kirby referred questions about the Baghdad strike to the Department of Defense.
- He emphasized the U.S. role in Iraq is advisory, supporting Iraqi forces against ISIS.
- The U.S. respects Iraqi sovereignty and takes seriously the protection of its personnel.
- The partnership with Iraq continues despite regional tensions.
#### Humanitarian Efforts and UNRWA in Gaza
The briefing addressed concerns about U.S.-funded agencies like UNRWA operating in Gaza amid allegations that Hamas uses civilian infrastructure for military purposes. Admiral Kirby defended UNRWA's critical humanitarian role and noted that Hamas's use of civilian sites for military activities complicates aid delivery. The U.S. supports UNRWA's work despite these challenges.
- Admiral Kirby highlighted UNRWA's important role in delivering humanitarian aid in Gaza.
- He noted that Hamas uses hospitals and schools for military purposes, endangering civilians.
- The U.S. does not hold UNRWA responsible for Hamas's actions.
- UNRWA operates in harm's way to assist the civilian population.
#### U.S. Congressional Support for Ukraine Aid
Admiral Kirby stressed the urgency of Congress approving supplemental funding to continue U.S. military assistance to Ukraine, particularly for air defense systems. He noted that allies are watching U.S. leadership closely, and failure to act could undermine support. The administration is prioritizing air defense replenishment to counter ongoing Russian attacks.
- Admiral Kirby called on Congress to approve supplemental funding for Ukraine without delay.
- He emphasized the importance of air defense capabilities in the aid package.
- Allied nations are monitoring U.S. commitment to supporting Ukraine.
- Failure to provide funding risks weakening Ukraine's defense and U.S. credibility.
#### White House Relations with Congress on Border Security and Supplemental Funding
Ms. Jean-Pierre discussed the administration's interactions with Congress regarding border security and supplemental funding. She criticized House Republicans for leaving in December and obstructing progress, while highlighting ongoing bipartisan negotiations in the Senate. The administration maintains communication with Senate Republicans and Democrats but notes challenges with the House Republicans' engagement.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre stated House Republicans left in mid-December, hindering border security discussions.
- She emphasized ongoing bipartisan negotiations in the Senate on immigration and supplemental funding.
- The administration is in regular contact with Senate Republicans and Democrats.
- She characterized House Republicans' actions as playing politics and obstructing progress.
#### President Biden's Engagement with Historians and Democracy Speech
Ms. Jean-Pierre described President Biden's meeting with a diverse group of historians ahead of January 6th commemorations to discuss democracy. She noted the President's commitment to hearing diverse perspectives and his intention to speak about the importance of democracy, emphasizing the seriousness of the January 6th attack on U.S. democratic institutions.
- The President met with historians to discuss democracy ahead of January 6th events.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre highlighted the President's value on diverse viewpoints.
- She underscored the President's view of January 6th as an unprecedented attack on democracy.
- The upcoming speech is part of the President's ongoing focus on democratic values.
#### Federal Financial Aid Application Rollout
Ms. Jean-Pierre addressed concerns about the delayed and phased rollout of the federal financial aid application system. She explained the soft launch approach aims to ensure system stability and a better user experience. The Department of Education is actively working to improve the process, with over 400,000 applications completed shortly after launch.
- The Department of Education initiated a soft launch to monitor and improve system performance.
- Over 400,000 financial aid applications were completed shortly after the launch.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre emphasized the goal of making college more affordable and accessible.
- The administration is committed to improving the application process for students and families.
#### Gun Violence Prevention Efforts
Ms. Jean-Pierre reiterated the administration's commitment to combating gun violence following the recent school shooting. She highlighted the bipartisan 2022 legislation signed by President Biden and the over two dozen executive actions taken. The administration continues to push for further legislative measures, including bans on assault weapons and high-capacity magazines, to address the epidemic of gun violence.
- The 2022 bipartisan gun violence legislation was an important step but not sufficient.
- President Biden has signed over two dozen executive actions targeting gun violence.
- The administration calls on Congress to enact universal background checks and assault weapons bans.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre emphasized the need for continued efforts to protect communities and schools.
