### Overview
Senior administration officials detailed the United States' measured response to recent Russian government actions that threaten American sovereignty, including the SolarWinds cyber espionage campaign and interference in the 2020 US election. They formally attributed the SolarWinds hack to the Russian Foreign Intelligence Service's APT29 unit and announced a new executive order signed by President Biden to strengthen sanctions authorities. Key measures include prohibiting US financial institutions from purchasing newly issued Russian sovereign debt starting June 14, 2021, and sanctioning Russian companies supporting intelligence cyber programs, as well as individuals involved in election interference and repression in Crimea. The officials emphasized that these actions are proportionate and aimed at deterring further harmful activities while seeking a stable and predictable US-Russia relationship. They also addressed intelligence assessments regarding Russian efforts to encourage Taliban attacks on US forces in Afghanistan, clarified the rationale for the sanctions' timing and scope, and outlined efforts to enhance cybersecurity partnerships and modernize federal cybersecurity in response to the SolarWinds incident. The US government is committed to defending national interests and working with allies to counter malign cyber activities, while remaining open to diplomatic engagement, including a proposed summit between Presidents Biden and Putin.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### US-Russia Relationship Context
Senior administration officials outlined the US approach to Russia, emphasizing a desire for a stable and predictable relationship while affirming the commitment to defend US sovereignty and impose costs on harmful Russian actions. This context frames the subsequent measures taken in response to Russian activities that threaten US interests.
- A senior administration official stated that the US seeks a stable and predictable relationship with Russia but will defend national interests against harmful actions.
- President Biden raised concerns with President Putin about Russian harmful foreign activities and committed to responding accordingly.
- The US intends its responses to be resolute but proportionate, avoiding escalation.
#### Attribution of SolarWinds Cyberattack
The US formally attributes the SolarWinds cyber espionage campaign to the Russian Foreign Intelligence Service (SVR), specifically the APT29 unit known as Cozy Bear. This attribution updates previous assessments and underpins the sanctions and responses announced.
- A senior administration official confirmed high confidence in attributing the SolarWinds hack to the SVR's APT29 unit.
- This attribution updates the January 2021 assessment that the incident was likely of Russian origin.
- The US government is holding the responsible Russian intelligence service accountable through sanctions.
#### New Sanctions and Executive Order
President Biden signed a new executive order enhancing authorities to respond to Russian harmful foreign activities. Treasury issued directives prohibiting US financial institutions from participating in the primary market for Russian sovereign debt issued after June 14, 2021, and designated six Russian companies supporting Russian intelligence cyber programs. Additional sanctions target election interference and repression in Crimea.
- The new executive order strengthens US ability to impose economically impactful costs on Russia.
- Treasury prohibits US financial institutions from buying newly issued Russian sovereign bonds starting June 14, 2021.
- Six Russian companies supporting SVR cyber programs were designated under the new order.
- Thirty-two entities and individuals involved in Russian election interference and disinformation campaigns were sanctioned.
- Eight individuals and entities linked to Russia's occupation of Crimea were sanctioned in coordination with allies.
- The US is expelling 10 Russian officials from its Washington, D.C. diplomatic mission.
#### Impact of Sovereign Debt Sanctions
Officials explained that prohibiting US investors from purchasing newly issued ruble-denominated Russian sovereign debt will raise Russia's borrowing costs, cause capital flight, and weaken the ruble, thereby impacting Russia's economic growth and inflation. The sanctions apply only to the primary market and take effect on June 14 to allow orderly market adjustments.
- A senior administration official noted that over 80% of Russian sovereign debt was previously untouched by sanctions due to ruble-denominated bonds.
- Removing US investors from the primary market historically causes a chilling effect, raising borrowing costs and weakening the currency.
- The sanctions apply only to new issuances in the primary market, not existing debt or secondary markets.
- The June 14 effective date allows financial institutions to adapt and limits spillover effects to global markets.
#### Russian Bounties on US Soldiers in Afghanistan
The US intelligence community assesses with low to moderate confidence that Russian intelligence officers sought to encourage Taliban attacks on US and coalition forces through financial incentives. While this assessment informs diplomatic messaging, the current sanctions are not directly tied to this issue.
- A senior administration official stated the intelligence community's low to moderate confidence in reports of Russian bounties on US soldiers.
- The assessment relies partly on detainee reporting and the challenging Afghan environment.
- The US has conveyed strong messages to Russia on this behavior but is not linking current sanctions to it.
#### Cybersecurity Partnerships and Responses
The US is enhancing cybersecurity cooperation with allies and partners to counter Russian malicious cyber activities. This includes providing a course on cyber incident attribution and expanding participation in the Department of Defense's CYBER FLAG exercise to improve collective defense capabilities.
- The US is promoting responsible state behavior in cyberspace and cooperating internationally to counter malign cyber activities.
- A new course for policymakers on cyber incident attribution will be launched at the George C. Marshall Center in Germany.
- The CYBER FLAG exercise will include the UK, France, Denmark, and Estonia to build collective cyber defense capabilities.
#### Federal Cybersecurity Modernization
In response to the SolarWinds incident, the US government is rapidly modernizing federal cybersecurity across nine compromised agencies, focusing on encryption, multi-factor authentication, cloud security, logging, endpoint detection, and mature security operations centers. An upcoming executive order will set standards for software procurement to enhance security.
- The SolarWinds hack highlighted the need for rapid modernization of federal cybersecurity.
- Efforts include encryption, multi-factor authentication, cloud security, logging, and endpoint detection.
- An executive order will establish standards for software the federal government procures to improve security.
- These measures aim to counter sophisticated malicious cyber activity and benefit both government and private sectors.
#### Rationale for Response to SolarWinds
Officials explained that the SolarWinds attack's broad scope, potential for rapid escalation from espionage to disruption, and the burden on private-sector victims justify the US response. The attack poses national security and public safety concerns and is considered reckless and unacceptable behavior by Russia.
- The SolarWinds compromise affected over 16,000 systems worldwide, raising national security concerns.
- The speed at which espionage can escalate to disruption is a key concern given Russia's history of reckless cyber behavior.
- Private-sector victims bear high costs to mitigate the incident.
- The US response aims to make clear that such malicious cyber activities are unacceptable.
