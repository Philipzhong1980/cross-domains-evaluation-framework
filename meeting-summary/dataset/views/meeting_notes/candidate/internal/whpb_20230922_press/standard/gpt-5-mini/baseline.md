### Overview
  The briefing opens with the White House announcing the new White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention—overseen by Vice President Harris, led by Stefanie Feldman with deputies Greg Jackson and Rob Wilcox—and features Rep. Lucy McBath’s personal account to underscore the office’s goal of accelerating the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and past executive actions to reduce gun violence. Press Secretary Karine Jean‑Pierre sharply criticizes recent House Republican appropriations votes as extreme and repeatedly warns that they are risking a government shutdown that would cut critical programs, while also highlighting the President’s upcoming signing of bipartisan organ transplant reform and an upcoming Pacific Islands Forum Summit. In the subsequent Q&A Jean‑Pierre declines to comment on the active indictment of Senator Menendez, reiterates support for union workers while describing the administration’s mediated role in the UAW talks and explaining that Julie Su and Gene Sperling remain engaged from Washington by mutual agreement, and defends the administration’s border enforcement actions and funding posture after a reported migrant surge. She repeatedly defers to the National Security Advisor on sensitive diplomatic matters — including Canada‑India allegations, ATACMS reporting, and Israel‑Saudi normalization — affirms continued U.S. support for Ukraine, and says contingency planning by OMB is ongoing for possible shutdown effects on student loans, food safety, and other programs. Throughout, the briefing stresses that the administration intends to accelerate policy work on gun violence and organ transplants, will assist parties where requested, and frames averting a shutdown as primarily the responsibility of House Republicans to honor the bipartisan agreement they supported in May.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Gun violence office
  The White House announces the establishment of the first-ever White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention to accelerate implementation of the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and the administration's prior executive actions. Vice President Harris will oversee the office, Stefanie Feldman will serve as director, and Greg Jackson and Rob Wilcox will join as deputy directors. The office intends to improve coordination with states and federal partners to reduce gun violence and to focus on communities disproportionately affected.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre announces the creation of a White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention overseen by Vice President Harris.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the office will build on the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and the administration’s two dozen executive actions to accelerate work.
- Rep. Lucy McBath delivers a personal testimony about losing her son to gun violence and states that the new office marks a new era in the fight to keep communities safe.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre states Stefanie Feldman will be director and Greg Jackson and Rob Wilcox will serve as deputy directors.
  #### McBath testimony overview
  Representative Lucy McBath speaks about her personal loss to illustrate the human toll of gun violence and to explain why the new office matters. She frames gun violence as a nationwide epidemic that touches suburbs, cities, and rural areas and emphasizes trauma experienced by children. McBath calls the office a life-saving measure and highlights the urgency for sustained federal action.
- Rep. Lucy McBath recounts that her son Jordan was killed in 2012 and uses her story to underscore the nationwide scale of gun violence.
- Rep. Lucy McBath emphasizes that over 100 people are shot and killed daily in the United States and says children endure trauma from lockdown drills.
- Rep. Lucy McBath asserts that President Biden’s actions, including creating the new office, will save lives.
  #### Appropriations and shutdown risk
  Press Secretary Jean-Pierre condemns recent House Republican appropriations votes as extreme and warns they are pushing the country toward a needless government shutdown. She cites specific Republican quotes suggesting an intent to shut down and warns that cuts would threaten services including education, healthcare, Meals on Wheels, and nutrition assistance for millions. The administration frames the solution as House Republicans keeping the bipartisan agreement they supported in May.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre characterizes recent House Republican appropriations activity as extreme and likely to cause a harmful government shutdown.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre quotes Representatives Frank Lucas, Jerry Carl, and Matt Gaetz to illustrate GOP rhetoric about a shutdown.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre warns that proposed cuts would slash education and healthcare and threaten nutrition assistance for nearly seven million mothers and children.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre frames the remedy as House Republicans honoring the bipartisan budget agreement they supported in May.
  #### Organ transplant reform
  The President is signing a bipartisan law intended to reform the organ transplant system to serve more than 100,000 people on waiting lists. The law breaks up the current monopoly system to allow competition for contracts, removes a funding cap to modernize operations, and aims to increase transparency and accountability. The administration presents this action as a direct life-saving improvement to a long-broken system.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the President will sign a law to reform the organ transplant system and help more than 100,000 people on waiting lists.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre explains the law will break up a monopoly, allow HHS to contract with better providers, and eliminate the funding cap to modernize the system.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre frames the reform as creating a more transparent and accountable system that will save lives.
  #### Pacific Islands summit
  The administration previews a U.S.-Pacific Islands Forum Summit that President Biden will host, focusing on strengthening ties and addressing shared challenges. The agenda highlights climate change, economic growth, and sustainable development as primary topics. The White House signals ongoing efforts to deepen partnerships with Pacific Island countries and announces further briefings for the press.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says President Biden will host Pacific Islands Forum leaders to reaffirm support and discuss climate, economic growth, and sustainable development.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre calls this the administration’s second summit with Pacific leaders and says the U.S. is deepening partnerships.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre notes the press will have a follow-up call with further details later in the afternoon.
  #### Senator Menendez questions
  Reporters ask whether the White House knew about Senator Menendez’s indictment and whether the President believes he should resign. Jean-Pierre replies that the White House learned about the matter like the public and will not comment because it is an active legal matter. She repeatedly defers to prosecutors, the senator’s office, and Senate leadership for next steps.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the White House learned of Senator Menendez’s indictment the same way the public did and will not comment on an active matter.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre declines to advise whether Senator Menendez should resign and defers to Senate leadership and prosecutors.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre emphasizes caution and repeatedly states she will not comment because the case is ongoing.
  #### UAW strike and mediation
  Reporters ask whether President Biden will join UAW picket lines after Shawn Fain invited him and whether the administration will intensify efforts as the strike expands. Jean-Pierre says there is no change to the President’s schedule to announce, reaffirms that the President supports unions, and says Acting Secretary of Labor Julie Su and Gene Sperling have been in regular contact with all parties. She explains Su and Sperling remained in Washington by mutual agreement to assist negotiations and that the administration will provide help if requested.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says she has no update on the President joining the UAW picket line and that there is no change to his schedule at this time.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre states President Biden is a strong supporter of unions and believes the union built the middle class.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says Acting Secretary Julie Su and Gene Sperling are in regular contact with negotiation parties and remained in Washington by mutual agreement to be most productive.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the administration will do everything it can to help if the parties request assistance.
  #### Border surge and enforcement
  In response to a question about 10,000 people crossing in a single day, Jean-Pierre reiterates the administration’s view that the immigration system is broken and summarizes actions taken: a comprehensive reform proposal, removals of more than 250,000 individuals since May 12, record funding, and hiring to bolster CBP. She cites a surge response at Eagle Pass where CBP vetted and processed over 2,500 people and criticizes House Republican proposals to cut border resources. The administration frames its actions as enforcement plus advocacy for bipartisan congressional action.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the administration has removed more than 250,000 individuals without legal basis since May 12.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the President secured record funding and that the administration has hired roughly 25,000 additional personnel to support border efforts.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre reports CBP surged resources to Eagle Pass and processed more than 2,500 individuals to clear the area where migrants had congregated.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre criticizes House Republican proposals that she says would cut CBP resources and hinder efforts to reduce trafficking of drugs into the U.S.
  #### Campaign remarks and Hatch Act
  Reporters ask about President Biden repeating a Charlottesville story at a fundraiser; Jean-Pierre declines to discuss campaign-event comments because of Hatch Act constraints. She frames the repeated story as the President speaking passionately about why he ran and cites his record and the vote totals that made him president, but avoids addressing pool reporting specifics.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre declines to discuss campaign-event comments and cites the Hatch Act as the reason.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the President spoke from the heart about why he ran and his record protecting democracy and the economy.
  #### Diplomatic issues and Ukraine
  Reporters press on several international questions: whether Five Eyes raised Canada's allegations about Indian involvement in a murder at the G20, whether Biden promised ATACMS to Zelenskyy, whether U.S.-China talks will produce a Biden-Xi meeting at APEC, and whether Zelenskyy’s Hill visits changed lawmakers’ views. Jean-Pierre repeatedly defers details of private diplomatic conversations to the National Security Advisor and will neither confirm reporting about ATACMS nor a potential Xi meeting, while affirming continued U.S. support for Ukraine and referencing a new security assistance package.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre defers discussions about Canada’s diplomatic concerns to the National Security Advisor and says she will not discuss private diplomatic conversations.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says she cannot confirm reporting that President Biden promised ATACMS but notes ATACMS are not categorically off the table.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre highlights that the bilateral between Presidents Biden and Zelenskyy signaled continued U.S. support and that the administration announced another weapons package.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says she has no new information about a potential Biden‑Xi meeting on the sidelines of APEC and would share details if available.
  #### Auto suppliers and shutdown impacts
  Reporters ask whether the administration is considering loans or grants for auto suppliers amid the UAW strike and whether a government shutdown could affect such aid or other programs like student loan payments and food safety inspections. Jean-Pierre declines to disclose negotiation details, says OMB is developing contingency plans for a potential shutdown, and indicates discussions are ongoing without offering specifics.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says she will not detail ongoing negotiations about potential loan or grant programs for auto suppliers.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the Office of Management and Budget is working with agencies on contingency planning for a potential shutdown and has no specifics to share.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre acknowledges questions about student loan payment restart and food safety operations and says those areas are under review in OMB contingency discussions.
  #### Scope of new gun office
  Reporters ask why the Domestic Policy Council cannot perform the same work as the new gun violence office and whether the office will address intersectional impacts and international trafficking. Jean-Pierre explains that the new office will specifically implement and accelerate the President’s executive actions and newly passed legislation, demonstrates an elevated White House commitment by existing separate structure, and will coordinate with NSC and DHS to address trafficking and equity across communities.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the new office will implement and accelerate key executive actions and the bipartisan law, focusing resources on delivery.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre explains that creating an office separate from the Domestic Policy Council signals stronger White House commitment to reducing gun violence.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says the office will coordinate with the National Security Council and Homeland Security to combat international trafficking and ensure it addresses impacts across communities.
  #### Middle East normalization process
  When asked about reports that Israel and Saudi Arabia can reach a normalization deal, Jean-Pierre echoes National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan that many key elements are on the table but no formal framework is ready. She emphasizes that normalization would include a component addressing Israeli-Palestinian issues and that significant diplomatic legwork remains.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre cites National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan in saying key elements for normalization are on the table but no formal framework exists yet.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says a normalization agreement between Israel and Saudi Arabia would include a serious component dealing with Israeli-Palestinian issues.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre stresses that complex diplomatic work and coordination among stakeholders remain to finalize any agreement.
  #### Administration role on shutdown
  In response to whether the President will broker talks to prevent a shutdown, Jean-Pierre reiterates that the President already helped broker the May bipartisan agreement and argues that it is House Republicans’ responsibility to keep the government open. She emphasizes the administration’s position that a deal is a deal and urges House Republicans to honor their commitments to avoid harming Americans.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre says President Biden helped broker the bipartisan agreement in May and that a deal is a deal.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre states that averting a shutdown is the responsibility of House Republicans and urges them to do their job.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre warns that a shutdown would put American families and essential programs at risk and repeats the administration’s call for Republicans to keep their promise.
