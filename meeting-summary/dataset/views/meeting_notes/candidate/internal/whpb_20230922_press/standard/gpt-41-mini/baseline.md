### Overview
In this briefing, the White House announces the establishment of the first-ever Office of Gun Violence Prevention, led by Stefanie Feldman and overseen by Vice President Harris, to accelerate efforts against the gun violence epidemic. Congresswoman Lucy McBath shares her personal loss to gun violence, underscoring the urgency of this initiative. The administration criticizes House Republicans for advancing extreme budget cuts risking a government shutdown that would harm essential programs, urging them to honor the bipartisan agreement brokered in May. President Biden signs legislation reforming the organ transplant system to improve access and save lives. The ongoing UAW strike expands, with the administration supporting union negotiations remotely. Immigration enforcement actions and border security funding are highlighted, alongside strong bipartisan support for Ukraine following President Zelenskyy's visit and a new weapons package announcement. The White House comments on Israel-Saudi normalization talks, emphasizing the complexity and inclusion of Palestinian issues. The briefing also addresses the distinct role of the new gun violence office, potential government shutdown impacts, and the administration's call for House Republicans to prevent a shutdown. Throughout, MS. Jean-Pierre provides detailed responses to questions, reflecting the administration's positions and ongoing efforts across these critical issues.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Establishment of Gun Violence Prevention Office
President Biden announces the creation of the first-ever White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention, overseen by Vice President Harris. This office aims to build upon existing legislative and executive actions to address the gun violence epidemic in the U.S. It will be led by Stefanie Feldman with deputies Greg Jackson and Rob Wilcox, focusing on accelerating gun safety reforms and coordination between states.
- MS. Jean-Pierre states President Biden will establish the first-ever White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention.
- Vice President Harris will oversee the new office, highlighting her leadership in gun violence prevention efforts.
- Stefanie Feldman will serve as director, joined by advocates Greg Jackson and Rob Wilcox as deputy directors.
- Representative Lucy McBath emphasizes the office will increase coordination and ensure implementation of existing gun safety legislation.
#### Congresswoman Lucy McBath's Personal Testimony
Congresswoman McBath shares her personal experience of losing her son to gun violence, underscoring the urgency of addressing the epidemic. She highlights the widespread impact of gun violence across communities and schools, and praises the President's decisive action in creating the new office to save lives.
- Congresswoman McBath recounts the tragic loss of her son to gun violence in 2012.
- She notes that over 100 people are shot and killed daily in the U.S., affecting diverse communities.
- McBath describes the trauma experienced by children due to lockdown drills in schools.
- She commends President Biden's establishment of the Office of Gun Violence Prevention as a historic and life-saving measure.
#### House Republicans and Government Shutdown Risks
The briefing addresses the ongoing risk of a government shutdown due to House Republicans advancing extreme appropriation bills with severe cuts. The administration criticizes Republicans for failing to keep the government running and warns of the negative impacts on programs like education, healthcare, and nutrition assistance. The White House calls on Republicans to honor the bipartisan budget agreement from May.
- MS. Jean-Pierre criticizes House Republicans for pushing extreme bills that risk a government shutdown.
- She cites quotes from Republican representatives acknowledging the likelihood of a shutdown.
- The administration warns that cuts would harm essential programs including Meals on Wheels and healthcare.
- The White House urges Republicans to abide by the bipartisan agreement supported by two-thirds of House Republicans in May.
#### Organ Transplant System Reform
President Biden signs bipartisan legislation aimed at improving the organ transplant system for over 100,000 people on waiting lists. The law breaks up the existing monopoly, introduces competition, removes funding caps, and modernizes the system to increase transparency and access, ultimately saving lives.
- MS. Jean-Pierre announces the President's signing of a law to reform the organ transplant system.
- The law dismantles the monopoly and allows Health and Human Services to contract with efficient providers.
- It removes funding caps to enable modernization of the transplant system.
- The reform aims to save lives by improving transparency and access for patients.
#### UAW Strike and Administration's Role
The United Auto Workers strike expands to 20 states, with the administration maintaining communication with both parties. While President Biden appreciates the union's invitation to the picket line, no visit is scheduled. Acting Secretary of Labor Julie Su and Gene Sperling remain engaged in negotiations from Washington, D.C., with a mutual agreement to continue talks remotely to facilitate a resolution.
- MS. Jean-Pierre confirms no current plans for President Biden to visit the UAW picket line.
- The administration values unions and supports UAW workers as central to the auto industry.
- Julie Su and Gene Sperling continue negotiations remotely, as mutually agreed with involved parties.
- The White House emphasizes the importance of ongoing talks to reach a win-win agreement.
#### Immigration and Border Security
The administration addresses questions about border crossings and immigration enforcement, highlighting actions taken since May 12th, including removal of over 250,000 individuals without legal status. It notes record funding secured for border security, hiring of additional CBP agents, and efforts to combat drug trafficking. The White House criticizes House Republicans for opposing funding and attempts to defund DHS.
- MS. Jean-Pierre states the administration has removed over 250,000 individuals without legal status since May 12th.
- Record funding was secured to enhance border security and hire 25,000 additional CBP agents.
- The administration highlights efforts to prevent large quantities of drugs from entering the U.S.
- House Republicans are criticized for opposing funding and proposing cuts to border security resources.
#### Bilateral Engagement with Ukraine
Following President Zelenskyy's visit to Washington, the administration reiterates strong bipartisan support for Ukraine. It announces a significant weapons package to aid Ukraine's counteroffensive and air defense. While not confirming specific reports about ATACMS weapons, the White House emphasizes ongoing commitment to Ukraine's defense and the importance of congressional support.
- MS. Jean-Pierre highlights the importance of the bilateral meeting between Presidents Biden and Zelenskyy.
- A fourth security assistance package was announced to support Ukraine's military efforts.
- The administration does not confirm reports about ATACMS but states they are not off the table.
- Confidence is expressed in continued bipartisan congressional support for Ukraine aid.
#### Israel-Saudi Arabia Normalization Talks
The White House comments on ongoing efforts toward normalization between Israel and Saudi Arabia, noting key elements are under discussion but no formal framework exists yet. It emphasizes that any agreement will address fundamental Israeli-Palestinian issues. The administration refrains from discussing specifics, highlighting the complexity and ongoing nature of the negotiations.
- MS. Jean-Pierre states that key elements for normalization are on the table but no formal agreement exists.
- The process requires significant work and involvement from all stakeholders.
- Normalization will include serious components addressing Israeli-Palestinian issues.
- The administration avoids commenting on specific negotiation details.
#### Role and Scope of the Gun Violence Prevention Office
The new Office of Gun Violence Prevention is designed to implement and expand key executive actions and legislative measures on gun safety. It operates separately from the Domestic Policy Council to demonstrate the administration's commitment and to accelerate efforts against the gun violence epidemic affecting multiple communities nationwide.
- MS. Jean-Pierre explains the office will focus on implementing and accelerating gun violence executive actions and legislation.
- The office's separation from the Domestic Policy Council underscores its importance and dedicated focus.
- Gun violence is described as an epidemic affecting diverse communities across the country.
- The office aims to reduce fear and trauma caused by gun violence in schools and public spaces.
#### Government Shutdown Impact and Administration Response
The administration warns of severe consequences if a government shutdown occurs, including risks to food safety inspections and federal employee pay. It emphasizes that the President fulfilled his role by brokering a bipartisan budget deal and calls on House Republicans to uphold their commitments to avoid harming American families and essential services.
- MS. Jean-Pierre outlines potential impacts of a shutdown on food safety and federal programs.
- The administration is planning contingencies but lacks specifics on program operations during a shutdown.
- The President brokered a bipartisan budget agreement that two-thirds of House Republicans supported.
- The White House holds House Republicans responsible for preventing a shutdown and urges them to do their job.
