### Overview
The Finance Committee meeting focused on the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting and the presentation of the annual financial audit results for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2022, by auditor Benjamin Lau from MGO. Key discussions included the clean opinion on the city's financial statements, concerns regarding the $26 million balance in the equipment maintenance fund, and the tracking of community benefit revenues. The committee unanimously recommended the adoption of the comprehensive financial report to the City Council, with members expressing appreciation for the finance team's efforts.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting held on February 28, 2022. All members present vote unanimously in favor of the motion to approve the minutes, confirming that there are no objections or comments from the public.
- Sally Lieber calls for a motion to approve the minutes.
- Mayor Ramirez motions for approval, seconded by Council Member Manichek.
- Victoria Labrador confirms unanimous approval from all committee members.

#### Annual Financial Audit Overview
Jesse Takahashi introduces Benjamin Lau, the auditor from MGO, to present the results of the annual financial audit for the fiscal year ending June 30, 2022. The audit is required by the city charter and aims to provide assurance that the financial statements are free of material misstatements, with MGO having served as the city's external auditor for seven years.
- Jesse Takahashi welcomes Benjamin Lau to present the audit results.
- The audit provides a clean opinion on the city's financial statements.
- The general operating fund finished the year with a $17.5 million balance.

#### Audit Findings and Financial Statements
Benjamin Lau presents the audit findings, noting that the city received a modified opinion on its financial statements, indicating no material misstatements. He highlights the implementation of a new accounting standard related to leases, which significantly impacts the city's balance sheet, and confirms that there were no findings on internal controls this year.
- Benjamin Lau states the audit resulted in a clean opinion on financial statements.
- The implementation of new lease accounting standards resulted in a significant lease receivable.
- No findings on internal control over financial reporting were identified.

#### Discussion on Fund Balances
Mayor Ramirez raises concerns about the equipment maintenance and replacement fund, which has a balance of $26 million, questioning whether the charges are too high given the historical expenditure patterns. Jesse Takahashi acknowledges the need to review the fund's charges and balances to ensure they align with actual needs.
- Mayor Ramirez questions the justification for the $26 million balance in the equipment fund.
- Jesse Takahashi agrees to review the fund's charges and expenditure history.
- The committee discusses the potential for adjusting charges based on historical data.

#### Community Benefit Tracking
The committee discusses the tracking of community benefit revenues from developers, noting that these funds are not always recorded in the annual financial statements. Jesse Takahashi explains that while there are mechanisms for tracking these funds, they are maintained in separate ledgers and not included in the comprehensive financial report.
- Lucas Ramirez inquires about tracking community benefit revenues over time.
- Jesse Takahashi confirms that community benefits are tracked in separate ledgers.
- The committee acknowledges the challenge of tracking unrestricted community benefit funds.

#### Public Safety Expenditures
Lisa Matichak questions the decrease in public safety expenditures, noting a significant drop compared to previous years. Grace Zheng clarifies that this is due to investment income impacting the overall expenditures for public safety, which includes pension contributions.
- Lisa Matichak observes a decrease in public safety expenditures.
- Grace Zheng explains the impact of investment income on public safety costs.
- The committee discusses the implications of these financial changes.

#### Recommendations to City Council
The committee concludes the meeting by discussing the recommendation to the City Council to accept the annual comprehensive financial report. Mayor Ramirez motions for the recommendation, which is seconded and unanimously approved by the committee members.
- Mayor Ramirez motions to recommend the adoption of the comprehensive financial report to the City Council.
- Lisa Matichak seconds the motion, leading to unanimous approval.
- Sally Lieber expresses gratitude to the finance team for their work.
