### Overview
The Environmental Planning Commission meeting focuses on procedural actions, the status of the 2023–2031 Housing Element, and annual leadership elections. After unanimously approving the December 7, 2022 minutes and receiving no general public comments, the Commission hears Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson explain that, following a same-day meeting with HCD, staff received significant new guidance requiring recategorization of sites, stronger findings for non-vacant parcels, additional programs, and enhanced outreach to non-English speakers, leading staff to recommend continuing the housing element hearing to a future date. Commissioners Gutierrez and Nunez probe how community letters and advocacy, particularly from Spanish-speaking organizations, will feed into revisions and how staff will document both compliance and meaningful engagement, while Anderson and colleague Stephanie describe an iterative process with HCD that integrates public input, legal analysis (including points raised in outside counsel’s letter), and targeted outreach, all under an informal goal of Council adoption by late March to preserve grant eligibility. Public commenters from the Housing Action Coalition, League of Women Voters, and local advocacy groups use the continuance motion to urge a more robust, evidence-based sites inventory, stronger commitments on fair housing, displacement, and tools like COPA and CLTs, and clearer program timelines, and the Commission ultimately votes unanimously to continue the item in line with staff’s recommendation. In new business, Commissioner Nunez successfully nominates Vice Chair Yen as the new chair and Chair Yen nominates Commissioner Dempsey as vice chair, both elected by roll call after a brief opportunity for public comment, with Cranston remaining in the chair for the rest of this session; commissioners Haymeyer, Yen, and Dempsey publicly thank Cranston for his steady leadership during challenging remote years. The meeting closes with Anderson confirming that there will be no January 18 meeting, previewing a tentative February 1 item on an affordable housing and public storage project on Terra Bella, and outlining plans for the Commission to return to in-person chambers on February 1, after which Cranston adjourns the session at 7:38 PM.

#### Housing element hearing continuance recommendation
The Commission takes up Item 5.1, the 2023–2031 Housing Element, with staff recommending that the scheduled public hearing be continued to a future, unspecified date. Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson explains that the City recently met with the California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD), which provided substantive new comments requiring revisions to the draft. Staff concludes that the Environmental Planning Commission (EPC) should not act on the current draft until those changes are incorporated, and outlines alternatives including opening the hearing without action or proceeding despite HCD feedback, while emphasizing continuous community input opportunities.
- Chair Cranston introduces Item 5.1, the 2023–2031 Housing Element, and notes that staff has distributed a memo recommending continuance of the public hearing to a future date.
- Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson states that the City submitted a draft housing element to HCD in November and that the draft attached to the staff report reflects informal HCD comments and December Council direction.
- Eric Anderson reports that in a meeting held earlier the same day, HCD provided additional substantive comments, including recategorizing some parcels as pipeline versus inventory, requiring additional findings, adding programs, and expanding opportunities for non-English speakers to comment.
- Eric Anderson advises that, based on HCD’s new input, staff does not recommend EPC action on the current draft and instead will prepare a revised draft, post it publicly, and schedule a new noticed hearing.
- Eric Anderson states that alternatives include opening the public hearing and taking no action or opening the hearing while still forwarding a recommendation, but reiterates that staff will keep soliciting input on the sites inventory, programs, and other housing element components.
#### Public engagement on housing element and staff response
Commissioners question staff about how public comments and advocacy around the housing element will be handled during the continuance period. Commissioner Gutierrez asks how community members who submitted letters can further engage, and Eric Anderson confirms staff is willing to meet individually or with groups and notes that they have already held several meetings over recent months. Staff underscores that there will be multiple future opportunities for input through outreach, state-mandated draft publication, and EPC proceedings.
- Commissioner Gutierrez asks whether individuals and groups who submitted letters about housing element concerns can contact the staff member Eric Anderson mentioned to continue discussing their issues.
- Eric Anderson replies that staff is happy to talk with anyone who provided comments and notes that they have already met with several groups over the last several months while refining the draft housing element.
- Eric Anderson emphasizes that the current meeting is not the only opportunity for public input, citing ongoing outreach, state-required draft publication, and the requirement to publish materials ahead of any EPC deliberation or action.
#### Motion to continue housing element item
Following staff’s presentation, the Commission considers whether to continue the housing element item. Commissioner Clark moves to continue the item in line with staff’s recommendation, and Commissioner Nunez seconds. Chair Cranston then opens the motion itself to public comment before the Commission deliberates and votes. This step aligns the Commission’s process with staff’s request to defer action until HCD-related revisions are complete.
- Chair Cranston explains that the next step is for a commissioner to make a motion regarding the housing element item, which will then be open to discussion and public comment.
- Commissioner Clark moves to continue the housing element item as recommended by staff, and Commissioner Nunez seconds the motion.
#### Public comment on continuance and housing element content
Several public speakers comment on the motion to continue and offer substantive feedback on the housing element’s content, even though the Commission is not taking up the merits of the draft that evening. A representative of the Housing Action Coalition raises concerns about compliance, non-vacant site assumptions, and the adequacy of the site inventory, while speakers from the League of Women Voters and Mountain View advocacy groups emphasize program specificity, fair housing timing, and commitments to anti-displacement measures. Chair Cranston clarifies that the scope of the discussion is the continuance motion, but allows comments that also provide directional input to staff for future revisions.
- Clerk Whitehall identifies Allie Saperman as the first speaker on the motion to continue, and she speaks on behalf of the Housing Action Coalition about the housing element’s compliance issues.
- Allie Saperman states that without significant changes the housing element will not comply with state law and HCD requirements, citing non-vacant pipeline sites that lack evidence-based analysis showing existing uses will cease and urging the City to identify additional sites, especially in high-opportunity, transit-rich areas.
- Allie Saperman notes that approximately 1,500 units counted in a past RHNA cycle are included again and encourages adding more sites so Mountain View can maximize housing capacity and remain accessible to more residents.
- Allie Saperman offers to continue meeting with HCD, City staff, and Planning Commission members to help shape a compliant housing element that achieves a strong outcome for all stakeholders.
- Chair Cranston reminds attendees that the Commission is only considering the motion to continue the item and not taking public comment on the substance of the housing element itself at this time.
- Clerk Whitehall next recognizes Kevin Ma, who speaks on behalf of the League of Women Voters and expresses support for staff’s effort to properly categorize pipeline sites and document development likelihood over the next eight years.
- Kevin Ma voices concern that many programs in the December draft are vague, particularly regarding what the City will concretely do over the next eight years, and notes that fair housing-related programs are scheduled too late in the cycle contrary to HCD’s guidance.
- Kevin Ma criticizes low proposed densities for church sites compared to recent practice, the lack of specificity around the City’s financial contribution to accessory dwelling unit (ADU) fee programs, and the weak commitments around homelessness, displacement response, and tools such as COPA and community land trusts, which he says are framed as possible future pursuits rather than firm commitments.
- Clerk Whitehall then recognizes David Watson, who states he is with Mountain View and shares generally the same concerns as Kevin Ma about the housing element’s direction.
- David Watson invites commissioners and staff to consult his group’s website, which summarizes their housing element recommendations.
#### Alignment of HCD and community feedback; translation and engagement
Commissioners further probe how HCD’s feedback interacts with community advocacy and what evidence and outreach will be needed for HCD to deem the housing element compliant. Commissioner Nunez asks whether HCD’s guidance reflects community comments and what level of documentation HCD expects, particularly regarding outreach and engagement of non-English-speaking residents. Eric Anderson and staff member Stephanie explain that HCD considers public comments in its statutory review, expects an ongoing dialogue, and looks for clear translation and engagement efforts tailored to local needs rather than a fixed numeric standard. Commissioner Nunez stresses that translation alone does not equal engagement and urges staff to leverage proactive input from Spanish-speaking community organizations.
- Commissioner Nunez asks staff to what extent HCD’s additional guidance aligns with the types of feedback the City is receiving from community groups on the housing element.
- Eric Anderson explains that HCD acts as a judge for statutory compliance while also considering public comments submitted directly to the state, and that these comments can influence HCD’s review and future feedback to the City.
- Eric Anderson notes that the housing element process is iterative, with staff adjusting the draft in response to HCD guidance, Council direction, and public input, and that there are multiple ways to address comments including program changes, added analysis, or new data.
- Eric Anderson confirms that HCD indicated their review and integration of public comments is ongoing and that future community input can still affect state feedback and required adjustments.
- Commissioner Nunez expresses concern about missing opportunities to satisfy both legal compliance and community needs and asks what kind of evidence staff must provide at the next critical HCD meeting to demonstrate that requirements—such as outreach to non-English speakers—have been met.
- Eric Anderson responds that a key factor is making translated materials available early enough for meaningful review and invites colleague Stephanie to elaborate on standards for language access.
- Stephanie states that there is no single objective statewide metric for adequate translation; instead, expectations vary by community, and the City must clearly explain to HCD how it has provided translation and outreach to date and how it will address identified gaps going forward.
- Stephanie frames the translation and engagement approach as an ongoing conversation with HCD in which the City outlines past efforts, incorporates community and state feedback, and adjusts its process during upcoming revision rounds.
- Commissioner Nunez emphasizes that Mountain View performs relatively well on translation but argues that translation is not the same as engagement, highlighting that proactive Spanish-speaking groups have submitted specific asks that should be valued and integrated into the process.
#### Addressing legal comments and Spanish-speaking outreach
Commissioners turn to the legal and analytical demands raised by HCD and outside counsel, as well as broader outreach to Spanish-speaking residents. Commissioner Gutierrez asks about a letter from the Farella Braun + Martel law firm and how staff plans to respond to its issues. Eric Anderson confirms that staff discussed the letter with HCD, has a plan and path forward, and must complete analysis and findings to meet statutory expectations for non-vacant sites, particularly demonstrating the absence of barriers to residential development. Gutierrez also suggests holding future in-person meetings specifically geared toward Spanish-speaking community members once revisions are more concrete, and Eric Anderson welcomes the input while noting the need to develop a detailed game plan.
- Commissioner Gutierrez asks Eric Anderson whether HCD discussed issues raised in a letter from attorney Thomas Mayhew of Farella Braun + Martel and whether staff has a plan to address those points.
- Eric Anderson confirms that staff discussed the letter in detail with HCD and says there is a plan and analytical work underway, although he declines to specify exact responses before the analysis is complete.
- Eric Anderson stresses that HCD is particularly focused on findings and evidence for non-vacant sites, clarifying that the state does not require proof or guarantees of redevelopment but does expect analysis showing there are no major barriers to residential use.
- Commissioner Gutierrez asks whether, once language and program revisions are more defined, staff might hold a meeting to present updates to Spanish-speaking residents more broadly, potentially at City Hall or a local park.
- Eric Anderson responds that HCD’s new guidance is still fresh and staff must develop a comprehensive game plan, but he appreciates the suggestion for expanded in-person outreach to Spanish-speaking community members.
#### Timeline and grant eligibility for housing element
Commissioner Haymeyer inquires about the expected timeline for housing element revisions and when the item might return to the Commission and Council. Eric Anderson states that staff will work as quickly as possible but cannot commit to specific meeting dates yet, given unknown analytical workloads. He notes that some grants have application timelines beginning around the end of March, and staff aims to have Council adoption by then to preserve eligibility for those funding opportunities. This frames the schedule pressures around the housing element update while acknowledging inherent uncertainties.
- Commissioner Haymeyer asks whether staff can share a general timeline for the next steps on housing element revisions.
- Eric Anderson replies that staff will work on revisions as fast as they can but cannot yet promise specific EPC or Council dates because the amount of required analysis is not fully known.
- Eric Anderson notes that some grant application periods begin around the end of March and identifies that as an informal target for Council adoption so the City remains eligible for as many grants as possible.
#### Vote to continue housing element item
After discussion and public comment, the Commission votes on the motion to continue the housing element item. With no further commissioner comments, Clerk Whitehall calls the roll. All commissioners present vote in favor, and the motion to continue the item to a future date passes unanimously. This defers formal action on the housing element until staff incorporates HCD and community feedback into a revised draft and schedules a new public hearing.
- Chair Cranston confirms there is no further Commission discussion on the housing element continuance motion and asks Clerk Whitehall to call the roll.
- Clerk Whitehall conducts a roll call vote in which all commissioners present vote ‘aye’ on the motion to continue the housing element item.
- Clerk Whitehall announces that the motion carries unanimously, formally continuing Item 5.1 to a future, unspecified date.
