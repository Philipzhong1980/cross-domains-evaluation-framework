### Overview
The Environmental Planning Commission convened to approve prior meeting minutes, discuss the 2023-2031 Housing Element, elect new leadership, and receive staff updates. The commission unanimously approved the December 7, 2022 minutes. Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson presented substantive comments from the California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD) on the draft Housing Element, leading staff to recommend continuing the public hearing to allow for revisions and further community input. Public commenters, including representatives from the Housing Action Coalition and League of Women Voters, voiced concerns about site inventory compliance and program clarity. Commissioners emphasized the importance of meaningful engagement with non-English speaking communities. The commission unanimously elected Vice Chair Yen as Chair and Commissioner Dempsey as Vice Chair for 2023, with outgoing Chair Cranston recognized for his service. Staff announced a tentative February 1 meeting to review affordable housing and storage projects and preparations for returning to in-person meetings. The meeting was adjourned at 7:38 PM.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Minutes Approval
The Environmental Planning Commission (EPC) reviewed and unanimously approved the minutes from their December 7, 2022 meeting. This procedural step ensures accurate record-keeping and continuity for the commission's work.
- Chair Cranston called for a motion to approve the minutes, which Commissioner Clark moved and Vice Chair Yen seconded.
- The roll call vote resulted in unanimous approval of the minutes as confirmed by Clerk Whitehall.
#### 2023-2031 Housing Element Continuance
The EPC discussed the draft 2023-2031 Housing Element, which the city submitted to the California Department of Housing and Community Development (HCD) in November. Staff recommended continuing the public hearing to a future date due to substantive additional comments from HCD requiring revisions. This matter is critical as the Housing Element guides local housing policy and compliance with state law.
- Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson summarized HCD's informal comments and council direction, noting the need for recategorizing sites and additional findings.
- Staff recommended continuing the item to allow for revisions and further public input before EPC action.
- Commissioner Gutierrez confirmed that community members can continue to provide input to staff and HCD.
- Commissioner Clark moved to continue the item, seconded by Commissioner Nunez, and the motion passed unanimously.
- Public commenters, including Allie Saperman of the Housing Action Coalition and Kevin Ma of the League of Women Voters, expressed concerns about site inventory compliance, program vagueness, and fair housing commitments.
- Eric Anderson explained that HCD's review incorporates public comments and that staff is developing a plan to address all feedback, including outreach to Spanish-speaking communities.
- Commissioners discussed the importance of meaningful engagement beyond translation and the timeline aiming for council adoption by the end of March to qualify for grants.
#### Election of EPC Chair and Vice Chair
The commission conducted elections for the 2023 Chair and Vice Chair positions. Vice Chair Yen was nominated and accepted the Chair role, while Commissioner Dempsey was nominated and accepted the Vice Chair role. This leadership transition ensures continued governance and direction for the EPC.
- Commissioner Nunez nominated Vice Chair Yen for Chair, who accepted the nomination.
- The commission unanimously approved Vice Chair Yen as Chair with one abstention from Yen.
- Vice Chair Yen nominated Commissioner Dempsey for Vice Chair, who accepted.
- The commission unanimously approved Commissioner Dempsey as Vice Chair.
- Outgoing Chair Cranston was thanked for his two years of service, with appreciation expressed by Commissioners Haymeyer, Yen, and Dempsey.
#### Commission Staff Announcements and Updates
Staff provided updates on upcoming meetings and logistics. The next EPC meeting is tentatively scheduled for February 1, 2023, to consider an affordable housing project and a Public Storage project. The commission will return to in-person meetings at the chambers with safety protocols.
- Eric Anderson confirmed no meeting on January 18, 2023, and tentatively scheduled items for February 1.
- Staff will reacquaint commissioners with the chambers and conduct small group visits to prepare for in-person meetings.
- Chair Cranston adjourned the meeting at 7:38 PM.
