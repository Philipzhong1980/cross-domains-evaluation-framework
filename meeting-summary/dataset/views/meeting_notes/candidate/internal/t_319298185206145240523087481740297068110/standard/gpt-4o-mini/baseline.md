### Overview
The Environmental Planning Commission meeting on January 4, 2023, focused on reviewing the minutes from the previous meeting, discussing the 2023-2031 housing element, and electing new leadership. The minutes were approved unanimously, and no public comments were received during the initial segments. Eric Anderson presented a recommendation to continue the public hearing on the housing element due to substantive comments from HCD, prompting public input that raised concerns about compliance and site inventory adequacy. Following discussions, Vice Chair Yen was elected as the new Chair, and Commissioner Dempsey was elected as Vice Chair. The meeting concluded with announcements about the next meeting scheduled for February 1, 2023.

### Detailed Summary
#### Minutes Review
The meeting begins with a review of the minutes from the Environmental Planning Commission meeting held on December 7, 2022. The minutes are approved unanimously after a motion by Commissioner Clark and a second by Vice Chair Yen, indicating a smooth start to the meeting.
- Commissioner Clark moves approval of the minutes.
- Vice Chair Yen seconds the motion.
- The motion carries unanimously.

#### Public Comment Period
During the public comment period, no members of the public express interest in commenting on the minutes or any non-agenda items. This indicates a lack of immediate public concern or interest in the topics at hand during this segment of the meeting.
- No public comments are received on the minutes.
- No public comments are received on non-agenda items.

#### Housing Element Discussion
The main agenda item is the discussion on the 2023-2031 housing element. Staff recommends continuing the public hearing due to substantive comments received from the Housing and Community Development (HCD) department, which necessitate further revisions to the draft housing element. Eric Anderson, the Advanced Planning Manager, outlines the need for additional findings and community input before proceeding.
- Eric Anderson summarizes the memo recommending continuance of the public hearing.
- Staff met with HCD earlier and received substantive comments that require addressing.
- The recommendation is to continue the item until necessary changes are made.

#### Public Input on Housing Element
Public comments are received regarding the housing element, highlighting concerns about compliance with state law and the adequacy of the site inventory. Allie Saperman from the Housing Action Coalition emphasizes the need for evidence-based analysis of non-vacant sites and encourages the city to identify more sites for housing development.
- Allie Saperman stresses the importance of compliance with HCD requirements.
- Saperman urges the city to maximize housing opportunities by adding more sites.
- Kevin Ma from the League of Women Voters expresses concerns about the vagueness of the draft programs.

#### Commissioner Discussion on Housing Element
Commissioners engage in a discussion about the feedback from HCD and the public. They express the importance of balancing community input with statutory compliance. Eric Anderson reassures that public comments will influence the revisions and that there will be ongoing opportunities for community engagement.
- Commissioner Nunez asks about the alignment of HCD feedback with community input.
- Eric Anderson explains the ongoing nature of the HCD review process and public comment integration.
- Commissioner Gutierrez suggests proactive outreach to Spanish-speaking communities.

#### Election of New Chair and Vice Chair
The meeting transitions to the election of a new Chair and Vice Chair for the Environmental Planning Commission. Vice Chair Yen is nominated and accepts the position of Chair, while Commissioner Dempsey is nominated and accepts the position of Vice Chair, marking a leadership change within the commission.
- Vice Chair Yen is nominated and accepts the role of Chair.
- Commissioner Dempsey is nominated and accepts the role of Vice Chair.
- The motion for both nominations carries unanimously.

The meeting concludes with announcements regarding future meetings. The next meeting is tentatively scheduled for February 1, 2023, with a focus on affordable housing projects. Staff will ensure that commissioners are prepared for the return to in-person meetings.
