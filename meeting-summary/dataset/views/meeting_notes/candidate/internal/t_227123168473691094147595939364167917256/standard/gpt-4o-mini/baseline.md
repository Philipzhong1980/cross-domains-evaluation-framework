### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting, led by Annette Lin, covered various topics including the approval of previous meeting minutes, discussions on absence policies, and an overview of the CDBG and HOME programs presented by Harsha Ramchandani. The commission reviewed proposed policy recommendations for these programs, addressed the potential use of funds for earthquake retrofitting, and received updates from subcommittees on their ongoing projects. Annette Lin announced her resignation, prompting expressions of gratitude from fellow commissioners. The meeting concluded with announcements regarding upcoming events and the continuation of virtual meetings.

### Detailed Summary
#### Meeting Opening and Roll Call
Annette Lin calls the meeting to order and outlines the meeting's virtual format due to the state of emergency. Roll call is conducted by Laurel James, confirming attendance and absences of commissioners.
- Annette Lin announces the meeting's virtual format due to emergency regulations.
- Laurel James confirms attendance of commissioners and notes absences.

#### Absence Policy Discussion
The commission discusses the classification of absences as excused or unexcused according to Council policy K02. General consensus is reached on the status of absent commissioners, with specific reasons provided for each absence.
- Annette Lin proposes to classify Vice Chair Sylvester's absence as unexcused due to personal matters.
- Commissioners unanimously agree on the classification of absences.

#### Approval of Previous Meeting Minutes
The minutes from the October 6th, 2022 meeting are presented for approval. After confirming no corrections, a motion is made and passed unanimously to approve the minutes.
- Annette Lin asks for a motion to approve the minutes from the previous meeting.
- Commissioner Jill Rakestraw motions to approve the minutes, which is seconded and passed.

#### Public Communications
Annette Lin opens the floor for public comments but notes no attendees are present. The commission moves on to the next agenda item without public input.
- Annette Lin states there are no public comments as no attendees are present.

#### CDBG and HOME Programs Overview
Harsha Ramchandani presents an overview of the Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and Home Investment Partnership (HOME) programs, detailing funding, goals, and upcoming policy recommendations. The presentation aims to gather feedback from the commission.
- Harsha Ramchandani outlines the purpose and funding of CDBG and HOME programs.
- The city receives approximately $620,000 in CDBG funds and $295,000 in HOME funds annually.

#### Policy Recommendations for CDBG and HOME
The commission discusses proposed policy recommendations for the upcoming funding cycle, including minimum funding thresholds and allocation strategies. The recommendations aim to enhance program implementation and align with the city's consolidated plan.
- Harsha Ramchandani recommends a minimum funding threshold of $7,500 for public service grants.
- The commission discusses the need for flexibility in funding allocations based on project needs.

#### Discussion on Earthquake Retrofitting
Commissioner Solomon raises the issue of earthquake retrofitting for buildings in Mountain View, questioning if CDBG or HOME funds could be utilized for this purpose. Staff agrees to investigate the feasibility of using federal funds for retrofitting.
- Julie Solomon inquires about funding for earthquake retrofitting projects.
- Michaela Helman-Tincher agrees to research the potential use of federal funds for retrofitting.

#### Subcommittee Updates
The commission receives updates from various subcommittees, including the Bystander Intervention Training and Housing and Security subcommittees. Each subcommittee shares their current focus and plans for future meetings.
- Commissioner Jill Rakestraw updates on the Bystander Intervention Training subcommittee's progress.
- Commissioner Webb and Annette Lin discuss the Housing and Security subcommittee's plans for community storytelling.

#### Chair Resignation Announcement
Annette Lin announces her resignation from the commission, effective immediately, to allow for the recruitment of new members. The commission expresses gratitude for her service and contributions.
- Annette Lin announces her resignation to facilitate the recruitment of new commissioners.
- Commissioners express their appreciation for Annette's leadership and contributions.

#### Upcoming Events and Announcements
Laurel James shares information about United Against Hate Week and upcoming events at the library. The commission discusses the continuation of virtual meetings through December 1st and plans for in-person meetings in January.
- Laurel James announces events for United Against Hate Week, including an author conversation and film screening.
- The City Council extends virtual meetings through December 1st, with plans to return to in-person meetings in January.
