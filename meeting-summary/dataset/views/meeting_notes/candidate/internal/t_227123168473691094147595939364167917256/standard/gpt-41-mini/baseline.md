### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting, chaired by Annette Lin, focused on several key areas including the approval of prior meeting minutes and a comprehensive presentation by housing staff on the City's Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and HOME Investment Partnership programs. Staff outlined proposed policy recommendations to enhance program administration and detailed the upcoming funding cycle, prompting commissioner inquiries about funding flexibility, particularly regarding earthquake retrofitting and minor home repairs. Subcommittee updates highlighted early-stage work on bystander intervention training and housing security initiatives, emphasizing community engagement and storytelling. Recruitment efforts for vacant Commission positions are progressing with interviews scheduled. Chair Lin announced her forthcoming resignation, receiving commendations from fellow commissioners. Staff also shared information about United Against Hate Week events and upcoming changes to meeting formats. The meeting concluded with plans to continue advancing housing and community programs and to welcome new Commission members.
  
### Detailed Summary

Roll call was conducted, and absences were classified as excused or unexcused based on Council policy K02, with Vice Chair Sylvester's absence marked unexcused and Commissioner Ball's absence excused due to illness.
- Chair Annette Lin explained the meeting is conducted via video conference under emergency provisions.
- Principal Management Analyst Laurel James conducted roll call and noted absences.
- Chair Lin proposed classifying absences per Council policy K02, which was agreed upon by commissioners.
- Vice Chair Sylvester's absence was deemed unexcused; Commissioner Ball's absence was excused due to illness.
#### Minutes Approval
The Commission reviewed and approved the minutes from the October 6th, 2022 meeting without corrections. A motion was made and seconded, followed by a roll call vote confirming approval.
- Chair Lin presented the October 6th minutes for approval.
- Commissioner Jill Rakestraw moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Commissioner Rakestraw.
- A roll call vote was conducted with all present commissioners voting to approve the minutes.
#### Overview of CDBG and HOME Programs
Housing staff presented an overview of the City's Community Development Block Grant (CDBG) and HOME Investment Partnership programs, including funding sources, program goals, and upcoming funding cycles. They proposed new policy recommendations to improve program implementation and sought feedback from the Commission.
- Housing Specialist Harsha Ramchandani and colleagues presented on CDBG and HOME programs.
- The City receives annual federal grants requiring a five-year consolidated plan and annual action plans.
- Proposed policy principles include aligning expenditures with consolidated plan goals, flexible capital fund administration, and establishing funding thresholds.
- Upcoming funding cycle includes issuing a Notice of Funding Availability (NOFA) in December 2022 with applications due in January 2023.
#### Commissioner Questions on Housing Programs
Commissioners asked about the possibility of using funds for earthquake retrofitting, the adequacy of minor home repair funding, and the status of affordable housing projects in the pipeline. Staff responded with historical context, program limitations, and plans for flexible fund allocation.
- Commissioner Julie Solomon inquired about funding for earthquake retrofitting; staff indicated federal restrictions and ongoing evaluation.
- Commissioner Jill Rakestraw questioned the $30,000 allocation for minor home repairs, noting it may be insufficient; staff explained the cautious approach based on past demand and capacity.
- Staff confirmed a robust affordable housing pipeline with several projects approved or anticipated, ensuring funds will be utilized effectively.
- Staff emphasized the policy recommendations formalize existing practices and provide flexibility for fund administration.
#### Subcommittee Updates: Bystander Intervention Training
The Bystander Intervention Training Subcommittee provided an oral update on their initial efforts to assess existing community training and develop accessible explanations of bystander intervention. They are in early stages and plan to report more in future meetings.
- Commissioner Jill Rakestraw reported on subcommittee meetings focusing on community training assessment.
- The subcommittee is working on simplifying explanations of bystander intervention for broader understanding.
- Next meeting scheduled to further develop plans and share findings.
#### Subcommittee Updates: Housing and Security
The Housing and Security Subcommittee met with community member Malia Perez from Reach Potential to discuss ideas and potential projects. They are in early planning stages, considering community storytelling and outreach efforts to amplify resident voices and support housing initiatives.
- Chair Annette Lin and Commissioners Webb and Sylvester form the subcommittee.
- Malia Perez from Reach Potential joined to provide community insights.
- Discussions include creating social media artifacts to share resident stories and enhance community engagement.
- The subcommittee is developing a framework and timeline for future activities.
#### Commissioner Reports and Recruitment Update
Commissioners shared updates on ongoing tasks, including the history of housing project transcript review. Staff reported successful recruitment with applicants for all vacant positions and upcoming interviews scheduled for November 21st with City Council.
