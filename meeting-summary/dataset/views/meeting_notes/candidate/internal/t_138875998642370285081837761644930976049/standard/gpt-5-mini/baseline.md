### Overview
  The January 10, 2023 Mountain View City Council meeting returns to the chambers in a new hybrid format and covers a mix of ceremonial recognitions, routine approvals, public input, and leadership transitions. The Council opened by announcing two closed-session liability claims (Paul Mansorian and Vijay Kumar) and recessed to closed session, later reporting unanimous denials of both claims. The meeting presented a proclamation honoring outgoing Council Member Sally Lieber for her city and regional leadership, with remarks from Lieber and public commendations including Supervisor Joe Simitian; Congresswoman Anna Eshoo and Foothill–De Anza representatives provided federal and district funding updates, including ARPA allocations and bond-funded affordable-housing plans. The Council unanimously approved the consent calendar—continuing virtual meetings under AB 361 and amendments to designate bus-only lanes on Charleston Road—heard oral comment requesting a temporary parking enforcement moratorium to protect people living in vehicles during storms, and formally recognized outgoing Mayor Lucas Ramirez. Re-elected council members took the oath of office, and the Council unanimously elected Allison Hicks as Mayor and Pat Showalter as Vice Mayor. The meeting concludes with procedural announcements about the hybrid format, upcoming meeting logistics, and adjournment.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Closed session announcement
  The meeting began with an announcement of two closed session liability claim items and an invitation for public comment on those items. The City Attorney identified the claimants and the relevant Government Code section. No members of the public spoke on the closed session items prior to recess into closed session.
- City Attorney Jennifer Logue announces two closed session items under Government Code Section 54956.9 involving claimants Paul Mansorian and Vijay Kumar.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez invites public comment on the closed session items and explains the process for in-person and virtual speakers.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez reports there were no members of the public who wished to speak on the closed session items before recessing into closed session.
  #### Hybrid meeting procedures
  Council outlined new hybrid meeting procedures now that meetings return in person after nearly three years. Staff explained restrictions on sharing audio/video from the lectern and directed presenters to submit materials ahead of meetings. The Council requested patience as staff and members work through procedural and technical issues in the hybrid format.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez explains this is the first in-person meeting in the chambers in almost three years and asks for patience with hybrid procedural and technical issues.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez instructs that audio/video presentations must be submitted to city.clerk@mountainview.gov by 4:30 PM on the meeting date, and that in-person speakers must complete a Blue Speaker Card.
- City Clerk Heather Glaser confirms all council members are present for the hybrid, in-person meeting.
  #### Proclamation for Sally Lieber
  The Council presented a proclamation recognizing outgoing Council Member Sally Lieber for her service and regional leadership, summarizing her committee work, policy achievements, and contributions to housing, environment, and equity. Lieber offered remarks thanking staff and colleagues and pledged continued community engagement in her new role on the State Board of Equalization. Several elected officials and community members provided public commendations.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez reads a proclamation detailing Sally Lieber's city and regional roles and accomplishments, including housing approvals and environmental leadership.
- Sally Lieber thanks city staff and council colleagues and commits to remain engaged with Mountain View while serving on the State Board of Equalization in her remarks.
- Supervisor Joe Simitian praises Lieber's tenacity and history of pushing bold measures in public service in his public comment.
- Meghan Fraley thanks Lieber for listening to activists and representing vulnerable community members in her public comment.
- Thida Cornes credits Lieber with accessibility advocacy and mentorship in her public comment.
  #### Consent calendar adoption
  The Council approved the consent calendar in a single motion with unanimous support, including two resolutions read by title. One resolution continued virtual meetings under AB 361 and the other amended a prior resolution to designate exclusive bus-only lanes on Charleston Road. The motion to adopt was introduced and seconded by councilmembers and passed without discussion.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei moves to adopt resolutions authorizing continued virtual meetings under AB 361 and amending a resolution to designate bus-only lanes on Charleston Road, as announced during the consent item presentation.
- Council Member Pat Showalter seconds the consent motion and the City Council votes unanimously to approve the consent calendar.
- City Clerk Heather Glaser notes the reading of titles for the attached resolutions was waived as part of the consent process.
  #### Oral communications: encampment concern
  A resident raised concerns about people living in their cars during storms and requested a temporary moratorium on enforcing the 72-hour parking rule during emergency weather conditions. The speaker asked the Council to consider the request as an emergency accommodation to reduce hardship for people temporarily sheltering in vehicles. No immediate action was taken; the comment was recorded as public input.
- Public speaker Maria Marroquin requests a moratorium on enforcement of the 72-hour parking rule during the recent storms to reduce hardship for people living in vehicles.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez acknowledges the public comment and records the request as input during the oral communications period.
- City Council does not take action on non-agenda oral communications due to state law restrictions, as noted by Mayor Ramirez.
  #### New business public remarks
  Regional and state officials and community partners addressed the Council on partnerships, funding, and collaboration. Congresswoman Anna Eshoo congratulated elected officials, highlighted $15.6 million secured through the American Rescue Plan for Mountain View, noted funding to convert the Crestview Hotel into permanent supportive housing, and referenced funding for a pedestrian undercrossing at Evelyn and Bernardo avenues. Patrick Ahrens of Foothill–De Anza College discussed bond allocations for affordable housing and county health clinic collaboration.
- Congresswoman Anna Eshoo congratulates reelected council members and states she secured $15.6 million for Mountain View through the American Rescue Plan in her remarks.
- Congresswoman Anna Eshoo announces federal support for converting the Crestview Hotel to permanent supportive housing and mentions funding for a pedestrian undercrossing at Evelyn and Bernardo avenues in her remarks.
- Patrick Ahrens, board president for Foothill–De Anza College and district director for Assemblymember Evan Low, reports the district's $898 million bond includes over $200 million allocated for affordable housing for students, faculty, and staff.
- Patrick Ahrens thanks the City Council for collaboration on housing options and mentions partnership with county supervisors on a county health clinic initiative.
  #### Recognition of outgoing Mayor
  The Council presented a formal proclamation and gifts recognizing outgoing Mayor Lucas Ramirez for his leadership in 2022, listing accomplishments across housing, economic recovery, parks, and regional representation. Colleagues offered brief remarks praising his listening skills, teamwork, policy analysis, and humor. Ramirez delivered parting remarks thanking staff, reflecting on the mayoral role as convenor, and expressing gratitude before transitioning back to a council role.
- Vice Mayor Alison Hicks reads a proclamation enumerating Mayor Lucas Ramirez's accomplishments, including housing approvals, economic recovery initiatives, parks openings, and regional service roles.
- Multiple council members praise Mayor Ramirez's listening, collaboration, analytical skills, and dedication to housing and community engagement during their remarks.
- Outgoing Mayor Lucas Ramirez thanks staff across departments by name, reflects on the mayor's power to convene, and offers closing remarks about public service and teamwork.
  #### Oaths and council remarks
  The City Clerk administered the oath of office to re-elected council members Allison Hicks, Ellen Kamei, and Lucas Ramirez. Re-elected members and newly seated officials gave remarks expressing priorities for their terms, emphasizing housing, climate action, transportation safety, inclusivity, and continued collaboration with staff and community. Several council members also thanked family and campaign teams for support.
- City Clerk Heather Glaser administers the oath of office to Allison Hicks, Ellen Kamei, and Lucas Ramirez as the newly elected/reelected council members are sworn in.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei outlines priorities for her second term including housing opportunities, transportation improvements, sustainability, and support for vulnerable residents in her remarks.
- Vice Mayor Alison Hicks thanks voters, campaign volunteers, and family, and emphasizes listening to residents at community events such as ice cream socials in her remarks.
  #### Mayor and Vice Mayor election
  Council held nominations and voted unanimously to elect Allison Hicks as Mayor and Pat Showalter as Vice Mayor. Council Member Ellen Kamei nominated Allison Hicks with a second from Lisa Matichak, and Margaret Abe-Koga nominated Pat Showalter with a second from Lisa Matichak. The City Clerk conducted roll call votes and confirmed unanimous support for both leadership positions.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei nominates Allison Hicks for Mayor and Council Member Lisa Matichak seconds the nomination.
- City Clerk Heather Glaser conducts a roll call and reports unanimous affirmative votes to elect Allison Hicks as Mayor.
- Council Member Margaret Abe-Koga nominates Pat Showalter for Vice Mayor with a second from Lisa Matichak, and the Council unanimously elects Pat Showalter as Vice Mayor per the City Clerk's roll call.
  #### Closed session final actions
  At the end of the meeting the City Attorney reported that the Council took final action in closed session to deny both liability claims that had been announced earlier. The claims involved alleged conduct by a Mountain View police sergeant and a misdirected mail-related claim; both denials passed unanimously. The report conveyed the claim names, underlying allegations, requested damages, and the Council's vote outcomes.
- City Attorney Jennifer Logue reports that the Council denied the liability claim filed by Paul Mansorian seeking $450,000 related to an alleged Fourth Amendment violation by a Mountain View Police sergeant.
- City Attorney Jennifer Logue reports that the Council denied the liability claim filed by Vijay Kumar regarding misdirected mail that sought multiple millions in damages.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez confirms the Council's closed session votes were six ayes, zero nos, and zero abstentions on both claim denials.
  #### Adjournment and next meeting
  The incoming Mayor adjourned the meeting and announced the next City Council meeting date and a post-meeting reception. The council confirmed the meeting end time and invited attendees to a Rotunda reception following the next meeting. No further substantive business was conducted.
- Incoming Mayor Alison Hicks announces the next City Council meeting will be in person with a virtual component on January 24, 2023 and invites attendees to refreshments in the Rotunda.
- Mayor Alison Hicks adjourns the meeting at approximately 8:03 PM.
