### Overview
The Mountain View City Council held a significant meeting on January 10, 2023, marking their first in-person session in nearly three years. Key topics included the recognition of former Council Member Sally Lieber for her contributions, the approval of routine consent items, and public comments addressing community concerns. The council elected Alison Hicks as the new mayor and Pat Showalter as vice mayor, with both leaders outlining their visions for the upcoming term focused on community engagement and addressing pressing issues like housing and climate change. Additionally, the council reported on closed session discussions regarding liability claims, which were denied unanimously, reflecting a cohesive approach to legal matters.

### Detailed Summary
#### Closed Session Announcements
City Attorney Jennifer Logue announces two items on the closed session agenda regarding liability claims against the city. These claims are significant as they involve potential financial implications for the city and require careful legal consideration.
- City Attorney Logue states that the first item involves a liability claim from Paul Mansorian regarding an alleged Fourth Amendment violation.
- The second item concerns a claim from Vijay Kumar related to mail forwarding issues, which seeks millions in damages.

#### Recognition of Sally Lieber
The council recognizes former Council Member Sally Lieber for her service and contributions to the city. This recognition is important as it highlights the impact of her work on various community issues, including housing and environmental policies.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez reads a proclamation detailing Sally Lieber's achievements during her tenure, including her advocacy for affordable housing and environmental issues.
- Council members express their gratitude and share personal anecdotes about Sally's contributions and character.

#### Public Comments on Presentation
Public comments are taken following the presentation recognizing Sally Lieber. This segment allows community members to express their appreciation and share their thoughts on the council's work and future initiatives.
- Community members, including Supervisor Joe Simitian, commend Sally Lieber for her dedication and service to the city.
- Several speakers express their hopes for continued advocacy on housing and community issues.

#### Consent Calendar Approval
The council moves to approve the consent calendar, which includes routine items that do not require separate discussion. This process is essential for maintaining the efficiency of council meetings.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei motions to adopt the consent calendar, which includes resolutions for virtual meetings and bus-only lanes.
- The motion passes unanimously, indicating council agreement on the routine matters.

#### Oral Communications
This section allows the public to address the council on any non-agenda items. It serves as a platform for community members to voice concerns or suggestions directly to their elected officials.
- Maria Marroquin speaks about the challenges faced by individuals living in their cars during severe weather, urging the council to consider a moratorium on parking regulations.
- Community members express gratitude for the council's efforts and share personal stories related to city services.

#### Election of New Mayor and Vice Mayor
The council conducts the election for the new mayor and vice mayor, marking a significant transition in leadership. This process is vital for ensuring continuity and fresh perspectives in city governance.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei nominates Vice Mayor Alison Hicks for mayor, citing her experience and commitment to community issues.
- Pat Showalter is nominated for vice mayor, with council members highlighting her extensive community involvement and leadership qualities.

#### Incoming Mayor's Remarks
Incoming Mayor Alison Hicks shares her vision for the upcoming term, emphasizing community engagement and addressing key issues such as housing and climate change. Her remarks set the tone for her leadership and priorities.
- Mayor Hicks expresses gratitude for the opportunity to serve and highlights the importance of making the city a fun and livable place.
- She outlines her goals for the term, including enhancing community services and addressing environmental challenges.

#### Closed Session Report
City Attorney Logue provides a report on the outcomes of the closed session discussions regarding liability claims. This transparency is crucial for public trust and accountability in city governance.
- The council voted to deny both liability claims presented during the closed session, indicating a unified stance on legal matters.
- The details of the claims are shared, emphasizing the council's commitment to addressing legal challenges responsibly.
