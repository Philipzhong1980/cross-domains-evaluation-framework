### Overview
The Mountain View City Council convened for a hybrid in-person meeting marking their first session in the chambers in nearly three years. The meeting began with a closed session addressing two liability claims, both of which the Council unanimously denied. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to honoring former Council Member Sally Lieber for her impactful service and recent election to the California Board of Equalization, with heartfelt remarks from council members and community leaders. The Council approved consent calendar items related to virtual meetings and bus lane amendments without opposition. Public comments highlighted concerns about parking regulations affecting vulnerable populations. Dignitaries including Congresswoman Anna Eshoo and local officials extended congratulations to re-elected council members and acknowledged federal and regional support for community projects. Outgoing Mayor Lucas Ramirez was recognized for his leadership during 2022, with colleagues praising his collaborative approach and dedication. Re-elected council members Allison Hicks, Ellen Kamei, and Lucas Ramirez took their oaths of office and shared their visions for the future, emphasizing housing, sustainability, and community engagement. The Council unanimously elected Alison Hicks as the new Mayor and Pat Showalter as Vice Mayor, both of whom expressed commitment to civility and teamwork. The meeting concluded with a closed session report and adjournment, setting the stage for continued governance in a hybrid format.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Closed Session Legal Claims
The City Council held a closed session to discuss two liability claims pursuant to Government Code Section 54956.9. The claims involved Paul Mansorian alleging unlawful seizure by a police sergeant and Vijay Kumar regarding misdirected mail by the USPS. The Council denied both claims unanimously, concluding the closed session without public comments.
- City Attorney Jennifer Logue announced two liability claims for closed session discussion.
- Council denied Paul Mansorian's claim alleging violation of Fourth Amendment rights with a 6-0 vote.
- Council denied Vijay Kumar's claim related to USPS mail forwarding errors with a 6-0 vote.
- No public comments were received on the closed session items.
#### Recognition of Former Council Member Sally Lieber
The Council presented a proclamation honoring Sally Lieber for her dedicated service on the Mountain View City Council and her recent election to the California Board of Equalization. Her contributions included leadership on housing, climate action, gun safety, and advocacy for vulnerable populations. Multiple council members and community speakers expressed gratitude and shared personal reflections on her impact.
- Mayor Lucas Ramirez and Vice Mayor Alison Hicks presented a proclamation recognizing Sally Lieber's service.
- Sally Lieber was acknowledged for her leadership on housing, environmental issues, and social justice.
- Council members Ellen Kamei, Pat Showalter, Margaret Abe-Koga, and Lisa Matichak shared personal appreciations for Lieber's contributions.
- Community members including Supervisor Joe Simitian and activists praised Lieber's dedication and impact.
- Lieber committed to staying engaged with the community despite her new role at the State Board of Equalization.
#### Consent Calendar Approval
The Council approved the consent calendar items unanimously, which included continuing virtual meetings pursuant to AB361 and amending a resolution for exclusive bus lanes on Charleston Road. No items were pulled for discussion and no public comments were received.
- Council Member Ellen Kamei moved to adopt resolutions related to virtual meetings and bus lane amendments.
- The motion was seconded by Council Member Pat Showalter and passed unanimously.
- No public comments or council member requests to discuss consent items were made.
#### Oral Communications from Public
Members of the public addressed the Council on various topics, including gratitude to Sally Lieber and concerns about the 72-hour parking rule affecting people living in cars during storms. The Council acknowledged these comments and expressed appreciation for community input.
- Maria Marroquin spoke about the hardships faced by people living in cars during storms and requested a moratorium on the 72-hour parking rule.
- Other public speakers expressed thanks to Sally Lieber for her service.
- No virtual public comments were received during this segment.
#### New Business Public Comments and Greetings
Several dignitaries and community representatives offered congratulations to re-elected council members and outgoing officials. Congresswoman Anna Eshoo highlighted federal funding secured for Mountain View projects. Patrick Ahrens from Foothill-De Anza College discussed affordable housing initiatives. Local school board member Thida Cornes expressed eagerness to collaborate with the Council.
- Congresswoman Anna Eshoo congratulated council members and highlighted $15.6 million in federal funding for Mountain View.
- Patrick Ahrens announced over $200 million allocated for affordable housing in the community college district.
- Thida Cornes, newly elected to the local school board, expressed commitment to working with the Council.
- Public speakers praised the Council's efforts and leadership.
#### Proclamation and Recognition of Outgoing Mayor Lucas Ramirez
Vice Mayor Alison Hicks presented a detailed proclamation honoring Mayor Lucas Ramirez for his leadership during 2022. Ramirez was recognized for advancing housing, economic vitality, community safety, and sustainability initiatives. Council members shared personal reflections on his collaborative style, thoughtfulness, and dedication. Ramirez delivered farewell remarks emphasizing the mayor's role as a convener and praised city staff.
- Vice Mayor Alison Hicks read a proclamation listing Ramirez's accomplishments as mayor.
- Council members Ellen Kamei, Lisa Matichak, Margaret Abe-Koga, and Pat Showalter praised Ramirez's leadership qualities.
- Mayor Ramirez thanked city staff and colleagues, highlighting the importance of collaboration.
- Ramirez reflected on the mayoral role as a convening position rather than unilateral policymaking.
- Gifts from local artists were presented to Ramirez in appreciation of his service.
#### Oath of Office for Re-elected Council Members
Council Members Allison Hicks, Ellen Kamei, and Lucas Ramirez took the oath of office administered by City Clerk Heather Glaser, affirming their commitment to support and defend the constitutions of the United States and California and to faithfully discharge their duties.
- City Clerk Heather Glaser administered the oath of office to Hicks, Kamei, and Ramirez.
- Each council member solemnly affirmed their duties and allegiance to the constitutions.
- The ceremony marked the official start of their new terms.
#### Remarks by Re-elected Council Members
Council Members Ellen Kamei, Allison Hicks, and Lucas Ramirez shared remarks reflecting on their service, the challenges of the pandemic, and their priorities moving forward. They emphasized collaboration, community engagement, housing, climate action, and making Mountain View a welcoming and livable city.
- Council Member Kamei highlighted the Council's collegiality and commitment to housing and sustainability.
- Vice Mayor Hicks expressed gratitude to voters and emphasized the value of hybrid meetings for community participation.
- Council Member Ramirez thanked supporters and staff, acknowledging the challenges of balancing public service with personal commitments.
- All expressed enthusiasm for continuing to serve and address community needs.
#### Nomination and Election of New Mayor and Vice Mayor
Council Member Ellen Kamei nominated Vice Mayor Alison Hicks as the new Mayor, seconded by Lisa Matichak. Hicks was unanimously elected by roll call vote. Council Member Margaret Abe-Koga nominated Pat Showalter as Vice Mayor, seconded by Lisa Matichak, and Showalter was also unanimously elected. Both incoming leaders shared remarks emphasizing collaboration, civility, and community engagement.
- Ellen Kamei nominated Alison Hicks for Mayor, seconded by Lisa Matichak.
- Alison Hicks was unanimously elected Mayor by roll call vote.
- Margaret Abe-Koga nominated Pat Showalter for Vice Mayor, seconded by Lisa Matichak.
- Pat Showalter was unanimously elected Vice Mayor by roll call vote.
- Both Hicks and Showalter delivered remarks focusing on teamwork, community priorities, and the importance of respectful governance.
#### Closed Session Report and Meeting Adjournment
City Attorney Jennifer Logue reported on the Council's actions in closed session, confirming the denial of two liability claims. The meeting was adjourned at 8:03 PM with an announcement of the next meeting date and an invitation for refreshments in the Rotunda.
- City Attorney Logue summarized the denial of claims by Paul Mansorian and Vijay Kumar.
- The Council took final action with unanimous votes on both claims.
- The meeting was adjourned at 8:03 PM by Mayor Alison Hicks.
- The next City Council meeting was announced for January 24, 2023, with refreshments offered.
