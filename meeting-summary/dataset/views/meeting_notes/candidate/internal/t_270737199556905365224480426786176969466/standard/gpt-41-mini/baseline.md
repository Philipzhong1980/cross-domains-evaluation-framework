### Overview
The Visual Arts Committee meeting, chaired by Jesse Cupp with key contributions from John Lang and Vice Chair Susi Merhar, focused on advancing Mountain View's public art initiatives. The committee approved previous meeting minutes and discussed aligning the newly approved North Special Master Plan's community spaces with the visual arts strategy. Staff previewed a busy September agenda featuring public art projects and street treatments. Updates on ongoing art installations and the public art inventory highlighted efforts to catalog and map artworks citywide, addressing challenges like missing pieces and defining public art scope. Economic impact studies underscored the value of arts to the local economy, supporting advocacy for funding. The committee unanimously approved the updated work plan, setting priorities for the coming year. A public inquiry clarified the purpose of the public art strategy as a roadmap for policy and program development. The committee reviewed and provided detailed feedback on the strategy's vision, goals, and initiatives, emphasizing inclusivity, diversity, broader collaboration, and flexible funding mechanisms. Finally, scheduling adjustments for the Call for Artists and expanded exhibition space were discussed to enhance artist participation and community engagement.

Committee members considered aligning this with the visual arts strategy to advocate for artist spaces, galleries, and makerspaces early in the planning process. #### Public Art Inventory and Mapping
The ad hoc subcommittee presented progress on cataloging Mountain View's public art, including city-commissioned and privately accessible pieces. Challenges include missing artworks, unclear ownership, and defining what constitutes public art, especially regarding indoor pieces. The committee discussed updating the public art map using GIS technology and considered expanding collaboration with other city departments and organizations to maintain and enhance the inventory.
- Susi Merhar and Cliff Grant detailed efforts to update and expand the public art inventory spreadsheet.
- Discussion addressed missing artworks and the need for a formal deaccessioning process.
- John Lang proposed leveraging GIS and story maps to improve public access and visualization of art locations.
- Committee considered the inclusion of indoor artworks and the challenges of private property access.
#### Economic Impact of Arts

Data showed significant spending by event attendees, supporting local businesses and generating jobs and tax revenue. The committee recognized the importance of quantifying arts' value to advocate for continued support and funding.
- Jesse Cupp presented studies demonstrating arts' positive economic impact in Santa Clara County.
- John Lang emphasized using this data to educate the community and support public art initiatives.
- Committee discussed leveraging these findings to justify policy changes and funding requests.

#### Public Inquiry on Art Strategy
A member of the public, Jasmin Aber, inquired about the nature and final product of the public art strategy. Staff explained that the strategy serves as a roadmap for increasing public art in Mountain View through policy changes, partnerships, and funding requests. The strategy is advisory to City Council and will be refined with public and stakeholder input before adoption.
- Jasmin Aber asked for clarification on the public art strategy's purpose and outcomes.
- John Lang described the strategy as a framework to guide policy and program development for public art.
- The strategy aims to facilitate more art opportunities and community engagement in Mountain View.
#### Public Art Strategy Vision, Goals, and Initiatives
Staff presented the vision statement, goals, and detailed initiatives of the public art strategy. The vision emphasizes enriching Mountain View's culture and supporting local artists. Goals focus on community building, placemaking, heritage reflection, and economic development. Initiatives include policy development, increasing art opportunities, and fostering collaborations. The committee provided feedback on inclusivity, diversity, participation, and broadening collaboration beyond arts organizations.
- John Lang reviewed the strategy's vision and goals developed with consultant input.
- Committee members suggested adding diversity, participation, and broader collaboration to the goals and initiatives.
- Discussion included expanding public art definitions to include events and interactive experiences.
- Suggestions were made to clarify funding mechanisms and policy language, especially regarding private development contributions.
#### Committee Feedback on Strategy Details
Committee members offered detailed comments on specific strategy points, such as expanding the scope of public art to include events and interactive art, emphasizing diversity and representation, and improving visibility through guided tours and online maps. They discussed funding allocation flexibility, maintenance of utility box art, and the importance of creating spaces for artists to work, live, and exhibit. Staff acknowledged the need for iterative refinement and planned to incorporate feedback for the September meeting.
- Vice Chair Susi Merhar emphasized including diversity, participation, and representation in goals.
- Jesse Cupp recommended adding pedestrian and bike trails and easements as locations for public art.
- Committee discussed creating flexible funding pools for art projects beyond specific CIP projects.
- Members suggested broadening collaboration to include businesses, government entities, and residents.
- John Lang noted the strategy document will be refined iteratively with committee input.

Committee members debated whether to have a theme for the call or allow open submissions, favoring the latter to encourage diverse and new artwork.
