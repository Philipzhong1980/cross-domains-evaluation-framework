### Overview
  Today’s briefing highlights two executive orders the President signs on International Women’s Day: one creating the White House Gender Policy Council and another directing a Department of Education review to ensure education free from sexual violence. Co-chairs Julissa Reynoso and Jennifer Klein describe the Council’s mission to address systemic bias, economic security, caregiving, healthcare, gender-based violence, education equity, and global gender equality, and Klein outlines staffing, reporting requirements, and interagency coordination including Special Assistants focused on gender-based violence and women of color. Klein also explains the Title IX review process and the Secretary of Education’s authority to suspend or revise inconsistent regulations while affirming fairness in procedures. Reporters press on immediate implementation questions — including whether DeVos-era Title IX rules remain in force (Klein says the Secretary should act as quickly as practicable), how the Council will respond to state transgender athlete bans (Klein points to federal tools and cross-council coordination), and details from a Presidential-directed border visit (Reynoso and Psaki report the delegation spoke with children, will brief the President, and emphasize protecting minors’ privacy). Psaki provides an extended overview of the American Rescue Plan’s benefits and timing, emphasizes the administration’s expectation that many Americans will receive stimulus relief by month’s end while deferring technical distribution details to Treasury, and reiterates the President’s preference to preserve the filibuster while continuing bipartisan outreach. The briefing also addresses capacity needs for unaccompanied children, vaccine disinformation and supply discussions (including upcoming industry meetings), international security concerns around Taiwan and Houthi attacks, and the administration’s continued support for voting-rights efforts while prioritizing pandemic relief and economic recovery.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Gender Policy Executive Orders
  The President signs two executive orders establishing a White House Gender Policy Council and directing the Department of Education to review Title IX and related guidance to guarantee education free from sexual violence. This action formalizes a government-wide commitment to gender equity and signals immediate administrative review of education policies. The announcement frames the orders as foundational to the Biden-Harris administration's approach to gender equity domestically and internationally.
- Ms. Psaki says the President signs two executive orders establishing the White House Gender Policy Council and directing the Department of Education to review regulations to guarantee education free from sexual violence.
- Julissa Reynoso says the Council will be formally established within the Executive Office of the President with a role in domestic and foreign policy development.
  #### Council Mission and Priorities
  Co-chair Julissa Reynoso outlines the Council's broad mission to advance gender equity as a matter of human rights, economic growth, health, and security. The Council intends to address systemic bias, sexual harassment, economic security, caregiving, healthcare access, gender-based violence, education equity, and global gender equality. Reynoso emphasizes attention to intersectional discrimination affecting people of color and LGBTQ+ communities.
- Julissa Reynoso says the Council will combat systemic bias and discrimination, including sexual harassment.
- Julissa Reynoso says the Council will focus on economic security, caregiving needs, and reducing wage and wealth gaps.
- Julissa Reynoso says the Council will advance gender equality globally through diplomacy, development, trade, and defense.
  #### Council Structure and Staffing
  Jennifer Klein describes the Council's operational role, including producing a government-wide strategy and an annual progress report, and evaluating policies, budgets, and data collection through a gender lens. The executive order designates multiple Special Assistants focused on gender-based violence and equity for women and girls of color, and requires Cabinet members to name senior designates to integrate work across agencies. Klein emphasizes collaboration with other White House policy councils and external partners at federal, state, local, tribal, private sector, and multilateral levels.
- Jennifer Klein says the Council must submit a government-wide strategy and an annual report to measure progress.
- Jennifer Klein says the Council will include a Special Assistant and Senior Advisor on Gender-Based Violence and at least two additional Special Assistants for gender policy.
- Jennifer Klein says one Special Assistant will focus specifically on equity for Black, Indigenous, Latina, and other women and girls of color.
- Jennifer Klein says the Council will coordinate closely with the Domestic Policy Council, Cabinet agencies, and outside partners.
  #### Title IX Review Order
  Klein explains the second executive order directs the Department of Education to review all regulations, guidance, and policies to ensure consistency with the administration's commitment to education free from sexual violence. The Secretary of Education is asked to consider suspending, revising, rescinding, or proposing for notice-and-comment any agency actions inconsistent with administration policy. The order affirms protections against sex discrimination including sexual orientation and gender identity and recognizes intersections with race, disability, and national origin.
- Jennifer Klein says the Secretary of Education will review existing regulations and consider suspending, revising, or rescinding inconsistent agency actions.
- Jennifer Klein says the administration's policy is that all students are entitled to an educational environment free from sexual harassment and violence.
- Jennifer Klein says the order explicitly recognizes discrimination based on race, disability, national origin, and that LGBTQ+ students experience significant discrimination.
  #### DOE Rules Enforcement Timeline
  Reporters ask whether the DeVos-Trump era Title IX rules remain in effect during the Department of Education's review and how quickly changes could occur. Klein states the Secretary is asked to act as quickly as practicable and to consider immediate rescission or revision where appropriate, but she does not pre-commit to specific regulatory outcomes. The administration emphasizes both urgency and a process-driven review.
- Aamer asks whether the administration will continue to enforce DeVos-era Title IX rules in the interim and how quickly the review will proceed.
- Jennifer Klein says the executive order asks the Secretary to consider immediate rescission or revision and to act as quickly as practicable.
  #### State Transgender Athlete Bans
  Reporters ask about state-level laws, such as Mississippi's ban on transgender athletes, and whether the Council will address those local measures. Klein responds that the federal government has tools—laws, policy clarity, and the bully pulpit—and that the Council will coordinate across the White House, particularly with the Domestic Policy Council and the National Security Council, to consider equity issues. The administration signals it will weigh federal responses but does not detail a specific enforcement plan in the briefing.
- Aamer asks whether the Council will address state laws banning transgender athletes from girls' or women's sports.
- Jennifer Klein says the administration will use federal laws, policy clarity, and coordination across the White House to consider those state-level equity issues.
  #### Border Visit and Migrant Children
  Julissa Reynoso describes a Presidential-directed visit to border facilities to evaluate conditions and reports that the delegation spoke with children and facility staff while acknowledging the system's challenges. She says the delegation plans to brief the President this week and emphasizes managing the humanitarian and logistical complexities. Psaki later confirms the delegation visited Border Patrol and Carrizo Springs facilities, stresses privacy protections for children, and says the administration plans to allow media access in a safe manner.
- Julissa Reynoso says the delegation was asked by the President to evaluate the situation on the ground and that they spoke with children and staff.
- Julissa Reynoso says the administration inherited a broken system and aims to manage the situation with attention to human costs.
- Ms. Psaki confirms the delegation visited a CBP facility and the Carrizo Springs shelter in Texas and says the White House plans to brief the President.
- Ms. Psaki says the administration is committed to allowing camera access to facilities in a way that protects children's privacy and safety.
  #### Due Process in Title IX
  Reporters ask whether the administration intends to change Title IX language guaranteeing due process for the accused in campus sexual misconduct cases. Klein affirms the administration's commitment to fair processes for all involved and says the Secretary of Education will review regulations with fairness as a guiding principle. No specific regulatory change is promised pending the Department's review.
- An unidentified reporter asks whether removing due process protections for the accused is contemplated under the review.
- Jennifer Klein says the administration endorses fair and full processes for both accusers and the accused and will have the Secretary examine regulations with that principle in mind.
  #### Women and Economic Recovery
  Reporters ask how the Council will address the disproportionate economic harm COVID-19 has caused women, particularly women of color, and what role the American Rescue Plan (ARP) plays. Reynoso and Klein explain the ARP provides immediate relief—direct payments, child tax credit expansion, unemployment insurance, school reopening support, and childcare stabilization—while the Council plans long-term structural reforms addressing caregiving, paid leave, affordable childcare, and wage inequities. They emphasize collaboration across White House councils to integrate gender equity into economic policy.
- Geoff asks about plans to boost women's workforce participation, especially for Black and Latino women hit hardest by the pandemic.
- Julissa Reynoso says the American Rescue Plan will advance economic interests and that the Council will pursue long-term structural changes to address discrimination and caregiving barriers.
- Jennifer Klein says ARP provisions provide immediate relief like child tax credit expansion and childcare stabilization while longer-term policies like paid family leave require structural change.
  #### Council Short-Term Role
  Reporters press whether the Gender Policy Council will act immediately to help women currently dropping out of the workforce due to pandemic pressures. Reynoso and Klein describe active, immediate coordination with the Domestic Policy Council, National Economic Council, and other entities to integrate gender equity into ongoing economic policy and to leverage ARP provisions for near-term relief. They position the Council as both an immediate advocate and a driver of systemic, long-term policy changes.
- Katie asks whether the Council will make immediate recommendations to help women dropping out of the workforce now.
- Julissa Reynoso says the Council is coordinating closely with other White House councils and already integrates gender equity into economic plans.
- Jennifer Klein says the Council will address immediate needs through existing ARP measures while pursuing long-term structural solutions.
  #### Third Gender Federal IDs
  Reporters ask whether the President will sign an executive order adding a third gender option on federal IDs at the administration's request. Klein says the White House uses an inclusive definition of gender and intends to address discrimination affecting LGBTQ+ people but notes she has not yet determined whether an executive order is the appropriate or required mechanism. The administration says it will look into what steps would be necessary.
- An unidentified reporter asks whether the President will sign an executive order to create a third gender option on federal IDs.
- Jennifer Klein says the administration is inclusive in its definition of gender and will examine whether an executive order is required to implement such a change.
  #### American Rescue Plan Details
  Ms. Psaki reviews ARP highlights, including direct payments, expanded child tax credit, unemployment insurance extensions, rental and homeowner assistance, investments in childcare, and reductions in health insurance premiums for many families. She frames the bill as transformative for working families, with projected impacts like lifting millions out of poverty and enabling school reopenings and job creation. Psaki reiterates the administration's emphasis on passing and implementing the plan quickly.
- Ms. Psaki says the American Rescue Plan will deliver $1,400 checks to millions and significantly expand the child tax credit to cut child poverty in half this year.
- Ms. Psaki says ARP funding will support school reopenings, keep essential workers employed, provide rental and homeowner assistance, and invest in childcare.
- Ms. Psaki says the President will deliver a primetime address to mark the COVID-19 shutdown anniversary and to outline the path forward.
  #### Stimulus Payment Logistics
  Reporters ask when Americans will receive stimulus checks and whether the IRS/Treasury can process payments quickly, especially for non-filers. Psaki defers to Treasury for implementation details but says the administration expects many Americans to receive relief by the end of the month and that Treasury and Secretary Yellen focus on timely distribution. She acknowledges prior gaps for non-filers and says Treasury will work on mechanisms to reach eligible people who did not previously file tax returns.
- A reporter asks whether the stimulus checks will begin arriving quickly and how the IRS will process them during tax season.
- Ms. Psaki says the Treasury Department is working through mechanics and expects a large number of Americans to receive relief by the end of the month.
- Ms. Psaki says Secretary Yellen is focused on ensuring a clear process so eligible low-income people who did not file tax returns receive assistance.
  #### Filibuster and Legislative Strategy
  Reporters question Senator Manchin's comments about tweaking the filibuster and whether the President supports reforms to accelerate his agenda. Psaki reiterates the President's preference to keep the filibuster intact and to pursue bipartisan collaboration, while remaining open to ideas from senators like Manchin. The White House emphasizes recent legislative achievements accomplished without removing the filibuster and signals willingness to engage on future proposals.
- Aamer asks whether President Biden agrees with Senator Manchin that the filibuster needs tweaks.
- Ms. Psaki says the President prefers to keep the filibuster and continues to pursue bipartisan solutions while remaining open to senators' ideas.
  #### Unaccompanied Children Capacity Needs
  Reporters press on capacity for unaccompanied migrant children and whether the delegation will recommend increasing shelters. Psaki and other officials acknowledge a clear need for more safe capacity given the number of children crossing and COVID-related constraints, and they say the administration prioritizes moving children out of Border Patrol facilities into appropriate shelters with medical, legal, and educational resources. The CDC guidance update may allow increased capacity in some sites while the administration continues to address logistical challenges.
- Steve asks whether the delegation will recommend additional capacity for unaccompanied children.
- Ms. Psaki says the White House sees a need for more capacity and prioritizes getting children into safe shelters with access to health, education, and legal resources.
- Ms. Psaki says COVID protocols and CDC guidance affect facility capacity and the administration is working to balance safety and capacity constraints.
  #### Vaccines: Disinformation and Supply
  Reporters raise concerns about foreign disinformation targeting Pfizer and Moderna vaccines and about vaccine supply chain issues including Emergent and potential export requests. Psaki says the administration is aware of and monitoring disinformation efforts and will use every tool to combat false narratives while leaning on health experts to promote vaccine safety. She confirms the President will meet Merck and J&J executives to discuss supply and distribution, says the administration is auditing the Strategic National Stockpile, and defers technical questions about production and exports to health and national security teams.
- Steve warns that U.S. officials identified Russian-linked online publications targeting Pfizer and Moderna vaccines.
- Ms. Psaki says the administration is monitoring disinformation and will fight it using public-health experts and other tools.
- Ms. Psaki confirms the President will host Merck and Johnson & Johnson executives to discuss vaccine supply and distribution.
- Ms. Psaki says the administration is undertaking a review of the National Stockpile and has no current concern about vaccine production timelines.
  #### International Security Issues
  Reporters ask about elevated tensions involving China and Taiwan and about Houthi attacks on Saudi Arabia and possible Iranian involvement. Psaki reiterates U.S. policy continuity on Taiwan, affirms longstanding commitments and assistance for Taiwan self-defense, and calls Houthi attacks unacceptable and dangerous. The White House says it is reviewing options to support Saudi defensive capabilities and that negotiations with Iran continue through diplomatic channels without a current public timeline.
- An unidentified reporter asks about China's demand to reverse U.S. support for Taiwan and growing rhetoric in the region.
- Ms. Psaki says the United States maintains longstanding commitments to Taiwan and will continue to assist in its self-defense capability.
- An unidentified reporter asks about Houthi attacks on Saudi Arabia and reports those attacks may originate from Iran or Iraq.
- Ms. Psaki says the administration views escalating Houthi attacks as unacceptable and will work with Saudi Arabia to improve defenses while continuing interagency deliberations.
  #### Voting Rights Sequencing
  Reporters ask why voting rights legislation is not receiving higher priority relative to executive actions and the ARP. Psaki affirms voting access as a presidential priority and notes the administration supports the House bill, but she explains that the immediate focus is on passing ARP because the pandemic and economic recession are the top public concerns. The administration signals continued support for legislative and state-level approaches to expand voting access while balancing immediate recovery efforts.
- Isaac asks why voting rights legislation is not higher in the administration's legislative sequencing.
- Ms. Psaki says the President supports voting rights and House passage, but frames the ARP and pandemic response as the current, top legislative priority.
