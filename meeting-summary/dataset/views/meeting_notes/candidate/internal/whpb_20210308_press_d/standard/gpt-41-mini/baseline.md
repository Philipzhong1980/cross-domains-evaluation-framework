### Overview
On International Women's Day, President Biden signed two executive orders establishing the White House Gender Policy Council and directing a review of education policies to ensure environments free from sexual violence. Julissa Reynoso and Jennifer Klein, co-chairs of the Council, outlined its comprehensive agenda addressing systemic discrimination, economic security, healthcare, and global gender equality. The Council emphasizes both immediate relief through the American Rescue Plan and long-term structural reforms, particularly for women disproportionately affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. The administration also addressed state-level transgender athlete bans, affirming federal commitment to LGBTQ+ rights. A delegation's recent visit to border facilities highlighted ongoing efforts to manage unaccompanied migrant children humanely. Legislative discussions focused on the American Rescue Plan's progress, bipartisan strategies amid filibuster debates, and priorities including voting rights and infrastructure. The administration reaffirmed its COVID-19 response plans, vaccine distribution efforts, and commitment to combating disinformation. Internationally, the U.S. maintains its stance on Taiwan and regional security, condemning attacks on Saudi Arabia and engaging in diplomacy with Iran. Throughout, spokesperson Jen Psaki and Council co-chairs provided detailed insights into policy directions, challenges, and ongoing initiatives.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Establishment of Gender Policy Council
President Biden signed an executive order establishing the White House Gender Policy Council to advance gender equity and equal rights for women and girls. The Council will have a role in domestic and foreign policy development, addressing systemic discrimination and promoting economic security, healthcare access, and education equity. This initiative reflects the administration's commitment to human rights, justice, and economic growth.
- Julissa Reynoso, Chief of Staff to the First Lady, emphasized the Council's role in tackling systemic bias and promoting gender equality.
- Jennifer Klein, Executive Director of the Council, highlighted the Council's responsibility to submit a government-wide strategy and annual progress report on gender equity.
- The Council will focus on issues including sexual harassment, economic participation, caregiving support, healthcare, and global gender equality.
#### Executive Order on Education Free from Sexual Violence
A second executive order directs the Department of Education to review all existing regulations and policies to ensure consistency with the administration's commitment to education free from sexual violence. The Secretary of Education will consider revising or rescinding policies inconsistent with this goal, ensuring fair and equitable procedures for all involved.
- Jennifer Klein explained the Department of Education's review of Title IX regulations to guarantee an educational environment free from sex discrimination and sexual harassment.
- The administration recognizes discrimination based on sexual orientation, gender identity, race, disability, and national origin, with particular attention to LGBTQ+ students.
- The policy aims to balance protections for victims and due process rights for the accused.
#### Impact of COVID-19 on Women and Workforce Recovery
The Council acknowledges that women, especially women of color, have been disproportionately affected by the COVID-19 pandemic, facing economic and caregiving challenges. The American Rescue Plan (ARP) includes provisions to provide immediate relief and support women's return to work, while the Council aims for long-term structural changes to address systemic barriers in the labor force.
- Julissa Reynoso noted the disproportionate impact of the pandemic on women in sectors like hospitality and retail.
- Jennifer Klein detailed ARP provisions such as direct payments, child tax credits, unemployment insurance, and childcare support to aid women.
- The Council collaborates with other White House policy councils to integrate gender equity into economic recovery efforts.
#### Federal Response to State Transgender Athlete Bans
The Council is aware of state-level legislation banning transgender athletes from girls' and women's sports and plans to address these issues using federal laws, policy clarity, and interagency collaboration. The Council works closely with the Domestic Policy Council and National Security Council on equity issues affecting LGBTQ+ individuals.
- Jennifer Klein stated the Council will use federal tools and policy advocacy to respond to discriminatory state laws.
- The administration emphasizes inclusivity in its definition of gender and commitment to equal rights for LGBTQ+ people.
- The Council coordinates with other White House offices and external partners to address these challenges.
#### Visit to U.S.-Mexico Border Facilities
Julissa Reynoso and other senior officials conducted a visit to border facilities, including Carrizo Springs, to assess conditions for unaccompanied migrant children. They engaged with children and staff to understand challenges and plan improvements. A briefing to President Biden is forthcoming, with a commitment to transparency balanced against privacy and safety concerns.
- Julissa Reynoso described the visit as an effort to evaluate the inherited broken system and manage it humanely.
- The delegation spoke with children and officials to gather firsthand information.
- The administration plans to increase capacity and improve shelter conditions while protecting children's privacy.
#### American Rescue Plan Legislative Progress and Impact
The Senate passed a significant relief bill, the American Rescue Plan, which aims to provide direct payments, reduce child poverty, extend unemployment benefits, and invest in childcare and education. The administration emphasizes the bill's historic and progressive nature and its potential to create jobs and support working families.
- MS. Psaki highlighted the bill's provisions, including $1,400 checks to millions of Americans and historic child tax credit expansion.
- The plan includes investments in schools, essential workers, and public health measures to combat COVID-19.
- The administration is actively working to ensure the bill's passage and implementation.
#### Filibuster and Bipartisan Legislative Strategy
President Biden prefers to maintain the filibuster but remains open to discussions on its use and potential reforms. The administration continues to engage with both Democrats and Republicans to advance legislative priorities, including infrastructure and voting rights, emphasizing bipartisan cooperation where possible.
- MS. Psaki stated the President's preference is not to eliminate the filibuster but to work within existing rules.
- The President has held bipartisan meetings on infrastructure and other issues.
- The administration acknowledges challenges but remains optimistic about bipartisan progress.
#### Vaccination and COVID-19 Response Efforts
The administration is focused on ensuring vaccine supply and distribution, with plans to vaccinate all American adults by mid-May. Meetings with pharmaceutical executives aim to expedite production. The CDC issues guidelines on vaccinated individuals, and the administration combats disinformation about vaccine safety.
- MS. Psaki confirmed the President will meet with Merck and J&J executives to discuss vaccine supply.
- The administration is aware of and addressing Russian disinformation campaigns targeting vaccines.
- CDC guidelines on vaccinated individuals' travel and activities are developed independently by the CDC.
#### Voting Rights and Legislative Priorities
While voting rights remain a priority for President Biden, the immediate focus is on pandemic relief and economic recovery. The administration supports legislation to expand voting access and is exploring multiple avenues to address state-level voting restrictions, maintaining commitment to civil rights.
- MS. Psaki affirmed the President's support for voting rights legislation passed by the House.
- The administration is working with Congress and states to improve voting access.
- Voting rights efforts continue alongside other urgent legislative priorities.
#### International Relations and Security Concerns
The administration maintains a clear stance on Taiwan, supporting its self-defense and regional security commitments. It condemns Houthi attacks on Saudi Arabia and continues diplomatic efforts with Iran. The administration monitors global developments and coordinates with allies to address security challenges.
- MS. Psaki reiterated U.S. commitments to Taiwan under longstanding agreements.
- The administration condemns Houthi attacks and supports Saudi Arabia's defense capabilities.
- Diplomatic negotiations with Iran remain ongoing without a set timeline.
