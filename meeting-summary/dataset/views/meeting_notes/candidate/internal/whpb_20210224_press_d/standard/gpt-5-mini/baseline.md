### Overview
  Senior White House officials briefed the press on a forthcoming executive order that launches a whole-of-government effort to strengthen U.S. supply chains, directing immediate 100-day reviews of semiconductors, large-capacity batteries, pharmaceuticals/APIs, and critical minerals, plus six sectoral reviews to be completed within a year, according to National Security Council senior director Peter Harrell and National Economic Council deputy director Sameera Fazili. Officials stress the EO will prioritize action—using procurement, incentives, stockpiling, surge-capacity planning, and potentially all available authorities—to close identified gaps, and they commit to broad stakeholder engagement and congressional consultation to secure necessary tools and funding. Fazili frames the initiative as an economic opportunity to create well‑paying jobs, strengthen R&D and manufacturing ecosystems, and expand supplier diversity; Harrell emphasizes tailored recommendations by supply chain and cooperation with allies. The briefing also covered the administration’s plan to distribute more than 25 million American-made masks via community health centers and food pantries, updates on winter-storm recovery and vaccine shipments, the status of the President’s COVID testing, and a wide-ranging Q&A on topics including the Neera Tanden nomination, Johnson & Johnson vaccine manufacturing, immigration and unaccompanied-minor facilities, Saudi relations and the forthcoming Khashoggi report, and legislative priorities tied to the American Rescue Plan and minimum-wage proposals, with officials generally deferring detailed operational or diplomatic specifics to the relevant agencies while reiterating the administration’s immediate priorities and commitments.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Executive order announced
  The White House announces that the President will sign an executive order to take a whole-of-government approach to securing critical U.S. supply chains. The EO aims to make supply chains more resilient to crises, reduce vulnerability to manipulation by competitor nations, and create domestic jobs. Officials present key objectives and signaled immediate next steps for review and action.
- MS. PSAKI states the President will sign an executive order to secure critical U.S. supply chains and bolster domestic manufacturing.
- MR. HARRELL describes the EO as a formal launch of a comprehensive initiative to prevent future supply chain crises rather than only reacting to them.
- MR. HARRELL cites examples like PPE shortages early in the pandemic and current semiconductor constraints to explain why the action matters.
#### 100-day product reviews
  The EO directs immediate 100-day reviews of four critical product supply chains: semiconductors, large-capacity batteries, pharmaceuticals and active pharmaceutical ingredients, and critical minerals/strategic materials. These fast reviews are intended to rapidly identify near-term risks and recommend actions to close gaps. The White House frames these as urgent, actionable reviews rather than academic reports.
- MR. HARRELL outlines the four 100-day product reviews, including chips, batteries, pharmaceuticals/APIs, and critical minerals.
- MR. HARRELL emphasizes the reviews will result in actions to close gaps rather than just producing reports.
- MS. FAZILI explains the reviews will consider broad risks including climate, geopolitics, workforce shortages, and manufacturing capacity.
#### Sectoral one-year reviews
  The EO also directs six sector-specific reviews to be completed within one year, covering defense, public health/biological preparedness, information and communications technology, transportation, energy, and food production. These reviews are modeled on Defense Department processes for evaluating the industrial base and intend to examine systemic vulnerabilities. The administration says these will produce tailored recommendations and leverage government authorities to mitigate risks.
- MR. HARRELL says six sectoral reviews will be finished within a year and modeled after Defense Department industrial base assessments.
- MR. HARRELL indicates the reviews will span defense, public health/biological preparedness, ICT, transportation, energy, and food production.
- MS. FAZILI notes the sectoral reviews will recommend actions including publicly available data, procurement levers, and potential stockpiling.
#### Implementation tools and authorities
  Officials emphasize the EO will use a mix of policy tools to close identified gaps, including procurement authority, stockpiling, incentives, surge capacity planning, and coordination with allies. They state that all appropriate tools—potentially including the Defense Production Act—are on the table, though specific applications will be determined by review findings. The White House commits to translating reviews into concrete actions rather than only producing analyses.
- MR. HARRELL says the administration expects to use a mix of incentives, stockpiling, and surge-capacity planning to encourage domestic production.
- MR. HARRELL confirms the administration will tailor recommendations by supply chain and that ‘all tools are on the table’ including potential DPA use.
- MS. FAZILI explains procurement authority is one lever the government can use to support domestic production or stockpiling.
#### Jobs and economic opportunity
  The administration frames supply-chain resilience as an economic opportunity to create well-paying jobs, expand R&D, and support supplier networks and small businesses. Officials link investments in manufacturing, workforce training, and paired research-manufacturing ecosystems to long-term broad-based growth. They emphasize inclusion, saying efforts will aim to bring jobs to communities across the country, including communities of color.
- MS. FAZILI says resilient supply chains present opportunities to create well-paying jobs and build back better.
- MS. FAZILI highlights that investments should pair research and manufacturing to sustain innovation and economic growth.
- MS. FAZILI notes opportunities to support small-business development and diversify supplier networks to reduce concentration risk.
#### Stakeholder consultation plan
  The administration commits to broad stakeholder engagement—including business, labor, local communities, and academia—to design public-private partnerships and policy responses. Officials stress government action alone will not solve complex supply chain problems and that consultations will inform recommendations and potential requests for new congressional authorities. They plan sustained outreach as part of the review process.
- MS. FAZILI announces the administration will consult broadly with business, labor, local communities, and academia on supply chain solutions.
- MS. FAZILI frames public-private partnerships as essential and says government action alone will not resolve supply chain challenges.
- MS. FAZILI says the administration will look for opportunities to work with Congress to obtain additional tools if needed.
#### Measuring job impact and success
  Reporters ask about metrics for job creation and success measurement stemming from the EO, and officials urge a broad lens that captures direct manufacturing jobs as well as spillover R&D and supplier network effects. The administration intends to track broader economic contributions rather than only counting factory headcount. Officials did not provide numeric job targets during the briefing.
- Unidentified reporter asks how many factory jobs the initiative can create and what metrics will be used.
- MS. FAZILI responds the administration will use a broad view of job impacts that includes R&D, supplier networks, and spillover effects.
- MS. FAZILI indicates the need to start tracking manufacturing contributions beyond narrow headcount measures.
#### Budget and semiconductor funding
  Reporters ask whether the upcoming budget will propose federal funding to increase semiconductor production; officials say the budget is still being formulated and specifics are not available. Harrell says the administration will propose a tailored, comprehensive set of recommendations that vary by supply chain and may include incentives. The administration pledges to work with Congress on funding requests informed by review findings.
- Unidentified reporter asks whether the administration will propose funding for semiconductors in its budget.
- MS. FAZILI says the administration is still formulating the budget and cannot provide specifics yet.
- MR. HARRELL says the administration will develop a comprehensive suite of recommendations tailored to each supply chain and will use incentives where appropriate.
#### DPA and international cooperation
  On whether the Defense Production Act (DPA) would be used for semiconductors and about Taiwan’s cooperation on chips, officials decline to specify operational use of the DPA and avoid detailing diplomatic conversations. They state that all tools are on the table and that Taiwan is an important partner with whom the U.S. has had constructive conversations. No commitments on DPA actions or specific diplomatic responses are announced.
- Unidentified reporter asks about potential DPA use for semiconductors.
- MR. HARRELL says the administration is not detailing DPA use for any specific supply chain at this point but confirms all tools are on the table.
- MR. HARRELL says Taiwan is an important partner and the U.S. has had constructive conversations but declines to discuss diplomatic nuances.
#### Mask distribution program
  The White House announces a program to deliver more than 25 million American-made masks to food banks and community health centers starting next month, funded through existing HHS resources. The masks will be distributed via more than 1,300 community health centers and about 60,000 food pantries and will adhere to CDC guidance. The administration frames this as targeted action to improve access for vulnerable populations.
- MS. PSAKI announces the administration will deliver more than 25 million masks to food banks and community health centers starting next month.
- MS. PSAKI says the masks will be available at over 1,300 community health centers and roughly 60,000 food pantries and meet CDC guidance.
- MS. PSAKI notes the program uses existing HHS funding and is aimed at vulnerable populations lacking access to quality masks.
#### CEOs support for Rescue Plan
  Psaki highlights a letter from over 160 CEOs urging Congress to act on the President’s Rescue Plan, which the administration says reflects growing consensus and bipartisan public support. The administration cites polling and business community voices to underscore urgency for additional federal relief. Officials present the business letter as part of broader outreach and support for the package.
- MS. PSAKI notes a letter from more than 160 CEOs calling on Congress to pass the President's Rescue Plan.
- MS. PSAKI says the letter signals growing consensus and bipartisan backing reflected in polling on the package.
- MS. PSAKI frames the letter as evidence that additional federal relief is needed for a durable recovery.
#### Winter storm recovery update
  Psaki provides an update on a recent winter storm noting improving temperatures, remaining water system outages and boil-water advisories, and ongoing federal assistance in affected regions. She reports that delayed vaccine shipments are expected to be filled and that major transportation systems have largely resumed normal operations. The administration continues to evaluate additional federal support as needed.
- MS. PSAKI reports temperatures are returning to normal in storm-impacted states but water system outages and boil-water advisories remain a concern.
- MS. PSAKI says delayed COVID-19 vaccine shipments should be filled in coming days and vaccination sites are reopening.
- MS. PSAKI confirms airports, rail carriers, highways, transit agencies, and ports are operating under normal circumstances.
#### President COVID test transparency
  Psaki informs the press that the President tested negative for COVID-19 on Monday and says the administration will continue to provide such updates as part of regular safety protocols. The White House reiterates ongoing mitigation measures across the complex, including mask wearing and social distancing. This is presented as part of routine transparency rather than a policy announcement.
- MS. PSAKI shares that the President tested negative for COVID-19 on Monday and commits to continued transparency on testing cadence.
- MS. PSAKI reiterates that the White House complex adheres to strict masking, social distancing, and other mitigation protocols.
#### Mask type and manufacturer questions
  Reporters ask why the administration is distributing cloth masks rather than N95s and request a list of manufacturers; Psaki responds that the masks meet CDC guidance and that she will try to provide more details on manufacturers. No manufacturer list or change in mask type policy is announced. The administration emphasizes adherence to federal standards.
- Unidentified reporter asks why cloth masks are being distributed rather than N95s and requests a list of manufacturers.
- MS. PSAKI says the distributed masks adhere to CDC guidance and offers to follow up on manufacturer details.
#### Russia tensions and investigative process
  Psaki addresses a question about rising tensions with Russia and reiterates the administration's concern about actions attributed to the Kremlin, referencing the President’s direction to national security staff to review incidents including election-related hacking and allegations about bounties on U.S. troops. She notes the administration expects an assessment in the coming weeks and that the President raised concerns directly with President Putin. No new policy steps are announced pending the review.
- Unidentified reporter asks if tensions with Russia are increasing given recent comments by President Putin.
- MS. PSAKI says the administration has asked the national security team to review allegations including hacking and bounties and expects a process that will take weeks, not months.
- MS. PSAKI notes the President recently spoke with President Putin and expressed concerns directly.
#### Call with Saudi King and Khashoggi report
  Reporters ask about a potential call between the President and King Salman and whether it will occur before an unclassified Khashoggi report; Psaki says the administration expects the DNI report soon and is scheduling a call but will provide a readout after it happens. She reiterates the administration's intent to recalibrate engagement with Saudi Arabia and to speak out on human rights concerns while maintaining channels on shared security matters. No detailed timeline or content is provided.
- Unidentified reporter asks whether the President will call King Salman ahead of the unclassified Khashoggi report.
- MS. PSAKI says the DNI will release an unclassified report soon and that the President plans to speak with the King, with a readout to follow.
- MS. PSAKI states the administration intends to recalibrate engagement with Saudi Arabia and will not shy from raising human rights concerns.
#### Neera Tanden nomination status
  Several reporters raise the delay in the Senate confirmation vote for Neera Tanden to lead OMB; Psaki says the White House is actively fighting for her nomination, that Tanden and the team have engaged directly with many senators, and that outreach continues. She declines to frame the vote delay as a setback and reiterates the administration’s support for the nominee. No contingent replacement plans are announced.
- Unidentified reporter asks whether the White House will withdraw Neera Tanden's nomination given delays on Capitol Hill.
- MS. PSAKI says the administration is 'fighting for' Tanden, notes active outreach to senators, and will continue engagement to build support.
- MS. PSAKI confirms the administration remains focused on fighting for the President's nominated team rather than discussing alternative nominees.
#### Johnson & Johnson vaccine supply
  A question about Johnson & Johnson’s projected initial shipments elicits Psaki's response that the administration was surprised to learn manufacturing output is lower than previously reported and that J&J now expects 3–4 million doses pending authorization rather than the earlier ~10 million. The White House says it will continue to work with J&J to expedite manufacturing if the vaccine receives authorization. No further operational details are provided.
- Kaitlan asks whether the President is disappointed that Johnson & Johnson may only have 3–4 million doses available rather than the ~10 million previously reported.
- MS. PSAKI says the administration was surprised by the reported manufacturing shortfall and will work with J&J to expedite doses if the vaccine is authorized.
#### Unaccompanied minors and facilities
  Reporters press about HHS facilities for unaccompanied minors, including images of containers and questions whether the administration’s approach differs from prior policies; Psaki stresses the administration is not separating children from parents, describes the HHS facilities as an intermediate option with medical and educational services, and explains some CBP-to-HHS transfer delays occurred because of weather and power outages. She refers press access questions to DHS and underscores the goal of moving children quickly to vetted sponsors.
- Unidentified reporter asks about images of containers at an HHS facility in Carrizo Springs and whether the White House will describe those conditions.
- MS. PSAKI insists the administration is not separating children from parents and says HHS facilities have been revamped with medical and educational services.
- MS. PSAKI explains some transfer delays from CBP to HHS facilities occurred due to weather and facility capacity, and refers press-access questions to DHS.
#### Texas visit and infrastructure response
  Reporters ask about the President's planned visit to Texas and whether federal oversight of the electric grid will be part of discussions; Psaki says the President will focus on surveying damage, supporting recovery, and visiting a vaccine distribution site, and that policy debates about grid oversight will take place later. She emphasizes immediate aid and assistance rather than policy judgments during the visit.
- Unidentified reporter asks whether modernizing the electric grid requires federal oversight of Texas' grid.
- MS. PSAKI says the President’s visit is to support recovery and see impacts on the ground, while policy debates about infrastructure and grid oversight will come later.
- MS. PSAKI notes the trip timing was coordinated with experts and FEMA on where the visit would be most appropriate.
#### Variants, vaccines, and public health pacing
  When asked about a California COVID variant and the effect of variants on timelines for returning to normalcy, Psaki defers to health experts and notes the administration is cautious in predicting timelines because variants introduce uncertainty. She confirms the President receives regular COVID briefings and that public-health authorities will assess the variant's impact. No new modeling or policy changes are announced in this briefing.
- Unidentified reporter asks how the California variant factors into the administration’s modeling for a return to normalcy.
- MS. PSAKI defers to public-health experts and stresses caution in predicting timelines due to unpredictable virus developments.
- MS. PSAKI confirms the President receives regular briefings on COVID-19 and related developments.
#### Legislative priorities and Rescue Plan timing
  Reporters ask about a potential pathway for undocumented essential workers in future reconciliation and contingency planning if unemployment insurance (UI) benefits lapse on March 14; Psaki reiterates the administration’s preference for congressional action on the Rescue Plan and emphasizes urgency to prevent benefit lapses. She says detailed conversations on immigration components are ongoing and that a backup plan would likely involve Congress. The administration is focused on getting the Rescue Plan passed quickly.
- Unidentified reporter asks whether a pathway for undocumented essential workers could be included in a second reconciliation package.
- MS. PSAKI says the President will consult with Speaker Pelosi and others and that discussions about components of immigration policy are ongoing.
- Unidentified reporter asks about backup plans for UI benefits expiring March 14, and MS. PSAKI responds that Congress is the primary path to prevent lapses and the administration is urging swift action.
#### Foreign policy: Iran, Iraq, and Yemen
  Questions on Iran address linkage between nuclear diplomacy, ballistic missile programs, and regional actions; Psaki reiterates that the U.S. remains open to diplomacy if Iran returns to compliance and that the administration will hold Iran accountable for proxy actions while avoiding escalation. On Yemen, Psaki says humanitarian concerns remain and refers detailed assessments to State. The administration signals a calibrated approach combining diplomacy and accountability.
- Unidentified reporter asks if ballistic missile development and regional interference will be linked to nuclear diplomacy with Iran.
- MS. PSAKI says the U.S. will seek to build on compliance if Iran returns to its obligations and that concerns about missiles and regional behavior remain part of broader objectives.
- MS. PSAKI says the U.S. holds Iran accountable for proxy attacks in Iraq and will respond in a calculated manner to avoid destabilizing escalation, and refers humanitarian assessments in Yemen to the State Department.
#### Voting rights and targeted relief
  Reporters ask whether the administration will work with Congress to pass legislation expanding voting access and whether targeted relief for communities disproportionately harmed by COVID-19 (including Black communities) is under consideration. Psaki confirms expanding access to voting is a priority and that Ambassador Rice is the lead on the issue, and she asserts the American Rescue Plan includes targeted relief measures for hardest-hit communities while broader structural programs will be considered later. No new legislative commitments are announced beyond support for the Rescue Plan.
- Unidentified reporter asks if the administration will work with Congress to pass legislation to expand access to voting.
- MS. PSAKI says expanding voting access is a priority for the President and Vice President and refers follow-up to Ambassador Rice.
- Unidentified reporter asks about targeted relief for Black communities, and MS. PSAKI says the Rescue Plan is designed to provide relief targeted to hardest-hit communities and the administration will consider broader measures subsequently.
#### Minimum wage and reconciliation
  On whether the President supports anything below a $15 minimum wage in the COVID relief package, Psaki reiterates the President’s support for a $15 federal minimum wage and explains the inclusion depends on Senate parliamentary processes. She stresses the administration’s preference for the $15 figure while acknowledging the legislative process and parliamentarian rulings will shape the outcome. The White House does not announce concessions or alternative proposals in this briefing.
- Kristen asks if the President would support a minimum wage lower than $15, such as $11.
- MS. PSAKI says the President put a $15 minimum wage in his package and supports that increase, while noting the Senate parliamentary process will determine the final outcome.
- MS. PSAKI explains the Byrd Rule and parliamentary review will influence whether the minimum wage provision is included in the reconciliation package.
#### Other policy and operational questions
  Reporters ask a range of operational and diplomatic questions—military vaccine requirements, South Korea-Iran funds reports, and state decisions on vaccine prioritization—and Psaki generally defers to relevant agencies or says the White House will follow up. She suggests the Pentagon and State Department as appropriate contacts for operational or diplomatic specifics while reaffirming federal guidelines for vaccine prioritization. No new executive actions are announced in response to these operational questions.
- Steve asks whether the President would consider requiring vaccines for military members, and MS. PSAKI suggests contacting the Pentagon and Secretary Austin for decisions of that nature.
- Unidentified reporter asks about reports South Korea may seek a U.S. waiver to unfreeze Iranian funds, and MS. PSAKI says she has not seen those reports and will refer to the State Department.
- Unidentified reporter asks about a governor deviating from federal vaccine prioritization and MS. PSAKI says the administration stands by its national guidelines while governors make local decisions.
