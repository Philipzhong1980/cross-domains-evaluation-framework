### Overview
The White House press briefing, led by Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre and Admiral John Kirby, covers a range of critical national and international issues. Key topics include President Biden's upcoming engagements focused on national security funding and economic initiatives in North Carolina. Admiral Kirby provides detailed updates on the Middle East, highlighting ongoing hostage negotiations with Hamas, Israel's shift to lower-intensity military operations in Gaza, and U.S. military actions against Houthi missile capabilities in Yemen aimed at protecting Red Sea shipping lanes. The administration expresses concern over regional stability threats posed by a recent Ethiopia-Somaliland agreement and underscores the urgency of congressional approval for supplemental funding to support Ukraine and border security. Domestically, Jean-Pierre addresses challenges at the Texas border, criticizing state actions that impede federal law enforcement and emphasizing bipartisan efforts to reform immigration policy. The briefing also reaffirms the administration's commitment to election integrity, condemns conspiracy theories related to January 6th, and acknowledges Special Envoy John Kerry's departure after significant climate achievements. Security matters, including a recent swatting incident at the White House and communication between the President and Secretary of Defense, are briefly discussed. Throughout, the administration stresses its dedication to national security, humanitarian concerns, and democratic principles.
  
### Detailed Summary

Admiral John Kirby provides an update on the Middle East, focusing on ongoing efforts to secure the release of hostages held by Hamas and the shift in Israeli military operations in Gaza. The administration continues diplomatic and military efforts to address the humanitarian crisis and security threats in the region.

Despite some retaliatory strikes by the Houthis, the U.S. assesses its actions as effective in degrading their offensive capabilities while seeking to avoid escalation.

Israeli Defense Forces have begun withdrawing troops from northern Gaza, signaling a transition to lower-intensity operations, which may facilitate humanitarian access and civilian movement.

#### Red Sea Attacks and U.S. Response

- Kirby explains most missile attacks on commercial ships have been ineffective or intercepted.
- He notes merchant ships have small crews and large freeboard, reducing injury risk.
- The U.S. took additional strikes to neutralize ballistic missiles before launch.
- Kirby stresses the importance of continued action to prevent escalation and protect shipping lanes.

- Kirby acknowledges potential national security risks posed by increased al-Shabaab activity.
