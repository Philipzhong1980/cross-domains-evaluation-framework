### Overview
  National Economic Adviser Lael Brainard opens by describing how the pandemic produced severe supply-chain stress and shortages and outlines past administration steps (a day-one executive order, a Supply Chain Disruptions Taskforce, Ocean Shipping Reform Act, Trucking Action Plan, and major investment laws) that underpin current resilience work. The administration announces that the President will launch a White House Council on Supplier Resilience and unveil 30 new actions, including a presidential determination to broaden HHS authority under the Defense Production Act, $35 million in HHS investments for sterile injectable starting materials, and a new HHS supply-chain resilience and shortage coordinator (to be named later), while the Department of Defense will publish a pharmaceutical supply-chain report. The Department of Homeland Security will stand up a Supply Chain Resilience Center with tabletop exercises focused on port and critical-infrastructure risks, and the Department of Commerce will create a supply-chain center that uses industry expertise and data analytics to build risk-assessment tools; DHS and Commerce will collaborate to strengthen the semiconductor supply chain and implement the CHIPS Act. In the background Q&A, a Senior Administration Official emphasizes a strategy of diversifying supply chains through domestic tools and international partnerships, explains that DHS and Commerce bring complementary authorities and data (including CBP cross-border information), describes Flow as an interoperable industry dataset that incentivizes participation, and states that the HHS coordinator will be created with multiyear funding and that the presidential DPA determination will be released this week.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Pandemic supply chain damage
  Director Lael Brainard frames the pandemic as causing widespread supply-chain stress that produced shortages across semiconductors, vehicles, appliances, and household goods. She links those shortages to broader economic pressure and situates the administration’s supply-chain work as a response to that disruption. The remarks set the context for a package of policy actions intended to reduce vulnerabilities and lower consumer costs.
- Director Brainard says the pandemic produced unprecedented stress in supply chains and shortages across a wide range of goods.
- Director Brainard says those supply-chain pressures contributed to higher input costs and elevated consumer prices.
- Director Brainard frames the administration’s actions as a response to those disruptions to improve resilience and lower costs for consumers.
#### Administration actions taken
  Brainard reviews the administration’s prior actions to strengthen supply chains, citing a day-one executive order, a Supply Chain Disruptions Taskforce, the Ocean Shipping Reform Act, and a Trucking Action Plan. She also highlights major legislative achievements—Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, CHIPS and Science Act, and Inflation Reduction Act—that enable investments in transport, inputs, and semiconductor capacity. These items are presented as the foundation for current and future resilience efforts.
- Director Brainard says the President signed a day-one executive order and established a Supply Chain Disruptions Taskforce.
- Director Brainard says the administration secured the Ocean Shipping Reform Act and the Trucking Action Plan to address shipping and trucking challenges.
- Director Brainard says the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, CHIPS and Science Act, and Inflation Reduction Act support investments in transportation systems and key inputs.
#### Metrics: stress and inflation
  Brainard reports measurable improvement, saying supply-chain stress has fallen from record highs to record lows and that inflation has declined about 65 percent from its peak. She references the relationship between the Global Supply Chain Pressure Index and CPI inflation as evidence of progress. The point emphasizes that work has produced measurable economic benefits but that further action remains necessary.
- Director Brainard says supply-chain stress has fallen from record highs to record lows under the administration’s efforts.
- Director Brainard says inflation has declined by roughly 65 percent from its peak and notes the relationship between the Global Supply Chain Pressure Index and CPI inflation.
- Director Brainard says the administration is pleased with progress but will continue working to bring down prices for consumers.
#### Council and 30 actions
  Brainard announces that the President will launch a White House Council on Supplier Resilience and unveil 30 new actions intended to strengthen American supply chains and reduce costs for families. The council and actions are presented as the next phase of sustained work on resilience and affordability. These announcements are positioned as near-term deliverables tied to the administration’s supply-chain strategy.
- Director Brainard says the President will launch a White House Council on Supplier Resilience tomorrow.
- Director Brainard says the White House will announce 30 new actions to strengthen supply chains and bring down costs for American families.
- Director Brainard positions the council and actions as continuation of the administration’s long-term supply-chain strategy.
#### HHS DPA expansion and investments
  Brainard says the President will make a presidential determination broadening HHS authorities under the Defense Production Act to mitigate drug shortages and invest in essential medicines made in the United States. HHS has identified $35 million for domestic production of key starting materials for sterile injectable medicines and will designate a new supply-chain resilience and shortage coordinator. The administration frames these moves as steps to address persistent pharmaceutical vulnerabilities.
- Director Brainard says the President will announce a presidential determination to broaden HHS authorities under the Defense Production Act to mitigate drug shortages.
- Director Brainard says HHS has identified $35 million for investments in domestic production of key starting materials for sterile injectable medicines.
- Senior Administration Official says HHS will designate a new supply-chain resilience and shortage coordinator and that the coordinator will be announced at a later date.
#### DOD pharmaceutical report
  Brainard says the Department of Defense will soon release a new report on pharmaceutical supply-chain resilience. The report is presented as a complementary analytical input to the administration’s pharmaceutical resilience actions. No further details about the report’s timing or contents are provided in the call.
- Director Brainard says the Department of Defense will soon release a report on pharmaceutical supply-chain resilience.
- Director Brainard presents the DOD report as part of a whole-of-government effort on pharmaceutical supply chains.
#### DHS resilience center and ports
  Brainard announces that the Department of Homeland Security will launch a new Supply Chain Resilience Center and run tabletop exercises to test critical cross-border supply-chain resilience. She identifies near-term priorities that include addressing threats and vulnerabilities inside U.S. ports. The DHS center is framed as a focal point for analyzing infrastructure-related risks to the flow of goods.
- Director Brainard says the Department of Homeland Security will launch a Supply Chain Resilience Center and new tabletop exercises to test cross-border supply-chain resilience.
- Director Brainard says near-term priorities include addressing risks and vulnerabilities inside U.S. ports.
- Senior Administration Official says the DHS center will focus on risks from critical infrastructure as it relates to the flow of goods.
#### Commerce center and semiconductors
  Brainard says the Department of Commerce will launch a first-of-its-kind supply-chain center that integrates industry expertise and data analytics to develop risk-assessment tools. She also says DHS and Commerce will collaborate to strengthen the semiconductor supply chain and further CHIPS Act implementation. The emphasis is on combining economic trade expertise with cross-border security data to support a resurgence of chip production in the United States.
- Director Brainard says the Department of Commerce will launch a supply-chain center to integrate industry expertise and data analytics for risk assessment.
- Director Brainard says DHS and Commerce will collaborate to strengthen the semiconductor supply chain and advance CHIPS implementation.
- Senior Administration Official says Commerce brings trade and economic authorities while DHS brings cross-border data and CBP access, making their partnership complementary.
#### Pharma sourcing and diversification
  In response to a question about whether the government will shift pharmaceutical sourcing from China to other countries, a Senior Administration Official says the goal is diversification rather than a simple geographic switch. The administration plans to use domestic tools and international partnerships to reduce hyperconcentration and mitigate geopolitical, economic, and climate risks. The answer underscores a strategy of strategic diversification across partners and allies.
- Paris Huang asks whether DOD efforts mean shifting pharmaceutical sourcing from China to India or other countries.
- Senior Administration Official says the goal is to diversify supply chains using domestic tools and international partnerships rather than shifting concentration to a single new region.
- Senior Administration Official says hyperconcentration in one region creates heightened risk from geopolitical, economic, and climate shocks.
#### DHS-Commerce centers roles
  A reporter asks what the two new centers will do that existing agencies are not already doing, and a Senior Administration Official explains that the offices will leverage complementary authorities. DHS provides cross-border information and CBP data while Commerce provides trade and economic expertise, and the partnership aims to support domestic chip production. The centers are described as coordinated efforts to combine different data sets and authorities for improved risk assessment and industrial support.
- Jeff Mason asks what the two new centers will do that differs from current agency work.
- Senior Administration Official says the DHS-Commerce partnership matters because DHS has cross-border data and industry security partnerships while Commerce focuses on trade and economic authorities.
- Senior Administration Official says the partnership will help identify major U.S. industry players and support a resurgence of chip production domestically.
#### Flow data sharing and cybersecurity
  A reporter asks about interoperability and cybersecurity risks in data sharing for port and supply-chain visibility, citing international incidents. Senior Administration Officials describe 'Flow' as an interoperable dataset that industry participates in because it provides mutual benefits, and they say the DHS center will concentrate on critical-infrastructure risks to goods flow. Officials note new partnerships along these lines will be announced and emphasize incentives for industry participation in shared data solutions.
- Lori Anne asks how data sharing systems will be secured to prevent hacking and disruptions to port operations.
- Senior Administration Official says Flow creates an interoperable dataset and that industry is incentivized to participate because of shared benefits.
- Senior Administration Official says new partnerships and DHS attention to critical-infrastructure risks will strengthen data sharing and flow resilience.
#### HHS coordinator and DPA timing
  Reporters ask whether the HHS coordinator will be an external hire and when the Defense Production Act determination will take effect. A Senior Administration Official says HHS will announce the creation of the coordinator tomorrow, that the coordinator’s identity will be announced later, and that multiyear funding has been secured to make the role durable. The official also says the presidential determination under the DPA will be released this week and that the administration seeks rapid results while recognizing the complexity of drug shortages.
- Morgan asks whether the HHS coordinator will be an external hire and when the DPA action will be used.
- Senior Administration Official says HHS will announce creation of the coordinator tomorrow and will name the coordinator at a later date.
- Senior Administration Official says HHS secured multiyear funding for the coordinator position and that the presidential determination under the DPA will be released this week.
