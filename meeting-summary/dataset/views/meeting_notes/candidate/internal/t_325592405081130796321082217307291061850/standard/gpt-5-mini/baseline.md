### Overview
  The subcommittee convenes to review staff's proposed process and timeline for developing the FY 2023–24 and 2024–25 Council work plan; Principal Management Analyst Laurel James outlines a January–June schedule, ties project evaluation to the City's seven strategic priorities, and proposes analysis criteria, an advisory body participation process, and three priority buckets (A/B/C). Public commenter Bruce England urges improved hybrid meeting access and flags specific agenda items for attention. Committee members request additional analysis criteria for urgency/timeliness, clearer alignment to the strategic priorities, and integration of the Race, Equity, and Inclusion action plan into equity analysis; they also ask staff to better surface existing, non-discretionary workload so Council can set realistic priorities. Staff agrees to attend advisory body meetings with consistent questions, provide clearer visuals and a standardized project status table (including estimated completion quarters and priority buckets), and return a revised work plan development item to Council in January as a consent item with staff-proposed bucket assignments for Council to adjust during the April prioritization session.
  
  ### Detailed Summary

  #### Minutes approval
  The subcommittee reviews and votes to approve the minutes from the December 15, 2020 meeting. Committee member Hicks moves to accept the minutes and Committee member Kamei seconds, and the roll call vote passes unanimously. No public comment or corrections are offered.
- Alison Hicks moves to accept the December 15, 2020 minutes as written.
- Ellen Kamei seconds the motion and Chair Ramirez conducts a roll call vote that passes unanimously.
  #### Work plan presentation overview
  Principal Management Analyst Laurel James presents the proposed process and timeline for developing the Fiscal Years 2023–24 and 2024–25 Council work plan, including check-ins during implementation. The presentation frames projects against the Council's seven adopted strategic priorities and shows study sessions and council items from January through June 2023. Staff requests subcommittee feedback on process elements so the item can be brought to Council in January.
- Laurel James explains the presentation purpose as outlining the proposed work plan development process and soliciting feedback on analysis criteria and participation.
- Laurel James identifies the Council's seven strategic priorities as the organizing framework for evaluating projects.
- Laurel James describes a timeline of study sessions and council business items from January through June concluding with adoption alongside the FY 2023/24 budget.
  #### Analysis criteria proposed
  Staff proposes criteria to analyze potential work plan projects, including impact on strategic priorities, equity effects on underserved communities, project dependencies and future capacity impacts, staff capacity, and estimated costs or revenues. These criteria are intended to guide staff recommendations to Council and to help prioritize projects realistically against existing commitments. The subcommittee discusses adding a sense of urgency/timeliness and clearer alignment to strategic priorities as additional criteria.
- Laurel James lists proposed analysis criteria including alignment with strategic priorities, equity impacts, project dependencies, staff capacity, and cost estimates.
- Laurel James says staff will estimate what resources each potential project requires to help prioritize and phase work.
- Committee member Alison Hicks requests an added criterion for urgency or timeliness to capture projects that require earlier action.
  #### Advisory body participation process
  Staff proposes that City Manager's Office staff and advisory body liaisons attend council advisory body meetings to solicit structured, high-level feedback on potential work plan projects and priorities. Feedback will be summarized by staff and provided to Council for use in prioritization. The subcommittee asks for a consistent approach so all advisory bodies receive the same framing and questions.
- Laurel James proposes CMO staff and advisory body liaisons attend regular advisory body meetings to solicit feedback on potential projects.
- Laurel James says staff will summarize advisory body input and provide it to Council for project prioritization discussions.
- Chair Ramirez asks for a consistent process so every advisory body is given the same scope and questions, and City Manager staff agree to provide consistent representation and framing.
  #### Project prioritization buckets
  Staff recommends categorizing proposed projects into three priority buckets (A, B, C) to reflect relative staff allocation and sequencing, with A as highest priority. The buckets are intended to account for limited staff capacity and to provide a practical way to phase work during the two-year cycle. Staff explains they will propose an initial categorization and Council will have the ability to adjust assignments during the prioritization meetings.
- Laurel James describes categories A, B, and C where A projects receive first consideration for staff allocation and phasing.
- Kimbre (City Manager) clarifies that staff will propose bucket assignments to avoid an unwieldy one-through-50 prioritization and to help the council focus on top priorities.
- Kimbre says staff will be prepared to move items between categories based on Council direction during prioritization.
  #### Subcommittee feedback questions
  Staff asks the subcommittee four specific questions: whether the January Council work plan process item should be consent or new business, whether additional analysis criteria are needed, feedback on advisory body participation, and feedback on the proposed project prioritization categories. Staff outlines next steps including incorporating subcommittee input into a January Council report and a February status update. The subcommittee deliberates on each question and requests clearer materials and visuals for Council.
- Laurel James asks whether the January Council item should be presented as a consent item or new business.
- Laurel James requests input on any additional analysis criteria beyond those staff proposed.
- Laurel James asks for subcommittee feedback on advisory body participation and the three-bucket prioritization approach.

  #### Attachments and housing element
  Committee member Alison Hicks asks whether the attachment to the memo contains the full list of candidate projects and whether housing element implementation projects are listed. Staff clarifies the attachment is a recent status update and that housing element actions are non-discretionary items spread across the eight-year cycle, with staff identifying which items are prioritized for the upcoming two-year work plan. The City Manager explains that Council will be able to prioritize housing element items among themselves but staff must consider capacity and sequencing.
- Alison Hicks asks whether the attachment to the memo reflects the complete list of projects to be considered during the study sessions.
- Laurel James clarifies the attachment is a status update on the existing FY21–23 work plan rather than a final candidate list.
- Kimbre (City Manager) explains housing element projects are non-discretionary over the eight-year cycle and Council will prioritize which of those to address in the next two-year period given staff capacity.
  #### Advisory body input constraints
  Staff and the subcommittee discuss the historical practice of advisory bodies providing feedback as a consultative process rather than creating full lists of project requests, and staff notes practical timing constraints. Audrey Ramberg and City Manager staff confirm prior processes (including 2020 civic engagement) and emphasize that advisory body input tends to be targeted feedback on staff/council-identified items. Staff commits to attending advisory body meetings with consistent questions and framing, but explains that obtaining advisory body input before the February Council milestone is not feasible this cycle.
- Kimbre (City Manager) recalls the 2020 process and notes advisory bodies have historically provided feedback rather than generate exhaustive new project lists.
- Audrey Ramberg explains that advisory body input typically serves as feedback on items staff and Council already identify rather than a 'blue sky' generation of 50 new projects.
- Audrey Ramberg says CMO staff will attend advisory body meetings this cycle to present consistent questions and collect comparable feedback for Council.
  #### Transparency and prioritization format
  Committee members discuss the need for increased transparency about the city's existing workload and clearer visual/status reporting to inform realistic prioritization. Chair Ramirez urges staff to surface the 'underwater' workload so Council understands how non-discretionary programs, the housing element, and other initiatives consume capacity, and Committee members request standardized tables or visuals showing status, department leads, projected completion quarters, and how items align with strategic priorities. The subcommittee also asks staff to propose a clear process for how new ideas are submitted, preliminarily scoped, analyzed, and either prioritized, deferred, or removed.
- Chair Lucas Ramirez asks staff to make the full scope of existing commitments more visible so Council can prioritize with realistic expectations.
- Ellen Kamei requests a consistent table format showing project status, department lead, update, projected completion quarter, and priority bucket.
- Alison Hicks asks that staff provide a clear template or guidance for how Council members submit new project ideas including preliminary scoping information.
  #### Decisions and next steps
  The subcommittee reaches consensus guidance for staff: present the work plan development process as a consent item in January, incorporate subcommittee feedback into the staff report, and provide clearer visuals and status tables for Council. The subcommittee supports staff providing preliminary A/B/C bucket recommendations for Council to adjust in April, standardizing advisory body questions with CMO attendance, and adding criteria for urgency/timeliness and explicit alignment to strategic priorities while integrating the REI action plan into equity analysis. Staff commits to incorporating the subcommittee's input and returning to Council per the proposed timeline.
- Chair Ramirez summarizes the subcommittee preference to present the January work plan process item as a consent item unless pulled by Council.
- Audrey Ramberg confirms staff will incorporate the subcommittee's feedback and provide visual timelines and clearer status reporting in the January Council report.
- Kimbre (City Manager) commits that staff will present preliminary bucketed recommendations and attend advisory body meetings to collect standardized input.
