### Overview
The meeting focused on the development of the fiscal years 2023-24 and 2024-25 work plan, with key discussions led by Principal Management Analyst Laurel James. Public comments highlighted accessibility issues with the Zoom meeting link and the importance of hybrid meetings for public participation. The subcommittee unanimously approved the minutes from the previous meeting and reviewed the proposed work plan development process, emphasizing the need for community input and alignment with strategic priorities. Key feedback included the addition of urgency as a criterion for project evaluation and the establishment of a consistent process for engaging council advisory bodies. The categorization of projects into priority buckets was discussed, with a focus on ensuring clarity and transparency in project status and prioritization.

### Detailed Summary
#### Public Comment on Meeting Access
Bruce England raises concerns about the accessibility of the Zoom meeting link, suggesting it should include the meeting ID directly in the URL for ease of access. He emphasizes the importance of maintaining hybrid meeting options for public participation, especially for equity and inclusion.
- Bruce England points out that the Zoom link lacks the meeting ID, making it inconvenient for mobile users.
- He advocates for hybrid meetings to ensure public participation, especially as in-person meetings are planned for February.

#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The subcommittee reviews and approves the minutes from the December 15th, 2020 meeting without any corrections or additions. The unanimous approval reflects the committee's agreement on the documented proceedings.
- Committee member Hicks motions to approve the minutes as written.
- Committee member Kumay seconds the motion, leading to a unanimous approval.

#### Work Plan Development Process Overview
Principal Management Analyst Laurel James presents the proposed timeline and process for developing the fiscal years 2023-24 and 2024-25 work plan. The discussion includes strategic priorities and the importance of community input in project categorization and prioritization.
- Laurel James outlines the strategic priorities adopted in 2021, which will guide the upcoming work plan.
- The proposed process includes regular updates to the council and community advisory bodies for feedback.

#### Feedback on Analysis Criteria
The subcommittee discusses the criteria for analyzing proposed projects, emphasizing the need for alignment with strategic priorities and consideration of equity impacts. Suggestions include adding urgency as a criterion to prioritize timely projects.
- Committee member Hicks suggests incorporating a sense of urgency into the analysis criteria for projects.
- The need for alignment with the REI action plan is highlighted as a critical factor in project evaluation.

#### Council Advisory Body Participation Process
The subcommittee discusses how to engage council advisory bodies in the work plan process, advocating for consistent questions across all bodies to ensure equitable input. The importance of managing expectations regarding capacity is also emphasized.
- Committee member Kamei proposes narrowing the scope of questions for advisory bodies to ensure focused feedback.
- The city manager's office will attend advisory body meetings to facilitate discussions and gather input.

#### Project Prioritization Categories
The proposed categorization of projects into A, B, and C buckets is discussed, with the understanding that projects in progress will likely be prioritized as A. The subcommittee seeks clarity on how to handle ongoing projects and new proposals.
- Staff will categorize projects based on priority, with in-progress items likely falling into category A.
- Committee member Hicks expresses concern about ensuring that new projects are not relegated to lower priority categories.

#### Process for Adding and Removing Projects
The subcommittee discusses the need for a clear process for adding new projects to the work plan and removing outdated or unnecessary items. This includes establishing a standardized format for project proposals to facilitate evaluation.
- The city manager suggests creating a standardized template for project proposals to streamline the process.
- Committee member Ramirez emphasizes the importance of having a removal process for stale projects.
