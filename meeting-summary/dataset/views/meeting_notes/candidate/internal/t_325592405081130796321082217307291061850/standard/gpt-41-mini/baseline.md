### Overview
The Mountain View City Council Goal Setting Subcommittee convened to discuss the development process for the fiscal years 2023-24 and 2024-25 Council work plan. Key participants included Chair Lucas Ramirez, committee members Alison Hicks and Ellen Kamei, Principal Management Analyst Laurel James, City Manager Kimbre McCarthy, and Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg. The meeting addressed public comments on meeting accessibility and hybrid formats, approval of prior meeting minutes, and a detailed presentation on the proposed work plan development timeline and criteria. The subcommittee engaged in a thorough discussion on enhancing transparency around the full scope of city projects and staff capacity to inform realistic goal setting. They emphasized the importance of consistent and equitable advisory body participation, standardized project submission methods, and clear project prioritization categories incorporating urgency and alignment with strategic priorities. The subcommittee reached consensus to present the work plan process as a consent item to the full Council, with staff tasked to refine analysis criteria, improve project status reporting, and facilitate structured advisory body input. This approach aims to foster a disciplined, transparent, and inclusive goal setting process that balances new initiatives with ongoing commitments and staff capacity constraints.

#### Work Plan Development Process Overview
Principal Management Analyst Laurel James presented the proposed timeline and process for developing the fiscal years 2023-24 and 2024-25 Council work plan. The process includes study sessions, council business items, and regular check-ins, culminating in adoption alongside the fiscal year 2023-24 budget. Staff seeks feedback on analysis criteria, advisory body participation, and project prioritization categories to guide the work plan development.
- Laurel James outlined the strategic priorities framing the work plan, including equity and sustainability.
- The proposed process spans from January to June 2023 with multiple council study sessions and updates.
- Staff proposed analysis criteria including impact on strategic priorities, equity considerations, staff capacity, and cost.
- A structured advisory body participation process is proposed to gather consistent feedback for Council.
- Projects will be categorized into priority buckets (A, B, C) to manage staff capacity and phasing.

#### Discussion on Work Plan Transparency and Prioritization
Chair Ramirez and committee members discussed the need for greater transparency regarding the full scope of city projects and staff capacity to better inform council goal setting. They considered integrating discretionary and non-discretionary projects into a comprehensive view to set realistic expectations and prioritize effectively. Staff acknowledged challenges but expressed commitment to improving clarity and analysis criteria.
- Chair Ramirez advocated for a holistic view of all city projects and staff workload to inform goal setting.
- Assistant City Manager Ramberg explained efforts to quantify staff capacity and project impacts, acknowledging limitations.
- Committee members suggested clearer presentation formats and inclusion of project status and timelines.
- There was consensus on incorporating equity aligned with the Race Equity and Inclusion (REI) action plan into analysis criteria.
#### Council Advisory Body Participation Process
The subcommittee reviewed the proposed process for advisory body input, emphasizing the need for consistency across bodies and focused questions to elicit meaningful feedback. Staff committed to attending advisory meetings to facilitate structured discussions and provide council with summarized input.
- Committee members recommended standardized questions for all advisory bodies to ensure equitable input.
- Staff will have a consistent representative attend advisory body meetings to solicit feedback.
- There was agreement that advisory bodies should understand staff capacity constraints to set realistic expectations.
#### Project Prioritization Categories and Process
Staff proposed categorizing projects into three priority buckets (A, B, C) to manage workload and resource allocation. The subcommittee discussed preferences for labeling, the need for clear criteria including urgency and alignment with strategic priorities, and the importance of transparency regarding project status and timelines. Staff will provide preliminary categorizations for council review and adjustment.
- Staff explained the rationale for three priority categories to focus resources effectively.
- Committee members suggested adding urgency/timeliness and strategic priority alignment as criteria.
- There was support for staff to recommend initial categorizations with council having the ability to adjust.
- Transparency on project status, estimated completion dates, and priority category was recommended.
#### Goal Setting Process and Project Submission Methods
The subcommittee discussed methods for council members to submit new project ideas, balancing brainstorming with realistic scoping. Staff described the timeline for project identification, analysis, and prioritization, emphasizing the need to limit the number of new projects to maintain a manageable work plan. Various methods such as dot voting, memos, and facilitated discussions were considered.
- Staff outlined a two-step process: council members identify projects, then staff analyze and categorize them.
- There was discussion about limiting new projects to maintain a feasible work plan size.
- Different methods for submitting and prioritizing projects were considered, including dot voting and written proposals.
- Staff will develop a standardized template for project submissions to aid analysis.
#### Summary and Next Steps
Chair Ramirez summarized the subcommittee's consensus: the work plan process will be presented as a consent item; analysis criteria will include urgency and strategic priority alignment; advisory body input will be standardized and facilitated by staff; project prioritization will use the proposed categories with staff recommendations; and staff will enhance transparency on project status and develop submission templates. Staff confirmed understanding and readiness to proceed accordingly.
- Chair Ramirez recapped majority support for consent item presentation of the process.
- Additional analysis criteria including urgency and strategic priority alignment were endorsed.
- Consistent advisory body participation with standardized questions was agreed upon.
- Project prioritization categories will be used with staff providing initial recommendations.
- Staff will improve transparency on project status and develop standardized project submission forms.
