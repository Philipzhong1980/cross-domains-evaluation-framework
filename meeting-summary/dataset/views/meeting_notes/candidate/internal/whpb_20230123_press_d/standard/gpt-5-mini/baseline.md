### Overview
  White House spokesperson Ian Sams briefs reporters on two principal developments: a White House Counsel letter responding to the House Oversight Committee and a voluntary, DOJ-conducted search of President Biden's Wilmington residence. Sams frames the counsel letter as a measured response that emphasizes the White House does not possess documents taken by the Archives and DOJ and underscores the need to protect the integrity of law enforcement investigations while engaging with the committee in good faith. On the search, Sams explains that the President's personal lawyers discovered additional material, informed the President, and proactively offered DOJ access for a thorough, nearly 13-hour consensual search that produced additional materials now in DOJ custody; he repeatedly defers detailed questions about contents, counts, and investigatory steps to the Department of Justice. Reporters probe payment for the President's personal attorneys, whether the President was aware of the materials, potential searches of other locations, search procedures and clearances, distinctions between White House Counsel and personal attorneys, and cooperation with Congress; in every instance, Sams emphasizes full cooperation with DOJ, points to public statements from the President's personal attorneys for procedural details, and offers to provide the White House Counsel letter on request while signaling continuing communication with both DOJ and congressional offices.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Counsel letter to Oversight
  Ian Sams opens by summarizing a letter the White House Counsel sent to the House Oversight Committee chairman responding to requests for documents. He says the letter clarifies that the White House does not possess documents that the National Archives and DOJ have taken and emphasizes protecting the integrity of law enforcement investigations. The White House offers to provide the letter to reporters on request and intends to engage with the committee in good faith to determine next steps.
- Ian Sams says the White House Counsel sent a response to the House Oversight Committee chairman and offered reporters a copy if they email Sharon.
- Ian Sams states that the White House does not have possession of documents that the Archives and DOJ have taken into their possession.
- Ian Sams emphasizes that the letter stresses protecting the integrity and independence of law enforcement investigations.
- Ian Sams says the White House intends to engage in conversations with the committee to determine next steps.
  #### Voluntary DOJ search
  Sams reports a voluntary, DOJ-conducted search of the President's Wilmington residence that lasted about 13 hours and gave DOJ access to every room in the house. He frames the offer as an unprecedented and transparent step demonstrating the President's cooperation and commitment to ensuring government possession of government records. DOJ described the action as a "planned consensual search," and some additional material was identified and taken into DOJ custody.
- Ian Sams says the President's personal lawyer disclosed a voluntary DOJ search of the President's Wilmington residence that lasted about 13 hours.
- Ian Sams describes the search as an unprecedented offer that gave DOJ access to every room to ensure any government documents in the home were returned to the government.
- Ian Sams reports that DOJ identified additional material and took possession of it during the consensual search.
- Ian Sams notes that DOJ asked the President's personal lawyer not to disclose the search until it concluded, and the White House agreed to protect the integrity of the investigation.

  Reporters ask who is paying the President's personal attorney and whether legal bills are covered by the President, his family, the DNC, or a super PAC.
