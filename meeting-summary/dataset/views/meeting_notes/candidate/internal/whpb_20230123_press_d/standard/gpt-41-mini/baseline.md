### Overview
In a detailed briefing led by White House spokesperson Ian Sams, the administration addressed inquiries regarding the discovery and handling of classified documents found at President Biden's residence. The White House Counsel sent a letter to the House Oversight Committee clarifying their position and emphasizing cooperation while protecting investigative integrity. The President proactively offered the Department of Justice a voluntary, consensual search of his Wilmington home, which lasted approximately 13 hours and resulted in the seizure of six items with classified markings, including materials from his Senate and Vice Presidential years. Throughout the discussion, the White House underscored the President's transparency and active cooperation with DOJ, while deferring detailed questions about the documents to the Justice Department to preserve the investigation's integrity. The briefing also clarified the roles of the President's personal attorneys versus the White House Counsel's Office, highlighted ongoing communication with Congress, and addressed media requests for greater transparency. Overall, the administration maintains a stance of full cooperation with law enforcement and congressional oversight, balancing this with a commitment to protect the independence of investigations and continue focusing on legislative priorities.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### White House Counsel Letter to Oversight
The White House Counsel sent a letter to the House Oversight Committee Chairman addressing inquiries about documents related to an ongoing investigation. The letter congratulated the Chairman on his new role, clarified that the White House does not possess the documents held by the Archives and DOJ, and emphasized protecting the integrity of law enforcement investigations. The White House expressed willingness to engage in good faith discussions with the committee regarding next steps.
- White House spokesperson Ian Sams stated the letter was sent in response to inquiries from the House Oversight Committee Chairman.
- The letter clarified that the White House does not have possession of the documents taken by the Archives and DOJ.
- The White House stressed the importance of protecting the integrity and independence of law enforcement investigations.
- Ian Sams noted the White House's commitment to work in good faith with Congress and engage in conversations with the committee.
#### DOJ Search of President's Residence
The President's personal lawyer disclosed a voluntary, consensual DOJ search of the President's Wilmington residence lasting about 13 hours. The search was unprecedented in scope, allowing DOJ access to all rooms and decades of materials. The White House emphasized the President's active cooperation and seriousness in addressing the issue. DOJ confirmed the search was planned and consensual, and additional materials were taken into possession.
- Ian Sams described the DOJ search as voluntary and consensual, lasting approximately 13 hours.
- The President offered DOJ access to his home to ensure government documents were properly in government possession.
- The search included access to handwritten notes, files, papers, and memorabilia spanning decades.
- DOJ took possession of additional material identified during the search.
- The White House Counsel's Office issued a statement affirming the President's commitment to cooperate with DOJ.
#### Details on Classified Documents Found
Questions arose regarding the nature and quantity of classified documents found at the President's residence. The White House referred to statements from the President's personal attorney indicating six items with classified markings were taken, including materials from the President's Senate and Vice Presidential years. The White House declined to provide further details, deferring to DOJ due to the ongoing investigation's integrity.
- Ian Sams referenced the President's personal attorney's statement about six items with classified markings being taken.
- Some materials included documents from the President's Senate and Vice Presidential service.
- Handwritten notes from the Vice Presidential years were also taken for review.
- The White House declined to comment further on the contents, citing respect for DOJ's ongoing investigation.
#### Search Procedures and Legal Coordination

Questions about whether FBI agents should conduct future searches were raised. The White House emphasized full cooperation with DOJ and adherence to agreed protocols. Decisions on future investigative steps remain under DOJ's purview, with the White House following their lead.
- Ian Sams explained that initial searches were coordinated with DOJ and conducted by the President's personal attorneys.
- The White House is fully cooperating with DOJ throughout the investigative process.
- Future searches and investigative steps are decisions for DOJ, with the White House not preempting those decisions.
- The President's personal attorneys and White House Counsel's Office were present during the DOJ search.
