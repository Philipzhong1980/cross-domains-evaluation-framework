### Overview
The meeting featured key briefings from MS. JEAN-PIERRE and Admiral John Kirby on several critical issues. The administration announced a Consumer Financial Protection Bureau proposal to reduce excessive bank overdraft fees, aiming to save American families billions annually. Positive economic indicators, including strong retail sales and public optimism, were highlighted as successes of Bidenomics. Admiral Kirby detailed the U.S. designation of the Houthis as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group due to their attacks on maritime vessels, emphasizing continued humanitarian aid to Yemen and the flexibility of the sanctions regime. The administration underscored the urgent need for congressional support to continue aid to Ukraine amid ongoing conflict, with a scheduled meeting focusing on this issue. Discussions also covered bipartisan negotiations on border security, the Vice President's upcoming abortion rights tour, and responses to media reports and political statements. The administration addressed concerns about the economic impact of the Red Sea conflict and highlighted the President's engagement with small businesses as a sign of economic confidence. Throughout, the administration emphasized bipartisan cooperation, national security priorities, and the importance of continued support for Ukraine and domestic economic policies.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Overdraft Fee Regulation Proposal
The Biden-Harris administration announces a new Consumer Financial Protection Bureau rule proposal aimed at ending excessive overdraft fees charged by banks. This proposal intends to reduce the average overdraft fee by more than half, potentially saving millions of families an average of $150 annually and $3.5 billion nationwide. The administration criticizes some Republicans for defending big banks' rights to impose these fees, emphasizing the President's commitment to protecting consumers from exploitation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the administration proposes a rule to end excessive overdraft fees.
- The proposal could save families an average of $150 per year and $3.5 billion annually across the U.S.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes some Republicans defend big banks' rights to charge these fees.
- The President believes it is wrong for companies to exploit Americans through such fees.
#### Economic Outlook and Retail Sales
The administration highlights positive economic indicators, including retail sales surpassing expectations during the holiday season, attributed to improved supply chains. A recent Axios poll shows a majority of Americans feel optimistic about their financial situations, with 63% rating their finances as good and 85% expecting improvement. These developments are presented as evidence of the effectiveness of 'Bidenomics' policies aimed at strengthening the economy from the middle out and bottom up.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports retail sales exceeded expectations last month, marking a record holiday season.
- She credits supply chain improvements for affordable and timely gift availability.
- An Axios poll shows 63% of Americans rate their finances as good, and 85% expect improvement.
- The administration attributes these positive trends to Bidenomics policies.
#### Houthi Terrorist Designation
The U.S. designates the Houthis as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group due to their attacks on ships in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden, which threaten innocent lives and global commerce. The designation excludes the Yemeni people, with the U.S. reaffirming its humanitarian aid commitment. The designation takes effect in 30 days to allow aid organizations to adjust and ensure continued humanitarian assistance. The administration states it will reconsider the designation if the Houthis cease attacks but emphasizes no ongoing negotiations with the group.
- Admiral John Kirby announces the Houthi designation as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group.
- The designation targets the Houthis' attacks on maritime vessels, not the Yemeni people.
- The U.S. remains the leading donor of humanitarian aid to Yemen, supporting over 15 million in need.
- The designation takes effect in 30 days to allow aid organizations to adapt and continue assistance.
- Kirby clarifies there are no negotiations with the Houthis; the designation is a measure of accountability.
#### Humanitarian Aid and Sanctions Flexibility
The administration explains the choice of designation allows more flexibility for humanitarian aid delivery compared to a Foreign Terrorist Organization label. Licenses have been issued to ensure aid can continue without violating sanctions. The designation is part of a broader strategy using diplomatic, informational, military, and economic tools to pressure the Houthis to stop attacks.
- Kirby states the current designation provides more flexibility for aid organizations than an FTO designation.
- Licenses have been issued as waivers to allow humanitarian goods to flow despite sanctions.
- The administration uses all levers of national power (DIME) to hold the Houthis accountable.
- The designation supports continued humanitarian assistance while addressing security concerns.

A meeting with congressional leaders is scheduled to focus on the urgent need for continued U.S. support to Ukraine amid ongoing conflict. The administration stresses that no new security assistance package has been approved since December 27 due to funding delays. The meeting aims to update lawmakers on battlefield conditions and the critical need for weapons and capabilities to sustain Ukraine's defense against Russian aggression.
- Kirby notes the meeting will emphasize the urgent need for weapons and capabilities for Ukraine.
- No new security assistance package has been approved since December 27 due to funding issues.
- The administration will provide classified and unclassified briefings to Congress.
- Kirby highlights ongoing Russian attacks and Ukraine's depletion of certain munitions and air defense capabilities.
#### Houthi Attacks and Iran's Role
The administration confirms a private message was sent to Iran regarding Houthi attacks but declines to provide details. It rejects claims linking Houthi attacks to the Gaza conflict, noting that targeted ships were not destined for Israel. The U.S. has increased military resources in the region to protect national security interests and will continue to take action against Houthi aggression.
- Kirby confirms a private message was delivered to Iran about Houthi attacks but offers no details.
- He refutes claims that Houthi attacks are connected to the Gaza conflict, noting no ships targeted were bound for Israel.
- The U.S. has deployed additional military resources to the region to protect national security interests.
- The administration will continue to respond to Houthi aggression as necessary.

The President and national security team aim to inform Congress about battlefield realities and the consequences of funding delays, underscoring the importance of continued support for Ukraine's defense.

- There is no replenishment authority for Ukraine aid, limiting new assistance despite some authorized funds.
- The President's meeting with Congress will provide updates on battlefield conditions and funding urgency.

#### Economic Impact of Red Sea Conflict
The administration monitors the impact of the Red Sea conflict on shipping costs and supply chains. Despite significant increases in shipping container costs to Europe and North America, the U.S. has not yet seen major effects on supply availability or port capacity. National security and economic teams continue to assess the situation closely.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the administration is closely monitoring the Red Sea conflict's impact on shipping costs.
- Shipping container costs to Europe and North America have risen significantly over the past year.
- So far, there is limited impact on U.S. supply chains and port capacities.
- The administration continues to evaluate potential economic effects.
