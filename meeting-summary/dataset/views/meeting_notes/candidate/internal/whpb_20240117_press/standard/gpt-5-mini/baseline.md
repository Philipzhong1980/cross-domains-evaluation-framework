### Overview
  Karine Jean-Pierre and Admiral John Kirby lead the briefing, covering a range of economic and national security priorities. The administration announces support for a CFPB rule to curb excessive overdraft fees and highlights strong retail-sales data and consumer sentiment as evidence of its economic approach. Admiral Kirby explains the U.S. designation of the Houthis as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group in response to attacks on commercial shipping, emphasizing humanitarian carveouts, a 30-day implementation window to coordinate with aid organizations, and the option to rescind the designation if attacks stop; he frames the response as a coordinated use of diplomatic, informational, military, and economic tools while declining to discuss private messages to Iran. The briefing also centers on an urgent White House meeting with congressional leaders about new security assistance for Ukraine, with officials warning that no new U.S. assistance can flow without congressional funding and that certain Ukrainian munitions and air-defense capabilities are being drawn down; the White House lists attendees and says the meeting will include classified briefings and a readout afterward. Reporters press the administration on border-security negotiations, Turkey F-16 sales, an NSC dispute with HuffPost, the Vice President’s Roe anniversary events, condemnation of Jimmy Lai’s prosecution in Hong Kong, and the economic effects of shipping diversions around the Red Sea, and the White House reiterates its positions while declining to negotiate legislative details from the podium.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Overdraft fees and economy
  Press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre announces a CFPB proposal to curb excessive overdraft fees and highlights broader economic indicators showing consumer strength. She frames the proposal as saving millions of families roughly $150 per year and reducing aggregate costs by about $3.5 billion annually, and links retail-sales gains and an Axios poll to administration economic policy. The administration positions these actions as part of ongoing efforts to lower costs and build the economy from the middle out.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Biden-Harris administration is backing a Consumer Financial Protection Bureau rule to end excessive overdraft fees.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the proposal would cut average overdraft fees by more than half and save millions of families about $150 per year.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states that retail sales beat expectations over the holiday season and cites an Axios poll showing strong consumer financial sentiment.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE attributes these economic signs to administration policies she calls 'Bidenomics.'
#### Houthis designation announcement
  Admiral John Kirby announces the United States designates the Houthis as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group in response to their attacks on commercial shipping in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden. He stresses the designation targets the Houthi organization and not the Yemeni people and emphasizes protections for humanitarian assistance. The designation takes effect in 30 days to allow coordination with aid organizations and may be reconsidered if the Houthis cease attacks.
- Admiral John Kirby says the U.S. designates the Houthis as a Specially Designated Global Terrorist Group because of their reckless attacks on ships transiting the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden.
- Admiral John Kirby stresses the designation targets the Houthis and not the Yemeni people and notes the U.S. remains a major humanitarian donor to Yemen.
- Admiral John Kirby says the designation takes effect in 30 days to give aid organizations time to understand and adapt to the sanctions regime.
- Admiral John Kirby says the administration can reconsider the designation if the Houthis stop their attacks.
#### Humanitarian carveouts and licenses
  Reporters press Kirby on why the Houthis were not redesignated as a Foreign Terrorist Organization (FTO) and about the impact on aid delivery. Kirby explains the Specially Designated Global Terrorist designation provides greater flexibility and comfort for aid organizations, and the administration has already issued licenses (carveouts) to enable humanitarian goods to flow. The 30-day delay and licensing aim to preserve lifesaving assistance while applying economic pressure.
- Admiral John Kirby explains the SDGT designation provides more flexibility than an FTO designation and gives aid organizations greater assurance about continuing humanitarian deliveries.
- Admiral John Kirby says the administration has already issued a number of licenses as carveouts to allow humanitarian goods to continue to flow despite sanctions.
- Admiral John Kirby says preserving the ability of aid organizations to deliver food, water, and medicine is a major factor in the administration's approach.
#### Sanctions, military actions, Iran
  Kirby frames the administration’s response as a coordinated use of DIME—diplomacy, information, military, and economic tools—to hold the Houthis accountable and protect maritime commerce. He deflects questions about the content of a private message to Iran and notes the administration has sanctioned many Iranian entities in recent years while conducting air operations to degrade Houthi capabilities. He warns further actions remain on the table if attacks continue.
- Admiral John Kirby says the administration is using all levers of national power—diplomacy, information, military, and economic—to pressure the Houthis to stop attacks.
- Admiral John Kirby declines to describe the private message to Iran, stating only that a message was delivered.
- Admiral John Kirby reminds reporters the administration has sanctioned hundreds of Iranian entities in recent years and says the U.S. will take additional action if necessary.
- Admiral John Kirby says recent strikes were designed to disrupt and degrade Houthi offensive capabilities and that they had a degrading effect.
#### NSC response to HuffPost reporting
  Reporters ask about an NSC statement disputing quotes in a HuffPost article about U.S. post-war Gaza planning. Kirby says the NSC response addresses purported quotes from a document for which the administration has no record, and the intent was not to cast aspersions on journalistic ethics but to correct inaccuracies tied to a non-existent document. The administration says it stands by its response and will not expand beyond that explanation in the briefing.
- Admiral John Kirby says the NSC's statement responds to quotes attributed to a purported document for which the administration has no record.
- Admiral John Kirby says the NSC did not intend to impugn journalism or journalistic ethics with its response.
- Admiral John Kirby says the administration stands by its original statement correcting the document-based quotations in the article.
#### Ukraine funding urgency
  Kirby and the White House stress the urgent need for additional security assistance to Ukraine and describe a White House meeting with congressional leaders focused on that need. They note the last U.S. security assistance package was Dec 27 and that there is no replenishment authority to sustain continued transfers without congressional action. The administration warns Russian attacks continue, Ukrainian inventories are depleting on some munitions and air defense capabilities are especially critical.
- Admiral John Kirby says the White House meeting with congressional leaders will focus on the desperate, urgent need for weapons and capabilities for Ukraine.
- Admiral John Kirby says the last security assistance package for Ukraine was Dec. 27 and no further assistance can flow without new funding and replenishment authority.
- Admiral John Kirby says Ukrainian forces continue to come under artillery, missile, and drone attacks and are expending munitions at high rates.
- Admiral John Kirby warns that some Ukrainian inventories are running lower and highlights air defense as a particularly critical capability.
#### Congress meeting logistics
  Karine Jean-Pierre outlines the White House meeting participants and purpose: it is primarily about Ukraine and will include classified content and briefings from national security officials. She lists senior congressional leaders and committee chairs plus key White House and national security staff who will attend and notes the meeting is closed but will be followed by a readout and opportunities for press questions. The administration emphasizes the meeting’s focus on sustaining U.S. leadership and the flow of assistance.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the meeting's purpose is to underscore urgency for continued Ukraine assistance and to bring members up to speed on battlefield realities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE lists attendees including Speaker Johnson, Leader Schumer, Leader McConnell, Representative Jeffries, committee chairs and ranking members, and senior White House and national security officials.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says some of the discussion will be classified and that the meeting is closed with an expected readout afterward.
#### Border security negotiations
  Reporters press the White House on its engagement with House Republicans and the ongoing Senate negotiations over border security and immigration. Jean-Pierre emphasizes that bipartisan Senate talks continue, that the President is focused on reaching a bipartisan agreement, and declines to negotiate details publicly. She also defends the administration's critique that House Republicans have obstructed prior border efforts and reiterates support for continuing Senate negotiations.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the White House is supporting bipartisan negotiations in the Senate on border security and will not negotiate details from the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President is willing to hear members of Congress but will focus his meeting on Ukraine while remaining open to other conversations.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the House left town during prior negotiations and contends House Republicans have at times gotten in the way of border-security progress.
#### Claims about House GOP vote
  Reporters challenge the White House assertion that House Republicans voted to eliminate 2,000 Border Patrol agents via the Limit, Save, Grow Act. Jean-Pierre reiterates the administration’s characterization that the House voted for and touted an act that would have forced such eliminations, while the press corps disputes that framing. The White House maintains its position and ties the critique to larger arguments about Republican priorities on immigration and other policies.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says House Republicans voted for and touted the Limit, Save, Grow Act, which the White House asserts would have forced elimination of 2,000 Border Patrol agents.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration stands by its statements and frames the legislative history as evidence of House Republican priorities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE ties the administration's criticism to broader claims about Republican opposition to key domestic initiatives like the American Rescue Plan and Inflation Reduction Act.
#### DNC comment and apology
  Jean-Pierre addresses a DNC spokesperson’s flippant statement following Governor Hutchinson’s suspension of his presidential campaign and says the White House apologized privately. She says President Biden respects Governor Hutchinson, and Chief of Staff Jeff Zients called to convey an apology that the statement did not represent the President’s views. The White House declines to discuss any communications with the DNC directly.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says President Biden respects Governor Hutchinson and that Chief of Staff Jeff Zients called the governor to apologize for the DNC statement.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the DNC statement did not reflect the President's views and that the White House conveyed that apology to the governor.
#### Turkey F-16 sale update
  A reporter asks about the status of F-16 sales to Turkey and whether Sweden’s NATO ratification would clear the path. Kirby says the administration remains consistent in supporting F-16 sales and modernization for Turkey but offers no timeline or calendar details. He defers to process and provides no update on linkage to Sweden’s ratification.
- Admiral John Kirby says the administration continues to support additional F-16 sales and modernization for Turkey and that policy has not changed.
- Admiral John Kirby says he does not have a date or timeline update on the process or on whether Sweden’s NATO ratification would clear the path.
#### Vice President Roe anniversary tour
  Jean-Pierre previews Vice President Harris’s planned activities to mark the 51st anniversary of Roe v. Wade, saying both the President and Vice President will mark the anniversary. She frames the tour as part of the administration’s effort to defend reproductive rights against state-level restrictions and refers detailed logistics to the Vice President’s office. The White House will provide additional details about the President’s participation later.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Vice President will mark the 51st anniversary of Roe v. Wade and will begin a tour in Wisconsin to defend reproductive rights.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says both the President and Vice President will mark the anniversary and that the Vice President's office will release further tour details.
#### Hong Kong trial condemnation
  Jean-Pierre states the United States strongly condemns the prosecution of pro-democracy advocate Jimmy Lai under Hong Kong’s National Security Law and calls for his immediate and unconditional release. She says the U.S. will continue to monitor prosecutions under the law closely and declines to engage in hypotheticals about future actions. The statement positions the administration as actively following the trial's developments.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the U.S. strongly condemns the prosecution of Jimmy Lai under Hong Kong's National Security Law.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE calls for Jimmy Lai's immediate and unconditional release and says the U.S. will closely monitor developments.
#### Red Sea shipping and supply chains
  Reporters ask whether diverted shipping around the Red Sea is raising consumer prices; Jean-Pierre says the administration is monitoring the situation closely. She reports that so far diversions have had limited impact on U.S. supply chains and energy prices and have not materially affected product availability at U.S. ports. The White House economic and national security teams continue to track developments.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the White House is closely monitoring shipping diversions around the Red Sea but has seen limited impact on U.S. supply chains and energy prices to date.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says diversions to the Cape of Good Hope have not produced major availability issues at U.S. ports so far.
