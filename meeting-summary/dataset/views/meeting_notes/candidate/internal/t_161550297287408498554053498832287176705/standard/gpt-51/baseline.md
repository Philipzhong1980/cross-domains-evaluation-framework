### Overview
  The Senior Advisory Committee meeting focuses on establishing 2023 leadership, receiving subcommittee and staff updates, and planning for future engagement with Mountain View’s older adults. Members unanimously elect Deanne Hui as chair and Elna Tymes as vice chair for the 2023 calendar year, then hear from the Communications Subcommittee as Ish Sinha reports on outreach across diverse communities and proposes a vetted “friendly visitors” program to address widespread loneliness among both seniors and younger residents. The Senior Center Subcommittee notes limited recent activity and anticipates reconfiguring its membership, while Elna Tymes reports for the Senior Survey Subcommittee that an external partner has completed a survey including Mountain View seniors and will share results after its board review, enabling comparison with the 2018–2019 State of Mountain View Seniors report. Staff liaison Ashley Bittle describes strong participation in recent Senior Center offerings—including an Alzheimer’s workshop by Elna Tymes, a Master Gardener rose-care session, CTAP smartphone trainings, and the upcoming February 11 pancake breakfast—and discusses transportation access with Jim Nichols and Elna Tymes, who underscore the need to monitor shifting senior concentrations and use tools like the age-friendly resource guide, Google shuttle, VTA, and RoadRunners to sustain access. In roundtable, Elna Tymes announces a February 18 Il Volo benefit dinner-concert to raise funds for senior service organizations, and the meeting concludes with confirmation that the next SAC session will be held in person at the Senior Center on February 15, reflecting a broader return to in-person engagement.

#### Communications subcommittee outreach and loneliness concerns
  The Communications Subcommittee reports on outreach to diverse community members and identifies widespread lack of awareness about Senior Center activities and programs. Subcommittee member Ish Sinha emphasizes that both seniors and younger residents experience significant loneliness and social isolation, which impacts mental health and may contribute to conditions like Alzheimer’s. Ish proposes creating a ‘Friendly Visitors’ program where vetted volunteers regularly visit homebound seniors and possibly isolated younger residents to provide companionship and information about city resources. The committee listens to this conceptual program suggestion as a potential direction to improve communication, inclusion, and well-being among Mountain View residents of all backgrounds.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha reports that many people he meets from varied nationalities know the Senior Center exists but say they want more information about its activities.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha states that many older residents are very lonely, especially those who are homebound and rarely interact with others.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha suggests creating a ‘friendly visitors program’ where seniors and other volunteers visit isolated older adults, ideally matching language or cultural backgrounds when possible.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha notes that loneliness affects mental health and can contribute to Alzheimer’s risk among older adults.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha shares that many younger residents, including newcomers from other countries, also feel lonely and benefit from having an older ‘father figure’ to talk to about their difficulties in the United States.
- Communications Subcommittee member Ish Sinha emphasizes that volunteers in a friendly visitors program would need vetting and that such a program could strengthen communication across all nationalities and communities while promoting awareness of Mountain View’s services and volunteer work.

#### Transportation access to the Senior Center
  Committee members discuss transportation options and planning to ensure seniors can reach the Senior Center and other city services. Jim Nichols asks whether the center receives calls about transportation difficulties and stresses the need for city and transportation partners to track changing senior populations and neighborhood needs. Ashley Bittle responds that while such calls are occasional, staff use the age-friendly resource guide and promote options like the free Google shuttle, VTA, and RYDE/RoadRunners services to connect seniors to the center. Elna Tymes adds that transportation is a recurring concern citywide, noting that Mountain View’s coordination is relatively strong but depends on SAC to identify emerging clusters of seniors; she suggests considering an annual “state of seniors” update on where older residents live to inform access planning.
- Committee Member Jim Nichols asks whether the Senior Center is receiving calls from seniors who have difficulty accessing the center via existing transportation routes.
- Staff liaison Ashley Bittle confirms that the center occasionally receives transportation inquiries and uses the age-friendly task force’s resource guide to share available transportation options.
- Staff liaison Ashley Bittle identifies the free Google shuttle, VTA services, and RoadRunners as key transportation options available to help seniors reach the Senior Center.
- Committee Member Jim Nichols emphasizes that he wants assurance that inquiries about transportation receive prompt responses and that transportation providers reach neighborhoods where seniors are aging in place.
- Committee Member Elna Tymes notes that transportation consistently emerges as an issue in many city groups and that providers cannot always guarantee door-to-door service but make strong efforts to reach seniors.
- Committee Member Elna Tymes asserts that Mountain View’s transportation coordination for seniors is better than San Jose’s, citing attention to routing such as Google bus and other pick-up routes.
- Committee Member Jim Nichols expresses concern that transportation organizations track shifting concentrations of seniors in neighborhoods so new or aging communities are not overlooked.
- Committee Member Elna Tymes states that it is the SAC’s responsibility to inform the city and providers about emerging clusters of seniors, suggesting that an annual ‘State of Mountain View Seniors’ type report could highlight where older adults are concentrated.
- Committee Member Elna Tymes notes that such geographic analysis may not be included in the current year’s State of Seniors report but should be considered as a future addition.
