### Overview
The Senior Advisory Committee convened on January 18th, 2023, with key participants including Chair Deanne Hui, Vice Chair Elna Tymes, and committee members such as Vicki Gilfix, Jim Nichols, and Ish Sinha. The meeting began with approval of the previous meeting's minutes, followed by the election of Deanne Hui as Chair and Elna Tymes as Vice Chair for the year. Ish Sinha presented a proposal for a friendly visitors program to address senior loneliness, highlighting the need for enhanced communication across diverse community groups. Updates from subcommittees revealed limited recent activity in the Senior Center subcommittee and pending analysis of senior survey results. Ashley Bittle reported increased participation in Senior Center workshops and announced upcoming events, including a pancake breakfast and smartphone training sessions. Transportation access for seniors was discussed, emphasizing existing city resources and the importance of monitoring demographic changes to ensure service coverage. Elna Tymes announced a benefit concert fundraiser supporting senior services scheduled for February 18th. The committee confirmed the next meeting will be held in person on February 15th and adjourned at 2:39 PM. Overall, the meeting focused on leadership transitions, community outreach initiatives, program updates, and planning for upcoming events to support Mountain View's senior population.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The committee reviewed and approved the minutes from the November 16th, 2022 meeting. This formal approval ensures the accuracy and official record of prior discussions and decisions. The motion to approve was moved by Jim Nichols and seconded by Vicki Gilfix, with all members voting in favor.
- Jim Nichols moved to approve the November 16th, 2022 minutes.
- Vicki Gilfix seconded the motion to approve the minutes.
- Ashley Bittle confirmed unanimous approval from committee members.
#### Election of 2023 Chair and Vice Chair
The committee conducted elections for the 2023 Chair and Vice Chair positions. Vicki Gilfix nominated Deanne Hui for Chair, and Elna Tymes volunteered for Vice Chair. The nominations were closed and both were unanimously approved to lead the committee for the calendar year. This leadership transition is important for guiding future meetings and initiatives.
- Vicki Gilfix nominated Deanne Hui as Chair for 2023.
- Elna Tymes volunteered to serve as Vice Chair.
- Jim Nichols moved to close nominations, which was seconded and approved.
- The committee unanimously voted to elect Deanne Hui as Chair and Elna Tymes as Vice Chair.
#### Communications Subcommittee Update
Ish Sinha reported on outreach efforts indicating that many community members are aware of the Senior Center but desire more information. He proposed a 'friendly visitors program' to address loneliness among seniors, especially those homebound, by pairing them with volunteers for social interaction. This initiative aims to improve mental health and community connection among diverse senior populations.
- Ish Sinha noted community members want more information about Senior Center activities.
- He proposed a friendly visitors program to reduce loneliness among homebound seniors.
- Ish emphasized the mental health benefits of social interaction for seniors.
- He highlighted the importance of communication across diverse nationalities in the community.
#### Senior Center Subcommittee Status
Vicki Gilfix reported limited activity from the Senior Center subcommittee due to membership changes and other priorities. The subcommittee has not made significant progress recently but anticipates reorganization with the new committee year. This status update reflects the need for renewed focus on Senior Center initiatives.

#### Senior Survey Subcommittee Update
Elna Tymes provided an update on the senior survey results collected by Bonita and John Sink. Although the results have been received, the holiday season delayed analysis and reporting. The survey includes Mountain View residents and will be summarized and shared with the committee once finalized. This data is critical for understanding senior needs and informing city planning.
- Elna Tymes reported survey results have been received but not yet analyzed.
- She plans to email a summary to committee members once available.
- The survey includes Mountain View residents and updates prior data from 2018-2019.

#### Senior Center Program Updates
Ashley Bittle shared recent and upcoming Senior Center activities, including a successful Alzheimer's workshop, a rose care workshop by Master Gardeners, and upcoming California Telephone Access Program sessions teaching smartphone use. Additionally, the popular pancake breakfast event is scheduled for February 11th, with ticket sales progressing well. Attendance at workshops is increasing, indicating growing community engagement.
- Vice Chair Elna Tymes led a successful Alzheimer's workshop at the Senior Center.
- Master Gardeners conducted a workshop on rose care.
- Upcoming workshops on Android and iPhone use are scheduled for January.
- The pancake breakfast event is planned for February 11th with tickets selling steadily.
- Workshop attendance has increased, reflecting higher community participation.
#### Transportation Access for Seniors
Jim Nichols inquired about transportation inquiries from seniors and the city's efforts to provide access to the Senior Center. Ashley Bittle confirmed occasional questions and shared information about available transportation options including the free Google Shuttle, VTA, and Rd. runners. Elna Tymes emphasized ongoing city efforts to reach seniors and suggested the committee consider annual reporting on senior population concentrations to better inform transportation planning.
- Jim Nichols asked about senior inquiries regarding transportation to the Senior Center.
- Ashley Bittle confirmed availability of multiple transportation options for seniors.
- Elna Tymes noted transportation is a recurring topic across city groups.
- She suggested annual reporting on senior population clusters to aid service planning.
- Jim Nichols stressed the importance of monitoring demographic shifts to ensure access.
