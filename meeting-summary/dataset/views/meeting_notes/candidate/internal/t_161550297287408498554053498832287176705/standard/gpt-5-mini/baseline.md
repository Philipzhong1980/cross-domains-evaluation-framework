### Overview
  The Senior Advisory Committee convenes, approves the November 16, 2022 minutes, and elects Deanne Hui as chair with Elna Tymes as vice chair for 2023. Committee members review subcommittee statuses: Ish Sinha reports outreach findings and proposes a vetted 'friendly visitors' program to combat senior isolation; Vicki Gilfix reports limited recent activity in the Senior Center subcommittee; and Elna Tymes says survey results have arrived and she will circulate a summary once reviewed. Ashley Bittle updates the group on Senior Center programming, noting successful workshops, CTAP device training returning, higher attendance, and a pancake breakfast on February 11 with tickets on sale. Jim Nichols raises transportation-access concerns for neighborhoods with growing senior populations, and Elna Tymes suggests the SAC may need to map or report concentrations to inform service providers. Elna also announces a February 18 benefit dinner and concert to raise funds for local senior organizations, and members confirm the next meeting will be in person on February 15 at 2:00 PM before adjourning at 2:39 PM.

  #### Transportation access concerns
  Jim Nichols raises concern about whether transportation providers track shifting senior populations and ensure service to neighborhoods with growing numbers of older residents. Elna Tymes explains that transportation groups make efforts to reach seniors though they cannot guarantee door-to-door service and suggests the SAC could help identify concentrations of seniors for the city. The committee discusses the idea of including distribution or mapping of senior concentrations in future reporting.
- Jim Nichols expresses concern that transportation providers may not be keeping abreast of demographic shifts that create new concentrations of seniors.
- Elna Tymes notes transportation programs regularly try to reach seniors but cannot guarantee door-to-door service.
- Elna Tymes suggests the SAC could alert city staff to clusters of seniors and consider mapping concentrations in future reports.
