### Overview
The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee convened to review a development project at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Avenue involving a new three-story office building and a lot line adjustment combining two lots across two zoning districts. Planner Brittany Whitehill presented the project details, confirming compliance with zoning codes, the General Plan, and environmental regulations, including a CEQA negative declaration. Public comment was received from Weng Tam of the Norcal Carpenter Union, who advocated for local labor standards and hiring practices, seeking engagement with the developer. The committee discussed the permissibility and rationale for the lot line adjustment spanning two zoning districts, with staff affirming the site layout's compatibility and design merits. Following deliberation, the committee unanimously approved the lot line adjustment and the development review permit, with decisions subject to appeal within 10 calendar days. Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi presided over the hearing and confirmed the approvals, concluding the session with an invitation for further inquiries to the project planner.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Project Overview and Location
The committee discussed a development project located on the south side of Terra Bella Avenue, involving the construction of a three-story office building and associated site changes. This project includes a lot line adjustment to combine two lots spanning two zoning districts. Understanding the project's location and scope is essential for evaluating zoning and environmental compliance.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill described the project as a 19,958 square foot office building with surface parking on a 1.3-acre site.
- The project site spans the ML Limited Industrial and MMM General Industrial zoning districts.
- The lot line adjustment aims to combine two lots into one, resulting in a single lot spanning two zoning districts.
#### Zoning and Lot Line Adjustment
The committee examined the implications of the lot line adjustment that would create a single lot spanning two zoning districts. Staff explained that the subdivision committee has authority to approve such configurations if a consolidated single zoning designation would not improve neighborhood compatibility or site layout. Staff found the proposed layout compatible and consistent with zoning requirements.
- Planner Whitehill noted that the lot line adjustment is permissible under section 36.06 of the zoning code.
- Staff concluded that the project complies with zoning codes and that a single zoning designation would not yield a superior site layout or neighborhood compatibility.
- The site layout places parking at the rear and landscaping and pedestrian amenities at the front, aligning with desired design standards.
#### Environmental Compliance and Approvals
The project underwent environmental review under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA), resulting in a negative declaration indicating no significant environmental impacts. Staff recommended conditional approval of the project and associated lot line adjustment based on compliance with zoning, general plan, and environmental standards.
- An initial study and negative declaration were prepared and circulated for public comment from September 14 to October 14, 2022.
- Staff found the project consistent with the Mountain View Zoning Code, General Plan, and Subdivision Map Act.
- The project was determined to have no significant environmental impacts with standard city conditions incorporated.
#### Public Comment on Labor Standards
A representative from the Norcal Carpenter Union addressed the committee, emphasizing the importance of local labor standards and hiring for the project. The speaker highlighted benefits such as reduced traffic, greenhouse gas emissions, and support for local businesses, and requested engagement with the developer regarding labor language and apprenticeship opportunities.
- Weng Tam, representing over 7,000 carpenters in Santa Clara County, advocated for local hiring and labor standards.
- Tam stressed the importance of workers staying close to home to support family time and local economy.
- The union has reached out to the developer but has not yet received a response, seeking further discussion.
#### Committee Deliberation and Approval
The subdivision committee deliberated on the lot line adjustment and development permits. Questions were addressed regarding the findings related to zoning compatibility and site layout. The committee unanimously approved the lot line adjustment and the development review permit, with decisions subject to appeal within 10 calendar days.
- Committee member Fazely requested clarification on the findings regarding the lot spanning two zoning districts.
- Planner Whitehill explained the discretionary authority and rationale for approving the split zoning configuration.
- Fazely moved to approve the lot line adjustment, seconded by Pancholi, with unanimous committee approval.
- Zoning Administrator Pancholi approved the development review permit, confirming consistency with zoning and general plan.
- Both approvals are appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.
