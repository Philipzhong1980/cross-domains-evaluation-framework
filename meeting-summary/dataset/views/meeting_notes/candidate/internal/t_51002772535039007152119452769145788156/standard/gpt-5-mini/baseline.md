### Overview
  The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee hears a single item for 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Ave proposing a three‑story, 19,958 sq ft office building, surface parking, removal of three heritage trees, and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots into one. Project planner Brittany Whitehill explains the combined lot would span ML and MMM industrial zones, that staff finds the proposed split‑zoning layout consistent with the Zoning Code, General Plan, Subdivision Map Act, and that an IS/ND was circulated with no significant impacts, and staff recommends conditional approval. A NorCal Carpenters representative (Weng/Wing Tam) requests developer engagement on local hiring, labor standards, and apprenticeships and reports no developer response to prior outreach. Committee member Diana Fazely questions whether a consolidated single zoning designation would produce a superior layout, staff explains the code discretion and that the proposed design places parking to the rear and pedestrian amenities to the front, and the subdivision committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment. Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi approves the development review permit with the posted findings and conditions, notes both decisions are appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days, and closes the public hearing with directions to contact the project planner for follow‑up.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Project scope and requests
  The committee reviews a development at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Ave proposing a three‑story, 19,958 sq ft office building with surface parking on a 1.3‑acre site, a heritage tree removal permit for three trees, and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots into one. This item matters because the combined lot will span two zoning districts, requiring committee consideration of whether that configuration is appropriate. The item is presented as a single agenda item for the joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee to decide.
- Diana Pancholi states the project requests a development review permit for a three‑story 19,958 sq ft office building and associated surface parking at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Ave.
- Diana Pancholi states the proposal includes a heritage removal permit to remove three heritage trees and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots into one on a 1.3‑acre site.
  #### Zoning and compliance findings
  Project planner Brittany Whitehill explains the site sits between North Shoreline Blvd and Linda Vista Ave and straddles the ML Limited Industrial and MMM General Industrial zoning districts. Staff notes the lot line adjustment would create a single lot spanning two zones, which the code permits if a consolidated single zone would not yield greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout, and finds the proposed design meets zoning, the General Plan, the Subdivision Map Act, and CEQA requirements. An initial study and negative declaration were circulated for 30 days and staff recommends conditional approval with the posted findings and conditions. The committee is asked to adopt staff’s recommendation.
- Brittany Whitehill explains the combined lot would span ML Limited Industrial and MMM General Industrial zoning districts.
- Brittany Whitehill states the code allows a lot to span multiple zoning districts if a consolidated zone would not result in greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout.
- Brittany Whitehill reports staff finds the project consistent with the Zoning Code, General Plan, the Subdivision Map Act, and that an IS/ND was circulated with no significant environmental impacts identified.
- Brittany Whitehill recommends conditional approval of the project and associated lot line adjustment based on the staff analysis and posted conditions.
  #### Local labor and hiring
  A representative from NorCal Carpenters Union (Weng/Wing Tam) urges the city and developer to adopt local hiring and labor standards, emphasizing benefits such as proximity for workers, family time, local spending, reduced traffic and greenhouse gases, and apprenticeship opportunities. The speaker notes prior contact attempts with the developer produced no response and requests engagement between the union and developer to incorporate carpenter labor standards and local hiring into the project. This public comment registers a labor and community concern that the committee did not resolve during the hearing.
- Weng Tam states he represents NorCal Carpenters Local 405 and emphasizes the importance of local hiring and area labor standards on city projects.
- Weng Tam says local hiring reduces traffic and greenhouse gases and helps workers spend money locally and spend more time with family.
- Weng Tam requests discussion with the developer about Carpenter labor standards and reports the union has not yet received a response from the developer.
  #### Lot consolidation rationale
  Committee member Diana Fazely asks staff to clarify the basis for the determination that consolidating the lot into a single zoning designation would not produce greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout. Brittany Whitehill explains the code gives the decision‑making body discretion and that staff concluded the proposed split‑zoning configuration supports a good site layout by locating parking at the rear and pedestrian/landscaping elements at the front. The committee accepts staff’s explanation as supporting the lot line adjustment.
- Diana Fazely asks staff to explain why a consolidated single‑zone parcel would not result in greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout.
- Brittany Whitehill explains the code allows the decision‑making body discretion on split‑zoning and that staff finds the proposed layout is compatible with surrounding uses.
- Brittany Whitehill states the design prioritizes parking to the back of the site and landscaping and pedestrian amenities toward the front, supporting the proposed configuration.
  #### Lot line adjustment approval
  After deliberation, Diana Fazely moves to approve the lot line adjustment and Diana Pancholi seconds the motion, with the subdivision committee passing the motion unanimously in the absence of Ed Arango. The committee records that the decision is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days. The lot line adjustment approval completes the subdivision committee’s action on that portion of the project.
- Diana Fazely moves to approve the lot line adjustment at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Ave.
- Diana Pancholi seconds the motion and records the motion as passed unanimously with committee member Arango absent.
- Diana Pancholi states the subdivision committee decision on the lot line adjustment is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.
  #### Development permit approval
  As zoning administrator, Diana Pancholi reviews the posted materials, concurs with staff findings, and moves to approve the development review permit with the posted findings and conditions of approval. The approval is also appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days, and the public hearing is closed. Attendees are directed to contact the listed project planner for follow‑up questions.
- Diana Pancholi states she has reviewed the materials and concurs the project is consistent with the Zoning Ordinance and the General Plan.
- Diana Pancholi moves to approve the development review permit with the posted findings and conditions of approval.
- Diana Pancholi states the zoning administrator’s decision is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days and directs follow‑up questions to the listed project planner.
