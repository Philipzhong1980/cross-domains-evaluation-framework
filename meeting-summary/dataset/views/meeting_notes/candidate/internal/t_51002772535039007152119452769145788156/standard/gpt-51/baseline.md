### Overview
The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee holds a public hearing to consider a project at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Avenue involving a three-story, 19,958-square-foot office building, associated surface parking, removal of three heritage trees, and a lot line adjustment to merge two parcels in the ML and MMM industrial districts. Planner Brittany Whitehill explains that the merged lot will span two zoning districts under section 36.06, that staff finds the split-zoned configuration yields a compatible site layout with parking at the rear and pedestrian amenities and landscaping at the front, and that the project complies with the Zoning Code, General Plan, Subdivision Map Act, and CEQA based on an initial study and negative declaration showing no significant impacts with standard conditions. During public comment, union representative Weng Tam of NorCal Carpenters Union Local 405 urges the use of local, area-standard union carpenters, citing benefits for workers, local businesses, traffic, emissions, and apprenticeships, and notes the union’s desire to engage the developer on labor standards. In deliberations, city attorney representative Diana Fazely seeks clarification on the “superior site layout” standard, after which the subdivision committee—Pancholi and Fazely—unanimously approves the lot line adjustment, and then Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi separately approves the development review permit with staff’s findings and conditions, with both approvals appealable to the City Council within 10 days.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Terra Bella office project description
The committee reviews a proposal for a new three-story, 19,958-square-foot office building with a surface parking lot at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Avenue on a 1.3-acre site. The project also includes a heritage tree removal permit for three heritage trees and a lot line adjustment to merge two parcels into one. This matters because it changes site configuration, tree coverage, and development intensity in an industrial area spanning two zoning districts. The current state is that staff has completed its analysis and is presenting the project for subdivision and zoning actions.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi states that item 5.1 is a request for a development review permit for a three-story, 19,958 square foot office building with an associated surface parking lot at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Avenue.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi notes that the request includes a heritage removal permit to remove three heritage trees and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots into one on a 1.3-acre project site.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill confirms she is the project planner and will present staff’s analysis for the Terra Bella project.

#### Split-zoned lot and zoning consistency
Staff explains that the merged parcel will span two zoning districts—ML (Limited Industrial) and MMM (General Industrial)—and outlines the zoning code provisions that allow a single lot to include multiple districts. This is important because the subdivision committee must be comfortable that maintaining split zoning still yields a compatible and functional site design. Staff concludes that the proposed design meets the requirements of both districts and that forcing a single zoning designation would not improve neighborhood compatibility or site layout. The project is also found consistent with the Mountain View Zoning Code, General Plan, and Subdivision Map Act.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill explains that the project site is located on the south side of Terra Bella Avenue between North Shoreline Boulevard and Linda Vista Avenue in the MMM General Industrial and ML Limited Industrial districts.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill states that the proposed lot line adjustment will result in one lot spanning the ML Limited Industrial and MMM General Industrial districts pursuant to section 36.06 of the zoning code.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill notes that the subdivision committee may allow a lot to span two or more zoning districts if a consolidated single-zoned lot would not result in greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill reports that staff finds the proposed office building and site improvements consistent with the respective requirements of the ML and MMM zoning districts.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill states that staff finds the project would not achieve a superior site layout or neighborhood compatibility if the lot contained only one zoning designation.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill confirms that staff finds the project complies with the Mountain View Zoning Code, the Mountain View General Plan, and the Subdivision Map Act.

#### Environmental review and CEQA determination
Staff summarizes the environmental review conducted under the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA). An initial study and negative declaration were prepared and circulated for public comment, and the project was determined to have no significant environmental impacts with standard conditions. This matters because it establishes legal compliance with CEQA and supports the city’s ability to approve the project. The current state is that the CEQA analysis is complete and supports conditional project approval.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill states that an initial study of environmental significance and negative declaration were prepared in accordance with CEQA guidelines for the project.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill explains that the CEQA document was circulated for a 30-day public comment period from September 14, 2022, to October 14, 2022.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill reports that the project was determined to have no significant impacts on the environment with incorporation of city standard conditions of approval.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill concludes that, based on staff’s analysis, findings, and conditions, staff recommends conditional approval of the project and the associated lot line adjustment.

#### Public comment on labor standards and local hiring
A representative of the NorCal Carpenters Union Local 405 provides public comment focusing on labor standards and local hiring for the project’s construction. He emphasizes the benefits of using local union carpenters, including area-standard wages, family-supporting jobs, reduced commute-related emissions, and apprenticeship opportunities. This is important because it injects workforce and labor considerations into an otherwise land-use-focused hearing, although these issues are not formally part of the zoning decision. The union notes they have contacted the developer without receiving a response and expresses a desire to open discussions on labor standards for the project.
- Union representative Weng Tam identifies himself as representing NorCal Carpenters Union Local 405 and over 7,000 carpenters in Santa Clara County.
- Union representative Weng Tam stresses that local hiring and area-standard labor language are important for construction workers to work closer to home, spend more time with family, and support local businesses.
- Union representative Weng Tam states that local hiring can reduce traffic, lower greenhouse gas emissions, and expand apprenticeship opportunities to build projects to high standards in Mountain View.
- Union representative Weng Tam emphasizes that healthcare, livable wages, and the high cost of living make strong labor standards critical for carpenters and their families.
- Union representative Weng Tam reports that the union has already contacted the developer but has not received a response and requests an open discussion with the developer about carpenter labor standards for the project.

#### Subdivision committee deliberation on lot line adjustment
The subdivision committee discusses the basis for allowing a single parcel to span two zoning districts and evaluates whether the proposed layout is appropriate. The city attorney representative seeks clarification on how the code’s “superior site layout” concept applies, and staff explains their rationale for supporting the split-zoned lot based on site design and compatibility. Following this clarification, the committee moves to approve the lot line adjustment. The current state is that the subdivision committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment, subject to appeal.
- Subdivision committee member and city attorney representative Diana Fazely asks staff to elaborate on the basis for determining that a consolidated single-zoned parcel would not result in greater neighborhood compatibility or a superior site layout.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill responds that there is no codified required finding but that the code authorizes approval of split zoning within a single lot when a single zoning designation would not result in a superior layout.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill explains that staff reviewed the project and finds the proposed site layout compatible with surrounding land uses and functionally sound.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill notes that the project places parking toward the back of the site and landscaping and pedestrian amenities toward the front, which aligns with city preferences for such developments.
- City attorney representative Diana Fazely states that she has reviewed the materials, has no further questions, and makes a motion to approve the lot line adjustment at 1155 and 1185 Terra Bella Avenue.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi seconds the motion and records unanimous approval of item 5.1 by the subdivision committee, noting that member Ed Arango is absent.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi states that the decision of approval for the lot line adjustment is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.

#### Development review permit and project approval
Following the subdivision committee action, the zoning administrator considers the development review permit and associated entitlements for the project. She affirms that she has reviewed the staff materials, agrees that the project is consistent with the zoning ordinance and general plan, and formally moves to approve the project with staff-recommended findings and conditions. This decision, like the lot line adjustment, is appealable to the City Council within 10 days. The hearing then closes with instructions for follow-up questions to be directed to the project planner.
- City attorney representative Diana Fazely asks whether there is also an action required for the development review permit beyond the lot line adjustment.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi states that she has reviewed the materials posted online, including the findings report, and concurs that the project is consistent with the Zoning Ordinance and General Plan.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi moves to approve the project with the posted findings and conditions of approval recommended by city staff.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi notes that the decision of approval for the development permit is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.
- Zoning Administrator Diana Pancholi closes the public hearing at 4:14 PM and advises that any follow-up questions on agenda items should be directed to the listed project planner.
