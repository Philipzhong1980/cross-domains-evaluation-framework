### Overview
The Education Enhancement Reserve Joint Powers Board convened to discuss key topics including the approval of previous meeting minutes, updates on school district activities funded by the reserve, and the funding schedule for the upcoming fiscal year. Board members shared insights on the financial allocations for their respective districts, emphasizing the importance of these funds for educational programs. Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg provided an update on the negotiations for a successor agreement to the JPA, which is set to expire in June 2023, highlighting the need for a phased approach to address school capacity amidst ongoing developments. Public comments raised concerns about the adequacy of funding in light of increasing student populations. The board agreed to schedule a follow-up meeting in early 2023 to discuss a short-term agreement and the process for negotiating a longer-term solution.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The board discusses the approval of the minutes from the December 16th, 2021 meeting. The minutes are approved unanimously, ensuring that the record of the previous meeting is officially recognized.
- Ayinde Rudolph motions to approve the minutes.
- Doctor Meyer seconds the motion for approval.
- The minutes are adopted with all in favor.

#### Current Status of School District Activities
Board members provide updates on the activities funded by the Education Enhancement Reserve in their respective school districts. Doctor Meyer reports on the Mountain View Los Altos Union High School District, while Doctor Rudolph discusses the Mountain View Whisman School District, highlighting the financial allocations and their impacts on educational programs.
- Doctor Meyer states the high school district received $3.2 million from the reserve.
- Doctor Rudolph mentions the Whisman district received over $5 million, with $3.6 million spent on various educational programs.
- Both districts express gratitude for the funding, emphasizing its importance for student services.

#### Funding Schedule for Fiscal Year 2022-2023
Jesse Takahashi presents the funding schedule for the upcoming fiscal year, detailing the payments to be made to the school districts based on property tax revenue changes. The board discusses the implications of these payments and the economic conditions affecting future funding.
- Jesse Takahashi reports a 6.82% increase in payments due to strong real estate market performance.
- Mountain View School District is set to receive approximately $5.3 million, while the high school district will receive about $3.4 million.
- Takahashi notes potential economic slowdowns that could impact future assessed values.

#### Negotiations for Successor Agreement
Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg provides an update on the negotiations for a successor agreement to the Education Enhancement Reserve Joint Powers Authority (JPA) agreement, which is set to expire in June 2023. The discussion includes the need for a phased approach to address school capacity and funding amidst ongoing development in the Shoreline area.
- Audrey Ramberg outlines the history of the JPA and the need for a new agreement to continue funding.
- The board discusses the importance of timely negotiations to avoid funding gaps for the school districts.
- Ramberg emphasizes the city's commitment to maintaining current funding levels during the transition.

#### Public Comments and Concerns
Public comments highlight concerns regarding the funding and its adequacy in light of increasing student populations due to new developments. Community members urge the board to prioritize school funding in negotiations with developers and the city.
- Laura Blakely advocates for immediate negotiations to secure school funding before new developments increase student numbers.
- Pat Showalter emphasizes the city's commitment to addressing sea level rise while balancing school funding needs.

#### Next Steps and Future Meetings
The board agrees to schedule a follow-up meeting in early 2023 to discuss the short-term agreement and the process for negotiating a longer-term solution. This meeting aims to ensure that school districts are not adversely affected by funding gaps during the budget planning cycle.
- The board agrees to meet in February or March to finalize a short-term agreement.
- Ayinde Rudolph stresses the importance of having a commitment in place before the budget cycle begins.
