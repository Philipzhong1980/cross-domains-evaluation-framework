### Overview
The Education Enhancement Reserve Joint Powers Board convened to review the status of funding and activities supported by the Education Enhancement Reserve for the Mountain View Los Altos Union High School District and Mountain View Whisman School District. Key presentations included detailed reports on fund usage by the districts, the fiscal year 2022-23 funding schedule reflecting a 6.82% increase due to strong real estate market conditions, and an update on negotiations for a successor JPA agreement set to expire in June 2023. Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg outlined a proposed phased approach to increase payments over a 20-year horizon, contingent on updating the Shoreline Area Plan to include school capacity needs. Board member Dr. Rudolph raised concerns about potential funding shortfalls related to new residential developments and the timing of payments, emphasizing the districts' need to avoid budget cuts. Public comments from Laura Blakely and Council Member Pat Showalter underscored the importance of prioritizing school funding alongside infrastructure challenges like sea level rise. The board and city staff agreed on the necessity of a short-term agreement to guarantee payments for the upcoming fiscal year, with a follow-up meeting scheduled for the first quarter of 2023 to finalize this and continue negotiations for a long-term solution. Throughout, participants expressed commitment to collaborative problem-solving to balance the Shoreline community's infrastructure obligations with the educational needs of a growing student population.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### School District Funding Usage
Board members Dr. Meyer and Dr. Rudolph presented on how the Education Enhancement Reserve funds are utilized by the Mountain View Los Altos Union High School District and Mountain View Whisman School District. The funds support various programs including career pathways, technology, and classroom resources, which are critical for educational quality and capacity. Both districts detailed their expenditures and the importance of these funds for ongoing and future educational needs.
- Dr. Meyer explained that the high school district uses $3.2 million from the reserve for career pathways, computer science expansion, and technology support.
- Dr. Rudolph reported that Mountain View Whisman received over $5 million and spent $3.6 million on programs like Living Classroom and technology services.
- Laura Blakely clarified that unspent funds are reserved for future textbook adoptions, which are costly and require multi-year saving.
#### Fiscal Year 22/23 Funding Schedule
Treasurer Jesse Takahashi presented the funding schedule for fiscal year 2022-23, highlighting a 6.82% increase in payments due to strong real estate market conditions. The total payments to the two districts amount to approximately $8.7 million. He also noted the cumulative payments since 2011 total nearly $82 million, reflecting the long-term financial commitment of the Shoreline Regional Park community to education.
- Jesse Takahashi stated that Mountain View School District will receive about $5.3 million and the high school district about $3.4 million for the upcoming year.
- He noted the 6.82% increase reflects ongoing strength in the real estate market.
- The cumulative payments since 2011 total $76.7 million, with additional payments before the current agreements bringing the total to nearly $82 million.
#### JPA Agreement Negotiation Status
Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg provided an update on the negotiations for a successor agreement to the Education Enhancement Reserve Joint Powers Authority (JPA) agreement, which expires in June 2023. The city and districts have been engaged in discussions since 2019, focusing on balancing the Shoreline community's obligations with the districts' funding needs. A proposed phased approach aims to increase payments over time while addressing infrastructure and development plans, with an extension of 12 to 18 months suggested to finalize the agreement.
- Audrey Ramberg explained the Shoreline Regional Park Community's creation and its tax increment funding structure.
- She described ongoing negotiations to update the area plan to include school capacity funding and to extend payments to districts.
- A 12 to 18 month extension is proposed to allow completion of legal and fiscal analyses and to negotiate a successor agreement.
#### Concerns on Funding Adequacy and Timing
Board member Dr. Rudolph raised concerns about the adequacy of current funding relative to the operational costs of educating students from new developments, highlighting a potential $2.8 million shortfall. Questions were also raised about the county's share of tax increment funds and the implications if no successor agreement is reached. The city staff acknowledged these concerns and emphasized their intention to continue payments and work collaboratively to avoid budget cuts in the districts.
- Dr. Rudolph noted that the projected student population increase will require additional funding not currently covered by tax increment payments.
- He questioned the county's receipt of its full 20% share of tax increment funds and requested documentation for review.
- Audrey Ramberg affirmed the city's commitment to continue payments at least at current levels and to work with districts to address timing and funding needs.
#### Public Comments on Funding and Infrastructure
Public speakers, including Laura Blakely and Council Member Pat Showalter, emphasized the importance of directing Shoreline tax revenues to schools to support the anticipated 50% increase in student population. They highlighted the need for timely funding to build reserves and infrastructure, urged negotiations to proceed promptly, and acknowledged the challenges of funding regional issues like sea level rise. The city reaffirmed its commitment to negotiate in good faith and balance community needs.
- Laura Blakely urged prioritizing school funding and criticized delays in negotiations, emphasizing Google's capacity to contribute.
- Council Member Showalter stressed the necessity of funding sea level rise protections as a local responsibility and the importance of reasonable negotiations.
- Both speakers highlighted the interconnectedness of infrastructure needs and school funding in the Shoreline community.
#### Negotiation Process and Timeline
Board members and city staff discussed the negotiation timeline and process for the successor JPA agreement. There was consensus on the need for a short-term agreement to guarantee payments for the upcoming fiscal year to avoid budget cuts, alongside ongoing work toward a longer-term agreement. A follow-up meeting was planned for the first quarter of 2023 to address the short-term agreement and set the path forward, with commitments to maintain open communication and collaboration.
- Dr. Rudolph emphasized the urgency of securing funding commitments before the districts' budget cycles in February and May.
- Audrey Ramberg and Chair McCarthy committed to scheduling meetings as needed to finalize agreements and avoid payment gaps.
- The board agreed to meet in the first quarter of 2023 to discuss the short-term agreement and continue negotiations for a long-term solution.
#### Board and Staff Closing Remarks
Board members expressed appreciation for the collaborative efforts among the city and school districts despite differing perspectives and challenges. They acknowledged the complexity of balancing infrastructure obligations with educational funding needs and reaffirmed their commitment to partnership and continued dialogue. The meeting concluded with mutual thanks and holiday wishes.
- Dr. Rudolph thanked all parties for their willingness to engage in complex negotiations and acknowledged the shared community responsibilities.
- Chair McCarthy highlighted examples of successful collaboration and expressed optimism about the agreed negotiation path forward.
- The meeting was adjourned with commitments to maintain communication and schedule future meetings as necessary.
