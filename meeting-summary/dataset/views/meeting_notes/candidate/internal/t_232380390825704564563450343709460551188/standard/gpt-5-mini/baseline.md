### Overview
  The Education Enhancement Reserve Joint Powers Board meets to review district uses of Shoreline funds, the FY22/23 payment schedule, and the status of successor JPA negotiations ahead of the current agreement’s June 30, 2023 expiration. Dr. Nellie Meyer and Dr. Ayinde Rudolph summarize how their districts use EER funds for career pathways, computer science, technology, Chromebooks, Living Classroom science, and textbook reserves, and Treasurer Jesse Takahashi reports payments rose about 6.82% producing roughly $5.3 million to Mountain View Whisman and $3.4 million to the high school district for a total near $8.7 million. Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg outlines a proposed 12–18 month extension and a phased, roughly 20-year approach tied to updating the Shoreline Area Plan so Shoreline tax increment can support school capacity, and she affirms the city’s commitment to continue at least the current payment level during the short-term extension. District leaders press for clarity on timing and funding for incoming students—citing a potential $2.8 million shortfall—and request documentation about the county share; the board agrees to pursue a short-term written guarantee to avoid triggering district budget cuts, to keep staff-level negotiations active, and to reconvene in quarter one to finalize the short-term agreement and map the longer-term negotiation process.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Approval of minutes
  The board reviews and adopts the minutes from the December 16, 2021 meeting without corrections. A motion to approve is made and seconded, and the board votes to adopt the minutes. This completes the procedural opening business and establishes the meeting record.
- Board member Ayinde Rudolph moves to approve the December 16, 2021 minutes.
- Chair Kimba McCarthy confirms Doctor Meyer seconds the motion and the board votes to adopt the minutes.
#### District program funding
  Board members present how Education Enhancement Reserve (EER) funds are used by the two districts. Dr. Meyer reports the high school district uses roughly $3.2 million to support a communications and arts career pathway, computer science and engineering expansion, and technology equipment and staff. Dr. Rudolph reports Mountain View Whisman receives a little over $5 million in base and bonus funds, spent $3.6 million this year, and uses the remainder primarily for Chromebooks, software licensing, technology staff, Living Classroom science programs, and reserved textbook adoption funds.
- Dr. Nellie Meyer reports the high school district receives approximately $3.2 million from the reserve and uses funds to cover Freestyle Academy teacher salaries and benefits, computer science and engineering teacher salaries, and technology equipment and support staff.
- Dr. Ayinde Rudolph reports Mountain View Whisman receives a little over $5 million in base and bonus funds and spent about $3.6 million this year on technology, software licensing, Living Classroom science programming, and administrative support.
- Laura Blakely clarifies that the district reserves a portion of funds for future textbook adoptions that can total $2–3 million per adoption cycle.
#### FY22/23 funding schedule
  The Treasurer, Jesse Takahashi, presents the annual payment calculation under the JPA, noting property tax revenue growth led to increased payments for the upcoming fiscal year. He reports payments rise 6.82% this past year, producing about $5.3 million to the Mountain View Whisman district and about $3.4 million to the high school district, totaling roughly $8.7 million for the year. He also provides a historical cumulative payments summary and cautions that future assessed value growth could be affected by economic conditions and appeals.
- Treasurer Jesse Takahashi reports annual JPA payments increased by 6.82% and will provide about $5.3 million to Mountain View Whisman and about $3.4 million to the high school district for a total of roughly $8.7 million.
- Mr. Takahashi notes the Shoreline community has paid approximately $76.7 million since 2011 and nearly $82 million including earlier payouts.
- Mr. Takahashi cautions that recent economic slowdowns and layoffs could affect future assessed values and that appeals may spread impacts over several years.
#### JPA negotiations update
  Assistant City Manager Audrey Ramberg outlines the negotiation history and a proposed approach for a successor JPA because the current agreement expires June 30, 2023. City staff propose a 12–18 month extension that preserves at least current payments while the city updates the Shoreline Area Plan and completes legal and fiscal analysis tied to the forthcoming Google Master Plan; the update is necessary to authorize broader use of Shoreline tax increment for school capacity. Staff and district representatives discuss timing, legal constraints, phasing over a roughly 20-year development horizon, and the need to align payments with development timing and district budget cycles.
- Audrey Ramberg explains the current JPA expires June 30, 2023 and the city proposes a 12–18 month extension to preserve continuity while completing legal and fiscal analyses.
- Audrey Ramberg states the city commits to provide payments at least equal to current payments to the districts during any short-term extension.
- Audrey Ramberg explains legal analysis shows the Shoreline Area Plan must be updated to enable expanded payments for school capacity and that staff proposes to undertake that update.
- Audrey Ramberg describes a phased, roughly 20-year transition in which the Shoreline community moves from development and infrastructure build-out to a steady state where most increment flows to taxing entities, including schools.
- Dr. Ayinde Rudolph raises that Mountain View Whisman faces a potential $2.8 million operational shortfall tied to incoming students from Shoreline buildout and asks how that will be addressed.
- Jesse Takahashi clarifies the county is not receiving its full allocation and is receiving closer to about 40% of what it would otherwise get, and agrees to provide backup documentation.
- Audrey Ramberg reports Sobrato housing occupancy is expected to begin mid-2024 and says city staff will work to understand timing of student enrollment impacts.
- Board members express that districts need clarity early in the budget cycle and note potential February–March budget actions if there is uncertainty about payments.
#### Public comments on funding
  Two public commenters address the negotiation timing and priorities. Laura Blakely urges immediate allocation of Shoreline funds to schools, asks the board to negotiate before finalizing any large development agreements with Google, and calls for Google to contribute to infrastructure rather than divert Shoreline tax increments. Council Member Pat Showalter supports negotiating an extension and emphasizes the city's obligation to fund sea level rise and infrastructure to enable development and long-term school funding.
- Public commenter Laura Blakely urges the board to allocate Shoreline tax revenues to the school districts now and to press Google to help fund necessary infrastructure rather than diverting school funds.
- Laura Blakely states school districts face a projected 50% student population increase and need funds now to build reserves and capacity.
- Council Member Pat Showalter states the city commits to negotiate in good faith, emphasizes the local need to fund sea level rise protections, and supports a negotiated extension so infrastructure and community benefits can proceed.
#### Next steps and scheduling
  Board members and staff agree to pursue a near-term short agreement that guarantees at least the current payments to avoid triggering district budget cuts while staff continues negotiating a longer-term successor agreement and an update to the Shoreline Area Plan. They agree to schedule a follow-up meeting in quarter one (February–early March) to finalize the short-term guarantee and to continue staff-level discussions on process and timeline for the long-term agreement. The city and board representatives commit to collaborative, ongoing meetings and to provide documentation (e.g., county agreement backup) as requested by the districts.
- Chair Kimba McCarthy and Audrey Ramberg commit to work with district staff to execute a short-term agreement that guarantees at least the payment amounts received in the most recent year.
- Dr. Ayinde Rudolph requests a short-term written guarantee to prevent district budget cuts and asks that staff present a process and timeline for negotiating the long-term successor agreement.
- All parties agree to schedule a follow-up board meeting in quarter one (February–early March) to address the short-term guarantee and coordinate next steps.
- Treasurer Jesse Takahashi agrees to provide backup documentation regarding the county share and historical agreements when requested.
