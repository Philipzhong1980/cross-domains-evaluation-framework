### Overview
  This briefing covers a mix of domestic and foreign-policy items led by Press Secretary Karine Jean‑Pierre and NSC Coordinator Admiral John Kirby. Jean‑Pierre announced Invest.gov to map the President’s Investing in America agenda and highlighted that the bipartisan budget preserves those investments, while also announcing an initial $115 million EPA deployment to repair Jackson, Mississippi’s water system and situating that aid within broader Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and American Rescue Plan funding. She defended the Inflation Reduction Act’s Medicare drug-price negotiation provisions in light of a pharmaceutical lawsuit and expressed confidence in the administration’s legal position while deferring litigation details to DOJ. Admiral Kirby addressed the catastrophic Nova Kakhovka dam destruction in Ukraine, describing significant flooding, displacement, and early uncertainty about responsibility, and said the U.S. is coordinating humanitarian assistance and continuing military, economic, and diplomatic support to Ukraine. Reporters pressed on related topics — including potential battlefield impacts, supplemental Ukraine funding, Nord Stream leak reporting, Iran missile activity, NORAD intercept timing, F-16 capabilities, China‑U.S. diplomacy, and other regional questions — and Kirby generally deferred operational or intelligence specifics and emphasized ongoing assessments, diplomacy, and the administration’s intent to avoid escalation. The briefing also included questions about domestic matters such as border litigation and city support for migrants, antitrust and Saudi-linked sports business deals (on which the White House declines to comment), UFO-related inquiries referred to DOD, and whether the President’s recent stumble prompted procedural reviews (Jean‑Pierre said no review had been ordered and stressed the President’s record of delivery).
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Invest.gov launch
  Press Secretary Karine Jean‑Pierre announces the launch of Invest.gov, a new interactive site tracking investments and infrastructure projects tied to the President’s Investing in America agenda. The site highlights private-sector investments and state-by-state data to demonstrate economic impacts and job creation. The administration says the bipartisan budget agreement preserves these investments and will support an upcoming Investing in America tour during the congressional recess.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE announced the launch of Invest.gov and described it as an interactive map showing the President’s Investing in America agenda.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Invest.gov displays about $470 billion in private-sector investments mobilized by the administration’s agenda.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the site also shows 32,000 infrastructure projects announced or underway because of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the bipartisan budget agreement protects these investments and that administration officials will fan out for a second Investing in America tour around July 4th.
  #### Jackson water investment
  Jean‑Pierre announces an initial EPA deployment of $115 million to stabilize and rebuild Jackson, Mississippi’s water system after prolonged infrastructure failures. She frames the investment as part of the President’s commitment to safe drinking water and as an example of how federal infrastructure laws deliver targeted support. The administration links this effort to broader funding already secured for Jackson.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE announced that the EPA is deploying $115 million to help repair Jackson, Mississippi’s water system.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the investment is an initial step to stabilize and rebuild the system after the city experienced prior water crises and boil-water orders.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said this action is part of the President’s commitment that every American have access to safe, clean drinking water.
  #### Medicare negotiation lawsuit
  Jean‑Pierre addresses a lawsuit by pharmaceutical companies challenging Medicare’s new authority to negotiate drug prices under the Inflation Reduction Act. The administration defends the law’s provisions — including insulin caps and inflation rebates — and expresses confidence in prevailing in court while deferring legal specifics to DOJ. She emphasizes the policy goal of lowering costs for seniors and families and indicates the administration will continue work to reduce healthcare costs regardless of litigation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the Inflation Reduction Act allows Medicare to negotiate lower prescription drug prices and cited insulin caps and rebate provisions.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Big Pharma is challenging that authority in court and that the administration intends to fight those legal attempts.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration is confident the law is constitutional but declined to comment on legal strategy and referred legal questions to the Department of Justice.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said lowering healthcare and prescription costs remains a top priority of the President’s economic agenda.
  #### Nova Kakhovka dam
  NSC Coordinator Admiral John Kirby briefs on the destruction of the Nova Kakhovka dam in Ukraine, noting massive flooding, evacuations, and early but unquantified casualties while stressing the U.S. cannot yet conclude responsibility. He explains the dam’s scale and significance to southern Ukraine, Crimea, and the Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant, and says the IAEA reports no immediate nuclear safety risk. The administration is coordinating with Ukrainian authorities and humanitarian partners and will continue to monitor, provide assistance, and share information as it becomes available.
- MR. KIRBY said the destruction of the Nova Kakhovka dam has triggered massive flooding and at least thousands of evacuations with likely casualties that cannot yet be fully quantified.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. is closely monitoring the incident, working with Ukrainian authorities, and cannot yet say conclusively who is responsible.
- MR. KIRBY described the dam’s size and role in supplying southern Ukraine, Crimea, and the Zaporizhzhia Nuclear Power Plant, and noted the IAEA reported no immediate risk to that plant.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration will coordinate humanitarian assistance and continue military, humanitarian, and economic support to Ukraine as needed.
- MR. KIRBY said the deliberate destruction of civilian infrastructure, including dams, can violate the laws of war but clarified that the U.S. has not made a legal determination about this event.
  #### Counteroffensive impact uncertainty
  Reporters ask whether the dam’s destruction affects Ukrainian counteroffensive operations and fundraising timelines; Admiral Kirby repeatedly says it is too soon to assess battlefield effects. He emphasizes the immediate humanitarian focus and says the U.S. will review impacts as more information becomes available. Kirby reiterates that decisions about additional assistance or timing would be made when properly assessed.
- MR. KIRBY said it is too soon to know what impact the dam’s destruction will have on Ukrainian counteroffensive operations.
- MR. KIRBY said the immediate priority is humanitarian assistance for Ukrainians displaced by the flooding.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration is assessing damage and will determine at the appropriate time whether additional U.S. funding is needed for Ukraine.
  #### Nord Stream Discord reporting
  Reporters ask about leaked Discord messages and a Washington Post report suggesting Ukrainian involvement in Nord Stream sabotage; Kirby declines to discuss intelligence from the podium and notes the Post said those reports were not corroborated by U.S. agencies. He points to ongoing investigations led by Germany, Sweden, and Denmark and says the U.S. will not get ahead of those inquiries. Kirby reiterates U.S. policy of not supporting strikes inside Russia and says investigations remain ongoing.
- MR. KIRBY declined to discuss intelligence matters from the podium and noted the Washington Post reported the allegation was not corroborated by U.S. intelligence agencies.
- MR. KIRBY said Germany, Sweden, and Denmark are conducting independent investigations into the Nord Stream sabotage and that those inquiries are ongoing.
- MR. KIRBY reiterated that the U.S. does not support attacks inside Russia and will not enable Ukraine to conduct strikes into Russian territory.
  #### Iran missile activity
  Reporters ask about an Iranian hypersonic ballistic missile test and criticism from Senator Risch; Kirby says the administration has been clear and firm about pushing back on Iran’s destabilizing activities and has used sanctions and other measures against Iran’s improving missile program. He declines to comment on specific reports of the alleged hypersonic missile and rejects the characterization that the administration has been 'flat-footed.'
- MR. KIRBY said the Biden administration has been clear, concise, and firm in pushing back on Iran’s destabilizing activities, including its ballistic missile program.
- MR. KIRBY said he would not comment from the podium on the specific reports of an alleged hypersonic missile.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration has laid down sanctions and other measures to counter Iran’s activities in the region.
  #### Ukraine funding timeline
  In response to questions about whether the dam incident accelerates a need for supplemental Ukraine funding, Kirby says the U.S. has sufficient funds to support Ukraine through the rest of the fiscal year and that evaluations are ongoing. He expresses gratitude for bipartisan congressional support and says the administration will go back to Congress if needed at an appropriate time, but it is not the moment to do so. Kirby emphasizes focus on executing current funding to ensure Ukraine can succeed on the battlefield.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration currently has enough funds to support Ukraine throughout the rest of this fiscal year.
- MR. KIRBY said it would be imprudent to speculate now about whether the dam explosion will require additional supplemental funding from Congress.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration is grateful for bipartisan, bicameral support on Ukraine and will seek more assistance at the appropriate time if necessary.
  #### NORAD intercept review
  A reporter questions the timeliness of an intercept over the weekend; Kirby declines to adjudicate the operational details from the podium and refers procedural review to the Department of Defense. He says DOD responded in a 'textbook fashion' but will take a look and determine if procedures need adjustment. Kirby avoids specifics about timing and tactical choices and refers follow-ups to DOD.
- MR. KIRBY said he would not litigate the NORAD intercept over the weekend from the podium and referred operational questions to the Department of Defense.
- MR. KIRBY said DOD responded in a textbook fashion and will review whether procedures should be changed.
- MR. KIRBY said he could not speak to specific timing decisions about the intercept and again deferred to DOD.
  #### Ukraine strikes and consequences
  Reporters press whether the U.S. has imposed or would impose consequences if Ukraine strikes inside Russia; Kirby says the administration has made clear privately and publicly that it does not support attacks inside Russia and has received assurances from Ukrainian officials. He declines to detail private discussions and will not discuss conditionality publicly, while signaling continued security assistance for Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. does not support strikes inside Russia and has expressed those concerns to Ukrainian officials both privately and publicly.
- MR. KIRBY said Ukrainian officials have assured the U.S. they will not use U.S.-provided equipment to strike inside Russia.
- MR. KIRBY said he would not detail any private discussions or potential conditionality regarding U.S. assistance.
  #### F-16 capability claims
  Russian Foreign Minister Lavrov’s suggestion that U.S.-built F-16s can accommodate nuclear weapons prompts a response from Kirby, who stresses the jets are intended to help Ukraine defend its territory and that the U.S. will not escalate to the nuclear realm. Kirby declines to discuss the nuclear capabilities of any platform and contrasts U.S. restraint with what he characterizes as reckless nuclear rhetoric from Russian officials.
- MR. KIRBY said the F-16s are being provided to meet Ukraine’s long-term defense needs and to help defend its airspace.
- MR. KIRBY declined to discuss the nuclear capabilities of any platform from the podium.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. does not want to see escalation into the nuclear realm and criticized Russian officials for nuclear rhetoric.
  #### China engagement and diplomacy
  Reporters question how the administration will reduce tensions with China in the Indo-Pacific; Kirby emphasizes diplomacy, open lines of communication, and recent U.S. visits to Beijing aimed at restoring transparency and military-to-military contacts. He notes some contacts were rebuffed and defends active engagement as the path to lower tensions, citing the President’s intent to rebuild relations in the spirit of Bali.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration is prioritizing diplomacy and maintaining lines of communication with China to reduce tensions in the Indo-Pacific.
- MR. KIRBY said recent U.S. visits to Beijing were intended to keep communication channels open and discuss potential future high-level visits.
- MR. KIRBY said efforts at military-to-military contact have at times been rebuffed but insisted diplomacy is the correct approach.
  #### Regional and global Q&A
  Reporters raise a range of other foreign-policy questions—Kosovo elections, a potential presidential trip to Africa, and the role of State Department and other agencies; Kirby either refers queries to State or says he has no schedule to announce. He reiterates support for free and fair elections and the President’s intent to visit Africa while providing no detailed timeline.
- MR. KIRBY referred questions about Kosovo to the State Department and said the U.S. supports free and fair elections.
- MR. KIRBY said the President still intends to visit Africa but that he had no trip schedule to announce.
- MR. KIRBY said he would take back questions about regional tensions and provide follow-ups as appropriate.
  #### Sports merger and Saudi ties
  Reporters ask about a merger between U.S. golf interests and the Saudi Public Investment Fund; both Kirby and Jean‑Pierre decline to comment on the transaction from the podium. Jean‑Pierre reiterates that the administration raises human-rights concerns in bilateral meetings but has no public comment on the merger itself.
- MR. KIRBY said he had no comment on the announced golf merger and deferred to relevant parties.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the White House would not comment on the merger from the briefing room but reiterated the President raises human-rights issues with foreign leaders.
  #### Merck suit follow-ups
  Reporters press Jean‑Pierre on legal arguments Merck raises (Takings Clause, First Amendment) and whether other firms might follow suit; she reiterates confidence in the law, declines to litigate legal arguments from the podium, and refers specifics to DOJ. The administration says it will continue pursuing policies to lower drug costs and will not be deterred by litigation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she would not comment on specific legal arguments and referred such matters to the Department of Justice.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration is confident in the law and will continue efforts to lower drug costs for American families.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she could not predict whether other drug companies would file similar lawsuits.
  #### Border litigation and city support
  Jean‑Pierre declines to comment on potential appeals in an ongoing 11th Circuit ruling and says the administration is engaged with interior cities handling migrant arrivals. She notes more than $200 million in federal funding to interior cities, highlights a reported decline in legal migration since Title 42 was lifted, and attributes that decrease to the administration’s combined strategy of diplomacy, deterrence, and expanded legal pathways.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she would not comment on litigation strategy regarding the 11th Circuit ruling because it is ongoing litigation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration has provided more than $200 million to interior cities dealing with migrants and has been in touch with local officials.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said legal migration has declined since Title 42 was lifted and attributed that trend to the President’s border plan emphasizing diplomacy, deterrence, and expanded legal pathways.
  #### UFOs and whistleblower
  A reporter asks about a whistleblower allegation that the military has retrieved non-human craft; Jean‑Pierre refers that question to the Department of Defense. The briefing does not provide further comment or detail on the claim.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE referred questions about alleged retrieval of non-human craft to the Department of Defense.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE did not provide further comment from the briefing room on UFO-related allegations.
  #### Jackson/Flint infrastructure context
  Following questions about Jackson, Jean‑Pierre places the $115 million announcement in the context of broader infrastructure funding, noting funding streams from the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the American Rescue Plan and referencing additional funds secured for Jackson. She refers details on Flint and program implementation to the EPA. The administration frames these laws as delivering decade-scale infrastructure improvements across communities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the $115 million for Jackson builds on over $600 million Congress secured for the city and additional tens of millions already deployed.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the American Rescue Plan are the mechanisms through which the administration will help communities like Jackson and Flint.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE referred specific program questions about Flint to the EPA for details on implementation.
  #### Antitrust/regulatory questions
  Reporters ask whether the announced sports merger would attract FTC or DOJ antitrust scrutiny; Jean‑Pierre declines to comment from the briefing room and does not address regulatory oversight. The briefing contains no further details on potential federal review.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she would not comment on whether the golf merger would be subject to FTC or DOJ review.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declined to discuss regulatory scrutiny of private commercial transactions from the podium.
  #### President’s stumble and procedures
  A reporter questions whether the President’s recent trips and a visible stumble prompted a review of advance procedures for his appearances; Jean‑Pierre says she was not aware of the specific incident cited, says no change in advance staffing has been ordered, and emphasizes the President’s policy accomplishments. She notes that similar incidents have occurred with prior presidents and frames the broader focus on governance and delivery.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she was not aware of the specific stumbling incident the reporter described and therefore could not speak to that moment.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said no review of advance staffing had been ordered from her standpoint and characterized accidental trips as matters that have happened with other presidents.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasized the President’s record of delivering legislative and policy results as the primary measure of his performance.
