### Overview
The Human Relations Commission convened to welcome new members Jocelyn Baird, Molly Smith, and Eric Poicon, fostering introductions and sharing advice to build a collaborative foundation. The commission approved prior meeting minutes and received a mid-year update on their 2022-2023 work plan, highlighting key projects including a housing and security storytelling event, bystander intervention training, and a mental health civility roundtable. Subcommittee updates detailed ongoing planning efforts, partnership development, and the need for additional members, with new commissioners actively assigned to subcommittees based on their interests. Leadership elections resulted in Vice Chair Ida Rose Sylvester being unanimously elected Chair and Commissioner Nirvana Nwokido as Vice Chair, ensuring continuity and effective meeting facilitation. Staff provided important reminders about compliance training, disclosure filings, and upcoming in-person meetings. The commission expressed enthusiasm for the year ahead, emphasizing community impact and engagement opportunities.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Commissioner Introductions and Advice
The meeting began with introductions of both continuing and new Human Relations Commission (HRC) members. Continuing members shared their tenure, what they appreciate about the HRC, and advice for new commissioners. New members introduced themselves, their backgrounds, and their aspirations for their first year on the commission. This segment was important for building rapport and setting expectations for collaboration.
- Vice Chair Ida Rose Sylvester welcomed new members Jocelyn Baird, Molly Smith, and Eric Poicon and highlighted the opportunity for growth within the HRC.
- Commissioner Xavier Webb emphasized the impact of the commission's work and advised new members to be patient and proactive.
- Commissioner Nirvana Nwokido encouraged communication and preparation for effective meetings.
- New member Eric Poicon expressed enthusiasm for community involvement and representing diverse groups.
- New members Molly Smith and Jocelyn Baird shared their eagerness to learn and contribute to the community.
#### Approval of Previous Meeting Minutes
The commission reviewed and approved the minutes from the December 1st, 2022 meeting without corrections. This procedural step ensures accurate record-keeping and continuity of commission activities.
- Commissioner Nirvana Nwokido approved the minutes without corrections.
- Commissioner Xavier Webb seconded the approval.
- A roll call vote was conducted with unanimous approval from all present commissioners.
#### Mid-Year Work Plan Review
Principal Management Analyst Laurel James provided an update on the HRC's fiscal year 2022-2023 work plan, highlighting ongoing and upcoming projects. The review included discussion of subcommittee assignments to engage new commissioners and optimize participation. This review is critical for tracking progress and planning future activities.
- Laurel James noted ongoing participation in the annual Spring Family Parade and Council Neighborhoods Committee meetings.
- Three key projects for the fiscal year were highlighted: a storytelling project on housing and homelessness, bystander intervention training, and a civility roundtable on mental health.
- Subcommittee memberships were discussed, with some vacancies noted due to recent resignations.
- Events related to these projects are tentatively scheduled for May and June 2023.
#### Housing and Security Subcommittee Update
Vice Chair Sylvester and Commissioner Webb provided an update on the Housing and Security Subcommittee's efforts to organize a storytelling event addressing housing security and homelessness. The event aims to foster community dialogue and understanding, with innovative approaches to inclusivity. This subcommittee is actively seeking additional members to support its work.
- The subcommittee plans a public forum in May 2023 at the Mountain View Community Center.
- A unique approach involves mixing housed and unhoused participants anonymously in roundtable discussions.
- The subcommittee has two current members and one opening for new participants.
- Partnerships with local organizations like Reach Potential Movement are underway to ensure respectful and effective engagement.
#### Mental Health Subcommittee Update
Commissioner Nwokido and Vice Chair Sylvester updated the commission on the Mental Health Subcommittee's early-stage planning for a community event aimed at destigmatizing mental health issues. The subcommittee is exploring formats, partnerships, and resource sharing to create a safe space for dialogue. They are also seeking new members to join their efforts.
- The subcommittee has held two meetings and is defining the scope and format of the event.
- Goals include providing resources, expert speakers, and personal testimonies to support mental health awareness.
- The event is tentatively scheduled for late June 2023.
- There is one opening on the subcommittee for new members.
- Commissioners discussed the broad nature of mental health and the need to focus on accessible, day-to-day coping strategies.
#### Bystander Intervention Training Subcommittee
Commissioner Webb and staff provided information about the bystander intervention training initiative, which is a carryover from the previous fiscal year. The subcommittee is reviewing existing curricula and plans to adapt proven training methods for the Mountain View community. There are two openings on this subcommittee for interested commissioners.
- The subcommittee intends to leverage existing training programs rather than creating new curricula from scratch.
- Commissioner Webb emphasized the value of this training for all community members.
- Notes and ideas from previous commissioners are being reviewed to inform current planning.
- The subcommittee currently has one member and two openings.
#### Subcommittee Assignments and Participation
The commission conducted a straw poll to assign new commissioners to subcommittees based on their interests and availability. Discussions addressed scheduling conflicts and the flexibility of participation. The assignments aim to balance workloads and maximize engagement across the commission's projects.
- Molly Smith expressed interest in the Housing and Security Subcommittee but noted a scheduling conflict with the May event.
- New commissioners showed strong interest in multiple subcommittees, leading to some negotiation to fill openings.
- Final assignments included: Bystander Intervention Training (Webb, Baird, Poicon), Mental Health (Sylvester, Nwokido, Smith), and Housing and Security (Sylvester, Webb, Poicon).
- Commissioners are welcome to assist with events and attend subcommittee meetings as non-members, respecting quorum rules.
#### Election of Commission Chair and Vice Chair
The commission held elections for the Chair and Vice Chair positions for 2023. Vice Chair Sylvester was nominated and unanimously elected as Chair, and Commissioner Nwokido was nominated and unanimously elected as Vice Chair. These leadership roles are essential for agenda setting and meeting facilitation.
- Commissioner Xavier Webb nominated Vice Chair Sylvester for Chair, praising her leadership.
- Vice Chair Sylvester accepted the nomination and was elected unanimously by roll call vote.
- Commissioner Webb nominated Commissioner Nwokido for Vice Chair, who accepted and was also elected unanimously.
- Laurel James outlined the responsibilities and time commitments associated with these roles.
#### Staff and Commission Announcements
Staff and commissioners shared updates and reminders, including upcoming training requirements, meeting logistics, and community events. This segment supports ongoing communication and operational readiness within the commission.
- Laurel James reminded commissioners about the mandatory Brown Act refresher training in mid-January.
- New commissioners were informed about filing Form 700 disclosures via the Netfile platform.
- The next HRC meeting will be in person on February 2, 2023, at City Hall's Plaza conference room.
- Commissioners discussed the upcoming Spring Family Parade and their traditional participation by marching rather than tabling.
- Commissioner Webb highlighted opportunities for commissioners to attend external webinars and conferences related to HRC work.
