### Overview
  At this HRC meeting staff announces an immediate commissioner resignation, conducts roll call, and welcomes three new commissioners—Eric Poicon, Molly Smith, and Jocelyn Baird—who introduce their backgrounds and priorities. Staff provides a mid-year FY22-23 work plan update (Laurel James), highlighting the Spring Family Parade, a Housing & Security storytelling event scheduled for May 25, in-progress bystander intervention training, and a civility roundtable on mental health tentatively planned for late June; several subcommittee vacancies exist. Vice Chair Ida Rose Sylvester and Commissioner Nirvana Nwokido lead detailed subcommittee reports: Housing & Security will pilot mixed roundtable discussions and testimony opportunities and currently has two HRC members and one opening; the Mental Health subcommittee aims to destigmatize mental health via panels, testimonials, and resource sharing and seeks new members; bystander intervention training will leverage existing curricula and also needs volunteers. The Commission assigns members to subcommittees (Bystander Intervention: Webb, Baird, Poicon; Mental Health: Sylvester, Nwokido, Smith; Housing & Security: Sylvester, Webb, Poicon), clarifies that non-members may help at public events but cannot attend subcommittee meetings in a way that creates a quorum, and confirms administrative items including Brown Act refresher training, Form 700 submission via Netfile, and the next in-person meeting on February 2, 2023. The body elects Ida Rose Sylvester as Chair and Nirvana Nwokido as Vice Chair by unanimous roll call votes and adjourns with plans to advance event planning and outreach in the coming weeks.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Resignation and Roll Call
  Staff notifies the Commission of an immediate resignation and conducts roll call to confirm attendance for the meeting. This matters because the resignation creates vacancies that affect subcommittee composition and voting. The Commission records the present members to proceed with business.
- Laurel James: Announces that Commissioner Raigstraw is resigning from the HRC effective immediately.
- Laurel James: Conducts roll call and confirms attendance of Commissioners Jocelyn Baird, Nirvana Nwokido, Eric Poicon, Molly Smith, Xavier Webb, and Vice Chair Ida Rose Sylvester.
#### Returning Commissioners Introductions
  Vice Chair Sylvester invites returning commissioners to introduce themselves, share tenure, and give one piece of advice to new members. The discussion highlights the Commission's community impact and sets expectations for collaboration. Commissioners emphasize patience, preparation, and active participation.
- Xavier Webb: States he is in his first full term (about a year) and advises new commissioners to be patient, ask questions, learn, and contribute their own views.
- Nirvana Nwokido: Describes this as her second year on the HRC and advises commissioners to communicate and come prepared to keep meetings running smoothly.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: Notes she has served about 4.5 years, highlights the Commission's role in building community dialogue, and urges members to bring heart, curiosity, and their best effort.
#### New Commissioners Introductions
  Three new commissioners introduce their backgrounds and motivations for joining the HRC and state what they hope to accomplish in their first year. The introductions provide context on skills and community ties the new members bring. Each new commissioner expresses eagerness to engage and connect residents to resources.
- Eric Poicon: Introduces himself as a County Library Community Outreach Specialist, Vice President of Silicon Valley Young Dems, a local candidate, and says he wants to help the unhoused, elderly, and create community dialogue.
- Molly Smith: Says she is new to the community and wants to build strong bearings, learn, and help connect people to community resources.
- Jocelyn Baird: Describes being born and raised in Mountain View, working as a waste service manager, attending HRC events, and wanting to contribute through event work and community engagement.

#### Mid-year Work Plan Update
  Principal Management Analyst Laurel James provides an oral mid-year update on the FY 2022-23 HRC work plan, discusses ongoing items and three priority projects approved by Council, and outlines subcommittee assignment plans. Highlighted projects include the Spring Family Parade, the Housing & Security storytelling event (scheduled May 25), bystander intervention training (in progress), and a civility roundtable on mental health (tentatively late June). Staff notes some subcommittee vacancies and completed annual items.

- Laurel James: Identifies the Spring Family Parade as an annual item and says she will share the date once scheduled.

- Laurel James: Notes the bystander intervention training is in progress with Commissioner Webb currently serving on that subcommittee, and that the civility roundtable on mental health is tentatively scheduled for late June with Commissioners Nwokido and Vice Chair Sylvester on the subcommittee.
#### Housing & Security Subcommittee
  Vice Chair Sylvester outlines the Housing & Security storytelling event concept and current subcommittee status. The event will pair housed and unstably housed participants at roundtables (potentially anonymously) and include an opportunity for unstably housed participants to share experiences; planning is underway and partnership with local organizations will be required. The subcommittee currently includes Commissioners Webb and Sylvester and has one open seat.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: Describes a proposed format that mixes housed and unstably housed participants at round tables and includes a segment for unstably housed attendees to speak publicly about their experiences.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: States the subcommittee presently consists of herself and Commissioner Webb and notes an open seat for another commissioner.
- Jocelyn Baird: Asks whether the project is partnering with local safety-net service organizations and how participants will be invited.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: Responds that the subcommittee has worked extensively with Reach Potential Movement and will seek additional partners to ensure respectful outreach and inclusion.
#### Mental Health Subcommittee
  Commissioner Nwokido briefs the Commission on the Mental Health subcommittee's early work, goals, and planning status. The subcommittee aims to destigmatize mental health, create safe spaces for conversation, and provide resources or expert presentations; it is considering formats such as panels and testimonials for a June event. The subcommittee currently includes Commissioners Nwokido and Vice Chair Sylvester and has an opening for an additional member.
- Nirvana Nwokido: States the Mental Health subcommittee comprises herself and Vice Chair Sylvester and has held two meetings to date.
- Nirvana Nwokido: Explains the main goal is to destigmatize mental health by offering safe spaces, resources, and expert presentations for community members.
- Nirvana Nwokido: Describes possible event formats under consideration, including panels, presenters, and personal testimonies about service utilization and impacts.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: Notes the subcommittee is still clarifying specific outcomes and that the final direction will depend on who joins and what partners they identify.
#### Bystander Intervention Training
  Commissioners discuss the bystander intervention training project which the work plan carries forward, noting it is in progress and needs additional subcommittee members. The group intends to leverage existing curricula and partner organizations rather than create new content, and the subcommittee presently has openings. Commissioners emphasize practical outreach planning and cost considerations.
- Laurel James: Reminds the Commission that bystander intervention training was requested by the Council's Race, Equity and Inclusion Subcommittee and is an in-progress workplan item.
- Xavier Webb: Says the subcommittee intends to build on existing programs and partner with organizations rather than reinvent training curricula.
- Laurel James: Confirms that the bystander intervention subcommittee currently has two openings for commissioners to fill.
#### Subcommittee Assignments
  The Commission conducts a straw poll and assigns commissioners to subcommittees to fill vacancies and provide opportunities for new members. Final assignments place Commissioners Webb, Baird, and Poicon on Bystander Intervention; Vice Chair Sylvester, Nwokido, and Molly Smith on Mental Health; and Vice Chair Sylvester, Webb, and Poicon on Housing & Security. Staff clarifies that non-members may assist with events but may not attend subcommittee meetings in ways that would create a quorum and Brown Act issues.
- Ida Rose Sylvester: Explains subcommittee participation is optional, typically lasts through the work plan year, and usually involves one-to-two hour meetings monthly plus additional volunteer work on deliverables.
- Molly Smith: Asks if she can join the Housing & Security subcommittee even though she may miss the May 25 event, and Laurel James says participation is manageable with advance notice.
- Laurel James: Clarifies that non-members may assist with events but attending subcommittee meetings as non-members could create a quorum and violate the Brown Act.
- Laurel James: Summarizes final subcommittee compositions: Bystander Intervention—Webb, Baird, Poicon; Mental Health—Sylvester, Nwokido, Smith; Housing & Security—Sylvester, Webb, Poicon.
