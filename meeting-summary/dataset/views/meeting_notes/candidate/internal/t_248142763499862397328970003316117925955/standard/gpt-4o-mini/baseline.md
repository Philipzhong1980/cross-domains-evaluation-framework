### Overview
The Human Relations Commission (HRC) meeting, led by Vice Chair Ida Rose Sylvester, marks a significant transition with the introduction of new members and the announcement of the last virtual meeting. Key topics included the approval of previous meeting minutes, updates on the mid-year work plan, and subcommittee activities focusing on housing security, mental health, and bystander intervention training. The commission successfully elected Ida Rose Sylvester as Chair and Nirvana Nwokido as Vice Chair, ensuring continuity in leadership. Staff provided important updates regarding upcoming training and the next in-person meeting scheduled for February 2, 2023. The meeting concluded with positive remarks about the new members and the collaborative work ahead.

### Detailed Summary
#### Welcome and Introductions
Ida Rose Sylvester opens the meeting, welcoming new members and acknowledging the transition period for the Human Relations Commission (HRC). This meeting marks a significant change as it is the last virtual meeting after three years. The introduction of new members is seen as an opportunity for growth and evolution within the HRC.
- Ida Rose Sylvester expresses excitement about the new members joining the HRC.
- The meeting is noted as the last virtual meeting unless circumstances change.

#### Roll Call and Resignation Announcement
Laurel James conducts the roll call and announces the resignation of Commissioner Raigstraw, effective immediately. This resignation is acknowledged with sadness by the members, highlighting the ongoing changes within the commission.
- Laurel James informs the commission about Commissioner Raigstraw's immediate resignation.

#### Approval of Previous Meeting Minutes
The minutes from the December 1, 2022 meeting are presented for approval. After confirming there are no corrections, the minutes are unanimously approved by the members present, demonstrating a smooth transition into the meeting's agenda.
- Nirvana Nwokido approves the minutes from the previous meeting without corrections.
- Xavier Webb seconds the motion to approve the minutes.

#### Public Comments
Ida Rose Sylvester opens the floor for public comments, but no members of the public are present to provide feedback. This section is a standard part of the meeting agenda, allowing community input.
- Ida Rose Sylvester notes the absence of public comments during this section.

#### Mid-Year Work Plan Review
Laurel James provides an update on the HRC's work plan for the fiscal year 2022-2023, discussing ongoing projects and upcoming events. The review includes details about the Spring Family Parade and other community engagement activities planned for the year.
- Laurel James highlights the upcoming Spring Family Parade as a key event in the work plan.
- The storytelling project on housing insecurity is scheduled for May 25, 2023.

#### Subcommittee Updates
The meeting transitions into updates from various subcommittees, including Housing and Security, Mental Health, and Bystander Intervention Training. Each subcommittee shares their current status, upcoming events, and opportunities for new members to join.
- Ida Rose Sylvester discusses the Housing and Security subcommittee's plans for a public forum on housing issues.
- Nirvana Nwokido shares the Mental Health subcommittee's goal to destigmatize mental health discussions.

#### Subcommittee Assignments
The commission discusses the assignment of new members to subcommittees, emphasizing the importance of participation. Members express interest in various subcommittees, and assignments are finalized based on preferences and availability.
- Molly Smith expresses interest in the Mental Health subcommittee, swapping from Housing and Security.
- Jocelyn Baird and Eric Poicon confirm their interest in the Bystander Intervention Training subcommittee.

#### Election of Chair and Vice Chair
The commission conducts elections for the Chair and Vice Chair positions. Ida Rose Sylvester is nominated and unanimously elected as Chair, while Nirvana Nwokido is nominated and elected as Vice Chair, ensuring leadership continuity within the commission.
- Ida Rose Sylvester is unanimously elected as Chair of the HRC.
- Nirvana Nwokido is elected as Vice Chair, with all members supporting the nominations.

#### Staff Comments and Announcements
Laurel James provides updates on upcoming training sessions and the next in-person meeting scheduled for February 2, 2023. She emphasizes the importance of compliance with the Brown Act and the need for new commissioners to complete their Form 700.
- Laurel James reminds commissioners about the upcoming Brown Act training in January.
- The next HRC meeting will be held in person at City Hall.

#### Final Comments and Adjournment
The meeting concludes with final comments from members expressing excitement about the new commissioners and the work ahead. Ida Rose Sylvester adjourns the meeting, looking forward to future collaborations and community engagement.
- Commissioners express enthusiasm for the new members and the work of the HRC.
- Ida Rose Sylvester adjourns the meeting, thanking everyone for their participation.
