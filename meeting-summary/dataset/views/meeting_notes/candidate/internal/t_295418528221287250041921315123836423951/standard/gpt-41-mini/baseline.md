### Overview
The Mountain View Downtown Committee convened on October 4th with Chair Mike Kasperzak and members including John Lang and others. The meeting covered approval of prior meeting minutes and set the stage for upcoming agenda items such as busking music and police updates. Development updates highlighted key projects like the 590 Castro Street building and challenges with the Hope Street Lot financing. The committee received a Castro Street update focusing on pedestrian mall hearings and lighting improvements. A resolution to continue virtual meetings under AB 361 was unanimously adopted, reflecting the committee's preference for remote participation. A detailed presentation on the Personal Delivery Device pilot program outlined proposed policy augmentations to balance innovation and safety, with members noting the devices' appeal as a downtown attraction. Committee vacancies and bylaw changes were reviewed, with upcoming interviews and appointments scheduled. Finally, members shared community event announcements, discussed beautification concerns, and addressed budgetary planning for committee activities. Throughout, staff and members emphasized flexibility and responsiveness to evolving downtown needs and opportunities.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Minutes Approval
The committee reviewed and approved the minutes from the August 9th and September 6th meetings. This formal approval ensures accurate record-keeping and continuity for the committee's work.
- Maria Lange moved to adopt the minutes for both meetings, seconded by Jamil Shaikh.
- John Lang conducted a roll call vote with all members present approving the minutes.
#### Upcoming Agenda Topics
The committee discussed potential topics for upcoming meetings, including busking music, police updates, downtown maintenance, and holiday events. These topics are important for addressing community concerns and planning future activities.
- John Lang noted busking music will be discussed in November.
- Police Department update is planned for November meeting.
- David Lin raised concerns about trash and litter on Castro Street, suggesting a discussion on maintenance.
- Maria Lange requested police presence to address public urination and unlicensed music performances.
- Pamela Baird asked for updates on downtown ambassadors and city-sponsored holiday events.
- Kira Pascoe shared that a list of upcoming events is being prepared in collaboration with the Downtown Business Association.
#### Downtown Redevelopment Updates
Krisha Penollar provided updates on downtown development projects, including new commercial construction and tenant changes. These updates inform the committee about ongoing urban development and its impact on the downtown area.
- 590 Castro Street project approved for a four-story building with retail and public plaza.
- 11954 Villa Street office building received temporary occupancy with minor fire issues being resolved.
- 19110 Castro Street facade upgrades approved for a new Spanish tapas restaurant, Vita.
- Krisha Penollar announced her rotation out and Edgar will take over as downtown planner.
- John Lang updated on Hope Street Lot project financing extension and planning entitlement expiration on November 27th, with uncertainty about meeting the deadline.
#### Castro Street Update
John Lang provided a verbal update on Castro Street improvements, including pedestrian mall public hearings, lighting enhancements, and downtown maintenance efforts. These efforts aim to improve safety and aesthetics in the downtown area.
- Public hearing for pedestrian mall scheduled for October 11th and 25th City Council meetings.
- Additional lighting installed on 200 block of Castro Street; alternative lighting solutions being explored for alleyway areas.
- City working with businesses to remove tent remnants and declutter sidewalks.
- Discussion about tree maintenance and potential future challenges with large tree growth impacting infrastructure.
#### AB 361 Resolution on Virtual Meetings
The committee discussed and voted to adopt the AB 361 resolution allowing virtual meetings for another 30 days. This maintains flexibility for remote participation amid evolving public health guidelines.
- John Lang explained the resolution allows remote meetings if City Council extends their adoption.
- Merry Yen motioned to adopt the resolution; Mike Kasperzak seconded.
- Committee members unanimously voted in favor of continuing virtual meetings.
- Pamela Baird expressed support for virtual format given the committee's advisory role and meeting nature.
#### Personal Delivery Device (PDD) Program Update
John Lang presented updates on the PDD pilot program, including proposed augmentations to operational requirements, data reporting, and safety measures. The program aims to balance innovation with public safety and infrastructure considerations.
- Starship ceased operations in downtown; other providers interested but awaiting policy updates.
- Proposed changes include requiring operator presence in Mountain View, enhanced device information, and removal of human handler for remote operation.
- Increased data reporting requirements to assess usage, incidents, and infrastructure feedback.
- Discussion on challenges with sidewalk space, signal crossing, and potential for future aerial delivery devices.
- Committee members highlighted the PDDs as a tourist attraction and discussed infrastructure needs like signal activation.
- John Lang noted the program remains a pilot to allow flexibility as technology evolves.
#### Downtown Committee Vacancies and Bylaws
John Lang reviewed the current and upcoming vacancies on the Downtown Committee and the status of bylaw changes related to membership categories. This ensures proper representation and committee continuity.
- There are 11 positions divided among property owners, community at large, business at large, and neighborhood association.
- Bylaw changes approved by CPPC to address Downtown Business Association and Chamber representation, pending City Council approval.
- Several members expressed interest in continuing; some seats will open due to term expirations or resignations.
- Council Appointment Review Committee interviews scheduled for November 21st, with appointments in December.
- John Lang will send follow-up emails with key dates and development updates.
#### Committee and Staff Comments
Members shared updates on upcoming events, beautification concerns, and budgetary matters. These discussions support community engagement and operational planning.
- Pamela Baird announced a Downtown Walking Tour on October 23rd by the Mountain View Historical Association.
- Kira Pascoe described upcoming Halloween trick-or-treating and a meet-and-greet event for business owners.
- David Lin inquired about committee funds for facilitating activities; John Lang is drafting language for mid-year budget requests.
- Maria Lange raised concerns about storefront decorations and city beautification support.
- John Lang emphasized a flexible approach to business beautification given ongoing economic challenges.
