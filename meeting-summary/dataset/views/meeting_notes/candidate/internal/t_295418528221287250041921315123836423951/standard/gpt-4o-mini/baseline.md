### Overview
The Mountain View Downtown Committee meeting on October 4th focused on several key topics, including the approval of previous meeting minutes, upcoming agenda items, and updates on downtown development projects. John Lang provided insights into the pedestrian mall initiative and the status of personal delivery devices in the area. The committee also discussed the AB 361 resolution to continue virtual meetings, reviewed current vacancies on the committee, and shared updates on community events. Members expressed their commitment to enhancing downtown engagement and addressing maintenance issues, while also acknowledging the challenges faced by local businesses.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of minutes from the previous meetings held on August 9th and September 6th. All members present vote in favor of adopting the minutes, indicating a consensus on the recorded discussions and decisions from those meetings.
- Maria Lange moves to adopt the minutes for both meetings.
- Jamil Shaikh seconds the motion to adopt the minutes.
- All committee members present approve the minutes.

#### Upcoming Agenda Topics
John Lang outlines several upcoming agenda items for the November meeting, including a police update and the 2023 calendar of meeting dates. He invites suggestions from committee members for additional topics to discuss in future meetings.
- John Lang mentions the busking music item will be discussed in November.
- David Lin raises concerns about trash and litter on Castro Street.
- John Lang agrees to add downtown maintenance discussions to the agenda.

#### Public Comments
The committee opens the floor for public comments regarding the minutes and upcoming agenda topics. No public comments are received, indicating a lack of public engagement at this time.
- Mike Kasperzak asks if there are any public comments on the minutes, but none are raised.

#### Downtown Development Updates
Krisha Penollar provides updates on various downtown development projects, including the approval of a new commercial building at 590 Castro Street and the status of other ongoing projects. The committee discusses the implications of these developments for the downtown area.
- Krisha reports on the approval of a four-story building with retail space at 590 Castro Street.
- John Lang discusses the status of the Hope Street lot project and its planning entitlements.

#### Castro Street Update
John Lang gives a verbal update on the pedestrian mall project and improvements to lighting on Castro Street. He mentions upcoming council meetings where these topics will be discussed and highlights ongoing maintenance efforts in the area.
- John Lang announces the pedestrian mall will be discussed at the council meeting on October 11th.
- He mentions additional lighting is being added to Castro Street.

#### AB 361 Resolution
The committee discusses the AB 361 resolution, which allows for virtual meetings. Members express their preferences for continuing remote meetings, especially for quick sessions, and vote to adopt the resolution.
- Merry Yen motions to adopt the resolution for virtual meetings under AB 361.
- The committee votes unanimously in favor of continuing virtual meetings.

#### Personal Delivery Device Updates
John Lang presents updates on the personal delivery device (PDD) pilot program, discussing changes to the program parameters and the interest from new operators. The committee provides feedback on the proposed modifications.
- John Lang notes that Starship has ceased operations in downtown, prompting discussions with other PDD operators.
- The committee discusses the need for better data collection and infrastructure assessments from PDD operators.

#### Downtown Committee Vacancies
John Lang reviews the current vacancies on the Downtown Committee, noting the terms of various members and upcoming interviews for new candidates. He highlights the need for recruitment to fill open seats.
- John Lang confirms that both Marina and David wish to continue serving on the committee.
- He mentions that Eric and Maria will not be returning, creating opportunities for new candidates.

#### Committee Staff Comments
Committee members share updates on upcoming events and initiatives in downtown Mountain View, including a walking tour and Halloween activities. They discuss the importance of community engagement and beautification efforts.
- Pamela Baird announces a downtown walking tour on October 23rd.
- Kira Pascoe shares plans for Halloween trick-or-treating and a meet-and-greet with business owners.
