### Overview
The November 9 Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee hearing, presided over by Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan with participation from City Attorney’s Office representative Nicole Wright and Public Works representative Ed Arango, focuses on three land use items: an operations change to a cold weather shelter, a commercial project extension downtown, and a condominium project extension in the San Antonio Precise Plan area. Planner Aki Snelling presents a request by Trinity United Methodist Church and HomeFirst Services to extend the daily operating hours of a seasonal homeless shelter for women and families at 748 Mercy Street to better match school schedules; with no opposition and community support on record, Hagan approves the provisional use permit modification with staff-recommended conditions and notes a 10-day appeal period. Planner Eric Anderson then outlines a two-year extension request from Marwood Company for a previously approved four-story office and retail building with underground parking at 701–747 West Evelyn Avenue; after Marwood expresses appreciation and a union representative urges use of local labor and prevailing wage standards, Hagan finds the project materially unchanged and grants the extension under existing conditions, again subject to a 10-day appeal. Finally, planner Jeffrey Tsumura presents a one-year extension for the vesting tentative map and associated approvals for a six-story, 44-unit condominium project at 2645–2655 Fayette Drive, explaining that the applicant is finalizing building permit documents and that the project remains consistent with applicable plans, density bonus law, the Subdivision Map Act, and prior CEQA clearance; following a labor standards comment from the Carpenters Union, Arango and Wright move and second approval of the map extension, Hagan concurs and also approves the related permit extensions, and the item concludes with notice of a 10-day appeal period.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Cold weather shelter hour extension
The committee reviews a request from Trinity United Methodist Church and HomeFirst Services to modify an existing provisional use permit to extend the operating hours of a seasonal cold weather shelter for women and families at 748 Mercy Street. Planner Aki Snelling explains that the proposal shifts daily opening from 4:00 PM to 3:00 PM and closing from 6:30 AM to 9:00 AM during the November–March cold-weather season. The change aims to align with local school schedules so families with school-aged children have a safe place before school and immediately after dismissal. Staff finds the project consistent with the General Plan and Downtown Precise Plan, categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15301, and recommends approval with existing conditions, which the zoning administrator adopts.
- Planner Aki Snelling states that Trinity United Methodist Church and HomeFirst Services request to extend shelter hours from a 4:00 PM–6:30 AM schedule to a 3:00 PM–9:00 AM schedule for up to 50 women and family members.
- Planner Aki Snelling explains that the primary purpose of the extension is to align shelter operations with school hours so children have a secure place before and after school during the cold-weather months.
- Planner Aki Snelling notes that the shelter operates annually from around Thanksgiving through March 31 for approximately three and a half months.
- Planner Aki Snelling reports that Building and Fire departments reviewed the proposal and raised no concerns.
- Planner Aki Snelling states that a community meeting with the Old Mountain View Neighborhood Association on October 17 resulted in supportive comments and no voiced concerns.
- Planner Aki Snelling confirms the project complies with the General Plan, the P-19 Downtown Precise Plan zoning, and is categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15301.
- Kristi Blewis, speaking for Los Altos United Methodist Church, expresses strong support for the partnership with HomeFirst and emphasizes that extended hours will better serve families with school-aged children by keeping them safe and dry before and after school.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan states that she has reviewed the posted materials and finds the hour extension appropriate, particularly due to its alignment with typical school hours in the community.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan moves to approve the modification to the provisional use permit with conditions as outlined in the findings report.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan announces that the approval is subject to a 10-calendar-day appeal period for any interested party.
#### Office and retail project permit extension
The committee considers a two-year time extension for a previously approved planned community permit and development review permit for a four-story mixed commercial building at 701–747 West Evelyn Avenue. Planner Eric Anderson explains that the project includes approximately 28,090 square feet of office, 6,500 square feet of retail, a roof terrace, and three levels of underground parking accessed through a future adjacent public garage on City Parking Lot 4. He notes that the proposal remains consistent with the Downtown Precise Plan and the Downtown mixed-use General Plan designation, and is categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15332. Staff supports the extension based on the applicant’s ongoing coordination with the City to modify the project in line with the Downtown Parking Strategy and the timely response to City comments, and the zoning administrator grants the extension with existing conditions.
- Planner Eric Anderson identifies the applicant as Marwood Company and reiterates that the approved project consists of a four-story commercial building with 28,090 square feet of office, 6,500 square feet of retail, a roof terrace, and three levels of underground parking on the 0.25-acre site at 701–747 West Evelyn Avenue.
- Planner Eric Anderson states that the underground parking is designed to be accessed via a future adjacent public parking garage on City Parking Lot 4.
- Planner Eric Anderson confirms that the project is consistent with the Downtown mixed-use General Plan designation and the development standards of the Downtown Precise Plan.
- Planner Eric Anderson notes that the extension request is categorically exempt under CEQA Guidelines Section 15332 for infill development.
- Planner Eric Anderson explains that staff supports the extension because the applicant has been actively preparing and submitting project modifications to align with the Downtown Parking Strategy adopted by City Council on November 9, 2021, and has responded promptly to City comments.
- Planner Eric Anderson reports that no public comments were received in advance regarding this extension request.
- Applicant representative Tim McEnery from Marwood thanks staff for their support and collaboration on the extension request.
- Public commenter Wing Tam, representing NorCal Carpenters Union Local 405, urges the developer to adopt local hiring, healthcare benefits, and prevailing wage standards to support local families and maintain area labor standards.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan states that she has reviewed the online materials and finds that the project remains materially the same as previously approved, with only minor condition adjustments.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan moves to approve the two-year permit extension with staff-drafted conditions and notes that a 10-day appeal period applies.
#### Condominium project and map extension
The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee reviews a one-year extension for a previously approved six-story, 44-unit condominium project at 2645–2655 Fayette Drive, including a vesting tentative map and related permits. Planner Jeffrey Tsumura explains that the project, sponsored by Octane Fiat LLC, replaces six existing dwelling units and a 6,900-square-foot commercial building and includes a state density bonus, a roof deck, and heritage tree removal and relocation. He reports that since approval in November 2020, the applicant has been coordinating with staff and finalizing construction documents for imminent building permit submittal. Staff finds the request consistent with the San Antonio Precise Plan, the High Density Residential General Plan designation, State Density Bonus Law, the Subdivision Map Act, and prior CEQA documentation, and the committee grants the map extension and related permit extensions with conditions unchanged except for minor updates.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura notes that applicant Emerick McDonald for Octane Fiat LLC seeks a one-year extension for the vesting tentative map and related approvals for the 44-unit condominium project at 2645–2655 Fayette Drive.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura recalls that the project was originally approved on November 17, 2020 and replaces six dwelling units and a 6,900-square-foot commercial building with a six-story condominium building using a state density bonus.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura explains that the approvals include a planned community permit, a development review permit, a provisional use permit for a roof deck, a heritage tree removal permit to relocate one and remove eight heritage trees, and a vesting tentative map to create 44 condominium lots and one common lot.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura reports that the applicant has been diligently preparing a comprehensive building permit set, coordinating with staff on improvement plans and construction documents, and intends to submit permits in the coming weeks.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura states that the extension is consistent with the San Antonio Precise Plan, the High Density Residential General Plan designation, State Density Bonus Law, and the Subdivision Map Act.
- Planner Jeffrey Tsumura confirms that the project remains substantially unchanged from the originally approved version and continues to rely on the previously prepared Initial Study and Mitigated Negative Declaration under CEQA.
- Public commenter Wing Tam, representing Carpenters Union Local 405, asks the developer to incorporate prevailing wages, healthcare benefits, and local hiring standards and offers to coordinate via email to support labor standards on the project.
- Committee member Ed Arango states that he has reviewed the staff memorandum and supporting materials, has no questions, and moves to approve the one-year extension of the vesting tentative map subject to the existing conditions of approval.
- Committee member Nicole Wright seconds the motion to approve the vesting tentative map extension.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan votes in favor of the map extension, then separately moves to approve the related planned community permit, development review permit, and heritage tree removal permit extensions, noting that the project is substantially the same with minor condition updates and that a 10-day appeal period applies.
