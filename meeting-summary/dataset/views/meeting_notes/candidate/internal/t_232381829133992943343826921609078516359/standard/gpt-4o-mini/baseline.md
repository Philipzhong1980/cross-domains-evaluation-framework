### Overview
The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee meeting on November 9th, led by Lindsay Hagan, focused on several key topics including public comment procedures, a modification request for a cold weather shelter at 748 Mercy St., and permit extensions for projects at 7 O 1747 W Evelyn Ave. and 2645-2655 Fayette Drive. The committee discussed the importance of aligning shelter hours with school schedules, received community support for the shelter, and approved the requested extensions for the commercial building and condominium project, reflecting ongoing development efforts in Mountain View. Key participants included project planners Aki Snelling, Eric Anderson, and Jeffrey Tsumura, along with community representatives.

### Detailed Summary
#### Public Comment Procedures
The meeting begins with an overview of public comment procedures, allowing participants to address the zoning administrator on non-agenda items. This is important for transparency and community engagement, ensuring that public voices are heard. The public comment period is limited to three minutes per speaker.
- Lindsay Hagan outlines the public comment process, emphasizing the three-minute limit for each speaker.
- Participants can raise their hands in Zoom or press *9 to comment.

#### 350 Ellis St. Modification Request
The committee discusses a request for modifications to a previously approved planned community permit for 350 Ellis St. This item is continued from a previous meeting and will not be discussed further until a new hearing date is set. This highlights the ongoing nature of community development projects and the need for proper notification.
- Lindsay Hagan states that the item will not be heard today and will be re-noticed for a future date.

#### 748 Mercy St. Cold Weather Shelter
A proposal is presented to modify the hours of operation for a cold weather shelter at Trinity United Methodist Church. The modification aims to align shelter hours with school hours, providing better support for families with school-aged children. The project has received community support and is deemed consistent with local plans.
- Aki Snelling presents the request to extend shelter hours from 4 PM to 3 PM and from 6:30 AM to 9 AM.
- Kristi Blewis expresses support for the modification, emphasizing its benefits for families.

#### 7 O 1747 W Evelyn Ave. Permit Extension
The committee reviews a request for a two-year extension of a previously approved permit for a new commercial building. The project aligns with the Downtown Precise Plan and has received no public opposition. This extension reflects the ongoing development efforts in the area and the importance of timely project execution.
- Eric Anderson discusses the consistency of the project with the Downtown mixed-use designation.
- Tim McEnery from Marwood expresses gratitude for staff support on the extension request.

#### 2645-2655 Fayette Drive Permit Extension
A request for a one-year extension for a condominium project is presented. The project remains consistent with previous approvals and has undergone necessary preparations for building permit submission. The committee supports the extension, indicating a commitment to ongoing residential development in the area.
- Jeffrey Tsumura outlines the project's compliance with local plans and the need for an extension.
- Ed Arango motions to approve the extension, which is seconded by Nicole Wright.
