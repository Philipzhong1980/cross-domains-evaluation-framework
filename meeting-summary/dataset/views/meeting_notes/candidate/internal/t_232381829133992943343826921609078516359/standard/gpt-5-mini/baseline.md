### Overview
  The Joint Administrative, Zoning and Subdivision Committee meeting, presided over by Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan with City Attorney Nicole Wright and Public Works representative Ed Arango participating, addresses four primary agenda items. The 350 Ellis Street consent-calendar modification is continued and not heard. Staff presents and the committee approves a modification to Trinity United Methodist Church’s provisional use permit to extend cold-weather shelter hours (project planner Aki Snelling), citing building and fire department clearance and neighborhood support, with the church (Christy Lewis) endorsing the change to align with school schedules. The committee approves a two-year extension for Marwood Company’s planned commercial building at 1747 W Evelyn Ave after staff (Eric Anderson) confirms consistency with the Downtown Precise Plan and the Downtown Parking Strategy, while a NorCal Carpenters representative (Wing Tam) requests prevailing wage, local hire, and healthcare standards. Finally, the committee grants a one-year extension and approves the vesting tentative map and associated permits for the 2645–2655 Fayette Drive mixed-use condominium project following staff (Jeffrey Tsumura) findings that the project remains substantially unchanged and compliant with applicable plans and laws; Ed Arango and Nicole Wright formally move and second the subdivision approval. Each approval carries conditions as posted in staff reports, a 10‑calendar‑day appeal period, and follow-up contacts to the respective project planners.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### 350 Ellis Street modification
  Staff notes a previously continued request to modify a planned community permit and development review for minor site and façade changes to an existing office campus on a 19.6-acre site. The item is not heard at this meeting and will be re‑noticed when it is ready for public hearing. No action or discussion occurs on the project during this session.
- Assistant Community Development Director Lindsay Hagan said the 350 Ellis Street item will not be heard today and was previously continued from the October 26 Zoning Administrator hearing.
- Assistant Community Development Director Lindsay Hagan said the project will be re-noticed for a future hearing date when it is ready for public hearing.
- No staff or committee action was taken on the 350 Ellis Street item during this meeting.
  #### Cold weather shelter hours
  Trinity United Methodist Church and Home First Services request a modification to their provisional use permit to extend nightly operating hours for a cold-weather shelter serving up to 50 people so hours align better with school schedules. Staff reports the shelter would operate annually from roughly Thanksgiving through March 31, the building and fire departments raise no concerns, a community meeting yielded support, and staff recommends approval under a categorical exemption. The Zoning Administrator votes to approve the modification with the conditions in the findings report and notes a 10‑calendar day appeal period.
- Project planner Aki Snelling said the request is to change shelter operating hours from 4:00 PM–6:30 AM to 3:00 PM–9:00 AM to align with school schedules for families with school-aged children.
- Project planner Aki Snelling said the shelter operates only during cold-weather months from about Thanksgiving through March 31 and that the project is categorically exempt under Section 15301 of the CEQA guidelines.
- Project planner Aki Snelling said building and fire departments reviewed the project and expressed no concerns.
- Christy Lewis (Los Altos United Methodist Church, Trinity campus) said the church is eager to partner with Home First and supports the hour extension to provide safe, dry space for families before and after school.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan said she reviewed the materials, concurs with staff's recommendation, and moved to approve the provisional use permit with the conditions in the findings report.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan noted that interested parties have a 10-calendar day appeal period and directed follow-up questions to planner Aki Snelling.
  #### 1747 W Evelyn Ave extension
  The applicant (Marwood Company) requests a two-year extension of a previously approved planned community permit and development review to build a four-story commercial building with offices, retail, a roof terrace, and three levels of underground parking. Staff finds the project consistent with the Downtown Precise Plan, categorically exempt, and recommends approval noting the applicant's work to align design changes with the Downtown Parking Strategy. The Zoning Administrator approves the two‑year extension with staff conditions, and a public commenter from the NorCal Carpenters requests prevailing wage, local hiring, and healthcare standards be included.
- Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson said the project is consistent with the Downtown mixed-use General Plan designation and categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15332.
- Advanced Planning Manager Eric Anderson said staff recommends approval in part because the applicant prepared project modifications consistent with the Downtown Parking Strategy and responded to city comments in a timely manner.
- Tim McEnery (Marwood Company) thanked staff for their support and said the applicant had no additional comments at this time.
- Wing Tam (NorCal Carpenters Local 405) requested that the developer adopt local hiring, prevailing wage, and healthcare benefits standards for this project.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan said she reviewed the posted materials, concurs with staff's recommendation, and moved to approve the two-year extension with the conditions drafted by staff.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan noted a 10-day appeal period and directed follow-up questions to planner Eric Anderson.
  #### Fayette Drive one-year extension
  The applicant (Octane Fiat LLC / Emerick McDonald) seeks a one-year extension for a previously approved mixed-use development including a six-story, 44-unit condominium project with a state density bonus, 6,900 square feet of commercial space, a provisional use permit for a roof deck, heritage tree permits to relocate one and remove eight trees, and a vesting tentative map to create 44 condominium lots. Staff reports the project remains substantially unchanged since its November 17, 2020 approval, has an initial study and mitigated negative declaration, and recommends conditional approval; the applicant is finalizing building permit documents for imminent submittal. The Joint Administrative Zoning and Subdivision Committee approves the vesting tentative map and the associated extensions and permits, records unanimous votes, and notes a 10‑day appeal period.
- Project planner Jeffrey Tsumura said the project was originally approved November 17, 2020, and that the applicant has been preparing building permit submittals and expects to submit them in the coming weeks.
- Project planner Jeffrey Tsumura said staff finds the extension consistent with the San Antonio Precise Plan, the High Density Residential General Plan designation, State Density Bonus law, and the Subdivision Map Act.
- Project planner Jeffrey Tsumura said an initial study and mitigated negative declaration had been prepared and that the project remains substantially unchanged from the originally approved project.
- Wing Tam (NorCal Carpenters Local 405) asked the developer to adopt prevailing wage, local hiring, and healthcare labor standards for the project.
- Public Works representative Ed Arango said he had reviewed the staff materials and moved to approve the one-year extension of the vesting tentative map for 2645–2655 Fayette Drive subject to staff conditions.
- City Attorney Nicole Wright seconded Ed Arango's motion to approve the vesting tentative map.
- Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan said she reviewed the posted materials, moved to approve the remaining extension items and permits as conditioned by staff, and noted a 10-day appeal period and follow-up contact for planner Jeffrey Tsumura.
