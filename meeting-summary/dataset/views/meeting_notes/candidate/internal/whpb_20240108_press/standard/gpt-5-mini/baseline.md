### Overview
  Press Secretary Karine Jean‑Pierre and Admiral John Kirby lead the briefing, opening with the President's travel to Mother Emanuel AME Church in South Carolina and a follow-on trip to Dallas. Admiral Kirby reports that 210 humanitarian trucks entered Gaza in the last 48 hours and says Secretary Blinken is in the region to press for increased assistance while the U.S. urges de-escalation amid concerns about potential spillover into Lebanon. Much of the briefing centers on Secretary of Defense Lloyd Austin's recent hospitalization and a delayed notification to the White House; Admiral Kirby and Jean‑Pierre emphasize that Austin is recovering, has resumed his authorities, publicly accepted responsibility for the transparency lapse, and that the administration will review procedures while leaving medical and agency-protocol details to the Department of Defense. Jean‑Pierre also outlines the administration's view that a bipartisan funding framework reached over the weekend moves the country closer to avoiding a government shutdown and urges Congress to act, notes continued engagement on border supplemental negotiations expected this week, condemns efforts to relativize the January 6 attack, and previews that the President's Charleston remarks will focus on remembrance, dignity, and standing against racist hate.

  Admiral John Kirby updates the briefing on humanitarian access to Gaza, reporting that 210 trucks entered over the past 48 hours and calling that result insufficient. The Department of Defense remains the lead for medical details and internal protocol explanations.
