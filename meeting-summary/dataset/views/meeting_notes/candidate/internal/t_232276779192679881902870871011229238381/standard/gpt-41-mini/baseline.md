### Overview
The Visual Arts Committee meeting, chaired by Jesse Cupp with staff support from John Lang, focused on several key areas including future agenda planning, public art project updates, and policy discussions. The committee approved previous meeting minutes and explored future topics such as integrating art with the city's biodiversity strategy and enhancing artist engagement processes. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to reviewing the 2023 call for artists for the Center for the Performing Arts, resulting in a decision to feature eight artists with three alternates and discussions on exhibition duration and promotion. Public Works staff presented the Transit Center Grade Separation and Access Project, highlighting design elements and opportunities for public art integration in undercrossings and concourse areas, with committee members emphasizing safety, lighting, and thematic considerations. The committee received guidance on handling public inquiries and engaged in a detailed discussion on establishing a stipend policy for artists during RFP evaluations, favoring a default stipend approach for projects exceeding $60,000 with flexibility for exceptions. The meeting concluded with mutual appreciation and holiday wishes among members and staff, setting a collaborative tone for upcoming work.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Meeting Roll Call and Minutes Approval
The meeting commenced with a roll call confirming the presence of committee members and the approval of minutes from the previous meeting held on November 9, 2022. There were no corrections or additions to the minutes, and the approval was unanimous.
- John Lang conducted the roll call confirming attendance of committee members including Bryant, Merhar, Sakols, Thompson, Whitebread, and Chair Cupp.
- Jesse Cupp called for approval of the November 9, 2022 minutes, which were approved unanimously after a motion by Susi Merhar and second by Regina Sakols.

#### Future Agenda Topics Discussion
The committee discussed potential topics for future meetings, including placemaking opportunities related to Castro and Trim pedestrian mall, integration of art with landscaping in the biodiversity strategy, safety projects, and joint meetings with other committees. Members expressed interest in clarifying artist engagement processes and exploring alternative platforms for artist calls.
- John Lang clarified the biodiversity strategy's focus on native plants and potential integration of art into landscaping, noting ongoing research and future committee involvement.
- Susi Merhar raised concerns about inconsistent artist engagement strategies and difficulties with the current Cafe platform, suggesting a need for clearer expectations and possibly alternative platforms.
- Regina Sakols emphasized the importance of having access to original calls for artists and related materials to better evaluate submissions.
- Committee members discussed the possibility of using platforms like SlideRoom or Google Forms as alternatives to Cafe for artist submissions.

#### Public Art Integration with Landscaping
Committee members shared examples and ideas for integrating public art with landscaping, highlighting successful projects in nearby cities and discussing potential applications in Mountain View. The conversation emphasized the value of creative and attractive art installations that complement urban design and traffic calming measures.
- Clifford Bryant cited a Palo Alto project on Charleston Street featuring colorful sculptures integrated with vegetation as a model for art and landscaping integration.
- Jesse Cupp noted the potential for similar art installations in protected bike lanes and other public spaces.
- Bruce England supported the idea of integrating art beyond biodiversity strategy goals, including broader landscaping and community service planning.

#### Call for Young Artists Program Proposal
A proposal was introduced to pilot a program encouraging young artists, particularly from local schools, to display their artwork in public venues. The committee discussed existing programs, potential venues, and the need for formal agenda consideration to explore this initiative further.
- Toot Too Thomson suggested creating a call for young artists to display work in venues like the Center for the Performing Arts.
- Committee members noted existing programs by CSMA and the library that showcase children's artwork, indicating potential partnerships or models.
- It was agreed to place this topic on a future agenda for detailed discussion.

Staff provided updates on ongoing CIP public art projects including Reinstorff Park Aquatic Center, Fayette Park, and Reinstorff Park Maintenance Antennas Building. The main update was the receipt of updated insurance documents enabling execution of artist agreements, with no other significant changes since the last meeting.
- John Lang reported no substantial changes since the last update, with progress on insurance requirements for artist agreements at Reinstorff Park Maintenance.
- The artists involved include Fernanda and Haroma for the maintenance building murals.
- Committee members acknowledged readiness to proceed now that insurance issues are resolved.

#### Center for the Performing Arts Call for Artists Review
The committee reviewed the results of the 2023 call for artists for gallery displays at the Center for the Performing Arts. Staff presented scoring data, discussed artist selection criteria, and deliberated on the number of artists to feature and alternates. The committee decided to increase the number of featured artists from six to eight with three alternates, and discussed exhibition duration and promotional strategies.
- John Lang presented scoring results for 63 artist submissions, highlighting average and median scores and score ranges.
- Committee members discussed the interpretation of blank scores and the need for clarity in scoring methods.
- The committee agreed to feature eight artists instead of six, with three alternates, to accommodate scheduling uncertainties and increase opportunities.
- Discussion included potentially shortening exhibition durations to allow more artists to display and enhancing promotion through online and physical advertising.
- John Lang noted logistical and staffing considerations limit the number of artists displayed simultaneously and the duration of exhibitions.
- Committee members suggested exploring multiple venues and shared exhibitions in the future.

#### Transit Center Grade Separation and Access Project Presentation
Public Works staff presented an update on the Transit Center Grade Separation and Access Project, detailing project background, goals, schedule, design elements, and urban design guidelines. The project aims to improve safety, traffic flow, and pedestrianization, with significant opportunities for public art integration in undercrossings and concourse areas. The committee discussed design considerations, safety, lighting, and potential art themes.
- Joy Houghton outlined the project's scope including new vehicle ramps, shared use paths, closure of at-grade crossings, and undercrossings with vertical circulation.
- The project is in final design phase, scheduled for completion in July 2024, with construction from July 2024 to December 2026.
- Urban Design Guidelines emphasize safe, secure, and attractive undercrossings with opportunities for art in surfaces, lighting, landscaping, and fixtures.
- Committee members discussed the importance of lighting and safety in underpasses, suggesting art that enhances these aspects.
- Ideas such as living walls and lighted art installations were proposed to improve ambiance and mitigate odors.
- Staff noted the city's responsibility for maintenance and the potential for cost-sharing with Caltrain, though contributions from Caltrain are uncertain.
- The committee was encouraged to consider themes and locations for art integration as design progresses.

#### Public Inquiries to Visual Arts Committee Members
Staff provided guidance on handling public inquiries directed to committee members, advising that such inquiries be forwarded to staff for appropriate response coordination. This approach aims to ensure consistent communication and proper handling of public questions related to the Visual Arts Committee's role and activities.
- John Lang advised committee members to forward public inquiries to staff rather than responding directly.
- The clerk's office supports this process to maintain clarity and consistency in communications.
- No public comments were received on this update.

#### Policy on Artist Stipends for RFP Evaluations
The committee reviewed a memo summarizing historical practices regarding stipends for artists during Request for Proposal (RFP) evaluations, particularly for projects exceeding $60,000 in art budget. Discussion focused on establishing a threshold to trigger stipend consideration, balancing the need for refined proposals with project timelines and budget constraints. The committee favored a default stipend approach with flexibility to waive it, and emphasized the importance of clear artist engagement processes.
- John Lang presented historical data showing stipends ranging from $500 to $5,000 for projects with art budgets over $60,000.
- Committee members discussed the diversity of artist proposals and the need for a nuanced approach to stipends based on clarity of proposals.
- There was consensus to set a $60,000 threshold to trigger stipend discussions, with a default to provide stipends unless decided otherwise by majority vote.
- Concerns were raised about potential timeline impacts, with stipends adding approximately two months to the selection process.
- Suggestions included improving initial artist submissions with brief descriptions to better inform selection and leveling the playing field for emerging artists.
- John Lang committed to working with Public Works to refine the process and consider fast-track options.
- The stipend amount should be inflation-adjusted in line with the K5 policy.

- Committee members reciprocated the thanks and holiday wishes.
- The meeting was adjourned with positive remarks about the year's work.
