### Overview
The joint meeting of the Council, Finance Committee, and Investment Review Committee focused primarily on reviewing the city's investment portfolio performance, policy compliance, and economic context. Carlos Oblites of Chandler Asset Management provided a detailed presentation on the portfolio's conservative management under California government code, highlighting the impact of recent inflation and Federal Reserve interest rate hikes on bond valuations and yields. The portfolio, valued at approximately $930 million, maintained high credit quality and liquidity, with a cautious approach that mitigated losses during volatile market conditions. Committee members discussed potential future policy considerations, including limits on private placement securities, while affirming the current conservative stance aligns with the city's risk tolerance. Staff updated the committee on the ongoing refinancing of Shoreline bonds, expected to yield savings despite rising rates. Tyler Cook presented the draft fiscal year 2021-22 Investment Review Committee report, confirming policy compliance and portfolio growth. The meeting concluded with acknowledgments of committee and staff contributions, approval of prior meeting minutes, and anticipation of upcoming in-person events.
  
### Detailed Summary

#### City's Investment Portfolio Overview
Carlos Oblites from Chandler Asset Management presented an update on the city's investment portfolio and policy. He explained the portfolio's adherence to California government code emphasizing safety, liquidity, and return, with additional city-specific restrictions. The presentation covered recent economic events impacting the portfolio, including inflation, Federal Reserve interest rate hikes, and their effects on bond valuations and yields.
- Carlos Oblites highlighted the portfolio's compliance with California government code and city restrictions.
- He explained inflation's causes and the Federal Reserve's rate hikes to combat it.
- Oblites noted that rising interest rates have decreased bond values on paper but increased reinvestment yields.
- He emphasized the portfolio's conservative approach focusing on safety and liquidity.
#### Economic Environment Impact on Investments
The discussion detailed the current economic environment characterized by high inflation, Federal Reserve interest rate increases, and potential recession signals. The impact on bond yields, portfolio valuations, and reinvestment opportunities was analyzed. The presentation underscored the balancing act between controlling inflation and maintaining employment levels.
- Inflation reached 9.1% in June, the highest since the early 1980s, causing economic pressure.
- Federal Reserve raised interest rates multiple times to slow inflation, now targeting around 3.25% to 4.5%.
- Rising rates cause bond prices to fall, negatively impacting portfolio valuations temporarily.
- Despite economic softening, the job market remains strong with low unemployment at 3.7%.
#### Portfolio Composition and Management
The city's portfolio is primarily composed of high-quality fixed income securities with an average maturity around two years. The portfolio includes government securities, corporate credits limited to AA or higher, and some new private placement securities available to qualified institutional buyers. The conservative investment strategy aims to manage risk while seeking competitive returns.
- Portfolio average maturity is about 1.89 years with a duration near 1.96, maintaining liquidity.
- Corporate credit holdings increased to about 7.3% of the portfolio, all AA rated or higher.
- New private placement securities (144A) like MetLife and New York Life were added, representing less than 1% of the portfolio.
- The portfolio is valued at approximately $930 million with a focus on safety and compliance.
#### Portfolio Performance and Risk Management
The portfolio experienced a negative total return of approximately -3.38% over the past year due to rising interest rates and widening corporate credit spreads. However, the portfolio outperformed its benchmark, reflecting effective risk management. The conservative approach has mitigated losses during volatile market conditions, preserving capital and liquidity.
- The portfolio's negative return was driven mainly by unrealized losses from rising interest rates.
- Corporate credit spreads widened, causing greater volatility in corporate holdings.
- Despite losses, the portfolio outperformed its benchmark, indicating prudent management.
- The conservative strategy has protected the city’s investments during market volatility.
#### Investment Policy and Future Considerations
Committee members discussed the conservative nature of the investment policy relative to California code and market opportunities. Suggestions included considering limits on private placement securities and potential policy adjustments if the city opts for a less conservative approach. The advisor recommended maintaining the current conservative stance given the city's risk tolerance and investment culture.
- Steven Permut noted the portfolio's conservative approach has mitigated losses during volatile periods.
- Discussion about possibly setting limits on 144A private placement securities in the future.
- Carlos Oblites advised no major policy changes currently, respecting the city's conservative culture.
- Potential policy realignment could be considered if the city desires higher returns with increased risk.
#### Shoreline Bonds Refinancing Update
Staff provided an update on the refinancing of the city's Shoreline bonds. Despite rising interest rates, financial advisors concluded that refinancing as a private placement could still yield savings. The process is underway with a request for proposals circulated and final approval expected in November, followed by closing shortly thereafter.
- Jesse Takahashi reported the city is actively refinancing Shoreline bonds via private placement.
- Financial advisors found potential savings despite increased interest rates.
- Final approval is anticipated at the November council meeting with closing soon after.
- The city holds some of its own bonds, which will be called upon refinancing.
#### Investment Review Committee Report Presentation
Tyler Cook presented the draft fiscal year 2021-22 Investment Review Committee report. He highlighted portfolio metrics including a -3.3% total return, average duration compliance, and portfolio growth to $841.9 million. The report confirmed no policy violations and recommended no changes at this time. Committee members expressed appreciation for the regular reporting and the report's clarity.

- Portfolio duration remained within policy limits throughout the year.
- Portfolio size grew significantly over the past decade to $841.9 million.
- No investment policy violations occurred and no changes are recommended currently.
