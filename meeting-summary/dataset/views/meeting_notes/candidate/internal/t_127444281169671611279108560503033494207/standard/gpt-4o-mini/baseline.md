### Overview
The meeting, led by Sally Lieber, included discussions on the approval of previous minutes, public communications, and a detailed presentation on the city's investment portfolio by Carlos Oblites from Chandler Asset Management. Key topics included the impact of inflation and interest rates on the portfolio, with a reported -3.3% total return for the fiscal year. The committee reviewed compliance with investment policies and discussed the recent inclusion of private placements, considering future policy adjustments. Additionally, updates on the refinancing of Shoreline bonds were provided, indicating ongoing efforts to optimize the city's financial strategies.

### Detailed Summary

- Sally Lieber notes no public comments in the participant box.

#### Investment Portfolio Presentation
Carlos Oblites from Chandler Asset Management presents the status of the city's investment portfolio and investment policy. He discusses the impact of recent economic events, particularly inflation and interest rate changes, on the portfolio's performance and outlines the city's investment strategy focused on safety, liquidity, and return.
- Carlos Oblites emphasizes the importance of safety and liquidity in the investment strategy.
- He discusses the significant inflation rates affecting the economy and the portfolio.
- Oblites notes that the Federal Reserve's actions to combat inflation have led to rising interest rates.
#### Economic Conditions and Portfolio Impact
Oblites elaborates on the current economic conditions, including inflation rates and job market statistics. He explains how these factors influence the city's investment portfolio, particularly regarding bond values and interest income.
- Oblites reports inflation reached 9.1% in June, impacting the portfolio's bond values.
- He highlights the strong job market, with 315,000 jobs created in the last month.
- Oblites warns that unchecked inflation could lead to economic downturns.
#### Portfolio Performance Metrics
The committee reviews key performance metrics of the investment portfolio, including average duration, yield to maturity, and compliance with investment policies. Tyler Cook presents the draft fiscal year 2021-2022 Investment Review Committee report, confirming no policy violations occurred during the fiscal year.
- Tyler Cook reports a -3.3% total rate of return for the fiscal year, the largest negative return in recent history.
- He confirms compliance with investment policy limits on duration and diversification.
- Cook notes the portfolio's average earnings rate was 1.3%, reflecting low interest rates during the year.
#### Discussion on Private Placements
The committee discusses the recent inclusion of private placements in the investment portfolio, specifically MetLife and New York Life securities. Concerns about liquidity and the potential need for a cap on private placements are raised.
- Steven Permut suggests considering a cap on 144A securities in the future.
- Carlos Oblites reassures that the private placements are high quality and liquid.
- The committee acknowledges the need for a policy review regarding private placements.
#### Shoreline Bonds Refinancing
Lisa Matichak inquires about the status of the Shoreline bonds purchased by the city and the implications of refinancing them. Jesse Takahashi provides an update on the refinancing process, indicating potential savings despite rising interest rates.
- Matichak expresses concern about the dual impact of owning and refinancing the Shoreline bonds.
- Takahashi confirms the city is actively pursuing refinancing options and expects to present findings to the council soon.
