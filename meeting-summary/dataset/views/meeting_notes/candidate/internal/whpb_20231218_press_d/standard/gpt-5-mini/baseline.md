### Overview
  National Security Council spokesperson John Kirby delivers updates on multiple fronts: he announces Israel opened Kerem Shalom for direct humanitarian deliveries and reports nearly 200 trucks entered Gaza when combined with Rafah, while reiterating U.S. pressure on Israel to minimize civilian casualties—a message Secretary Austin reinforced during meetings in Tel Aviv. Kirby explains that the Defense Department has allocated remaining replenishment authority to restock U.S. supplies and that the administration plans one more Ukraine aid package this month but will need Congress to act afterward, noting recent Russian attacks underscore Ukraine's continued need for support. He describes intensive, ongoing efforts to secure another hostage release and humanitarian pause—work led in part by Jake Sullivan, Brett McGurk, and David Satterfield—while declining to discuss sensitive diplomatic or CIA travel details in public; the IDF's admitted mistake in killing hostages is under forensic review and may prompt ROE examination. The briefing also covers active White House involvement in negotiations that could link Ukraine and Israel aid to border-security compromises, plans to strengthen the Combined Maritime Forces against Houthi attacks, U.S. concerns about escalation on the Lebanon border, the Administration's view that a revitalized Palestinian Authority should have a role in post-conflict Gaza, and the central role the U.S. defense industry plays in replenishment and long-term Ukrainian requirements. Kirby flags the church shooting and other civilian incidents as matters the U.S. has raised with Israel and emphasizes the need for improved deconfliction channels, and he defers on several domestic or private-sector questions while advising reporters to follow up with the appropriate agencies.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Kerem Shalom humanitarian access
  John Kirby reports that Israel opened the Kerem Shalom crossing, allowing direct delivery of humanitarian assistance into Gaza for the first time since October 7. He notes nearly 80 trucks moved through Kerem Shalom and that, combined with Rafah, almost 200 trucks entered Gaza on the cited day. The Administration says it will continue working to get aid into Gaza to alleviate civilian suffering.
- John Kirby says Israel opened Kerem Shalom and that Sunday marked the first direct deliveries since October 7.
- John Kirby reports that almost 80 trucks passed through Kerem Shalom and that nearly 200 trucks entered Gaza when combined with Rafah.
- John Kirby states that the U.S. will continue efforts to get humanitarian assistance into Gaza.
  #### Minimizing civilian casualties
  Kirby emphasizes that the U.S. continues to urge Israel to prevent civilian casualties and to conduct operations as surgically and precisely as possible. He points to Secretary Austin's meetings in Tel Aviv where the same message was delivered to Israeli officials. The Administration frames preventing civilian harm as an ongoing engagement with Israeli authorities.
- John Kirby says the U.S. repeatedly urges Israel to do everything possible to prevent civilian casualties.
- John Kirby says Secretary Austin delivered the message to Defense Minister Gallant and other Israeli officials during his visit to Tel Aviv.
- John Kirby says the U.S. will continue to engage Israel on conducting operations in a careful and precise manner.
  #### Ukraine replenishment authority
  Kirby summarizes a Defense Department Comptroller notification that the remaining replenishment authority has been allocated to restock U.S. supplies and replace what is being sent to Ukraine. He says the administration used that authority to jumpstart and expand domestic production lines and to support U.S. jobs. The White House warns that after one more planned aid package later this month, no replenishment authority will remain and it needs Congress to act promptly.
- John Kirby references DOD Comptroller Mike McCord's notification that the remaining replenishment authority is allocated to restock U.S. supplies.
- John Kirby says the administration used replenishment authority to expand production lines across dozens of states and to support American jobs.
- John Kirby states that the administration is planning one more aid package to Ukraine this month and that Congress must act after that authority is exhausted.
  #### Russian attacks and Ukrainian needs
  Kirby describes recent Russian strikes, including dozens of drones and at least one cruise missile, and notes Russian attempts at a ground offensive in eastern Ukraine. He credits Ukrainian forces for defending successfully in many cases but stresses Ukraine still needs assistance. The administration frames continued U.S. support as essential to U.S. national security and urges Congressional action.
- John Kirby reports that Russia launched dozens of drones and at least one cruise missile at Ukrainian cities in the last 24 hours.
- John Kirby says Russian ground offensive efforts face limited success because Ukrainians are well-armed and well-resourced.
- John Kirby urges Congress to act to provide additional support because U.S. national security interests are at stake.
  #### Hostage negotiations status
  When asked about efforts to secure another hostage release and a humanitarian pause, Kirby says U.S. teams are working daily but cannot confirm an imminent deal. He notes Jake Sullivan and other negotiators have been testing possibilities on the ground and that conversations among negotiators have continued in recent days. Kirby repeatedly declines to discuss CIA Director travel or specific negotiation parameters to avoid jeopardizing talks.
- John Kirby says the U.S. is working literally every day to try to get another hostage deal and accompanying humanitarian pause.
- John Kirby says Jake Sullivan discussed testing possibilities for a deal during his recent regional visits.
- John Kirby refers questions about the CIA director's travel or meetings to the CIA and declines to discuss those details publicly.
  #### Israeli ROE and hostage deaths
  Kirby addresses the incident in which Israeli soldiers killed three hostages by saying the IDF admitted its mistake and will conduct forensics. He refuses to publicly adjudicate rules of engagement (ROE), noting he will not discuss Israeli ROE any more than American ROE. The Administration says forensics will help determine whether the issue is systemic rules, enforcement, or an individual error.
- John Kirby says the IDF admitted the mistake very soon after it occurred and that they will conduct forensics to learn what happened.
- John Kirby says he will not discuss Israeli rules of engagement in detail and treats them the same way he would U.S. ROE.
- John Kirby says the forensics will help determine whether the issue was the rules themselves, their enforcement, or an individual error.
  #### Border deal negotiations (aid tradeoffs)
  Kirby confirms active discussions over the weekend about a package that would tie Ukraine and Israel aid to border security compromises, and he says the White House is involved. He emphasizes the President's willingness to negotiate in good faith on both policy and border security. Kirby declines to discuss negotiating details in public to avoid jeopardizing the talks.
- John Kirby confirms there were active discussions over the weekend and that the White House is involved in those talks.
- John Kirby says President Biden is willing to negotiate in good faith and make compromises on policy and border security.
- John Kirby declines to provide details of the negotiations in public to avoid putting the discussions at risk.
  #### Maritime coalition against Houthis
  Kirby outlines efforts to strengthen and operationalize the Combined Maritime Forces under U.S. Fifth Fleet in Bahrain to respond to Houthi attacks in the Red Sea. He says the administration is working to bolster the existing maritime force and that Secretary Austin will have regional meetings on the topic. Kirby suggests more specifics will be available after Austin's discussions.
- John Kirby says the U.S. is working to strengthen and operationalize the Combined Maritime Forces under the Fifth Fleet.
- John Kirby says Secretary Austin will hold regional meetings about the maritime response and will likely provide more details afterward.
- John Kirby says the work is ongoing to bolster multinational maritime efforts against Houthi attacks.
  #### Northern Lebanon border concerns
  Responding to questions about the northern border with Lebanon, Kirby says the U.S. is concerned about attacks there and does not want the conflict to widen into a Hezbollah-Israel war. He emphasizes active discussions with Israeli counterparts and concern for attacks on the Lebanese Armed Forces, which the U.S. says should not be part of the conflict. The Administration seeks to keep the fight focused on Hamas rather than open additional fronts.
- John Kirby says the U.S. is concerned about continued attacks across the northern border and does not want to see the conflict widened.
- John Kirby says the U.S. is in active discussions with Israeli counterparts about activities on the northern border.
- John Kirby expresses deep concern about attacks on the Lebanese Armed Forces and says they should not be part of this conflict.
  #### Post-conflict Gaza governance
  On the 'day after' governance of Gaza, Kirby reiterates U.S. support for a future in which the Palestinian people have a vote and voice, and he endorses a two-state solution as the ultimate answer. He says the U.S. believes a revamped Palestinian Authority should have a significant interim role in post-conflict Gaza and cites Jake Sullivan's meeting with Mahmoud Abbas in Ramallah on this topic. Kirby acknowledges Israel's position but affirms the President's view supporting Palestinian participation in future governance.
- John Kirby says the Palestinian people deserve a vote and a voice in their future and that the U.S. ultimately supports a two-state solution.
- John Kirby says the U.S. believes a revamped Palestinian Authority could and should have a significant interim role in post-conflict Gaza.
- John Kirby notes Jake Sullivan met with Mahmoud Abbas in Ramallah to discuss possibilities for post-conflict governance.
  #### Church shooting and deconfliction
  Kirby addresses the deadly incident at a church compound in which a mother and daughter apparently died, calling every civilian death a tragedy and saying the U.S. has raised concerns with Israel. He stresses the need for safe evacuation of the wounded and better deconfliction channels to reduce civilian risk during Israeli operations. Kirby says the Administration will continue urging Israel to use deconfliction measures to minimize civilian harm.
- John Kirby says every civilian death is a tragedy and that the U.S. has raised specific concerns about the church incident with the Israeli government.
- John Kirby urges that those wounded be safely evacuated so they can receive appropriate medical treatment.
- John Kirby says the U.S. is pushing for better deconfliction channels to prevent endangering civilians and humanitarian workers during operations.
  #### Ukraine funding clarification
  When pressed about how much money remains for Ukraine, Kirby refers reporters to the Department of Defense for exact figures but reiterates his assessment that current replenishment authority supports one more aid package this month. He says he has not seen the draft of that package and cannot provide a precise total. The Administration reiterates that further support will require Congressional action.
- John Kirby says he would refer to the Department of Defense for exact figures on remaining Ukraine funding.
- John Kirby states his understanding that there is enough replenishment authority for one more aid package before the end of the month.
- John Kirby says he has not seen a draft of the upcoming package and cannot provide details on its contents.
  #### Hostage diplomacy urgency
  Kirby underscores the urgency of recovering hostages, saying officials like Brett McGurk and David Satterfield are working 'hour by hour' to secure pauses and releases. He highlights the severe risks hostages face and frames the effort as continuous and time-sensitive. Kirby refuses to publicly discuss negotiation parameters to avoid undermining prospects for success.
- John Kirby says Brett McGurk and David Satterfield continue to work almost by the hour to try to secure another pause and hostage deal.
- John Kirby emphasizes the 'tyranny of the clock' and the grave risks hostages face while in captivity.
- John Kirby refuses to negotiate in public or disclose parameters that might torpedo the chances for a deal.
  #### Hamas hostage video tactics
  Asked about a newly released video allegedly showing elderly hostages, Kirby says he has not seen or verified the footage and declines to comment on its veracity. He characterizes Hamas as brutal toward captives and says such tactics, if verified, would demonstrate depravity and further justify pressure for compliance with law-of-war norms. The Administration uses the example to contrast Hamas's actions with Israel's stated aims.
- John Kirby says he has not seen the newly released video and cannot validate its authenticity.
- John Kirby says that if the video is verified, it would be another example of Hamas's brutality and depravity.
- John Kirby contrasts Hamas's disregard for the law of armed conflict with the U.S. expectation that Israel abide by those norms.
  #### Defense industry and plan B
  Kirby says the defense industry is a key partner in replenishing U.S. stocks and supporting Ukraine, and that the administration sees industry as a force multiplier. He notes ongoing work by Secretary Austin and Deputy Secretary Kathleen Hicks to shore up relationships with manufacturers and to coordinate with Ukraine about future needs. The Administration frames industrial cooperation as essential both for aiding Ukraine and for strengthening U.S. defense industrial base.
- John Kirby says the defense industry has been and will be a significant partner in expanding production lines and replenishing U.S. stocks.
- John Kirby says Secretary Austin and Deputy Secretary Hicks are working to shore up relationships between the U.S. and defense industry.
- John Kirby says the Commerce Department and U.S. partners are coordinating with Ukrainians to align industrial support with Ukraine's long-term needs.
  #### Domestic/political and procedural items
  On questions outside core national security operations, Kirby declines to weigh in on campaign rhetoric and private-sector mergers, and offers no immediate update on the President's NDAA signing. He reiterates that immigration and border-security negotiations with Congress are ongoing and that the President is willing to negotiate on policy and security. For private-sector M&A and the NDAA signing timeline, the Administration directs reporters to follow up or 'stay tuned.'
- John Kirby declines to weigh in on campaign rhetoric and says the National Security Council will not comment on campaign trail remarks.
- John Kirby says he cannot comment on reported private-sector activity such as Nippon Steel's acquisition and will not discuss private-sector transactions.
