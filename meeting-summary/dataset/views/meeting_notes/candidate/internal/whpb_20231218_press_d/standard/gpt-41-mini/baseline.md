### Detailed Summary

The U.S. highlights Israel's decision to open the Kerem Shalom border crossing for direct humanitarian aid delivery to Gaza, marking a significant development since the conflict began. Nearly 200 trucks entered Gaza recently, combining aid through Kerem Shalom and Rafah crossings. The U.S. continues efforts to alleviate civilian suffering while urging Israel to minimize civilian casualties through precise military operations.
- Mr. Kirby states Israel opened Kerem Shalom crossing for humanitarian aid, a step the U.S. had requested.

- The U.S. continues to push Israel to conduct operations surgically to prevent civilian casualties.
- Secretary Austin conveyed to Israeli officials the importance of minimizing civilian harm during military operations.

The administration urges Congress to act promptly to continue supporting Ukraine's defense against ongoing Russian attacks.
