### Overview
The Mountain View Visual Arts Committee convened to discuss key topics including future agenda items, updates on public art projects within the Capital Improvement Program, and the development of a comprehensive public art strategy. Staff provided progress reports on several park-related art installations, with committee members offering insights on artist selection timing and artwork integration. The public art strategy presentation outlined a five-year vision emphasizing diversity, community building, and economic development, with initiatives to refine policies and foster artist collaborations. The committee unanimously approved continuing virtual meetings under AB 361 provisions and adopted the 2023 meeting schedule. Additionally, they deliberated on the upcoming Call for Artists for the Centre for Performing Arts, favoring an open call to encourage broad participation. Closing remarks highlighted appreciation for the committee's engagement and plans for ongoing coordination on pedestrian mall projects.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Future Agenda Topics
The committee reviewed ongoing and upcoming agenda items, including public art strategy discussions, placement landscaping integrations, and placemaking opportunities related to pedestrian improvements. Members emphasized the need to update the K5 policy to better align with real capital improvement projects and discussed the potential for public art integration in the pedestrianization of Castro Street. Collaboration with the Downtown Committee and activation of vacant storefronts were also highlighted as opportunities to enhance public art visibility and community engagement.
- John Lang noted three key future agenda topics: public art strategy, placement landscaping integrations, and placemaking opportunities related to pedestrian improvements.
- Jesse Cupp emphasized updating the K5 policy to ensure public art applicability to various projects like bike lanes and ballparks.
- Clifford Bryant advocated for active involvement in the Castro Street pedestrianization project to showcase public art and engage the community.
- Regina Sakols and others discussed leveraging vacant storefronts for pop-up art events, especially during holidays, in collaboration with the Downtown Committee.
#### Capital Improvement Program Updates
Staff provided progress updates on several public art projects within the Capital Improvement Program, including Rangstorf Park Aquatic Center, Fayette Park, Rangsdorf Park Maintenance and Tennis Buildings, and Pyramid Park. The committee reflected on the recent dedication of Pyramid Park, discussing the scale and integration of public art within park environments. Concerns were raised about the timing of artist selection relative to park construction and the visibility and placement of artworks. Suggestions included more frequent check-ins during art creation and site visits to better understand spatial context.
- John Lang reported Rangsdorf Park Aquatic Center is 25% complete and on track for late fall 2023 opening.
- Fayette Park construction has started, and Rangsdorf Park maintenance building design is nearing completion with bids expected by year-end.
- Committee members expressed mixed reactions to Pyramid Park's public art, noting issues with artwork visibility and integration with park features.
- Clifford Bryant proposed adjusting the artist selection process to occur after park features are finalized to better integrate art with the environment.
- John Lang acknowledged insurance-related delays in artist contracts for Rangsdorf Park maintenance buildings and plans to bring mural concepts back to the committee.
#### Public Art Strategy Development
John Lang presented a high-level framework for the city's public art strategy, outlining vision, goals, policies, and initiatives. The vision focuses on creating diverse public art that enriches Mountain View's culture and community vibrancy. Goals include building community, placemaking, reflecting heritage, and economic development. Strategies involve refining city policies, encouraging art appreciation and placekeeping, and pursuing collaborations with artists and organizations. Committee members discussed the importance of diversity, artist support, exhibition spaces, and outreach. A five-year timeframe was proposed with periodic reviews to measure success and adjust goals.
- John Lang outlined the public art strategy funnel: vision, goals, policies, and initiatives.
- The draft vision emphasizes diverse public art enhancing Mountain View's innovative culture and community vibrancy.
- Committee members stressed incorporating diversity as a lens throughout the strategy rather than a separate goal.
- Initiatives include developing private art fees, improving wayfinding with art, creating public art funds, and enhancing jury processes for artist representation.
- Members highlighted the need for affordable studio and exhibition spaces to support local artists and discussed partnerships with schools and arts organizations.
- A five-year strategy horizon was proposed with measurable goals and a review process to assess progress and adapt as needed.
#### Virtual Meeting Authorization
The committee discussed continuing virtual meetings under AB 361 provisions, which allow remote meetings through November 1st. Members expressed a preference to keep options open for virtual participation regardless of City Council's decisions. A motion was made and passed unanimously to authorize virtual meetings for the upcoming period, ensuring flexibility and accessibility for committee members.
- John Lang explained AB 361 allows virtual meetings through November 1st and the committee can opt to continue meeting virtually.
- Jesse Cupp moved to adopt the resolution authorizing virtual meetings; the motion was seconded and passed by unanimous roll call vote.
- Members agreed maintaining virtual meeting options supports comfort and accessibility.
#### Centre for Performing Arts Call for Artists
The committee discussed themes and parameters for the upcoming 2023 Call for Artists for the Centre for Performing Arts (CPA) gallery display. Past themes included environmental sustainability, global culture, and disability awareness, with varying levels of artist participation. Members recommended an open call without a restrictive theme to encourage broader submissions and higher quality artwork. The call will include information about the city's public art strategy to engage artists. Geographic scope was suggested to remain broad within the Bay Area to attract diverse talent.
- Jesse Cupp noted themed calls had mixed success; environmental themes attracted more participation than disability-focused themes.
- Committee members favored an open call without a specific theme to maximize artist submissions and quality.
- John Lang proposed including a public service announcement about the city's public art strategy in the call to engage artists.
- The geographic scope for submissions will mirror previous calls, covering a generous Bay Area region.
- Members discussed the need for large, striking artworks suitable for the CPA gallery space.
#### Committee Meeting Schedule
The committee reviewed and adopted the proposed meeting schedule for the 2023 calendar year, with meetings planned for the second Wednesday of select months at 6:00 PM. No meetings are scheduled for January, July, or August. Members expressed no concerns and agreed to the schedule as presented.
- John Lang presented the proposed 2023 meeting schedule with meetings on the second Wednesday of February, March, April, May, June, September, October, November, and December.
- Committee members confirmed availability and had no objections to the proposed dates.
- A motion to adopt the schedule was made, seconded, and passed unanimously by roll call vote.

He committed to following up on the pedestrian mall ad hoc committee and related updates. Jesse Cupp complimented the organization of the public art strategy presentation. No additional questions or comments were raised, and the meeting was adjourned.
