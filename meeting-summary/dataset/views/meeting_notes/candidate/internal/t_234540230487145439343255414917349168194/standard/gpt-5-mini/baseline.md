### Overview
  The VAC convenes with Chair Jesse Cupp and staff lead John Lang and focuses on coordinating public art work across CIP projects, refining a draft public art strategy, and improving opportunities for local artists. The committee confirms three carryover agenda themes for November (public art strategy, placement/landscaping integrations, and placemaking tied to pedestrian/CIP projects) and receives Council‑level updates from John Lang on the Castro Street pedestrian mall and its new ad hoc Council committee. Staff reports project statuses (Rangsdorf Aquatic ~25% complete; Fayette Park under construction; Rangsdorf maintenance/tennis nearing bid with artist contract insurance issues) and committee members critique Pyramid Park artwork siting and recommend site visits, interim artist checkpoints, and tighter coordination between art and park construction. John presents a funnel‑style public art strategy (vision, four goals, policy recommendations, initiatives such as artist spaces, wayfinding, a public art fund, jury improvements, and an art inventory) and the group endorses a five‑year horizon with measurable milestones and stronger emphasis on local artist support (studio/exhibit space, micro‑grants, storefront activations, festivals, Chamber partnerships) while integrating diversity as a guiding lens. The VAC votes to adopt AB 361 authorization to continue virtual meetings if needed, approves an open Call for Artists for the CPA (no theme, Bay Area scope, 6–8 exhibits plus alternates), and adopts the 2023 meeting schedule; staff will carry forward the Committee’s edits and follow up on Castro pedestrian mall engagement and CPA call logistics.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Upcoming agenda topics
  The committee identified three carryover agenda items to return for deeper discussion in November: public art strategy development, placement/landscaping integrations, and placemaking opportunities tied to pedestrian and capital improvements. Members recommended updating the K5 policy to better capture real CIP project contexts and discussed leveraging the planned Castro pedestrian mall for visible, high‑impact public art and storefront activation. Staff committed to share information about the City Council ad hoc pedestrian mall committee and to coordinate opportunities for the VAC to provide consolidated recommendations to that group.
- John Lang says the committee will bring more work on the public art strategy back at the November meeting.
- John Lang says he will bring Bruce England back next meeting to discuss placement and landscaping integrations for public art.
- Jesse Cupp recommends updating the K5 policy so public art applicability better matches real CIP projects.
- Clifford Bryant urges the committee to actively engage with Castro pedestrianization as a prime opportunity for public art and storefront activation.
- Regina Sakols notes prior collaboration with the Downtown Committee and proposes repeating that coordination on a regular basis for holiday storefront activations and pop‑ups.
- John Lang reports City Council approved resolutions to permanently close Castro Street pending ordinance adoption and established a short‑term ad hoc committee to develop design standards, and he offers to share the ad hoc committee agenda with the VAC.
  #### CIP public art updates
  Staff provided status reports across multiple CIP public art projects and opened a detailed discussion about the recently completed Pyramid Park dedication and the process for integrating art into park construction. Projects reported: Rangsdorf Aquatic Center ~25% complete and on track for late‑fall 2023 opening, Fayette Park construction started, and Rangsdorf maintenance/tennis building design nearing bid with some artist contract insurance issues to resolve. Committee members raised concerns about artwork visibility, siting, and timing of artist selection in relation to park construction and recommended site visits and interim checkpoints with artists and public works.
- John Lang reports Rangsdorf Park Aquatic Center is approximately 25% complete and on track to open late fall 2023.
- John Lang reports Fayette Park construction has started and Rangsdorf maintenance and tennis building design is wrapping up with an anticipated bid before year‑end.

- Clifford Bryant recommends developing checkpoints during artist fabrication and possibly selecting artists later in the process so artists can respond to the built environment.
- John Lang proposes arranging site visits and bringing public works staff to future VAC meetings to improve coordination between art, playground equipment, and construction sequencing.
  #### Public art strategy
  Staff presented a funnel‑style public art strategy draft that begins with a vision and goals and moves through policy changes, initiatives, and partnership actions to implement the vision. The draft vision emphasizes diverse public art that enriches Mountain View's culture and four goals (building community, placemaking, reflecting heritage, economic development); proposed strategies include developing/adjusting policies (e.g., private art fee, K5 refinement), pursuing collaborations, and launching initiatives such as artist spaces, wayfinding art, a public art fund, jury improvements, and a public art inventory. The committee recommended a five‑year strategy horizon with measurable milestones, prioritized outreach to support local artists (studio/exhibition opportunities, micro‑grants, retail partnerships, festivals), and integration of diversity as a guiding lens.
- John Lang frames the strategy as a funnel and reiterates the draft vision that diverse public art enriches Mountain View's innovative and creative culture.
- John Lang says the committee identified four goals in earlier work: building community, placemaking, reflecting heritage, and economic development.
- John Lang proposes strategies including policy changes (fee for private art, K5 refinement), initiatives (artist spaces, wayfinding, public art fund), jury process improvements, and a public art inventory.
- Toni Hsu recommends treating the strategy as a five‑year plan with planned review and measurable milestones.
- Don Whitebread urges stronger emphasis on creating exhibition and sales venues so local artists can earn income rather than only commissioning major installations.
- Committee members including Jesse Cupp, Regina Sakols and Toot Too Thomson suggest many small actions—pop‑ups, storefront activations, festivals, Chamber and Business Improvement Area partnerships, and retail display programs—to build art exposure and markets for local artists.
- Clifford Bryant asks that diversity be reflected in the vision and flow through program goals, and Toni Hsu recommends applying diversity as a consistent lens across initiatives rather than as a separate goal.
- John Lang agrees to incorporate committee feedback, develop milestones for a five‑year timeline, and pursue more regular attendance by public works staff to improve coordination.

  #### CPA call for artists
  The committee discussed themes, scope, and logistics for the upcoming Centre for the Performing Arts (CPA) Call for Artists for the 2023 gallery season and reviewed past experiences with themed calls. Members observed that very specific themes (for example accessibility) reduced submissions and that many artists submit existing work rather than producing new pieces for a theme, so the committee favored an open call (no theme) to maximize submissions. Staff will issue an open call targeting Bay Area artists, aiming to secure six to eight exhibits (plus alternates), note medium/size constraints of the CPA space, and include a brief mention of the public art strategy for outreach.
- John Lang explains the CPA call historically selects 6–8 artists to exhibit for 6–8 weeks and asks whether the VAC wants a specific theme for 2023.
- Jesse Cupp reports themed calls sometimes reduce submissions because many artists submit existing portfolios rather than producing new pieces for narrow themes.
- Toni Hsu and several members recommend an open call (no theme) for 2023 to attract a larger pool of artists and make the CPA an accessible exhibition opportunity.
- Don Whitebread cautions that CPA exhibits need larger, visually striking works, and Jesse Cupp notes the call should communicate gallery constraints.
- John Lang will issue an open call in late October/November with Bay Area scope and include information about the city’s public art strategy to encourage future engagement.
