### Overview
The meeting of the Mountain View Arts Committee commenced with a roll call and the approval of minutes from the previous meeting. The committee discussed upcoming agenda topics, including the public art strategy and capital improvement project updates. John Lang provided insights into the ongoing development of the public art strategy, emphasizing community engagement and diversity. The committee reviewed the status of various capital projects, including the Rangsdorf Park Aquatic Center and Pyramid Park, and shared feedback on public art installations. A significant discussion revolved around the upcoming call for artists for the Centre for Performing Arts, where the consensus favored an open call without a specific theme to encourage broader participation. Finally, the committee adopted the proposed meeting schedule for 2023.

### Detailed Summary
#### Roll Call and Minutes Approval
The meeting begins with a roll call of committee members, confirming attendance. The minutes from the previous meeting on September 14, 2022, are approved unanimously, allowing the committee to proceed with the agenda.
- John Lang confirms attendance of all committee members.
- Don Whitebread motions to approve the minutes, seconded by Regina Skalos.
- The motion passes unanimously.

#### Public Communications
The committee opens the floor for public communications, but no members of the public are present to address the committee. This section is reserved for public input on non-agenda items, but the absence of attendees leads to a quick transition to the next agenda item.
- Jesse Cupp notes the absence of public attendees for comments.
- The committee moves on to upcoming agenda topics.

#### Upcoming Agenda Topics
John Lang outlines three key topics for future meetings: further discussions on the public art strategy, updates on landscaping integrations, and exploring placemaking opportunities. The committee is encouraged to suggest additional topics for future agendas.
- John Lang mentions the need for further work on the public art strategy in November.
- The committee is open to suggestions for future agenda items.

#### Public Art Strategy Discussion
The committee discusses the ongoing development of a public art strategy, emphasizing the importance of integrating public art into community spaces. John Lang shares insights from recent conversations and outlines the goals of building community, placemaking, reflecting heritage, and economic development.
- John Lang emphasizes the need for a robust public art strategy.
- The committee discusses the importance of community engagement in public art.

#### Capital Improvement Program Updates
Updates are provided on various capital improvement projects, including the status of the Rangsdorf Park Aquatic Center and Fayette Park. The committee discusses the completion of Pyramid Park and shares feedback on the public art installations within these projects.
- John Lang reports that the Rangsdorf Park project is 25% complete and on track for a late fall 2023 opening.
- Clifford Bryant shares feedback on the public art installation at Pyramid Park.

#### Public Art Strategy Feedback
The committee reviews the draft public art strategy, discussing the importance of diversity in art and the need for community engagement. Suggestions are made to refine the strategy to better reflect the community's diverse culture and heritage.
- Toni Hsu suggests incorporating diversity as a lens in the public art strategy.
- Clifford Bryant emphasizes the importance of reflecting the community's diversity in public art.

#### Call for Artists for CPA
The committee discusses the upcoming call for artists for the Centre for Performing Arts, considering whether to implement a theme for submissions. After deliberation, the consensus leans towards an open call to attract a wider range of artists and artworks.
- Jesse Cupp shares past experiences with themed calls for artists, noting mixed results.
- The committee agrees to keep the call open without a specific theme to encourage broader participation.

#### 2023 Meeting Schedule
The committee reviews and adopts the proposed meeting schedule for 2023, which includes meetings on the second Wednesday of each month from February to December, excluding January, July, and August.
- Jesse Cupp motions to adopt the proposed meeting schedule for 2023.
- The motion passes unanimously.
