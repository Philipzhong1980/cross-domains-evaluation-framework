### Overview
On Earth Day, National Climate Advisor Gina McCarthy and Special Presidential Envoy for Climate John Kerry presented the United States' ambitious commitment to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 50 to 52 percent below 2005 levels by 2030, reaffirming the country's leadership after rejoining the Paris Agreement. They highlighted the whole-of-government approach, sector-specific strategies, and the economic opportunities in clean energy job creation. Kerry emphasized the global momentum at the climate summit, noting commitments from major economies and financial institutions, while acknowledging the challenges ahead leading to the Glasgow summit. Addressing political concerns, both officials expressed confidence in executive actions and market forces driving the clean energy transition, supported by bipartisan endorsements. The administration also discussed climate finance efforts, the upcoming American Families Plan with targeted tax proposals, and the President's forthcoming Joint Address to Congress outlining key priorities. Additional topics included COVID-19 protocols within the White House, support for D.C. statehood, diplomatic engagement on Ukraine, and ongoing bipartisan infrastructure negotiations. The briefing underscored the administration's commitment to climate action, economic recovery, and legislative collaboration to advance its agenda.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### US Climate Commitment and NDC
National Climate Advisor Gina McCarthy detailed the United States' commitment to reduce net economy-wide greenhouse gas emissions by 50 to 52 percent below 2005 levels by 2030, as part of rejoining the Paris Agreement. This target reflects a whole-of-government effort involving sector-by-sector analysis and engagement with various stakeholders, emphasizing the economic opportunities in clean energy and job creation.
- Gina McCarthy explained the 2030 emissions target as a formal submission to the UN under the Paris Agreement.
- McCarthy highlighted the involvement of multiple sectors and the use of existing clean technologies to achieve the target.
- She emphasized the link between tackling climate change and creating good-paying union jobs.
- McCarthy noted endorsements from labor and business groups recognizing the plan's potential to invest in America.
#### Climate Summit and Global Cooperation
Special Presidential Envoy for Climate John Kerry discussed the global context of the climate summit, highlighting the restoration of US credibility after rejoining the Paris Agreement and the importance of multilateral cooperation. He noted commitments from major economies and financial institutions, underscoring the critical role of diplomacy leading up to the Glasgow summit.
- John Kerry praised Gina McCarthy's leadership in coordinating the US NDC preparation.
- Kerry emphasized the global market shift towards clean energy and the role of private sector investments.
- He cited commitments from countries like Japan, Canada, Korea, India, and the EU to strengthen emissions reduction targets.
- Kerry stressed the importance of the upcoming Glasgow summit as a last best hope for global climate action.
#### Political Challenges and Market Forces
In response to questions about political opposition, Kerry and McCarthy explained that much of the climate plan can be executed through executive actions and is supported by market trends and private sector commitments. They expressed confidence that the momentum in clean energy industries and consumer demand will sustain progress regardless of political changes.
- Kerry stated that many climate initiatives are executable through executive orders.
- McCarthy highlighted bipartisan support for clean energy tax credits and job creation potential.
- Kerry pointed to the rapid growth in jobs like wind turbine technicians and solar panel installers as evidence of market-driven change.
- Both emphasized that the private sector's movement towards electric vehicles and clean technologies is irreversible.
#### International Relations and Diplomacy
Kerry addressed criticisms from leaders like Putin and Bolsonaro regarding historical emissions and discussed ongoing diplomatic efforts with Russia, China, and other countries. He highlighted the use of satellite technology for real-time emissions tracking and the importance of accountability in international climate commitments.
- Kerry noted ongoing conversations with Russia and the potential for future diplomatic engagement.
- He described Putin's remarks as rational and open to cooperation.
- Kerry mentioned the Trace Program's satellite capacity to monitor emissions globally.
- He acknowledged challenges but expressed hope for common ground with major emitters.
#### Climate Finance and Congressional Support
The administration discussed plans to secure funding for climate initiatives, including a $1.25 billion request for the Green Climate Fund, and the importance of working with Congress to advance infrastructure and climate-related legislation. They emphasized the need for bipartisan cooperation and multiple pathways to achieve emissions targets.
- Psaki confirmed the administration's intent to work with Congress on climate finance appropriations.
- She described the American Jobs Plan as a key component for infrastructure and clean energy investments.
- Psaki welcomed Republican proposals as a starting point for bipartisan discussions.
- The administration aims to pass the American Jobs Plan into law by summer, ahead of the Glasgow summit.
#### American Families Plan and Tax Policy
Jen Psaki outlined the upcoming American Families Plan, focusing on investments in child care, education, and economic security, with funding proposed through tax increases on the wealthiest Americans and corporations. The President remains committed to not raising taxes on individuals earning under $400,000 annually.
- Psaki stated the plan details will be unveiled in the President's Joint Address to Congress on April 28.
- She confirmed the tax increases would target individuals making over $400,000 per year.
- The administration is finalizing payfors and expects the plan to include historic investments in families and workers.
- Psaki emphasized the President's commitment to middle-class priorities and economic security.
#### Impact on American Workers and Economy
The discussion highlighted the economic benefits of the climate plan, including job creation in emerging clean energy sectors and infrastructure development. Officials stressed that the transition to clean energy is already underway in the private sector and that government investment will support sustainable growth and competitiveness.
- McCarthy and Kerry emphasized the creation of millions of good-paying jobs in clean energy industries.
- Kerry cited Bureau of Labor Statistics data showing rapid growth in renewable energy jobs.
- Officials noted that government incentives complement private sector innovation and market trends.
- Psaki addressed concerns about the economic impact, affirming the plan supports workers and families.
#### President Biden's Upcoming Joint Address
Psaki provided insights into the President's upcoming speech to Congress, which will focus on the American Families Plan, healthcare, police reform, and other priorities. The address aims to communicate directly with the American people and Congress, setting the agenda for the administration's next steps.
- The speech is scheduled for April 28 and will outline key policy proposals.
- Psaki noted the address will not cover every issue but will highlight major priorities.
- The President values the opportunity to speak from the Capitol and engage with Congress.
- The administration is finalizing the content to balance detail and accessibility.
#### COVID-19 and White House Operations
Psaki addressed questions about vaccination status among White House staff, mask guidance, and the President's personal time. She emphasized adherence to health expert recommendations and noted ongoing efforts to maintain a safe working environment.
- Vaccination of White House staff, especially those in close contact with the President, is a priority.
- Mask-wearing and other precautions remain in place following health guidance.
- The President spends personal time with family and enjoys movies and pets.
- Decisions on mask-wearing will follow medical expert advice.
#### Other Policy Issues and Congressional Relations
The briefing touched on various topics including D.C. statehood support, the situation in Ukraine, and the administration's approach to bipartisan negotiations. The White House expressed commitment to working with Congress and addressing diverse policy challenges.
- President Biden strongly supports D.C. statehood and advocates for its passage.
- The administration is monitoring troop movements near Ukraine and engaging diplomatically with Russia.
- Psaki emphasized openness to bipartisan infrastructure negotiations and multiple legislative pathways.
- The administration continues to address immigration and humanitarian assistance in Central America.
