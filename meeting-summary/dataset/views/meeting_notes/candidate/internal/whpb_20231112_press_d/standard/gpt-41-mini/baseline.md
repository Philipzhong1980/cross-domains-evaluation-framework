### Overview
Senior administration officials provide a comprehensive preview of the upcoming bilateral meeting between President Biden and Indonesian President Widodo, highlighting its significance as a precursor to the APEC summit and a step toward elevating U.S.-Indonesia relations to a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. Key discussion topics include cooperation on critical minerals and technology, expanded climate change initiatives, and enhanced security and defense collaboration focusing on maritime domain awareness and cybersecurity. The meeting will also launch important people-to-people programs, such as Georgetown University's new campus in Jakarta and restoration efforts for Indonesia's National Museum. Officials emphasize Indonesia's influential role in ASEAN and its critical perspectives on global issues like the Middle East conflict and the Myanmar crisis, with the U.S. seeking Indonesia's increased practical engagement. This bilateral engagement is part of a broader U.S. strategy to strengthen alliances in the Indo-Pacific region ahead of President Biden's meeting with President Xi, aiming to foster regional stability and cooperative dialogue.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Bilateral Meeting Preview
Senior administration officials preview the upcoming bilateral meeting between President Biden and President Widodo of Indonesia, emphasizing its significance ahead of the APEC summit. The meeting aims to discuss a broad range of issues including foreign policy, national security, and regional cooperation, reflecting the strong and warm relationship between the two leaders.
- Senior administration official highlights the importance of the meeting as a precursor to APEC and President Biden's meeting with President Xi.
- The officials note Indonesia's key role in ASEAN and its influence on regional issues like the South China Sea and Myanmar.
- The meeting will include a private Oval Office discussion followed by a larger bilateral session in the Cabinet Room.
#### Comprehensive Strategic Partnership
The United States plans to elevate its relationship with Indonesia to a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership, Indonesia's highest partnership tier. This move builds on recent similar partnerships with other Southeast Asian nations and aims to deepen cooperation across multiple sectors including security, technology, and climate change.
- Senior administration official 2 announces the upcoming Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Indonesia.
- The partnership underscores Indonesia's leadership role in ASEAN and the importance of bolstering ASEAN centrality.
- Officials emphasize the partnership as a continuation of deepening U.S. engagement in Southeast Asia.
#### Critical Minerals and Technology Cooperation
Discussions will focus on initiating a work plan for cooperation on critical minerals essential for 21st-century technologies, including semiconductors and batteries. While still in early stages, both sides express ambition to develop a consequential partnership that meets high labor and environmental standards.
- Senior administration officials acknowledge the early phase of critical minerals cooperation discussions.
- Indonesia's vast supply of critical resources is recognized as vital for future economies.
- A work plan will be established to prepare both countries for potential agreements, emphasizing labor and environmental standards.
#### Climate Change Initiatives
The meeting will announce new joint initiatives to combat the climate crisis, building on an existing energy transition partnership. These initiatives include carbon capture and storage, support for Indonesia's electricity grid, air quality improvement, and enhanced climate cooperation through ASEAN.
- Officials highlight the expansion of climate cooperation efforts between the U.S. and Indonesia.
- New initiatives will address carbon capture, electricity grid support, and air quality.
- Climate collaboration will also be strengthened within the ASEAN framework.
#### Security and Defense Cooperation
The U.S. and Indonesia plan to expand their defense relationship through increased military exercises, cybersecurity cooperation, and combating weapons of mass destruction. While no specific defense announcements are expected immediately, ongoing procurement and capability enhancements, especially in maritime domain awareness, are key focus areas.
- Senior administration official 2 outlines plans for broader defense cooperation including exercises and cybersecurity.
- Indonesia is focusing on maritime domain awareness capabilities such as radars and patrol boats.
- U.S. defense firms are actively engaged in Indonesia's procurement processes.
- Officials note a reduction in Jakarta's previous ambivalence towards U.S. defense engagement, influenced by regional security concerns.
#### People-to-People and Educational Initiatives
The U.S. and Indonesia will launch significant people-to-people programs, including the establishment of the first American university campus in Indonesia through Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service. Additionally, collaboration will focus on restoring Indonesia's National Museum, damaged by fire, with U.S. expert assistance.
- Officials announce the Georgetown University satellite program in Jakarta to educate future policymakers.
- This will be the first American university campus in Indonesia and second international university overall.
- U.S. experts will assist in restoring Indonesia's National Museum following a devastating fire.
#### Indonesia's Role in Middle East and Global Issues
Indonesia's perspectives on the Middle East conflict, particularly Gaza, are considered critical due to its status as the largest Islamic country and a major democracy. The U.S. seeks Indonesia's increased practical engagement and leadership in global issues, including a just peace and reconstruction efforts.
- Officials emphasize the importance of Indonesia's views on the Middle East and Gaza conflict.
- President Biden intends to listen to President Widodo's insights from recent Middle East engagements.
- The U.S. welcomes Indonesia's aspiration to move beyond voicing concerns to practical involvement in global issues.
#### Myanmar Situation and ASEAN Leadership
The deteriorating security situation in Myanmar is a key topic, with Indonesia playing a leading role in ASEAN's approach. The U.S. supports ASEAN's engagement efforts but acknowledges limited progress, highlighting the need for new collaborative strategies to address the ongoing crisis.
- Officials recognize Indonesia's critical leadership role on Myanmar within ASEAN.
- The ASEAN troika will maintain collective leadership on Myanmar issues following Indonesia's chairmanship.
- The U.S. expresses concern over Myanmar's negative trajectory and the need for joint next steps.
#### Preparation for President Xi Meeting at APEC
The bilateral meeting with Indonesia is part of a broader strategy to strengthen U.S. alliances and partnerships in the Indo-Pacific ahead of President Biden's meeting with President Xi at APEC. The U.S. aims to present a united front on shared challenges while supporting regional communication and avoiding exclusionary dynamics.
- Officials frame the Indonesia meeting as one element of extensive Indo-Pacific diplomatic efforts.
- The U.S. seeks to reassure partners and allies of its commitment and strength in the region.
- ASEAN countries desire closer U.S.-China communication without sidelining regional actors.
