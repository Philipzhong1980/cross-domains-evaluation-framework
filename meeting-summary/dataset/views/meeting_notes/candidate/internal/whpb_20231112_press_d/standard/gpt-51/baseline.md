### Overview
Senior administration officials brief reporters on President Widodo’s visit to Washington as a pivotal moment to elevate U.S.–Indonesia relations, previewing the announcement of a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership ahead of the 75th anniversary of diplomatic ties and in the run-up to APEC and President Biden’s meeting with President Xi. They describe a broad agenda covering regional security issues such as the South China Sea, China’s growing maritime activity near the Natuna Islands, and the deteriorating crisis in Myanmar, where Indonesia will retain a leadership role through an ASEAN troika, as well as global challenges including the war in Gaza and Ukraine, with President Biden expected to listen closely to Widodo’s post-Riyadh perspectives and explore how a “global Indonesia” can contribute more practically. The officials outline economic and technological cooperation on critical minerals—anchored in a new, standards-based work plan—semiconductor diversification, and an expanded climate package building on the Just Energy Transition Partnership, alongside deepening defense cooperation through expanded exercises, cybersecurity and WMD training, Coast Guard collaboration, and future procurement decisions to enhance Indonesia’s maritime domain awareness. They also highlight people-to-people and cultural initiatives, notably a Georgetown University School of Foreign Service satellite campus in Jakarta and U.S. support to restore Indonesia’s National Museum after a major fire, and stress that the visit reflects a whole-of-government effort that strengthens U.S. alliances and partnerships in Southeast Asia as part of a deliberate strategy to approach engagement with China from a position of diplomatic and economic strength while preserving ASEAN centrality and partner agency.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Purpose and context of visit
Senior administration officials outline President Widodo’s visit to Washington as the informal kickoff to a consequential Indo-Pacific and APEC-focused week, including President Biden’s upcoming meeting with President Xi. They emphasize that this may be one of the last Biden–Widodo meetings before Indonesia’s presidential campaign begins. The U.S. frames Indonesia as a central Indo-Pacific partner with a critical role in ASEAN and broader regional diplomacy. The visit includes an Oval Office meeting, small-group discussions, and a larger Cabinet Room bilateral session.
- A senior administration official states that President Widodo’s visit to Washington will occur just before APEC and President Biden’s bilateral with President Xi in San Francisco.
- A senior administration official notes that this is likely one of the last meetings between President Biden and President Widodo before Indonesia’s presidential campaign begins.
- A senior administration official explains that the program will include a private Oval Office meeting with a small group of aides followed by a larger bilateral session over high tea in the Cabinet Room.
- A senior administration official stresses that the U.S. views Indonesia as having an absolutely central and critical role in ASEAN and the wider Indo-Pacific.
#### Foreign policy and security agenda
Officials preview that the leaders will discuss major foreign policy issues, including the South China Sea, China’s regional activities, ASEAN’s role, and the crisis in Myanmar. They highlight Indonesia’s key perspectives on regional security and its efforts to foster dialogue on Burma/Myanmar within ASEAN. The U.S. also expects substantive discussion of the conflict in the Middle East, particularly Gaza, and Indonesia’s potential to play a larger role given its status as the world’s largest Muslim-majority democracy. These conversations aim to align approaches and explore where Indonesia can contribute practically beyond voicing concerns.
- A senior administration official says the leaders will review Indonesia’s perspectives on the South China Sea and China’s diplomacy and engagement in the region.
- A senior administration official notes Indonesia’s key role in ASEAN efforts to develop dialogue with Burma/Myanmar.
- A senior administration official states that the U.S. wants to hear Indonesia’s views on the ongoing conflict in the Middle East, including Gaza, given Indonesia’s status as the largest Islamic country and a major democracy.
- A senior administration official indicates that President Biden will ask Indonesia to play a larger role in addressing developments in the Middle East.
- A senior administration official later adds that Indonesia is increasingly interested in practical engagements on Middle East and global issues, not just providing a voice.
#### Comprehensive Strategic Partnership upgrade
The U.S. and Indonesia plan to announce a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership, Indonesia’s highest partnership tier, timed with the approach of the 75th anniversary of diplomatic relations. Senior officials present this as a major elevation of ties that builds on the recent U.S. Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Vietnam and broader engagement in Southeast Asia. The upgrade aims to formalize expanded cooperation across security, economic, climate, and people-to-people domains. It also reinforces ASEAN centrality and Indonesia’s leadership role within the grouping.
- Senior administration official 2 announces that the U.S. and Indonesia will elevate their relationship to a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership, Indonesia’s highest partnership tier.
- Senior administration official 2 notes that the upgrade comes as the two countries approach 75 years of diplomatic relations.
- Senior administration official 2 links this upgrade to the recent Comprehensive Strategic Partnership with Vietnam as part of deeper U.S. engagement in Southeast Asia.
- A senior administration official emphasizes that Indonesia is a critical leader in ASEAN and that the partnership will include joint work to bolster ASEAN centrality.
#### Economic and technology cooperation
Officials describe early-stage discussions on a “future pathway” for cooperation on critical minerals, reflecting Indonesia’s large resource base and its economic ambitions. They stress that while no full agreement is ready, both sides want to establish a structured process—referred to as a “work plan”—to explore a critical minerals agreement aligned with high labor and environmental standards. The U.S. also seeks to support Indonesia’s goal of moving up the technology and manufacturing value chain, including diversifying its semiconductor ecosystem. These initiatives are framed as vital for 21st-century industries such as batteries, electric vehicles, and semiconductors.
- A senior administration official notes that the U.S. and Indonesia are at very early stages of discussion on critical minerals and do not yet have a concrete announcement.
- A senior administration official explains that Indonesia has a vast supply of critical resources essential for 21st-century economies, including batteries, EVs, and semiconductors.
- Senior administration official 2 says the leaders will announce movement on a jointly agreed ‘work plan’ to define a pathway toward a possible critical minerals agreement.
- Senior administration official 2 emphasizes that any critical minerals cooperation must comply with the highest labor and environmental standards.
- A senior administration official highlights Indonesian ambitions to move up the ladder in technology, manufacturing, and fabrication, and says the U.S. is engaged on these technology issues.
- Senior administration official 2 states that the U.S. is looking closely at ways to cooperate with Indonesia to diversify its semiconductor ecosystem, which is a priority for President Jokowi.
#### Climate and energy collaboration
The meeting will feature several climate-related deliverables that build on the existing Just Energy Transition Partnership (JETP). Officials preview new initiatives on carbon capture and storage, support for Indonesia’s electricity grid, air quality improvements, and expanded climate cooperation through ASEAN. These efforts aim to strengthen Indonesia’s energy transition and position it as a regional leader on climate policy. The U.S. presents this climate package as a core pillar of the upgraded partnership.
- Senior administration official 2 announces that the U.S. and Indonesia will roll out new initiatives that build on their Just Energy Transition Partnership.
- Senior administration official 2 details that these initiatives will include work on carbon capture and storage, support for Indonesia’s electricity grid, and air quality improvements.
- Senior administration official 2 notes that the two countries will enhance climate cooperation through ASEAN as part of the broader regional agenda.
- A senior administration official earlier confirms that climate change is a key topic for the bilateral discussions alongside education and technology.
#### Security and defense cooperation
Officials preview a broad expansion of defense and security ties but clarify that no single major defense announcement is expected at this meeting. They describe progress toward a strengthened defense cooperation arrangement, expanded exercises, deeper training in cybersecurity and WMD countering, and new Coast Guard cooperation. The U.S. notes Indonesia’s growing interest in maritime domain awareness and capabilities, including radars, patrol vessels, and more advanced aircraft, in light of Chinese activities near the Natuna Islands and South China Sea. Secretary Austin is expected to carry forward many of these defense-related deliverables in upcoming regional meetings.
- Senior administration official 2 says the U.S. will signal substantial progress toward growing the defense relationship, including expanded exercises and deeper training in cybersecurity and combating WMD.
- Senior administration official 2 notes that new security initiatives will presage a strengthened defense cooperation arrangement and launch new Coast Guard cooperation and closer cybersecurity partnerships.
- Senior administration official 2 clarifies that there will not be a specific high-profile defense announcement like the B-52 deployment but rather a broad expansion of day-to-day defense cooperation.
- A senior administration official explains that Indonesia is increasingly focused on maritime domain awareness and capabilities such as radars, patrol boats, and advanced aircraft.
- A senior administration official points out that significant Indonesian defense procurement decisions are looming and that U.S. defense firms are actively engaged in those campaigns.
- A senior administration official says growing Chinese patrols near the Natuna Islands and broader South China Sea activity have reduced past ambivalence in Jakarta about closer defense ties with the United States.
- A senior administration official notes that Secretary Austin will advance defense cooperation with his Indonesian counterpart at ADMM-Plus, with some deliverables left for that venue.
#### People-to-people and educational initiatives
The U.S. and Indonesia will launch new people-to-people initiatives, with a flagship project involving Georgetown University’s School of Foreign Service establishing a satellite program in Jakarta. This will represent the first American university campus in Indonesia and only the second international university there, aimed at training current and future policymakers from both countries. Officials frame this as a strategic investment to align future policy communities and deepen societal ties. Additional efforts include support for restoring Indonesia’s National Museum after a major fire, symbolizing cultural solidarity and technical cooperation.
- Senior administration official 2 announces that Indonesia will partner with Georgetown University’s School of Foreign Service to create a satellite program in Jakarta offering degree programs for American and Indonesian policymakers.
- Senior administration official 2 notes that the Georgetown initiative will be the first American university campus and only the second international university campus in Indonesia.
- Senior administration official 2 describes the Georgetown campus as a critical bridge between U.S. and Indonesian societies and a way to ensure future policymakers’ views are increasingly aligned.
- Senior administration official 2 states that the U.S. will partner with Indonesia to help restore the National Museum in Jakarta, which suffered a devastating fire in September.
- Senior administration official 2 explains that U.S. experts will work with Indonesian experts to provide training and restoration services for the museum as a national landmark.
- A senior administration official earlier underscores the importance of expanding educational opportunities for Indonesians and increasing U.S. campus engagement in-country.
#### Indonesia’s regional and global diplomatic role
Officials repeatedly emphasize Indonesia’s role as a central player in ASEAN and as an increasingly “global Indonesia” that engages on issues beyond its immediate region. They highlight Indonesia’s recent ASEAN chairmanship and its continued leadership via an ASEAN “troika” mechanism on Myanmar. The U.S. welcomes Indonesia’s outspoken stance on conflicts such as Ukraine and the Middle East and wants to translate that activism into practical joint initiatives. This framing positions Indonesia as a key partner in sustaining ASEAN centrality and addressing global security challenges.
- Senior administration official 2 underscores that Indonesia is a critical leader in ASEAN and recently served as ASEAN chair.
- Senior administration official 2 says the visit will include discussions on how to bolster ASEAN centrality and collaborate on ASEAN-priority projects.
- A senior administration official describes Indonesia as increasingly acting as a ‘global Indonesia’ with a voice on issues like Ukraine and the Middle East.
- A senior administration official states that the U.S. welcomes Indonesia’s desire to go beyond statements and engage in practical initiatives on global issues.
- Senior administration official 2 explains that as ASEAN chair passes from Indonesia to Laos, ASEAN will maintain collective leadership on Myanmar through a troika in which Indonesia will continue to play a leading role.
#### Middle East conflict and ceasefire debates
In response to questions, officials acknowledge that President Widodo is arriving from an Islamic summit in Riyadh and will likely press for a Gaza ceasefire. They say President Biden intends to listen carefully to Widodo’s readout from Saudi and other regional leaders and treat Indonesia’s views as important in shaping U.S. thinking. While avoiding commitments on specific outcomes, U.S. officials anticipate that talks will cover immediate ceasefire concerns as well as medium- and long-term goals, including a just peace, a two-state solution, and reconstruction support. They present this as an opportunity to involve Indo-Pacific partners more deeply in addressing global crises.
- A senior administration official notes that Indonesia’s views on Gaza and the broader Middle East are very important for how the U.S. thinks about its next steps.
- A senior administration official says President Biden will listen carefully to President Widodo’s perspectives from discussions in Saudi Arabia and with other Middle East leaders.
- A senior administration official indicates that the conversation will span immediate ceasefire issues and longer-term goals such as a just peace, a two-state solution, and substantial resources to rebuild shattered lives.
- A senior administration official stresses that the U.S. has sought to ensure an active Indo-Pacific role in addressing global crises like Ukraine and now the Middle East, and sees Indonesia as central to that effort.
#### Critical minerals work plan and standards
Officials elaborate on the planned “work plan” for a potential critical minerals agreement with Indonesia. They describe it as an agreed pathway to determine what is feasible, prepare both systems institutionally and legally, and align expectations on both sides. The work plan will explicitly embed high labor and environmental standards in line with broader U.S. trade and economic policy. This staged approach underscores that while both governments are ambitious, they are still at the first phase of structured negotiations.
- Senior administration official 2 characterizes the upcoming announcement as a joint ‘work plan’ to explore what is possible by way of a critical minerals agreement.
- Senior administration official 2 explains that the work plan is meant to ready both countries’ systems to move forward together on critical minerals cooperation.
- Senior administration official 2 emphasizes that the work plan will ensure any future agreement aligns with the highest labor and environmental standards.
- A senior administration official notes that the U.S. and Indonesia must first exchange expectations and clarify what each side seeks before negotiating concrete arrangements.
- Senior administration official 2 reiterates that the leaders are at the very first phase of this process, and their discussion will focus on setting this pathway.
#### Meeting logistics and whole-of-government engagement
Officials briefly address timing and format details and stress that the visit reflects a whole-of-government U.S. effort. The leaders will meet privately in the Oval Office before moving to a larger high tea in the Cabinet Room involving key agencies. The White House underscores that actors across the U.S. government—USAID, the climate team, State, Commerce, Treasury, and others—maintain deep ties with Indonesia. This framing signals institutional depth and durability in the relationship beyond any single administration or department.

#### Indonesia’s role in Biden–Xi meeting context
When asked about the link between the Widodo visit and the upcoming Biden–Xi meeting, officials say this engagement should be seen as part of a multi-year strategy to strengthen alliances and partnerships before high-level U.S.–China talks. They highlight recent steps with Vietnam, India, Japan, and Australia as elements of a broader approach to present a unified front on shared challenges while avoiding a G2 dynamic. They note that regional partners want robust U.S.–China communication but also insist on having their own voices heard. Indonesia’s role as a leading Southeast Asian partner reinforces the U.S. posture of entering APEC from a position of economic and diplomatic strength.
- A senior administration official says the Widodo visit is part of a years-long effort to build stronger partnerships with allies and partners in the Indo-Pacific and Europe before engaging China.
- A senior administration official cites the upgraded partnership with Vietnam, recent outreach to India, the trilateral summit with Japan and South Korea at Camp David, and the Australian state visit as part of this broader effort.
- A senior administration official explains that partners want closer U.S.–China communication to reduce conflict risks but do not want a G2 in which Washington and Beijing make regional decisions without them.
- Senior administration official 2 stresses that President Biden will go to APEC with a historically strong U.S. economy and a historically strong alliance and partnership network in the Indo-Pacific.
- Senior administration official 2 describes Indonesia as a critical piece of the regional puzzle, especially in Southeast Asia, contributing to this position of strength.
#### Myanmar crisis and ASEAN troika
In response to questions, officials confirm that the deteriorating security situation in Myanmar/Burma will be a key topic for Biden and Widodo. The U.S. expresses disappointment at the limited progress of ASEAN’s efforts to implement its five-point consensus and concerns about an ongoing negative spiral inside Myanmar. They suggest it may soon be necessary to consider new joint steps because the situation is untenable. Indonesia’s continuing leadership on Myanmar is underscored through its role in an ASEAN troika mechanism, which will preserve Jakarta’s influence on the file even as the ASEAN chair shifts to Laos.
- A senior administration official identifies Myanmar’s worsening security environment as an issue of critical mutual interest for the United States and Indonesia.
- A senior administration official says the U.S. has supported ASEAN’s engagement with Myanmar’s leadership but remains discouraged by the lack of progress and the continuing negative spiral inside the country.
- A senior administration official indicates that it will soon be time for the U.S. and Indonesia to consider next steps together to address what they describe as an untenable situation in Myanmar.
- Senior administration official 2 explains that as ASEAN chair passes to Laos, ASEAN will create a troika in which Indonesia will remain a key leader on the Myanmar issue.
- Senior administration official 2 notes that this enduring Indonesian role is one reason the U.S. is focused on a substantive Biden–Widodo conversation about Myanmar.
