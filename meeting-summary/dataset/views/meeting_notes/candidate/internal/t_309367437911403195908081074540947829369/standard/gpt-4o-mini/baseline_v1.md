### Overview
The Council Policy and Procedures Committee convened on December 15th to discuss several key topics, including the approval of previous meeting minutes, public comments regarding the Advisory Body Handbook, and proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct. The committee addressed concerns about compliance with Form 700 filings among advisory body members, particularly the Design Review Committee, and discussed the handling of absences for committee members. Additionally, the committee reviewed its work plan and considered future agenda items, including the need for clearer policies regarding private developers using public land. Key participants included Chair Lisa Matichak, committee members Lucas Ramirez and Alison Hicks, and staff members Heather Glaser and Kimbra McCarthy.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting held on September 28, 2022. This is a standard procedural item that ensures all members are aligned on past discussions and decisions. The minutes are approved unanimously after a motion and second from committee members.
- Lucas Ramirez moves to approve the minutes from September 28, 2022.
- Alison Hicks seconds the motion for approval.
- The motion passes unanimously with all members voting in favor.

#### Public Comments on Non-Agenda Items
The committee opens the floor for public comments on items not listed on the agenda. Mr. England raises concerns about the clarity of the path forward for issues he previously mentioned, particularly regarding the Advisory Body Handbook update. This highlights the importance of community engagement and transparency in the committee's processes.
- Mr. England expresses confusion about the status of issues he raised previously.
- He emphasizes the need for clarity on the Advisory Body Handbook update.
- Lisa Matichak suggests Mr. England email his concerns to the City Council for further action.

#### Amendments to Council Code of Conduct
The committee reviews proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2, which includes provisions for advisory body members who fail to file required forms. The discussion centers on ensuring compliance and the implications of these changes for advisory body participation.
- Heather Glaser summarizes the proposed amendments, including suspension of members who fail to file Form 700.
- Lucas Ramirez questions the application deadline for advisory body applications and suggests a more flexible approach.
- The committee agrees on the need for a defined application deadline to ensure proper processing.

#### Discussion on Absences and Excused Status
The committee discusses the handling of absences for advisory body members, particularly the process for determining whether absences are excused or unexcused. This is crucial for maintaining accountability and ensuring that advisory bodies function effectively.
- Lucas Ramirez raises concerns about the inconsistency in handling absences across different advisory bodies.
- Heather Glaser clarifies that each advisory body can determine absence status by majority vote.
- Lisa Matichak notes the need for a common understanding of absence policies across all bodies.

#### Concerns Regarding DRC Compliance
The committee addresses concerns about compliance with Form 700 filings among members of the Design Review Committee (DRC). This discussion underscores the challenges faced in ensuring that all advisory body members adhere to legal requirements.
- Kimbra McCarthy flags potential challenges with DRC members filing Form 700 due to their professional backgrounds.
- Alison Hicks expresses concern about the implications of these compliance issues for the DRC's future.
- Lucas Ramirez emphasizes the importance of transparency regarding financial conflicts of interest.

#### Work Plan Updates and Future Items
The committee reviews its work plan and discusses potential future agenda items, including the need for clearer policies regarding private developers using public land. This reflects the committee's ongoing commitment to transparency and effective governance.
- Kimberly Thomas provides an update on the committee's action plan and accomplishments to date.
- Lucas Ramirez suggests a need for a policy on private developers using public land for transparency.
- Kimbra McCarthy agrees to prepare a memo for the council outlining the current decision-making process regarding public land use.
