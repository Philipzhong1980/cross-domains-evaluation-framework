### Overview
The Council Policy and Procedures Committee meeting on December 15th focused on several key topics, including the approval of previous meeting minutes, public communications regarding the Advisory Body Handbook, and proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct concerning Form 700 compliance. City Manager Kimbra McCarthy highlighted challenges faced by the Design Review Committee in meeting compliance requirements, prompting discussions on potential solutions. The committee also reviewed updates to their work plan and considered the addition of a new item regarding the process for private developers using public land. Public input was received, particularly concerning the clarity of residency requirements in the Advisory Body Handbook.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting held on September 28, 2022. This is a standard procedure to ensure that all members agree on the recorded actions and discussions. The minutes are approved unanimously after a motion and second from committee members.
- Committee member Ramirez moves to approve the minutes.
- Committee member Hicks seconds the motion.
- The minutes pass unanimously with all members voting in favor.

#### Public Communications
During the oral communications segment, Mr. England raises concerns about the clarity of the open public comments from the previous meeting's minutes. He seeks guidance on how to proceed with the issues he previously raised, particularly regarding the Advisory Body Handbook update.
- Mr. England expresses confusion about the path forward on issues he raised previously.
- Chair Matichak suggests Mr. England email his concerns to the City Council for further action.

#### Amendments to Council Code of Conduct
Heather Glaser presents proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct, specifically addressing the recourse for advisory body members who fail to file Form 700. The recommendation includes suspending participation until compliance is achieved, which aims to ensure accountability among committee members.
- Heather Glaser outlines the proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct.
- Committee member Ramirez asks about the application deadline for advisory body positions.
- Glaser recommends setting a deadline close to interview dates for better staff preparation.

#### Discussion on Absences and Excuses
The committee discusses the handling of absences for advisory body members, including how to classify them as excused or unexcused. There is a recognition of the need for a consistent approach across different advisory bodies to avoid confusion.
- Committee member Ramirez raises concerns about the inconsistency in handling absences.
- Heather Glaser explains that each advisory body can determine absence status by majority vote.

#### Concerns about DRC Compliance
City Manager Kimbra McCarthy highlights challenges faced by the Design Review Committee (DRC) regarding compliance with Form 700 filings. The committee discusses potential solutions to ensure that qualified members can continue serving without hindrance.
- Kimbra McCarthy notes difficulties in finding members willing to file Form 700 due to privacy concerns.
- Committee member Ramirez emphasizes the importance of compliance for transparency in decision-making.

#### Work Plan Updates
Kimberly Thomas provides an update on the committee's work plan, noting accomplishments and upcoming items. The committee discusses the potential addition of a new item regarding the process for private developers using public land.
- Kimberly Thomas reviews the completed items on the work plan.
- Committee member Ramirez suggests a memo to clarify the process for developers using public land.

#### Public Input on Advisory Body Handbook
Mr. England raises a concern regarding the Advisory Body Handbook's clarity on residency requirements for committee members. He suggests that this issue should be addressed to ensure consistency and transparency in the handbook.
- Mr. England points out inconsistencies in the residency requirements for advisory body members.
- Chair Matichak encourages Mr. England to email his concerns for further clarification.
