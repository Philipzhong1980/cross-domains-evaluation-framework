### Overview
The Council Policy and Procedures Committee convened to approve prior meeting minutes, discuss public comments, and consider amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2. Key participants included Chair Lisa Matichak, committee members Alison Hicks and Lucas Ramirez, City Manager Kimbra McCarthy, and staff Heather Glaser and Kimberly Thomas. The committee unanimously approved the September 2022 minutes after public input from Mr. England, who sought clarity on unresolved issues and the Advisory Body Handbook. The committee then deliberated on policy amendments to enforce Form 700 filing compliance among advisory body members, including suspension for non-filers, with particular attention to challenges faced by the Design Review Commission due to its architect members' reluctance to file disclosures. While committee member Hicks expressed concern about potential impacts on the DRC, the amendments passed with majority support. Staff updated the committee on the work plan and entertained a proposal from committee member Ramirez to address transparency around private developers' use of public land. City Manager McCarthy recommended an off-agenda memo to outline current processes before considering formal policy development. Public comments highlighted inconsistencies in the Advisory Body Handbook's residency requirements, prompting a commitment to follow up via email correspondence. The meeting concluded with consensus on next steps to enhance policy clarity and compliance.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of September Minutes
The committee reviewed and approved the minutes from the September 28th, 2022 meeting without any amendments or objections. Public comment was received from Mr. England, who raised concerns about the follow-up process for issues he previously highlighted, seeking clarity on next steps.
- Mr. England expressed uncertainty about the progression of issues he raised in prior meetings and indicated he might follow up via email.
- Committee member Ramirez moved to approve the minutes, and committee member Hicks seconded the motion.
- The committee unanimously approved the minutes by roll call vote.
#### Public Oral Communications
Mr. England addressed the committee regarding unresolved issues he had previously raised, particularly concerning the Advisory Body Handbook update. He emphasized the importance of having a clear path forward and suggested sending his concerns via email for possible action in the upcoming year.
- Mr. England requested guidance on how to proceed with issues he raised earlier, especially about the Advisory Body Handbook not reflecting recent advisory body changes.
- Chair Matichak encouraged Mr. England to submit his concerns via email to the City Council and relevant staff for response.
- No other public comments were made during this segment.
#### Amendments to Council Code of Conduct and Policy K2
The committee discussed proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2, focusing on enforcement measures for advisory body members who fail to file Form 700 disclosures. The recommendation includes suspending participation until compliance and classifying missed meetings during suspension as unexcused. The discussion also covered application deadlines for advisory bodies and the handling of excused versus unexcused absences.
- Heather Glaser summarized the referral from August 2021 to address non-filing of Form 700 by advisory body members, recommending suspension until filing is complete.
- Committee member Ramirez inquired about application deadlines and suggested flexibility to set deadlines closer to interview dates, which staff supported.
- The committee discussed the practice of advisory bodies voting to determine excused or unexcused absences, confirming it aligns with current policy.
- Committee member Hicks questioned if staff faced challenges with Form 700 compliance; staff acknowledged some difficulties, particularly with the Design Review Commission (DRC).
- City Manager McCarthy explained challenges with the DRC due to its composition of architects who may be reluctant to file Form 700, and staff is exploring options to address this.
- Committee member Hicks expressed concern about potential impacts on the DRC if Form 700 filing requirements are strictly enforced.
- Committee member Ramirez emphasized the importance of compliance with Form 700 filings for transparency and conflict of interest disclosures.
- The committee voted on the amendments, with committee member Hicks opposing due to concerns about the DRC, and the motion passed with two votes in favor.
#### Council Policy Work Plan Update
Staff provided an update on the committee's work plan, noting completed items and upcoming tasks including minor cleanups to Council Policy A2 and modifications to the Shoreline Ticket Policy. Committee members were invited to suggest additional items for inclusion.
- Kimberly Thomas reviewed the status of work plan items, highlighting accomplishments and pending tasks for 2023.
- Committee member Ramirez proposed adding a discussion on policies governing private developers' use of public land, noting a lack of transparency and formal guidance.
- City Manager McCarthy advised caution in assigning land use policy matters to the committee, suggesting alternative vehicles might be more appropriate.
- Committee members discussed the merits of addressing the developer use of public land topic within the committee versus other forums.
- Staff committed to preparing an off-agenda memo outlining current processes for developer requests to use public land to inform the council.
- Committee members agreed to review the memo before deciding on further action or formal policy development.
#### Public Comment on Advisory Body Handbook
Mr. England provided additional public input regarding inconsistencies in the Advisory Body Handbook, particularly about residency requirements for members of various advisory bodies. He highlighted the need for clearer, codified guidance to avoid ambiguity.
- Mr. England noted that the Handbook from late 2021 includes the Public Safety Advisory Board but has inconsistent residency requirements across advisory bodies.
- He cited a specific case involving a student member of PSAB to illustrate the ambiguity.
- Mr. England expressed intent to email the City Council for further clarification and action on the Handbook.
