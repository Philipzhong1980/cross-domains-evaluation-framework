### Overview
The Council Policy and Procedures Committee convened on December 15th to discuss several key topics, including the approval of previous meeting minutes, public communications regarding the Advisory Body Handbook, and proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct. The committee addressed concerns about the Development Review Committee's compliance with financial disclosure requirements and discussed the process for determining excused absences among advisory body members. Additionally, the committee reviewed their work plan and considered adding a discussion on the transparency of processes for private developers using public land. The meeting concluded with a commitment to provide further information on these topics.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting held on September 28, 2022. This is a standard procedural item that ensures all members are aligned on past discussions and decisions. The minutes are approved unanimously after a motion and second from committee members.
- Committee member Ramirez moves to approve the minutes.
- Committee member Hicks seconds the motion.
- The minutes pass unanimously with all members voting in favor.

#### Public Communications
During the oral communications segment, Mr. England raises concerns about the clarity of the open public comments from the previous meeting's minutes. He seeks guidance on how to address several issues he previously raised, particularly regarding the Advisory Body Handbook update.
- Mr. England expresses confusion about the path forward on issues he raised previously.
- Chair Matichak suggests Mr. England email his concerns to the City Council for further action.

#### Amendments to Council Code of Conduct
The committee reviews proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2, which includes provisions for advisory body members who fail to file required financial disclosures. The amendments aim to suspend participation until compliance is achieved, addressing a need for accountability among committee members.
- Heather Glaser summarizes the proposed amendments, emphasizing the need for compliance with Form 700 filings.
- Committee member Ramirez questions the application deadline for submissions related to advisory body appointments.

#### Discussion on Absences and Excused Status
The committee discusses the process for determining whether absences from meetings are excused or unexcused. This is important for maintaining attendance standards and ensuring accountability among committee members. The conversation reveals inconsistencies in how different advisory bodies handle this issue.
- Committee member Ramirez notes a lack of common understanding regarding excused absences across advisory bodies.
- Heather Glaser clarifies that each advisory body can determine absence status by majority vote.

#### Concerns about DRC Compliance
The committee addresses concerns regarding the Development Review Committee (DRC) and its compliance with Form 700 filings. The discussion highlights potential challenges in maintaining qualified members who may be reluctant to file due to privacy concerns.
- City Manager McCarthy flags potential challenges with DRC members filing Form 700 due to their professional backgrounds.
- Committee member Hicks expresses concern about the future viability of the DRC if compliance issues persist.

#### Work Plan Updates and Future Items
The committee receives an update on their work plan, noting accomplishments and upcoming items for discussion. Committee member Ramirez suggests adding a discussion on the process for private developers using public land, emphasizing the need for transparency in decision-making.
- Kimberly Thomas provides an overview of completed items and future agenda topics.
- Committee member Ramirez proposes a memo to clarify the process for private developers seeking to use public land.
