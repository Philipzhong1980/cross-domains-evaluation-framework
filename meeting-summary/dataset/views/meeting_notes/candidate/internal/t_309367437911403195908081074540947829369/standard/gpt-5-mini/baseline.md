### Overview
  The Council Policy and Procedures Committee (Chair Lisa Matichak, Committee Members Lucas Ramirez and Alison Hicks) meets on December 15 to approve prior minutes, consider amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2, review the committee work plan, and record public input. Staff (Heather Glaser, City Manager Kimbra McCarthy, and City Clerk liaison Kimberly Thomas) present a recommendation to suspend advisory body members who fail to file Form 700 disclosures until they file, treat missed meetings during suspension as unexcused, and make minor policy cleanups; the committee approves the staff recommendation with edits that remove prescriptive month-based application deadlines and give the City Clerk flexibility to set application deadlines “as near as practicable” to interview dates. The City Manager flags a substantive implementation challenge for the Design Review Commission (DRC), where recruiting architects willing to file Form 700s may be difficult, and staff commits to exploring options while noting state law still governs who must file disclosures. The committee also asks staff to prepare an off-agenda memo describing current practices for private developers requesting use of public land, and the public is directed to email the City Clerk with Advisory Body Handbook questions so staff can respond. The meeting ends with a recorded roll call vote passing the amendments and staff tasked with follow-up on the DRC issue, application-deadline wording, and the developer-use-of-public-land memo.
  
  ### Detailed Summary

  #### Council Code/K2 amendments
  Staff (Heather Glaser) presents proposed amendments to the Council Code of Conduct and Council Policy K2 to provide recourse when advisory body members fail to file Form 700 financial disclosures. The principal recommendation is to suspend a member's participation on their advisory body until a proper filing is made and to treat missed meetings during that suspension as unexcused, with a few miscellaneous, non-substantive clarifications. The committee discusses the proposal, asks clarifying questions, and prepares to consider edits addressing related procedural timing and enforcement.
- Staff member Heather Glaser summarizes the referral to provide recourse for advisory body members who fail to file a Form 700 and recommends suspension of participation until filing is completed.
- Heather Glaser states missed meetings during suspension would be recorded as unexcused under the proposed amendment.
- Chair Lisa Matichak and staff note other minor cleanup changes accompany the Form 700 enforcement recommendation.
  #### Application deadline timing
  Committee members question how application deadlines for advisory bodies should be set and whether the policy should specify a fixed timeline or allow staff flexibility. Heather Glaser advises against tying the deadline simply to 'before agenda publication' because staff needs time to assemble packets; she proposes language that lets the City Clerk set deadlines 'as near as practicable' to interview dates to ensure packets are complete. The committee agrees to add policy language granting staff flexibility and to remove rigid month-based deadlines from Council Policy K2.
- Committee Member Lucas Ramirez asks whether application deadlines can be defined as 'before agenda publication' or should be more flexible, referencing a recent Council item.
- Heather Glaser recommends against a deadline tied to publication and proposes policy language allowing staff to set application deadlines as near as practicable to interview dates.
- Chair Lisa Matichak and Committee Member Ramirez support removing prescriptive month-based deadlines and instead adding language to give the City Clerk flexibility to align deadlines with interview schedules.
  #### Excused/unexcused absences
  Committee members seek clarification on how advisory bodies determine whether absences are excused or unexcused, following an unusual instance where a commission voted to classify an absence. Heather Glaser explains the current policy permits the advisory body, via the chair and staff liaison proposal and a majority or general consent, to determine the status of an absence and that this determination must be recorded in minutes. The committee discusses whether more consistent guidance is needed across advisory bodies, with the charter deferring excused-absence authority for council members to the mayor.
- Lucas Ramirez asks about a Human Relations Commission vote to classify an absence and whether there is a standard way to determine excused versus unexcused absences.
- Heather Glaser explains that policy allows the advisory body chair, in consultation with the staff liaison, to propose whether an absence is excused and the body can accept that by general consent or majority vote.
- Chair Lisa Matichak and Committee Member Ramirez note inconsistent practice across advisory bodies and express interest in clearer, common guidance.
  #### DRC Form 700 impact
  City Manager Kimbra McCarthy raises concerns that the Design Review Commission (DRC) may face difficulty recruiting members who are willing or able to file Form 700 disclosures because the DRC often attracts practicing architects. Staff flags the possible need to reconsider the DRC's structure or role—possibly using consultants rather than appointed commissioners—but notes state law still requires filing if the body remains an ordinance-created legislative/advisory body. Committee members express concern about losing the DRC and ask staff to pursue options.
- City Manager Kimbra McCarthy informs the committee that the DRC may struggle to find members willing to file Form 700 disclosures because many are practicing architects.
- Kimbra McCarthy says staff is exploring options for the DRC, including different structures or consultant-based approaches, while noting that state law may still require filings if the body remains an advisory/legislative entity.
- Committee Member Alison Hicks expresses concern about losing the DRC or reducing its qualifications and asks staff to work through alternatives before enforcement causes unintended consequences.
  #### Committee vote outcome
  After discussion and proposed edits, Committee Member Lucas Ramirez moves to accept staff recommendations with the added revisions on application-deadline flexibility and Chair Lisa Matichak seconds. The committee takes a roll call vote and the motion passes with the majority in favor, while Committee Member Alison Hicks records a 'no' vote expressing concern about unintended impacts on the DRC. The committee directs staff to continue working with the City Clerk and City Manager on implementation and on addressing DRC-related challenges.
- Lucas Ramirez moves to adopt the staff recommendations with revisions to application deadline language and Chair Lisa Matichak seconds the motion.
- A roll call vote is taken and the motion passes with Chair Matichak and Committee Member Ramirez voting yes and Committee Member Alison Hicks voting no.
- The committee directs staff to continue refining the policy language with the City Clerk and to work on solutions for DRC challenges flagged by the City Manager.
