### Overview
  Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre opens by welcoming The L Word and Generation Q cast for Lesbian Visibility Week and emphasizes the administration’s commitment to LGBTQI+ representation and support, a theme echoed by creator Ilene Chaiken and cast member Leisha Hailey. The briefing then centers on domestic policy and political developments: Jean-Pierre details analyses showing Speaker McCarthy’s Limit, Save, Grow proposal would risk a recession and cut healthcare and education supports and repeatedly states the White House will not negotiate away avoiding a default, urging House Republicans to take default off the table. She praises Washington State’s assault-weapons ban, offers a personal tribute to Harry Belafonte, and answers a wide range of reporter questions—stressing White House counsel guidance on Hatch Act compliance, describing high-level engagement with allies after unauthorized leaks, outlining U.S. diplomacy and contingency planning related to a 72-hour ceasefire in Sudan, and deferring technical timelines on the debt ceiling and campaign-specific questions to Treasury and the 2024 campaign respectively. Jean-Pierre defends the President’s economic record while acknowledging Americans’ cost concerns, reiterates support for the Equal Rights Amendment, expresses grave concern about Uganda’s anti-LGBTQ+ legislation, urges an end to holds that impede Defense nominees, and highlights the administration’s position on police accountability, NATO accession procedures, and the need for legislative solutions to gun violence; on multiple foreign-policy and personnel questions she declines to preview leader-level discussions or internal deliberations and refers reporters to the appropriate agencies for operational details.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Lesbian Visibility Week
  Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre welcomes the cast and creators of The L Word and Generation Q to the White House to mark Lesbian Visibility Week and to emphasize the importance of representation. The visit highlights the administration's commitment to visibility for LGBTQI+ people and includes a planned meeting between the cast and LGBTQI+ White House staffers. Speakers framed representation as lifesaving and necessary amid ongoing attacks on LGBTQI+ rights.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says Lesbian Visibility Week underscores how important visibility and representation are and introduces Jennifer Beals, Leisha Hailey, Katherine Moennig, and Ilene Chaiken to meet with LGBTQI+ staffers.
- MS. CHAIKEN says The L Word brought queer stories into homes worldwide and helped people, especially youth, feel embraced and not alone.
- MS. HAILEY says visibility is not only the act of being seen but also the ability to see, and she affirms support for librarians, faith leaders, and LGBTQIA people facing local attacks.
#### MAGA proposal analysis
  Jean-Pierre summarizes new analyses showing Speaker McCarthy’s Limit, Save, Grow legislation would have significant negative impacts on the economy and services. She cites Moody’s Analytics, HHS, and the Department of Education findings to argue the bill increases recession risk, cuts Medicaid access, and reduces support for students and teachers. The administration frames the proposal as breaking House Republicans' commitments and as unacceptable to negotiate in its current form.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says Moody’s Analytics finds Speaker McCarthy’s bill increases the odds of a recession, costs 780,000 jobs, and weakens the economy.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Department of Health and Human Services analysis shows the proposal would jeopardize healthcare for 21 million Medicaid enrollees and could remove access for 630,000 rural patients.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Department of Education reports the bill would remove up to 60,000 teachers, reduce support for 7.5 million children with disabilities, and cut Pell grants and student debt relief for millions.
#### Default negotiation stance
  Jean-Pierre repeatedly states the administration will not negotiate over avoiding default and accuses Speaker McCarthy and MAGA-aligned House Republicans of holding the economy hostage. She says discussions about budget priorities are appropriate but rejects tying default avoidance to acceptance of the House bill's full agenda. The White House urges Republicans to take default off the table and engage in conventional budget talks.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President and Senate will not negotiate over avoiding default and calls on House Republicans to do their constitutional duty.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE argues Speaker McCarthy’s approach ties default to accepting the entire House agenda, which the White House calls unreasonable and dangerous.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says it is the responsibility of Speaker McCarthy and the MAGA wing of the Republican Party to answer questions about why they are pursuing this strategy.
#### Washington State gun ban
  Jean-Pierre announces Washington State has become the 10th state to ban assault weapons and commends state leaders and advocates for the action. She reiterates the President’s push for federal measures, including universal background checks and an assault weapons ban, while noting prior federal actions like the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act. The White House urges other states to follow Washington’s lead to reduce gun violence.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says President Biden commends Governor Jay Inslee and state legislative leaders for banning assault weapons in Washington State.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterates the President’s continued advocacy for federal background checks for all gun sales and a federal ban on assault weapons and high-capacity magazines.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes the President has taken executive actions and supported the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act but calls for more action to save lives.
#### Tribute to Harry Belafonte
  Jean-Pierre offers a personal tribute to Harry Belafonte following his passing, describing him as a legendary, barrier-breaking artist and mentor. She recounts his influence on her life and praises his contributions to culture and activism. The White House frames his death as a significant loss and pledges to cherish his memory.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE calls Harry Belafonte an incredible legend and barrier-breaker who inspired generations and personally impacted her life.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says Belafonte’s guidance, kindness, and wisdom made her and many others better and that the world lost a one-of-a-kind figure.

She says the White House Counsel will provide guidance and that limited involvement of select officials in reelection activity follows past norms. Specifics about who will participate are deferred to the 2024 reelection campaign.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration believes in the rule of law and will continue to respect the Hatch Act.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the White House Counsel’s Office will provide guidance to staff about protocol and compliance.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says limited involvement by select officials in a reelection campaign is allowed under past norms and refers further questions to the 2024 campaign.

She describes the U.S. commitment to the Republic of Korea (ROK) as ironclad and says the issue is being taken very seriously in consultations with partners. The White House does not preview bilateral discussions.

She repeatedly refers such questions to the 2024 reelection campaign and says she learned of the announcement at the same time as the public. The White House avoids discussing internal campaign decision-making from the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says she has nothing to share about the President’s private decision-making and refers questions to the 2024 campaign.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says she learned of the President’s announcement the same way the public did and will not discuss campaign deliberations from this podium.
#### Sudan ceasefire diplomacy
  Jean-Pierre outlines U.S. diplomatic engagement following intense negotiations in Sudan and reports a nationwide 72-hour ceasefire that began April 24. She says the President’s senior national security team is in direct contact with SAF and RSF leaders and that the U.S. is coordinating with regional partners to form an oversight committee for a durable ceasefire and humanitarian arrangements. She emphasizes there is no military solution to Sudan’s political crisis and stresses protection of civilians and the return to civilian government.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President’s senior national security team has been in direct contact with SAF and RSF leadership urging an immediate end to hostilities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says following negotiations the parties agreed to a nationwide 72-hour ceasefire starting at midnight on April 24 and the U.S. will urge full compliance.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the U.S. is coordinating with regional and international partners and Sudanese civilian stakeholders to help create a committee to oversee a permanent ceasefire and humanitarian arrangements.
#### Naval assets and evacuation planning
  In response to questions about American evacuations from Sudan, Jean-Pierre reiterates the National Security Advisor’s comment that naval assets are moving within the region to assist potential contingencies off Port Sudan. She does not provide further operational details and defers to National Security briefings for more specifics. The administration frames the movement as a precaution to protect U.S. personnel and interests.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE repeats that the administration is moving naval assets within the region to assist with potential contingencies off the coast of Port Sudan.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says she has nothing more to share beyond what National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan already described.
#### Debt ceiling timing
  When asked about how long Treasury’s extraordinary measures will last, Jean-Pierre defers to Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen and the Treasury Department for timelines and oversight. The White House avoids providing an estimate from the podium and emphasizes Treasury is closely monitoring the situation. The administration continues to call for avoiding default through conventional negotiation rather than acquiescing to the House bill.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says Secretary Yellen and the Treasury Department are overseeing the timeline for extraordinary measures and she will refer timeline questions to Treasury.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterates the administration’s position that avoiding default should not be negotiated away and should be taken off the table by House Republicans.
#### Senator Manchin relations
  Jean-Pierre responds to Senator Joe Manchin’s criticism about the administration breaking its word, defending the administration’s record and partnership with him. She underscores accomplishments like the Inflation Reduction Act and says the administration will faithfully implement laws even where they disagree with individual provisions. She reiterates the administration’s intent to continue working with Senator Manchin on shared priorities including deficit reduction.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration has a strong relationship with Senator Manchin and is proud of legislative accomplishments achieved during the President’s tenure.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration is faithfully implementing all parts of the Inflation Reduction Act and will follow the law even on components they may not prefer.
#### Finishing the job theme
  Reporters ask Jean-Pierre to explain the President’s recurring theme of 'finishing the job;' she describes it as completing the agenda he ran on in 2020 and implementing passed legislation. She cites examples like Medicare drug negotiation, bipartisan infrastructure, the CHIPS Act, job creation, and economic policies intended to build from the bottom up and middle out. The White House positions 'finishing the job' as a message of ongoing delivery rather than an open-ended promise.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says 'finishing the job' means delivering and implementing the agenda the President ran on in 2020, including legislation like Medicare drug negotiation and infrastructure.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE cites the CHIPS and Science Act and other laws as examples of tangible actions that fulfill the President’s agenda and create jobs and economic security.
#### Market concerns and Speaker meeting

She deflects questions about market perception back to Speaker McCarthy and the MAGA wing and emphasizes the President’s expectation that Republicans meet their constitutional duty to avoid default. The White House frames the situation as a responsibility of congressional leadership rather than the Executive.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says it is Speaker McCarthy and the MAGA wing of the Republican Party who are holding the American economy hostage, not the White House.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says questions about market reactions are for Speaker McCarthy and Republicans who are advancing the proposed conditions tied to default avoidance.
#### AI-generated political videos

She emphasizes that technology companies bear responsibility to ensure products are safe before release. The White House is undertaking an interagency process to address AI risks and opportunities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says she has not yet seen the AI-generated video and will consult with her team before commenting on that specific instance.

#### Gun violence public health idea
  Reporters ask whether the President would declare gun violence a public health emergency; Jean-Pierre says the administration has looked into the legal grounds and found them questionable and unlikely to provide meaningful funds or authorities. She says the administration prefers securing funding through the budget, increasing ATF funding, and pursuing new legislation to ban assault weapons and require safer storage. The White House frames the solution as legislative and budgetary rather than a public-health declaration.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says legal grounds for declaring gun violence a public health emergency are questionable and such a declaration would not provide meaningful funds or authorities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration prioritizes securing more funding for ATF through the budget and pursuing legislation to ban assault weapons and require safer firearm storage.
#### Uganda anti-LGBTQ+ bill
  Jean-Pierre expresses grave concerns about Uganda’s recently advanced anti-LGBTQ+ legislation, calling it one of the most extreme such laws in the world. She says the U.S. is engaging the Ugandan government at the highest levels and reiterates the administration’s stance that human rights are universal. The White House warns against attacks on people for who they are or whom they love.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration has grave concerns about Uganda’s anti-LGBTQ+ bill and is engaged with the Ugandan government at the highest levels.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterates that human rights are universal and no one should be attacked, imprisoned, or killed for their identity or whom they love.

#### Economic performance and messaging
  Jean-Pierre responds to questions about rising consumer costs and economic indicators by citing data showing moderating inflation, higher wages, strong consumer spending, and robust job creation since the President took office. She highlights 12.6 million jobs created, full recovery of pandemic job losses plus growth, and near 50-year low unemployment, arguing the administration’s policies are producing results. The White House frames ongoing economic action as part of delivering the President’s agenda.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says annual inflation has fallen over the past nine months, wages and incomes are up, and consumer spending remains strong.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says 12.6 million jobs have been created during the President’s tenure and unemployment is at a near 50-year low, underscoring the administration’s economic record.
#### NATO accession and Ukraine
  When asked whether Ukraine will join NATO during the President’s term, Jean-Pierre reiterates the longstanding U.S. position of supporting an open-door policy while noting accession decisions rest with NATO member states. She says the administration’s current focus is on providing economic and humanitarian assistance to help Ukraine defend itself. The White House does not commit to a timeline for any NATO accession.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the U.S. supports an open-door policy for NATO accession but that any decision is between NATO Allies and aspirant countries.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the immediate focus is ensuring Ukraine receives economic, humanitarian, and defensive assistance to resist Russian aggression.

She frames the ERA as essential to enshrining gender equality in the Constitution and argues it is a moral and practical priority for economic prosperity and democratic health. - MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration supports the Equal Rights Amendment and calls on Congress to act swiftly to enshrine gender equality in the Constitution.
