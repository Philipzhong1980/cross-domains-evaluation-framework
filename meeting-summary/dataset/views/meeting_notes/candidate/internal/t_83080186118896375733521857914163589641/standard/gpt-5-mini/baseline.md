### Overview
  The Neighborhoods Committee convenes a virtual meeting to gather resident questions, provide updates, and share resources for Mountain View neighborhoods. Chair Pat Showalter and Vice Mayor Allison Hicks frame the meeting and staff present on community programs: Robert Maitland promotes the CERT volunteer training and upcoming academies, and Anky van Deursen outlines the Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance (MHRSO) and program access. The bulk of the meeting addresses resident questions—Public Works Director Don Cameron will contact AT&T about missing excavation permits for New Frontier and explains PG&E service arrangements are between PG&E and park owners; Community Services Director John Marchant and Police Captain Michael Canfield describe Live Nation’s hired traffic management, ongoing coordination with Police, and planned post-season debriefs to improve ingress/egress; Traffic staff explain NTMP thresholds and options for Sylvan Ave traffic calming and offer to refresh faded striping and signage; Karen Tiedemann and Anky describe MHRSO mechanisms allowing park-owner petitions to preserve a fair return and confirm the AGA formula ties to CPI with a 2%–5% band; Martin Eichner and Police Captain Canfield summarize mobile home residency rules and city cannabis and submetering practices, including rights to organize, billing limits on submetered utilities, and a moratorium on outdoor cannabis cultivation; Danielle Lee commits to researching solar feasibility on mobile homes; housing staff outline city grant and contract support for nonprofits like CSA; and transportation staff provide Safe Routes education and upcoming bicycle-safety events. Staff share actionable resources (rent stabilization site, CPUC, NTMP, mountainview.gov/askMV, Live Nation hotline, and state mobile home webpages), commit to follow-ups where needed, and adjourn with encouragement for residents to continue reporting issues through Ask Mountain View.

  #### MHRSO alignment with other cities
  A resident asks whether the MHRSO can be aligned to another city's rent ordinance; Anky explains the MHRSO ties annual general adjustments (AGAs) to CPI with a 2%–5% floor and cap. She clarifies that changing the ordinance is a policy decision for elected officials and is not currently on staff's work plan. Residents requesting policy changes are advised to bring proposals to the City Council for consideration.
- Anky van Deursen explains the MHRSO's annual general adjustment is indexed to CPI with a 2% minimum and 5% maximum.

  #### Sunset Estates rent concerns
  Residents express concern that rent control at Sunset Estates could make park ownership unprofitable; Karen Tiedemann explains the MHRSO provides a process for park owners to petition for an individual rent adjustment to maintain a fair return. She describes that petitions are heard by a hearing officer and that the ordinance aims to prevent owners from suffering a loss that would force them out of business. The Rental Housing Committee process exists to balance resident protections with owners' rights.
- Karen Tiedemann explains park owners may file a petition to the Rental Housing Committee for an individual rent adjustment to maintain a fair return.
- Karen Tiedemann says a hearing officer would review a petition and could grant a rent increase if the owner is not maintaining prior operating income.
- Karen Tiedemann emphasizes the ordinance is designed to protect both resident stability and a fair rate of return for park owners.

  #### Cannabis growing and use rules
  Residents ask whether marijuana can be planted in park spaces; Martin Eichner explains the Mobile Home Residency Law requires homeowner permission from the park owner to plant trees and bars activities that constitute a nuisance, which can include criminal violations. Police Captain Michael Canfield clarifies that Mountain View has a moratorium on outdoor cannabis cultivation, indoor cultivation is not a criminal offense for the PD, and outdoor smoking/vaping in public is prohibited while indoor use is allowed with caveats. Audrey Seymour Ramberg adds the city's smoking ordinance restricts indoor smoking in multifamily properties.
- Martin Eichner explains the Mobile Home Residency Law requires owner permission to plant trees and allows park owners to act against activities that constitute a nuisance.
- Michael Canfield states there is a moratorium on external/outdoor cannabis planting in Mountain View and that indoor cultivation is not a criminal enforcement priority for the Police Department.
- Michael Canfield clarifies outdoor smoking or vaping of cannabis is not lawful in public areas, while indoor consumption is treated differently.
- Audrey Seymour Ramberg notes the city's smoking ordinance prohibits smoking inside multifamily residential units.

  Residents question how the MHRSO's fair rate of return and the 5% AGA were determined; Anky explains the ordinance prescribes the AGA formula based on regional CPI with a 2% floor and 5% cap, and last year's CPI exceeded 5% so the AGA was set at the cap. She reiterates that hardship-based adjustments for landlords would be a policy change requiring elected-body action. Staff refer policy questions back to the Council for consideration.
- Anky van Deursen explains the MHRSO ties annual general adjustments to regional CPI with a 2% minimum and 5% maximum.
- Anky van Deursen notes this formula is in the ordinance and last year's CPI exceeded 5%, so the AGA was set at the 5% cap.
- Anky van Deursen confirms requiring landlords to prove hardship for increases would be a policy change for elected officials.

  #### Homeowners association formation
  Residents ask about forming homeowners associations (HOAs) and retaliation risk; Martin Eichner explains Mobile Home Residency Law guarantees residents the right to meet the owner and organize, use community rooms for political meetings, and, if suitably organized, form a nonprofit to bargain to buy a park. He cautions that enforcement of state protections often requires court action, which can be costly, but references the new Mobile Home Residency Protection Program at the state Department of Housing and Community Development that may provide complaint intake and legal referrals. Karen adds the MHRSO provides eviction protections but does not explicitly address harassment.
- Martin Eichner explains homeowners have statutory rights to meet with park management and to use community rooms for political meetings under the Mobile Home Residency Law.
- Martin Eichner describes the option to form a nonprofit to negotiate the park sale if residents want to pursue a cooperative purchase.

  #### Submetered gas billing practices
  A resident asks why parks monitor gas and bill residents rather than PG&E billing directly; Martin Eichner explains parks may master-meter and submeter utilities but billing must only pass through actual utility costs and an appropriate share of common-area utilities. He emphasizes park owners cannot include their own operational costs in resident bills and must fairly allocate common-area charges, and homeowners should request billing breakdowns if they suspect overcharges. Martin points residents to state mobile home resources for further guidance.
- Martin Eichner explains parks can master-meter and submeter utilities but must pass through only actual utility costs to residents.
- Martin Eichner says park owners can include residents' share of common-area utility costs but must allocate them fairly and cannot charge their own overhead to residents.
- Martin Eichner advises homeowners to request itemized billing and use state mobile home resources for additional questions.
