### Overview
The Mountain View Council Neighborhoods Committee convenes a virtual meeting to engage residents on neighborhood concerns, city programs, and services. Key participants include Chair Pat Showalter, Vice Mayor Allison Hicks, Rent Stabilization Program Manager Anki Van Deursen, Public Works Director Don Cameron, Police Captain Michael Canfield, and other city staff. Presentations cover the Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) program and the Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance (MHRSO), highlighting protections for mobile home tenants and landlords. Residents raise questions about internet service permitting, traffic management during Live Nation events, utility submetering, cannabis regulations, and traffic safety improvements. City officials provide detailed responses, clarify policies, and share resources for further assistance. The meeting emphasizes resident rights under the Mobile Home Residency Law, community safety programs, and ongoing efforts to improve infrastructure and services. Staff encourage use of city portals for reporting issues and commit to follow-ups where needed. The session concludes with appreciation for community participation and a call for continued engagement to enhance neighborhood well-being.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Meeting Purpose and Format
Chair Pat Showalter opens the virtual Council Neighborhoods Committee meeting, explaining its purpose to engage residents directly with city staff and council members about neighborhood issues. The meeting is held virtually with Spanish interpretation available, aiming to gather community input and provide updates on city services.
- Chair Pat Showalter explains the meeting is virtual and aims to hear residents' concerns and questions.
- The meeting includes Spanish interpretation provided by Elena Garcia.
- Council Neighborhoods Committee members present include Chair Showalter and Vice Mayor Hicks, with Mayor Ramirez expected to join later.
#### Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) Program
Robert Maitland from the Office of Emergency Services presents on the CERT program, a FEMA initiative educating residents on disaster preparedness and organizing volunteer teams to support the community during emergencies. He encourages residents to join upcoming CERT academies and events to enhance community resilience.
- Robert Maitland describes CERT as a program to train volunteers for disaster preparedness and response.
- Mountain View has 18 recognized CERT neighborhood groups participating in training and drills.
- Residents are invited to join the next CERT Academy in March and other upcoming events.
#### Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Program Overview
Anki Van Deursen, Rent Stabilization Program Manager, introduces the Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance (MHRSO) implemented in October last year. The program stabilizes rents, provides eviction protections for mobile home tenants, and ensures fair returns for park owners. Staff and legal counsel are available for resident support.
- Anki Van Deursen explains MHRSO covers both space rent and mobile home rentals.
- The program includes regular office hours and outreach to assist residents.
- Martin Eichner and Karen Tiedemann support the program with facilitation and legal expertise.
#### Internet Service and Permitting Issues
Residents inquire about improving internet access, particularly at New Frontier and Sunset Estates mobile home parks. Public Works Director Don Cameron clarifies that no current permit applications from AT&T exist for New Frontier and that Sunset Estates has a private wireless agreement with Ridge Wireless. The city encourages coordination between residents, park management, and providers.
- Don Cameron confirms no AT&T permit application is on file for New Frontier Mobile Home Park.
- Sunset Estates has an agreement with Ridge Wireless for onsite wireless service.
- City staff will assist with permitting if providers initiate applications.
#### Traffic and Parking Management for Live Nation Events
Community Services Director John Marchant addresses traffic concerns during Live Nation concerts. Live Nation employs professional traffic and parking management and coordinates with police. The city plans a post-season review to improve traffic flow and explore incentives to stagger arrivals.
- John Marchant notes Live Nation hires professional traffic control for events.
- Police coordinate on-site to manage incidents and improve ingress and egress.
- A post-season wrap-up meeting is planned to evaluate and enhance traffic management strategies.
#### Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance Details
Questions arise about aligning MHRSO with other cities, calculation of fair rate of return, and protections for landlords and tenants. Staff clarify that CPI-based rent adjustments are capped between 2% and 5%, with hardship petitions available for landlords. Policy changes require elected body consideration.
- Anki Van Deursen explains MHRSO rent increases are tied to regional CPI with limits.
- Landlords can petition for individual rent adjustments if income is insufficient.
- Policy changes such as aligning with other cities or hardship proof require council approval.
#### Mobile Home Residency Law and Resident Rights
Legal experts Martin Eichner and Karen Tiedemann discuss residents' rights to form homeowner associations, protections against retaliation, and the Mobile Home Residency Protection Program. While residents have rights to organize and meet with management, enforcement mechanisms are limited and often require legal action.
- Martin Eichner outlines residents' rights to meet with park management and form associations.
- The Mobile Home Residency Law protects against retaliation but enforcement is challenging.
- A new state program offers complaint mechanisms and legal aid referrals for mobile home residents.
#### Public Safety and Community Support Programs
Housing and Neighborhood Services Manager Micaela Hellman-Tincher and Assistant City Manager Audrey Seymour Ramberg describe city partnerships with nonprofits like Community Services Agency (CSA). The city provides grants and contracts supporting rent relief, basic income pilots, and other safety net services.
- Micaela Hellman-Tincher details grant programs supporting CSA and other nonprofits.
- Audrey Seymour Ramberg highlights city funding for rent relief and Guaranteed Basic Income pilot.
- The city encourages nonprofits to apply for upcoming funding cycles.
#### Utility Services and Submetering in Mobile Home Parks
Residents ask about gas and electric submetering practices. Legal counsel explains that parks may submeter utilities but must charge fairly without including owner costs. The city cannot regulate PG&E service arrangements, which are between PG&E and property owners, but will facilitate permits if PG&E initiates projects.
- Martin Eichner clarifies submetering must reflect actual usage and fair common area cost allocation.
- Don Cameron states PG&E service is regulated by the California Public Utilities Commission, not the city.
- City staff will cooperate with PG&E on permits but cannot mandate service changes.
#### Cannabis Planting and Consumption Regulations
Questions about marijuana planting and consumption are addressed by Police Captain Michael Canfield and Assistant City Manager Audrey Seymour Ramberg. Exterior planting is prohibited by city moratorium, while indoor cultivation is allowed. Smoking cannabis is legal indoors but prohibited in public outdoor spaces, with enforcement and education available.
- Captain Canfield explains outdoor cannabis planting is banned; indoor cultivation is permitted.
- Smoking or vaping cannabis is legal inside homes but illegal in public outdoor areas.
- The city has a smoking ordinance prohibiting tobacco use inside multi-family units.
#### Traffic Safety and Bicycle Infrastructure Improvements
Traffic Engineer Lorenzo Lopez and transportation staff discuss concerns about speeding, dangerous turns, and bicyclist safety on streets like Sylvan Ave and El Camino Real. The city is implementing traffic calming measures, collaborating with Caltrans on repaving and bike lanes, and conducting Safe Routes to School education programs.
- Lorenzo Lopez notes prior neighborhood traffic management efforts and potential for renewed speed hump requests.
- Caltrans will repave El Camino Real with added bicycle and pedestrian improvements.
- Safe Routes to School program has trained over 3,200 students on safe biking practices recently.
#### Noise Complaints and Enforcement
Police Captain Michael Canfield explains that while Mountain View lacks a specific noise ordinance, Penal Code provisions apply to loud parties or vehicles. Residents are encouraged to report incidents via the non-emergency police line for enforcement and mediation.
- Captain Canfield advises residents to call the police non-emergency number for noise issues.
- Noise enforcement is based on Penal Code sections rather than a local ordinance.
- Community Services Director John Marchant will provide a Live Nation noise complaint hotline in the chat.
#### Community Resources and Contact Information
Staff compile and share key resources including websites for rent stabilization, traffic management, PG&E regulation, and Safe Routes programs. They encourage residents to use city portals for reporting issues and accessing information, emphasizing ongoing communication and support.
- Micaela Hellman-Tincher shares multiple resource websites and encourages screenshots.
- Don Cameron promotes the city's Ask Mountain View portal for submitting concerns.
- Staff provide contact emails and phone numbers for follow-up on specific topics.
#### Meeting Conclusion and Final Remarks
Chair Pat Showalter thanks participants and staff, encouraging residents to use the shared resources to report concerns. Vice Mayor Allison Hicks reflects on the diverse neighborhood issues discussed. The meeting adjourns with appreciation for the interpreter and all attendees.
- Chair Showalter emphasizes the importance of resident engagement and reporting.
- Vice Mayor Hicks notes the unique concerns of different neighborhoods, especially mobile home parks.
- The meeting closes with thanks to staff, interpreters, and participants.
