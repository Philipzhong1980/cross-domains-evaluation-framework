### Overview
The Council Neighborhoods Committee meeting, led by Chair Pat Showalter, focuses on engaging residents from mobile home parks in Mountain View. Key topics include the Community Emergency Response Team (CERT) program, the Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance (MHRSO), and traffic management during events. City officials, including Robert Maitland and Anki Van Dursen, provide updates on community programs and address resident concerns about internet access, traffic issues, and cannabis regulations. The meeting emphasizes the importance of resident feedback in improving city services and encourages participation in various city programs.

### Detailed Summary
#### Purpose of the Meeting
Chair Pat Showalter outlines the meeting's purpose, which is to engage with residents and address their concerns directly. This meeting serves as a platform for residents to ask questions and share ideas with city staff and council members, helping to improve city services.
- Pat Showalter emphasizes the importance of community engagement during the meeting.
- The meeting aims to gather feedback on city services and resident concerns.

#### Community Emergency Response Team (CERT)
Robert Maitland presents information about the CERT program, which educates residents on disaster preparedness and organizes volunteer teams to assist during emergencies. He encourages community involvement and participation in upcoming training sessions.
- Robert Maitland explains the role of CERT in disaster preparedness.
- He invites residents to join the next CERT Academy in March.

#### Mobile Home Rent Stabilization Ordinance (MHRSO)
Anki Van Dursen discusses the MHRSO, which aims to stabilize rents and provide eviction protections for mobile home tenants. She highlights recent changes and the ordinance's coverage for both space and mobile home renters.
- Anki Van Dursen details the protections offered under the MHRSO.
- She mentions the ordinance's implementation date and its goals.

#### Internet Service Issues
Dawn Cameron addresses concerns from residents about internet access at New Frontier Mobile Home Park, explaining that AT&T needs a permit to install necessary equipment. The city is reaching out to AT&T to facilitate this process.
- Dawn Cameron confirms that no permit application from AT&T is currently on file.
- The city is actively working to assist residents in improving internet access.

#### Traffic Concerns During Events
John Marchant discusses traffic management during Live Nation events, noting that a professional service is hired for traffic control. He mentions plans for a post-season review to improve traffic flow for future events.
- John Marchant acknowledges traffic issues during concerts and outlines current management strategies.
- He commits to reviewing traffic control measures with Live Nation and police.

#### Rent Control Alignment
Anki Van Dursen responds to a question about aligning the MHRSO with other cities' rent ordinances, stating that any changes would require policy consideration by the elected body and are not currently on the staff's agenda.
- Anki Van Dursen clarifies that alignment with other ordinances is a policy decision.
- She notes that the current ordinance is based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI).

#### Mobile Home Park Community Engagement
Martin Eichner explains the rights of mobile home residents to form associations and meet with park management. He emphasizes protections against retaliation for organizing and the importance of legal avenues for enforcement.
- Martin Eichner outlines the legal protections for mobile home residents under state law.
- He mentions the new Mobile Home Residency Protection Program for complaints.

#### Traffic Management Requests
Lorenzo Lopez discusses the process for residents to request traffic calming measures in their neighborhoods. He encourages residents to use the Neighborhood Traffic Management Program to submit their concerns.
- Lorenzo Lopez explains the criteria for implementing speed humps and other traffic measures.
- He invites residents to participate in the traffic management process.

#### Cannabis Regulations
Michael Canfield clarifies the legality of cannabis cultivation and consumption in Mountain View, noting that while indoor cultivation is allowed, outdoor planting is not. He encourages residents to reach out for further questions.
- Michael Canfield confirms that outdoor cannabis planting is illegal in Mountain View.
- He offers his contact information for residents with cannabis-related inquiries.

#### Solar Panel Installation
Danielle Lee discusses the potential for solar panel installation on mobile homes, noting structural considerations and the need for further research. She commits to following up with more information.
- Danielle Lee acknowledges the challenges of installing solar panels on mobile homes.
- She offers to provide additional resources and information on the topic.
