### Overview
The Downtown Committee meeting on November 1st covered several key topics, including the approval of October minutes, upcoming agenda items related to busking and downtown maintenance, and concerns raised by Marina Keith regarding hot dog stands and environmental issues. Edgar Maravilla provided updates on downtown development, including new restaurant openings and a proposed cap on office space. John Lang discussed Castro Street updates, including the adoption of a pedestrian mall ordinance and vacancy statistics. Matthew Atkins from the MVPD addressed public safety concerns, highlighting the success of the downtown ambassador program. The committee also adopted the meeting schedule for 2023, maintaining a 10:00 AM meeting time.

### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of October Minutes
The committee discusses the approval of the minutes from the previous meeting held in October. All members present vote in favor of the minutes, indicating no objections or comments. This approval is a routine procedural step necessary for maintaining accurate records of the committee's activities.
- Mike Kasperzak motions to approve the minutes from October.
- Jamil Shaikh seconds the motion for approval.
- All committee members present vote in favor of the minutes.

#### Upcoming Agenda Items
The committee reviews upcoming agenda items, including discussions on busking and music in downtown, general maintenance and cleanliness, and the Castro bike way feasibility study. John Lang indicates that the busking issue will be addressed in the new year, while updates on maintenance and the bikeway study are expected in future meetings.
- John Lang states that busking and music discussions will be postponed until the new year.
- Updates on general maintenance and cleanliness will be provided in the next meeting.
- The bikeway feasibility study is scheduled for discussion in February.

#### Concerns About Hot Dog Stands
Marina Keith raises concerns regarding hot dog stands on Castro Street, specifically mentioning issues with oil dumping into storm drains. She emphasizes the environmental impact and the need for city intervention. The committee discusses the importance of maintaining cleanliness in downtown and the role of local businesses in this effort.
- Marina Keith reports hot dog stands are dumping oil into storm drains, raising environmental concerns.
- Keith mentions that the fire department is investigating the issue after receiving video evidence.
- Mike Kasperzak highlights the importance of storm drain maintenance to protect the Bay.

#### Downtown Development Updates
Edgar Maravilla provides updates on downtown development, including facade improvements and new restaurant openings. He mentions specific addresses where improvements are taking place and discusses the implications of a proposed cap on office space in downtown, which is set to be reviewed by the Environmental Planning Commission.
- Edgar Maravilla reports on facade improvements at several locations, including a new restaurant named Limon.
- He mentions a proposed cap on office space that will be discussed at the Environmental Planning Commission.
- Maravilla notes that the cap is in response to state regulations affecting parking requirements.

#### Castro Street Updates
John Lang shares updates regarding Castro Street, including the adoption of a pedestrian mall ordinance and the extension of the Castro Streets program. He discusses efforts to declutter the area and provides statistics on vacancy rates for retail spaces, highlighting the challenges faced by local businesses.
- John Lang announces the adoption of the pedestrian mall ordinance for Downtown Castro Street.
- He discusses the extension of the Castro Streets program to allow businesses to operate on the street.
- Lang provides vacancy statistics, noting that the 400 block has the highest vacancy rate.

#### Public Safety and Crime Updates
Matthew Atkins from the MVPD discusses public safety concerns in downtown, particularly regarding auto burglaries and the unhoused population. He highlights the success of the downtown ambassador program in reducing crime and emphasizes the importance of community involvement in reporting suspicious activities.
- Matthew Atkins reports a decrease in auto burglaries due to the downtown ambassador program.
- He emphasizes the importance of community reporting for effective policing.
- Atkins discusses outreach efforts for the unhoused population in downtown.

#### Meeting Schedule for 2023
The committee discusses the proposed meeting schedule for 2023, which includes specific dates and times. There is a consensus to maintain the 10:00 AM meeting time, although some members express a preference for earlier meetings. The committee votes to adopt the schedule as presented.
- John Lang presents the proposed meeting schedule for 2023, noting no meetings in January, July, and August.
- Pamela Baird motions to adopt the schedule, which is seconded by Merry Yen.
- The committee votes in favor of the proposed schedule.
