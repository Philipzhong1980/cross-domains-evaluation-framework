### Overview
The Downtown Committee meeting on November 1st, chaired by Mike Kasperzak, covered a range of topics critical to the vitality and safety of downtown Mountain View. Key participants included John Lang providing city updates, Edgar Maravilla on development projects, Maureen Grzn-Pieracci on city events, and Matthew Atkins from the Mountain View Police Department on public safety. The committee approved the previous meeting's minutes and discussed upcoming agenda items such as busking regulations, maintenance updates, and a bikeway feasibility study. Concerns were raised about environmental issues like oil dumping into storm drains by hot dog vendors, prompting investigations. Development updates highlighted new restaurant permits and facade improvements, alongside a proposed ordinance to cap office space downtown in response to state parking regulations. The recently adopted pedestrian mall ordinance for Castro Street was reviewed, with detailed vacancy analyses revealing challenges in retail occupancy and discussions on potential economic incentives to support local businesses. The committee unanimously approved continuing remote meetings under AB 361 while awaiting further guidance on in-person requirements. Holiday and community events were previewed, emphasizing efforts to engage residents and support downtown retailers through interactive activities and promotions. Public safety reports detailed successful crime reduction initiatives and outreach programs for unhoused individuals, with guidance provided on reporting non-emergency issues. Traffic signal reactivations aim to enhance pedestrian safety amid behavioral adjustments. The committee adopted the 2023 meeting schedule, balancing member availability and public engagement considerations, and addressed member attendance issues with forthcoming council appointments. Overall, the meeting reflected a collaborative approach to addressing downtown challenges and opportunities, with active participation from city staff, committee members, and community stakeholders.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Meeting Minutes Approval
The committee reviewed and approved the minutes from the October meeting without any questions or comments, establishing a quorum and moving efficiently through procedural matters.
- Mike Kasperzak called for a motion to approve the October minutes, which was moved by David Lin and seconded by Jamil Shaikh.
- John Lang conducted the roll call vote, and all present committee members approved the minutes.
#### Upcoming Agenda Items
The committee discussed upcoming agenda topics including busking and music in downtown, general maintenance and cleanliness, and the Castro bikeway feasibility study. It was noted that busking and music issues will be addressed in the new fiscal year, while updates on maintenance and the bikeway study are expected in upcoming meetings.
- John Lang stated busking and music will be tackled in the new year, not this fiscal year.
- General maintenance and cleanliness updates are expected at the next meeting.
- The Castro bikeway feasibility study is scheduled for February.
#### Storm Drain Pollution Concern
Marina Keith raised concerns about hot dog stands dumping oil into storm drains on Castro Street, which poses environmental hazards as storm drains lead directly to the Bay. The issue has been documented and reported to the fire department for investigation.
- Marina Keith observed hot dog vendors dumping oil into storm drains near Mervyn's on Castro Street.
- She forwarded video evidence to the fire department for investigation.
- Eric Cormier requested Marina to share the video to assist in addressing the issue.
#### Downtown Development Updates
Edgar Maravilla provided updates on facade improvements and new permits for downtown properties, including a new Peruvian restaurant named Limon at 800 California Street. He also mentioned a proposed ordinance to cap office space downtown in response to state regulations limiting parking requirements, which is under review by the Environmental Planning Commission.
- Edgar reported facade improvements at 372 Castro Street and 707-737 Miller Street.
- A new restaurant, Limon, is planned at 800 California Street with tenant improvements under review.
- An ordinance to cap office space downtown and address parking minimums (AB 2097) is being considered by the Environmental Planning Commission.
#### Castro Street Pedestrian Mall and Business Updates
John Lang updated the committee on the recent City Council adoption of the pedestrian mall ordinance for Castro Street, effective within 30 days. The Castro Streets program was extended through 2023 to allow time for developing design guidelines. He also presented a detailed vacancy analysis showing varying vacancy rates across blocks, with the 400 block having the highest overall vacancies. The committee discussed market dynamics affecting retail and restaurant spaces and potential economic incentives to support retail businesses.
- The pedestrian mall ordinance for the 100-300 blocks of Castro Street was adopted with no objections.
- The Castro Streets program was extended through 2023 to transition to the pedestrian mall with new design guidelines.
- Vacancy analysis showed 19 ground floor retail vacancies, with some in permit or ownership change processes.
- Pamela Baird expressed concern about losing retail spaces to other uses.
- John Lang noted market dynamics and the possibility of retail reverting to restaurant use and vice versa.
- Discussion included potential economic incentives and vacancy taxes to encourage occupancy.
#### AB 361 Resolution for Remote Meetings
The committee discussed the continuation of remote meetings under AB 361, noting that the city manager indicated City Council may return to in-person meetings early next year. The committee voted to adopt the AB 361 resolution to continue virtual meetings for the time being, pending further clarification on state law and city policies.
- Mike Kasperzak noted the city manager's indication that City Council may resume in-person meetings in early 2023.
- John Lang explained the council is considering hybrid meetings rather than fully virtual ones.
- The committee unanimously approved the AB 361 resolution to continue remote meetings.
#### 2022 City-Sponsored Events and Holiday Activities
Maureen Grzn-Pieracci presented updates on recent and upcoming city-sponsored events, including the recent Monster Bash, the upcoming tree lighting on December 5th with a new stage location on Castro Street, and plans for Arbor Day in March. The committee discussed holiday decorations, event promotions, and community engagement efforts to support downtown businesses during the holiday season.
- The tree lighting event will move the stage onto Castro Street to allow more space.
- Holidays Around the World and other interactive activities will be part of the tree lighting celebration.
- Arbor Day will be held on March 11th with a focus on interactive booths and activities.
- Holiday decorations will include light-up penguins and wreaths, though some decorations have been stolen in past years.
- Kira Pascoe noted downtown businesses plan to extend hours during the tree lighting to encourage patronage.
- The DBA is partnering with the city to distribute coupons to event participants to support retailers.
#### Public Safety in Downtown Mountain View
Matthew Atkins from the Mountain View Police Department reported on efforts to reduce auto burglaries through a downtown ambassador program involving volunteers and increased lighting. He also described outreach efforts to assist unhoused individuals with housing and services. The committee discussed appropriate responses to street musicians and public disturbances, with guidance on reporting non-emergency issues to the police department.
- The downtown ambassador program helped reduce auto burglaries and overall crime.
- Officer McPherson leads outreach to unhoused individuals, connecting them with services and housing.
- Residents are encouraged to report suspicious or problematic activity via the police non-emergency line or online reporting.
- There is no loitering ordinance in Mountain View, limiting enforcement options for non-obstructive behavior.
- Jaywalking is no longer a crime in California as per recent legislation.
#### Pedestrian Safety and Traffic Signal Updates
John Lang updated on the phased reactivation of traffic signals on Villa Street to improve pedestrian safety. The city is educating the public about the changes and monitoring near misses. The long-term goal is to have signalized intersections and encourage mid-block crossings in the pedestrian mall area.
- Traffic signals on Villa Street are being reactivated in phases to adjust to learned pedestrian behaviors.
- Public education and signage are being used to inform residents and businesses.
- The long-term plan includes narrowing intersections and encouraging mid-block pedestrian crossings.
#### 2023 Meeting Schedule Discussion
John Lang presented the proposed 2023 meeting schedule with meetings on the first Tuesday of most months at 10:00 AM, excluding January, July, and August. The committee discussed preferred meeting times considering public engagement and member availability, with some favoring 10:00 AM or early evening meetings over early morning. The committee agreed to adopt the proposed schedule with the possibility of revisiting the time based on legal and logistical considerations.
- The proposed schedule includes meetings on the first Tuesday at 10:00 AM, excluding three months.
- Members discussed the inconvenience of 10:00 AM meetings versus early morning or evening options.
- John Lang will consult with city clerks and attorneys regarding virtual meeting provisions and possible schedule adjustments.
- The committee voted to adopt the proposed 2023 meeting schedule as presented.
#### Committee Member Attendance and Reappointments
Pamela Baird raised concerns about a committee member missing multiple meetings. John Lang explained that the council is informed and that the member is expected to voluntarily resign due to conflicts. The council will conduct interviews for new appointments in November.
- A committee member has missed several consecutive meetings and is expected to resign voluntarily.
- The City Council will hold interviews for new committee appointments on November 21st.
#### Community Events and Promotions
Kira Pascoe shared updates on upcoming events including the meet the business owners event, Taste of Mountain View, and holiday promotions such as a Santa scavenger hunt and Elf on a Shelf to encourage downtown shopping. The committee discussed the importance of supporting local retailers through these initiatives.
- The meet the business owners event is scheduled for the following day.
- Taste of Mountain View is upcoming with efforts to increase attendance through promotion.
- Holiday activities include a Santa scavenger hunt and Elf on a Shelf to drive foot traffic to downtown businesses.
- The DBA and city partnership on coupon distribution has been successful in supporting retailers.
