### Overview
  The Downtown Committee convenes with staff and community participants to address procedural matters, policy updates, development activity, public safety, events, and scheduling for 2023. Members approve the October minutes and adopt an AB 361 resolution to continue remote participation while staff monitors city guidance on hybrid/in-person meetings. Development staff reports tenant improvements and façade permits (including a new restaurant, Limon) and flags a Planning Commission item proposing a temporary cap on new downtown office space in response to AB 2097; the Hope Street hotel entitlement still faces a November 27 expiration if not re-secured. John Lang reports that the pedestrian mall ordinance for Castro Street is effective in 30 days, the Castro Streets program is extended through 2023 to allow drafting of design guidelines, and a block-level vacancy inventory identifies concentrated vacancies (notably the 400 block) with several units already in permit or ownership change. Parks and Recreation previews the December 5 tree lighting on Castro and other 2023 events while the DBA outlines promotional efforts and asks for outreach support for Taste of Mountain View. The Police Department describes a successful reduction in auto burglaries after a downtown ambassador program and commits to increased staffing, outreach, and referral work through the holidays while advising residents to use the PD non-emergency line or online reporting for timely responses. The committee adopts a first-Tuesday 10:00 AM meeting calendar for 2023, asks staff to confirm meeting modality options as state and city emergency guidance evolves, and notes member recruitment and community activity updates before adjourning.

  #### Castro Street changes
  John Lang summarizes recent policy and operational changes for Castro Street: the City Council adopts a pedestrian mall ordinance effective within 30 days, the Castro Streets operational program is extended through 2023 to allow time to draft functional and design guidelines, and a Business Improvement Assessment District second reading is scheduled for November 15. Staff also reports decluttering efforts and a block-by-block vacancy inventory that identifies the 400 block as having the highest vacancies and quantifies ground-floor and upper-floor vacancies with several spaces already progressing through permits or ownership changes.
- John Lang: The City Council adopted the pedestrian mall ordinance for the 100–300 blocks of Castro Street and it becomes effective in 30 days.
- John Lang: The Castro Streets program is extended through 2023 to bridge the transition until design and functional guidelines for the pedestrian mall are adopted.
- John Lang: A block-level vacancy inventory shows 19 ground-floor street-facing vacant units with three in permit processing and two in ownership change, and the 400 block has the highest vacancy rate.
- Pamela Baird: I am concerned that the downtown is losing retail space as market dynamics favor restaurants or other uses in some large footprints.
- John Lang: Market dynamics and parking-in-lieu fees influence use conversions, and the council can consider policy tools such as vacancy registration or vacancy taxes during the Precise Plan update.

Staff instructs residents to use the PD non-emergency line or online reporting for timely incident reporting and clarifies that there is no specific loitering ordinance downtown.
- Matthew Atkins: Downtown saw an uptick in auto burglaries in prior years that declined substantially after a collaborative ambassador program and lighting and outreach improvements.

  #### Traffic and crossings
  Staff and Police discuss recent signal reactivations and pedestrian-safety work on Villa Street and other downtown intersections with a phased approach to reintroducing signals and signage. The committee notes near misses as drivers and pedestrians re-learn signal behavior and agrees on continued signage, public education, and monitoring by traffic engineers and enforcement units. Long-term pedestrian-mall design will seek to narrow intersections and adapt crossings, but that remains a multi-year effort.
- John Lang: The City is phasing signal reactivation and posting signage to manage learned behaviors and reduce near misses at Villa Street and other intersections.
- John Lang: Traffic engineers and PD are monitoring intersections and the city continues public outreach to educate pedestrians and drivers about restored signals.
- John Lang: The long-term pedestrian mall plan anticipates narrowed intersections and mid-street crossings as a future design goal.
