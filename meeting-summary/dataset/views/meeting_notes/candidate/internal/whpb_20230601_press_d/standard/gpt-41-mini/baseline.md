### Overview
In this briefing, a senior U.S. administration official detailed the ongoing conflict in Sudan between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces, which erupted on April 15th and disrupted efforts toward civilian governance. The official outlined the severe humanitarian impact, including displacement and violence against civilians. To address the crisis, the U.S. has imposed targeted sanctions on four companies affiliated with both warring parties to restrict their access to weapons and funding, alongside visa restrictions on individuals undermining Sudan's democratic transition. The official emphasized that diplomacy remains the primary tool, highlighting ongoing talks in Jeddah and coordination with international partners such as the African Union and Arab League. Humanitarian assistance exceeding $245 million has been provided, with safeguards to ensure aid delivery despite sanctions. The briefing addressed questions on the sanctions' timing, enforcement, and potential impact, underscoring a comprehensive approach combining accountability measures, diplomatic engagement, and multilateral cooperation to restore peace and civilian rule in Sudan.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Sudan Conflict Overview
The senior administration official outlined the outbreak of fighting on April 15th between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), which disrupted over a year of negotiations for civilian rule and dashed hopes for Sudan's democratic transition. The violence has led to severe humanitarian consequences, including internal displacement and refugee flows. The U.S. government has been actively involved in ceasefire negotiations and evacuation efforts but notes repeated violations and ongoing suffering.
- The senior administration official stated that fighting erupted on April 15th between SAF and RSF, ending hopes for civilian transition.
- President Biden called the violence a betrayal of Sudanese people's demand for democracy.
- The official reported over 1 million internally displaced and 375,000 refugees due to the conflict.
- The U.S. has assisted evacuations and negotiated ceasefires, which have been repeatedly violated.
#### U.S. Sanctions on Sudanese Entities
The U.S. Department of Treasury imposed sanctions on four companies affiliated with the SAF and RSF to cut off resources fueling the conflict. These include Sudan's largest defense manufacturer and companies linked to RSF leadership involved in multiple economic sectors, including gold mining. The sanctions aim to restrict access to weapons and funding, thereby pressuring the parties to cease hostilities.
- The Treasury sanctioned four companies: two linked to SAF and two to RSF.
- Defense Industries System manufactures weapons and vehicles for SAF.
- Sudan Master Technology produces arms and vehicles for SAF.
- Al Junaid Multi Activities and Tradive General Trading are controlled by RSF leaders and involved in gold and vehicle production.
#### Visa Restrictions and Business Advisory
The Department of State imposed visa restrictions on individuals from SAF, RSF, and the former Bashir regime who undermine Sudan's democratic transition. Additionally, multiple U.S. agencies updated a business advisory to warn about risks related to the conflict for U.S. businesses and individuals. These measures aim to hold accountable those perpetuating violence and to inform stakeholders of the deteriorating situation.
- Visa restrictions target officials from SAF, RSF, and former Bashir regime.
- The business advisory update highlights growing risks to U.S. businesses due to the conflict.
- The official emphasized the visa sanctions are confidential and referred detailed questions to the State Department.
#### Diplomatic Efforts and International Coordination
The U.S. continues to pursue a diplomatic solution, emphasizing that there is no military resolution to the conflict. The official highlighted ongoing talks in Jeddah, despite SAF's formal suspension, and coordination with international partners including the African Union, Arab League, and IGAD. The goal remains to silence the guns and restore civilian-led governance in Sudan.
- The official stated diplomacy is the main approach to end the conflict.
- SAF and RSF remain in talks in Jeddah seeking confidence-building measures.
- The U.S. coordinates closely with African Union, Arab League, IGAD, and Saudi Arabia.
- The ultimate goal is civilian-led transition and peace.
#### Humanitarian Assistance and Impact of Sanctions
The U.S. has provided over $245 million in humanitarian aid to Sudan and neighboring countries. The official assured that sanctions include carveouts to prevent disruption of humanitarian assistance and medical supplies. The conflict has severely impacted civilians, with reports of atrocities and destruction of essential infrastructure.
- The U.S. has delivered more than $245 million in humanitarian aid.
- Sanctions are designed with exemptions to allow humanitarian aid flow.
- Reports include airstrikes, rape, pillaging of hospitals, and displacement.
- The official emphasized the need to protect civilians and support their aspirations.
#### Accountability and Enforcement of Sanctions
The official emphasized the importance of holding parties accountable for violence and destruction. Enforcement involves cooperation with Treasury and diplomatic channels to ensure compliance internationally. The sanctions target companies enabling the conflict, aiming to choke off resources and create incentives for peace. Future sanctions remain possible depending on developments.
- Sanctions aim to change behavior and hold perpetrators accountable.
- The U.S. works with other countries to enforce sanctions compliance.
- Targeting companies is intended to restrict access to weapons and funding.
- The official indicated readiness to impose further sanctions if necessary.
#### Questions on Sanctions' Effectiveness and Next Steps
In response to questions, the official explained that sanctions were considered before the collapse of talks and are part of a broader strategy including diplomacy. The effectiveness will be assessed based on parties' willingness to comply with ceasefires. The official acknowledged the complexity of the conflict and the need for coordinated international efforts, including legislative and multilateral engagement.
- Sanctions were planned prior to the formal suspension of talks.
- Effectiveness will be judged by compliance with ceasefires and conflict de-escalation.
- The U.S. collaborates with international partners and legislative bodies.
- Diplomacy remains central alongside sanctions to resolve the crisis.
