### Overview
The briefing, led by Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre and NSC spokesperson John Kirby aboard Air Force One, covers President Biden’s trip to Milwaukee to highlight a boom in Black small-business growth and infrastructure-driven investments, alongside a wide-ranging set of national security and domestic policy questions. Kirby details expanding humanitarian aid flows into Gaza, U.S. conditions for a new U.N. Security Council resolution, the push for another Gaza pause tied to hostage negotiations, condemnation of rising civilian casualties, and positions on China–Taiwan, Alexei Navalny, post-conflict governance in Gaza, Red Sea maritime security, foreign election interference including Cuba’s role, and the administration’s philosophy on complex prisoner swaps. He also underscores the urgency of new Ukraine funding amid congressional gridlock, warning that only one more aid package remains this year as Russia intensifies winter offensives. Jean-Pierre, after declining to comment on the Colorado Supreme Court’s Trump ballot decision due to legal and Hatch Act constraints, focuses on ongoing negotiations over a combined national security supplemental covering Ukraine, Israel, border security, and other priorities, insisting it is an emergency package and urging Republicans not to hold it up while also stressing Congress’s duty to fund the government. She contrasts what she calls Republican border “stunts” with the administration’s push for bipartisan immigration solutions, defers to regulators and USTR on matters like the Apple patent dispute and Nippon Steel’s bid for U.S. Steel, and explains the strategy behind Biden’s Milwaukee visit and broader media outreach—including appearances with figures like Conan O’Brien—as efforts to communicate economic gains and policy priorities directly to Americans, while referring space policy questions about the Artemis program to Vice President Harris’s team.
