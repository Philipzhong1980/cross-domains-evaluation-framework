### Overview
The COVID Response briefing, led by Acting Administrator Andy Slavitt with updates from Dr. Walensky and Dr. Fauci, focused on vaccination progress, pandemic status, and variant concerns. The administration highlighted significant vaccination efforts prioritizing vulnerable populations, with over 4.8 million doses administered in long-term care settings and more than 17 million doses given to seniors. Despite declines in cases and hospitalizations, COVID-19 remains a serious threat, compounded by emerging variants like B117. Dr. Fauci emphasized the importance of completing the two-dose vaccine regimen for optimal protection, including against variants, and cautioned against relying on a single dose. Enhanced genomic surveillance is underway to better understand variant prevalence. Officials advised against relaxing public health measures, underscoring the need for continued masking, distancing, and cautious travel. The administration maintains active collaboration with state leaders to promote adherence to mitigation strategies and plans to release updated CDC guidance on school reopening soon. Overall, the briefing conveyed a cautious optimism grounded in vaccination progress while stressing vigilance against evolving viral threats.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Vaccination Progress and Prioritization
The briefing began with an update on the COVID-19 vaccination efforts, focusing on the prioritization of vulnerable populations such as the elderly, frontline healthcare workers, and essential workers. The Long-Term Care Program has administered over 4.8 million doses to more than 3.7 million vulnerable individuals, with nearly 80% of residents in skilled nursing facilities receiving at least one dose. This progress is significant as it reduces the risk of death among the most at-risk groups and allows vaccine supplies to be redirected to other priority groups.
- Acting Administrator Slavitt highlighted that over 17 million doses have been administered to people aged 65 or older.
- Slavitt emphasized the American spirit of selflessness in prioritizing seniors and healthcare workers for vaccination.
- Slavitt noted ongoing efforts to increase vaccine supply, expand vaccination sites, and mobilize vaccinators, including retired medical professionals and the military.
- Slavitt committed to a whole-of-government approach and anticipated further acceleration once the American Rescue Plan is enacted.
#### Current Pandemic Status and Variant Concerns
Dr. Walensky provided a state-of-the-pandemic update, noting that while COVID-19 cases and hospital admissions have declined over the past weeks, the numbers remain high compared to previous peaks. Deaths showed a slight increase due to reporting delays. The emergence and spread of variants, particularly the B117 variant, pose a significant threat that could reverse positive trends. Continued adherence to public health measures and vaccination is critical to controlling the pandemic.
- Dr. Walensky reported a nearly 20% decline in new cases and a 17% decline in hospital admissions over recent weeks.
- She cautioned that hospitalizations remain high, with over 83,000 Americans currently hospitalized.
- Walensky confirmed 699 variant cases across 34 states, mostly the B117 variant.
- She urged continued mask-wearing, social distancing, avoiding travel and crowds, and vaccination when available.
#### Vaccine Dosing Strategy and Variant Efficacy
Dr. Fauci addressed questions about the vaccine dosing regimen, explaining that while a single dose provides some protection, the two-dose regimen yields a significantly stronger and broader immune response, including against variants. He noted that studies to evaluate single-dose efficacy would take months and may be moot given vaccine availability. The current vaccines remain effective against the B117 variant, which is expected to become dominant by March, underscoring the importance of completing the two-dose series and maintaining public health measures.
- Dr. Fauci explained that the booster dose increases antibody titers tenfold and broadens protection against variants.
- He cautioned that suboptimal immunity from a single dose could promote mutant virus selection.
- Fauci stated that vaccines are highly effective against the B117 variant but less so against the South African 351 variant.
- He emphasized vaccinating as many people as possible and continuing public health measures as the best defense against variants.
#### Variant Surveillance and Testing for Travel
In response to questions, Dr. Walensky described efforts to increase genomic sequencing of variants by tenfold and plans to collaborate with state, commercial, and academic labs to improve surveillance. She declined to estimate the true number of variant cases currently. Regarding domestic travel testing, the officials recommended avoiding travel if possible and noted that testing could help detect asymptomatic cases, serving as an additional mitigation measure.
- Dr. Walensky reported a tenfold increase in variant sequencing over the past three weeks.
- She outlined plans for expanded collaboration to enhance variant detection nationwide.
- Walensky declined to provide an estimate of actual variant prevalence beyond confirmed cases.
- Slavitt and Walensky advised against non-essential travel and acknowledged testing as a potential mitigation tool during travel.
#### Virus Mutation and Vaccine Efficacy Over Time
Dr. Fauci addressed concerns about virus mutation, stating there is no current evidence that SARS-CoV-2 is mutating to a less virulent form. While increased transmissibility may lead to more cases and hospitalizations, the virulence on a per-case basis is not confirmed to be higher. Vaccine efficacy remains high against the dominant strains, and ongoing studies will monitor long-term vaccine effectiveness as the virus evolves.
- Dr. Fauci noted no data indicating the virus is becoming less virulent.
- He acknowledged initial reports suggesting increased virulence of the UK variant require confirmation.
- Fauci explained that vaccine trials continue for two years to assess long-term efficacy.
- He emphasized that current vaccines are highly effective against the wild-type and B117 variant.
#### Vaccination Data and Registration Systems
Questions about the Long-Term Care vaccination program's total doses and the new pharmacy vaccination registration systems were addressed. Acting Administrator Slavitt clarified that 4.8 million doses have been administered to over 3.7 million people but detailed breakdowns of first and second doses or full vaccination percentages are not yet available. Pharmacy registration systems vary, and while integration is being considered, no new announcements were made regarding a unified system.
- Slavitt provided vaccination numbers for long-term care but noted data gaps on dose breakdowns and full vaccination rates.
- He acknowledged that many pharmacies have their own registration systems.
- Slavitt stated the administration is exploring ways to assist people in navigating multiple registration systems.
- No immediate changes to registration processes were announced.
#### State Mitigation Measures and School Reopening Guidance
Dr. Walensky discouraged states from relaxing public health measures given the high case counts and variant threats, recommending continued masking and distancing for the first 100 days. Regarding schools, she noted that most infections originate from the community rather than in-school transmission when mitigation is followed. CDC guidance on school reopening is forthcoming, and reducing community spread is key to safe reopening.
- Dr. Walensky advised against relaxing mask mandates and social distancing at this time.
- She highlighted that school transmissions are low with proper mitigation and mostly reflect community spread.
- Walensky announced that CDC school reopening guidance will be released soon.
- She emphasized the importance of community transmission control to enable safe school operations.
#### Federal-State Collaboration on Public Health Guidance
In response to inquiries about the administration's role in influencing state decisions on mitigation measures, Acting Administrator Slavitt and Dr. Walensky described frequent communication with governors, including weekly calls and one-on-one discussions. They emphasized a collaborative approach, sharing data and guidance to encourage adherence to public health measures, while recognizing the pressures on state officials and respecting state and local authority.
- Dr. Walensky participates in weekly calls with governors and maintains direct contact to encourage mitigation efforts.
- Slavitt emphasized collaboration and data sharing with states to promote sensible public health measures.
- They acknowledged the political and social pressures faced by governors.
- The administration refrains from disclosing specifics of state conversations to maintain productive relationships.
