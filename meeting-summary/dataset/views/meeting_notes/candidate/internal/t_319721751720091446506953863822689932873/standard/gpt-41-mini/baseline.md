### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting on December 1st, 2022, led by Vice Chair IdaRose Sylvester, focused on several key topics including enforcing the Council's attendance policy, approving prior meeting minutes, and receiving updates from subcommittees. The Housing and Security Subcommittee is planning an innovative in-person event in May to engage unstably housed individuals in community dialogue, while the Mental Health Subcommittee is in early stages of defining its focus and partnerships. Staff member Laurel James provided important updates on upcoming Brown Act training, Form 700 filings, attendance policy clarifications, the return to in-person meetings in February 2023, and Council appointments for new Commissioners. Commissioner Julie Solomon shared progress on a project documenting Mountain View's history of discrimination and inclusion. The meeting concluded with heartfelt farewells and reflections from departing Chair Annette Lynn, Commissioner Julie Solomon, and Commissioner Kevin Ball, who emphasized the importance of community and expressed gratitude for their service. Remaining Commissioners acknowledged their contributions and expressed commitment to continuing the Commission's work in the coming year.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Absence Policy Enforcement
The Commission discussed enforcing the Council policy K-02 regarding excused and unexcused absences. Commissioner Raystraw's absence due to personal illness was proposed and agreed upon as excused. The policy aims to align the Commission's attendance tracking with Council standards.
- Vice Chair Sylvester proposed defining absences as excused or unexcused per Council policy K-02.
- Laurel James clarified Commissioner Raystraw's absence was due to personal illness, qualifying as excused.
- Commissioners reached consensus to mark the absence as excused.
- Laurel James explained the policy's purpose to align attendance recording with Council guidelines.
#### Minutes Approval
The Commission reviewed and approved the minutes from the November 3rd, 2022 meeting without corrections. A motion was made and seconded, followed by a roll call vote confirming approval.
- Vice Chair Sylvester called for approval of November 3rd meeting minutes.
- Commissioner Nwokidu moved to approve the minutes, seconded by Commissioner Ball.
- All Commissioners present voted aye in the roll call vote.
- The minutes were officially approved without amendments.
#### Housing and Security Subcommittee Update
Vice Chair Sylvester provided an oral update on the Housing and Security Subcommittee's progress. The subcommittee is planning an in-person event in May focused on housing insecurity, involving unstably housed individuals in discussions and storytelling to foster community understanding. The event concept is still being refined, with involvement from community members and partners.
- Vice Chair Sylvester described the event concept involving roundtable discussions with unstably housed individuals.
- The subcommittee broadly defines unstable housing to include various living situations such as couch surfing and overcrowding.
- The event aims to build community and understanding through facilitated dialogue and storytelling.
- Commissioner Solomon expressed enthusiasm and support for the event's innovative approach.
- Commissioner Nwokidu praised the subcommittee's thoughtful planning and progress.
- Vice Chair Sylvester highlighted Malia Perez's valuable contributions to the event planning.
#### Mental Health Subcommittee Update
Commissioner Nwokidu reported on the Mental Health Subcommittee's first meeting, focusing on brainstorming goals, defining mental health, and exploring potential partners like NAMI and Parents Helping Parents. The subcommittee is still developing its program focus and logistics, with plans to discuss event timing in future meetings.
- Commissioner Nwokidu explained the subcommittee's efforts to define mental health and set goals.
- The group considered partnering with credible organizations such as NAMI.
- No event date was set during the initial meeting; scheduling will be addressed later.
- Commissioner Webb inquired about event timing to aid planning, which was acknowledged for future discussion.
- Vice Chair Sylvester confirmed subcommittee assignments and participation will be reviewed in January.
#### Staff Updates and Attendance Policy Clarification
Staff member Laurel James provided multiple updates including upcoming Brown Act training for Commissioners, reminders about Form 700 filings, and clarification on the attendance policy enforcement. She explained the policy applies to all Council advisory bodies and apologized for any confusion regarding the voting process on excused absences. Laurel also announced the return to in-person meetings starting February 2023 and upcoming Council appointments for new Commissioners.
- Laurel James announced virtual Brown Act training sessions in January 2023 for all Commissioners.
- She reminded Commissioners to file Form 700 disclosures with the Fair Political Practices Commission.
- Laurel clarified the attendance policy is Council-wide and explained the process for excused and unexcused absences.
- Commissioner Nwokidu questioned the need for voting on excused absences when policy defines them; Laurel acknowledged the feedback.
- Laurel announced that meetings will return to fully in-person format starting February 1, 2023, at City Hall.
- She informed the Commission about the December 13th Council meeting to consider new and incumbent appointments.
#### History of Discrimination and Inclusion Project Update
Commissioner Solomon updated the Commission on ongoing work related to the history of discrimination and inclusion in Mountain View. The subcommittee continues transcript edits from interviews, particularly ensuring participant sign-off on Spanish language transcripts, in collaboration with a Stanford partner.
- Commissioner Solomon reported progress on transcript edits from community interviews.
- The subcommittee is ensuring participant approval for Spanish language transcripts.
- Work continues in partnership with Camilla from Stanford.
- The project aims to document and understand local history of discrimination and inclusion.
