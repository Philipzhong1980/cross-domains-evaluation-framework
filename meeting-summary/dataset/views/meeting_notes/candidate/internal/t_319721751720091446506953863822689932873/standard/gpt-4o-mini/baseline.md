### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting on December 1, 2022, focused on year-end reflections, the introduction of a new absenteeism policy, and the approval of previous meeting minutes. Key updates included plans for a Housing and Security Subcommittee event aimed at addressing housing insecurity, insights from the Mental Health Subcommittee's first meeting, and announcements regarding upcoming Brown Act training and the return to in-person meetings in February 2023. The meeting concluded with emotional farewells to departing commissioners Kevin Ball and Julie Solomon, who were recognized for their significant contributions to the commission and the community.

### Detailed Summary
#### Reflection on Year-End Changes
IdaRose Sylvester opens the meeting by acknowledging the emotional weight of saying goodbye to departing commissioners and welcoming new ones. This transition is significant as it marks the end of the year and the beginning of new leadership within the commission.
- IdaRose Sylvester emphasizes the emotional nature of the meeting as they reflect on the year and the changes ahead.
- She mentions the importance of supporting each other during this transition.

#### Absenteeism Policy Discussion
IdaRose introduces a new policy regarding absenteeism, which requires the commission to determine whether absences are excused or unexcused based on Council policy K-02. This policy aims to ensure accountability among commission members.
- IdaRose Sylvester explains the need for a consensus on the status of absences as per the new policy.
- Laurel James clarifies that the policy has been in place for some time and aims to align the commission with Council standards.

#### Approval of Meeting Minutes
The commission reviews and approves the minutes from the previous meeting held on November 3rd, 2022. This procedural step is essential for maintaining accurate records of the commission's activities.
- IdaRose Sylvester calls for a motion to approve the minutes, which is seconded by Kevin Ball.
- The commission conducts a roll call vote to finalize the approval of the minutes.

#### Housing and Security Subcommittee Update
IdaRose provides an update on the Housing and Security Subcommittee's plans for an upcoming event aimed at fostering dialogue about housing insecurity. The event will include participants from various backgrounds to share their experiences.
