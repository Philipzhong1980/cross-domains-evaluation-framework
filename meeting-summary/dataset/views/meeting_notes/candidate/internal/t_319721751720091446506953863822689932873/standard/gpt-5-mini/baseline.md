### Overview
  Vice Chair IdaRose Sylvester chairs the Human Relations Commission meeting with staff analyst Laurel James and commissioners present. The Commission records roll call, applies Council policy K-02 to classify a recent absence as excused, and unanimously approves the November 3, 2022 minutes. Major project updates include a detailed concept for a Housing and Security Subcommittee in-person event planned for May that will center unstably housed participants in facilitated table conversations and storytelling (Vice Chair Sylvester), and an early-stage Mental Health Subcommittee that is defining scope and exploring partners such as NAMI (Commissioner Nirvana Nwokidu). Staff updates (Laurel James) announce City Attorney-hosted Brown Act training in January, remind commissioners about Form 700 filings, clarify attendance-policy procedures, and confirm that meetings will return to fully in-person format beginning February 1, 2023; Council will consider HRC appointments on December 13. Commissioner Julie Solomon reports ongoing transcript work for the history-of-discrimination project. The meeting closes with formal acknowledgments for departing Chair Annette Lynn and Commissioners Kevin Ball and Julie Solomon, who offer reflections on community and continued engagement, and the Commission adjourns to its next meeting on January 5, 2023.

  #### Housing and security
  Vice Chair Sylvester provides an oral update from the Housing and Security Subcommittee, describing progress on an in-person May event. The event concept features roundtable discussions with unstably housed individuals seated at tables (not initially identified), facilitated conversation starters, storytelling or panel presentations, and youth art displays; the date remains to be finalized. The Commission expresses support and appreciation for partner involvement as planning continues.
- Vice Chair IdaRose Sylvester reports the subcommittee met once since the last meeting and is developing an in-person May event concept.
- Vice Chair IdaRose Sylvester explains the roundtable format that pairs community members with unstably housed participants who will later identify themselves for storytelling or a panel.
- Vice Chair IdaRose Sylvester defines 'unstably housed' broadly to include people who are homeless, vehicle residents, couch-surfers, in crowded subsidized housing, or otherwise housing-insecure.
- Commissioner Julie Solomon and Commissioner Nirvana Nwokidu express enthusiasm and support for the event concept and note interest in attending.
