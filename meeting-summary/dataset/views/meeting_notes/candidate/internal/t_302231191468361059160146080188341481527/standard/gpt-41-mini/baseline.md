### Overview
The Visual Arts Committee meeting on November 9th, 2022, led by Chair Jesse Cupp and Vice Chair Toni Hsu, covered several key topics including approval of prior meeting minutes, public comments from local artists, and updates on upcoming agenda items such as downtown public improvements and public art strategies. Staff provided progress reports on Capital Improvement Program public art projects, highlighting construction milestones and contract finalizations. The committee discussed the transition back to in-person meetings mandated for February 2023, weighing accessibility concerns. A significant portion of the meeting focused on refining public art selection processes, emphasizing scale, placement, integration with park elements, and enhanced communication with artists, including potential stipends and iterative reviews. The committee also addressed the need for checkpoints during art fabrication to ensure alignment with approved designs. Updates on the Center for the Performing Arts Call for Artists were shared, with plans for jurying submissions before the December meeting. Finally, the committee sought clarification on protocols for public communications. Throughout, staff member John Lang facilitated discussions and committed to follow-ups on thresholds for art project processes and communication guidelines.

#### AB 361 Resolution and Remote Meeting Policy
The committee discussed the AB 361 resolution to continue virtual meetings during the state of emergency. Staff informed that starting February 1, 2023, all advisory committees and City Council will return to in-person meetings, with hybrid options available in some rooms. Committee members expressed mixed feelings about returning to in-person meetings, noting the convenience of virtual attendance. The committee acknowledged that voting on the resolution now would be largely symbolic as City Council's decisions will govern future meeting formats.
- John Lang shared a letter from the Clerk's office directing advisory committees to return to in-person meetings by February 1, 2023.
- Committee members discussed the pros and cons of virtual versus in-person meetings, including accessibility and comfort.
- Jesse Cupp noted that public will retain virtual attendance options even when committee members meet in person.
- The committee recognized that the AB 361 resolution vote would not affect the mandated return to in-person meetings.
- Members were encouraged to provide feedback to the Clerk's office regarding meeting format preferences.
#### Public Art Scale and Placement Discussion
Staff presented a verbal update on lessons learned from recent public art projects, focusing on scale, placement, and integration with park elements. Suggestions included using 3D modeling for larger projects, better communication of scale through comparisons, site visits by committee members, and coordination with playground equipment procurement. The committee discussed developing a checklist for artist communications regarding orientation, size, and materials. They also considered adjusting the selection process to include a larger pool of finalists for more detailed review and possible stipends for additional artist work.
- John Lang proposed using 3D modeling for larger projects to better understand art placement and scale.
- Committee members suggested site visits as a group to evaluate potential art locations.
- Coordination between public art and playground equipment selection was emphasized to ensure cohesive design.
- Toot Too Thomson recommended a standard checklist for artist communications covering color, size, material, and orientation.
- Susi Merhar suggested pre-selecting a larger pool of finalists to allow for detailed feedback and possible revisions.
- Discussion included balancing artist creativity with committee input and the potential need for stipends to compensate artists for additional work.
#### Public Art Project Checkpoints and Contractual Process
The committee discussed the need for checkpoints during the fabrication of public art to ensure the final product aligns with approved designs. Staff explained that the approved schematic is attached to the artist contract and serves as the binding document. Suggestions included contractual obligations for periodic progress updates and clearer documentation of scale, color, and materials. The committee emphasized the importance of avoiding surprises while respecting artistic freedom. The recent maintenance building mural project was noted as an outlier with additional advisory committee involvement.
- Clifford Bryant expressed concern about discrepancies between approved designs and final installations.
- John Lang proposed contractual requirements for quarterly progress documentation during fabrication.
- The schematic approved by the committee and City Council is the definitive reference for the artwork.
- Committee members agreed on the value of receiving work-in-progress updates without micromanaging artists.
- Susi Merhar highlighted the unique process for the maintenance building murals involving youth and senior advisory committees.
- The committee discussed balancing oversight with artistic flexibility.

Staff provided an update on the Call for Artists for the 2023 Center for the Performing Arts gallery display. The call closes soon with 29 submissions received so far. Committee members will be invited to jury the submissions before the December meeting to select approximately five artists for display. Questions were raised about submission criteria, including whether older works are acceptable, and the possibility of group exhibitions. Staff clarified that only solo exhibitions are contracted currently. The committee discussed promoting the call to increase submissions.
- John Lang reported 29 artist submissions received with the call closing soon.
- Committee members will review submissions and select finalists before the December 13th meeting.
- Older artwork submissions are allowed; there is no requirement for new work only.
- Group exhibitions are not currently contracted; only solo artist shows are planned.
- Jesse Cupp and others agreed to promote the call to attract more applicants for a broader selection.
