### Overview
  Staff and committee members convene to approve the October minutes, hear public comment from a local artist and a resident reporting agenda-PDF accessibility issues, and review upcoming priorities and project updates. John Lang briefs the group on near-term agenda items (downtown pedestrian mall, public art strategy, biodiversity integration, and the Great Separation project) and provides CIP updates: Reinsdorf Aquatic Center is ~25% complete and on track for fall 2023 with one outstanding insurance contract, Fayette Park construction has commenced, and Rengstorff Park maintenance/tennis design nears completion with bidding expected by year-end. The committee discusses AB 361 and a City Clerk direction to resume in-person advisory meetings on February 1, 2023, while noting interest in preserving virtual/hybrid access; staff encourages members to submit feedback to the Clerk. A lengthy discussion on public art scale, placement and process yields consensus around practical improvements — selective 3D modeling for larger projects, clearer scale references, organized site walks, sharing playground procurement details with artists, a standard checklist of placement/spec items, contractual checkpoints during fabrication, and a thresholded selection pathway with stipends for more iterative finalist processes — and John Lang commits to researching past thresholds and drafting recommendations. Staff also reports the Center for the Performing Arts gallery call has 29 submissions and closes Saturday, asks members to jury via CaFE, and will circulate the call link; finally staff will follow up with the Clerk's office on public-communication protocols and with document processing on agenda PDF/linking concerns so the committee can act on these items in subsequent meetings.

#### Art scale and placement
  Staff leads a wide-ranging discussion of lessons learned on public art scale, massing, orientation and integration with CIP elements (playground equipment, landscaping, bike lanes). Proposed process improvements include selective use of 3D modeling for larger projects, clearer scale comparisons in calls for artists, organized site walks for the committee, sharing playground procurement details with artists, a standard checklist (size, color, rotation, materials) attached to contracts, and contractual checkpoints and documentation during fabrication to prevent surprises. Committee members debate thresholds to trigger iterative processes and stipends for finalists, community involvement in park-specific selections, and the balance between artist freedom and committee input; John Lang commits to researching historical thresholds and returning with recommendations.
- John Lang proposed using 3D modeling for larger projects, improving scale comparisons in materials, organizing group site visits, and sharing playground equipment and palette details with artists to improve fit.
- John Lang highlighted that some on-site adjustments are typically possible but stressed the value of clarifying orientation and placement expectations with artists before approval.
- Toot Too Thomson proposed creating a standard checklist (color, size, material, rotation) to ensure the VAC addresses placement and orientation consistently, and John Lang suggested attaching such checklists to contracts.
- Susi Merhar recommended a two-stage selection process with a larger pool of finalists so the committee can apply site-specific information and then narrow candidates, and Toni Hsu emphasized the need to offer stipends to finalists if the process requires additional artist work.
- Clifford Bryant requested that staff explore incorporating resident input used during playground selection into the public art selection process to improve community alignment.
- Don Whitebread cautioned against over-specifying requirements in calls for artists so as not to exclude creative solutions.
- John Lang committed to researching past dollar thresholds and processes that triggered more iterative selection methods and to return with a proposed threshold-based approach and contract/checkpoint language.
