### Overview
The meeting is an Administrative Zoning public hearing led by Zoning Administrator Lindsay Hagan, focusing on consent calendar items and a single public hearing item. For consent item 3.1, a proposed development agreement at 1040 Terra Bella Avenue, Hagan announces that the application is continued to a future date with no discussion or action and will be renoticed when rescheduled. For item 3.2 at 2500 Grant Road, she explains that the hospital-site landscape project seeks permits to remove 10 trees, including five heritage trees, under a CEQA categorical exemption, takes no public comment, and then approves the project with the posted conditions after confirming she supports the tree removals and replacement plan. In the main public hearing item 4.1 at 2580 West El Camino Real, project planner Philip Brennan outlines Carbon Health’s request for a provisional use permit, development review permit, and planned community permit for a 3,000‑square‑foot medical clinic and awning replacements, noting staff’s finding of consistency with the San Antonio Precise Plan, the General Plan, and a CEQA exemption for existing facilities; with no public comments and no separate applicant presentation, Hagan concurs that the use is appropriate for the mixed‑use shopping center, moves to approve the project subject to staff’s conditions, and closes by noting that all actions from the meeting are appealable to the City Council within 10 days via the project planner.

#### Consent calendar project 2500 Grant Road trees
Lindsay Hagan presents item 3.2 for 2500 Grant Road, involving landscape modifications and tree removals on an approximately 40‑acre hospital site, including removal of 10 trees, five of which are heritage trees. She notes that the request includes a planned community permit, development review permit, and heritage tree removal permit, along with a categorical CEQA exemption under Section 15304. After opening public comment and receiving no speakers, she states she has reviewed the project materials, tree information, and improvement plans and concurs with the proposed removals and replacement plan. The current state is that she approves item 3.2 with the conditions of approval posted online.
- Lindsay Hagan describes item 3.2 at 2500 Grant Road as a request for a planned community permit, development review permit, and heritage tree removal permit for landscape modifications on an existing nearly 40‑acre hospital site.
- Lindsay Hagan specifies that the project includes removal of 10 trees, including five heritage trees, with a determination that the project is categorically exempt under Section 15304 of the CEQA Guidelines.
- Lindsay Hagan opens public comment on items 3.1 and 3.2 and records that no members of the public choose to speak.
- Lindsay Hagan states that she has reviewed the posted materials and tree information and concurs with both the tree removals and the replacement plan.
- Lindsay Hagan approves item 3.2 with the conditions of approval as posted online.
#### Provisional use permit for 2580 W El Camino Real
The meeting turns to public hearing item 4.1 for 2580 West El Camino Real, a request to allow a new medical service use (Carbon Health) in an existing approximately 3,000‑square‑foot building within a larger 5.4‑acre shopping center. Project planner Philip Brennan explains that the application includes a provisional use permit for the medical use, a development review permit, and a planned community permit to replace existing awnings, and that staff finds the project consistent with the San Antonio Precise Plan, the General Plan mixed‑use center designation, and categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15301 for existing facilities. After opening public comment and receiving no speakers, Lindsay Hagan states that she has reviewed the materials and conditions of approval and agrees that the medical use is appropriate for the location and will provide needed medical services to the area. The current state is that she moves to approve item 4.1 with staff’s recommended conditions, and she notes that any actions taken at the meeting are appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days by contacting the project planner.
- Lindsay Hagan introduces item 4.1 at 2580 West El Camino Real as a request for a provisional use permit for a new medical service use, Carbon Health, in an existing roughly 3,000‑square‑foot commercial building on a 5‑acre site.
- Lindsay Hagan notes that the request also includes a development review permit and a planned community permit to replace awnings, with a determination that the project is categorically exempt under Section 15301 of the CEQA Guidelines.
- Philip Brennan states that the project site lies within the P40 San Antonio Precise Plan area and that the proposed use and improvements are consistent with the Precise Plan standards and the General Plan’s mixed‑use center designation.
- Philip Brennan reports that staff determines the project qualifies for a categorical exemption as an existing facility under CEQA Section 15301 and recommends conditional approval based on staff’s analysis, findings, and conditions.
- Philip Brennan notes that the applicant is present on the line but does not have a separate presentation and is available only for questions.
- Lindsay Hagan opens public comment on item 4.1 and records that no members of the public request to speak.
- Lindsay Hagan states that she has reviewed the online materials and recommended conditions of approval and finds the use appropriate for the location and beneficial for providing medical services to that area of the city.
- Lindsay Hagan moves to approve item 4.1 with the conditions of approval recommended by City staff.
- Lindsay Hagan informs attendees that all actions taken at the meeting may be appealed to the City Council within 10 calendar days by contacting the project planner.
