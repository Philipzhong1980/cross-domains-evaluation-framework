### Overview
The administrative zoning public hearing on December 14, 2022, led by Lindsay Hagan, addressed several key topics including public communication, consent calendar items, and a public hearing for a new medical service. The meeting began with no public comments, followed by the continuation of the 1040 Terabella Ave project and the approval of the 2500 Grant Rd project concerning tree removals. The public hearing for 2580 W El Camino Real featured a presentation by Phillip Brennan, who recommended approval for a new medical service, which was supported by Hagan. The meeting concluded with a reminder about the appeal process for the decisions made.

### Detailed Summary
#### Public Communication Section
The meeting begins with an opportunity for public communication, allowing individuals to address the zoning administrator on any non-agenda items. This section is important as it provides a platform for community input, although no public comments were received during this time.
- Lindsay Hagan notes that speakers are limited to three minutes each.
- State law prohibits action on non-agenda items, as stated by Lindsay Hagan.

#### Consent Calendar Items
The consent calendar includes two project applications: 1040 Terabella Ave and 2500 Grant Rd. The first item is continued to a future date, while the second item, concerning tree removals and modifications at a hospital site, is approved by the zoning administrator after review.
- Lindsay Hagan announces that item 3.1 is continued to a future date and requires renoticing.
- Lindsay Hagan approves item 3.2, agreeing with the tree removal and replacement plan.

#### Public Hearing Item 4.1
The meeting transitions to a public hearing for a provisional use permit for a new medical service at 2580 W El Camino Real. The project planner, Phillip Brennan, presents the application, which is deemed consistent with local planning standards and is recommended for approval.
- Phillip Brennan presents the project details and confirms it meets the San Antonio Precise Plan standards.
- Lindsay Hagan expresses support for the medical service use, stating it is appropriate for the location.

#### Approval and Appeal Process
After reviewing the public hearing item, the zoning administrator motions for approval of the project with conditions. The meeting concludes with information on the appeal process for any decisions made during the session.
- Lindsay Hagan motions to approve item 4.1 with conditions as posted by city staff.
- Lindsay Hagan reminds attendees that actions can be appealed to the City Council within 10 days.
