### Overview
  The briefing began with a disruption in the press room that Karine Jean-Pierre called unacceptable and used to emphasize decorum and respect for guests. Jean-Pierre welcomed the cast of Ted Lasso, and Jason Sudeikis highlighted mental-health advocacy ahead of their meeting with the President and First Lady. NSC Coordinator John Kirby led a substantive briefing focused on President Xi’s visit to Moscow, urging China to press Russia to withdraw from Ukraine and cautioning that any ceasefire that leaves Russian forces in Ukraine would legitimize illegal conquests; Kirby also discussed broader China-Russia relations, the freeze in military-to-military channels, and ongoing diplomatic efforts to restore high-level engagement. Kirby reviewed developments on Israel, praising understandings reached in Sharm el-Sheikh and relaying President Biden’s call with Prime Minister Netanyahu about security and judicial-reform compromises, announced the recovery of U.S. hostage Jeff Woodke with no concessions made, marked the 20th anniversary of Operation Iraqi Freedom while reaffirming partnerships with Iraq, and announced an additional $350 million assistance package for Ukraine. Jean-Pierre then summarized domestic priorities: the President’s budget and a daily rebuttal of the House Freedom Caucus MAGA budget, Nowruz greetings and support for Iranian women’s rights, and a series of exchanges on banking stability where she said regulators have acted to protect depositors, warned Congress it must give regulators stronger tools, and affirmed confidence in the Fed Chair; she also addressed pending items including the COVID origins bill, abortion-related ballot initiatives and mifepristone litigation, climate and conservation actions (including upcoming White House events), and cultural awards, while declining to comment on ongoing investigations or operational security specifics. The briefing covered U.S. diplomatic posture across multiple regions, reiterated commitments to allies and to bringing Americans home, and called for continued diplomatic engagement with China while stressing that any peace process for Ukraine must include Ukraine’s voice.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Briefing room decorum
  The briefing opens with a disruptive exchange in the briefing room about who is being called on, prompting repeated calls for decorum. Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre addresses the disturbance, apologizes to guests, and warns that disrespect toward colleagues and visitors will not be tolerated. The exchange delayed the start of the substantive briefing and set a tone emphasizing respect for guests and the press corps.
- An unidentified reporter challenged question selection and accused the briefing team of discrimination during the opening, saying they had been ignored for months.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE apologized to guests and told the room that the behavior that had just occurred was unacceptable and that the briefing would continue only with decorum.
- An unidentified press corps member apologized on behalf of reporters to the public for the earlier disruption and stressed the press’s responsibility to viewers.
  #### Ted Lasso mental health
  Karine Jean-Pierre introduces actor Jason Sudeikis and cast members visiting the White House to raise awareness about mental health, noting the President has prioritized mental health in his Unity Agenda. Sudeikis emphasizes that mental-health struggles cut across political and demographic lines, encourages people to ask for help and to check in with others, and thanks the White House for the meeting with the President and First Lady. He also took one light question about the 2026 World Cup and closed the short appearance.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President has made mental health the centerpiece of his Unity Agenda and welcomed the cast to discuss the issue.
- MR. SUDEIKIS said mental-health struggles affect people regardless of background and encouraged everyone to check in with loved ones and seek help when needed.
- MR. SUDEIKIS said the cast was honored to meet the President and First Lady and to highlight mental-health conversations.
  #### Xi visit to Moscow
  NSC Coordinator John Kirby briefed on President Xi’s visit to Moscow and urged China to press Russia to withdraw forces from Ukraine and to stop attacks on civilians and infrastructure. Kirby stressed that any ceasefire that leaves Russian forces on Ukrainian sovereign territory would effectively legitimize Russia’s conquests and violate the U.N. Charter. He encouraged China to engage directly with Ukrainian leadership and warned that the world is watching for China’s stance.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States urges President Xi to press President Putin to respect Ukraine’s sovereignty and to withdraw Russian forces from Ukrainian territory.
- MR. KIRBY warned that a ceasefire that leaves Russian forces inside Ukraine would ratify illegal conquests and could enable Russia to entrench and restart the war later.
- MR. KIRBY encouraged China to speak directly with President Zelenskyy so that Chinese leaders hear Ukrainian perspectives and not only Russia’s.
  #### Israel-Sharm el-Sheikh talks
  Kirby detailed U.S. welcome for understandings reached in Sharm el-Sheikh between senior Israeli and Palestinian Authority officials to improve security and economic stability. He described the meetings as the highest level in nearly a decade and a foundation to deescalate tensions ahead of religious holidays. Kirby also relayed President Biden’s call with Prime Minister Netanyahu, where Biden reaffirmed U.S. security commitments and urged compromise on judicial reforms consistent with democratic checks and balances.
- MR. KIRBY said the understandings in Sharm el-Sheikh were candid, constructive, and a step toward reducing violence and improving security coordination.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States looks forward to continued discussions during Ramadan, Passover, and Easter to maintain stability.
- MR. KIRBY said President Biden reaffirmed unwavering commitment to Israel’s security while urging Israeli leaders to pursue judicial-reform compromises with broad public support.
  #### Jeff Woodke recovery
  Kirby announced that U.S. citizen Jeff Woodke, held hostage in Africa for more than six years, has been recovered and is in U.S. government custody. He thanked the Nigerien government and the interagency team—military, law enforcement, and intelligence—that worked on the recovery, and said Woodke will receive medical and post-isolation care and privacy as he reunites with family. The administration emphasized its ongoing commitment to bringing Americans wrongfully detained abroad home.
- MR. KIRBY said Jeff Woodke is safe, in U.S. government custody, and will receive full medical screening and post-isolation support.
- MR. KIRBY thanked the government of Niger and credited a multi-pronged interagency effort, with French support, for securing Woodke’s release.
- MR. KIRBY said there were no concessions or swaps and described the recovery as hard, deliberate diplomatic work.
  #### Iraq anniversary & partnerships
  Kirby marked the 20th anniversary of Operation Iraqi Freedom by reaffirming the U.S. partnership with Iraqi security forces in an enable/advise/assist role and noting ongoing threats from ISIS in Iraq and Syria. He emphasized the administration’s commitment to U.S. service members and military families, memorializing the fallen and those permanently changed by the war. Kirby said the President and First Lady remain committed to supporting veterans and families affected by the conflict.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States continues to partner with Iraqi Security Forces in an enable, advise, and assist role because of the ongoing ISIS threat.
- MR. KIRBY noted about 4,399 U.S. troops did not return from the war and stressed the administration’s continuing commitment to military families.
- MR. KIRBY said the President and First Lady remain committed to remembering and supporting those who served and sacrificed.
  #### Black Sea grain deal
  In response to a question, Kirby welcomed a 60-day extension of the grain-export arrangement that helps deliver food to low- and middle-income countries. He highlighted recent shipments and credited Turkey and the U.N. for facilitating exports, while declining to speculate on retaliatory steps if Moscow suspends the deal later. The immediate focus, he said, is getting ships loaded and delivering grain where it is needed.
- MR. KIRBY said the 60-day extension of the grain arrangement is a positive development and that ships have already left with hundreds of thousands of metric tons of corn.
- MR. KIRBY credited Turkey and the U.N. for helping move grain to countries suffering food insecurity due to the war.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. will focus on ensuring shipments reach destinations and declined to speculate on specific reprisals if Russia halts the deal.
  #### China potentially aiding Russia
  Reporters pressed Kirby on whether China might provide lethal assistance to Russia and how the U.S. would respond. Kirby reiterated the U.S. has not seen confirmation that China intends to supply lethal weapons and argued it would be inconsistent with President Xi’s stated goals; he cautioned it would not be in China’s interest. He also emphasized continued diplomacy and the importance of keeping communication lines open.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States has seen no indication that China has decided to provide lethal weapons to Russia and that such action would be against China’s stated goals.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration believes it is not in China’s interest to aid efforts that would accelerate civilian suffering in Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. will watch the outcomes of the Xi-Putin meeting and keep diplomatic channels open to manage tensions.
  #### US-China communications
  Kirby addressed the status of high-level U.S.-China engagements, noting the administration still seeks to resume Secretary Blinken’s postponed Beijing trip and is discussing potential visits by Secretaries Yellen and Raimondo. He said the White House wants to keep lines of communication open despite tensions and that the President is willing to speak with President Xi at an appropriate time. Kirby also said some military-to-military channels remain frozen and are a problem the U.S. wishes to address.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration is working to restore dialogues, including a hoped-for return of Secretary Blinken to Beijing and potential visits by Secretaries Yellen and Raimondo.
- MR. KIRBY said President Biden will speak with President Xi when the timing and context are appropriate for both leaders.
- MR. KIRBY said certain military-to-military lines with China remain closed and that reopening those channels is important.
  #### Domestic security and protests
  Reporters asked whether the White House has been briefed on security threats or operational planning in light of calls by the former President for protests around a potential indictment. Kirby said he was not aware of specific briefings or operational federal moves but emphasized constant monitoring, especially after January 6. Jean-Pierre later reiterated the administration is always prepared and repeatedly urged peaceful protest without commenting on ongoing investigations.
- MR. KIRBY said he is not tracking any specific threats or federal operational moves related to potential protests but noted continuous monitoring.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President has consistently urged Americans to protest peacefully and that the administration is preparing as necessary.
- MR. KIRBY said monitoring is routine and ongoing, particularly given recent history and security concerns.
  #### Russia-China relationship
  In answering questions about the Xi-Putin meetings, Kirby described the relationship as burgeoning and largely transactional—a marriage of convenience—rather than built on deep trust or longstanding affinity. He said both countries chafe at U.S. leadership and the postwar international order, and that their tightening ties are partly a response to U.S. alliances and influence. The administration will continue to watch interactions and react diplomatically to any actions undermining international norms.
- MR. KIRBY described the Russia-China relationship as a burgeoning, convenience-based partnership rather than a long-standing alliance.
- MR. KIRBY said both countries chafe at U.S. leadership and the international rules-based order established after World War Two.
- MR. KIRBY cautioned that closer ties do not equate to deep mutual trust given differing long-term interests.
  #### Indian consulate attack & U.S.-India ties
  Reporters raised an incident involving vandalism at the Indian consulate in San Francisco; Kirby condemned the attack and said the State Department’s Diplomatic Security Service is coordinating with local authorities. He also noted the recent Senate confirmation of a U.S. ambassador to India and said the bilateral relationship remained a priority even when the post was vacant. Kirby emphasized that an ambassador is valuable but that competent embassy staff advanced U.S. interests in the interim.
- MR. KIRBY condemned the vandalism at the Indian consulate and said the Diplomatic Security Service is working with local authorities to investigate.
- MR. KIRBY said having a Senate-confirmed U.S. ambassador to India is important but that a competent chargé and career staff sustained the relationship when the post was vacant.
- MR. KIRBY said the Biden administration prioritizes bilateral ties with India given the country’s regional and global importance.
  #### Alliances and AUKUS
  Asked how the U.S. is countering the growing Russia-China relationship, Kirby highlighted revitalizing alliances and partnerships, citing AUKUS and cooperation with allies as a primary tool. He pointed to the recent AUKUS announcement as an example of concrete collaboration and argued the United States leads a broad network of partners that Russia and China lack. Kirby framed U.S. policy as advancing shared goals with partners rather than merely countering competitors.
- MR. KIRBY said the administration focuses on revitalizing alliances and partnerships to advance shared foreign policy and security goals.
- MR. KIRBY cited AUKUS and the President’s engagements with allied leaders as examples of strengthening collaboration.
- MR. KIRBY said U.S. convening power and voluntary coalition-building are key advantages the country holds globally.
  #### Ukraine military assistance
  Kirby announced another U.S. assistance package for Ukraine worth $350 million, largely for ammunition, and reiterated U.S. support for Ukraine’s defense. He emphasized that Russia has lost ground and faces battlefield setbacks, and that the U.S. will continue to help Ukraine be well-postured for future defense needs. Kirby reiterated the position that the war could end if Russia simply withdrew its forces.
- MR. KIRBY announced a new $350 million assistance package for Ukraine, primarily providing ammunition.
- MR. KIRBY said Russia has lost significant territory and continues active fighting only in limited areas like Bakhmut.
- MR. KIRBY reiterated that the war could end immediately if Russia withdrew its forces from Ukraine.
  #### Military-to-military lines with China
  During exchanges, Kirby confirmed that military-to-military lines with China remain frozen and that reopening them was one of the objectives of planned diplomatic visits. He said those channels are a problem and that efforts—such as a hoped-for Blinken trip—aim to thaw those communications. Maintaining diverse diplomatic channels remains a priority as tensions rise.
- MR. KIRBY said military-to-military lines with China are currently closed and that reopening them is important to reduce risk.
- MR. KIRBY said Secretary Blinken’s postponed trip to Beijing was intended in part to address frozen military channels.
- MR. KIRBY said diplomatic avenues are still open and the administration is pursuing visits by economic and diplomatic officials.
  #### China's diplomatic role
  Reporters asked about China’s broader diplomatic initiatives, including its 12-point Ukraine proposal and its role brokering the Saudi-Iran détente. Kirby said the U.S. sees no evidence China has engaged directly with Kyiv on the proposal and urged any peace effort to include Ukrainian input. He also said it is too soon to assess broader regional security implications from China-brokered deals and emphasized that any negotiated settlement must start with Ukraine’s consent.
- MR. KIRBY said to date he was not aware of direct conversations between China and Kyiv regarding China’s 12-point Ukraine proposal.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States believes any negotiated settlement requires Ukraine’s voice at the table and should not freeze in illegal Russian occupation.
- MR. KIRBY said it is too early to evaluate security impacts of China-brokered moves like the Saudi-Iran rapprochement.
  #### President's budget and MAGA critique
  Karine Jean-Pierre summarized the President’s recently released budget priorities—investing in America, lowering costs, strengthening Social Security and Medicare, and reducing the deficit—and contrasted it with the House Freedom Caucus MAGA budget. She said the administration will spend the week highlighting how the MAGA proposal would harm public safety, raise costs for families, and undermine national security. Jean-Pierre outlined immediate examples of cuts alleged in the MAGA plan and said the White House will continue to rebut that proposal.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President’s budget focuses on investing in America, lowering costs, protecting Social Security and Medicare, and reducing the deficit.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the House Freedom Caucus’s MAGA budget would endanger public safety and weaken national security and that the White House will highlight those impacts.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE listed examples the administration says the MAGA budget would cut, including law enforcement and transportation safety inspections.
  #### Nowruz and Iran human rights
  Jean-Pierre offered Nowruz greetings and used the occasion to reaffirm U.S. support for Iranian women protesting for human rights and fundamental freedoms. She said the administration will continue holding Iranian officials accountable for abuses and stands with citizens inspiring the world through their conviction. The Nowruz statement links cultural observance to broader human-rights messaging.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE wished Nowruz greetings to those celebrating and highlighted the holiday’s themes of renewal and hope.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the United States continues to stand with the women of Iran fighting for human rights and fundamental freedoms.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration will work with partners to hold Iranian officials accountable for attacks on their people.
  #### Banking stability and regulatory response
  In a series of exchanges, Jean-Pierre addressed recent banking disruptions, saying the administration’s focus is on ensuring financial-system stability and that regulators are handling deposit protections. She declined to announce new deposit-insurance measures, reiterated that taxpayer funds are not at risk in recent actions, and called on Congress to give regulators stronger tools—such as clawbacks of executive compensation and civil penalties. She also said the President has confidence in Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell and that the administration will continue to work with regulators and urge congressional action.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration’s priority is financial-system stability and that Treasury and bank regulators are leading on deposit protections.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said there is no new announcement on expanding insurance above $250,000 and that regulators have taken decisive actions to protect depositors.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President supports giving regulators tools to claw back executive compensation, impose civil penalties, and ban executives from reentering the industry, and she called on Congress to act.
  #### COVID origins bill & legal matters
  Jean-Pierre said the White House is reviewing the COVID origins bill forwarded by Congress and had no decision to announce on whether the President will sign it. She reiterated the administration’s practice of not commenting on ongoing investigations when asked about related issues and declined to speculate on congressional requests tied to legal cases. The White House is maintaining customary restraint on matters that connect to active legal processes.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration is reviewing the COVID origins bill and had no announcement about signing it at this time.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declined to comment on ongoing investigations or congressional probes that connect to active legal matters.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterated the administration will focus on governing priorities rather than litigating hypotheticals from the podium.
  #### Abortion ballot initiatives & mifepristone litigation
  Reporters asked about state-level ballot initiatives to expand abortion access and pending litigation over mifepristone; Jean-Pierre reiterated the President’s commitment to protecting reproductive rights. She said the administration supports legislation expanding access to reproductive healthcare and emphasized that FDA decisions should be based on science rather than court rulings. On mifepristone, she said the administration is awaiting judicial outcomes before commenting further.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President supports efforts to protect women’s rights to health care and welcomes legislation that expands access to abortion services.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said decisions about FDA-approved medications like mifepristone should be grounded in safety, science, and data, and she declined to speculate ahead of court rulings.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE called proposals for a national abortion ban shameful and said the administration will continue to speak out for reproductive freedom.
  #### Climate and conservation actions
  Responding to questions about a new IPCC report and the Willow oil project, Jean-Pierre defended the administration’s conservation record and said tomorrow’s White House conservation summit will highlight historic investments in nature. She noted legal constraints shaped the Willow decision and recounted accomplishments such as protecting lands and waters and declaring parts of the Arctic off-limits to new leasing. The administration frames its approach as advancing conservation while following legal processes.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President is proud of a record of conservation actions, including protecting more lands and waters than any president since JFK.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the Willow decision reflected legal constraints and that the administration continues to pursue climate protections and conservation investments.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the conservation summit will showcase administration actions and historic investments for environment and nature.
  #### Arts and Humanities Awards
  Jean-Pierre announced that the President will host a White House ceremony to present the 2021 National Humanities Medals and National Medals of Arts, with the First Lady attending. She said more information on recipients would be shared later and that the ceremony will take place in the East Room. The event marks a return of presidential recognition for contributions to arts and humanities during the administration.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President will host a ceremony in the East Room to present the 2021 National Humanities Medals and National Medals of Arts.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said First Lady Jill Biden will attend and that more details on recipients will be provided later.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said this ceremony is the administration’s first such awards event in the past two years.
  #### Waco rally security
  At the end of the briefing, reporters asked whether the administration is concerned about anti-government activity around a planned Waco rally marking the 30th anniversary of the Branch Davidian standoff. Jean-Pierre said she was not tracking any security concerns and had nothing more to share, declining to speculate on the former President’s remarks or future speech content. The administration emphasized preparedness but did not provide operational details.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the White House was not tracking specific security concerns related to the Waco rally and had nothing further to read out.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declined to speculate about what the former President might say or do at the event.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterated general preparedness without discussing operational security measures.
