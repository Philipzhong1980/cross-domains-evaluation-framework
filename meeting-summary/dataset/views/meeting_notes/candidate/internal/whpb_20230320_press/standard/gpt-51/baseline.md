### Overview
The briefing opens with a tense confrontation over pressroom decorum before Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre introduces “Ted Lasso” star Jason Sudeikis and cast members, who visit the White House to meet President Biden and the First Lady about mental health as part of the President’s Unity Agenda; Sudeikis uses the podium to urge open conversations, mutual support, and reduced stigma, praising the administration’s efforts to expand mental health care. After reasserting decorum standards, Jean-Pierre brings in National Security Council spokesman John Kirby, who focuses on Chinese President Xi Jinping’s visit to Moscow, warning that any China-backed ceasefire that leaves Russian troops in Ukraine would entrench illegal gains, calling for Xi to speak directly with President Zelenskyy, and underscoring U.S. concerns about potential Chinese lethal aid to Russia in the context of a deepening but asymmetric Russia-China partnership. Kirby also details U.S. efforts to support Israeli-Palestinian security talks and encourage compromise on Israeli judicial reforms, announces the release of longtime U.S. hostage Jeff Woodke without concessions, reflects on the Iraq War’s 20th anniversary and the ongoing ISIS threat, addresses the Black Sea grain deal, and fields questions on topics ranging from China’s global influence and Africa outreach to Trump-related protest risks, India relations, and Biden’s upcoming trip to Canada. In the domestic segment, Jean-Pierre contrasts Biden’s budget—which she says invests in America, protects Social Security and Medicare, and cuts the deficit without taxing those under $400,000—with what she calls a dangerous “MAGA budget” from the House Freedom Caucus that would slash border, law enforcement, rail, and air safety resources, while also extending Nowruz greetings and pledging continued support for Iranian women. She then answers extensive questions on banking turmoil, insisting that decisive regulatory actions have stabilized the system and protected depositors without using taxpayer funds, while calling on Congress to strengthen rules on bank executives and mid-sized banks, affirming the Federal Reserve’s independence and her confidence in Chair Jerome Powell, and declining to confirm reported outreach to Warren Buffett. Throughout, Jean-Pierre refuses to comment on the specifics of Trump’s potential indictment or related House Republican oversight, but reiterates that any protests must remain peaceful; she also defends Biden’s climate record amid criticism over the Willow project by citing legal constraints and offsetting conservation measures, signals support for state-level efforts to expand abortion access and warns of the unprecedented implications of a Texas case targeting the abortion pill mifepristone, previews a conservation summit and arts and humanities awards ceremony, and notes ongoing review of a COVID origins bill and forthcoming Economic Report of the President before adjourning the briefing.
