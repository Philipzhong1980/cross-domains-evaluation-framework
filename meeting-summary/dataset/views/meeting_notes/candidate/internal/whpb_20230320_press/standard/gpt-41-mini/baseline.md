### Overview
The White House press briefing featured key discussions on mental health advocacy, highlighted by Jason Sudeikis and the "Ted Lasso" cast's visit to promote awareness and support, aligning with President Biden's Unity Agenda. National Security Council spokesperson John Kirby provided detailed updates on President Xi's visit to Moscow, emphasizing U.S. concerns over China's role in the Ukraine conflict and urging respect for Ukraine's sovereignty. Kirby also discussed recent Israeli-Palestinian security talks, the safe recovery of hostage Jeff Woodke, and the 20th anniversary of Operation Iraqi Freedom, underscoring ongoing U.S. commitments. The administration addressed banking system stability, criticizing the House Freedom Caucus's MAGA budget and calling for stronger regulations to protect public safety and economic security. Additionally, the briefing reaffirmed support for women's reproductive rights and outlined the President's climate and conservation initiatives. Throughout, the administration emphasized diplomatic engagement, alliance strengthening, and a focus on advancing American interests globally.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Mental Health Advocacy by Ted Lasso Cast
Jason Sudeikis and his castmates from the show "Ted Lasso" visit the White House to discuss mental health, a key focus of President Biden's Unity Agenda. Sudeikis emphasizes the importance of checking in on others and destigmatizing mental health struggles, encouraging open conversations and seeking help when needed. The visit highlights the administration's commitment to mental health awareness and support.
- Jason Sudeikis states the visit is an honor and stresses mental health's importance to all Americans.
- Sudeikis encourages people to check in sincerely with friends, family, and coworkers about their mental health.
- He acknowledges the stigma around mental health and advocates for seeking professional or personal support.
- Sudeikis notes the President's team is working to make mental health resources widely available.
#### China-Russia Relations and Ukraine Conflict
John Kirby addresses President Xi's visit to Moscow and China's 12-point plan for Ukraine, emphasizing the U.S. position that any ceasefire must include Russian withdrawal from Ukrainian territory. Kirby expresses concern that China may support a ceasefire that legitimizes Russian occupation, which the U.S. opposes. The administration encourages China to engage directly with Ukraine and press Russia to end hostilities and respect sovereignty.
- Kirby states the U.S. urges China to advocate for Russian troop withdrawal from Ukraine.
- He warns against ceasefires that leave Russian forces in Ukraine, which would violate international law.
- Kirby notes China has not communicated directly with Ukraine regarding its peace plan.
- He describes the China-Russia relationship as a "marriage of convenience" challenging U.S. global leadership.
#### Israel-Palestinian Security Discussions
Kirby reports on recent high-level meetings in Sharm el-Sheikh between Israeli and Palestinian officials, facilitated by the U.S., Egypt, and Jordan, aimed at improving security and economic stability. These talks mark a significant step after nearly a decade without such engagement and are intended to reduce violence and deescalate tensions, with ongoing discussions planned during upcoming religious observances.
- Kirby welcomes the security and economic discussions between Israel and the Palestinian Authority.
- He highlights U.S. involvement alongside Egypt and Jordan in facilitating these talks.
- The meetings are the first of their kind in nearly 10 years, aiming to reduce violence.
- President Biden supports urgent collaborative steps to maintain a two-state solution and condemns terrorism.
#### Hostage Recovery of Jeff Woodke
The administration announces the safe recovery of Jeff Woodke, a U.S. citizen held hostage in Africa for over six years. Kirby details the multi-agency effort involving military, law enforcement, and intelligence to secure his release without concessions. The White House requests privacy for Woodke and his family as he receives medical care and reintegrates into society.
- Kirby confirms Woodke's safe recovery and ongoing medical care.
- He credits a coordinated interagency effort and Nigerien government cooperation for the release.
- No concessions or ransom payments were made in securing Woodke's freedom.
- The administration remains committed to bringing Americans held hostage abroad home safely.
#### Operation Iraqi Freedom 20th Anniversary
Kirby reflects on the 20th anniversary of Operation Iraqi Freedom, acknowledging the sacrifices of U.S. military personnel and their families. He emphasizes ongoing U.S. partnership with Iraqi Security Forces to counter ISIS threats and reiterates the administration's commitment to supporting veterans and their families affected by the conflict.
- Kirby notes 4,399 U.S. troops died and over 30,000 were wounded in the Iraq war.
- He highlights continued U.S. support for Iraqi Security Forces in an advisory role.
- The administration honors the sacrifices of service members and their families.
- President Biden and the First Lady remain committed to supporting military families.
#### U.S. Position on China Providing Aid to Russia
Kirby addresses concerns about potential Chinese military aid to Russia amid the Ukraine conflict. He states the U.S. has seen no indication China has decided to provide lethal assistance and believes it is not in China's interest. The administration continues diplomatic engagement with China, emphasizing the importance of open communication channels and discouraging actions that would prolong the war.
- Kirby says the U.S. has no confirmation China will provide lethal aid to Russia.
- He asserts such aid would contradict China's stated goals for peace and sovereignty.
- The administration is working to maintain diplomatic and economic communication with China.
- Kirby stresses that providing weapons would not help end the war faster.
#### U.S. Efforts to Strengthen Alliances Against China-Russia Partnership
Kirby explains the U.S. strategy to counter the growing China-Russia relationship by revitalizing alliances and partnerships globally, including the recent AUKUS agreement with Australia and the UK. He emphasizes that the U.S. approach focuses on advancing shared goals rather than containment, leveraging a broad network of allies to uphold international norms and security.
- Kirby highlights the AUKUS deal to provide Australia with nuclear-powered submarines.
- He describes U.S. efforts to strengthen alliances in the Indo-Pacific and worldwide.
- The U.S. aims to advance foreign policy goals through partnerships, not just counter adversaries.
- Kirby notes the unique global network of U.S. allies compared to China and Russia.
#### U.S. Banking System Stability and Regulatory Actions
MS. Jean-Pierre outlines the administration's response to recent banking sector concerns, emphasizing actions taken to ensure financial system stability and protect depositors. She contrasts the President's budget with the House Freedom Caucus's MAGA budget, highlighting risks posed by the latter. The administration calls for congressional action to strengthen regulations, hold bank executives accountable, and prevent future crises.
- Jean-Pierre states the President's budget invests in America and protects Social Security and Medicare.
- She criticizes the MAGA budget proposal for endangering public safety and national security.
- The administration supports stronger banking regulations and executive accountability.
- Jean-Pierre assures that no taxpayer money is at risk in recent banking interventions.
#### U.S. Support for Women's Rights and Abortion Access
Jean-Pierre affirms the President's commitment to protecting women's rights to healthcare and reproductive freedom, condemning efforts to impose national abortion bans. While not commenting on specific state ballot initiatives, the administration welcomes legislation expanding abortion access and opposes extreme measures that restrict fundamental freedoms.
- Jean-Pierre states the President supports protecting women's healthcare rights.
- She calls national abortion bans shameful and vows to continue opposing them.
- The administration welcomes legislation that expands abortion access.
- She emphasizes the importance of women making their own healthcare decisions.
#### Climate Policy and Conservation Efforts
Jean-Pierre addresses questions about the administration's climate policies, highlighting the President's record on conservation, including protections for lands and waters and new regulations in Alaska. She clarifies that recent decisions, such as the Willow project approval, were legally constrained and that the administration remains committed to historic investments in climate and environmental protection.
- Jean-Pierre cites the President's protection of more lands and waters than any since JFK.
- She notes the U.S. Arctic Ocean has been declared off-limits to new oil and gas leasing.
- The administration is preparing new regulations to protect millions of acres in Alaska.
- She emphasizes the President's ongoing commitment to climate action and conservation.
