### Overview
  The briefing covers a mix of international security and domestic policy issues. MS. JEAN-PIERRE opens with Department of Transportation updates for holiday travel and details of a $750 million enforcement action against Southwest to protect consumers. MR. KIRBY then outlines Operation Prosperity Guardian, a newly formed multinational maritime task force (Task Force 153) to deter and defend against Houthi drone and missile attacks on commercial shipping in the Red Sea, noting broad coalition backing and a 44-signatory condemnation; he says Iran supplies Houthi capabilities, China could use influence with Tehran, and the U.S. is reviewing whether to designate the Houthis as a terrorist group. Reporters press on deterrence, unnamed partner states, potential escalation, the operation’s ability to intercept drones, shipping reroutes, and broader allied views amid U.N. diplomacy, with Kirby emphasizing that the effort is just starting, will scale to the threat, and supports targeted humanitarian pauses rather than a permanent ceasefire. The briefing also addresses other priorities: the administration warns Congress the U.S. has one last Ukraine security package available unless replenishment authority is renewed; officials discuss North Korea–Russia–China dynamics and continued efforts to secure detained Americans in Russia; MS. JEAN-PIERRE condemns an extreme Texas immigration law while describing ongoing supplemental negotiations to address border security and humanitarian needs; she expresses condolences over a child’s death in a migrant shelter and reiterates that family travel remains private. Finally, the White House touches on trade and regulatory matters (U.S. Steel purchase and Apple Watch ruling), domestic political initiatives (the Vice President’s reproductive-rights tour and Bidenomics messaging), the administration’s response to coordinated threats against Jewish institutions, and support for the Vatican’s decision to permit blessings of same-sex unions.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Holiday Air Travel
The briefing opens with the Department of Transportation previewing holiday travel conditions and consumer protections. MS. JEAN-PIERRE highlights record travel demand over Thanksgiving, historically low cancellation rates, and DOT oversight of airline preparedness. The administration emphasizes holding airlines accountable and protecting passengers, citing a major enforcement action against Southwest.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says Thanksgiving saw record travel demand and cancellation rates below half a percent.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports that cancellation rates are the lowest since 2016 and that DOT is pressing airlines to prepare for holiday demand.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the Department of Transportation will hold airlines accountable to protect consumers.
  #### Southwest Enforcement Action
MS. JEAN-PIERRE describes DOT’s recent enforcement action against Southwest Airlines tied to last year’s holiday disruption. The package totals $750 million in passenger relief and penalties, including a record $140 million fine and more than $600 million in refunds, and requires a $90 million reserve for future passenger compensation. The administration frames the action as precedent-setting to deter airline failures to care for customers.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE announces DOT has secured $750 million from Southwest for passenger relief and penalties, including a $140 million penalty.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says over $600 million of refunds and reimbursements have already been paid and Southwest must reserve $90 million for future compensations.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE frames the settlement as a historic step that signals airlines will be held accountable for failing passengers.
  #### Red Sea Security Operation
MR. KIRBY outlines Operation Prosperity Guardian, a new U.S.-led multinational effort to counter Houthi attacks on commercial shipping in the Red Sea. He explains the operation involves maritime surveillance and defensive actions under Combined Maritime Forces Task Force 153 and stresses the economic importance of the Red Sea for global trade. The administration presents the operation as collective international action to protect commerce and deter unacceptable attacks.
- MR. KIRBY says the Department of Defense has announced Operation Prosperity Guardian to address Houthi threats in the Red Sea.
- MR. KIRBY states the operation operates under Combined Maritime Forces Task Force 153, focused on the Red Sea.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes the Red Sea’s strategic importance, noting it conveys significant shares of global trade, grain, and seaborne oil.
  #### Coalition and International Response
Officials describe a broad international response that accompanies the U.S. operation, including an initial group of participating navies and a 44-signatory joint statement condemning Houthi attacks. MR. KIRBY lists partner countries joining maritime surveillance and notes senior-level regional consultations convened by Secretary Austin. The administration emphasizes international backing as evidence of U.S. convening power rather than isolation.
- MR. KIRBY lists partners such as the United Kingdom, Bahrain, Canada, France, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Seychelles, and Spain as joining the effort.
- MR. KIRBY says 44 signatories, including NATO, the EU, G7, Australia, Canada, and others, joined a joint statement condemning Houthi actions.
- MR. KIRBY reports Secretary Austin convened a regional meeting with representatives from 43 countries, the EU, and NATO to coordinate responses.
  #### Houthi Targeting & Drones
Reporters question the Houthis’ intent after incidents that included up to 14 incoming drones intercepted from the USS Carney, and MR. KIRBY explains the task force’s focus is protecting commercial ships. He notes Houthis have predominantly targeted merchant shipping and may launch multiple drones at once, but assessing precise intended targets during engagements can be difficult. The task force exists to detect and disrupt such threats in real time.
- Reporter asks about the Houthis’ targets after the USS Carney intercepted 14 drones, and MR. KIRBY says the Houthis largely focus on commercial shipping.
- MR. KIRBY explains that multiple-drone launches are consistent with Houthi behavior and that intercepts do not always reveal an attacker’s precise intended target.
- MR. KIRBY says the maritime task force is established to detect, disrupt, and protect merchant vessels from these attacks.
  #### Houthi Terrorist Designation Review
MR. KIRBY confirms the administration is actively reviewing whether to designate the Houthis as a terrorist organization but has not made a decision or set a timeline. The topic matters because a designation would have legal and diplomatic implications for U.S. policy toward Yemen and humanitarian actors. MR. KIRBY frames the status as under review without further commitment.
- Reporter asks whether the administration will redesignate the Houthis as terrorists, and MR. KIRBY says the review is ongoing with no decision made.
- MR. KIRBY says he does not have a timeline for any decision on designation at this time.
  #### Anonymity of Some Participants
Reporters note some countries are participating in the maritime initiative anonymously, and MR. KIRBY stresses that sovereign partners decide how public to be. He rejects the notion that anonymity indicates reluctance to stand with the U.S., and instead frames it as a sovereign choice by partners to manage domestic or regional sensitivities. The administration leaves public disclosure to those countries.
- Reporter asks why some countries are unnamed in the coalition, and MR. KIRBY says participating nations choose how public they want to be.
- MR. KIRBY says he will leave it to sovereign partners to describe their level of participation or public posture.
  #### Deterrence and Military Options
Questioners press whether the multinational naval presence will deter the Houthis and whether the U.S. is considering broader military action. MR. KIRBY emphasizes the operation has just begun, will scale with the threat, and is meant to be multinational and defensive without telegraphing future actions. He reiterates that attacks are unacceptable and the U.S. will protect national and international security interests.
- Reporter asks if the task force will deter the Houthis, and MR. KIRBY says the operation is newly established and will deploy naval hardware and partners to see how it performs.
- MR. KIRBY declines to telegraph military moves and says the coalition will scale to the threat while protecting shipping and security interests.
- MR. KIRBY reiterates that the attacks are unacceptable and that the U.S. and partners will act to counter threats.
  #### Saudi and UAE Participation
Reporters ask specifically why Saudi Arabia and the UAE are not publicly listed as coalition participants and whether the U.S. wants them to join. MR. KIRBY avoids detailing diplomatic conversations, reiterates the coalition is voluntary, and says each nation decides its participation and public posture. He underscores continued partnerships with both countries across a range of issues.
- Reporter asks why Saudi Arabia and the UAE are not part of the coalition, and MR. KIRBY says each nation decides its participation and public disclosure.
- MR. KIRBY notes both countries remain significant U.S. partners across regional issues without detailing private diplomatic conversations.
  #### Iran and China Influence
Questions probe Iran’s role in supplying Houthi capabilities and whether China can influence Tehran to restrain the Houthis. MR. KIRBY states Iran provides weapons and capabilities to the Houthis while the Houthis conduct the attacks, and he encourages China to use its influence with Iran. He frames Iran as enabling the attacks and invites China to pressure Tehran diplomatically.
- Reporter asks about Iran’s direct involvement, and MR. KIRBY says Iran provides the means and tools while the Houthis execute attacks.
- MR. KIRBY says China could be helpful by leveraging its influence with Tehran to discourage support for the Houthis.
  #### Maritime Trade and Rerouting
Reporters raise commercial reactions, including shipping companies rerouting around the Red Sea, and MR. KIRBY cautions it is too soon to know the global economic impact. He says companies must decide on safety for vessels and crews and that the coalition aims to make transit safer to limit disruptions. The administration is monitoring potential supply-chain effects.
- Reporter notes shipping companies are rerouting around the Red Sea, and MR. KIRBY says it is too early to assess the full impact on global supply chains.
- MR. KIRBY explains that companies make route decisions based on vessel and crew safety and that the coalition seeks to reduce threats so transit remains viable.
  #### Protecting Merchant Vessels
Reporters ask whether Operation Prosperity Guardian can intercept drones headed for merchant ships and whether escorts will accompany convoys closely. MR. KIRBY confirms the coalition has already disrupted threats by engaging drones in flight and says naval assets will operate flexibly and in coordination with commercial traffic. He emphasizes the operation’s practical ability to detect and defeat airborne threats when possible.
- Reporter asks if the coalition can shoot down drones headed for merchant vessels, and MR. KIRBY says yes, the coalition has already disrupted such threats.
- MR. KIRBY explains ships will operate flexibly as task-force assets and will be positioned relative to commercial vessels and the threat environment.
  #### UN Vote and Allies' Positions
Questioners press whether the U.S. risks appearing isolated given differing allied calls for a ceasefire and an upcoming Security Council vote. MR. KIRBY rejects the isolation charge, pointing to broad coalition support and the 44-signatory statement, while acknowledging allied nations may use different language on ceasefires. He reiterates U.S. support for more targeted humanitarian pauses rather than a permanent ceasefire at this time.
- Reporter asks whether the U.S. is isolated on the world stage, and MR. KIRBY points to 44 signatories and multiple nations joining the maritime effort as evidence of U.S. leadership.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. does not support a permanent ceasefire now and prefers smaller humanitarian pauses to recover hostages and deliver aid.
  #### Hostages and Gaza Casualties
MR. KIRBY provides estimates and policy context on hostages and casualties in Gaza, saying the U.S. believes about eight Americans remain held and roughly 140 total hostages are held by Hamas and other groups. He cautions that casualty figures are difficult to verify precisely, reiterates the administration’s push for humanitarian assistance, and says conflict could end if Hamas released hostages and disarmed. Discussion links humanitarian pauses to hostage recovery efforts.
- MR. KIRBY says U.S. assessments indicate about eight Americans and roughly 140 total hostages remain held by Hamas and other groups.
- MR. KIRBY cautions exact casualty figures in Gaza are difficult to verify but emphasizes thousands have been killed or wounded across the conflict.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. supports humanitarian pauses targeted to free hostages and increase aid rather than a permanent ceasefire at this time.
  #### Ukraine Security Assistance
Reporters press the limits of U.S. replenishment authority for security assistance to Ukraine, and MR. KIRBY warns the administration has one more package available before the end of the year unless Congress provides supplemental funding. He stresses the need for congressional action to replenish defense stockpiles and maintain support. The administration frames urgency around the supplemental request.
- Reporter asks whether Ukraine should expect more aid this year, and MR. KIRBY says the administration has one more security assistance package available before replenishment authority runs out.
- MR. KIRBY says replenishing DOD inventories depends on Congress passing the requested supplemental funding and there is no 'magic pot of money.'
  #### DPRK-Russia-China Relations
Questions address North Korea’s ballistic missile activity and reported military cooperation with Russia, and MR. KIRBY characterizes growing communication among Russia, China, and North Korea as increasing alignment against the rules-based order. He reiterates U.S. commitments to allies in the Indo-Pacific, rejects attributing DPRK-Russia bilateral actions automatically to Chinese blessing, and maintains willingness to engage diplomatically with North Korea on denuclearization.
- Reporter asks about North Korea’s missile launches and meetings with Russia and China, and MR. KIRBY says those nations are increasing communications that challenge the rules-based order.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. has not seen evidence that China is directly providing lethal military capabilities to Russia and notes the DPRK makes sovereign decisions.
  #### Detainee Negotiations with Russia
Reporters ask about efforts to secure the release of detained Americans Evan Gershkovich and Paul Whelan, and MR. KIRBY confirms the U.S. made a serious proposal that Russia rebuffed. He says the administration continues to pursue other options and works hard to return wrongfully detained Americans home. Details of negotiations remain classified for operational security.
- Reporter asks about prospects for Evan Gershkovich and Paul Whelan, and MR. KIRBY says a recent U.S. proposal was rebuffed by Russia.
- MR. KIRBY says the administration has not given up and is working hard to find other avenues to secure their return.
  #### Post-Conflict Gaza Governance
Reporters raise proposals for international trusteeship or other post-conflict governance frameworks for Gaza, and MR. KIRBY says the administration is not yet endorsing a specific model. He says whatever follows must not return to Hamas control and that the Palestinian Authority should have a role while it reforms to be a credible governance actor. The White House continues to weigh modalities for post-conflict governance.
- Reporter asks about international trusteeship proposals for post-war Gaza, and MR. KIRBY says the U.S. is not endorsing one option yet.
- MR. KIRBY says the Palestinian Authority can and should have a role in Gaza’s future but needs reforms to be a credible partner.
  #### Immigration and Texas Law
MS. JEAN-PIERRE condemns a new Texas immigration law as extreme and unsafe, arguing it dehumanizes immigrants and endangers communities and enforcement personnel. She frames the President’s longstanding push for comprehensive immigration reform and describes the current supplemental as including border security provisions. The DOJ will determine any federal legal action in response to the state law.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE calls the Texas immigration law extreme and says it does not make communities safer.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President put forward comprehensive immigration legislation on his first day and later submitted an emergency supplemental that includes border security measures.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Department of Justice will decide whether to file suit challenging the Texas law.
  #### Migrant Shelter Death and Busing Debate
Reporters ask about the death of a five-year-old boy in a migrant shelter and local claims that Texas busing policies contributed to shelter strain. MS. JEAN-PIERRE expresses condolences and reiterates the administration’s view that Texas gubernatorial policies are political stunts that put migrants and communities at risk. She declines to litigate specific local cases from the podium while emphasizing the need to fix a broken immigration system.
- Reporter asks about the death of a five-year-old in a migrant shelter, and MS. JEAN-PIERRE extends the President’s condolences and calls the situation devastating.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reiterates that Governor Abbott’s busing and other policies are political stunts that dehumanize migrants and can create unsafe conditions.
  #### Domestic Regulatory and Trade Issues
Reporters inquire about several domestic economic topics, including the proposed sale of U.S. Steel, an Apple Watch import ruling, and potential administration actions. MS. JEAN-PIERRE avoids commenting on active regulatory or legal reviews but says the President supports protecting American manufacturing, union jobs, competition, and that USTR will consider the Apple case per its delegated authority. The White House limits comments to high-level principles while deferring to regulatory agencies.
- Reporter asks about the U.S. Steel sale, and MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President supports Steelworkers and competition but will be careful because the transaction may be under regulatory review.
- Reporter asks whether the administration will intervene in the Apple Watch import ban, and MS. JEAN-PIERRE says USTR and Ambassador Tai have delegated authority and are considering the factors in the case.
  #### Jewish Community Bomb Threats
MS. JEAN-PIERRE confirms the administration is tracking hundreds of emailed bomb threats against Jewish institutions and that federal and local law enforcement are investigating. She states the President is aware, condemns antisemitism and threats of violence, and emphasizes the administration’s commitment to protect religious communities. She refers further operational questions to the FBI and local authorities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration is closely tracking hundreds of emailed bomb threats to Jewish synagogues, schools, and institutions and that law enforcement is investigating.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President condemns antisemitism and threats of intimidation and is committed to protecting religious communities.
  #### Vatican Blessing of Same-Sex Unions
MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes that the President and many Catholics welcomed the Vatican’s announcement allowing priests to bless same-sex couples. She defers theological questions to the Church but frames the development as a welcomed step toward inclusion in religious ministry. The White House expresses general support for expanded pastoral care.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President welcomes the Vatican’s declaration permitting blessings for same-sex couples and that many Catholics share that welcome.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE defers doctrinal details to the Church while expressing support for pastoral outreach to LGBTQ people.
  #### Family Travel and Privacy
Reporters ask whether the White House will announce family members traveling on Marine One; MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration does not publish family travel rosters and treats such travel as private. She reiterates sensitivity around family matters after a recent somber anniversary and refers legal questions about Hunter Biden to his representatives. The White House maintains prior practice on family travel disclosures.
- Reporter asks whether the White House will announce Hunter Biden’s presence on Marine One, and MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration does not publish lists of family members who travel with the President.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says family travel is treated privately and refers legal questions about Hunter Biden to his personal representatives.
  #### Vice President Abortion Tour
MS. JEAN-PIERRE previews the Vice President’s nationwide reproductive-freedoms tour beginning in Wisconsin on January 22 and says it will highlight harm from restrictive abortion bans and mobilize supporters. She does not provide the President’s travel plans but stresses the administration’s ongoing commitment to protect reproductive rights and spotlight affected people’s stories. The tour aims to rally public support in key states.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Vice President will kick off a nationwide reproductive-freedoms tour in Wisconsin on January 22 to highlight harms from extreme abortion bans.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the tour will bring together people across states to discuss impacts and push back against restrictive policies.
  #### Bidenomics and Economic Messaging
Facing polling showing economic concerns, MS. JEAN-PIERRE defends the administration’s economic record, citing actions like the Inflation Reduction Act and support for small businesses. She acknowledges that Americans feel affordability pressures and says the administration will continue to focus on lowering costs through policies targeting prescription drugs, junk fees, and energy. The President plans public events to articulate Bidenomics achievements and priorities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President believes Bidenomics is working and cites the Inflation Reduction Act and record small-business formation as evidence.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE acknowledges that many Americans still feel unaffordable pressures and says the administration will prioritize lowering costs such as drug prices and junk fees.
