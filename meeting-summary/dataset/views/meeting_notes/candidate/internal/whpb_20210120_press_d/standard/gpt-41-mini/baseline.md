### Overview
In this inaugural press briefing, Press Secretary Jen Psaki outlines President Biden's immediate executive actions addressing critical issues such as the COVID-19 pandemic, climate change, racial equity, and immigration reform. She emphasizes the administration's commitment to transparency, truth, and rebuilding trust with the American public. Psaki details plans for foreign engagement, including an upcoming call with Canadian Prime Minister Trudeau, and underscores efforts to pass a comprehensive COVID-19 relief package with bipartisan support while remaining open to alternative legislative strategies. The briefing covers environmental policies, White House COVID-19 safety protocols, and the administration's approach to unity and political challenges, including the Capitol insurrection and impeachment proceedings. Psaki highlights President Biden's focus on governance over politics, his dedication to public health and economic recovery, and the intent to restore America's global leadership through diplomacy and climate action. The session concludes with commitments to ongoing communication and transparency as the administration advances its agenda.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Executive Actions Overview
Press Secretary Jen Psaki outlines the 15 executive actions signed by President Biden on his first day, emphasizing their significance in addressing urgent national issues such as the COVID-19 pandemic, climate change, racial equity, and immigration reform. These actions mark a swift policy shift from the previous administration and set the tone for the Biden-Harris administration's priorities.
- Jen Psaki highlights the 100 Day Masking Challenge and federal mask mandates to combat COVID-19.
- She notes the reversal of the U.S. withdrawal from the World Health Organization to strengthen global health efforts.
- The President appoints a COVID-19 response coordinator and reestablishes the national security team for global health security.
- Executive orders include extending eviction and foreclosure moratoriums and pausing federal student loan payments.
- Biden reenters the Paris Climate Agreement and rolls back environmental regulatory rollbacks from the prior administration.
- A government-wide effort to advance racial equity and end systemic racism is launched.
- The administration ends the Muslim ban and halts border wall funding and construction.
- President Biden sends the U.S. Citizenship Act to Congress to modernize immigration and provide citizenship pathways.
#### Role of Press Secretary and Transparency
Jen Psaki discusses her commitment to truth and transparency in her role as Press Secretary, emphasizing the importance of sharing accurate information with the American public even when it is difficult. She acknowledges the role of a free press in democracy and the potential for disagreement within the briefing room.
- Psaki states her respect for a free and independent press and the role journalists play.
- She commits to delivering accurate information and transparency on behalf of the President and the American people.
- Psaki acknowledges that disagreements will occur but stresses a common goal of truth sharing.
#### Foreign Leader Engagement
Psaki outlines President Biden's initial plans for foreign leader communications, focusing on rebuilding alliances and addressing global challenges. The first scheduled call is with Canadian Prime Minister Trudeau, with no immediate plans to speak with President Putin. The administration reserves the right to respond to cyberattacks such as SolarWinds at an appropriate time.
- First foreign leader call scheduled with Prime Minister Trudeau to discuss bilateral relations and Keystone Pipeline.
- No current plans to announce a call with President Putin; early calls will focus on partners and allies.
- The administration is monitoring the SolarWinds cyberattack and reserves the right to respond accordingly.
#### COVID-19 Relief and Legislative Strategy
The administration emphasizes the urgency of passing the COVID-19 relief package, which includes unemployment benefits, school reopening funds, and vaccine distribution support. President Biden is actively engaging with both Democrats and Republicans to seek bipartisan support but remains open to using budget reconciliation if necessary.
- Psaki notes President Biden's focus on controlling the pandemic and financial relief for Americans.
- The administration hopes for bipartisan support but will consider all legislative tools to pass the relief package.
- Biden's extensive Senate experience is expected to aid in negotiations.
- Discussions on the package's price and components are ongoing, with no predetermined cuts.
#### School Reopening and Public Health Measures
Questions about the administration's plans for reopening schools highlight the need for clear public health guidance. Psaki indicates that health experts will provide detailed criteria on vaccination and testing levels required to meet reopening targets.
- Psaki commits to bringing in health experts to provide specifics on school reopening plans.
- She acknowledges the importance of vaccination and testing in safely reopening schools.
#### Environmental Policies and Legal Considerations
The administration addresses climate change by rejoining the Paris Agreement and implementing executive orders to protect public lands and review oil and gas leasing. Psaki confirms the commitment to ending new oil and gas leasing on federal lands, with legal reviews ongoing to ensure compliance.
- Temporary moratorium on oil and gas leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge is enacted.
- Executive orders include reviewing national monument boundaries and reinstating greenhouse gas cost assessments.
- All executive actions were reviewed by career staff at the Office of Legal Counsel for legality.
#### White House COVID-19 Safety Protocols
Psaki describes the COVID-19 safety measures implemented in the White House to protect the President, staff, and press corps, including daily testing, mask mandates, social distancing, and deep cleaning.
- Daily COVID-19 testing and N95 mask usage are mandatory within the White House.
- Stringent social distancing rules are enforced to maintain safety.
- The General Services Administration oversees deep cleaning and safety preparations.
#### International Relations and Climate Diplomacy
The administration plans to rebuild global partnerships and address climate change collaboratively, including reengagement with international bodies like the U.N. and the Paris Agreement. Specific bilateral relations, such as with Brazil, are to be developed over time.
- Rejoining the Paris Agreement restores U.S. participation in global climate efforts.
- The U.S. submitted its reentry to the U.N. Secretary General, effective in approximately 30 days.
- Further discussions with countries like Brazil on climate issues are anticipated.
#### Iran Nuclear Deal and Diplomacy
President Biden aims to pursue diplomacy to strengthen nuclear constraints on Iran, contingent on Iran's compliance with the original deal. Early foreign discussions will include this topic among partners and allies.
- Biden supports lengthening and strengthening nuclear constraints through diplomacy.
- Iran must resume compliance for negotiations to proceed.
- Early foreign leader conversations will address Iran's nuclear program.
#### Presidential Transition and Inaugural Reflections
Psaki shares insights into President Biden's demeanor on Inauguration Day, highlighting his calm, joy, and eagerness to begin work. She notes his sense of 'coming home' after serving as Vice President and his focus on policy and COVID-19 response.
- Biden spent time with family and expressed calm and joy on Inauguration Day.
- He feels a sense of returning home to the White House after previous service.
- The President is eager to engage immediately on policy and pandemic response.
#### Unity and Political Climate
The administration emphasizes President Biden's commitment to unity and bipartisan cooperation, while acknowledging ongoing political processes such as impeachment. Biden aims to govern for all Americans and encourages Congress to fulfill their duties alongside legislative business.
- Biden's inaugural address focused on unity and forward-looking governance.
- He has reached out to members of both parties at various government levels.
- The President leaves impeachment timing and process to Congress to decide.
- Biden believes the Senate can multitask and conduct business while handling impeachment.
#### Political Plans and Focus
Psaki states that President Biden's current focus is on governance and problem-solving rather than political ambitions. He plans to discuss political plans later in his term, prioritizing immediate national challenges.
- Biden is concentrating on addressing the nation's problems over political campaigning.
- He will share political plans later in his first term.
- The administration emphasizes work over politics at this stage.
#### Social Issues and Policy Questions
Questions about policies such as the Hyde Amendment, Mexico City Policy, and the death penalty are addressed with limited details. Psaki indicates forthcoming statements on some issues and reiterates the President's personal views where applicable.
- More information on the Mexico City Policy is expected in the coming days.
- Biden is a devout Catholic and attends church regularly.
- He opposes the death penalty but has no immediate announcements on moratoriums.
#### Accountability for Capitol Insurrection
Psaki confirms President Biden's strong condemnation of the January 6th Capitol attack and defers to Congress on decisions regarding accountability and potential disqualification of former President Trump from future office.
- Biden has publicly condemned the Capitol insurrection.
- He has discussed the events with members of Congress.
- Decisions on accountability and consequences are left to the legislative branch.
