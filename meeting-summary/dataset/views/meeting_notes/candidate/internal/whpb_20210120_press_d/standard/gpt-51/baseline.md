### Overview
The briefing, led by Press Secretary Jen Psaki on President Biden’s first day in office, centers on the administration’s immediate actions to reset U.S. policy and communication practices while confronting multiple crises. Psaki outlines a sweeping set of day-one executive measures on COVID-19—including a 100-day masking push, a federal mask mandate, renewed engagement with the WHO, eviction and foreclosure protections, and extended student loan relief—and previews broader plans to use the Defense Production Act and pursue a $1.9 trillion relief package that Biden intends to negotiate personally with both parties. She highlights major reversals of Trump-era policies on climate (rejoining Paris, pausing Arctic drilling, restoring climate cost analysis), racial equity and immigration (fortifying DACA, ending the Muslim ban, halting the border wall, and introducing a comprehensive immigration bill), and international engagement, signaling that early foreign outreach will prioritize allies such as Canada. Throughout, Psaki stresses Biden’s commitment to truth, transparency, and unity, promising daily press briefings, regular science-led COVID updates, and bipartisan outreach while leaving decisions on impeachment, Trump’s accountability, and some specific policy details—such as death penalty steps, abortion-related rules, and personnel questions like FBI Director Wray—to established institutional processes or future announcements. She portrays Biden as calm, focused, and “coming home” to the White House, intent on governing for all Americans and restoring U.S. credibility at home and abroad.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Biden’s opening transparency commitments
Press Secretary Jen Psaki outlines President Biden’s directive to restore truth and transparency to White House communications and to rebuild public trust. She emphasizes respect for a free and independent press and frames the briefing room as a venue for honest engagement, even amid disagreements. Psaki positions herself and the President as ultimately accountable to the American people and committed to sharing accurate information, including when it is difficult. This sets the tone and expectations for how the administration plans to communicate going forward.
- Ms. Psaki explains that President Biden asked her to prioritize truth, transparency, and clear communication about administration policies on behalf of the American people.
- Ms. Psaki states that disagreements with the press are acceptable and part of democracy, but that rebuilding trust with the public will be central to the White House’s work.
- Ms. Psaki affirms deep respect for a free and independent press and says the shared goal is to provide accurate information to the American people.
- Ms. Psaki says that President Biden sees himself as working for the American people, and therefore she does as well, with a commitment to share the truth even when it is hard to hear.
#### Initial COVID-19 executive actions
Psaki details several day-one executive actions aimed at combating COVID-19 and its economic fallout. These include the 100 Day Masking Challenge, a federal mask mandate on government property and certain transportation, reengagement with the World Health Organization, and the appointment of a COVID-19 response coordinator. She also highlights efforts to extend eviction and foreclosure moratoriums and to pause federal student loan payments and interest in response to pandemic-driven hardship. These moves are presented as immediate steps toward a unified national response and economic relief while the broader COVID strategy is developed.
- Ms. Psaki announces that President Biden has launched a 100 Day Masking Challenge, asking Americans to wear masks for 100 days to help combat COVID-19.
- Ms. Psaki states that President Biden signed a mask mandate requiring masks in federal buildings, on federal lands, and on certain public transportation.
- Ms. Psaki explains that President Biden reversed the previous administration’s decision to withdraw from the World Health Organization and that Dr. Fauci will address the WHO as head of the U.S. delegation the next day.
- Ms. Psaki notes that President Biden formally appointed a COVID-19 response coordinator and reestablished the national security team for global health security and biodefense to create a unified national response.
- Ms. Psaki says the President asked agencies to extend nationwide moratoriums on evictions and foreclosures due to an acute housing affordability crisis triggered by the pandemic.
- Ms. Psaki adds that President Biden requested the Department of Education extend the pause on federal student loan payments and interest to ease hardship on borrowers.
#### Day-one climate and environmental actions
The briefing highlights Biden’s decision to rejoin the Paris climate agreement and to roll back several Trump-era environmental regulatory changes. Psaki describes an executive order that will review national monument boundaries, impose a temporary moratorium on oil and gas leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, and reestablish the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases. She frames these as steps to restore U.S. climate leadership globally while protecting natural resources and public health domestically. The administration also reiterates its broader campaign commitment to end new oil and gas leasing on federal lands, with implementation details to come.
- Ms. Psaki reports that President Biden signed an order for the United States to rejoin the Paris climate agreement, restoring U.S. leadership on global climate objectives.
- Ms. Psaki explains that a broad environmental executive order will roll back harmful regulatory reversals from the previous administration to protect public health and the environment.
- Ms. Psaki says the order directs a review of the boundaries of several national monuments and imposes a temporary moratorium on all oil and natural gas leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.
- Ms. Psaki notes that the order reestablishes the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases to better account for climate impacts in policymaking.
- Ms. Psaki confirms that the administration still intends to end new oil and gas leasing on federal lands, while stating that existing leases and legal flexibility will be reviewed by the team.
- Ms. Psaki emphasizes that all executive actions issued that day were reviewed by career staff at the Office of Legal Counsel before release.
#### Racial equity and immigration reversals
Psaki outlines executive actions advancing racial equity and reversing key Trump immigration policies. Biden launches a whole-of-government effort to root out systemic racism in federal programs and directs DHS to preserve and fortify DACA protections for DREAMers. He ends the so-called Muslim ban and halts further funding and construction of the border wall, terminating the related national emergency declaration. Psaki also announces that Biden has sent a comprehensive immigration reform bill to Congress aimed at modernizing the system, creating a pathway to citizenship, managing the border responsibly, and addressing root causes of migration.
- Ms. Psaki says President Biden has launched a whole-of-government initiative to advance racial equity and remove systemic racism from federal programs and institutions.
- Ms. Psaki reports that President Biden directed the Secretary of Homeland Security, in consultation with the Attorney General, to take all appropriate actions to preserve and strengthen DACA.
- Ms. Psaki states that President Biden has ended the so-called Muslim ban, describing it as a policy rooted in religious animus and xenophobia.
- Ms. Psaki explains that Biden signed a proclamation immediately halting further funding and construction of the previous administration’s border wall and ending the associated national emergency declaration.
- Ms. Psaki announces that President Biden sent the U.S. Citizenship Act to Congress to modernize the immigration system and provide a pathway to citizenship for long-term, hardworking residents.
- Ms. Psaki notes that the immigration bill aims to responsibly manage the border, keep families together, grow the economy, address migration root causes in Central America, and maintain America as a refuge for those fleeing persecution.

She stresses a strong preference for a bipartisan bill but leaves reconciliation on the table, while declining to specify any immediate willingness to scale back the topline number despite GOP concerns.

- Ms. Psaki notes that although Biden spent inauguration day focused on staffing and agenda setup, he will soon resume phone calls with lawmakers and plans to meet congressional leaders in person relatively soon.
