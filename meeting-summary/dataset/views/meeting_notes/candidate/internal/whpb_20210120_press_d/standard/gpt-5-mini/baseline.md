### Overview
  Ms. Psaki opens the briefing by framing her role as restoring truth and transparency and then summarizes roughly 15 executive actions the President signed on day one addressing COVID-19, climate, immigration, racial equity, and environmental protections. Key immediate steps include a 100 Day Masking Challenge and federal mask requirement, rejoining the WHO and Paris Agreement, appointing a COVID-19 response coordinator and national security team for global health, pausing ANWR leasing while reviewing monument boundaries, directives to preserve DACA, ending the Muslim ban, halting further border wall funding, and sending the U.S. Citizenship Act to Congress. In the Q&A she commits to regular, data-driven briefings with public-health officials, confirms the first foreign leader call will be with Prime Minister Trudeau, and emphasizes a preference for bipartisan COVID relief while reserving all tools to deliver urgent aid; she also says the administration will consider the Defense Production Act to scale vaccine supplies. Ms. Psaki repeatedly defers certain operational or legal specifics to ongoing processes (e.g., FBI investigations, GSA facility questions, OLC legal reviews) and signals follow-ups on topics including the Mexico City policy, potential death penalty actions, and the Air Force One color scheme, while stressing the President’s immediate focus on governing and rebuilding alliances.

She frames the actions as immediate steps on COVID-19, climate, immigration, racial equity, and public health protections. The White House intends to highlight key elements publicly and follow up where necessary.

  #### 100‑Day Masking Challenge
The President launches a 100 Day Masking Challenge asking Americans to wear masks and issues a federal mask requirement for federal buildings, lands, and certain public transportation. The measure aims to reduce transmission quickly and set an example of public health behavior. The White House positions this as a visible, immediate public-health action the administration is undertaking.
- MS. PSAKI said the President launched a 100 Day Masking Challenge asking Americans to mask up for 100 days.
- MS. PSAKI said the President issued a mask mandate for federal buildings, federal land, and certain public transportation.
  #### WHO reengagement
The President signs an order reversing the U.S. withdrawal from the World Health Organization to strengthen global pandemic response. Ms. Psaki notes Dr. Fauci will participate remotely in an upcoming WHO meeting as the U.S. head of delegation. The move is presented as a step to improve global coordination on COVID-19.

  #### COVID response coordination
The President formalizes a COVID-19 response coordinator role and team to establish a unified national strategy, and he reconstitutes the national security team for global health security and biodefense. Ms. Psaki frames these moves as necessary to centralize and accelerate response efforts. The administration is making these appointments official after prior announcements.
- MS. PSAKI said the President officially appointed a COVID-19 response coordinator and team to create a unified national response.
- MS. PSAKI said the President reestablished the national security team responsible for global health security and biodefense.
  #### Housing and student relief
Ms. Psaki reports executive actions aimed at addressing pandemic-related housing and education burdens, including asking agencies to extend eviction and foreclosure moratoriums and asking the Department of Education to extend the pause on federal student loan payments and interest. These steps seek to mitigate financial hardship triggered by COVID-19. The actions are framed as immediate administrative relief while broader legislative efforts continue.
- MS. PSAKI said the President asked agencies to extend nationwide moratoriums on evictions and foreclosures.
- MS. PSAKI said the President asked the Department of Education to extend the pause on federal student loan payments and interest.
  #### Paris Agreement reentry
The administration rejoins the Paris Climate Agreement to restore U.S. engagement in international climate efforts and exercise global leadership. Ms. Psaki emphasizes that reentry is an early, symbolic step and that domestic policy actions are also required to reduce U.S. emissions. The submission to the U.N. Secretary-General will take effect after the applicable administrative period.

  #### Environmental regulatory review
A broad executive order directs review and reversal of regulatory rollbacks affecting public health and the environment, including reassessing national monument boundaries and pausing oil and gas leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge (ANWR). The order also reestablishes the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases. Ms. Psaki says these actions protect public lands and restore environmental protections.
- MS. PSAKI said a broad order will roll back harmful regulatory reversals affecting public health and the environment.
- MS. PSAKI said the order reviews monument boundaries, places a temporary moratorium on oil and gas leasing in ANWR, and reestablishes the Interagency Working Group on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases.
  #### Racial equity initiative
The President launches an administration-wide effort to advance racial equity and address systemic racism in federal programs and institutions. Ms. Psaki presents this as a whole-of-government priority aimed at longstanding disparities. The initiative sets a framework for agency reviews and policy changes to pursue equity objectives.
- MS. PSAKI said the President launched a whole-of-government effort to advance racial equity and root out systemic racism from federal programs and institutions.
  #### Immigration: DACA and Muslim ban
Ms. Psaki announces directives to preserve and fortify DACA in consultation with DHS and the Attorney General, and the President ends the prior administration's Muslim ban. These actions seek to protect DREAMers and remove policies the administration characterizes as discriminatory. The administration also halts further border wall funding and terminates the previous 'national emergency' declaration used to divert funds.
- MS. PSAKI said the President directed DHS, in consultation with the Attorney General, to take all appropriate actions to preserve and fortify DACA.
- MS. PSAKI said the President officially ended the Muslim ban, which the briefing characterized as rooted in religious animus and xenophobia.
- MS. PSAKI said the President signed a proclamation halting further funding or construction of the prior administration’s border wall and terminating the diverted 'national emergency' funding.
  #### U.S. Citizenship Act proposal
The President sent the U.S. Citizenship Act to Congress to modernize the immigration system and provide a pathway to citizenship for many long-term residents. Ms. Psaki outlines priorities of managing the border responsibly, keeping families together, growing the economy, addressing root causes of Central American migration, and preserving refuge for those fleeing persecution. The bill represents the administration's legislative approach while agency actions proceed.
- MS. PSAKI said the President sent an immigration bill called the U.S. Citizenship Act to Congress to provide opportunities to earn citizenship.
- MS. PSAKI said the bill prioritizes border management, family unity, economic growth, root‑cause migration solutions, and refuge for those fleeing persecution.
