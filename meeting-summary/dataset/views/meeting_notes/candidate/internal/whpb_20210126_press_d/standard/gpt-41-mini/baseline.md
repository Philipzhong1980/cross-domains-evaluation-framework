### Overview
The meeting featured Ambassador Susan Rice outlining the Biden administration's comprehensive approach to advancing racial equity through executive orders targeting systemic racism in housing, criminal justice, and federal agency operations. Emphasizing the disproportionate impact of COVID-19 on communities of color, Rice detailed efforts to embed equity in recovery plans and highlighted the American Rescue Plan's potential to significantly reduce poverty. The administration also addressed vaccine distribution challenges, focusing on equitable access and overcoming hesitancy in marginalized communities. Discussions included the President's executive actions to end contracts with private prisons and enhance tribal consultations, alongside plans to combat domestic violent extremism. Press Secretary Psaki provided updates on COVID-19 vaccine supply, legislative negotiations for relief packages, and foreign policy engagements, including a call with President Putin. The administration underscored its commitment to bipartisan dialogue, urgent pandemic response, and long-term legislative solutions to codify equity initiatives.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Racial Equity Executive Order
Ambassador Susan Rice discusses the President's commitment to advancing racial justice and equity through a whole-of-government approach. The executive order aims to embed racial equity across federal policies and institutions, addressing systemic racism and expanding opportunity for marginalized communities. This initiative is critical given the persistent economic and health disparities faced by communities of color, exacerbated by the COVID-19 pandemic.
- Ambassador Rice states the President has committed the entire government to advancing racial justice and equity.
- She highlights systemic racism's impact, noting the wealth gap and disparities in homeownership between Black and white families.
- Rice emphasizes the disproportionate effects of COVID-19 on communities of color, including higher infection, death rates, and economic hardships.
- The executive order directs agencies to place equity at the core of policy design and program delivery to reach marginalized communities.
- Rice notes the President's economic plans include historic investments in underserved communities to foster equitable recovery.
#### COVID-19 Vaccine Equity Efforts
The administration acknowledges vaccine hesitancy and access challenges in communities of color and outlines efforts to address these issues. Targeted outreach campaigns and partnerships with local health centers aim to increase vaccine awareness and availability. Data collection on vaccination demographics remains a challenge, but mobile outreach and community engagement are planned to improve equity in vaccine distribution.
- Ambassador Rice notes the COVID task force includes an equity effort focusing on health disparities and vaccine skepticism in communities of color.
- She mentions targeted campaigns to reach communities with high vaccine hesitancy and improve access to appointments.
- Press Secretary Psaki confirms plans for mobile outreach and partnerships with pharmacies and health centers to facilitate vaccine access in underserved areas.
- Yamiche discusses the challenge of incomplete racial data in vaccination reporting, with Psaki deferring to CDC for specifics.
- The administration recognizes vaccine hesitancy as a significant challenge requiring multifaceted communication strategies.
#### Executive Actions on Housing and Criminal Justice
President Biden is signing executive actions to address racial bias in housing and reduce reliance on private prisons. The Department of Housing and Urban Development is directed to mitigate racial bias and enforce fair housing laws. The Department of Justice will not renew contracts with private prisons, aiming to reduce mass incarceration and improve safety. Additionally, federal agencies will enhance consultation with tribal governments and combat xenophobia against Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders.
- Rice states the President will sign a memorandum directing HUD to mitigate racial bias in housing.
- An executive order will direct DOJ not to renew contracts with private prisons due to safety and ethical concerns.
- The administration commits to reducing mass incarceration while enhancing community safety.
- Federal agencies will be required to engage in meaningful consultation with tribal governments.
- A memorandum will direct agencies to combat xenophobia and violence against Asian American and Pacific Islander communities.
#### Legislative and Policy Priorities
The administration plans to work with Congress on advancing equity in economic, criminal justice, healthcare, and education systems. The American Rescue Plan is highlighted as a key legislative effort to reduce poverty and support underserved communities. The President emphasizes the importance of legislative action to codify executive measures for lasting impact. Voting rights and democratic reforms are also priorities, with concerns about efforts to restrict access to the ballot in communities of color.
- Rice emphasizes the need for congressional action to make equity measures permanent.
- The American Rescue Plan aims to cut child poverty in half and reduce overall poverty by 30%.
- Psaki notes ongoing discussions with Congress on voting rights and democratic reforms.
- The administration is monitoring efforts to roll back voting procedures in swing states with large minority populations.
- Rice indicates more criminal justice reform announcements, including policing, will be forthcoming.
#### Addressing Domestic Extremism and White Nationalism
The administration recognizes the threat posed by white nationalists and domestic violent extremists, especially following the January 6 Capitol events. The President has tasked the intelligence community with assessing this threat comprehensively. The National Security Council has established a capacity to coordinate interagency efforts to address domestic violent extremism and develop policy responses.
- Rice acknowledges the serious problem of white supremacist violence in the U.S.
- The President has ordered an intelligence community assessment on domestic violent extremism.
- The NSC has created a dedicated capacity to focus on domestic violent extremism coordination.
- Efforts are underway to develop policies to counteract these threats.
- Rice emphasizes the administration's commitment to addressing this challenge seriously.
#### COVID-19 Vaccine Distribution and Supply
The administration is actively working to increase vaccine supply and improve coordination with state and local officials. The President will provide updates on vaccine availability and distribution plans. Challenges include ensuring sufficient vaccinators and distribution sites, as well as providing states with timely information to manage supply effectively. The goal remains to administer 100 million vaccine doses in 100 days, with hopes to exceed this target.
- Psaki states the President is focused on increasing vaccine supply and cooperation with states.
- A call with governors is planned to update them on vaccine distribution efforts.
- Challenges include limited vaccinators and distribution sites in some areas.
- The administration monitors vaccine supply data through systems like Tiberius.
- The 100 million doses in 100 days goal remains, with the President pushing to surpass it.
#### Legislative Negotiations and Political Strategy
The administration is engaged in ongoing discussions with congressional members from both parties regarding the COVID relief package. While Republicans have expressed opposition and proposed alternatives, the President and his team continue to negotiate and seek bipartisan support. The President prefers private and small group discussions to advance the legislative agenda efficiently. The administration emphasizes urgency in passing relief measures to avoid economic and social crises.
- Psaki notes active communication with both Democratic and Republican lawmakers on relief legislation.
- Republicans have publicly and privately expressed concerns about package size and targeting.
- The President favors one-on-one and small group talks over large public negotiations.
- The administration stresses the cost of inaction and the need for urgent legislative progress.
- Psaki indicates that final agreements often come together shortly before votes.
#### International Relations and Security Issues
The President has engaged in a call with President Putin to discuss extending the New START treaty and address concerns including Russian aggression in Ukraine, election interference, and human rights issues. The administration maintains a critical stance on the Nord Stream 2 pipeline, considering sanctions and consultations with European partners. The approach to Venezuela focuses on humanitarian support and multilateral diplomacy for democratic transition.
- Psaki confirms a recent call between Presidents Biden and Putin covering arms control and security concerns.
- The administration views Nord Stream 2 as detrimental to Europe and is reviewing related sanctions.
- The U.S. supports a peaceful democratic transition in Venezuela through multilateral efforts.
- Concerns raised include the SolarWinds hack, bounties on U.S. soldiers, and treatment of protestors.
- Further updates on these foreign policy issues are forthcoming.
