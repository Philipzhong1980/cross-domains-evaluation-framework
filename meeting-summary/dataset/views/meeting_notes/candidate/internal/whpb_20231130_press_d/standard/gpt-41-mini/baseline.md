### Overview
The briefing previewed President Biden's meeting with Angolan President Lourenço, marking a milestone in US-Angola relations after 30 years of diplomatic ties. The discussion highlighted a historic year of partnership characterized by significant US investments, notably over a billion dollars committed to the Lobito Corridor infrastructure project connecting Angola, DRC, and Zambia to global markets. The US administration emphasized Angola's leadership in regional security, particularly regarding the eastern DRC conflict, and plans to deepen defense cooperation through a 2024 high-level dialogue. Angola's signing of the Artemis Accords was announced, underscoring its role in global space governance. While President Biden has not yet visited Africa this year, the administration stressed extensive engagement through multiple Cabinet-level visits and initiatives supporting African representation in global institutions. The meeting will also address economic collaboration, regional security challenges, and democratic governance concerns, reflecting a comprehensive and evolving US-Angola partnership.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### US-Angola Partnership Overview
The meeting previewed President Biden's upcoming engagement with Angolan President Lourenço, highlighting a historic year of strengthened US-Angola relations coinciding with 30 years of diplomatic ties. The partnership has deepened through high-level visits, infrastructure investments, and collaboration on regional and global issues, reflecting Angola's leadership and the Biden administration's commitment.
- Senior Administration Official states the relationship has transformed due to President Lourenço's leadership and US investment.
- The official notes multiple high-level US visits to Angola, including the first-ever Secretary of Defense visit.
- The partnership includes major infrastructure projects and cooperation on security and global priorities.
#### Lobito Corridor Investment
The US has committed over a billion dollars to the Lobito Corridor, a major infrastructure project connecting Angola, DRC, and Zambia to global markets via the Lobito port. This investment supports rail, digital connectivity, solar power, and rural bridges, aiming to boost regional trade and attract further investment, including from American businesses.
- Senior Administration Official describes the Lobito Corridor as the largest US rail investment in sub-Saharan Africa.
- The project includes upgrading 4G/5G networks, introducing mobile money, and adding 500 megawatts of solar power.
- US economic agencies like the Development Finance Corporation and EXIM Bank offer tools to support US companies investing in the corridor.
- Additional investments have been mobilized from the African Development Bank and the EU, totaling over a billion dollars.
#### Regional Security and Diplomacy
The US and Angola collaborate closely on regional security challenges, particularly the conflict in eastern DRC. Angola's diplomatic efforts through the Luanda Process are supported by the US, which has engaged in consultations including DNI Avril Haines's recent trip. The upcoming meeting will review these efforts and discuss ongoing regional concerns.
- Senior Administration Official highlights Angola's leadership in regional security and the Luanda Process.
- The US appreciates Angola's diplomatic role in addressing eastern DRC conflict.
- DNI Avril Haines's recent visits to DRC and Rwanda were coordinated with Angolan officials.
- The meeting will include discussions on regional security and upcoming Congolese elections.
#### Defense Cooperation Plans
The US and Angola plan to deepen defense cooperation, with a high-level dialogue scheduled for 2024 to discuss agreements and military modernization. The relationship has grown since Secretary Austin's historic visit, focusing on education, maritime security, peacekeeping, and English language capacity, reflecting Angola's interest in strengthening military ties.
- Senior Administration Official confirms plans for a 2024 high-level defense dialogue.
- Secretary Austin's September visit marked the first by a US Secretary of Defense to Angola.
- Discussions include military modernization and expanded mil-to-mil exchanges.
- Focus areas include education, maritime security, peacekeeping, and language training.
#### Artemis Accords Signing
Angola will sign the Artemis Accords, becoming the third African country to do so, signaling its commitment to peaceful and sustainable space exploration. The Accords establish principles for civil space activities, and Angola's participation reflects its growing role in global space governance, aligning with US efforts to include African partners in future space policy discussions.
- Senior Administration Official announces Angola's signing of the Artemis Accords during the meeting.
- The Accords promote peaceful, transparent cooperation in space exploration.
- Angola follows Rwanda and Nigeria as the third African signatory.
- President Biden has longstanding knowledge of Angola's history and relationship with the US.
#### US Engagement with Africa and Presidential Travel
The administration emphasizes extensive engagement with Africa through multiple Cabinet-level visits and initiatives, despite President Biden not having traveled to the continent yet this year. The official underscores ongoing investments, diplomatic efforts, and inclusion of African voices in global governance as evidence of strong commitment, without announcing a presidential trip date.
- Senior Administration Official cites 16 Cabinet-level trips to Africa this year.
- The administration launched an $800 million Digital Transformation initiative with Africa.
- Efforts include supporting African representation in the G20, IMF, and UN reforms.
- No announcement was made regarding President Biden's travel plans to Africa.
#### US-Angola Economic and Security Collaboration
The meeting will cover economic investments, including the Lobito Corridor, and regional security issues such as the eastern DRC conflict and Zimbabwean sanctions. Angola's leadership in SADC and regional peace efforts will be discussed, alongside US support for diplomatic solutions and security cooperation to protect key infrastructure.
- Senior Administration Official notes Angola's role as SADC head and regional security leader.
- Discussions will include Zimbabwe sanctions and regional election concerns.
- The US and Angola coordinate on de-escalation efforts in eastern DRC.
- Security of the Lobito Corridor is a consideration amid regional terrorism threats.
