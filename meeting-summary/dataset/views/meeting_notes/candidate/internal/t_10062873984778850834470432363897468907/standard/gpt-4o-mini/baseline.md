### Overview
The Public Safety Advisory Board meeting, led by Chair Cleave Frink, focused on updates regarding services for residents living in vehicles and the management of public right-of-way. Key participants included Kimberly Thomas, Parneet Dhindsa, and Captain Scott Nelson, who presented data on homelessness trends and discussed the enforcement of parking regulations. Public comments highlighted concerns about the impact of towing on vulnerable residents, the need for more public restrooms, and the desire for hybrid meeting options. The meeting concluded with a commitment to ongoing dialogue and collaboration to improve services for the community.

#### Update on Services for Residents Living in Vehicles
Kimberly Thomas and her team present an update on services for residents living in vehicles, discussing the city's approach to homelessness and the importance of collaboration among various city departments. They highlight the need for compassion in enforcement and outreach efforts.
- Kimberly Thomas emphasizes the need for a collaborative approach to address homelessness.
- The city has committed nearly $11 million over six years to support residents experiencing homelessness.

#### Data on Homelessness and Vehicle Residents
Parneet Dhindsa shares data from the County's point-in-time count, indicating a decrease in homelessness in Mountain View. The city has also conducted its own counts of vehicles used for living, showing a downward trend in recent years.
- Parneet Dhindsa reports a 43% decrease in homelessness from 2019 to 2022 in Mountain View.
- The city has counted between 200 to 300 vehicles used for living, with recent counts trending downward.

#### Public Right of Way Management
Lieutenant Matt Atkins discusses the management of public right-of-way and the regulations governing parking. He explains the enforcement process for oversized vehicles and the rationale behind the 72-hour parking rule.
- Lieutenant Atkins outlines the regulations for managing public right-of-way and parking enforcement.
- The 72-hour parking rule is enforced to maintain public safety and accessibility.

#### Police Outreach and Enforcement Team Structure
Captain Scott Nelson describes the structure of the Police Outreach and Enforcement team, emphasizing the role of the Neighborhood and Event Services Unit in addressing homelessness and vehicle residents.
- Captain Nelson explains the composition of the Youth and Community Services Unit.
- The NES unit focuses on education, outreach, and enforcement for those experiencing homelessness.

#### Community Feedback and Concerns
Board members and public participants express concerns about the enforcement of parking regulations, the need for more public restrooms, and the challenges faced by residents living in vehicles. Suggestions for improvement are discussed.
- Community members advocate for more public restrooms and reconsideration of the 72-hour parking rule enforcement.
- Concerns are raised about the impact of towing on vulnerable residents living in vehicles.
