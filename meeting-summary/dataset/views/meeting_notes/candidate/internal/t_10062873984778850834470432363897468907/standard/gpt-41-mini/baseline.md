### Overview
The Public Safety Advisory Board meeting, chaired by Cleave Frink, focused primarily on services and enforcement related to residents living in vehicles within Mountain View. Key city staff including Kimberly Thomas and Parneet Dhindsa provided a detailed update on the continuum of care programs, safe parking initiatives, and partnerships addressing homelessness. Police representatives outlined enforcement strategies for parking regulations, including Measure C and 72-hour parking rules, emphasizing education and outreach by the Neighborhood and Event Services Unit. Board members engaged in a robust discussion about balancing enforcement with compassion, accessibility of hygiene facilities, and the dignity of vehicle residents. Public commenters echoed these themes, advocating for increased services, reconsideration of enforcement policies, and improved communication. The meeting concluded with clarifications on safe parking lot operations, towing procedures, and the legal context of residential parking permits for vehicle residents. Officer Jenleah McPherson's outreach work received particular commendation for its positive community impact.

#### Update on Services for Residents Living in Vehicles and Parking Enforcement
Assistant to the City Manager Kimberly Thomas, Human Services Manager Parneet Dhindsa, Police Captain Scott Nelson, Lieutenant Matt Atkins, and Community Outreach Officer Jenleah McPherson presented a comprehensive update on services and enforcement related to residents living in vehicles. The presentation covered the city's continuum of care approach, partnerships with community organizations, safe parking programs, public right of way management, and enforcement data on parking violations and oversized vehicles. The city has invested nearly $11 million over six years to address homelessness, including safe parking and hygiene services. Enforcement of Measure C and 72-hour parking restrictions resumed in October 2022 with a focus on education and outreach by the Neighborhood and Event Services Unit (NES).
- Kimberly Thomas outlined the city's coordinated approach to homelessness and services for vehicle residents.
- Parneet Dhindsa shared data showing a 43% decrease in homeless individuals since 2019 and trends in vehicle counts.
- Lieutenant Atkins explained public right of way management and parking regulations.
- Captain Nelson described the NES unit's role in outreach and enforcement, including Measure C and 72-hour parking rules.
- Enforcement data showed compliance with narrow street ordinances and ongoing citations for 72-hour violations.
- The city operates three safe parking lots with services including restrooms, showers, and case management.
#### Board Member Questions and Discussion
Board members asked detailed questions about current active programs, restroom accessibility for vehicle residents, safety concerns including fire hazards, enforcement procedures, and the balance between neighborhood needs and resident dignity. Concerns were raised about the feasibility of restroom access for residents living far from service locations and the impact of enforcement on vulnerable populations. The discussion also covered the interpretation of parking ordinances, communication between police units, and the challenges of providing adequate services and housing options.
- Member Kavita Aiyar questioned which programs remain active and advocated for more accessible restroom facilities near vehicle residents.
- Member Kalwant Sandhu inquired about safety checks for fire hazards and assistance for non-functional vehicles.
- Member Joan Brodovsky asked about the number of people seeking help versus those served and clarification on sheltered versus unsheltered definitions.
- Member Eva Tang raised concerns about communication failures leading to vehicle towing and sought improvements.
- Members discussed the interpretation and enforcement of the 72-hour parking rule and the challenges of balancing enforcement with compassion.
#### Public Comments on Services and Enforcement
Public commenters expressed support for compassionate approaches and raised concerns about enforcement practices, including the 72-hour parking rule and its impact on residents living in vehicles. Suggestions included reallocating enforcement funds to services, reconsidering the 72-hour rule for oversized vehicles, and providing more accessible restroom facilities. Some highlighted the need for dignity and autonomy for vehicle residents and questioned the effectiveness of current enforcement strategies.
- Tim McKenzie supported the transition from police to civilian outreach roles and questioned the enforcement approach to the 72-hour rule.
- Marguerite advocated for permanent restrooms in neighborhoods and reconsideration of the 72-hour rule for oversized vehicles.
- Dana PD proposed exploring a residential parking permit program for vehicle residents to reduce enforcement burdens.
- Brown emphasized respect for autonomy and cautioned against coercive service requirements.
- Public comments highlighted the need for improved communication and more supportive services.
#### Final Board Comments and Clarifications
Board members and staff discussed the number and locations of safe parking lots, the towing and vehicle retrieval process, and the challenges of balancing neighborhood concerns with the needs of vehicle residents. The importance of Officer McPherson's outreach work was recognized. Questions about residential parking permit programs for vehicle residents were addressed, noting past legal considerations. The board expressed interest in reviewing past staff reports on this topic and continuing to explore solutions.
- Kimberly Thomas confirmed three city-operated safe parking lots: Shoreline, Evelyn, and Terra Bella.
- Captain Nelson explained the towing process, fees, and community assistance for vehicle retrieval.
- Kimberly Thomas noted that a residential parking permit program for vehicle residents was explored but deemed legally problematic.
- Board members requested access to past staff reports on residential parking permits for further review.
- Chair Frink praised Officer McPherson's compassionate outreach efforts in the community.
