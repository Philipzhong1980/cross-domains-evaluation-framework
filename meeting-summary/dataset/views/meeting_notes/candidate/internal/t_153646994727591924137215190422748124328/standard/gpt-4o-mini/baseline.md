### Overview
The Joint Administrative Zoning and Subdivision Committee convened on October 12, 2022, led by Zoning Administrator Stephanie Williams, with key contributions from Ed Arango and Diana Fizely. The meeting included public communications, the approval of a consent calendar item, and detailed discussions on three main projects: a new single-family home at 339 Franklin St, a one-year extension for a condominium project at 1411 to 1495 W El Camino Real, and a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave. All projects received unanimous approvals, reflecting their compliance with local planning standards and community needs.

The meeting begins with an opportunity for public comments, allowing individuals to address the zoning administrator on any non-agenda items. This segment is crucial for community engagement, although no public speakers are present during this time.

- No members of the public choose to speak during this segment.

#### Consent Calendar Approval
The consent calendar includes a single item for approval, specifically regarding 2134 Old Middlefield Way. The item is approved without any public discussion, indicating a smooth consensus on the matter.
- Stephanie Williams moves to approve the consent calendar after confirming no public interest in discussion.

#### 339 Franklin St Project Review
The committee discusses a project at 339 Franklin St, which involves a planned community permit, development review permit, and a heritage tree removal permit. The project aims to construct a new single-family home and remove a heritage tree due to safety concerns, with the proposal deemed consistent with local planning standards.
- Ella Carraccian presents the project details, emphasizing the need for tree removal due to health risks.
- Jenny Gong, the applicant, supports the project by highlighting the current structure's poor condition.

#### Approval of 339 Franklin St Project
Following public comments, the committee deliberates on the project and unanimously approves the planned community permit and development review permit. The decision reflects the project's alignment with zoning regulations and community standards.
- Stephanie Williams confirms the approval of the planned community permit and heritage tree removal, subject to conditions.
- The lot line adjustment is also approved unanimously by the subdivision committee.

#### 1411 to 1495 W El Camino Real Extension
The committee reviews a request for a one-year extension of a previously approved vesting tentative map for a condominium project. The extension is necessary for the applicant to finalize construction documents and permits, and staff recommends approval based on compliance with planning standards.
- Brittany Whitehill presents the request for a one-year extension, confirming its consistency with the El Camino Real Precise Plan.
- Diana Fizely motions for approval, which is seconded and unanimously supported by the committee.

#### 890 Central Ave Lot Line Adjustment
The final item involves a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave, which aims to relocate a lot line between two existing lots. The project is found to be consistent with zoning standards and is also recommended for approval by staff.
- Brittany Whitehill outlines the details of the lot line adjustment, confirming its compliance with zoning regulations.
- Ed Arango motions for approval, which is seconded and unanimously approved by the committee.
