### Overview
  The Subdivision Committee meeting, led by Zoning Administrator Stephanie Williams with City Engineer Ed Arango and Diana Fizzelli (City Attorney's office), addresses four actionable items. The committee approves the consent calendar (including 2134 Old Middlefield Way) with no public requests to pull items. At 339 Franklin Street staff (Ella Carraccian) presents a proposal for a new single-family home, heritage tree removal, and a lot line adjustment; staff finds the project CEQA-exempt and consistent with planning policies, the Zoning Administrator approves the development permits and tree removal, and the Subdivision Committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment with conditions including a balcony-size limit and required modifications to address tree health and construction-related root impacts. Brittany Whitehill presents two subdivision matters: a one-year extension for the vesting tentative map at 1411–1495 W El Camino Real to allow preparation of construction documents (53 condominium units), and a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Avenue; staff recommends and the committee unanimously grants both approvals with the recommended conditions, and applicants (including Jenny Gong and Steve Saray of Lux Larva) address the committee as needed. Stephanie Williams notes approvals are appealable to City Council within 10 calendar days, and the meeting adjourns at 4:15 PM.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Consent calendar approval
  The committee approves the consent calendar in a single motion because no public members requested items to be pulled for discussion. The consent calendar included one item at 2134 Old Middlefield Way. The approval is subject to the standard appeal period.
- Zoning Administrator Stephanie Williams states the consent calendar will be approved in one motion unless a member of the public pulls an item.
- Stephanie Williams confirms there are no public requests to speak on the consent calendar and approves the consent calendar.
- Stephanie Williams notes the consent calendar item is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.
#### 339 Franklin St project
  Staff presents a project at 339 Franklin Street proposing a new ~500-square-foot single-family home, a planned community permit, a development review permit, a heritage tree removal permit, and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots into one on a 0.17-acre site. Staff finds the project consistent with the Downtown Precise Plan and General Plan, determines a categorical exemption under CEQA, and recommends approval with conditions. The Zoning Administrator approves the development permits and heritage tree removal, and the Subdivision Committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment with conditions including a balcony-size limitation.
- Planner Ella Carraccian explains the project requests a planned community permit, development review permit, heritage tree removal, and a lot line adjustment to combine two lots on a 0.17-acre site.
- Ella Carraccian states staff determines the project is categorically exempt from CEQA and consistent with the P19 Downtown Precise Plan and the General Plan density allowances.
- Ella Carraccian describes the heritage tree as structurally compromised with narrow angled crotches and notes construction would cause nearly 50% root loss, creating risk to adjacent homes and supporting removal.
- Applicant Jenny Gong says the existing duplex is uninhabitable and that constructing a new two-story single-family home will improve the neighborhood.
- Zoning Administrator Stephanie Williams approves the planned community permit, development review permit, and heritage tree removal subject to the draft findings and conditions of approval.
- City Engineer Ed Arango moves to approve the lot line adjustment and the Subdivision Committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment, as seconded by Diana Fizzelli.
- Ella Carraccian states a condition requires the applicant to remove or modify existing balconies so they do not exceed 150 square feet prior to submitting to the Building Department.
#### Vest. tentative map extension
  The applicant requests a one-year extension of a previously approved vesting tentative map for 1411–1495 W El Camino Real to create 53 condominium lots plus one common lot on a 1.25-acre site. Staff finds the extension consistent with the El Camino Real Precise Plan and the subdivision map, determines a CEQA categorical exemption under Section 15332 (infill), and recommends conditional approval. The Subdivision Committee unanimously grants the one-year extension to allow time for preparation of construction documents and permits.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill explains the extension request is for a vesting tentative map to create 53 condominium lots and one common lot on a 1.25-acre site in the P38 El Camino Real Precise Plan area.
- Brittany Whitehill states staff finds the requested one-year extension consistent with the Precise Plan and General Plan land use designation and categorically exempt under CEQA Section 15332.
- Applicant representative Steve Saray of Lux Larva Development states the extension is requested so the team can complete construction documents and obtain permits.
- Diana Fizzelli moves to approve the one-year extension and Ed Arango seconds the motion, and the Subdivision Committee unanimously approves the extension.
#### 890 Central Ave lot adjustment
  Staff presents a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Avenue to relocate a line between two existing lots on a 0.44-acre site in the R1 Single-Family Residential District. Staff finds the proposal consistent with R1 zoning and the low-density residential General Plan designation, issues a CEQA categorical exemption under Section 15305, and recommends conditional approval. The Subdivision Committee reviews the materials and unanimously approves the lot line adjustment subject to the recommended conditions.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill states the project requests a lot line adjustment between two existing lots on a 0.44-acre site and is located in the R1 Single-Family Residential District.
- Brittany Whitehill says staff determines the proposal is consistent with R1 zoning standards and categorically exempt from CEQA under Section 15305.
- Ed Arango reports he has reviewed the project materials and moves to approve the lot line adjustment subject to the stated conditions of approval.
- Diana Fizzelli seconds the motion and the Subdivision Committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment.
