### Overview
The Joint Administrative Zoning and Subdivision Committee public hearing, led by Zoning Administrator Stephanie Williams with subdivision committee members City Engineer Ed Arango and City Attorney’s Office representative Diana Fizely, focuses on three land use items. First, Planner Ella Carraccian presents a proposal at 339 Franklin St for a new 2,500 square foot single-family home, associated heritage tree removal, and a lot line adjustment, with applicant Jenny Gong emphasizing the replacement of an uninhabitable duplex and staff citing consistency with the Downtown Precise Plan and safety concerns from tree instability; Williams approves the development and tree removal permits, and the subdivision committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment, all subject to conditions and appealable to the City Council. Second, Planner Brittany Whitehill outlines a one-year extension of a vesting tentative map for 53 condominium lots and one common lot at 1411–1495 W El Camino Real, where applicant representative Steve Saray notes the need for more time to complete construction documents; the committee finds the request consistent with applicable plans and CEQA exemptions and grants the extension unanimously with standard conditions and an appeal window. Finally, Whitehill presents a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave in an R1 district to relocate a lot line between two existing lots, which staff confirms complies with zoning, the General Plan, the Subdivision Map Act, and CEQA, and the subdivision committee unanimously approves the adjustment subject to conditions before adjourning the meeting.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Planned home and heritage tree removal at 339 Franklin St
Staff presents a proposal at 339 Franklin St for a planned community permit and development review permit to construct a new 2,500 square foot single-family home, along with a heritage tree removal permit and a lot line adjustment on a 0.17-acre site. The project sits within the P-19 Downtown Precise Plan Area B and must meet design standards and transition goals between the downtown core and surrounding neighborhoods. A key issue is the removal of a shared heritage tree that poses structural and safety risks and would be destabilized by the new foundation work. The zoning administrator and subdivision committee ultimately approve the entitlements, subject to conditions, including balcony size limits, and note the appeal period to City Council.
- Ella Carraccian explains that the project at 339 Franklin St requests a planned community permit and development review permit to construct a 2,500 square foot single-family home on a 0.17-acre site.
- Ella Carraccian states that the project also includes a heritage tree removal permit to remove one heritage tree located on the shared property line with the neighboring lot.
- Ella Carraccian notes that the project is consistent with the P-19 Downtown Precise Plan, particularly Area B’s intent to transition from the downtown core to surrounding one- and two-story single-family homes.
- Ella Carraccian reports that the project complies with development standards for building height, floor area ratio, setbacks, and open space, and is consistent with the General Plan allowance of up to 35 dwelling units per acre.
- Ella Carraccian explains that the heritage tree has narrow-angle crotches near ground level that are at substantial risk of breakage and that both leaning trunks add pressure, increasing the likelihood of failure that could damage adjacent homes.
- Ella Carraccian adds that foundation construction for the new home would cause nearly 50% root loss to the tree, further increasing instability and creating a safety concern for both neighbors.
- Ella Carraccian states that a lot line adjustment is proposed to remove the existing lot line and combine two lots into one on the 0.17-acre project site.
- Ella Carraccian highlights that a key condition of approval requires the applicant to remove or modify existing balconies so that balcony area does not exceed 150 square feet.
- Applicant Jenny Gong states that the existing duplex structure on the property is uninhabitable and not rentable due to lack of utilities and basic functionality.
- Applicant Jenny Gong asserts that replacing the current duplex with a new two-story single-family home will improve both the neighborhood and the broader community.
- Stephanie Williams, acting as Zoning Administrator, states that she has reviewed the project, staff’s recommendation, draft findings, and recommended conditions of approval.
- Stephanie Williams announces that she will approve the planned community permit, development review permit, and heritage tree removal permit subject to the staff draft findings and conditions of approval.
- Ed Arango states that he has read the staff memo, conditions of approval, and plans for the project.
- Ed Arango moves to approve the lot line adjustment to remove the existing lot line and combine two lots into one lot on the 0.17-acre project site at 339 Franklin St, subject to the conditions of approval and conformance with the General Plan and Zoning Ordinance.
- Diana Fazely seconds the motion to approve the lot line adjustment at 339 Franklin St.
- Stephanie Williams records a unanimous vote in favor of the lot line adjustment by the subdivision committee.
- Stephanie Williams notes that the entire project at 339 Franklin St is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.

#### One-year map extension at 1411–1495 W El Camino Real
The committee reviews a request for a one-year extension of a previously approved vesting tentative map for a mixed-use infill project at 1411–1495 W El Camino Real. The extension covers the creation of 53 condominium lots and one common lot on a 1.25-acre site in the P-38 El Camino Real Precise Plan area. Staff finds that the extension remains consistent with the precise plan, mixed-use corridor general plan designation, and the Subdivision Map Act, and that the project qualifies for a CEQA infill exemption. The subdivision committee unanimously grants the extension to allow the applicant time to complete construction documents and secure building permits.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill describes the project as located on the south side of West El Camino Real between Rich Avenue and Mountain View Avenue in the P-38 El Camino Real Precise Plan area.
- Brittany Whitehill states that the applicant requests a one-year extension of a previously approved vesting tentative map to create 53 condominium lots and one common condominium lot on a 1.25-acre project site.
- Brittany Whitehill explains that staff finds the proposed vesting tentative map extension consistent with the El Camino Real Precise Plan and the mixed-use corridor General Plan land use designation.
- Brittany Whitehill notes that staff determines the project is consistent with the Subdivision Map Act requirements.
- Brittany Whitehill states that staff has determined the project is categorically exempt from CEQA under Section 15332, infill development, of the CEQA Guidelines.
- Brittany Whitehill reports that staff has reviewed the proposal and comments and recommends conditional approval of the one-year extension.
- Applicant representative Steve Saray, from the Lux Larva development, states that the team is requesting the one-year extension to complete construction documents and obtain permits.
- Steve Saray offers to answer any questions from the committee regarding the extension request.
- Diana Fazely states that she has reviewed the materials for the item and has no questions.
- Diana Fazely moves to approve the one-year extension of the vesting tentative map at 1411–1495 W El Camino Real.
- Ed Arango seconds the motion to approve the vesting tentative map extension.
- Stephanie Williams records a unanimous approval of the one-year vesting tentative map extension by the subdivision committee.
- Stephanie Williams notes that the extension decision is appealable to the City Council within 10 calendar days.

#### Lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave
The committee considers a lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave, located at the northeast corner of Sterling Rd and Central Ave in an R1 single-family residential district. The proposal seeks to relocate a lot line between two existing lots on a 0.44-acre site without changing overall density. Staff confirms that the adjustment complies with R1 zoning standards, the low-density residential general plan designation, the Subdivision Map Act, and qualifies for a minor alterations CEQA exemption. The subdivision committee unanimously approves the lot line adjustment subject to standard conditions of approval.
- Planner Brittany Whitehill states that the project is located at 890 Central Ave on the northeast corner of Sterling Road and Central Avenue in the R1 Single Family Residential District.
- Brittany Whitehill explains that the applicant requests a lot line adjustment to relocate a lot line between two existing lots on a 0.44-acre project site.
- Brittany Whitehill reports that staff finds the proposed lot line adjustment consistent with R1 zoning standards and the low-density residential General Plan land use designation.
- Brittany Whitehill notes that staff determines the project complies with the Subdivision Map Act.
- Brittany Whitehill states that the project is categorically exempt from CEQA under Section 15305, minor alterations in land use limitations, of the CEQA Guidelines.
- Brittany Whitehill recommends conditional approval of the lot line adjustment based on staff’s review and analysis.
- Ed Arango states that he has read the project materials and has no questions about the proposal.
- Ed Arango moves to approve the lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave, subject to the conditions of approval and conformance with the General Plan and Zoning Ordinance.
- Diana Fizely seconds the motion to approve the lot line adjustment at 890 Central Ave.
- Stephanie Williams records a unanimous approval of the lot line adjustment by the subdivision committee.
