### Overview
  Press secretary Mr. Bates opens the briefing by announcing the President’s trip to Allentown to showcase Bidenomics, highlights record small-business formation and manufacturing job gains in Pennsylvania, and introduces implementation of a SAVE plan provision that will cancel remaining student debt for certain low-balance borrowers after 10 years in repayment. Admiral Kirby fields most questions about U.S. strikes on Houthi targets, saying the battle damage assessment is ongoing, asserting the strikes targeted military capabilities to store and launch drones and missiles, and emphasizing the United States does not seek a wider war while reserving the right to act to protect forces and commerce; he expresses confidence in the legal authorities used, confirms Secretary Austin’s full participation, and notes the administration is reviewing the Houthis’ FTO status and continuing to consider sanctions on Iran. The White House says it is monitoring economic effects such as rising oil prices but sees no immediate macroeconomic impacts, defers to the Department of Justice on the Buffalo shooter death-penalty decision while calling the attack horrific, urges House Republicans to honor a bipartisan funding framework to avoid a shutdown, and contrasts the administration’s economic approach with opponents’ tax proposals. Overall, the briefing centers on immediate national-security actions in the Red Sea, ongoing assessments and legal and diplomatic considerations surrounding those strikes, and a set of domestic policy talking points on the economy, student loan relief, and budget preparedness.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Presidential visit Allentown
  Mr. Bates outlines the President’s travel to Pennsylvania to visit small businesses in Lehigh Valley and Allentown as a demonstration of the administration’s economic agenda. He frames the visit as highlighting a local comeback tied to Bidenomics and the Investing in America agenda. The trip is intended to showcase small business growth and community revitalization on the ground.
- Mr. Bates says the President is visiting several small businesses in Lehigh Valley and the Allentown area today.
- Mr. Bates argues Allentown’s recovery illustrates Bidenomics and the Investing in America agenda producing an economic comeback.
  #### Small business growth data
  Mr. Bates emphasizes record levels of new business applications as evidence the economic agenda is working, citing 16 million new applications in the last three years. He presents this as greater than the four years of the prior administration combined and as a sign of entrepreneurial confidence. The administration is using these statistics to argue for continued support of its policies.
- Mr. Bates states 16 million new business applications have been filed in the last three years.
- Mr. Bates frames each new business application as an act of hope and confidence in the economy.
  #### Manufacturing jobs Pennsylvania
  Mr. Bates attributes a resurgence in manufacturing to Bidenomics, citing 26,000 new manufacturing jobs created in Pennsylvania under President Biden. He contrasts that with a reported loss of 23,000 manufacturing jobs in Pennsylvania under the prior administration. The point is framed to support the administration’s claim of restoring American manufacturing.
- Mr. Bates says Bidenomics has created 26,000 new manufacturing jobs in Pennsylvania under President Biden.
- Mr. Bates contrasts that figure with his claim that Pennsylvania lost 23,000 manufacturing jobs under Donald Trump.
  #### SAVE plan implementation
  Mr. Bates announces implementation of a key SAVE plan provision that will cancel remaining student loan balances for certain borrowers starting next month. Borrowers who took out less than $12,000 and have been in repayment for 10 years will receive cancellation, which the administration says will particularly help community college and low-income borrowers. The White House encourages eligible borrowers to enroll in SAVE at StudentAid.gov and cites current enrollment numbers.
- Mr. Bates announces that starting next month borrowers with less than $12,000 in loans and 10 years in repayment will get remaining debt cancelled under SAVE.
- Mr. Bates encourages eligible borrowers to sign up for the SAVE plan at StudentAid.gov and notes 6.9 million borrowers are already enrolled.
  #### MLK Day event Philadelphia
  Mr. Bates notes the President will travel to Philadelphia next Monday for a service event in honor of Martin Luther King Jr. Day. The mention is a logistical heads-up and ties into the administration’s public engagement schedule. No additional details or policy actions are attached to the announcement.
- Mr. Bates says the President will travel to Philadelphia next Monday for a Martin Luther King Jr. Day service event.
  #### Battle damage assessment
  Admiral Kirby reports that a battle damage assessment (BDA) of strikes against Houthi targets is ongoing and could take hours before a clearer picture of damage emerges. He reiterates that every target struck was a valid military target focused on degrading the Houthis’ ability to store, launch, and guide drones and missiles. The administration is still compiling operational reports and assessments.
- Mr. Kirby says a battle damage assessment is ongoing and could take some hours before a clearer sense of damage is known.
- Mr. Kirby emphasizes the strikes targeted Houthi capabilities to store, launch, and guide drones and missiles.
  #### Avoiding wider war
  Admiral Kirby repeatedly states the United States is not seeking a war with Yemen and frames the strikes as intended to prevent escalation. He stresses that the President’s actions aim to protect U.S. forces, facilities, and international commerce while deterring further reckless attacks. The White House maintains that strikes are proportionate and defensive in nature.
- Mr. Kirby says the U.S. is not interested in a war with Yemen and seeks to prevent escalation.
- Mr. Kirby quotes the President’s reservation of the right to take further action to protect troops, facilities, and international commerce.
  #### Legal authority confidence
  Admiral Kirby responds to suggestions the War Powers Resolution was violated by expressing confidence in the legal authorities used to conduct the strikes. He frames the administration as comfortable with the legal basis for action. The statement addresses congressional concerns without outlining specific legal rationales in the briefing.
- Mr. Kirby says the administration is very comfortable and confident in the legal authorities the President exercised to conduct the strikes.
  #### Risk of retaliation
  Reporters ask whether the administration expects Houthi retaliation in the Red Sea; Admiral Kirby acknowledges the possibility and says no one was Pollyannaish about that risk. He lacks operational reports at the moment but reiterates the Houthis face a choice to stop reckless attacks. The administration remains vigilant for further actions.
- Mr. Kirby says the possibility of Houthi retaliation is real and that nobody assumed otherwise going into the strikes.
- Mr. Kirby says he does not have operational reports to confirm whether an attack is imminent or underway.
  #### Decision-making timeline
  Admiral Kirby explains the President approved strike options after a large Houthi attack on Tuesday that involved about 18 drones plus cruise and ballistic missiles, and that the President had been kept up to speed as that attack unfolded. He describes a sequence of briefings and the President convening his national security team before approving responses. Secretary Austin is portrayed as fully engaged in the process despite being in the hospital.
- Mr. Kirby says the President was kept up to speed during Tuesday’s large attack and approved response options after being briefed.
- Mr. Kirby says Secretary Austin participated fully in the decision-making process and briefed the President from the hospital.
  #### Context for timing
  Admiral Kirby places the strikes in a broader context of diplomatic and military steps taken since late November, including U.N. engagement, building Operation Prosperity Guardian for Red Sea defense, and issuing a final warning to the Houthis. He says Tuesday’s large-scale attack violated that warning and prompted the response. The administration frames the action as the culmination of stepped-up deterrence and defensive measures.
- Mr. Kirby says the administration has pursued diplomatic measures, a U.N. Security Council effort, and Operation Prosperity Guardian since late November.
- Mr. Kirby says Tuesday’s large attack violated what could be understood as a final warning to the Houthis and prompted the strikes.
  #### Strikes: signal or destruction
  When asked whether the strikes were primarily a signal, Admiral Kirby insists they were intended to disrupt and degrade Houthi military capabilities rather than merely send a message. He reiterates that each target was militarily significant and that assessments of effectiveness are ongoing. The administration frames the strikes as kinetic actions with concrete military objectives.
- Mr. Kirby says the strikes were designed to disrupt and degrade Houthi military capabilities and were not just signaling exercises.
- Mr. Kirby stresses that each target struck was militarily significant to the Houthis’ operational ability.
  #### Iran role and sanctions
  Admiral Kirby acknowledges Iran’s backing of the Houthis and notes the administration will continue to hold Iran accountable for destabilizing activities, including use of sanctions. He says economic sanctions remain a tool on the table but declines to pre-announce specific measures. Kirby also declines to discuss any diplomatic channels with Iran or other regional actors at this time.
- Mr. Kirby says Iran backs the Houthis and that the administration will continue to hold Iran accountable for destabilizing behavior.
- Mr. Kirby says economic sanctions remain on the table but will not prejudge any decisions that have not been made.
  #### FTO designation review
  Admiral Kirby confirms the administration is reviewing the Foreign Terrorist Organization finding for the Houthis after previously delisting them. He says the review is ongoing and primarily led by the State Department, and he declines to provide a timeline for a decision. The matter remains unresolved at the time of the briefing.
- Mr. Kirby says the administration is reviewing the Foreign Terrorist Organization finding on the Houthis and that no decision has been made.
- Mr. Kirby says he cannot provide a specific timeline and that the State Department is largely handling the review.
  #### Coalition participation
  Admiral Kirby declines to detail which partners participated operationally and which did not but says many coalition partners offered support, both operational and rhetorical. He notes that several nations provided nonoperational backing or rhetorical support for the U.S. action. The administration emphasizes multilateral cooperation without elaborating on specific diplomatic exchanges.
- Mr. Kirby says many coalition partners signed on to support the action in operational and nonoperational ways.
- Mr. Kirby declines to get into the specifics of diplomatic conversations with partners regarding participation.
  #### Taiwan election monitoring
  Admiral Kirby says the United States is watching Taiwan’s elections and supports free, fair, and transparent democratic processes without taking a position on outcomes. He adds that it would be unacceptable for any actor to interfere in that democratic exercise and that the U.S. will monitor developments closely. The administration stresses respect for Taiwan’s democratic institutions.
- Mr. Kirby says the U.S. will watch Taiwan’s elections and supports free, fair, and transparent processes.
- Mr. Kirby says it would be unacceptable for any actor to interfere with Taiwan’s democratic exercise.
  #### Economic impacts and oil prices
  Reporters ask about oil prices topping $80 and any supply disruptions; Mr. Bates says the White House is monitoring conditions and will coordinate with international partners on long-term impacts. He reiterates that the Houthis are endangering freedom of navigation and that their behavior is causing unjust costs to many countries. Admiral Kirby earlier said he is not seeing economic impacts at this time.
- Mr. Bates says the administration is monitoring oil market conditions and coordinating with international partners about potential impacts.
- Mr. Bates blames the Houthis for endangering freedom of navigation and causing unjust costs to other countries.
  #### Buffalo shooter death penalty
  Mr. Bates responds to a DOJ announcement that prosecutors will seek the death penalty in the Buffalo supermarket case by calling the shooting grotesque and tragic. He notes the President met with families in 2022 and reiterates the administration’s stance that hate has no place in America. Bates defers to the Department of Justice on case-specific charging and sentencing decisions, while noting the President’s broader views on the death penalty.
- Mr. Bates calls the Buffalo shooting grotesque and heartbreaking and recalls the President’s 2022 meeting with victims’ families.
- Mr. Bates says the White House will leave case-specific sentencing decisions to the Department of Justice.
  #### Government funding contingency
  Mr. Bates discusses a bipartisan funding framework reaffirmed by Speaker Johnson and urges House Republicans to keep their word to avert a shutdown. He says OMB and agencies prepare for contingencies but frames such preparations as cautionary. The administration emphasizes that a shutdown can be prevented if Republicans honor the existing agreement.
- Mr. Bates says a bipartisan funding framework exists and urges House Republicans to keep their word to avoid a shutdown.
- Mr. Bates says OMB and agencies are preparing for every contingency as a matter of caution.
  #### Message to progressives on war
  In response to concerns from progressives about escalation, Mr. Bates reiterates Admiral Kirby’s point that the administration is not seeking a war and describes the strikes as proportionate and an act of self-defense with bipartisan support. He frames the action as a measured response intended to protect U.S. interests while avoiding broader conflict. The administration seeks to reassure domestic constituencies that it will not be drawn into unnecessary large-scale war.
- Mr. Bates says the administration is not looking for any kind of war and describes the strikes as proportionate and justified.
- Mr. Bates emphasizes the action is framed as self-defense and notes it has bipartisan support.
  #### Reaction to tax cut proposals
  When asked about Donald Trump’s proposal to make tax cuts permanent, Mr. Bates declines to comment directly on 2024 politics but contrasts Bidenomics with a characterization of MAGAnomics that would favor tax giveaways for the wealthy. He highlights recent economic indicators — lower inflation, higher employment, more small businesses, and expanded health coverage — as reasons to continue the current course. The administration reiterates its preference for policies aimed at growing the middle class rather than cutting taxes for the wealthy.
- Mr. Bates declines to comment on the 2024 elections but contrasts Bidenomics’ progress with what he calls MAGAnomics’ potential tax giveaways for the wealthy.
- Mr. Bates cites recent economic improvements, including lower inflation and more small businesses, as reasons to continue the current economic agenda.
