### Overview
The meeting is an in-flight press gaggle led by White House officials Mr. Bates and Admiral Kirby, covering President Biden’s economic agenda, new student loan relief, Middle East security operations, and domestic policy issues. Bates opens by framing the President’s trip to small businesses in Allentown as evidence of a Bidenomics-driven recovery, citing record small business applications, manufacturing job gains in Pennsylvania, and broader improvements in employment and wages, and he announces imminent implementation of a key SAVE plan provision that will cancel remaining student debt for certain low-balance borrowers. Admiral Kirby then fields extensive questions on the U.S. and partner airstrikes against Houthi targets in Yemen, stressing that the administration does not seek a war, is confident in the President’s legal authority, is still assessing battle damage, and is prepared to act again to protect Red Sea shipping after a major Houthi escalation on Tuesday, while also explaining Iran’s role, existing sanctions, allied support, and Secretary Austin’s full involvement from the hospital. Kirby also briefly touches on monitoring Taiwan’s upcoming elections and reports no immediate economic fallout from the Red Sea crisis, before Bates returns to address rising oil prices by blaming Houthi disruptions, to respond to questions on DOJ’s decision to seek the death penalty in the Buffalo shooting case by separating Biden’s general opposition to capital punishment from case-specific decisions, to urge House Republicans to honor a bipartisan funding framework to avoid a shutdown even as agencies prepare contingencies, to reassure progressives that the Middle East strikes are limited acts of self-defense rather than steps toward a wider war, and to contrast what he presents as the economic successes of Bidenomics with Trump’s proposal to make his tax cuts permanent, which the administration opposes as favoring wealthy interests at the expense of the middle class.

Bates cites record-high small business applications, job growth, and a manufacturing rebound as evidence of this turnaround. The current state is that the administration is using the visit to highlight local and national economic progress attributed to its policies.

- Mr. Bates notes that the administration sees these business formations as 16 million acts of hope and confidence in the economy since President Biden took office.
- Mr. Bates claims Allentown and similar communities are seeing a small business boom, more good-paying jobs with rising wages, record-low unemployment, and lower costs for families.
- Mr. Bates says Bidenomics has helped bring manufacturing back, citing 26,000 new manufacturing jobs created in Pennsylvania under President Biden compared to 23,000 manufacturing jobs lost under President Trump.

The administration emphasizes the benefit to community college and low-income borrowers and encourages eligible borrowers to enroll. The current state is that the review and processing for early relief under SAVE are underway, with millions already enrolled and many paying $0 monthly.

- Mr. Bates says this action will particularly help community college borrowers, low-income borrowers, and those struggling to repay their loans, as part of efforts to give borrowers breathing room.
- Mr. Bates encourages potentially eligible borrowers to sign up for the SAVE plan at StudentAid.gov.

Kirby explains the decision timeline and rationale for the strikes against Houthi assets, linking them to a significant escalation in attacks on shipping. He describes a large-scale Houthi attack on Tuesday involving nearly 20 drones and missiles directed at multiple naval and commercial vessels and notes that this followed a final warning from the U.S. and partners. The current state is that the administration presents the strikes as a response to repeated Houthi attacks culminating in the large Tuesday incident, rather than a delayed or overdue action.
- Admiral Kirby recounts that after Tuesday’s large Houthi attack involving about 18 drones, cruise missiles, and a ballistic missile, the President was kept updated as U.S. defenses successfully responded.
- Admiral Kirby says that once the Tuesday attack was confirmed to have been accurately and effectively defended, the President convened his national security team that afternoon, was presented with response options, and approved those options at that time.
- Admiral Kirby notes that since Houthi attacks began in late November, the administration has pursued diplomatic efforts, including working through the U.N., coordinating with coalition partners, and establishing Operation Prosperity Guardian to defend shipping and boost military presence in the Red Sea.
