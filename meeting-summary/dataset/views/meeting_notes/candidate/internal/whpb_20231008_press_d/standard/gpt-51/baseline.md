### Overview
A senior U.S. administration official briefs reporters on the American response to Hamas’s unprecedented assault on Israel, describing a rapid sequence of overnight and early-morning engagements led by President Biden, National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan, and senior national security principals with Israeli, Jordanian, Egyptian, Lebanese, Palestinian, and broader regional counterparts. The official underscores that the United States unequivocally condemns Hamas, views it as an international terrorist organization, and firmly supports Israel’s right to defend itself, while coordinating closely on Israel’s immediate military and intelligence needs and exploring future replenishment of key systems such as interceptors, subject in part to congressional action amid House leadership uncertainty. They detail efforts to prevent regional escalation, particularly involving Hezbollah and Lebanon, and to keep the crisis contained to Gaza even as Israel considers its response and grapples with hostage-taking of families and children. The official defends the administration’s broader diplomacy with Palestinians and its pursuit of regional normalization, insists there was no specific U.S. advance warning of the attack from partners such as Egypt, and stresses that Iran’s long-term support for Hamas will continue to draw sanctions even as it remains too early to assess Tehran’s direct operational role. Throughout, the official rebuts domestic claims linking a recent U.S.-Iran prisoner swap to the attack as false and irresponsible, highlights intensive engagement with key regional players including Jordan and Egypt, and reiterates hopes for a bipartisan U.S. stance that clearly signals America stands with Israel during the crisis.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Initial U.S. situational response
  The senior administration official outlines the U.S. government’s immediate response once rocket attacks in Israel begin overnight, emphasizing rapid, high-level engagement. Through the early morning, National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan, President Biden, and top national security principals convene repeatedly to assess developments and coordinate outreach. President Biden speaks directly with Prime Minister Netanyahu and later King Abdullah of Jordan, while Secretaries Austin and Blinken engage Israeli and regional counterparts. This establishes that the U.S. treats the Hamas assault as an acute crisis requiring continuous, real-time coordination and leader-level diplomacy.
- The Senior Administration Official states that around 2:30 a.m. Washington was alerted to rocket attacks in Israel and immediately engaged Israeli and regional counterparts to understand events.
- The Senior Administration Official explains that at 7:00 a.m. Jake Sullivan spoke with his Israeli counterpart and then convened a 7:30 a.m. national security team call including Secretary Blinken and others.
- The Senior Administration Official notes that around 8:15 a.m. President Biden held a call with his national security team, including Sullivan, Blinken, Austin, and Burns, and directed full engagement with Israeli and regional counterparts.
- The Senior Administration Official reports that immediately after the principals’ call, President Biden phoned Prime Minister Netanyahu, who described the day as unprecedented and declared that Israel is at war.
- The Senior Administration Official says President Biden expressed full support for Israel, highlighted close military and intelligence coordination, and agreed with Netanyahu to remain in regular contact at leader and working levels.
- The Senior Administration Official adds that Secretary Austin spoke with Defense Minister Gallant to reaffirm U.S. support for Israel’s right to defend itself and that a readout of the call was issued.
- The Senior Administration Official notes that President Biden also called King Abdullah of Jordan and agreed to keep in close touch through national security channels.
- The Senior Administration Official highlights that Secretary Blinken, from the White House, spoke with Israel’s President Herzog, Palestinian Authority President Abbas, Israel’s foreign minister, and counterparts from Turkey, Egypt, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Italy, and others.
- The Senior Administration Official emphasizes that Jake Sullivan, Brett McGurk, Jon Finer, and senior State Department officials including Victoria Nuland and Barbara Leaf maintain continuous contact with regional, G7, European, and Lebanese counterparts.
- The Senior Administration Official underscores that U.S. engagement is ongoing and continuous because the situation remains fluid and serious.
#### U.S. support stance toward Israel
  The official reiterates that the U.S. position is unequivocal support for Israel in the face of Hamas’s attacks. The administration condemns the assault as an appalling terrorist operation and affirms Israel’s right to defend itself. The U.S. signals readiness to provide all appropriate means of support in coordination with Israeli leaders and institutions. While avoiding speculation about future military developments, the administration frames its actions around ensuring Israel has what it needs as the crisis unfolds.
- The Senior Administration Official states that, as President Biden said, the United States stands with Israel and unequivocally condemns the assault by Hamas terrorists from Gaza.
- The Senior Administration Official stresses that the U.S. has made clear to Prime Minister Netanyahu and Israeli officials across the political and national security spectrum that Washington stands ready to offer all appropriate means of support.
- The Senior Administration Official affirms that Israel has a right to defend itself and its people, describing this position as a full stop.
- The Senior Administration Official declines to speculate on how events will evolve in the coming days and weeks but notes that U.S. engagement and support are rooted in the President’s early and ongoing involvement.
#### Military aid coordination and Israel’s needs
  In response to questions about specific military aid, the official confirms intensive military-to-military coordination but withholds operational details. U.S. Central Command’s General Kurilla and Israeli Chief of Staff General Halevi, along with broader defense chains, are in regular contact to identify Israel’s anticipated needs. The administration recalls prior U.S. support such as the 2021 billion-dollar supplemental for interceptor replenishment and commitments in the Jerusalem Declaration. The official indicates that more information on current support measures could come soon but will depend on evolving assessments and, potentially, congressional action.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Yuna that U.S. and Israeli military leadership, including General Kurilla and General Halevi, are in regular coordination on Israel’s anticipated needs.
- The Senior Administration Official declines to detail specific aid requests or numbers but notes that support discussions are very much underway and that more may be shared as early as the next day.
- The Senior Administration Official reminds reporters that the Biden administration previously secured a billion-dollar allocation for interceptor replenishment after the 2021 Gaza conflict.
- The Senior Administration Official cites the Jerusalem Declaration signed by President Biden and Prime Minister Lapid as reaffirming U.S. commitment to ensuring Israel has what it needs to protect its citizens and defend itself.
- The Senior Administration Official reiterates to Barak that while specific Iron Dome or interceptor funding details were not discussed on the Biden–Netanyahu call, the U.S. has historically supported system replenishment and remains committed in principle.
#### Intelligence sharing, warning questions, and normalization talks
  The official addresses questions about whether the U.S. had advance warning of the attack and how the incident affects regional diplomacy, including potential Saudi-Israel normalization. The official refuses to discuss specifics of any intelligence or intelligence sharing but underscores that the U.S. maintains deep, timely intelligence cooperation with Israel and will intensify that support. On normalization, the official says the process still has a way to go and calls it premature to assess impacts, while insisting that Hamas and similar groups will not derail such efforts if they become viable. The official emphasizes that the administration has consistently prioritized Israel’s security requirements, including through major funding and political commitments, independent of this attack.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Steve that they will not comment on specifics of intelligence or intelligence sharing related to the attack.
- The Senior Administration Official affirms that the U.S. will continue close, deep intelligence sharing with Israel and consistently provides timely intelligence about regional threats.
- The Senior Administration Official notes it is premature to assess how the attack will affect normalization talks between Saudi Arabia and Israel but asserts that Hamas and similar terrorist groups will not derail such an outcome if it becomes possible.
- The Senior Administration Official reiterates that the administration has focused on Israel’s military needs from day one, referencing the 2021 billion-dollar interceptor replenishment and commitments in the Jerusalem Declaration.
#### Regional escalation risks and Hezbollah/Lebanon
  The official discusses efforts to prevent the conflict from expanding beyond Gaza, particularly with respect to Lebanon and Hezbollah. The U.S. has been in contact with Lebanese counterparts and other regional actors to urge restraint and warn hostile parties against exploiting the situation. While acknowledging Hezbollah’s threats regarding a possible ground operation in Gaza, the official refuses to speculate on Hezbollah’s actions and emphasizes that Washington’s position and deterrent messaging are clear. Containment of the conflict and avoiding a northern front are identified as major diplomatic priorities.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Nadia that President Biden has made clear that any hostile party seeking to exploit the situation, including actors in Lebanon, is strongly warned against doing so.
- The Senior Administration Official notes that U.S. discussions with Lebanese and other regional interlocutors focus on containing the conflict in Gaza and preventing it from spreading elsewhere.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Hiba that the U.S. has conveyed clear messages to all relevant parties, including those who might seek to take advantage of the crisis, that doing so would be a huge mistake.
- The Senior Administration Official declines to speculate on what Hassan Nasrallah or Lebanese Hezbollah might do but stresses that U.S. engagement across the region is designed to make Washington’s stance unmistakable.
#### Protection of Gaza civilians and hostage concerns
  Responding to questions about Prime Minister Netanyahu’s reported call for Gazans to leave and the humanitarian situation, the official says they have not seen the specific statement and declines to parse it. The official notes that Israel is still determining its response and that the existence of multiple hostages, including families and children believed to be held both in Israel and Gaza, factors heavily into Israeli deliberations. The U.S. indicates it will work with Israel, Egypt, and others on contingency scenarios as the conflict evolves but avoids providing operational detail on potential civilian evacuation or safe passage options. The underlying message balances recognition of the humanitarian stakes with a focus on Israel’s security response to a terrorist attack.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Nadia they have not yet seen Netanyahu’s specific statement urging Gazans to leave and therefore will not parse it.
- The Senior Administration Official explains that Israel is in intensive consultations about how to respond, taking into account that a number of hostages, including families and children, have been taken from their homes and streets and are held in Israeli towns and, in some cases, in Gaza.
- The Senior Administration Official describes the hostage-taking as one of the horrific aspects of the day and says this reality is being deeply considered by Israeli decision-makers.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Asma that the U.S. will work with Israelis, Egyptians, and others on contingency scenarios concerning what may unfold in coming days and weeks, but declines to speculate on specific arrangements for Gazan civilians.
#### U.S. engagement with Palestinians and conflict diplomacy
  The official defends the administration’s broader diplomatic track on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict when questioned about possible failures. They argue that the administration inherited severed ties with the Palestinians and has since restored engagement, including frequent contacts and two rare face-to-face summits in Aqaba and Sharm. Working with Israel, the Palestinian Authority, Egypt, Qatar, and others, the U.S. has sought to establish security ‘rules of the road’ in the West Bank and Gaza. The current Hamas assault is characterized as an unprovoked terrorist act by an actor excluded from the diplomatic process, which, in the administration’s view, does not invalidate but rather underscores the importance of ongoing political efforts with legitimate Palestinian representatives.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Nadia that, despite the absence of a final resolution, the U.S. has been in deep and regular discussions with both Palestinians and Israelis.
- The Senior Administration Official highlights that the U.S. brought Israelis and Palestinians together for face-to-face talks in Aqaba and Sharm for the first time in almost a decade.
- The Senior Administration Official explains that U.S. efforts focus on security and other issues, particularly in the West Bank and Gaza, in coordination with Egypt, Qatar, and others.
- The Senior Administration Official emphasizes that the Hamas attack is an unprovoked assault by a terrorist group and that Hamas has never been part of the diplomatic process the administration is pursuing.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Ben that the administration entered office with U.S.-Palestinian ties largely severed and has worked to restore engagement while seeking to contain violence, especially preventing the current crisis from spreading from Gaza to the West Bank.
#### Iran’s role, sanctions, and nuclear/hostage deal politics
  The official addresses Iran on two fronts: its relationship with Hamas and political criticism over a recent U.S.-Iran prisoner swap involving limited Iranian funds. On operational culpability, the official says it is too early to determine whether Iran directly supported or planned the specific attack, while acknowledging that Iran has long funded, armed, and equipped Hamas and Hezbollah. The administration stresses it has repeatedly sanctioned Iran for this support and will continue to do so while treating Hamas as an international terrorist organization. Responding sharply to domestic critics, the official insists there is no link between the prisoner deal and the attack, stating that none of the associated funds has been spent and that they can only be used by vetted third parties for humanitarian goods, never accessed by Iran itself.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Felicia it is too early to say whether the state of Iran was directly involved in planning or supporting the current assault, and commits to examining the issue closely.
- The Senior Administration Official states unequivocally that Hamas is funded, equipped, and armed by Iran and that the administration has imposed sanctions on Iran for support to Hamas and other terrorist organizations, with such pressure to continue.
- The Senior Administration Official characterizes Hamas as an international terrorist organization responsible for an unprecedented assault involving incursions into Israeli cities, murder of families, and kidnapping of children.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Hiba that while there is no indication Iran was involved in this specific operation, Hamas would not exist in its current form without long-term Iranian support, reinforcing the rationale for continued U.S. efforts to hold Iran accountable.
- The Senior Administration Official responds to Asma that claims linking the Iran prisoner swap to the Hamas attack are completely false and irresponsible, noting that none of the funds in question has been spent or accessed by Iran and that, by design, they can only be used via vetted third-party vendors for food, agricultural products, medicine, and medical supplies.
#### Congressional role, Senate confirmations, and aid constraints
  The official acknowledges that congressional dynamics, including the absence of a Speaker of the House, may complicate certain aspects of security assistance such as supplemental support for Iron Dome or other systems. The administration is still working through how to address these constraints but points out that the Senate also plays a significant role, including in confirming key personnel. The official highlights that Jack Lew, the nominee for U.S. ambassador to Israel, remains unconfirmed, even as having an ambassador in place would be valuable during the crisis. At the same time, the chargé d’affaires in Israel is praised for performing strongly under pressure, underscoring continuity of representation despite vacancies.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Felicia that there is likely a role for Congress in providing security assistance, and the current lack of a House Speaker creates a unique situation that the administration is working through.
- The Senior Administration Official notes that the Senate has an important role to play as well, including confirming Jack Lew as ambassador to Israel, which the administration views as critical during this crisis.
- The Senior Administration Official praises chargé d’affaires Stephanie Hallett for doing a superb job managing the U.S. mission in Israel during the crisis and in prior months.
#### Bipartisan response and domestic political criticism
  When asked about the prospects for a bipartisan U.S. response, the official refrains from partisan commentary but expresses hope for unified support for Israel in the face of a terrorist assault. The official then directly addresses Republican claims linking the Iran prisoner swap to the Hamas attack, labeling them factually wrong and irresponsible. They underscore Treasury Department clarifications and explain the technical controls on the humanitarian funds to rebut the criticism. The remarks frame disinformation as an unfortunate distraction at a time when the administration is focused on crisis management and support for Israel.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Asma they will not delve into the political dimension but hopes for a unified ‘U.S. stands with Israel’ response when Israel is under terrorist attack.
- The Senior Administration Official says those working the crisis in the White House were largely insulated from partisan commentary but finds claims tying the hostage deal to the attack to be highly irresponsible.
- The Senior Administration Official notes that the Treasury Department clarified that the September agreement to free five U.S. citizens from Iran is unrelated to the current events and that none of the associated funds has been used.
- The Senior Administration Official stresses that Iran will never directly access those funds, which are restricted to humanitarian purchases via vetted, non-Iranian vendors, and calls contrary claims ridiculous and disinformation.
#### Potential Israeli ground operation in Gaza
  The official is asked whether the U.S. supports a possible Israeli ground operation in Gaza. They decline to comment on what Israel might or should do operationally, citing the limits of their remit on the call. Instead, the official reiterates the general U.S. commitment to Israel’s right to self-defense and to ensuring that Israel has what it needs, including replenishment of key defense systems when appropriate. This response reflects a deliberate U.S. choice to avoid public prescription of Israeli military tactics while emphasizing strategic support and close consultation.
- The Senior Administration Official tells Barak that the President’s call with Prime Minister Netanyahu did not go into specific details such as emergency funding for Iron Dome interceptors, contrary to some Israeli reports.
- The Senior Administration Official reiterates that the U.S. has a track record, including the 2021 billion-dollar supplemental, of helping Israel replenish systems when needed and expects to be committed similarly now.
- The Senior Administration Official explicitly declines to comment on whether the U.S. supports or opposes an Israeli ground operation in Gaza, stating that this is not their remit for the call.

  In closing, the official addresses reports that Egypt warned multiple parties of a potential Hamas attack and questions about U.S.-Egypt engagement. They state that the U.S. has been in intensive contact with Cairo, particularly with intelligence chief Abbas Kamel, and expects a leader-level conversation with President Sisi at some point. On claims that Egypt provided specific warning, the official says they have seen no evidence that the U.S. received such actionable intelligence and distinguishes between general concerns about rising tensions and the sophisticated nature of the current operation. The administration avoids characterizing Israeli or Egyptian intelligence assessments while asserting that allegations of specific ignored warnings to the U.S. are inaccurate.
