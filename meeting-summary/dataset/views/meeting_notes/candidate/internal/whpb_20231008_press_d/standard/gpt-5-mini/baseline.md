### Overview
  Senior U.S. officials provide a sequential account of the United States' rapid, multi-level response to Hamas’s unprecedented attack on Israel, describing early alerts around 2:30 a.m., national security convenings led by Jake Sullivan, and a presidential national security call followed by immediate leader-to-leader outreach including President Biden’s calls with Prime Minister Netanyahu and King Abdullah of Jordan. The administration reiterates unequivocal support for Israel, affirms Israel’s right to defend itself, and describes ongoing military and intelligence coordination (including General Kurilla’s engagement with Israeli counterparts) while declining to disclose operational intelligence. Senior officials report intensive diplomatic engagement across the region through Secretary Blinken, other State Department principals, and bilateral contacts with Egypt, Qatar, Turkey, Saudi Arabia, and Palestinian leaders to contain escalation, address humanitarian concerns, and plan contingencies. On specific issues, the administration says military aid discussions are underway and may be addressed publicly soon, notes procedural challenges tied to Congress’s role given the lack of a House Speaker, rejects claims that a recent prisoner swap with Iran contributed to the attack, and says it is too early to determine any direct Iranian operational role while underscoring Iran’s long-term support for Hamas. The U.S. warns all parties against exploiting the situation, is coordinating on contingency planning for civilian protection and hostage issues, and continues to gather facts and maintain high-level contact with partners as the crisis evolves.
