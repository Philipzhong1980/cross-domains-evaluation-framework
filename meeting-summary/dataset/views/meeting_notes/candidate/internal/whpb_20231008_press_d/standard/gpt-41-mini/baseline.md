### Overview
In this briefing, a senior U.S. administration official provided a comprehensive update on the U.S. government's response to the unprecedented Hamas terrorist attack on Israel. The official outlined continuous high-level engagement involving President Biden, Secretary of State Blinken, Secretary of Defense Austin, and national security teams coordinating closely with Israeli and regional counterparts. The U.S. reaffirmed its unequivocal support for Israel's right to self-defense and is actively coordinating military aid and intelligence sharing, though specific details remain confidential. Diplomatic efforts continue with Israeli and Palestinian leaders despite the crisis, emphasizing that Hamas is excluded from peace negotiations. The official addressed concerns about regional escalation, particularly regarding Lebanon and Hezbollah, and confirmed ongoing engagement with Egypt and other partners. Questions about Iran's role were met with caution, acknowledging Iran's longstanding support for Hamas but no confirmed direct involvement in the current attack. The administration also confronted domestic political misinformation, underscoring the need for bipartisan support and rejecting false claims linking the attack to recent prisoner swaps with Iran. Humanitarian issues, including the safety of civilians in Gaza and hostage situations, were acknowledged as complex and under active consideration. Overall, the briefing conveyed a coordinated, multifaceted U.S. response aimed at supporting Israel, managing regional stability, and addressing the broader Israeli-Palestinian conflict amid a rapidly evolving crisis.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Initial Response to Hamas Attack
The senior administration official detailed the U.S. government's immediate response to the Hamas terrorist attack on Israel, highlighting continuous engagement with Israeli and regional counterparts since the early hours. The President and his national security team held multiple calls to assess the situation and coordinate support, emphasizing the unprecedented nature of the attack and Israel's right to defend itself.
- The senior administration official stated the U.S. was alerted to rocket attacks around 2:30 a.m. and engaged throughout the night with Israeli and regional partners.
- President Biden convened a national security call at 8:15 a.m. and immediately called Prime Minister Netanyahu, who declared Israel is at war.
- Secretary Austin spoke with Israel's Defense Minister Gallant, affirming full support for Israel's military response.
- Secretary Blinken engaged with leaders from Israel, Palestine Authority, Turkey, Egypt, Jordan, Saudi Arabia, Italy, and others throughout the day.
#### U.S. Support and Military Aid Coordination
Discussions covered ongoing coordination regarding Israel's military needs, including potential aid and replenishment of defense systems like the Iron Dome and Patriot interceptors. While specifics were not disclosed, the official confirmed deep military-to-military engagement and a commitment to provide all necessary support.
- The senior administration official confirmed ongoing coordination between U.S. and Israeli military officials, including Generals Kurilla and Halevi.
- The official referenced a previous billion-dollar allocation for Patriot interceptor replenishment following the 2021 Gaza conflict.
- The U.S. remains committed to fulfilling its Memorandum of Understanding with Israel to ensure Israel's defense capabilities.
- No specific details on emergency funding or aid requests were disclosed, but more information may be forthcoming.
#### Intelligence and Regional Security Concerns
The official addressed questions about intelligence warnings and regional security, emphasizing close intelligence sharing with Israel but refraining from commenting on specific intelligence failures. The U.S. is actively engaged with regional partners to prevent escalation, particularly concerning Lebanon and Hezbollah.
- The official declined to comment on specific intelligence or intelligence sharing details.
- The U.S. maintains a close partnership with Israel, providing timely intelligence on regional threats.
- The President warned any hostile parties, including Hezbollah, against exploiting the situation.
- Engagement with Lebanese and other regional leaders aims to contain the conflict and prevent its spread beyond Gaza.
#### Diplomatic Engagement and Peace Process
The official highlighted ongoing diplomatic efforts with Israeli and Palestinian leaders, including recent face-to-face talks, despite the current crisis. The U.S. continues to focus on the broader Israeli-Palestinian conflict while condemning Hamas's unprovoked assault and emphasizing that Hamas is not part of peace negotiations.
- Secretary Blinken spoke with Palestinian Authority President Abbas and other Palestinian officials.
- The U.S. facilitated face-to-face talks between Israelis and Palestinians in Aqaba and Sharm for the first time in nearly a decade.
- The official stressed that Hamas, as the controlling authority in Gaza, is excluded from peace processes.
- The U.S. remains committed to addressing fundamental issues despite the current violence.
#### Humanitarian Concerns and Civilians in Gaza
Questions about the safety of civilians in Gaza and Israeli calls for Gazans to leave certain areas were raised. The official acknowledged the complexity and horrific nature of the situation, noting hostage-taking by Hamas and ongoing Israeli consultations on response measures, but refrained from commenting on specific Israeli statements or plans.
- The official had not seen Prime Minister Netanyahu's statement urging Gazans to leave and did not speculate on its implications.
- Hostages taken by Hamas, including families and children, are a significant concern influencing Israeli response.
- The U.S. is working with regional partners, including Egypt and Qatar, on contingency planning for the unfolding situation.
- No specific plans for civilian evacuation or protection were detailed.
#### Iran's Role and Terrorist Support
The official addressed Iran's involvement, stating it is too early to determine direct Iranian involvement in the current attack but reaffirmed Iran's longstanding support for Hamas and other terrorist groups. The U.S. continues to hold Iran accountable through sanctions and other measures.
- The official said there is no current evidence of direct Iranian involvement in the attack.
- Iran funds, equips, and arms Hamas, which the U.S. designates as a terrorist organization.
- The administration has imposed sanctions on Iran for its support of terrorist groups.
- The official emphasized the unprecedented nature of the Hamas assault, including incursions and hostage-taking.
#### U.S. Domestic Political Context and Bipartisan Support
The official responded to questions about the political response in the U.S., rejecting claims that recent prisoner swaps with Iran contributed to the attack. They expressed hope for bipartisan unity in supporting Israel and condemned misinformation and disinformation surrounding the issue.
- The official called accusations linking the attack to prisoner swaps with Iran irresponsible and false.
- Treasury clarified that funds from the prisoner swap are restricted to humanitarian uses and have not been accessed by Iran.
- The official emphasized the need for a united U.S. response to Israel under attack.
- Disinformation about the situation is being actively countered by the administration.
#### Engagement with Egypt and Regional Intelligence Sharing
The official confirmed intensive engagement with Egyptian officials, including multiple calls with Abbas Kamel, Egypt's key point of contact. While declining to confirm specific intelligence sharing claims, the official noted general regional concerns and ongoing efforts to understand the attack's sophistication.
- Jake Sullivan spoke multiple times with Abbas Kamel, Egypt's key interlocutor.
- The U.S. has not seen specific intelligence warnings from Egypt about the attack beyond general concerns.
- Discussions with Egypt and other regional partners continue to manage the crisis.
- A call between President Biden and Egyptian President Sisi is anticipated but has not yet occurred.
