### Overview
  A senior administration official briefs journalists on a coordinated U.S. response to a recent campaign of Houthi attacks against commercial shipping in the Red Sea, describing 23 attacks since November 19 that have used anti-ship ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, drones, and fast boats and characterizing the Houthi justification tied to Gaza as illegitimate. The official outlines a combined diplomatic and military approach: U.N. engagement, a December 19 44-country foreign ministry statement, formation of Operation Prosperity Guardian on December 18, and a 13-country warning issued at the President’s direction that the Houthis will bear consequences for further attacks. He recounts key incidents on December 30–31 in which U.S. naval forces shot down two anti-ship ballistic missiles and U.S. helicopters returned fire against attacking fast boats, sinking three vessels in self-defense with no U.S. casualties. The official attributes Houthi capabilities to Iranian enabling, describes ongoing interdiction efforts, and emphasizes that protecting the Bab el-Mandeb shipping route is distinct from the U.N.-brokered Yemen truce the U.S. seeks to preserve. He declines to provide rules-of-engagement details but says U.S. forces operate under self-defense authorities, that the U.S. has substantial regional assets in place (including the Eisenhower strike group and other naval and air forces), and that diplomacy — including imminent travel by Amos Hochstein and Secretary Blinken — will continue alongside coalition military measures to contain escalation and protect global commerce.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Houthi attack overview
  The senior administration official briefs that since November 19 the Iranian-backed Houthi rebels have launched 23 attacks against commercial shipping in the Red Sea using a mix of anti-ship ballistic missiles, land-attack cruise missiles, unmanned aerial vehicles, and fast boats. The official highlights that the use of anti-ship ballistic missiles is unprecedented and that most targeted vessels have no connection to Israel. This pattern of attacks creates a direct threat to global commerce and freedom of navigation.
- Senior administration official said Houthi rebels have attacked commercial vessels 23 times since November 19.
- Senior administration official said attackers have used anti-ship ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, unmanned aerial vehicles, and fast boats.
- Senior administration official said most targeted ships lack any connection to Israel, making the attacks indiscriminate threats to global shipping.
#### Diplomatic and military response
  The administration outlines a combined diplomatic and military response that includes U.N. engagement, a 44-country foreign ministry statement on December 19, and the December 18 formation of a defensive naval coalition called Operation Prosperity Guardian. Naval assets from multiple countries are now operating in coordination with U.S. naval forces in the Red Sea to protect shipping. The official frames these efforts as consistent with U.S. national defense doctrine to protect critical global trade routes.
- Senior administration official said the U.N. Security Council issued a statement on December 1 and that diplomatic action continues in New York.
- Senior administration official said 44 countries joined a December 19 foreign ministry statement condemning the attacks.
- Senior administration official said Operation Prosperity Guardian formed on December 18 and now deploys coalition naval assets in coordination with U.S. forces.
#### Dec 30–31 incidents
  The official recounts two major incidents: on December 30 the USS Gravely shot down two anti-ship ballistic missiles targeting the Singapore-flagged, Denmark-operated Maersk Hangzhou; on December 31 fast boats attacked the same ship in what appeared to be a hijacking or suicide-boat attempt and fired on U.S. helicopters. U.S. helicopters and other ships returned fire in self-defense, sinking three of four attacking boats with no U.S. casualties.
- Senior administration official said USS Gravely shot down two anti-ship ballistic missiles on December 30 targeting the Maersk Hangzhou.
- Senior administration official said on December 31 fast boats attacked the Maersk Hangzhou and fired on U.S. helicopters, which returned fire and sank three of four boats.
- Senior administration official said there were no U.S. personnel or equipment casualties and praised the professionalism of the naval forces involved.
#### 13‑country warning statement
  Acting at the President's direction, the administration helped convene 13 close allies and partners to issue a joint statement warning the Houthis that they will bear full consequences for further attacks against commercial vessels. The grouping includes major shipping nations from Europe, North America, Asia, and the Middle East and is intended to signal global resolve to protect freedom of navigation. The official presents the statement as building on earlier multilateral diplomatic work.
- Senior administration official said the President requested an allied statement to warn the Houthis they will bear full consequences for further attacks.
- Senior administration official said 13 countries issued the statement today, including the United States, UK, Japan, Denmark, Singapore, and others.
- Senior administration official said the statement builds on prior diplomatic and military efforts to protect international shipping lanes.
#### Rules of engagement and self‑defense
  When pressed, the senior official declines to detail rules of engagement or anticipatory operational plans but stresses that U.S. actions to date are defensive and grounded in authorities to protect ships and U.S. personnel. The official characterizes the December 30 and 31 responses as clear cases of self-defense and states that similar force would likely be used again under comparable circumstances. The administration intends to let the coalition statement stand as its public warning.
- Senior administration official said he will not discuss rules of engagement but emphasized that current actions are defensive.
- Senior administration official said U.S. forces acted under self-defense authorities in the December 30–31 incidents.
- Senior administration official said the administration would likely respond similarly if U.S. forces or protected ships face the same threats again.
#### Private warnings and coalition posture
  Reporters ask whether the Houthis received private diplomatic warnings; the official declines to discuss private communications but indicates extensive behind-the-scenes engagement is routine. He notes that elements of the defensive coalition are public while others are not, and that future measures may involve different groupings of partner countries. The overall posture emphasizes coordinated, flexible coalition responses rather than unilateral escalation.
- Senior administration official said he will not comment on private warnings or communications with the Houthis.
- Senior administration official said some coalition participants in Operation Prosperity Guardian are public while others are not.
- Senior administration official said future measures could involve different collections of partner countries acting in concert.
#### Iran’s role and arms flow
  The official attributes Houthi capabilities to Iranian enabling and support, saying Iran is arming the Houthis while also allowing that the Houthis have some domestic production capacity. The U.S. pursues interdictions and other mechanisms to disrupt arms flows but regards Iran's role as central to the campaign. This assessment underpins the administration’s framing of the problem as a broader regional security concern.
- Senior administration official said Iran is arming the Houthis and enabling their capabilities.
- Senior administration official said the United States employs interdictions and other mechanisms to try to stem the flow of weapons.
- Senior administration official said the Houthis also maintain some domestic capacity to produce weaponry.
#### Yemen truce and distinct issues
  The official stresses that the campaign to protect shipping is distinct from the Yemeni civil war and ongoing diplomatic efforts to sustain a U.N.-brokered truce that has largely held since March 2022. He says the U.S. does not want military responses to imperil the Yemen peace process and remains in close consultation with Saudi and U.N. partners. Protecting the Bab el-Mandeb shipping route is framed as a separate international-security imperative.
- Senior administration official said the Yemen civil war has largely been paused since the U.N.-brokered truce in March 2022.
- Senior administration official said protecting commercial shipping in the Bab el-Mandeb is distinct from the Yemen peace process.
- Senior administration official said the United States consults closely with the Saudis, the U.N., and other partners to avoid reigniting the civil war.
#### Regional escalation risks
  The discussion addresses related regional tensions: the targeted killing of senior Hamas figure al-Arouri, reports of a bombing in Tehran that the official says resembles ISIS methodology, and exchanges along the Israel-Lebanon border involving Hezbollah. The official says there is regular border fire but no clear current desire from Hezbollah for full-scale war, and the U.S. is pursuing diplomatic containment. U.S. officials are actively engaging regional partners to limit escalation.
- Senior administration official said the targeted killing of al-Arouri was precisely aimed at a senior Hamas figure and was an accountability action.
- Senior administration official said the Tehran bombing appears, as an initial assessment, to resemble past ISIS attacks.
- Senior administration official said Hezbollah and Israel exchange fire along the northern border but that he sees no clear desire for a full-scale Hezbollah-Israel war and the U.S. is working to contain tensions.
#### U.S. military posture and assets
  The official explains U.S. force posture in the region: the Gerald Ford carrier departed the Mediterranean after an extended deployment and its capabilities are being replaced and augmented by other assets, while the USS Eisenhower Carrier Strike Group remains in the region and participated in the December 31 response. He stresses that the U.S. maintains a significant forward military presence and is prepared for all contingencies while pursuing diplomatic measures. Task Force 153 and regional navies operate closely with U.S. forces.
- Senior administration official said the Gerald Ford returned after a year-long deployment and other assets replaced and augmented its capabilities.
- Senior administration official said the USS Eisenhower Carrier Strike Group remains in the region and took part in the December 31 response.
- Senior administration official said U.S. naval and air assets in the region are significant and ready to respond to contingencies.
#### Political context and global impact
  Facing criticism from some U.S. lawmakers about perceived weakness, the official argues the administration is acting forcefully and deliberately to avoid unnecessary escalation, citing recent targeted airstrikes in Iraq and the coalition statement as evidence. He emphasizes the global scope of the problem — over 40 countries have been affected and crews from multiple nations remain hostage on a pirated vessel — which informs the multinational response. The official frames actions as consistent with U.S. defense doctrine and responsibility to protect international commerce.
- Senior administration official said the administration is acting forcefully but seeks to avoid unnecessary escalation and cited recent strikes against Hezbollah in Iraq.
- Senior administration official said over 40 countries have been directly impacted by the Red Sea attacks and cited the Galaxy Leader piracy and the multinational composition of its crew.
- Senior administration official said the coalition and statement include global partners such as Japan, Singapore, Australia, New Zealand, Canada, and Gulf partners including Bahrain.
#### Diplomatic follow‑ups and next steps
  The official describes ongoing diplomatic work to contain the crisis: Amos Hochstein will travel to Israel and Secretary Blinken is departing for regional visits, while the administration continues consultations with Israeli and Lebanese counterparts. The official frames diplomacy as complementary to deterrence and military readiness and presents further coalition coordination and briefings to Congress as continuing follow-ups. The administration intends to let the public coalition statement serve as a clear warning to the Houthis.
- Senior administration official said Amos Hochstein will travel to Israel and Secretary Blinken will visit regional capitals to pursue diplomatic containment.
- Senior administration official said the U.S. remains in close consultations with Israeli and Lebanese officials to try to limit escalation.
- Senior administration official said briefings to Congress and ongoing multinational coordination will continue as follow-ups to current actions.
