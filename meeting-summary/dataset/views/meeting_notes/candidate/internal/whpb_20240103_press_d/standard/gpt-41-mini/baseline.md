### Overview
In this briefing, a senior U.S. administration official detailed the escalating threat posed by Iranian-backed Houthi rebels attacking commercial shipping in the Red Sea, with 23 attacks since November 19th using advanced weaponry including anti-ship ballistic missiles. The U.S. has responded by forming Operation Prosperity Guardian, a multinational naval coalition that has successfully defended vessels such as the Maersk Hangzhou from missile and fast-boat attacks, demonstrating effective military coordination. Alongside military measures, the U.S. and 13 allied nations issued a strong diplomatic statement warning the Houthis of full consequences for continued aggression, emphasizing the global impact of these attacks on over 40 countries. The official clarified that these actions are distinct from the Yemeni civil war, which remains under a U.N.-brokered truce, and highlighted Iran's role in arming the Houthis despite its denials. Addressing regional tensions, the briefing covered targeted actions against Hamas and Hezbollah figures and ongoing efforts to prevent escalation on Israel's northern border. The U.S. maintains a robust military presence in the Middle East, balancing force readiness with diplomatic initiatives to contain conflicts. Overall, the meeting underscored a comprehensive, coalition-based approach combining defense, diplomacy, and sanctions to protect vital international shipping lanes and uphold global security interests.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Houthi Attacks in Red Sea
The senior administration official detailed the recent and unprecedented attacks by Iranian-backed Houthi rebels on commercial shipping in the Red Sea, highlighting 23 attacks since November 19th using advanced weaponry including anti-ship ballistic missiles. These attacks are indiscriminate and unrelated to the Gaza conflict, posing a significant threat to global commerce and international shipping lanes.
- The senior administration official stated that Houthis have launched 23 attacks on commercial vessels since November 19th using anti-ship ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, UAVs, and fast boats.
- The official emphasized that the Houthis' justification linking attacks to Gaza is illegitimate and condemned by the U.N. Security Council.
- The attacks target ships with no connection to Israel, underscoring their indiscriminate nature.
#### Operation Prosperity Guardian
In response to the Houthi threat, a defensive naval coalition named Operation Prosperity Guardian was formed on December 18th, involving multiple countries coordinating naval assets to protect shipping in the Red Sea. The coalition's effectiveness was demonstrated during recent incidents where U.S. naval forces intercepted missile attacks and repelled fast-boat assaults.
- The senior administration official described Operation Prosperity Guardian as a coalition formed to defend shipping in the Red Sea, operational for about two weeks.
- On December 30th, USS Gravely shot down two anti-ship ballistic missiles targeting the Maersk Hangzhou, a Singapore-flagged, Denmark-operated ship.
- On December 31st, U.S. helicopters and naval forces repelled a fast-boat attack on the same ship, sinking three of four attacking boats after they fired on U.S. forces.
#### Diplomatic and Military Responses
The U.S. and 13 allied countries issued a strong statement warning the Houthis of full consequences for continued attacks, reflecting a coordinated diplomatic effort alongside military actions. The administration emphasized acting in self-defense and building coalitions to protect international shipping, while avoiding escalatory measures that could deepen regional conflicts.
- The senior administration official announced a joint statement from 13 countries including the U.S., UK, Japan, and others, warning Houthis of consequences for further attacks.
- The President convened his national security team to discuss options and endorsed the coalition statement as a clear warning.
- The official stressed that U.S. actions are defensive, aimed at protecting global commerce, and consistent with national defense strategy.
#### Rules of Engagement and Military Actions
Questions about rules of engagement and potential escalations were addressed with emphasis on self-defense authorities and measured responses. The U.S. forces acted decisively when attacked, including sinking Houthi boats after they fired on U.S. helicopters, but the administration refrains from discussing specific rules or anticipatory actions publicly.
- The senior administration official declined to detail rules of engagement but confirmed defensive actions taken in response to attacks.
- The sinking of Houthi boats on December 31st was a clear act of self-defense after the boats fired on U.S. helicopters.
- The official indicated similar responses would be taken if attacks continue but avoided discussing preemptive or escalatory measures.
#### Yemen Civil War and Regional Context
The official distinguished the Houthi attacks on shipping from the broader Yemeni civil war, noting the truce in place since March 2022 and ongoing diplomatic efforts to end the conflict. The Red Sea threat is treated as a separate global security issue, with continued cooperation with Gulf partners to prevent escalation of the civil war while addressing the shipping lane threat.
- The senior administration official clarified that the Yemeni civil war has been largely halted since the U.N.-brokered truce in March 2022.
- The Red Sea attacks are a distinct issue focused on protecting a major commercial shipping route, Bab el-Mandeb.
- The U.S. continues diplomatic efforts with the Saudis and U.N. envoy Hans Grundberg to support peace in Yemen.
#### Iran's Role and Arms Flow to Houthis
Iran is identified as the primary supplier of advanced weaponry to the Houthis, including anti-ship ballistic missiles, with the Houthis also producing some weapons domestically. The U.S. works to interdict arms shipments and counters Iran's enabling role, which is denied by Iran but considered clear by the administration.
- The senior administration official confirmed Iran arms the Houthis and enables their domestic weapons production.
- Efforts to interdict weapons shipments to the Houthis are ongoing.
- Iran denies involvement, but the U.S. views the evidence as clear and significant.
#### Regional Tensions and Hezbollah Activity
The official addressed recent targeted killings of Hamas and Hezbollah figures, emphasizing accountability for senior Hamas members and ongoing efforts to contain tensions on Israel's northern border. Diplomatic initiatives are underway to prevent escalation, with no current indication of a desire for full-scale war between Hezbollah and Israel.
- The killing of Hamas official al-Arouri was described as a precise targeted action with accountability for attacks on Americans.
- Tensions on the Israel-Lebanon border persist with regular exchanges of fire, but no clear intent for war from Hezbollah or Israel.
- Diplomatic efforts involve U.S. officials and regional partners to manage and de-escalate border tensions.
#### U.S. Military Presence and Regional Preparedness
The U.S. maintains a significant military presence in the Middle East, including carrier strike groups and Marine expeditionary units, to respond to contingencies. The withdrawal of USS Gerald Ford from the Mediterranean is offset by augmented capabilities, reflecting confidence in current force posture to manage regional threats and potential escalations.
- The senior administration official explained the USS Gerald Ford's withdrawal is balanced by additional naval assets and ongoing carrier strike groups in the region.
- The Eisenhower Carrier Strike Group remains active and was involved in recent defensive actions.
- The U.S. is prepared for all contingencies with a forward military presence and continues diplomatic efforts to contain conflicts.
#### Global Impact and Coalition Building
The administration highlighted the global nature of the Houthi threat, affecting over 40 countries through attacks on international shipping. The coalition statement and Operation Prosperity Guardian include countries from multiple continents, reflecting a broad international commitment to securing maritime commerce and freedom of navigation.
- The senior administration official noted that over 40 countries have been directly impacted by Houthi attacks in the Red Sea.
- The coalition includes countries from Europe, North America, Asia, and the Middle East, such as Australia, Bahrain, Japan, and Singapore.
- The global response underscores the importance of protecting international shipping lanes as a shared security priority.
