### Overview
The City Council convened a special meeting to interview seven applicants for the Environmental Planning Commission (EPC). After agreeing to modify the first interview question to better suit incumbents, each candidate presented their background and perspectives, highlighting diverse experiences from long-term residency to environmental expertise. Discussions centered on the city's General Plan and precise plans, with candidates expressing views on their adequacy and the need for updates reflecting recent societal changes. The balance between private property rights and community benefits was a key topic, emphasizing the importance of public input and shared spaces in a growing city. Following the interviews, the Council deliberated and initiated a voting process, showing strong support for reappointing incumbents Bill Cranston and Jose Gutierrez, while considering other qualified candidates for the remaining seat. The meeting concluded with plans for a second voting round and future formal appointments, underscoring the Council's commitment to inclusive and effective community representation.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Interview Process and Question Modifications
The City Council discussed the interview process for Environmental Planning Commission (EPC) applicants, including the format, timing, and questions. A key modification was agreed upon to tailor the first question for incumbents, asking about their personal contributions to the EPC, while non-incumbents would describe what unique qualities they would bring. This ensures fairness and relevance in the evaluation process.
- Mayor Ramirez explains the interview format and timing for EPC applicants.
- Council Member Matichak suggests modifying the first question for incumbents to focus on their contributions.
- City Clerk Glaser confirms policy requires asking all applicants the same questions but allows for add-ons.
- Mayor Ramirez captures the consensus to modify the first question accordingly.
#### Applicant Introductions and Perspectives
Seven applicants introduced themselves and shared their backgrounds, motivations, and unique perspectives they would bring to the EPC. Their experiences ranged from long-term residency, small business ownership, environmental science teaching, to community advocacy. This diversity highlights the varied expertise and community ties among candidates.
- Anne Cavanaugh emphasizes her long-term residency and dual perspective as a resident and small landlord.
- Bill Cranston highlights his experience as a small business owner and focus on housing affordability.
- Paul Donahue notes his extensive advisory experience and passion for good governance.
- Jose Gutierrez discusses his community involvement and efforts to promote affordable housing.
- Celia Paymer brings an environmental science background and active transportation insights.
- Molly Smith shares her recent move to the area and environmental studies degree.
- Lee Zhang mentions his long-term residency, scientific background, and sustainability focus.
#### Views on City's General and Precise Plans
Applicants shared their thoughts on the city's existing General Plan and precise plans, discussing their effectiveness, challenges, and the need for updates. While some advocated for revising the General Plan to reflect changing circumstances like COVID-19, others emphasized amending details rather than a full overhaul. The importance of aligning plans with sustainability goals and community needs was a common theme.
- Bill Cranston notes the General Plan's age and impact of economic changes, suggesting potential redevelopment areas.
- Jose Gutierrez supports revising the General Plan to strengthen the city's negotiating position with developers.
- Molly Smith emphasizes the need to review plans due to societal changes and COVID-19 impacts.
- Lee Zhang highlights the need to align zoning with the General Plan and precise plans.
- Celia Paymer appreciates the General Plan's themes but notes implementation challenges.
- Paul Donahue prefers amending rather than reopening the entire General Plan, focusing on complete neighborhoods.
- Anne Cavanaugh values the plans but acknowledges adaptation to changing conditions is necessary.
#### Balancing Private Property Rights and Community Benefits
Applicants discussed how to balance private property rights with community benefits, emphasizing the importance of public comment and community engagement. Many leaned towards prioritizing public benefits in dense urban settings while respecting private ownership rights. The role of public input in decision-making and finding common ground was highlighted as essential for effective governance.
- Paul Donahue stresses listening to public input and favors public open spaces accessible to all.
- Celia Paymer leans towards public benefit in denser areas, emphasizing shared outdoor spaces.
- Lee Zhang advocates for broader community outreach to gather diverse public comments.
- Anne Cavanaugh supports balancing both private and public interests and values public comment.
- Jose Gutierrez highlights the democratic process and the challenge of finding consensus.
- Molly Smith values public comment and stresses the importance of green spaces in new developments.
- Bill Cranston supports property owners working within rules and community input to update those rules as needed.
#### Council Deliberations and Voting Process
The City Council deliberated on the applicants, expressing appreciation for all candidates and discussing the voting process. Several members supported reappointing incumbents Bill Cranston and Jose Gutierrez, citing their contributions and diverse perspectives. Others also supported candidates like Celia Paymer and Paul Donahue. The council agreed to follow the prescribed voting method and planned a second round of voting due to no majority for the second seat in the first round.
- Council Member Kamei suggests Commission training to improve effectiveness.
- Vice Mayor Hicks moves to reappoint Bill Cranston and Celia Paymer.
- Mayor Ramirez emphasizes reappointing incumbents with good track records.
- Council members express appreciation for all applicants and note the challenge of selection.
- Council agrees to use the existing voting process and proceed with rounds of voting.
- First round results favor Bill Cranston and Jose Gutierrez, leading to a second round for the remaining seat.
