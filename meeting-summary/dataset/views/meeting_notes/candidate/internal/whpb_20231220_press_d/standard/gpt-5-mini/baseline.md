### Overview
  Senior administration officials announce a negotiated agreement that returns 10 Americans from Venezuela — including six the U.S. designates as wrongfully detained — and secures the extradition of Leonard Francis ('Fat Leonard') to face U.S. charges. The administration says President Biden granted clemency to Alex Saab as part of the arrangement, and Venezuelan authorities agreed to free 20 prisoners, release Roberto Abdul, and suspend three arrest warrants (24 Venezuelans in total), actions that officials say realign outcomes with the earlier Barbados agreement. U.S. negotiators led by National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan and Principal Deputy NSA Jon Finer, with facilitation from Qatar and coordination with regional partners, frame the deal as a fragile but constructive step toward competitive, inclusive Venezuelan elections in 2024; officials stress sanctions will be calibrated based on concrete political outcomes and confirm Maduro still faces U.S. charges. The administration withholds detainee names pending family notifications, emphasizes continued vigilance to prevent future detentions, and presents the package as the product of months of high-level diplomacy that prioritizes bringing Americans home while pursuing democratic progress in Venezuela.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Embargo and background rules
  The call opens under an embargo and is held on background, with remarks attributable to senior administration officials. Participants are warned that events remain fluid and that statements are speculative while actions on the ground continue. The moderators notify journalists that family notifications will precede public naming of detainees.
- Moderator states the call is under an embargo and on background and that officials on the call are senior administration officials.
- Moderator warns that events remain underway and that remarks are speculative and subject to change.
- Senior administration official says family notifications will occur before releasing detainee names to the press.
  #### Agreement to release Americans
  Senior administration officials announce an agreement to secure the release of 10 Americans, including six the U.S. designates as wrongfully detained in Venezuela. Officials emphasize that the Americans are expected to be returned to the United States soon and will receive healthcare and support upon arrival. They present the outcome as the product of months of negotiations and high-level engagement.
- Senior administration official announces an agreement to secure the release of six wrongfully detained Americans and four additional Americans, for a total of 10.
- Senior administration official says the Americans are expected to be on their way home soon and will receive health care and support upon return.
- Senior administration official characterizes the outcome as the result of many months of negotiations and regular consultations with the President.
  #### Extradition of 'Fat Leonard'
  Officials announce that Leonard Francis, known as 'Fat Leonard,' will be extradited from Venezuela to the United States to face accountability for a major bribery and fraud conspiracy involving U.S. Navy officials. They recount his admitted crimes, guilty plea, escape from house arrest, and arrest in Venezuela. The extradition is presented as part of the package that yields the Americans' return.
- Senior administration official states Leonard Francis ('Fat Leonard') will be extradited from Venezuela and returned to a U.S. federal detention facility.
- Senior administration official summarizes that Francis pled guilty to a long-running bribery conspiracy involving tens of millions in fraud and millions in bribes and gifts to Navy officials.
- Senior administration official notes Francis cut off his ankle monitor, fled to Venezuela while on house arrest, and was arrested there as he prepared to board a flight to Florida.
  #### Clemency for Alex Saab
  Officials disclose that President Biden granted clemency to Alex Saab as part of the exchange, describing the decision as extremely difficult but necessary to secure the Americans' return. They emphasize that clemency is an exercise of presidential authority and that the White House does not interfere with Department of Justice investigations. The administration frames the trade as one fugitive and a clemency decision exchanged for 10 Americans and Venezuelan releases.
- Senior administration official says the President granted clemency to Alex Saab to secure the release of 10 Americans and the return of a fugitive.
- Senior administration official clarifies that the White House does not interfere with Department of Justice investigations and that clemency is an exercise of the President's authority.
- Senior administration official describes the clemency decision as difficult but taken to reunite families and ensure accountability for returning the fugitive.
  #### Venezuelan prisoner releases
  Officials state that Venezuelan authorities will release 20 prisoners, Roberto Abdul of Súmate, and suspend three arrest warrants, totaling 24 Venezuelan detainees to be freed. This element is presented as part of the broader agreement and as aligning Venezuela back with the previously negotiated Barbados agreement. Officials emphasize these releases support the goal of returning Venezuela to a path toward competitive elections.
- Senior administration official reports Venezuela has agreed to release 20 prisoners and to release Roberto Abdul and suspend three arrest warrants.
- Senior administration official says the total number of Venezuelans involved in releases or warrant suspensions is 24.
- Senior administration official frames the Venezuelan releases as restoring alignment with the earlier Barbados agreement.
  #### Negotiation leadership and process
  Officials attribute the agreement to months of intensive negotiations led and overseen by senior U.S. leaders, including National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan and Principal Deputy NSA Jon Finer, with regular consultation with the President. They highlight sustained bilateral and multilateral engagement, internal deliberations, and facilitation by partners such as Qatar. Officials stress the process remains fragile and could face unexpected hurdles.
- Senior administration official states Jake Sullivan and Jon Finer oversaw and regularly engaged in negotiations and briefed the President throughout the process.
- Senior administration official says the outcome reflects months of internal deliberations and sustained U.S. leadership engagement.
- Senior administration official acknowledges the process remains extremely complex and fragile and may encounter unanticipated hurdles.
  #### Regional diplomacy and election goals
  Officials explain the agreement fits a broader U.S. strategy to encourage competitive, inclusive elections in Venezuela through dialogue with both Maduro representatives and the Unitary Platform opposition. They describe coordinated regional diplomacy — including engagement with Guyana and Brazil to reduce border tensions — and link progress on detainees to incentives for electoral processes. The administration frames this as building a foundation for democratic restoration ahead of the 2024 Venezuelan elections.
- Senior administration official says the deal advances U.S. efforts to encourage competitive and inclusive elections in Venezuela and to engage both government representatives and the Unitary Platform.
- Senior administration official reports Jake Sullivan engaged with regional counterparts, including President Irfaan Ali of Guyana and Brazil's national security advisor, to discourage military confrontation.
- Senior administration official says Jon Finer's convening in Bogotá helped lay groundwork for direct U.S.-Venezuela negotiations and expand international consensus for competitive elections.
  #### Qatar's facilitation role
  Officials acknowledge Qatar as an important facilitator that hosted and aided conversations between U.S. officials and Maduro representatives, helping to clear a path toward elections and detainee releases. They express appreciation for Qatari mediation while emphasizing U.S. leadership in negotiations. The role is characterized as supportive rather than decisive in isolation.
- Senior administration official says Qatar facilitated conversations between Maduro authorities and U.S. officials over several months.
- Senior administration official describes Qatar's role as providing good offices and expresses U.S. gratitude for that facilitation.
- Senior administration official emphasizes that U.S. officials, including Sullivan and Finer, led the negotiation process supported by partners.
  #### Sanctions, legal status, and policy calibration
  Officials state that sanctions relief has not been traded for the return of wrongfully detained Americans or for oil and gas concessions, and that U.S. sanctions will be calibrated based on concrete outcomes from negotiations. They confirm Nicolás Maduro still faces pending charges in the United States and that prior limited sanctions relief tied to the Barbados agreement remains in place. The administration says it will continue to consider sanctions adjustments in coordination with Venezuelan parties and the Unitary Platform as talks progress.
- Senior administration official asserts that sanctions relief was never offered in exchange for wrongfully detained Americans or for oil and gas concessions.
- Senior administration official says the U.S. will calibrate sanctions based on concrete outcomes between Venezuelan parties and will consider relief as talks continue.
- Senior administration official confirms that Nicolás Maduro still faces pending charges in the United States.
  #### Information control and family notifications
  Officials decline to name the six wrongfully detained Americans immediately, explaining that they will first notify families and then follow up with the press. This reflects a deliberate communication plan to ensure families learn the news from authorities before public reporting. The administration promises to provide names later in the morning after family notifications are complete.
- Senior administration official declines to provide the names of the six wrongfully detained Americans pending family notifications.
- Senior administration official says the administration will follow up later in the morning with detainee names after families are informed.
- Moderator notes the embargo lift time will be communicated to participants at a later time.
  #### Preventing future detentions
  Officials say they secured commitments from Venezuelan counterparts to avoid detaining additional Americans and warn U.S. citizens against travel to Venezuela. They acknowledge the need to monitor compliance and state the U.S. will take appropriate action if commitments are violated. The administration frames continued vigilance as necessary to avoid incentivizing future hostage-taking.
- Senior administration official says the U.S. secured commitments from Venezuela not to detain additional Americans.
- Senior administration official advises Americans to continue avoiding travel to Venezuela and says the U.S. will act if Venezuela violates its commitment.
- Senior administration official emphasizes the need to observe how the commitments play out in practice and to remain vigilant.
