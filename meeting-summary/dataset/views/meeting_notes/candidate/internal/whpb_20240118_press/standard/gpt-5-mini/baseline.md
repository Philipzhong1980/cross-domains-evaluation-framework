### Overview
  The briefing opens with MS. DALTON previewing a White House announcement of an $82 million American Rescue Plan investment to expand high-speed Internet in North Carolina and noting the state’s role in U.S. fiber-optic manufacturing and job creation. MR. KIRBY then provides a series of national security updates: U.S. strikes under Central Command have targeted Houthi missiles and anti-ship weapons in the Southern Red Sea, the administration is closely monitoring reported Pakistan‑to‑Iran strikes while stressing it seeks to avoid regional escalation, and it reiterates a longstanding policy of preventing Iran from acquiring nuclear weapons. Kirby defends the legal and strategic basis for recent defensive strikes, declines to preview future military options, and affirms continued U.S. engagement with Israel to encourage more discriminant operations and lower civilian harm; he also announces that NSA Jake Sullivan will meet with families of Americans held hostage by Hamas. MS. DALTON addresses domestic issues: she frames ongoing bipartisan border talks as productive while urging Congress to pass the President’s national security supplemental, underscores the urgent need for timely Ukraine aid following the President’s meeting with congressional leaders, notes isolated EV charging issues amid extreme weather, and commits to act on recommendations from the DOJ Uvalde report while pressing Congress for further gun‑safety legislation. Throughout, the administration emphasizes protecting U.S. forces and international shipping, supporting allies, working in good faith with Congress, and pursuing both immediate and longer‑term policy objectives.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### North Carolina broadband investment
  MS. DALTON previews the President’s announcement of $82 million from the American Rescue Plan Capital Project Fund to expand high-speed Internet in North Carolina, emphasizing closing the digital divide. She frames the investment as part of the broader Investing in America agenda and cites the impact on rural residents and households that gained discounted service. The announcement is presented as a policy and political win with immediate local relevance.
- MS. DALTON says the President will announce an $82 million investment to connect thousands more North Carolina homes and businesses to high-speed Internet.
- MS. DALTON notes the announcement advances the Biden-Harris goal to close the digital divide and ensure affordable, reliable Internet by 2030.
- MS. DALTON highlights that the American Rescue Plan has already helped more than 880,000 North Carolinians save on monthly Internet bills.
  #### Fiber-optic manufacturing jobs
  MS. DALTON highlights North Carolina’s role producing a large share of U.S. fiber-optic cable and connects federal investment to private-sector manufacturing growth. She cites companies and planned private investment that will expand local production and create hundreds of jobs, framing this as both economic development and supply-chain resilience. The administration positions the investment as supporting workers, families, and small businesses in the state.
- MS. DALTON states that North Carolina produces 40 percent of the fiber-optic cable used in the U.S. and that local manufacturing matters to the economy.
- MS. DALTON points to private investment by firms like CommScope and Corning as driving more than $550 billion in local production and creating 650-plus jobs in the state.
- MS. DALTON says the investments are a win for North Carolina workers, families, and small businesses.
  #### Strikes against Houthis
  MR. KIRBY reports recent U.S. strikes under Central Command to destroy Houthi missiles and anti-ship weapons prepared for imminent use in the Southern Red Sea. He says strikes occurred last night and again this morning to disrupt capabilities that threatened merchant and naval shipping. Central Command plans to release a formal statement and the administration frames the operations as defensive measures to protect international shipping and U.S. forces.
- MR. KIRBY reports that U.S. forces struck Houthi missiles prepared to fire into the Southern Red Sea last night and struck anti-ship missiles again this morning.
- MR. KIRBY tells reporters that Central Command will issue a formal statement about the strikes.
- MR. KIRBY frames the strikes as actions to protect U.S. ships, sailors, merchant ships, and international shipping in the region.
  #### Pakistan–Iran exchange monitoring
  MR. KIRBY says the administration is closely monitoring reports that Pakistan fired missiles into Iran and expresses concern about potential escalation in South and Central Asia. He declines to characterize Pakistan’s military operations and says the U.S. is in touch with Pakistani counterparts. The administration emphasizes preventing broader conflict and avoiding escalation between well-armed regional actors.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. is monitoring reports that Pakistan fired missiles into Iran and stresses concern about escalation in the region.
- MR. KIRBY says the administration is in touch with Pakistani counterparts but will let Pakistan speak to its military operations.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. did not receive prenotification of the reported Pakistani strikes, to his knowledge.
  #### U.S. position on Iran nuclear
  MR. KIRBY reiterates the administration’s longstanding policy that it does not want Iran to acquire nuclear weapons and prefers diplomacy but will maintain options to prevent that outcome. He frames a nuclear-armed Iran as a regional and global threat and says the President will ensure the Commander-in-Chief has the capabilities to act if necessary. The comments emphasize deterrence continuity amid regional tensions.
- MR. KIRBY states that the U.S. does not want to see Iran acquire nuclear weapons and prefers diplomatic means to prevent that outcome.
- MR. KIRBY says the President will maintain capabilities and options to prevent Iran from achieving a nuclear weapons capability.
- MR. KIRBY frames an Iran with nuclear weapons as dangerous for the region and globally.
  #### Houthi strike strategy rationale
  MR. KIRBY explains that U.S. strikes aim to disrupt and degrade Houthi capability so merchant and naval vessels can operate safely, and he warns strikes will continue as needed. He rejects the notion that continuing strikes means the strategy is ineffective, arguing each strike removes capability and raises the cost of continued attacks. The administration signals it has additional options and will act in self-defense if necessary.
- MR. KIRBY says strikes will continue for as long as they need to in order to disrupt and degrade the Houthis’ ability to conduct attacks.
- MR. KIRBY argues that each strike removes Houthi capability and makes it harder for them to continue attacks.
- MR. KIRBY warns that the Houthis have a choice to stop reckless attacks, otherwise the U.S. has additional options it will not hesitate to use.
  #### Possible broader military options
  Reporters press MR. KIRBY about potential escalation, including strikes on Iran, but he declines to preview or telegraph future military actions. He reiterates that the administration will not publicly detail options but will continue to act to disrupt threats and protect U.S. and international interests. The response underscores deliberate operational secrecy while signaling readiness to escalate if necessary.
- MR. KIRBY declines to discuss specific future military options or telegraph potential strikes on Iran.
- MR. KIRBY reiterates that the administration will continue disruptive actions to protect international shipping and U.S. interests.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes operational discretion while warning that the U.S. retains additional options.
  #### Taliban remarks and expectations
  When asked about the Taliban calling for peace between Iran and Pakistan, MR. KIRBY defers to the Taliban and to other nations to speak for themselves but stresses that Afghanistan’s de facto authorities must meet commitments on human rights and international law to be taken seriously. He uses the question to reiterate U.S. concerns about Taliban governance and treatment of women and girls. The administration links diplomatic credibility to concrete actions by the Taliban.
- MR. KIRBY says he will let the Taliban speak for themselves and notes other nations can weigh in.
- MR. KIRBY says the Taliban must meet commitments on treating women and girls appropriately and abiding by international law to be taken seriously on the world stage.
- MR. KIRBY reiterates U.S. skepticism about the Taliban’s fulfillment of governing commitments.
  #### Two‑state solution stance
  MR. KIRBY reaffirms President Biden’s support for a two-state solution and says the administration continues to work toward that outcome despite recent Israeli statements rejecting a Palestinian state. He emphasizes that the U.S. seeks a post-conflict Gaza with representative governance, no reoccupation, and Palestinian participation in determining the future. The comments also stress continued U.S. focus on ensuring Israel’s defense and humanitarian access to Gaza.
- MR. KIRBY reiterates that President Biden believes a two-state solution is the best outcome for Israelis, Palestinians, and the region.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. wants post-conflict Gaza governance that is representative and does not involve reoccupation.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes that the immediate focus includes ensuring Israel has what it needs to defend itself and that humanitarian aid reaches Gaza.
  #### Iraq U.S. military presence
  MR. KIRBY addresses reports that the Iraqi Prime Minister asked U.S.-led military leadership to leave, saying the U.S. is in Iraq at the invitation of Iraqi authorities and will consult with them on mission posture. He declines to disclose diplomatic conversations but stresses ongoing cooperation to counter ISIS in Iraq and Syria. The administration frames its presence as consultative and mission-focused.
- MR. KIRBY says U.S. forces remain in Iraq at the invitation of Iraqi authorities to assist Iraqi security forces against ISIS.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. will consult with the Iraqi government about the mission’s future posture and resources.
- MR. KIRBY declines to characterize private diplomatic conversations about the Iraqi request publicly.
  #### U.S.-Israel coordination and casualties
  MR. KIRBY defends U.S. engagement with Israel, saying U.S. counsel has shaped Israeli operational choices to reduce civilian harm and that Israel has been receptive to U.S. guidance. He acknowledges that civilian casualties remain unacceptable and says the U.S. has urged Israel toward more discriminant, lower-intensity operations, including recent troop reductions in the north. The administration stresses continued dialogue while respecting Israeli sovereignty over military decisions.
- MR. KIRBY argues that Israel has not ignored U.S. advice and that U.S. counsel influenced operational changes such as adding humanitarian corridors and reducing reliance on airstrikes.
- MR. KIRBY says one civilian casualty is one too many and that the U.S. has urged Israel to tailor operations to reduce civilian harm.
- MR. KIRBY notes Israel has announced removal of a division of troops, which he frames as a beginning of transitioning to lower-intensity operations.
  #### Legal basis for strikes
  MR. KIRBY explains that recent strikes are conducted under the President’s Article II authorities and are consistent with U.N. Charter Article 51 self-defense principles. He addresses a reporter’s question on congressional authorization by arguing current actions fall within executive authority. The administration thus positions its actions as lawful executive conduct of self-defense operations.
- MR. KIRBY says the strikes are conducted under the President’s Article II authorities as Commander-in-Chief and pursuant to U.N. Charter Article 51 self-defense.
- MR. KIRBY indicates the administration views these actions as legally authorized without new congressional authorization.
  #### Hostage families meeting
  MR. KIRBY announces that National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan will meet with families of American hostages held by Hamas to discuss ongoing efforts and reassure them the administration is working hard to secure returns. He cautions there is no immediate announcement expected and frames the meeting as part of sustained diplomatic work on a possible hostage deal. The administration signals active engagement and continuing negotiations.
- MR. KIRBY says Jake Sullivan will meet this afternoon with families of American hostages still held by Hamas.
- MR. KIRBY cautions that the meeting is not expected to produce an immediate announcement but will convey how hard the administration is working to secure hostages’ return.
- MR. KIRBY says serious discussions are ongoing about trying to arrange another hostage deal.
  #### Border negotiations and Trump influence
  MS. DALTON frames the President as open to bipartisan border negotiations while declining to comment on former President Trump’s influence due to Hatch Act constraints. She reiterates the administration’s view that productive discussions are underway and urges Congress to act on the national security supplemental to add personnel for border enforcement. The White House emphasizes readiness to work in good faith and calls on Republicans to engage constructively.
- MS. DALTON says the President remains open to working in good faith with Senate Democrats and Republicans on the border and believes conversations are productive.
- MS. DALTON states federal officials cannot comment on Trump as a candidate under the Hatch Act.
- MS. DALTON urges Congress to pass the President’s national security supplemental request to fund more border agents and law enforcement resources.
  #### Ukraine aid urgency
  MS. DALTON describes the President’s meeting with congressional leaders as emphasizing the urgent stakes for Ukraine and warns that obstructing aid heightens national security risks. She cites the President’s national security team (Jake Sullivan and Avril Haines) as having outlined specific consequences of failing to secure timely assistance and stresses the need to learn from history about unchecked aggression. The administration presses Congress for prompt action on supplemental funding.
- MS. DALTON says the President conveyed urgency about the stakes for Ukraine in his meeting with congressional leaders and that the briefing was robust.
- MS. DALTON says national security officials laid out specific examples of what failing to provide aid would mean for Ukraine and NATO partners.
- MS. DALTON warns that failing to check aggression can lead to broader consequences and that Congress must act to prevent those outcomes.
  #### Electric vehicle charging issues
  MS. DALTON responds to questions about EV battery charging problems in extreme weather by saying weather affects all vehicle types and that reported incidents appear isolated. She underscores continued administration efforts to make EVs affordable and reliable, noting EV sales growth since the President took office. The administration says it is investigating the reported charging incidents while supporting broader adoption of electric vehicles.
- MS. DALTON says extreme weather impacts the functioning of gas, hybrid, and electric vehicles and that recent charging problems appear isolated.
- MS. DALTON highlights that EV sales have quadrupled since the President took office and that the administration is committed to making EVs affordable and reliable.
- MS. DALTON says the administration is looking into reported EV charging incidents to see where it can help.
  #### Uvalde DOJ report response
  MS. DALTON addresses the Justice Department’s Uvalde report, expressing the President’s commitment to act on its recommendations and support affected families and communities. She praises the advocacy of Uvalde families that helped pass the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and says the White House will equip the Office of Gun Violence Prevention to better assist communities. The administration reiterates a call for congressional action on national red flag laws, universal background checks, and restrictions on assault weapons and high-capacity magazines.
- MS. DALTON says the President is committed to moving forward on lessons from the DOJ Uvalde report and will issue a detailed statement after the Attorney General’s press conference.
- MS. DALTON praises Uvalde families for catalyzing the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and says that law is saving lives.
- MS. DALTON calls for congressional action on a national red flag law, universal background checks, and limits on assault weapons and high-capacity magazines.
