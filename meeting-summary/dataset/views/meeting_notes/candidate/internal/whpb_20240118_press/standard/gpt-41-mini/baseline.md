### Overview
The meeting features key updates from MS. Dalton and Admiral Kirby on a range of domestic and international issues. MS. Dalton outlines the President's announcement of an $82 million investment to expand high-speed Internet access in North Carolina, emphasizing efforts to close the digital divide and support local job growth. Admiral Kirby provides detailed briefings on recent U.S. military strikes against Houthi missile threats in the Red Sea, ongoing tensions between Pakistan and Iran, and the administration's stance on the Israeli-Palestinian conflict, reaffirming support for a two-state solution despite differing views from Israeli leadership. The discussion also covers the U.S. military presence in Iraq, congressional negotiations on border security and Ukraine aid, and challenges related to electric vehicle performance in extreme weather. Additionally, MS. Dalton addresses the release of the Uvalde shooting report, highlighting the administration's commitment to gun violence prevention and calls for legislative action. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan's planned meeting with families of American hostages held by Hamas underscores ongoing diplomatic efforts. Throughout, the administration emphasizes collaboration, cautious diplomacy, and a focus on security and community support.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### American Rescue Plan Internet Investment
The President announces an $82 million investment from the American Rescue Plan’s Capital Project Fund to expand high-speed Internet access in North Carolina. This initiative aims to bridge the digital divide affecting millions of Americans, particularly in rural areas, by 2030. The investment also supports local fiber-optic cable production and job creation in the state.
- MS. Dalton highlights the $82 million investment to connect thousands of North Carolina homes and businesses to high-speed Internet.
- MS. Dalton notes the President's commitment to ending the digital divide by 2030, ensuring affordable, reliable Internet for all Americans.
- MS. Dalton emphasizes North Carolina's role in producing 40% of the nation's fiber-optic cable and the creation of over 650 jobs due to private sector investments.
#### Military Strikes Against Houthi Missiles
Admiral Kirby updates on recent U.S. military strikes targeting Houthi missile launch preparations in the Southern Red Sea. These actions aim to disrupt imminent threats to international shipping and regional security. The U.S. continues to monitor the situation closely and maintains readiness to act in self-defense.
- Admiral Kirby reports strikes against Houthi missiles prepared for firing into the Southern Red Sea.
- The strikes are conducted under the President's Article 2 authorities and U.N. Charter Article 51 for self-defense.
- Kirby states the U.S. will continue strikes as necessary to degrade Houthi capabilities and prevent attacks.
#### Pakistan-Iran Tensions and U.S. Monitoring
The U.S. is closely monitoring missile attacks between Pakistan and Iran, expressing concern over potential escalation in South and Central Asia. The administration maintains communication with Pakistani counterparts but refrains from detailed commentary on military operations. The U.S. opposes nuclear weapons development by Iran and supports diplomatic efforts to prevent it.
- Admiral Kirby acknowledges missile attacks between Pakistan and Iran and the risk of escalation.
- The U.S. is in contact with Pakistani officials and avoids operational commentary.
- Kirby reiterates U.S. policy against Iran acquiring nuclear weapons and preference for diplomatic solutions.
#### Hostage Family Meeting with National Security Advisor
National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan is scheduled to meet with families of American hostages held by Hamas. The meeting aims to maintain communication, reassure families of ongoing efforts, and discuss potential hostage negotiations. No public announcements are expected immediately following the meeting.
- Admiral Kirby confirms Jake Sullivan's meeting with families of American hostages held by Hamas.
- The meeting is intended to update families on efforts to secure hostages' release.
- Kirby notes serious ongoing discussions about a possible hostage deal.
#### U.S. Position on Israeli-Palestinian Conflict
The administration reaffirms support for a two-state solution as the best outcome for Israelis and Palestinians. Despite recent statements by Prime Minister Netanyahu rejecting a Palestinian state post-conflict, the U.S. continues diplomatic efforts to achieve peace. The focus remains on supporting Israel's defense and ensuring humanitarian aid to Gaza.
- Admiral Kirby states the Biden administration supports a two-state solution for Israel and Palestine.
- Kirby acknowledges Netanyahu's public rejection of a Palestinian state but emphasizes ongoing U.S. efforts for peace.
- The U.S. urges Israel to conduct military operations with caution to minimize civilian casualties.
#### Iraq U.S. Military Presence and Cooperation
The U.S. maintains its military presence in Iraq at the invitation of the Iraqi government to combat ISIS threats. Discussions with Iraqi officials continue regarding the future posture and resources of U.S. forces. The administration refrains from commenting on reports about requests for U.S. troop withdrawal.
- Admiral Kirby confirms U.S. forces remain in Iraq to assist against ISIS at Iraq's invitation.
- Kirby emphasizes ongoing consultations with Iraqi government about mission and force posture.
- The administration does not disclose details of diplomatic conversations regarding troop presence.
#### Congressional Border Security Discussions
The President engages with Senate Democrats and Republicans to find bipartisan solutions for border security. While no final agreement has been reached, the administration remains optimistic about progress. The President urges Congress to act on national security supplemental requests to enhance border enforcement and combat fentanyl trafficking.
- MS. Dalton reports productive bipartisan conversations on border security funding and policy.
- The President calls on congressional Republicans to support requests for additional border agents.
- Dalton emphasizes the President's openness to compromise and good faith negotiations.
#### Ukraine Aid and Congressional Funding
The President stresses the urgency of congressional approval for aid to Ukraine, highlighting the risks of delayed support to NATO partners and regional security. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan and DNI Director Haines provide detailed assessments of the stakes involved. The administration resists public discussion of private congressional negotiations.
- MS. Dalton notes the President's clear communication to Congress about the critical need for Ukraine aid.
- Senior officials have outlined specific consequences if aid is obstructed.
- The administration refrains from commenting on private discussions with Congress.
#### Electric Vehicle Performance in Extreme Weather
The administration acknowledges challenges electric vehicles face in extreme weather but notes that such impacts are common across all vehicle types. Efforts continue to improve affordability and reliability of EVs, with sales having quadrupled since the President took office. Reported charging issues are considered isolated and under review.
- MS. Dalton states extreme weather affects all vehicle types, including electric vehicles.
- The administration is committed to advancing affordable and reliable EV technology.
- Recent reports of charging difficulties are isolated incidents under investigation.
#### Uvalde Report and Gun Violence Prevention
The Department of Justice releases a report on the Uvalde school shooting, detailing the tragedy and its impact on families. The President commits to implementing lessons learned and enhancing support for affected communities. The administration calls for congressional action on gun safety measures including red flag laws and background checks.
- MS. Dalton highlights the DOJ report's detailed account of the Uvalde tragedy and its impact.
- The President pledges to act on recommendations and support communities through the White House Office of Gun Violence Prevention.
- Dalton reiterates the need for Congress to pass comprehensive gun safety legislation.
