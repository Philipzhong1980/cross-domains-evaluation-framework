### Overview
  Senior White House officials briefed reporters on the administration’s rapid and multi-dimensional response to the October 7 attacks on Israel, emphasizing sustained U.S. support for Israel’s defense, enhanced regional deterrence, and urgent hostage-recovery efforts. National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan described stepped-up military assistance—including immediate Iron Dome interceptors, forthcoming U.S. aircraft deliveries, and repositioned naval and air assets—and said specialized U.S. experts are deploying to advise on intelligence, planning, and hostage recovery while U.S. combat forces are not being sent to the ground in Israel. Sullivan and Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre report at least 14 American deaths and say roughly 20 Americans remain unaccounted for, note ongoing coordination with Israeli and regional partners, and confirm the administration will request Congress fund aid for Israel while renewing Ukraine assistance. Officials stress the need to protect civilians and conduct operations consistent with the law of war, warn against regional escalation tied to Iranian-backed militias, and underscore domestic vigilance to protect communities and counter potential threats and hate incidents at home. Administrations’ procedural and diplomatic items—such as the President’s Special Counsel interview, the urgent push to confirm an ambassador to Israel, and the temporary deprioritization of broader normalization work—are handled alongside these central national security priorities.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### U.S. support to Israel
  Senior officials describe an immediate and sustained U.S. commitment to support Israel militarily, diplomatically, and via intelligence following the October 7 attacks. The President and Vice President repeatedly consult with the national security team and with Prime Minister Netanyahu to direct assistance. The administration emphasizes the long-standing U.S.-Israel relationship and frames assistance as both humanitarian and defensive.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the President and Vice president continue to meet with the national security team and have held more than three dozen meetings, briefings, and calls since the attacks.
- Mr. Sullivan said the United States will be relentless in ensuring Israel has what it needs to defend itself in the days, weeks, months, and years ahead.
- Mr. Sullivan said the President pledged that American planes will be landing in Israel in the days ahead to deliver capabilities requested by Israel.
  #### Casualties and missing Americans
  Officials provide updated casualty figures, describe ongoing efforts to account for the missing, and identify American victims among the dead and unaccounted. The administration reports at least 14 Americans killed and believes 20 or more Americans are currently unaccounted for, while cautioning that unaccounted-for does not equal confirmed hostages. The U.S. is coordinating with Israeli authorities to determine whereabouts, recover bodies, and support families.
- Mr. Sullivan said more than a thousand people were killed in the attacks and at least 14 Americans have died.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. believes 20 or more Americans are currently unaccounted for but cautioned that unaccounted-for does not necessarily mean they are hostages.
- Mr. Sullivan said the State Department has been in contact with families and that the U.S. will update families and the public as information becomes available.
  #### Hostage recovery efforts
  The administration prioritizes locating and recovering hostages and missing Americans by sharing intelligence and deploying specialized U.S. experts to advise Israel. Officials emphasize that hostage recovery is a top priority and that the U.S. is actively coordinating with Israeli and regional partners. The U.S. will continue to provide operational and intelligence support while respecting operational secrecy.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. is working with Israeli authorities and regional partners on every aspect of the hostage crisis, including sharing intelligence and deploying experts.
- Mr. Sullivan said the President has designated the safety and wellbeing of American citizens held hostage overseas as the highest priority.
- Mr. Sullivan said deployed U.S. experts will focus on intelligence, planning, and coordination to assist hostage recovery efforts.
  #### Iron Dome and arms shipments
  The United States is rapidly supplying Iron Dome interceptors and other military capabilities to help Israel defend against rocket attacks. Some interceptors are drawn from in-country U.S. stocks and additional shipments will follow; the U.S. is also exploring broader air defense augmentation. Officials indicate U.S. aircraft will deliver requested capabilities and that Congress will be asked to support continued resupply.
- Mr. Sullivan said some Iron Dome interceptors have come from U.S. stocks in country and additional interceptors will be flowed to Israel.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. is exploring other ways to augment Israeli air defenses beyond Iron Dome.
- Mr. Sullivan said American planes will deliver additional military capabilities and that the administration is coordinating with Congress to ensure continued supply.
  #### U.S. military posture and deterrence
  The U.S. has enhanced its force posture in the region to strengthen deterrence, including moving a carrier strike group to the Eastern Mediterranean and positioning aircraft in theater. Officials emphasize that these moves aim to deter other actors from widening the conflict rather than target Hamas directly. The administration remains prepared to reposition or add assets as necessary and continues contingency planning with allies.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. has moved a carrier strike group into the Eastern Mediterranean to strengthen deterrence and is prepared to move additional assets as necessary.
- Mr. Sullivan said the carrier movement is intended to send a deterrent message to other states or non-state actors that might seek to widen the war, not to target Hamas directly.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. is engaged in contingency planning for escalation scenarios and is consulting with allies and partners about potential developments.
  #### U.S. ground forces and experts
  Officials distinguish between deploying combat forces to the ground in Israel and sending specialized U.S. experts to assist with intelligence, planning, and coordination. The administration says it is not planning to send U.S. ground forces to Israel at this time but is sending non-combat experts to support hostage recovery and operational coordination. Any future changes in the posture would be made only if necessary.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. is not currently planning to put American ground forces on the ground in Israel.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. is sending experts who will work on intelligence, planning, and coordination with the Israeli government.
- Mr. Sullivan said operational discussions between the President and the Prime Minister are being kept in discrete channels.
  #### Iran’s role and related questions
  The administration describes Iran as broadly complicit in supporting Hamas through funding, training, and capabilities, while saying it has no confirmation that Iran directly planned or knew of the October 7 attack in advance. U.S. intelligence is actively reviewing information and consulting with Israeli counterparts to assess Iran’s role. Questions about freezing funds tied to prior agreements receive a limited response, with officials noting no dollars have been spent from the referenced package.
- Mr. Sullivan said Iran has provided funding, training, and capabilities to Hamas over years and is broadly complicit in enabling the group.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. currently has no confirmation that Iran planned or knew of this specific attack in advance and is reviewing intelligence on that question.
- Mr. Sullivan said not a dollar of the referenced $6 billion has been spent and left policy questions about freezing funds to other decision-makers.
  #### Congressional aid and funding requests
  The administration plans to request funding from Congress to support Israel and to renew the request for Ukraine assistance, though details about package composition will be provided by OMB and other officials. The White House signals it will work with Congress to secure the resources while not preempting the OMB’s forthcoming proposal. Officials emphasize the intention to pursue both priorities simultaneously.
- Mr. Sullivan said the President will make a request to Congress that includes funding to support Israel and will renew the request for aid to Ukraine.
- Mr. Sullivan said the precise form of the congressional request will be worked out and presented by other agencies, not from his podium.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the administration intends to press Congress to act quickly on Israel aid while continuing to support Ukraine.
  #### Protection of civilians and law of war
  U.S. officials stress the importance of distinguishing between Hamas targets and civilians, underscoring commitments to the law of war and urging that military operations avoid deliberate targeting of civilians. The administration says it is discussing civilian protection, safe passage, and evacuation logistics with Israeli and Egyptian counterparts while keeping operational details private. Officials reject characterizations that their counsel is a warning and emphasize collaborative dialogue with Israel.
- Mr. Sullivan said the United States does not deliberately target civilians and insists that military operations be conducted consistent with the rule of law and the law of war.
- Mr. Sullivan said the administration is consulting with Israel and Egypt about civilian movement and safe passage but declined to share operational specifics publicly.
- Mr. Sullivan said reports that Israel plans a total siege of Gaza are not consistent with his understanding and that consultations on Israeli actions are ongoing.
  #### Regional escalation risks
  Officials warn that other regional actors and militias could try to exploit the situation and indicate that the U.S. has sent deterrent messages and military assets to discourage widening conflict. The administration highlights threats from militias, Hezbollah, and other actors while asserting readiness to respond firmly to provocations. U.S. military presence and posture aim to protect regional stability and U.S. interests.
- Mr. Sullivan said various Iranian-backed militias across the region pose an urgent threat and could try to exploit the situation, prompting clear U.S. warnings of consequences.
- Mr. Sullivan said the U.S. has assured the presence of F-16s, F-35s, and A-10s in the theater to handle contingencies and protect U.S. forces and interests.
- Mr. Sullivan said the movement of the carrier strike group and other assets serves to deter states or non-state actors from widening the conflict.
  #### Domestic security and communal safety
  The administration expresses concern about potential spillover effects in the U.S., including threats to Jewish and Muslim communities and the risk of violent demonstrations. Officials say they are on heightened alert, coordinating with law enforcement, and condemning violence while protecting civil liberties. The White House pledges vigilance and support to keep communities safe and to counter hate and extremist exploitation of the crisis.
- Mr. Sullivan said the President directed the national security team to focus on protecting Americans at home and places of worship in light of potential threats and hate incidents.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the administration denounces any acts of violence against any community and is monitoring demonstrations and clashes across the country.
- Mr. Sullivan said U.S. agencies remain vigilant about terrorist threats to the homeland and will mobilize assets as needed to address any plots.
  #### Diplomatic and administrative matters
  Reporters ask about related administrative topics including the President’s Special Counsel interview, the status of the U.S. ambassador nomination to Israel, and the effect on broader diplomatic initiatives such as normalization deals. The White House defers detailed legal questions to the White House Counsel and DOJ, says it will work with the Senate to confirm the ambassador urgently, and indicates normalization discussions are not the main focus while the immediate crisis demands attention.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the White House will not go beyond the White House Counsel’s public statement regarding the President’s Special Counsel interview.
- Mr. Sullivan said the administration will move urgently to secure Senate confirmation of the ambassador to Israel and get the nominee to the region as soon as possible.
- Mr. Sullivan said the question of ongoing normalization efforts is not being characterized publicly because the administration’s immediate focus is on responding to the attack and supporting Israel.
