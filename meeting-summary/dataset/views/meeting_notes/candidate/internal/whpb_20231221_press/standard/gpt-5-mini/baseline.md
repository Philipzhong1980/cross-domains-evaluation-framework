### Overview
  Karine Jean-Pierre opens the briefing with condolences for the Prague university shooting and reviews the Biden-Harris administration’s year-end accomplishments on healthcare (including insulin caps and Medicare drug negotiation), jobs, small-business formation, consumer protections, and climate investments. Admiral John Kirby joins to brief on President Biden’s call with Mexico’s president about unprecedented migratory flows and announces that senior officials will travel to Mexico to coordinate further action; he also provides an operational update on humanitarian aid into Gaza and outlines the complex, shifting bottlenecks—combat conditions, inspection capacity, and truck availability—that affect deliveries. Reporters press on Gaza’s humanitarian crisis, U.N. Security Council negotiations, Israel’s transition from high-intensity operations, and ongoing hostage-and-pause diplomacy; Kirby emphasizes persistent U.S. engagement while declining to negotiate operational or U.N. language from the podium. The briefing covers the Venezuela detainee swap and related electoral commitments, the administration’s supplemental funding request to address border staffing and to replenish Ukraine support, domestic political critiques over Israel policy, and immigration negotiations with Congress and Mexico; the team stresses the need for congressional funding and bipartisan policy agreements. Additional topics include detainee safety (Paul Whelan), RSV vaccine supply discussions with manufacturers, a statutory review of China tariffs, economic indicators and Bidenomics messaging, concern about depleting U.S. stocks for Ukraine assistance, and routine end-of-year logistics and holiday remarks.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Prague shooting condolences
  MS. JEAN-PIERRE opened by acknowledging a mass shooting at Charles University in Prague and offering the President and First Lady's condolences. She notes U.S. federal authorities are in contact with Czech counterparts and the U.S. stands ready to provide support. This statement frames the briefing with an immediate international tragedy and a U.S. offer of assistance.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President and First Lady are praying for families affected by the shooting.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the United States sends condolences and wishes survivors a speedy recovery.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports federal authorities are in touch with Czech authorities and the U.S. stands ready to provide additional support.
  #### Administration accomplishments overview
  MS. JEAN-PIERRE reviewed progress under the Biden-Harris administration on healthcare, reproductive rights, and broader domestic priorities as the year closes. She highlighted actions enabled by the Inflation Reduction Act, health insurance enrollment gains, and executive measures on abortion access. The remarks summarize accomplishments intended to frame the administration's record heading into the new year.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Inflation Reduction Act enabled insulin cost caps, Medicare drug negotiation, and average annual savings for 15 million Americans.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports seven million more Americans have health coverage through the Affordable Care Act.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the President and Vice President took executive actions addressing state abortion bans.
  #### Economy, jobs, businesses
  MS. JEAN-PIERRE highlighted economic gains including job creation, business formation, and wage growth. She cited over 14 million jobs created, record small-business applications, and rising wages and wealth above pre-pandemic levels. These points are used to underscore the administration's economic claims and support messaging around 'Bidenomics.'
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says over 14 million jobs have been created under President Biden.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports a record 15 million applications to start new businesses.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states wages and household wealth are now higher than before the pandemic.
  #### Consumer protections and fees
  MS. JEAN-PIERRE described administration efforts to curb hidden 'junk' fees across industries and claimed those actions are saving Americans billions annually. She listed sectors affected by new rules, such as airlines, credit cards, banking, healthcare, and rentals. The point highlights domestic regulatory activity aimed at lowering consumer costs.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration is banning hidden junk fees in airline tickets, credit cards, banking, healthcare, and apartment rentals.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states these consumer protections are saving Americans billions of dollars each year.
  #### Climate agenda and investments
  MS. JEAN-PIERRE summarized the administration's climate initiatives, referencing COP28 outcomes, the American Climate Corps, methane standards, and private investment in clean energy. She emphasized targets like tripling renewable energy by 2030 and cited over $600 billion in private investments. The remarks position climate policy as both an environmental and industrial strategy that is driving manufacturing and clean energy growth.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the administration secured a COP28 agreement to transition away from fossil fuels and triple renewable energy globally by 2030.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports launching the American Climate Corps to mobilize clean energy and conservation workers.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the administration’s climate agenda has unlocked over $600 billion in private sector investments.

He announced that Secretary Blinken, Secretary Mayorkas, and Homeland Security Advisor Liz Sherwood-Randall will travel to Mexico to discuss further actions. The discussion signals a diplomatic push to coordinate enforcement and address root causes with Mexican authorities.

- MR. KIRBY reports President Biden has asked Secretary Blinken, Secretary Mayorkas, and Homeland Security Advisor Liz Sherwood-Randall to travel to Mexico in coming days.
- MR. KIRBY says both leaders agreed additional enforcement actions are urgently needed to reopen key ports of entry.
  #### Gaza humanitarian assistance update
  MR. KIRBY provided a logistical update on humanitarian aid into Gaza, reporting roughly 120 trucks through Kerem Shalom and over 71 through Rafah in a 24-hour period. He emphasized continued U.S. efforts to increase flow and ensure aid reaches people in need, while noting operational complexities in a war zone. The update frames U.S. activity around alleviating Gaza's humanitarian crisis amid ongoing conflict.
- MR. KIRBY reports about 120 trucks entered Gaza through Kerem Shalom and more than 71 through Rafah in the last 24 hours.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. will continue working to increase aid flow and establish processes to get assistance to people inside Gaza.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes the complexity of delivering aid inside a war zone and the need for secure local distribution.
  #### Gaza bottlenecks and inspections
  Reporters pressed MR. KIRBY on why trucks are not getting in consistently, inspection regimes, and where bottlenecks occur. He described multiple, shifting constraints—including fighting on the ground, inspection capacity, and truck availability—and declined to detail U.S. negotiating positions on specific U.N. resolution language. The exchange underscores operational challenges and diplomatic sensitivity around inspection authority and distribution responsibilities.
- MR. KIRBY says bottlenecks vary daily and include active fighting, inspection capacity, and availability of aid and trucks.
- MR. KIRBY states the U.N. is leading local distribution efforts while the U.S. coordinates to increase safe, sustained access.
- MR. KIRBY declines to negotiate UN resolution language from the podium and affirms Israel has had a key role in the inspection regime.
  #### Israel operations transition
  Reporters asked whether the U.S. is pushing Israel to move from high-intensity operations to a lower-intensity phase and whether a timetable exists. MR. KIRBY said Israel acknowledges the need to transition and will decide timing and tactics, while U.S. officials are sharing lessons and asking tough questions. The U.S. emphasizes dialogue and influence but does not dictate operational timelines to Israeli military leadership.
- MR. KIRBY says Israeli leaders recognize the need to transition to a different, lower-intensity phase of operations.
- MR. KIRBY reports U.S. officials, including Secretaries Austin and Blinken, have discussed lessons and tough questions with Israeli counterparts.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes the U.S. is not dictating operational timelines to Israel but believes a near-term transition is the best outcome.
  #### Venezuela swap and reforms
  Reporters sought clarification on President Biden’s description of the Maduro regime agreeing to electoral proposals as part of a swap that returned wrongfully detained Americans. MR. KIRBY avoided granular diplomatic details, saying the regime agreed to certain electoral proposals supported by opposition parties and stressing the importance of meeting commitments. The administration framed the exchange as a difficult but positive outcome that prioritized returning Americans while protecting national security interests.
- MR. KIRBY says the Maduro regime agreed to certain electoral proposals that were supported by opposition parties, and the administration expects those commitments to be met.
- MR. KIRBY declines to discuss specific diplomatic negotiating details from the podium.
- MR. KIRBY says the administration got 10 Americans back home and emphasized the President’s obligation to protect Americans abroad while considering national security.
  #### Border surge and supplemental funding
  Reporters raised resource shortfalls at CBP and asked what the White House is doing to address the surge and smuggler tactics that endanger migrants. MR. KIRBY pointed to the administration’s supplemental request as a vehicle to add Border Patrol officers, asylum officers, and physical security measures, and said the request is central to further action. He also reiterated that diplomacy with Mexico aims to tackle root causes and reduce migratory pressure.
- MR. KIRBY says the supplemental request would fund more Border Patrol officers, asylum court officers, and physical security measures.
- MR. KIRBY states the administration is working with Mexico to address root causes of migration and slow flows through coordinated measures.
- MR. KIRBY acknowledges CBP officials report being resourced for roughly half the volume they face and says additional funding is needed.
  #### Domestic criticism on Israel policy
  Reporters cited moderate Democratic lawmakers urging stronger U.S. action to change how Israel conducts operations; MR. KIRBY said the President leads from principles that balance Israel's right to defend itself with reducing civilian harm. He reiterated U.S. efforts to push Israel to limit civilian casualties and noted ongoing day-to-day engagement with Israeli counterparts. The exchange highlights internal U.S. political pressure and the administration’s messaging about principle-driven diplomacy.
- MR. KIRBY states the President balances support for Israel’s defense with efforts to limit civilian harm and secure humanitarian relief.
- MR. KIRBY says U.S. officials are in daily conversations with Israeli counterparts and that Israel has shown efforts to tailor operations.
- MR. KIRBY acknowledges the risk of turning tactical victories into strategic defeats if civilian harm is not minimized.
  #### U.N. Security Council negotiations
  When asked about tensions at the U.N. Security Council and potential vetoes, MR. KIRBY said the U.S. is still negotiating resolution language and does not want to get ahead of the process. He reiterated the administration’s emphasis on addressing the humanitarian situation and advancing a two-state solution as part of its broader approach. The response underscores diplomatic work with UN partners while managing domestic and international expectations.
- MR. KIRBY says the U.S. is actively working with U.N. partners on resolution language and will not preannounce positions from the podium.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes the importance of addressing the humanitarian crisis in Gaza as part of any Security Council text.
- MR. KIRBY says the administration continues to pursue a two-state solution as a long-term objective.

KIRBY said it is too soon to comment on specific proposals and reiterated the need for the administration’s supplemental request. The exchange stresses the urgency of Congressional action to sustain U.S. support for Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY says the supplemental request remains necessary because there is no ready, unlimited pool of U.S. funds to support Ukraine.
