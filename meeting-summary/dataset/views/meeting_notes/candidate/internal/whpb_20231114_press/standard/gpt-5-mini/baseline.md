### Overview
  The session opens with MS. Jean-Pierre highlighting the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law’s two-year achievements and broader Bidenomics gains before National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan outlines a busy diplomacy week in San Francisco. Sullivan previews a bilateral elevation with Indonesia, frames the upcoming summit with President Xi as focused on strengthening communications and responsibly managing competition, and previews economic engagement across APEC, IPEF, and private-sector events. He provides an extensive Middle East update: securing hostages in Gaza remains the Administration’s top priority, the U.S. is pressing for increased humanitarian access and tactical pauses while urging Israel to protect civilians, and negotiations with Qatar and Israel continue. Reporters probe limits of U.S. intelligence on Hamas’s use of civilian infrastructure, proof-of-life for hostages, and the Administration’s responses to regional strikes; Sullivan stresses constrained visibility and the need for caution around classified details. The briefing also addresses domestic risks from a potential government shutdown and the Administration’s ongoing push for supplemental funding for Ukraine, Israel, and the Indo-Pacific, while MS. Jean-Pierre reiterates domestic crime initiatives (American Rescue Plan, Safer America proposal) and defers security-specific questions to the Secret Service. Across the session, the White House emphasizes leader-level diplomacy, continued engagement with Congress, and the Administration’s priorities without divulging classified intelligence or negotiating legislative specifics from the podium.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Bipartisan Infrastructure Law
  MS. Jean-Pierre opens by highlighting the two-year anniversary of the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law and the administration’s claimed outcomes. She frames the law as central to Bidenomics, citing investments in roads, bridges, clean water, broadband, transit, resilience projects, and private-sector manufacturing growth. The Administration presents the law as creating jobs and enabling large-scale domestic investment.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law has launched 40,000 infrastructure projects in 4,500 communities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE reports the law helped more than 21 million low-income households access free or discounted high-speed internet through the Affordable Connectivity Program.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the law supported purchases of over 3,000 clean transit buses and 2,400 clean school buses and launched 2,300 resilience projects.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE attributes more than $614 billion in private-sector manufacturing and clean energy investments to the broader Investing in America agenda.
  #### Indonesia Bilateral Meeting
  Jake Sullivan previews President Biden’s short meeting with President Joko Widodo, announcing the elevation of U.S.-Indonesia relations to a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership. He emphasizes the bilateral focus on clean energy, critical minerals, security, and defense cooperation. The Administration presents the upgrade as a tangible step to deepen strategic and economic ties ahead of APEC week.
- MR. SULLIVAN says President Biden will host President Widodo and elevate the bilateral relationship to a Comprehensive Strategic Partnership.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the leaders will discuss the clean energy transition, critical minerals, and deepening security and defense cooperation.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the elevation will drive innovation, prosperity, and cooperation between the two countries.
  #### U.S.-China Summit Goals
  Sullivan outlines key objectives for President Biden’s summit with President Xi: strengthening open communication, managing competition responsibly, and using face-to-face diplomacy to reduce misperception and avoid conflict. He emphasizes prior engagement at multiple levels and expects discussions on shared concerns such as fentanyl, military-to-military communications, Russia, Ukraine, and the Middle East. The Administration frames leader-level diplomacy as essential for both managing risk and seeking cooperative outcomes.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the summit will focus on strengthening open lines of communication and managing competition so it does not veer into conflict.
- MR. SULLIVAN notes recent high-level exchanges on both sides and says leader-to-leader face-to-face diplomacy has no substitute.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the meeting should address shared interests including fentanyl, reestablishing military-to-military communications, and global issues like Ukraine and the Middle East.
- MR. SULLIVAN says U.S. officials have engaged frequently with Chinese counterparts, and China has reciprocated with senior visits to the United States.
  #### APEC Economic Agenda
  Sullivan describes President Biden’s broader agenda during APEC week in San Francisco, including promoting the U.S. economic vision for the Indo-Pacific, advancing IPEF deliverables, and hosting private-sector events. The White House plans a Partnership for Global Infrastructure and Investment (PGI) event and CEO-focused discussions to showcase U.S. economic leadership and attract investment that supports American jobs. These events aim to demonstrate durable commercial engagement alongside diplomatic outreach.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the President will present an economic vision for the Indo-Pacific and stress U.S. leadership in inclusive, sustainable growth.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the Administration expects clear outcomes on pillars of the Indo-Pacific Economic Framework in record time.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the President will host PGI and CEO events to show partnership with the U.S. private sector and highlight investments creating U.S. jobs.
  #### Hostages and Gaza Humanitarian Effort
  Sullivan provides a detailed update on U.S. efforts to secure the release of hostages held by Hamas and to expand humanitarian assistance into Gaza, describing the issue as a top administration priority. He outlines direct engagement with families, conversations with Qatar and Israel, and U.S. efforts to increase aid flow while urging tactical humanitarian pauses. The Administration stresses continued pressure on Israel to protect civilians while affirming Israel’s right to self-defense.
- MR. SULLIVAN says securing the release and safe return of hostages is President Biden’s highest priority and that the President spoke with the Emir of Qatar about negotiations.
- MR. SULLIVAN says U.S. officials are meeting with families of American hostages and that the government is working day by day to progress negotiations.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the U.S. is leading efforts to increase sustained humanitarian assistance into Gaza and urges longer and tactical pauses to enable aid and hostage releases.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the U.S. has raised protection of civilians and hospitals with Israeli counterparts and that evacuation routes must be safe when transfers occur.
  #### Hospitals, Intelligence Limits
  Reporters press Sullivan about claims that Hamas uses hospitals for military purposes and whether U.S. intelligence can corroborate Israeli assertions about specific facilities like Al-Shifa. Sullivan repeatedly says he cannot divulge sensitive U.S. intelligence but references open-source reporting indicating misuse of civilian infrastructure. The Administration emphasizes legal and operational complexity and reiterates that Israel must comply with the laws of war while it pursues Hamas.
- MR. SULLIVAN says he cannot comment on classified U.S. intelligence but cites open-source reporting about Hamas using hospitals and civilian infrastructure for military purposes.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the United States insists hospitals be protected and that evacuation routes must remain safe for patients.
- MR. SULLIVAN says operational decisions about specific tunnels or bunkers lie with militaries on the ground and that the U.S. cannot dictate tactical military actions.
  #### Hostage Proof of Life Status
  When asked whether the U.S. has proof of life for hostages, including Americans, Sullivan acknowledges limited visibility and declines to provide definitive numbers. He confirms the U.S. has information suggesting some hostages are alive and potentially releasable but says exact counts remain uncertain until releases occur. The Administration maintains transparency about limitations in its knowledge while emphasizing active negotiation efforts.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the U.S. has limited visibility into the whereabouts and condition of many hostages and cannot state exactly how many Americans remain alive.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the U.S. has information indicating a substantial number of hostages could be part of a release but will only know for certain upon safe return.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the President is personally engaged and that negotiations continue hour by hour.
  #### U.S. Responses to Regional Attacks
  Sullivan defends recent U.S. strikes against Iran-linked targets in Syria as part of a broader effort to deter attacks on U.S. forces and protect service members. He acknowledges that incidents against U.S. forces have continued and frames deterrence as a process measured over time, committing to respond if strikes persist. The Administration underscores its duty to safeguard U.S. personnel and signals continued readiness to act.
- MR. SULLIVAN says U.S. strikes in Syria aim to protect U.S. forces and deter further attacks by Iranian proxies.
- MR. SULLIVAN acknowledges additional strikes against U.S. forces after recent U.S. actions and says deterrence must be assessed over time.
- MR. SULLIVAN warns that the U.S. will continue to respond if proxy attacks on American forces persist.
  #### Funding, Shutdown Risks
  Reporters ask about the impact of a potential government shutdown and missing House provisions for Ukraine and Israel funding. Sullivan and Jean-Pierre warn a shutdown would harm service members and signal disunity to global partners while underscoring active White House engagement with Congress to secure supplemental national security funding. The Administration expresses confidence in ultimately securing necessary appropriations but declines to negotiate specifics from the podium.
- MR. SULLIVAN says a government shutdown would have a human impact on service members and families and would send a negative signal internationally.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the Administration has requested supplemental funding for Israel, Ukraine, the Indo-Pacific, and the border and is working daily with members of Congress to secure it.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the White House will continue close contact with Democratic leaders and calls the House laddered continuing resolution proposal 'untested' and concerning.
  #### China Military Communications & Issues
  Sullivan reiterates the U.S. position that military-to-military communications with China should remain intact to avoid escalation and that the U.S. has been ready to sustain these links. He declines to speculate on Chinese motives for recent engagement and says specifics of the leader-to-leader exchange (including the spy balloon and AI topics) are for the President to conduct in person. The Administration expresses cautious optimism about progress but defers post-meeting readouts to the President.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the U.S. has been ready to sustain military-to-military communications and sees them as essential to reduce mistakes and avoid escalation.
- MR. SULLIVAN declines to speculate on China’s motives for increased engagement and says those questions should be posed to Chinese officials.
- MR. SULLIVAN says the President will address issues like the spy balloon and AI topics person-to-person with President Xi and the Administration will report appropriately after the meeting.
  #### Domestic Security & Crime Policy
  Reporters press Jean-Pierre about a Secret Service shooting related to an attempted vehicle theft near the President’s family and broader crime in D.C.; she refers specific security questions to Secret Service and reiterates the Administration’s domestic policy responses. Jean-Pierre underscores the American Rescue Plan and the President’s Safer America proposal as actions to fund local policing and crime prevention while urging congressional cooperation. She emphasizes continued intergovernmental engagement with mayors and governors on public safety.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE refers questions about the Secret Service incident to the Secret Service and declines to discuss personal security matters from the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President used the American Rescue Plan to send billions to cities for policing and public-safety resources and proposed a Safer America Plan to provide additional funding.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the Administration is in regular contact with Mayor Bowser and other local leaders on public-safety issues and calls for bipartisan action in Congress.
  #### Other Oversight and Policy Questions
  Reporters raise a range of additional topics including the Supreme Court ethics code, the House Oversight subpoena for former counsel Dana Remus, platform AI-ad disclosures, and intraparty divisions over Israel policy. Jean-Pierre largely declines to add beyond prior statements, refers classified or security matters to appropriate agencies, and reiterates the President’s positions on Israel and the need to uphold laws of war. The White House emphasizes existing executive actions on AI and declines to comment on certain congressional oversight requests from the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE says the President has previously spoken about Supreme Court ethics and underscores public expectations for high ethics and transparency.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declines to comment on the House Oversight Committee subpoena for Dana Remus and refers oversight questions to standard processes.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes the President issued a comprehensive AI executive order and declines to comment on private-sector platform policies from the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE acknowledges Democratic differences on Israel policy but reiterates the Administration’s position that Israel has the right to defend itself and must protect civilians.
