### Overview
The meeting featured key presentations and discussions primarily focused on the Youth Services Unit (YSU) and mental health crisis response initiatives. Sergeant David Shen and Lieutenant Wahim McKee delivered a comprehensive annual report on the YSU's activities, emphasizing their restorative justice approach, extensive training, and diverse youth engagement programs such as Dreams and Futures and PAL boxing. Board members probed into operational details including data collection, demographic tracking, and program inclusivity, highlighting the unit's efforts to address emerging challenges like vaping and substance use among youth. Public comments underscored community concerns about safety incidents and the importance of transparent reporting. Additionally, the Mental Health Crisis Response Subcommittee provided updates on the implementation of North County Trust and 988 crisis hotline integration, with Chief Canfield outlining current challenges in call routing and inter-agency coordination. Board members sought clarifications on these systems, reflecting the complexity of evolving mental health emergency responses. The meeting concluded with discussions on clarifying subcommittee structures and roles to enhance governance. Throughout, participants demonstrated a commitment to collaborative, data-informed approaches to youth safety and mental health support in Mountain View.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Public Comment on Gun Violence Case
Margaret Petros, Executive Director of Mothers Against Murder, addressed the board regarding a 2021 gun violence incident at Rank Strove Park involving gang-related shootings. She praised the Mountain View Police Department's swift arrest but expressed concern over the light sentencing of the suspect and the public availability of victim and witness names. This highlights community concerns about accountability and victim privacy.
- Margaret Petros emphasized the importance of common sense legislation to prevent gun violence and hold illegal gun users accountable.
- She commended the Mountain View Police Department's effective tactics in apprehending the suspect.
- Petros questioned the adequacy of the 90 to 120 days jail sentence given to the suspect.
- She raised concerns about victim and witness names being public records, which could impact their safety and privacy.
#### Youth Services Unit Annual Report
Sergeant David Shen and Lieutenant Wahim McKee presented the Youth Services Unit (YSU) and School Resource Officer (SRO) annual report for fiscal year 2022-2023. The report covered unit composition, values, goals, training, programs, data on school visits, home visits, arrests, and partnerships. The focus is on building trust, safe school environments, restorative justice, and empowering youth through proactive engagement and education.
- Sergeant Shen introduced the YSU team and emphasized the unit's core values: trust, safety, restorative justice, and youth empowerment.
- The unit conducted 264 SRO-led events reaching over 2,000 participants and made 3 arrests out of 113 incidents, reflecting a restorative justice approach.
- Training included 154 hours covering school safety, active shooter preparedness, crisis intervention, and substance abuse education.
- Key programs include Dreams and Futures summer sessions, mentoring, PAL boxing, and vaping education, with partnerships such as Stanford Reach Lab and neurodiverse community organizations.
- Data showed 280 school visits categorized by purpose, 61 home visits addressing truancy, support, wellness, and intervention, and approximately 200 vaping devices seized.
- The unit aims to improve data collection, increase vaping and substance education, expand trauma-informed practices, and enhance collaboration with schools and community partners.
#### Board Member Questions on YSU Operations
Board members inquired about specifics of the Youth Services Unit's operations, including handling of seized vaping devices, harm reduction education, Narcan availability in schools, mentoring programs, proactive visits, demographic data collection, and gender representation. The discussion highlighted the unit's efforts to adapt to emerging youth issues and improve inclusivity and data transparency.
- Sergeant Shen explained that seized vaping devices are either donated for research or processed and destroyed by the department.
- The harm reduction approach educates youth on safer substance use rather than abstinence-only messaging.
- Narcan (naloxone) is available in high schools and middle schools to address opioid overdoses, including fentanyl.
- New mentoring programs empower exemplary students to mentor at-risk peers, aiming for a pay-it-forward effect.
- Proactive school visits are underreported in data but are more frequent, especially at elementary schools.
- The unit is exploring ways to collect demographic data to monitor equity and address concerns about disproportionate impacts.
- Efforts are underway to increase female officer involvement and expand mentoring beyond SROs to include other department members.

Public and board members provided feedback on the YSU report, emphasizing the importance of demographic data, concerns about safety incidents like a student firing a bean bag shotgun, and the value of the unit's restorative justice approach. The board acknowledged the need for clear subcommittee roles and expressed support for ongoing youth engagement and program development.
- Bruce England inquired about the staffing and continuation of the SRO subcommittee, which was clarified as completed with ongoing annual reporting by the YSU.
- Tim McKenzie praised the report and highlighted the importance of structured activities and safety concerns raised in the report.
- Board members expressed interest in demographic data collection to ensure equitable treatment of students.
- Concerns were raised about rumors of unstructured visits and the need for clear communication about program activities.
- Members acknowledged the success of long-standing programs like PAL boxing and Dreams and Futures.
- The board discussed the importance of clear subcommittee naming and roles for effective oversight.
#### Mental Health Crisis Response Subcommittee Update
A verbal update was provided on the Mental Health Crisis Response Subcommittee's work supporting the implementation of North County Trust and Mobile Crisis Response Team (MCRT) services, including coordination with the 988 crisis hotline. Challenges with call routing and data sharing between 988 and 911 dispatch centers were discussed, with ongoing efforts to improve integration and collaboration among regional agencies.
- The subcommittee met with North County Trust Director Corona Brown to discuss implementation status and data collection limitations.
- Updates from Trust will be provided biannually, with further presentations planned for the PSAB.
- Chief Canfield explained that 988 is a national crisis hotline routed regionally, currently lacking full dispatch center capabilities and integration with 911 systems.
- There is a pause in local procedural development pending state-level upgrades to 988 call center capabilities.
- The goal is to divert non-violent mental health calls from police to specialized crisis teams, improving response appropriateness.
- Mountain View is actively collaborating with neighboring cities and county agencies to advance these efforts.
#### Board Member Questions on Mental Health Response
Board members asked clarifying questions about the 988 call routing system, challenges with caller location identification, and the role of EMS in mental health crisis responses. The discussion underscored the complexity of integrating mental health crisis services with traditional emergency response systems and the importance of state guidance for sustainable implementation.
- Chief Canfield explained that 988 call routing is based on phone area codes, which can misroute calls if the caller is outside the area.
- Currently, 988 centers cannot transfer calls directly to 911 dispatch centers, limiting coordination.
- State guidelines and funding decisions are pending to improve 988 capabilities and integration.
- EMS does not typically respond to mental health crises without medical needs in this region, differing from some other jurisdictions.
- Mountain View and partner agencies are motivated to implement effective diversion programs while awaiting state direction.
#### Public Comments and Board Discussion on Mental Health Response
Public commenters expressed appreciation for updates on mental health crisis response and suggested making information about call routing and response processes publicly available. Board members had no additional comments, and the meeting proceeded to staff comments and reports.
- Tim McKenzie highlighted the importance of mental health response work and suggested public flow charts to explain information flow.
- He emphasized thoughtful deployment of armed officers and appropriate response tools for mental health calls.
- No further board comments were made on this item.
