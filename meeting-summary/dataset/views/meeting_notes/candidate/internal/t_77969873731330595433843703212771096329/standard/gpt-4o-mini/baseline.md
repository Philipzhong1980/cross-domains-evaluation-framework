#### Youth Services Unit Annual Report
Sergeant David Shen and Lieutenant Wahed McKee present the annual report for the School Resource Officer (SRO) and Youth Services Unit (YSU). They discuss the unit's goals, activities, and outcomes, emphasizing their commitment to restorative justice and community engagement.
- Sergeant Shen introduces the YSU and outlines its values, including building trust and empowering youth.
- The report details various programs, including home visits and community partnerships, aimed at reducing juvenile delinquency.
- Lieutenant McKee supports the presentation, highlighting the importance of community collaboration.

#### Youth Services Unit Goals and Outcomes
The presentation continues with a focus on the goals of the Youth Services Unit, including creating a safe school environment and reducing referrals to the criminal justice system. The report provides quantitative data on the unit's activities over the past year.
- The YSU aims to create a safe school environment both physically and mentally for students.
- Data shows 264 sessions of SRO-led events reaching over 2000 participants, with a focus on positive engagement.
- The unit conducted 61 home visits, primarily related to truancy and wellness checks.

#### Training and Community Partnerships
The report outlines the extensive training completed by SROs, including active shooter training and crisis intervention. It also highlights partnerships with organizations like Stanford Reach Lab to address youth substance abuse issues.
- SROs completed over 154 hours of training, focusing on school safety and youth engagement.
- The partnership with Stanford Reach Lab aims to provide education on substance use and harm reduction strategies.
- Community partnerships are essential for fostering positive relationships with youth and families.

#### Discussion on Vaping and Substance Abuse
The board discusses the rising issue of vaping among youth, with data indicating a significant number of vaping devices confiscated. The need for increased education and prevention efforts is emphasized.
- Sergeant Shen reports approximately 200 vaping devices seized, indicating a concerning trend among students.
- Board members express the need for more comprehensive education on the dangers of vaping and substance use.
- The importance of engaging parents in these discussions is highlighted.

#### Public Comments and Concerns
Members of the public provide comments regarding the SRO program and mental health crisis response. Concerns are raised about the effectiveness of current practices and the need for clear communication and data collection.
- Tim McKenzie emphasizes the importance of structured activities on campus and the need for clear communication about SRO roles.
- Public comments reflect a desire for more transparency and accountability in the SRO program.
- Concerns are raised about the handling of mental health crises and the involvement of various agencies.
