### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting focused primarily on updates and discussions related to the Multicultural Engagement Program (MEP), subcommittee appointments, ongoing projects, and administrative requirements. Nancy Ducos and Lenka Wright presented a comprehensive update on the MEP's expanded services, including translation, interpretation, leadership academies, and community outreach efforts targeting Mountain View's diverse population. Commissioners expressed appreciation and inquired about program expansion and leadership pipelines. The Commission then revisited subcommittee appointments for Housing and Security Storytelling, Bystander Intervention Training, and Mental Health, balancing continuity with incoming membership as some commissioners prepare to vacate their seats. Assignments were confirmed with flexibility for future adjustments. Updates on the Discrimination and Inclusion Housing project included progress on interview releases and outreach to community members interested in sharing their stories, with plans to enhance web presence and utilize video platforms. The Commission also addressed a request from the Mountain View Police Department for LGBTQ+ community representation, emphasizing the need for clearer communication and outreach coordination. Recruitment efforts for new commissioners were discussed, with optimism about the applicant pool and clarification sought on procedures. Finally, staff informed commissioners about the new requirement to file the Statement of Economic Interest (Form 700), detailing filing timelines, penalties, and offering support for compliance. Throughout, commissioners engaged actively, demonstrating commitment to community engagement, transparency, and effective governance.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Multicultural Engagement Program Update
Nancy Ducos and Lenka Wright provided an update on the Multicultural Engagement Program (MEP), highlighting its role in supporting public access to information and resident involvement through translation, interpretation, and outreach. The program has expanded its team and services, including leadership academies in Spanish and Chinese, outreach via community events and messaging apps, and support for housing-related initiatives. The MEP also focuses on leadership development and community engagement to build bridges within Mountain View's diverse population.
- Nancy Ducos explained MEP's support for public access through translations, interpretation, and multilingual outreach.
- The program has grown from a part-time team to seven full-time members supporting various city initiatives.
- MEP runs Spanish and Chinese Language Civic Leadership Academies with high retention and community impact.
- Outreach includes use of WhatsApp and WeChat, door-to-door flyer distribution, and support for housing and eviction programs.
- Nancy and Lenka shared census data showing significant non-English speaking populations, guiding language access efforts.
- Commissioners expressed appreciation and inquired about program expansion and leadership pipeline.
- Nancy noted graduates from academies serve on commissions and nonprofits, aiding civic engagement.
- Lenka mentioned ongoing development of a language access policy to guide translation priorities.

#### Form 700 Economic Interest Filing Requirements
Laurel James informed commissioners about the new requirement for Human Relations Commission members to file the Statement of Economic Interest (Form 700) due to their substantive recommendations to City Council. The form must be filed annually, with the first filing covering the period from September 27, 2021, to September 27, 2022. Penalties for non-filing will include unexcused absences from meetings. Commissioners asked clarifying questions about filing frequency, exit procedures, and potential penalties, with Laurel offering to consult City Clerk and City Attorney offices for detailed answers.
- HRC members are now designated officials required to file Form 700 annually.
- The first filing covers the 12 months prior to September 27, 2022, with a due date in October.
- Future penalties for non-filing include unexcused absences from meetings.
- Commissioners inquired about filing upon leaving the Commission and other potential penalties.
- Laurel James offered to seek clarification from City Clerk and City Attorney offices and provide follow-up.
- Commissioners were encouraged to reach out individually for assistance with filing.
