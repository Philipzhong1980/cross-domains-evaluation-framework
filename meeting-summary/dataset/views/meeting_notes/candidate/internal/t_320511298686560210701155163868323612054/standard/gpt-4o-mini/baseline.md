### Overview
The Human Relations Commission meeting, led by Chair Annette Lin, focused on several key topics including the approval of previous meeting minutes, public comments, and subcommittee appointments. Community Outreach Coordinator Nancy Ducos provided an update on the Multicultural Engagement Program, highlighting its growth and initiatives aimed at improving community involvement. Commissioners discussed their roles in various subcommittees, ensuring a balance between outgoing and continuing members. The meeting concluded with staff reports on new economic interest disclosure requirements and the continuation of virtual meetings, with the next meeting scheduled for November 3rd, 2022.

### Detailed Summary

The meeting is called to order by Chair Annette Lin, who outlines the legal framework for conducting the meeting virtually due to a declared state of emergency. Roll call is conducted by Principal Management Analyst Laurel James, confirming the attendance of all commissioners except for Commissioner Webb, who is expected to join later.
- Annette Lin announces the meeting's legal compliance with California Government Code Section 54953.
- Laurel James confirms attendance of all commissioners except Commissioner Webb.

Chair Lin requests approval for the minutes from the September 1st meeting. After confirming there are no corrections, a motion is made and seconded to approve the minutes, which is unanimously accepted by the commissioners present.

- The motion is seconded by Vice Chair Sylvester and unanimously approved.

The commission discusses subcommittee appointments, particularly focusing on the Housing and Security Storytelling Subcommittee. Chair Lin suggests moving the discussion of the Multicultural Engagement Program update ahead in the agenda to accommodate a commissioner who needs to leave early. The commission reviews interests in various subcommittees and confirms participation.

#### Multicultural Engagement Program Update
Community Outreach Coordinator Nancy Ducos presents an update on the Multicultural Engagement Program (MEP), detailing its initiatives to improve resident involvement through translation services and community outreach. The program has expanded its team and activities significantly over the past two years, focusing on building relationships within diverse communities.
- Nancy Ducos highlights the MEP's role in translating essential documents and conducting outreach.
- The program has grown to a team of seven, significantly increasing its outreach efforts.

#### Commissioner Questions on MEP
Commissioners engage with Nancy Ducos regarding the MEP's activities, including the success of the Chinese Language Civic Leadership Academy and the demand for additional language services. Ducos provides insights into community engagement and future plans for the program.
- Commissioner Ball praises the addition of the Chinese Language Leadership Academy.
- Ducos discusses the success and retention rates of the leadership academies.

#### Subcommittee Appointments Finalization
The commission finalizes subcommittee appointments, ensuring a balance between outgoing and continuing commissioners. It is agreed that Chair Lin will serve on the Housing and Security Storytelling Subcommittee until her term ends, while other commissioners confirm their roles in various subcommittees.
- Chair Lin will serve on the Housing and Security Storytelling Subcommittee until her term ends.
- Commissioners confirm their participation in the Mental Health and Bystander Intervention Training Subcommittees.

Commissioners provide updates on various initiatives, including outreach efforts to the LGBTQ+ community and the status of the housing project. Staff reports include updates on the new requirements for economic interest disclosures and the continuation of virtual meetings.
