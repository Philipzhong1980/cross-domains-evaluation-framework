### Overview
  At today’s briefing Karine Jean-Pierre and Admiral John Kirby cover a wide set of issues: Jean-Pierre opens on severe weather and previews President Biden’s trip to Allentown to highlight Bidenomics, then Admiral Kirby details intelligence confirming Russia’s use of North Korean ballistic missiles against Ukraine and announces an international joint statement condemning those arms transfers while warning the administration will raise the matter at the U.N. Security Council and continue sanctions. Kirby urges Congress to approve supplemental funding for Ukraine to provide air defenses, and both officials address the controversy around Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin’s hospitalization and delayed disclosure of his prostate cancer diagnosis: the President is informed only today, Austin has taken responsibility, the Pentagon will conduct a 30‑day review, and Chief of Staff Jeff Zients issued a memo directing agencies to submit delegation-of-authority protocols by January 12 and to follow interim robust procedures. The briefing also addresses Middle East policy (the U.S. supports humanitarian pauses but not a general ceasefire now), concerns about press access to Gaza, Chinese activity near Taiwan, human-rights concerns in Burundi, the FAA’s grounding of certain Boeing aircraft after the Alaska Airlines incident and the NTSB investigation, ongoing bipartisan border negotiations, and the administration’s economic messaging emphasizing job growth and falling inflation. The White House repeatedly frames the Austin episode as a transparency and notification failure to be rectified while affirming the President’s confidence in the Secretary and deferring specific legal or investigative questions to the Pentagon, Department of Justice, or White House Counsel as appropriate.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Severe weather alerts
  Karine Jean-Pierre opens by noting severe weather across the country, with blizzards in the West and heavy rain and winds in the East, and stresses public safety measures. She highlights state emergency declarations, early school dismissals, and encourages Americans to monitor local warnings and download the FEMA app. The White House expresses readiness to support affected communities as needed.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said severe weather is impacting the country from blizzards in the West to heavy rain and high winds in the East.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said multiple states have declared states of emergency and some schools are sending students home early.
- Karine Jean-Pierre encouraged Americans to heed local warnings and to download the FEMA app for real-time alerts.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the administration stands ready to support communities nationwide as needed.
#### President’s Allentown visit
  Jean-Pierre previews President Biden’s upcoming trip to Allentown, Pennsylvania, to discuss economic progress under ‘Bidenomics’ and the Investing in America agenda. She frames the visit as highlighting small-business growth, good-paying jobs, rising wages, strengthened local economies, and cost reductions for families. The trip is presented as an example of the administration's economic record to date.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President will travel to Allentown, Pennsylvania, to discuss how communities are recovering under Bidenomics and the Investing in America agenda.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the administration views Bidenomics as driving a small-business boom, job creation with rising wages, and stronger local economies.
#### Russia-North Korea munitions
  Admiral John Kirby reports that Russia is using ballistic missiles and launchers provided by North Korea in strikes against Ukraine, causing civilian casualties. He announces a joint statement by foreign ministers of 47 countries, Secretary Blinken, and the EU High Representative condemning DPRK exports and Russia’s use of those missiles, and says the U.S. will raise the issue at the U.N. Security Council. Kirby underscores that these transfers violate U.N. resolutions and that the U.S. will continue sanctions and public disclosures targeting facilitators.
- Admiral John Kirby said Russia recently launched aerial attacks against Ukraine using missiles and UAVs that have killed and injured Ukrainian civilians.
- Admiral John Kirby said the DPRK provided Russia with ballistic missile launchers and several dozen ballistic missiles, which Russia has since deployed against Ukraine.
- Admiral John Kirby said foreign ministers of 47 countries, Secretary Blinken, and the EU High Representative released a joint statement condemning the DPRK’s export and Russia’s procurement and use of North Korean ballistic missiles.
- Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. will raise these arms transfers at the U.N. Security Council and continue sanctions against those facilitating these deals.
#### Support for Ukraine requested
  Kirby emphasizes that the most effective response to Russia’s missile and UAV campaign is to provide Ukraine with air defense and other military equipment, and reasserts the administration’s call for Congress to approve supplemental funding without delay. He warns Russia seeks assistance from states like North Korea and Iran and frames U.S. support as necessary to defend Ukrainian people and infrastructure. The appeal to Congress is presented as urgent to meet immediate defense needs.
- Admiral John Kirby said providing Ukraine with vital air defense capabilities is the most effective response to ongoing Russian missile and UAV attacks.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration needs Congress to approve the supplemental funding request for Ukraine without further delay.
- Admiral John Kirby said Russia is turning to countries like North Korea and Iran for assistance, increasing urgency for allied support to Ukraine.
#### Austin hospitalization disclosure
  Discussion shifts to Defense Secretary Lloyd Austin’s hospitalization and the timing of disclosure about his prostate cancer diagnosis. Kirby and Jean-Pierre describe a complex timeline: Austin was diagnosed in early December, underwent a procedure on December 22, Jake Sullivan learned last Thursday, and the President was informed only that morning. The exchange raises questions about notification practices and transparency within the Pentagon and across the interagency.
- Admiral John Kirby said Secretary Austin was diagnosed with prostate cancer in early December and underwent a procedure later that month.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President was informed this morning about Secretary Austin’s prostate cancer diagnosis.
- Admiral John Kirby said the National Security Advisor learned Secretary Austin was hospitalized late Thursday afternoon and, together with the Chief of Staff, informed the President that evening.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said Secretary Austin’s statement took full responsibility for disclosure decisions and the administration hopes he recovers fully and returns to the Pentagon.
#### Delegation of authorities protocols
  Karine Jean-Pierre details a memo from Chief of Staff Jeff Zients directing Cabinet agencies to submit written delegation-of-authority protocols to the White House Office of Cabinet Affairs and the Chief of Staff by Friday, January 12. The memo also directs agencies to use interim robust protocols during the review to ensure proper notification and continuity. The White House frames this as part of a broader effort to improve transparency and prevent future lapses.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said Chief of Staff Jeff Zients sent a memo asking every Cabinet agency to submit their current delegation-of-authority protocols in writing by Friday, January 12.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the Zients memo directs Cabinet agencies to use a set of interim robust protocols while the White House reviews existing procedures.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President believes robust protocols are necessary across the Cabinet and that the memo is intended to ensure better notification practices.
#### Pentagon review and accountability
  The Pentagon announces a 30-day review of how information about Austin’s hospitalization and the delegation of authority was handled. Kirby and Jean-Pierre say the department will investigate notification paths and that decisions about accountability for DOD officials who failed to report are up to Pentagon leadership. The White House says it will also review interagency notification procedures and emphasizes learning lessons to avoid recurrence.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the Pentagon has announced a 30-day review of how Austin’s hospitalization and associated notifications were handled.
- Admiral John Kirby said it is for Defense Department leadership to determine any accountability measures for officials who may have failed to report information.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the White House will also take steps to tighten alignment across the interagency on notification procedures for delegation of authority.
#### Continuity of operations
  Kirby defends continuity of command during Austin’s absence, saying U.S. national security and military operations were never compromised. He notes the Deputy Secretary, Kathleen Hicks, had delegated authority and full communications while on leave and that the President approved operations such as a dynamic strike and defensive actions in the Red Sea. The administration uses this to argue systems functioned despite disclosure problems.
- Admiral John Kirby said at no time was U.S. military ability to defend national security interests compromised during Secretary Austin’s hospitalization.
- Admiral John Kirby said Deputy Secretary Kathleen Hicks had delegated authority and full communications capability while acting in Austin’s stead.
- Admiral John Kirby said the President had approved military actions and that operational command-and-control remained intact throughout the period in question.
#### Transparency and public trust
  Reporters press the administration on credibility and whether the President should await the Pentagon’s review before expressing confidence in Austin. Jean-Pierre and Kirby repeatedly emphasize Austin’s taking responsibility, the need to learn lessons, and that the President continues to have full confidence in the Secretary. They acknowledge the episode was 'suboptimal' for transparency and say it will prompt procedural fixes.
- Admiral John Kirby said the disclosure timeline was 'not optimal' and that the administration recognizes the credibility challenge created by the episode.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said Secretary Austin took full responsibility in his statement and the President appreciates that acceptance of responsibility.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President continues to have full confidence in Secretary Austin while the Pentagon and the White House review notification and transparency processes.
#### Israel, Gaza, and press access
  Questions address the Israel-Hamas conflict, including the President’s stance on ceasefire, press access to Gaza, and Secretary Blinken’s meetings in Israel. Kirby reiterates the administration does not support a general ceasefire now but supports humanitarian pauses, and he says he will follow up on reports about foreign journalists being denied access. He defers detailed diplomatic readouts to the State Department while promising further follow-up.
- Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. does not support a general ceasefire at this time but continues to support humanitarian pauses.
- Admiral John Kirby said Secretary Blinken is still on his trip and the State Department will provide detailed readouts of his meetings when available.
- Admiral John Kirby said he will check reports that the Israeli Supreme Court denied requests from a coalition of international news organizations to enter Gaza.
#### Journalists and civilian casualties
  Reporters raise concerns about journalist deaths and civilian casualties in Gaza and whether U.S. policy on arms support to Israel should change. Kirby says the administration has not seen evidence that Israel is deliberately targeting journalists and emphasizes protecting journalists is important. He commits to looking into reports and reiterates U.S. insistence on protecting civilians and press freedom.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration has seen no indication that Israeli forces are deliberately targeting journalists, and he emphasized the need to protect journalists covering conflict.
- Admiral John Kirby said he will seek additional information to verify reports about international press being denied entry to Gaza.
#### China activity near Taiwan
  Kirby comments on Chinese military and space activity around Taiwan, noting recent satellite and aircraft movements that raise questions about intent and timing ahead of Taiwan’s election. He stresses U.S. support for Taiwan’s democratic institutions and calls for respect for a free, fair, and transparent election. The U.S. states readiness to work with Taiwan’s next government irrespective of the outcome.
- Admiral John Kirby said China’s recent satellite launch over Taiwan’s airspace and military activity raise questions about intent and timing ahead of Taiwan’s election.
- Admiral John Kirby said the United States believes Taiwan’s democratic institutions should be respected and stands ready to work with whoever the people of Taiwan elect.
#### Human rights concerns in Burundi
  When asked about Burundi’s president calling for stoning gay people to death, Kirby condemns such proposals as horrific and signals the administration takes civil and human rights seriously in its engagement with African governments. He says there are no decisions to announce but suggests the U.S. will monitor and consider appropriate responses. The remark underscores human-rights considerations in U.S. foreign policy.
- Admiral John Kirby said Burundi’s president’s comments calling for stoning gay people would be a horrific form of punishment and an affront to human rights.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration takes civil and human-rights issues seriously and will watch developments closely, though he had no decisions to announce.
#### FAA and Alaska Airlines incident
  Jean-Pierre addresses the Alaska Airlines flight incident, confirms all passengers and crew are safe, and says the FAA is grounding certain Boeing aircraft pending enhanced inspections. She points to the NTSB leading the investigation and says the President is being kept updated. The administration frames the FAA’s grounding as an abundance-of-precaution safety measure for travelers.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the White House is relieved that all passengers and crew on the Alaska Airlines flight are safe.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the FAA is grounding the affected Boeing aircraft until operators complete enhanced inspections and will support the NTSB’s investigation.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President is receiving updates and believes Americans should feel safe flying while the FAA takes precautionary measures.
#### Border security negotiations
  Jean-Pierre says the President supports bipartisan talks to address border security and that Senate negotiators are engaging productively, with negotiators suggesting text could appear this week. She emphasizes the President’s optimism for a bipartisan agreement to address long-standing immigration challenges and declines to preempt negotiators’ work by discussing sticking points publicly. The administration positions itself as supportive of a negotiated solution.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the President supports a bipartisan agreement on border security and is optimistic about ongoing Senate negotiations.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said negotiators indicated it is likely some text could be available this week, but she declined to preview details to avoid stepping into the negotiators’ process.
#### Economic record and messaging
  Jean-Pierre rebuts claims about an impending recession, citing strong labor-market data, declining inflation, and rising wages as evidence of 'Bidenomics' success. She cites Secretary Yellen’s characterization of a 'soft landing' and contrasts the administration’s approach with Republican policy proposals it labels as 'MAGAnomics.' The White House frames its messaging as emphasizing job growth, low unemployment, and falling inflation.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said consumer sentiment is rising, inflation is falling, and the administration credits Bidenomics for economic gains.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said the U.S. created 2.7 million jobs last year, maintained unemployment under 4 percent, and saw inflation decline, which she said is inconsistent with a recession.
- Karine Jean-Pierre quoted Treasury Secretary Janet Yellen describing current conditions as a 'soft landing.'
#### Ongoing legal and political items
  Reporters ask about matters including presidential immunity claims in former President Trump’s case, the First Son’s art sales and related testimony, and an alleged business link between Kyiv’s mayor and the First Son’s former business. Jean-Pierre declines to comment on ongoing legal cases and refers questions to the Department of Justice or White House Counsel as appropriate. She also characterizes planned House Republican impeachment hearings of Secretary Mayorkas as political games.
- Karine Jean-Pierre declined to comment on legal questions about presidential immunity and referred reporters to the Department of Justice.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said questions about the First Son’s art sales and any related business ties should be directed to the White House Counsel’s Office.
- Karine Jean-Pierre said House Republicans’ planned impeachment hearing against Secretary Mayorkas is a political stunt and that the administration views it as a misuse of priorities.
