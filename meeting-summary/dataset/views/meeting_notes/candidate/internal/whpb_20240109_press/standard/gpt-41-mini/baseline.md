Several states have declared emergencies, and schools in Washington D.C. are sending students home early. The administration is monitoring the situation and encourages Americans to heed local warnings and use the FEMA app for real-time alerts.

- Jean-Pierre encouraged Americans to monitor weather updates and heed local officials' warnings.

Kirby stressed the need for Congress to approve supplemental funding to provide Ukraine with air defense capabilities to counter these threats.
- Admiral Kirby reported Russia's use of North Korean ballistic missiles against Ukraine causing civilian deaths and injuries.

- He urged Congress to approve supplemental funding to supply Ukraine with vital air defense systems.

The briefing addressed the delayed disclosure of Secretary of Defense Lloyd Austin's prostate cancer diagnosis. Kirby and Jean-Pierre explained the President was informed only recently despite the diagnosis occurring in early December. They acknowledged shortcomings in the notification process and emphasized ongoing reviews to improve protocols. Secretary Austin has taken full responsibility for the delay and is expected to recover and return to duty.

- Jean-Pierre detailed a memo from the Chief of Staff directing agencies to review delegation of authority protocols.
- Both acknowledged the notification delay was suboptimal and is under review.
- Secretary Austin publicly took full responsibility for the delayed disclosure and is recovering.

Jean-Pierre explained a memo from Chief of Staff Jeff Zients requiring Cabinet agencies to submit their delegation of authority protocols for review by January 12. In the interim, agencies must follow robust protocols when delegating authority. This aims to prevent future lapses in communication like the one involving Secretary Austin's hospitalization and ensure transparency and readiness.
- The Chief of Staff issued a memo to Cabinet agencies on delegation of authority protocols.
- Agencies must submit existing protocols for review by January 12.
- Until reviews are complete, agencies must follow enhanced interim protocols.
- The goal is to improve transparency and prevent future notification lapses.
