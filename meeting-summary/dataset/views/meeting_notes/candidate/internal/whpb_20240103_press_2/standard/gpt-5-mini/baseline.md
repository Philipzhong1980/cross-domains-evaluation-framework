### Overview
  The briefing opens with Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre warning of a potential government shutdown in 16 days and criticizing House Republicans for prioritizing impeachment over funding; she urges Congress to do its basic duty. The majority of the session features Admiral John Kirby’s laydown on the Middle East: he describes strengthened U.S. force posture and coalition activity to protect Israel, surge humanitarian assistance to Gaza, and defend freedom of navigation in the Red Sea, where the U.S.-led Operation Prosperity Guardian and more than 20 partner nations counter Houthi attacks on commercial shipping. Kirby declines to assign responsibility for a recent bombing in Iran or a reported airstrike that killed a Hamas leader, says the U.S. has no indication Israel was involved, stresses efforts to prevent regional escalation, and explains that Israeli forces can militarily degrade Hamas’s ability to attack though ideology may persist; he also reports ongoing hostage-negotiation engagement. Reporters press about Ukraine and munitions, and Kirby reiterates that Russia continues missile and drone strikes and that the U.S. needs congressional supplemental funding to sustain security assistance. Back at the podium, Jean-Pierre addresses a range of domestic issues: she will follow up on an offshore wind milestone, summarizes the President’s 2024 focus on implementing major legislative achievements and the Unity Agenda, defends Secretary Mayorkas amid House impeachment proceedings while supporting bipartisan Senate negotiations on border policy and funding, and calls for congressional action on rail safety after the East Palestine derailment; she also responds to questions about the national debt, an imam shooting in New Jersey, Senator Menendez’s legal matters, and the need for moral clarity after university controversies. Across the briefing, the administration emphasizes a defensive U.S. posture abroad, diplomatic engagement on hostages and regional partners, the urgent need for congressional action on supplemental funding and appropriations, and continued implementation of domestic priorities.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Imminent government shutdown
  Press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre opens by warning that parts of the government face a shutdown in 16 days and criticizes House Republicans for leaving Congress in December. She frames the issue as a failure to do basic appropriations work and charges Republicans with prioritizing impeachment over funding. The administration urges House Republicans to return to negotiations to keep the government open.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the government faces a shutdown in 16 days and that House Republicans must do their basic duty to fund the government.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said House Republicans are prioritizing impeachment stunts instead of delivering for the American people.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration expects Congress to work with the President to address issues that matter to citizens.
  #### Middle East force posture
  Admiral John Kirby provides a detailed laydown of U.S. force posture in the Middle East, describing carrier strike groups, an amphibious ready group with the 26th Marine Expeditionary Unit, shore-based squadrons, and destroyers configured for missile defense. He frames the deployments as defensive measures to help Israel, surge humanitarian assistance for Gaza, and protect U.S. national security interests. The administration emphasizes presence and flexibility to deter escalation and defend freedom of navigation.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States maintains a significant force presence in the Middle East, including the USS Dwight D. Eisenhower carrier strike group and an amphibious ready group centered on USS Bataan.
- MR. KIRBY said these sea-based forces provide flexible options for the President and contribute air and missile defense as well as ISR and offensive and defensive power.
- MR. KIRBY said the deployments aim to help Israel defend itself, surge humanitarian assistance to Gaza, and defend U.S. national security interests.
  #### Red Sea security operation
  Kirby outlines Operation Prosperity Guardian and coalition activity in the Red Sea to counter Houthi attacks on commercial shipping, noting more than 20 nations are contributing capabilities. He stresses the operation’s defensive focus to protect global trade routes and cites recent successful ship-based air and missile defense actions. The U.S. highlights coalition statements condemning the attacks and warns of economic consequences if shipping detours persist.
- MR. KIRBY said U.S. and coalition ships are operating in the Red Sea to counter Houthi attacks on civilian maritime shipping.
- MR. KIRBY said more than 20 nations are contributing to the defensive Operation Prosperity Guardian to protect freedom of navigation and international commerce.
- MR. KIRBY said the coalition’s outreach culminated in a joint statement condemning the Houthi attacks, which had 13 signatories after Singapore joined.
  #### Responsibility for Iran bombings and assassination
  Reporters ask whether the U.S. has assessed responsibility for bombings in Iran and the reported killing of a Hamas leader in Beirut; Kirby says the U.S. has no indications implicating Israel and lacks definitive details on the Iran bombings. He reiterates condolences for victims and declines to attribute the Beirut strike, noting the U.S. is not involved. The administration stresses it judges actions not words and refrains from speculating without evidence.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States has no indication at this time that Israel was involved in the bombing in Iran or the airstrike that reportedly killed the Hamas leader.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. does not have detailed information on the Iran bombing and therefore cannot identify responsibility.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States had no involvement in the reported Lebanon airstrike and is not conducting an assessment of U.S. responsibility.
  #### Risk of regional escalation
  Reporters press about whether the Lebanon strike or other incidents could broaden the conflict; Kirby emphasizes U.S. efforts to prevent widening the war and describes force posture adjustments as designed to deter escalation. He says the U.S. continues diplomatic messaging to partners and private and public warnings to dissuade second-front openings. The administration maintains that it will defend itself and partners if necessary while seeking to avoid broader conflict.
- MR. KIRBY said the President’s force posture changes are designed to prevent escalation or widening of the conflict beyond Israel and Hamas.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. is working with regional partners to prevent a second front from opening in the north.
- MR. KIRBY said the U.S. will judge actions rather than rhetoric regarding leaders like Hassan Nasrallah and continues to message against broadening the war.
  #### Israel’s military goals and Hamas future
  When asked whether eliminating Hamas is attainable, Kirby says the Israeli military can degrade and defeat Hamas’s ability to conduct attacks and target its infrastructure and leadership, but he cautions that ideology typically outlasts military campaigns. He compares the approach to prior counterterror efforts against ISIS and al Qaeda and notes that while the threat to Israeli civilians can be removed, organizations or ideas may persist. The U.S. defers to Israel on specific military objectives but affirms the right to defend against Hamas.
- MR. KIRBY said the Israeli military can militarily degrade and defeat Hamas’s ability to conduct attacks inside Israel.
- MR. KIRBY said military operations are unlikely to eliminate the ideology underpinning Hamas, drawing parallels to al Qaeda and ISIS.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States expects Israeli operations to be conducted in accordance with international law.
  #### Hostage negotiations and humanitarian assistance
  Kirby reports ongoing U.S. engagement with partners, including calls between U.S. officials and Israeli counterparts, to secure the release of remaining hostages and to surge humanitarian assistance in Gaza. He says conversations with mediators like Qatar continue, that progress is real but not reportable today, and that the U.S. will keep working those channels. The administration stresses hope and sustained diplomatic energy to revive hostage exchanges.
- MR. KIRBY said National Security Advisor Sullivan spoke with Israel’s Minister of Strategic Affairs about ongoing operations and hostage-release efforts.
- MR. KIRBY said conversations with partners and mediators, including Qatar, remain ongoing and energetic though he had no specific progress to report.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States will continue pursuing diplomatic channels to try to secure the return of hostages.
  #### Ukraine attacks and supplemental funding
  Kirby summarizes recent Russian missile and drone attacks on Ukraine, characterizing them as continued efforts to strike military and civilian infrastructure and to ‘‘weaponize winter.’’ He argues that Russia’s actions underscore the need for the President’s supplemental funding request, noting the U.S. has expended the last security assistance package it had authority to provide. Kirby warns that, absent supplemental congressional approval, future shipments and support will pause once current resources are exhausted.
- MR. KIRBY said Russia continues to launch drone, missile, and ballistic attacks against military and civilian targets in Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States has distributed the last security assistance package it had funds for and needs Congress to pass the supplemental to continue support.
- MR. KIRBY said that without supplemental funding, shipments would have to stop once existing authorities are exhausted.
  #### Ukraine equipment accountability and Russian capabilities
  Reporters ask about ATACMS reports, Russian capability, and materiel losses; Kirby cannot confirm media reports on destroying expired ATACMS and defers to the Department of Defense. He assesses that Russia has taken heavy losses in munitions and remains reliant on external sources for shells and drones, while retaining some air and ground capabilities and manpower advantages. The U.S. emphasizes accountability measures for weapons provided to Ukraine and ongoing tracking of battlefield effects.
- MR. KIRBY said he cannot confirm media reports about destroying or transferring expired ATACMS and directed the question to the Department of Defense.
- MR. KIRBY said Russia has suffered significant munitions and manpower costs and relies on foreign-supplied drones and munitions to sustain operations.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States and Ukraine are working on accountability measures to ensure provided systems are used appropriately on the battlefield.
  #### South Africa lawsuit against Israel
  A reporter asks about South Africa’s lawsuit accusing Israel of genocide; Kirby calls the submission meritless, counterproductive, and without basis. The U.S. position rejects the legal claim and views the filing as lacking factual support. The administration frames the stance as a defense of Israel’s actions being judged on their merits rather than on what it deems baseless legal assertions.
- MR. KIRBY said the United States finds South Africa’s submission accusing Israel of genocide to be meritless and without basis in fact.
- MR. KIRBY said the submission is counterproductive to constructive engagement on the conflict.
  #### Red Sea coalition participation question
  Reporters note the joint statement had 13 countries versus 44 in December and question why some partners did not sign; Kirby responds that 13 signatories is significant and that many nations contribute to Operation Prosperity Guardian without public acknowledgment. He explains some countries prefer to provide capabilities discreetly while awareness grows as commerce is impacted. The administration frames growing participation as a response to the tangible threat to shipping.
- MR. KIRBY said 13 total nations signing the recent statement is significant and that other contributing nations sometimes prefer not to be public about participation.
- MR. KIRBY said countries are increasingly willing to express discomfort as the threat to commerce in the Red Sea affects shipping decisions.
- MR. KIRBY said many nations are contributing capabilities to protect international commerce even if they do not sign public statements.
  #### Offshore wind farm milestone
  Reporters note the first U.S. offshore wind farm began delivering electricity off Massachusetts and ask whether the President supports it; Jean-Pierre says she will check with relevant teams and notes the administration’s broader support for clean energy and wind turbine manufacturing. She references the President’s prior visits to factories producing components but offers no immediate policy reaction. The White House commits to follow up with details.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she does not have an immediate reaction but will check with the team and follow up on the offshore wind milestone.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President has supported clean energy and has visited factories involved in wind turbine manufacturing.
  #### January 6 historians meeting and elections
  Jean-Pierre confirms the President hosted scholars and historians to discuss threats to democracy ahead of January 6 and refrains from announcing policy changes. She reiterates the administration’s commitment to free and fair elections and says agencies are working to ensure Americans can vote freely in November. The White House frames the meeting as part of broader attention to democratic resilience rather than a precursor to immediate policy proposals.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President met with scholars and historians to discuss threats to democratic institutions ahead of January 6.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration is focused on ensuring the conditions for free and fair elections in November, though she announced no new policy changes.
  #### Federal debt and economic priorities
  Jean-Pierre responds to Treasury data showing a record $34 trillion debt by attributing most debt growth to past Republican tax cuts and highlighting administration measures to reduce deficits. She cites legislation signed to lower the deficit by $1 trillion, the Inflation Reduction Act’s fiscal effects, and the President’s proposals to make wealthy taxpayers pay more as part of deficit reduction. The White House contrasts these measures with Republican proposals it says would add to the debt.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Republican tax cuts account for about 90 percent of the increase in debt as a share of the economy over two decades.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President signed legislation that lowers the deficit by $1 trillion and pointed to the Inflation Reduction Act as a tool to reduce costs and enforcement against tax cheats.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President’s agenda would cut the deficit further by asking wealthy Americans to pay their fair share.
  #### Imam shooting in New Jersey
  Jean-Pierre acknowledges awareness of a shooting that wounded an imam outside a New Jersey mosque, expresses prayers for recovery, and refers investigative questions to local law enforcement and the FBI. She says she has not briefed the President but signals administration concern about potential hate crime motivations. The White House follows law enforcement as investigations proceed.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the White House is aware of the imam shooting in New Jersey and prays for his speedy recovery.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said local law enforcement and the FBI are investigating and the White House deferred to them for specifics.
  #### President’s 2024 priorities and implementation
  Jean-Pierre outlines the President’s focus on implementing major first-term legislative accomplishments—such as infrastructure, CHIPS, and the Inflation Reduction Act—and on a ‘‘Unity Agenda’’ that includes veterans’ care, fentanyl enforcement, and cancer research. She emphasizes implementation of enacted laws and continued work to expand the middle class through Bidenomics. The administration presents 2024 as a year to finish and implement priorities rather than announce sweeping new proposals at the podium.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President will prioritize implementing major enacted laws and advancing a Unity Agenda focused on veterans, fentanyl, and health initiatives.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Bidenomics and historic legislation like the Inflation Reduction Act demonstrate the administration’s economic approach and will guide 2024 work.
  #### Border negotiations and Mayorkas impeachment
  Jean-Pierre defends the administration’s border strategy, reiterates support for Secretary Mayorkas, and describes House impeachment moves as baseless political stunts that distract from bipartisan efforts in the Senate. She emphasizes that the President included border resources in the national security supplemental and that both policy and funding solutions are needed to fix a decades-long broken immigration system. The White House says it is engaged with Senate negotiators and expects bipartisan progress, while asserting the President’s confidence in Secretary Mayorkas.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President has confidence in Secretary Mayorkas and called House Republicans’ impeachment proceedings baseless and politically motivated.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President included border security measures in the national security supplemental because DHS needs more resources and capacity.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration supports bipartisan negotiations in the Senate to address both policy and funding components of immigration reform.
  #### Razor wire and local enforcement
  Reporters raise concerns about Texas-installed razor wire and whether it impedes Border Patrol operations; Jean-Pierre says razor wire makes apprehension more difficult and can put agents at risk while calling Governor Abbott’s actions a political stunt. She reiterates that successful border management requires federal resources and bipartisan policy agreements. The administration highlights diplomatic and enforcement cooperation with Mexico to reduce irregular migration.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Governor Abbott’s razor wire makes it more difficult for Border Patrol agents to apprehend migrants and can endanger personnel.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said unilateral state actions do not replace the need for federal resources and bipartisan policy agreements to address migration effectively.
  #### East Palestine rail safety and visit
  Jean-Pierre says the administration continues to oversee a robust recovery operation in East Palestine, Ohio, and will visit when it is most helpful to the community while ensuring Norfolk Southern is held accountable. She calls on Congress to pass bipartisan rail-safety legislation to address systemic issues uncovered by the derailment. The White House pledges ongoing support for the affected community and urges congressional action on rail safety.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration maintains a robust recovery effort in East Palestine and will visit when a Presidential visit would be most helpful.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Congress needs to pass bipartisan rail-safety legislation to address gaps revealed by the derailment and hold Norfolk Southern accountable.
  #### Senator Menendez indictment
  Reporters ask whether the President thinks Senator Menendez should resign after an additional indictment; Jean-Pierre says that decision is up to the senator and reiterates that Menendez stepped down from his chairmanship. The White House treats the matter as a serious legal issue but declines to call for resignation from the podium. The administration confines its comment to procedural and personnel changes already taken.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said whether Senator Menendez should resign is a decision for the senator to make.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE noted that the senator stepped down from his chairmanship, and the White House is treating the matter as a serious legal issue.
  #### Harvard leadership and moral clarity
  Jean-Pierre declines to comment on private institutional personnel decisions after Harvard’s president resigned, but she emphasizes the administration’s condemnation of calls for genocide and urges moral clarity from leaders. She cites university presidents’ apologies and frames the moment as one to call out hate and stand up against violent rhetoric. The White House underscores its commitment to denouncing antisemitism and other forms of hate publicly.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the White House will not comment on private universities’ personnel decisions but stressed that calls for genocide are monstrous and must be condemned.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said university leaders who testified have apologized and that the administration expects moral clarity and leadership against hate.
