### Overview
In this briefing, Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre and Admiral John Kirby address a range of pressing issues including the imminent risk of a government shutdown due to House Republicans' inaction and focus on impeachment, which Jean-Pierre criticizes as baseless and obstructive. Admiral Kirby provides a detailed update on U.S. military deployments in the Middle East, emphasizing defensive postures to support Israel, protect maritime commerce, and counter Houthi attacks, while rejecting claims that U.S. actions provoke regional escalation. The discussion covers the U.S. stance on recent bombings in Iran and the killing of a Hamas leader in Lebanon, affirming Israel's right to target Hamas leadership within international law. Kirby also updates on the Ukraine conflict, highlighting ongoing Russian attacks and the critical need for Congressional approval of supplemental aid. The administration reiterates its commitment to securing hostages held by Hamas through active negotiations. Domestically, Jean-Pierre outlines President Biden's focus on implementing significant legislative achievements in infrastructure, clean energy, healthcare, and economic policy, while addressing immigration challenges through bipartisan Senate negotiations and criticizing House Republican obstruction. The briefing concludes with a discussion on federal debt, attributing most increases to Republican tax policies and underscoring the administration's efforts to reduce the deficit and promote fiscal responsibility.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Government Shutdown Risk
Press Secretary Jean-Pierre highlights the looming risk of a government shutdown in 16 days due to House Republicans leaving Washington and prioritizing impeachment efforts over funding. She emphasizes the American people's desire for Congress to work with the President on substantive issues and urges Republicans to fulfill their basic duty of funding the government.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states House Republicans left Washington mid-December and are not back until next week.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes Republicans prioritize baseless impeachment stunts over government funding.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE quotes House Republicans threatening to shut the government down, asserting control over funding.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE calls on House Republicans to get serious and deliver for the American people.
#### Middle East Military Posture
Admiral John Kirby provides an update on U.S. military presence and diplomatic efforts in the Middle East amid ongoing conflicts. He details the deployment of naval and marine forces to the Eastern Mediterranean and Red Sea to support Israel, protect commerce, and counter Houthi attacks, emphasizing the U.S. commitment to defense without seeking conflict expansion.
- MR. KIRBY outlines a significant U.S. force presence including USS Dwight D. Eisenhower strike group and amphibious ready group in the Eastern Mediterranean.
- MR. KIRBY highlights coalition efforts with over 20 nations to counter Houthi attacks on civilian maritime shipping in the Red Sea.
- MR. KIRBY states the U.S. released a joint statement with 13 countries condemning Houthi attacks on commercial vessels.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes the U.S. does not seek conflict expansion but will defend national interests and commerce.
#### Iran Bombings and Hamas Leadership
In response to questions, Admiral Kirby states the U.S. lacks detailed information on recent bombings in Iran and does not have evidence implicating Israel. He acknowledges the killing of Hamas leader Saleh al-Arouri in Lebanon but defers to Israeli sources for confirmation, affirming the U.S. stance that Israel has the right to target Hamas leadership within international law.
- MR. KIRBY expresses condolences for victims of Iran bombings but lacks details on perpetrators.
- MR. KIRBY has no indication Israel was involved in the Iran bombings.
- MR. KIRBY confirms al-Arouri was a designated global terrorist and supports Israel's right to target Hamas leadership.
- MR. KIRBY expects Israeli operations to comply with international law.
#### Conflict Escalation Concerns
Admiral Kirby addresses concerns about regional conflict escalation, stating U.S. military deployments aim to prevent widening the Israel-Hamas conflict. He rejects assertions that U.S. actions provoke escalation, attributing provocations to Houthi missile attacks and emphasizing the defensive nature of U.S. operations in the Red Sea.
- MR. KIRBY says U.S. force posture is designed to prevent conflict escalation or widening.
- MR. KIRBY denies U.S. actions have provoked escalation, attributing provocations to Houthi attacks backed by Iran.
- MR. KIRBY highlights the defensive purpose of Operation Prosperity Guardian in protecting maritime commerce.
- MR. KIRBY stands by the position that the U.S. does not seek to widen the conflict.
#### Israel's Military Objectives Against Hamas
The U.S. supports Israel's military goal to degrade Hamas's operational capabilities and leadership but acknowledges that completely eradicating the ideology or group is unlikely. Admiral Kirby compares this approach to past U.S. efforts against terrorist organizations, emphasizing the focus on eliminating threats to Israeli civilians.
- MR. KIRBY states Israel has the right to eliminate the threat Hamas poses to its people.
- MR. KIRBY explains military efforts target Hamas infrastructure and leadership, not ideology.
- MR. KIRBY notes that degrading terrorist groups' capabilities is achievable, but ideology persists.
- MR. KIRBY refrains from providing detailed assessments of Israel's operational progress.
#### Ukraine Conflict and Military Aid
Admiral Kirby updates on the ongoing conflict in Ukraine, noting continued Russian missile and drone attacks and the depletion of U.S. security assistance funds. He stresses the need for Congress to approve supplemental funding to maintain support and highlights efforts to ensure accountability for military aid provided to Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY reports Russia continues attacks on military and civilian targets in Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY confirms the last U.S. security assistance package has been provided and further aid depends on Congressional approval.
- MR. KIRBY emphasizes accountability measures for U.S. military equipment supplied to Ukraine.
- MR. KIRBY notes Russia's military faces challenges in command, morale, and munitions supply.
#### Hostage Negotiations in Israel
The U.S. maintains ongoing, active efforts to secure the release of hostages held by Hamas, with continuous communication involving Qatar and other partners. Admiral Kirby expresses cautious optimism but reports no recent breakthroughs, underscoring the administration's commitment to these negotiations.
- MR. KIRBY confirms ongoing, real conversations to secure hostage releases.
- MR. KIRBY notes previous success in releasing about 50 hostages.
- MR. KIRBY states no specific progress to report currently but maintains hope.
- MR. KIRBY mentions continuous engagement by U.S. envoys in the region.
#### U.S. Domestic Policy and Priorities
Press Secretary Jean-Pierre outlines President Biden's focus on implementing historic legislation passed during his term, including infrastructure, clean energy, healthcare, and economic policies. She highlights the administration's commitment to addressing issues like veterans' care, fentanyl, cancer, and insulin affordability, emphasizing ongoing work rather than new policy announcements at this time.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states the President has done more in three years than many presidents in two terms.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE highlights implementation of infrastructure, CHIPS, and Inflation Reduction Act.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasizes focus on veterans, fentanyl, cancer, and insulin cost caps.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE notes no new policy announcements but ongoing efforts to strengthen democracy and economy.
#### Immigration and Border Security Negotiations
The administration discusses ongoing bipartisan negotiations in the Senate to address border security and immigration reform, emphasizing the need for both policy changes and funding. Press Secretary Jean-Pierre criticizes House Republicans for obstructing progress and highlights the President's confidence in Secretary Mayorkas and Vice President Harris's roles in managing the situation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE confirms bipartisan Senate talks on border security and immigration policy.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE criticizes House Republicans for blocking efforts and reducing Border Patrol agents.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE affirms President Biden's confidence in Secretary Mayorkas and Vice President Harris.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE acknowledges the immigration system has been broken for decades and needs reform.
#### Federal Debt and Economic Policy
Press Secretary Jean-Pierre attributes 90% of the increase in federal debt over two decades to Republican tax cuts, highlighting President Biden's efforts to reduce the deficit through legislation targeting prescription drug costs and tax enforcement. She contrasts the administration's approach with Republican proposals that would increase the debt.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE states Republican tax cuts are responsible for 90% of debt increase as a share of the economy.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE cites legislation signed by the President to lower the deficit by $1 trillion.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasizes efforts to crack down on wealthy tax cheats and reduce prescription drug costs.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE criticizes GOP proposals that would add over $3 trillion to the debt.
