### Overview
The gaggle, led by Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre, focuses on President Biden’s trip to Palo Alto to highlight over $600 million in climate adaptation funding at the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve, with Governor Gavin Newsom and other leaders joining to showcase community-driven climate solutions and Biden’s broader climate and clean energy jobs agenda. Jean-Pierre situates the trip in the context of Juneteenth, noting Biden’s role in making it a federal holiday and his response to a deadly shooting at an Illinois Juneteenth celebration, where he has offered federal assistance and continues to press for stronger gun reforms, including an assault weapons ban, while pointing to his bipartisan gun safety law and more than two dozen executive actions. On foreign policy, she characterizes Secretary Blinken’s meetings with President Xi and other Chinese officials as candid and constructive, emphasizing the administration’s goal of open communication, competition without conflict, and an unchanged One China policy, while deferring detailed questions to State and confirming Biden will receive a full briefing when Blinken returns. She also underscores the White House’s ongoing efforts to manage Russia’s war in Ukraine—still assessing responsibility for the dam destruction while holding Russia broadly accountable and providing USAID-backed relief—and refers reporters to State on a West Bank raid and declines comment on a new Russian dirty bomb allegation. Domestically, Jean-Pierre defends Biden’s climate record amid questions about the Mountain Valley Pipeline, affirms a strong working relationship with Governor Newsom and continued engagement on AI with tech leaders, criticizes Senator Tuberville’s hold on military promotions as harmful to national security, and details an intensive “full-court press” to confirm Julie Su as labor secretary, presenting an administration actively advancing its climate, national security, labor, and gun safety priorities on multiple fronts.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Biden California climate adaptation trip
Karine Jean-Pierre outlines President Biden’s trip to Palo Alto, California, where he will visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve and announce over $600 million in climate adaptation funding. She explains that the preserve, a former landfill turned salt marsh, exemplifies community-driven climate solutions that leverage nature to combat climate change. The funding, part of the President’s Investing in America agenda, aims to protect coastal and Great Lakes communities and the power grid from worsening climate impacts. Biden will also highlight his broader climate actions, job creation in clean energy, and environmental protection for future generations.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre states that President Biden will visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve in Palo Alto and announce more than $600 million in climate adaptation projects nationwide.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre explains that the preserve sits on what was once a city landfill and is now a leading example of community-driven climate solutions that harness nature to fight the climate crisis.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre notes that the new funding will protect coastal and Great Lakes communities and the national power grid from sea level rise, flooding, wildfires, and extreme heat.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre says the President will discuss his historic actions to combat climate change, create good-paying clean energy jobs, and protect the environment for future generations.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre adds that California Governor Gavin Newsom, Congresswoman Anna Eshoo, environmental justice leaders, and youth climate leaders will join the President at the event.
