### Overview
In this briefing, MS. Jean-Pierre outlines President Biden's upcoming trip to California to announce significant climate adaptation funding and highlights the administration's commitment to combating climate change and fostering clean energy jobs. The President and First Lady observe Juneteenth with reflection on its historical significance and recent related tragedies. Secretary Blinken's constructive diplomatic engagement with Chinese leaders is emphasized, with the administration seeking managed competition without conflict and maintaining open communication channels. The White House addresses ongoing challenges including military promotion holds by Senator Tuberville, the push to confirm Julie Su as Labor Secretary, and the assessment of Russia's responsibility for a dam explosion in Ukraine. The administration reiterates its strong stance on gun violence prevention, advocating for federal legislation including an assault weapons ban, while continuing executive actions to enhance community safety. The briefing also touches on AI engagement plans during the President's Silicon Valley visit and defers to the State Department on comments regarding Israeli actions in the West Bank.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### President's California Climate Trip
The President is traveling to Palo Alto, California, to visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve and announce over $600 million in funding for climate adaptation projects nationwide. This visit highlights community-driven climate solutions and the administration's commitment to combating climate change impacts such as sea level rise and wildfires. The event will include California Governor Gavin Newsom, Congresswoman Anna Eshoo, environmental justice leaders, and youth climate advocates.
- MS. Jean-Pierre states the President will announce more than $600 million in climate adaptation funding during the California trip.
- The preserve visited was formerly a city landfill and exemplifies community-driven climate solutions, according to MS. Jean-Pierre.
- Governor Gavin Newsom and Congresswoman Anna Eshoo will join the President at the event.
- The funding aims to protect coastal and Great Lake communities and the power grid from climate change effects like flooding and extreme heat.
#### Juneteenth Observance and Reflection
The President and First Lady acknowledge Juneteenth as a national holiday commemorating the end of slavery and emphasizing the ongoing work toward equality. They also express condolences and offer assistance following a recent shooting at an Illinois Juneteenth celebration. The administration stresses the importance of remembering history and continuing efforts to deliver on America's promise of equality.
- MS. Jean-Pierre notes the President and First Lady's thoughts are with victims of the Illinois Juneteenth shooting.
- The President established Juneteenth as a national holiday to mark emancipation and the promise of equality.
- The administration emphasizes that commemorating Juneteenth involves remembering history and not erasing it.
- The President has stated that America must not rest until it delivers on the promise of equality for all.

#### Gun Violence and Legislative Efforts
Following a recent shooting at an Illinois Juneteenth celebration, the President continues to advocate for federal gun reform legislation. He highlights past successes like the bipartisan gun violence prevention law and stresses the urgency of addressing gun violence, which remains the leading cause of death among children. The administration has taken numerous executive actions and calls on Congress to act to keep communities safe.
- MS. Jean-Pierre recounts the President's recent forceful remarks on gun violence in Connecticut.
- The President signed bipartisan gun violence prevention legislation last year after 30 years of effort.
- Gun violence is the leading cause of death among children, emphasizing the need for action.
- The administration has implemented over two dozen executive actions to address gun safety.
- The President urges Congress to pass further federal legislation to reduce gun violence.
#### Assault Weapons Ban Impact
The President believes that the previous assault weapons ban effectively reduced gun violence during its enforcement period. After its expiration, gun violence increased significantly. He advocates for reinstating such a ban, describing assault weapons as weapons of war that should not be accessible in civilian settings like schools and churches.
- MS. Jean-Pierre cites the President's view that the assault weapons ban lowered violence during its tenure.
- The expiration of the ban correlated with a tripling of gun violence, according to the President.
- The President calls assault weapons weapons of war that should not be on American streets or in public places.
- The administration continues to press Congress to enact an assault weapons ban.
