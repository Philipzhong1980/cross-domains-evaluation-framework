### Overview
  During a Juneteenth briefing, Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre outlines President Biden’s upcoming Palo Alto trip to announce more than $600 million in climate adaptation funding and to visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve, frames the White House’s ongoing climate agenda, and notes Governor Newsom will join the President. Reporters press on foreign policy after Secretary Blinken’s meetings in China; Jean-Pierre describes those talks as candid and constructive, emphasizes the need to maintain open channels to reduce miscalculation, says the President is receiving NSC updates and will get a full briefing when Blinken returns, and reiterates no change to the U.S. One China policy. The briefing also covers domestic political fights and personnel matters: Jean-Pierre condemns Senator Tuberville’s hold on military promotions as harmful to national security and describes a sustained White House effort to confirm Julie Su, including nightly war-room calls and outreach to key senators. On Ukraine, the administration is assessing a dam explosion while USAID provides assistance and the press secretary says Russian forces bear responsibility for destruction caused by the war; on Israel she refers questions to the State Department. Finally, Jean-Pierre connects Juneteenth observance to a recent Illinois shooting, notes the President’s offer of federal assistance, highlights the administration’s executive actions on gun violence, and reiterates the White House call for Congress to act on reforms. Reporters on the trip also raise AI engagement plans for Silicon Valley, which the White House says will be detailed later.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Palo Alto climate trip
  The White House previews President Biden’s trip to Palo Alto where he will visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve and announce more than $600 million for climate adaptation projects. The funding is presented as part of the Investing in America agenda and is intended to protect coastal and Great Lakes communities and critical infrastructure from sea level rise, flooding, wildfires, and extreme heat. The event will include state and local leaders and environmental justice and youth climate leaders and is framed as an example of community-driven climate solutions.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President will visit the Lucy Evans Baylands Nature Preserve in Palo Alto and announce more than $600 million in funding for climate adaptation projects.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the funding is part of the Investing in America agenda to protect coastal and Great Lakes communities and the nation’s power grid from worsening climate impacts.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the preserve transformed a former landfill into a community-driven example of harnessing nature to fight the climate crisis.
  #### Juneteenth observance and shooting
  The briefing connects Juneteenth observance to recent violence, noting the President and First Lady are thinking of those killed and injured in an Illinois Juneteenth celebration shooting and offering federal assistance to state and local leaders. The White House reiterates the President’s framing of Juneteenth as both a remembrance of slavery and a call to continue working toward equality. The administration signals continued engagement with communities affected by the shooting.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President and First Lady are thinking of those killed and injured in the Illinois Juneteenth celebration shooting and that the White House has offered assistance to state and local leaders.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President established Juneteenth as a national holiday to mark both the history of slavery and a promise to continue delivering on equality.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE quoted the President saying we must not rest until we deliver the promise of America for all Americans.
  #### Blinken meeting with Xi
  Reporters ask about Secretary of State Blinken’s meetings with President Xi and other Chinese officials; the press secretary describes the conversations as candid, substantive, and constructive without providing issue-level details. She emphasizes the Secretary’s message about maintaining open channels of communication to reduce the risk of miscalculation and says the administration will rely on the State Department for a full readout. The President is receiving regular updates from the National Security Council and will get a full briefing once the Secretary returns.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Secretary Blinken had candid, substantive, and constructive conversations with President Xi and Director Wang Yi.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Blinken emphasized the importance of maintaining open channels of communication across the full range of issues to reduce the risk of miscalculation.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE referred reporters to the State Department for a full readout and said the President is being regularly updated by his National Security Council.
  #### Military-to-military communications
  Reporters probe obstacles to military-to-military communications between the U.S. and China, including whether sanctions or other actions are impeding dialogue. The press secretary declines to detail specifics and points to Secretary Blinken’s public comments, reiterating the administration’s emphasis on diplomacy and responsible management of competition to avoid conflict. No operational or policy specifics are provided from the White House press office in this briefing.
- Josh asked whether sanctions on a Chinese defense official are obstructing military-to-military communications.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she would refer to Secretary Blinken’s briefing for specifics and declined to provide additional operational detail.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration emphasizes diplomacy and managing competition responsibly to prevent the relationship from veering into conflict.
  #### President-Xi engagement timing
  Reporters ask whether President Biden has spoken with Secretary Blinken after the meetings and whether a Biden-Xi call or meeting is imminent. The press secretary says the President is being kept abreast by the NSC and will receive a full briefing once Blinken returns, but she has no timeline to announce for a President-to-President engagement. The administration frames the contact as ongoing but does not confirm any near-term bilateral call or meeting.
- Josh asked whether the President had spoken with Secretary Blinken after his meetings in China.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President is being kept regularly updated by the National Security Council and will receive a full briefing when Secretary Blinken returns to the U.S.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she has no timeline to announce for a Biden-Xi meeting or call.
  #### Relations after Blinken trip
  Reporters ask whether U.S.-China relations are improved following Blinken’s meetings; the press secretary characterizes the talks as a constructive step and stresses the role of diplomacy. She reiterates the administration’s aim for competition rather than conflict and says the engagement is important for U.S. interests. The office stops short of declaring a definitive thaw in relations.
- Josh asked whether relations are better now after Secretary Blinken’s meetings.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE called the meeting a constructive step and said that constructive diplomacy is important for America’s interests.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration wants competition, not conflict, and views the talks as a positive step forward.
  #### Taiwan and One China
  Reporters question whether China is committed to maintaining the status quo on Taiwan; the press secretary declines to speak for China but reiterates U.S. priorities. She says the Secretary underscored the importance of peace and stability across the Taiwan Strait and confirms no change to the longstanding U.S. One China policy. The White House frames its role as upholding stability and defending U.S. interests.
- Josh asked whether China is committed to the status quo with Taiwan.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she cannot speak for China but reiterated the U.S. commitment to peace and stability across the Taiwan Strait.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said there has been no change to the longstanding U.S. One China policy.
  #### Relationship with Governor Newsom
  Reporters seek context on the President’s relationship with California Governor Gavin Newsom ahead of the Palo Alto event. The press secretary describes a frequent and positive working relationship, noting Newsom’s support for the President’s agenda and economic policies. She confirms the Governor will greet the President at the climate event in California.
- Josh asked how often the President speaks with Governor Newsom and about the nature of their relationship.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President and Governor Newsom have a very good working relationship and that Newsom has supported the President’s economic agenda.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Governor Newsom will greet the President and attend the climate event in California.
  #### Climate policy and pipelines
  Reporters ask how the President is engaging climate advocates upset by allowing permitting to continue for the Mountain Valley Pipeline. The press secretary points to the administration’s broader record on climate, saying the President has driven the most ambitious climate agenda in U.S. history and preserved the Inflation Reduction Act during budget negotiations. The White House frames its response as continuing to create clean energy jobs and pushing its climate agenda while acknowledging advocates’ concerns.
- Josh asked how the President is engaging climate advocates disappointed by the Mountain Valley Pipeline decision.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President is driving the most ambitious climate agenda in American history and highlighted the preservation of the Inflation Reduction Act.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration remains committed to creating clean energy jobs and continuing efforts to fight climate change.
  #### AI engagement in Silicon Valley
  Reporters ask whether the President will meet tech leaders or engage on AI during the Silicon Valley trip. The press secretary says the administration is committed to fostering responsible AI, mitigating risks, and has previously met with technology CEOs on the issue. She says more trip details will be released later but offers no firm schedule of meetings with tech leaders at this briefing.
- Josh asked whether the President will meet tech leaders or engage on AI during the trip to Silicon Valley.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration remains committed to fostering responsible AI and actively working to mitigate its risks.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President and Vice President have met with CEOs about AI and that more information about AI engagements on the trip will be shared later.
  #### Senator Tuberville promotions hold
  Reporters ask whether the White House has recently spoken with Senator Tuberville after he continues to block military promotions over DOD abortion policy. The press secretary says she has no recent conversations to read out, emphasizes respect for private congressional conversations, and condemns the senator’s action as hurting national security and unacceptable. The administration urges moving forward with key defense nominations.
- Josh asked whether anyone at the White House has recently spoken with Senator Tuberville about his hold on military promotions.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said she has no recent conversations to read out and said the White House tries to respect private conversations with Congress.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the administration has been very clear that Senator Tuberville’s actions are hurting national security and are unacceptable and called for moving forward with DOD nominations.
  #### Julie Su nomination effort
  Reporters press about the lack of a Senate vote on Julie Su’s nomination for Labor Secretary and whether the White House is reaching undecided senators. The press secretary affirms full commitment to her confirmation, describes nightly senior-official war room calls and outreach led by Chief of Staff Jeff Zients and Director Louisa Terrell to Schumer and other senators, and cites outside group and union support. The campaign for confirmation remains active with no vote scheduled at the time of the briefing.
- Josh asked whether the administration is committed to a confirmed Labor Secretary and whether the White House is engaging uncommitted senators on Julie Su’s nomination.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the White House is fully committed to confirming Julie Su and that senior officials hold nightly war room calls to advance her nomination.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Chief of Staff Jeff Zients and Director Louisa Terrell are speaking regularly with Leader Schumer and other key senators and that outside groups, including 55 labor unions, are vocally supporting Su’s confirmation.
  #### Dam explosion in Ukraine

She notes USAID partners are providing on-the-ground aid to affected civilians and reiterates that Russian forces controlled the dam and therefore bear ultimate responsibility for destruction caused by the war. The White House frames its response in terms of assessment and humanitarian assistance.
- Josh asked whether the U.S. government believes Russia is the most likely culprit behind the dam explosion in Ukraine.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the U.S. is still assessing what happened and is in touch with Ukraine while USAID partners provide aid on the ground to impacted civilians.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said Russian forces had control over the dam and bear ultimate responsibility for destruction caused by the war.
  #### Russian allegations and response

  #### Illinois shooting and gun reform
  Reporters ask whether the President will convene Congress to advance gun reform after a mass shooting in Illinois; the press secretary reiterates the President’s recent forceful remarks on gun violence and his call for congressional action. She says the President has tracked the shooting, offered federal assistance to state and local officials, and highlights that the administration has taken more than two dozen executive actions on gun violence. The White House also points to the historical effect of an assault weapons ban and continues to press for legislative solutions.
- Josh asked whether the President plans to convene members of Congress to advance gun reform following the Illinois shooting.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President is calling on Congress to act and that he used forceful remarks to underline the need to reduce gun violence.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President’s team has reached out to state and local officials to offer federal assistance and that the President has taken more than two dozen executive actions on gun violence.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE said the President believes an assault weapons ban previously reduced violence and continues to call for legislative action to keep communities safe.
