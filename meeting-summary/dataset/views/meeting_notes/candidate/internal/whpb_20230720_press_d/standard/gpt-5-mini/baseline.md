### Overview
  The briefing covers a range of policy and personnel issues led by MS. Dalton with questions from reporters Darlene, Akayla, and Eugene. The administration previews President Biden’s Philadelphia trip to highlight Bidenomics, a steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel tied to U.S. offshore wind job creation, and a first-ever Gulf offshore wind sale, while stressing large private-sector clean energy investments. The White House reports active multi-channel efforts with U.S. agencies and international partners to determine Private King’s whereabouts in North Korea and commits to follow up on a separate Sweden-Iraq diplomatic report. Officials express deep concern about recent Russian missile and drone attacks on Black Sea ports and the resulting food-security impacts, note TSMC’s Arizona plant delay while pointing to CHIPS Act workforce provisions as a remedy, and defend the President’s economic accomplishments while acknowledging it will take time for Americans to feel the recovery. The spokesperson has no confirmation on a reported Camp David trilateral meeting and urges Senator Tuberville to lift his hold on military nominations because of serious operational and family impacts.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Philadelphia trip and Bidenomics
  The briefing opens with details about President Biden’s trip to Philadelphia, where he will highlight Bidenomics and visit the Philly Shipyard for a steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel that will support offshore wind construction. The administration frames the visit as evidence of American-made clean energy projects, large private-sector investment, and union job creation. The President will also announce the first-ever offshore wind sale in the Gulf of Mexico and will be joined by local and congressional officials.
- MS. DALTON says President Biden will visit the Philly Shipyard for a steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel that will help build offshore wind farms.
- MS. DALTON says the Acadia and related offshore wind projects are expected to support hundreds of new union jobs.
- MS. DALTON says the administration is attracting more than $500 billion in private-sector manufacturing and clean energy investments tied to Bidenomics.
- MS. DALTON says the President will announce the first-ever offshore wind sale in the Gulf of Mexico during the trip.
- MS. DALTON lists Congresswoman Mary Gay Scanlon, Mayor Jim Kenney, and Representatives Brendan Boyle, Madeleine Dean, Chrissy Houlahan, Donald Norcross, and Joe Courtney as planned attendees.
  #### Private King in North Korea
  Reporters ask about the status of Private King and where he is being held in North Korea. The White House reports ongoing, multi-channel efforts with U.S. agencies and international partners to ascertain his whereabouts and well-being, and says outreach to the KPA is underway. The administration’s priority is to determine his condition and return him to the United States as quickly as possible.
- Darlene asks whether the U.S. is any closer to determining where Private King is being held in North Korea.
- MS. DALTON says the White House remains in close contact with DOD, the State Department, the U.N., Swedish partners, and South Korean partners to ascertain Private King’s whereabouts and well-being.
- MS. DALTON says the U.S. has reached out through multiple channels to the KPA to try to obtain more information.
- MS. DALTON says the top goal is to ascertain Private King’s well-being and get him home as quickly as possible.
  #### Sweden-Iraq diplomatic question
  A reporter asks whether the White House has a comment on reports that Iraq expelled Sweden’s ambassador over another Quran burning and whether that could affect Sweden’s NATO bid. The spokeswoman says she has not seen the reports and will take the question for follow-up. No substantive comment or assessment on NATO implications is provided during the briefing.
- Darlene asks if the White House has a comment on Iraq expelling the Swedish ambassador and whether it could affect Sweden’s NATO bid.
- MS. DALTON says she has not seen those news reports and will need to follow up on the question.
  #### Black Sea attacks and grain
  The briefing addresses rising concerns about Russian missile and drone strikes on Black Sea ports and the potential targeting of civilian vessels carrying grain. The White House reports three consecutive days of attacks, links them to attempts to reinstate a blockade of Ukrainian ports, and highlights immediate food-security impacts, including destroyed grain. The administration expresses deep concern and says it will continue efforts to support Ukraine and mitigate global food-security consequences.
- Darlene asks whether the administration is concerned that Russian attacks on grain shipments could draw other countries into the conflict.
- MS. DALTON says the administration is deeply concerned about recent attacks in the Black Sea and declines to engage in hypotheticals about escalation.
- MS. DALTON says the U.S. has seen three straight days of Russian missile and drone attacks on Black Sea port cities that the administration views as part of an effort to reinstate a blockade of Ukrainian ports.
- MS. DALTON says the attacks have significant food-security impacts beyond the region and that 60,000 tons of grain have already been destroyed this week.
- MS. DALTON says intelligence and reporting indicate Russia may consider civilian vessels in the Black Sea as possible vessels carrying military cargo, which raises concern about potential attacks on shipping.
  #### TSMC Arizona delay
  Reporters ask about TSMC delaying its Arizona manufacturing plant until 2025 due to worker shortages and higher costs. The White House defers to TSMC on construction timelines but emphasizes that the CHIPS and Science Act includes workforce development provisions designed to address labor needs. The administration expresses continued confidence that CHIPS-related workforce provisions will help secure the skilled labor necessary for such projects to proceed.
- Akayla notes that TSMC is delaying its Arizona manufacturing plant until 2025 because of worker shortages and higher prices and asks whether the President is concerned about delayed priorities.
- MS. DALTON says TSMC is best positioned to speak to its construction timelines.
- MS. DALTON says the CHIPS and Science Act contains workforce development provisions that the administration believes will enable the U.S. to build the skilled labor pool needed for projects like TSMC’s Arizona plant.
- MS. DALTON says the administration remains confident that CHIPS-related provisions will help companies follow through on their investments in America.
  #### Public reception of Bidenomics
  Reporters question why Bidenomics and the President’s economic record are not resonating with the public despite policy accomplishments. The White House points to legislative achievements—the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, the Inflation Reduction Act, and the CHIPS and Science Act—and emphasizes ongoing outreach to explain those results. Officials acknowledge recent global shocks (the pandemic and Russia’s invasion of Ukraine) that affected inflation and say it will take time for Americans to fully feel the recovery’s benefits despite positive macroeconomic indicators.
- Akayla asks why the President’s economic message is not resonating with the public given polling on economic management.
- MS. DALTON points to the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, the Inflation Reduction Act, and the CHIPS and Science Act as major accomplishments that enjoy broad public support.
- MS. DALTON says the administration is actively getting out to talk to American people about how Bidenomics is delivering results.
- MS. DALTON says the President inherited pandemic-related economic disruption and that Russia’s invasion of Ukraine contributed to global inflationary pressure, which affects public perception.
- MS. DALTON says the economy shows signs of resilience—record low inflation, record low unemployment, and rising wages—but says it will take time for Americans to feel the full impact.
  #### Camp David meeting query
  A reporter asks to confirm press reporting that President Biden will meet with Japanese and South Korean counterparts at Camp David in August. The White House spokesperson says she has nothing to confirm at this time. No further information or scheduling confirmation is provided during the briefing.
- Eugene asks whether the White House can confirm reports that President Biden will meet with his Japanese and South Korean counterparts at Camp David in August.
- MS. DALTON says she does not have anything to confirm regarding that report.
  #### Senator Tuberville hold
  Reporters press on Senator Tuberville’s continued hold on military nominations and the NDAA process after his statement that a failed Senate vote will not end the hold. The White House urges Tuberville to stop blocking nominations and calls on Senate Republicans to oppose that behavior, citing concrete consequences to military leadership and families. The spokesperson highlights immediate impacts, including the absence of a Marine Commandant for the first time in a century and ripple effects across military families.
- Eugene asks what the administration’s next steps are given Senator Tuberville’s refusal to lift his hold after a failed Senate vote.
- MS. DALTON says the administration will continue to call on Senator Tuberville to stop playing politics with the military and the NDAA process.
- MS. DALTON says the hold has already produced consequences for the military, including the absence of a Marine Commandant for the first time in 100 years.
- MS. DALTON says the administration is concerned about ripple effects to hundreds of military families and urges Republican senators and House members to stand up to the behavior.
