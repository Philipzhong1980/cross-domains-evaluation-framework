### Overview
The meeting focused on several key topics including President Biden's upcoming visit to Philadelphia to promote Bidenomics and announce significant clean energy initiatives, such as the steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel and the first offshore wind sale in the Gulf of Mexico. The White House provided updates on efforts to locate Private King in North Korea, emphasizing ongoing multi-agency and international coordination. Concerns were raised about recent Russian attacks on Ukrainian Black Sea ports and potential threats to grain shipments, highlighting global food security risks. The administration addressed TSMC's delay in its Arizona plant construction, expressing confidence in workforce development measures under the CHIPS and Science Act. Discussion also covered the public's lukewarm reception to Bidenomics despite legislative successes, with the Philadelphia trip intended to better communicate economic progress. The White House declined to confirm reports of an upcoming Camp David summit with Japanese and South Korean leaders. Finally, the administration criticized Senator Tuberville's continued hold on the NDAA nominations, underscoring the detrimental impact on military readiness and calling for bipartisan opposition to the obstruction.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Biden's Philadelphia Visit
The President is traveling to Philadelphia to highlight Bidenomics and its role in advancing America's clean energy future. The visit includes a steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel, which will support offshore wind farm construction and create union jobs. This event underscores the administration's commitment to American manufacturing and clean energy investments.
- MS. Dalton states President Biden will deliver remarks on Bidenomics fueling America's clean energy future.
- MS. Dalton notes the President will attend a steel-cutting ceremony for the Acadia vessel at Philly Shipyard.
- MS. Dalton highlights that the Acadia and offshore wind farms will support hundreds of new union jobs.
- MS. Dalton mentions Great Lakes Dredge & Dock credits Biden-Harris administration's policies for entering the offshore wind market.
- MS. Dalton announces the President will reveal the first-ever offshore wind sale in the Gulf of Mexico during the visit.
- MS. Dalton lists Congresswoman Mary Gay Scanlon, Mayor Jim Kenney, and several Representatives joining the President in Philadelphia.
#### Private King in North Korea
The White House is actively seeking information on Private King's whereabouts and well-being in North Korea. Multiple government agencies and international partners are engaged in efforts to ascertain his status. The administration's priority remains securing his safe and swift return to the United States.
- MS. Dalton reports ongoing close contact with DOD, State Department, U.N., Swedish and South Korean partners regarding Private King.
- MS. Dalton confirms multiple outreach attempts to the Korean People's Army to obtain information on Private King.
- MS. Dalton emphasizes the top goal is to ascertain Private King's well-being and facilitate his return home promptly.
#### Iraq Expelling Swedish Ambassador
The White House has not yet reviewed reports about Iraq expelling the Swedish ambassador over a Quran burning incident. Consequently, no immediate comment is available regarding potential impacts on Sweden's NATO bid. The administration intends to follow up once more information is obtained.
- MS. Dalton states she has not seen the news reports about Iraq expelling the Swedish ambassador.
- MS. Dalton commits to following up on the situation and providing information later.
#### Russian Attacks on Black Sea Grain Shipments
The administration expresses deep concern over recent Russian missile and drone attacks on Ukrainian Black Sea port cities, which threaten global food security. Russia's potential targeting of civilian grain vessels exacerbates the risk. The U.S. is committed to supporting Ukraine and mitigating the broader impact of these attacks.
- MS. Dalton describes three consecutive days of Russian attacks on Black Sea port cities affecting food security.
- MS. Dalton notes Russia's indications of preparing to attack civilian vessels carrying grain in the Black Sea.
- MS. Dalton explains Russia considers any civilian vessel in the Black Sea a potential military cargo carrier.
- MS. Dalton reports 60,000 tons of grain have been destroyed this week due to these attacks.
- MS. Dalton affirms the U.S. will continue efforts to support Ukraine and global partners affected by these attacks.
#### TSMC Arizona Plant Delay
TSMC announced a delay in its Arizona manufacturing plant construction until 2025, citing worker shortages and higher costs. The administration remains confident that workforce development provisions in the CHIPS and Science Act will address labor challenges and support such investments. The delay does not diminish the historic nature of TSMC's commitment to U.S. manufacturing.
- MS. Dalton defers to TSMC for construction timeline specifics.
- MS. Dalton highlights the CHIPS and Science Act's role in workforce development to support projects like TSMC's.
- MS. Dalton expresses confidence in having the skilled labor needed to fulfill manufacturing investments despite delays.
#### Public Reception of Bidenomics
Despite significant legislative achievements like the Bipartisan Infrastructure Law, Inflation Reduction Act, and CHIPS and Science Act, public approval of Biden's economic management remains low. The administration attributes this to lingering effects of the pandemic and global inflation pressures. The Philadelphia visit aims to communicate the tangible benefits of Bidenomics to the American people.
- MS. Dalton cites broad bipartisan support for major economic laws passed under Biden.
- MS. Dalton acknowledges the pandemic and global inflation have affected public perception.
- MS. Dalton points to record low inflation and unemployment alongside rising wages as economic successes.
- MS. Dalton states the administration's goal is to demonstrate how Bidenomics delivers results during the Philadelphia trip.
#### Upcoming Camp David Summit
Reports suggest President Biden will meet with Japanese and South Korean leaders at Camp David in August. The White House has not confirmed these plans and declines to comment at this time.
- MS. Dalton states no confirmation is available regarding the Camp David meeting with Japanese and South Korean counterparts.
#### Senator Tuberville's NDAA Hold
Senator Tuberville maintains his hold on the National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) nominations, refusing to relent despite a failed Senate vote. The administration condemns this as politicizing military readiness, highlighting the absence of a Marine Commandant for the first time in a century and adverse effects on military families. Calls are made for Republican colleagues to oppose this obstruction.
- MS. Dalton urges Senator Tuberville to stop politicizing military nominations and the NDAA process.
- MS. Dalton notes the historic bipartisan nature of the NDAA and the negative consequences of the hold.
- MS. Dalton highlights the lack of a Marine Commandant for the first time in 100 years due to the hold.
- MS. Dalton calls on Senate and House Republicans to stand against such political interference.
