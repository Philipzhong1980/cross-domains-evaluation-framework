### Overview
The meeting featured key briefings from National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan and Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre on pressing domestic and international issues. The administration highlighted progress in gun violence reduction through the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act and announced EPA methane emission standards as part of its climate agenda. Sullivan detailed ongoing diplomatic efforts regarding the Israel-Hamas conflict, emphasizing hostage negotiations, humanitarian aid to Gaza, and Israel's military operations balanced with civilian protection. The U.S. condemned attacks on commercial vessels in the Red Sea attributed to Iranian-enabled Houthis and discussed potential maritime security responses. Urgent congressional action was underscored to continue military aid to Ukraine, with the administration warning that failure to fund would aid Russian aggression. The White House also addressed questions on election infrastructure, domestic surveillance, rising antisemitism, and recent controversies, maintaining a consistent message of protecting democratic processes and vulnerable communities. Throughout, officials emphasized ongoing engagement with Congress and international partners to address these complex challenges.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Gun Violence Reduction Efforts
The administration highlighted progress in combating illegal gun trafficking and gun violence through the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act. This legislation has led to over 250 prosecutions for illegal gun purchases and trafficking, strengthened background checks, and funded red flag laws in 21 states. The administration emphasized ongoing commitments to fund law enforcement and called on Congress to enact further gun safety measures.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE stated the Department of Justice prosecuted over 250 individuals for illegal gun purchases under the Bipartisan Safer Communities Act.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE noted 21 states have implemented red flag laws supported by federal funding.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasized President Biden's consistent funding for police and opposition to Republican proposals that would defund law enforcement.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE urged Congress to pass universal background checks, a national red flag law, and assault weapons bans.
#### Methane Emissions and Climate Action
The administration announced the EPA's final standards to significantly reduce methane emissions from oil and gas operations, aiming to prevent 1.5 billion metric tons of greenhouse gases. This action aligns with President Biden's historic climate agenda and the U.S.'s leadership role in global climate efforts, including at COP28.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE announced EPA's methane emission standards to reduce greenhouse gases and deliver health and economic benefits.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE highlighted methane cuts as critical to slowing climate change.
- MR. SULLIVAN noted Vice President Harris's participation at COP28 emphasizing the U.S.'s ambitious climate agenda.
#### Israel-Hamas Conflict and Hostage Negotiations
National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan detailed ongoing diplomatic efforts with Israel, Egypt, Qatar, and others regarding the conflict following Hamas's October 7th attack. The U.S. is focused on securing the release of hostages, sustaining humanitarian aid to Gaza, and supporting Israel's military operations while urging protection of civilians. Talks have stalled due to Hamas's refusal to release civilian women, which ended a hostage agreement and a pause in hostilities.
- MR. SULLIVAN described intensive calls with regional partners about hostages, humanitarian aid, and military operations.
- MR. SULLIVAN met with families of American hostages and emphasized the administration's commitment to their release.
- MR. SULLIVAN stated Hamas's refusal to release civilian women caused the end of the hostage agreement and pause in fighting.
- MR. SULLIVAN affirmed Israel's right to defend itself but stressed the responsibility to protect civilians.
#### Humanitarian Assistance to Gaza
The U.S. has provided $100 million in aid to Palestinians through UN agencies and other actors, with efforts to increase and sustain humanitarian assistance via the Rafah Border Crossing. Recent U.S. airlifts delivered medical supplies and food to Gaza civilians, and the administration continues to work on overcoming obstacles to aid delivery.
- MR. SULLIVAN announced $100 million in humanitarian aid to Palestinians including through UNRWA.
- MR. SULLIVAN reported successful U.S. airlift of over 54,000 pounds of medical and food aid to Gaza.
- MR. SULLIVAN confirmed the Rafah Crossing is open and aid trucks are moving into Gaza.
#### Attacks on Commercial Vessels in Red Sea
Four attacks targeted three commercial vessels in the Southern Red Sea, involving ships linked to 14 nations. The USS Carney responded by neutralizing three UAV threats. The U.S. attributes the attacks to Houthi forces enabled by Iran and is consulting with international partners on appropriate responses, including discussions about maritime task forces to protect shipping.
- MR. SULLIVAN confirmed attacks on commercial vessels in international waters in the Southern Red Sea.
- MR. SULLIVAN stated USS Carney took down three UAVs threatening the ship.
- MR. SULLIVAN attributed the attacks to Houthis with Iranian support.
- MR. SULLIVAN noted ongoing talks about forming maritime task forces with partner nations for safe passage.
#### U.S. Support for Ukraine and Congressional Funding
OMB Director Shalanda Young warned that without congressional action, U.S. resources to support Ukraine will be depleted by year-end. Sullivan emphasized the importance of bipartisan congressional support to continue military aid, which has enabled Ukraine to reclaim over 50% of occupied territory. The administration views failure to fund Ukraine as aiding Russian aggression and urges Congress to approve the President's supplemental request promptly.
- MR. SULLIVAN highlighted OMB Director Young's letter about urgent congressional action needed for Ukraine funding.
- MR. SULLIVAN stated that continued aid has halted Russian advances and supported Ukraine's military successes.
- MR. SULLIVAN said a vote against Ukraine aid effectively helps Putin's strategic position.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasized the national security importance of passing the supplemental funding.
#### U.S. Military Assistance and Monitoring in Israel
The U.S. maintains close communication with Israeli Defense Forces regarding military operations and the use of U.S.-provided weapons. Sullivan declined to specify quantities of weapons sent but confirmed ongoing coordination. The administration supports Israel's right to target Hamas while urging efforts to minimize civilian casualties and facilitate humanitarian aid.
- MR. SULLIVAN confirmed constant communication between U.S. and Israeli military regarding operations and weapons use.
- MR. SULLIVAN declined to provide specific numbers of weapons sent to Israel, deferring to the Defense Department.
- MR. SULLIVAN reiterated Israel's right to defend itself against Hamas while emphasizing civilian protection.
#### Protection of Civilians and Journalists in Conflict
The administration stresses the importance of minimizing civilian casualties in Israel-Gaza conflict and protecting journalists reporting from the region. Sullivan and other officials have publicly condemned civilian deaths and called for adherence to laws of war. The White House monitors the safety of journalists and recognizes their critical role in reporting facts on the ground.
- MR. SULLIVAN and other officials have stated that any civilian death is too many.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE emphasized the importance of protecting journalists and their ability to report safely.
- MR. SULLIVAN highlighted the expectation that Israel designate no-strike zones and facilitate humanitarian access.
#### Diplomatic Engagements and Regional Stability
Vice President Harris attended COP28 and engaged with regional leaders on climate and security issues. The administration is in ongoing discussions with Arab foreign ministers and partners about the conflict's long-term resolution, refugee resettlement, and regional peace. Sullivan noted no current talks on resettling Palestinians from Gaza to other countries and emphasized the need for a political horizon based on a two-state solution.
- MR. SULLIVAN reported Vice President Harris's meetings with leaders at COP28 and subsequent detailed briefings to the President.
- MR. SULLIVAN stated there are no active talks on resettling Palestinians from Gaza at this time.
- MR. SULLIVAN expressed the administration's commitment to a two-state solution and regional peace efforts.
#### Congressional Relations and Legislative Process
The administration maintains regular communication with congressional leaders regarding national security priorities, including Ukraine aid. While not commenting on specific negotiations or legislative provisions, officials emphasize the urgency of passing supplemental funding. The President has engaged with lawmakers and will continue to do so as needed to secure bipartisan support.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE and MR. SULLIVAN confirmed ongoing engagement with Congress on Ukraine supplemental funding.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declined to discuss specifics of border security negotiations linked to funding.
- MR. SULLIVAN expressed confidence in bipartisan support if votes are held on the supplemental package.
#### Domestic Issues: Voting and Surveillance
The White House addressed questions on election infrastructure and voting access, emphasizing efforts to make voting easier and protect voting rights without commenting on specific 2024 election plans. Regarding domestic surveillance, no detailed information was provided about Section 702 of FISA or related programs, with referrals made to the White House Counsel's Office.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE stated the administration supports making voting easier and protecting voting rights.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE declined to comment on specific election monitoring plans for 2024.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE deferred questions about domestic surveillance programs to the White House Counsel's Office.
#### Responses to Recent Controversies and Public Statements
The administration responded to inquiries about comments by Congresswoman Jayapal on sexual violence, reiterating a clear condemnation of Hamas's actions and the use of rape as a weapon. Questions about Hunter Biden's business dealings were referred to the White House Counsel's Office. The White House also addressed concerns about rising antisemitism and hate crimes, committing to protect affected communities.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE condemned Hamas's actions and sexual violence unequivocally.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE referred questions about Hunter Biden's business payments to the White House Counsel's Office.
- MS. JEAN-PIERRE acknowledged increased antisemitism and hate crimes and affirmed efforts to protect communities.
