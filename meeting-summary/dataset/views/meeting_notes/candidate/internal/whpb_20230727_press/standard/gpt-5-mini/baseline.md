### Overview
  Carried by press secretary Karine Jean-Pierre with Admiral John Kirby handling foreign policy, the briefing covers a range of domestic and international issues: the White House previews a package of actions to protect communities from extreme heat and summarizes a Methane Summit aimed at reducing emissions and creating union jobs, and it announces the intent to nominate Martin O’Malley to head the Social Security Administration. On foreign policy, Admiral Kirby previews President Biden’s meeting with Italian Prime Minister Meloni and emphasizes U.S.-Italy cooperation on Ukraine, NATO, China, and regional challenges; he addresses unfolding unrest in Niger by condemning detention efforts and urging the release of President Bazoum; and he discusses the implications of Russia pulling out of the Black Sea grain deal while describing allied efforts to find overland alternatives. The briefing outlines plans for F-16 training for Ukrainian pilots in Romania and Denmark, reiterates that the U.S. discourages Americans from fighting in Ukraine (citing the Trevor Reed situation), and expresses concern about potential Russian outreach to the DPRK. It also touches on domestic political and legal matters: the White House reiterates that Hunter Biden’s case is an independent DOJ matter, condemns Senator Tuberville’s hold on military promotions as harmful to families and readiness while defending DoD policies to enable service members’ access to reproductive care, and notes DOJ appeals of a court injunction on new asylum rules. Additional topics include the administration’s support for an automaker EV-charging coalition as an example of Bidenomics, concern over a reported Russian close pass on a U.S. drone in Syria, and the President’s upcoming public outreach on mental health and an intended but undated visit to East Palestine.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Extreme heat protections
  Ms. Jean-Pierre previews additional White House actions to protect communities from extreme heat, including investments in NOAA weather prediction and Department of Interior grants for drinking water in the West. The announcement accompanies an event where FEMA and NOAA leaders and mayors from heat-impacted cities will discuss local responses. This is part of the administration’s broader climate agenda tied to the Inflation Reduction Act and related initiatives.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said President Biden will announce actions tomorrow to protect communities from extreme heat, including NOAA investments and Department of Interior grants.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said FEMA Administrator Deanne Criswell and NOAA Administrator Dr. Rick Spinrad will speak at the event about protecting Americans from extreme heat.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the mayors of Phoenix and San Antonio will join to discuss local impacts and responses.
  #### Social Security nomination
  Ms. Jean-Pierre announces President Biden’s intent to nominate former mayor and Maryland Governor Martin O’Malley as Commissioner of the Social Security Administration. The nomination is framed as consistent with the President’s commitment to strengthen and defend Social Security. The White House thanks the acting commissioner for steady interim leadership.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre announced the President’s intent to nominate Martin O’Malley as Commissioner of the Social Security Administration.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the President has fought to strengthen and defend Social Security since day one.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre expressed gratitude to the acting Social Security Commissioner for steady leadership during the interim period.
  #### Hunter Biden legal matter
  Ms. Jean-Pierre reiterates that Hunter Biden is a private citizen and that his legal matter was handled independently by the Department of Justice under a prosecutor appointed by the prior administration. She says the President and First Lady support their son as he rebuilds his life and refers detailed questions to the DOJ and Hunter’s representatives. The White House declines to comment further on prosecutorial decisions.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said Hunter Biden is a private citizen and that the case was handled independently by the Department of Justice.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the President and First Lady love and support their son as he continues to rebuild his life.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre referred further questions on the case to the Department of Justice and Hunter Biden’s legal representatives.
  #### Italy bilateral meeting preview
  Admiral John Kirby previews President Biden’s bilateral meeting with Italian Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni, emphasizing the strong U.S.-Italy partnership across NATO, G7, trade, and people-to-people ties. He says leaders will discuss Russia’s war in Ukraine, developments in North Africa, transatlantic coordination on China, and Italy’s upcoming 2024 G7 presidency. Kirby commits to a full readout after the meeting.
- Mr. Kirby said President Biden will host Prime Minister Meloni to reaffirm the strong U.S.-Italy bilateral relationship.
- Mr. Kirby said discussion topics will include support for Ukraine, regional challenges in North Africa, and coordination on the People’s Republic of China.
- Mr. Kirby said Italy hosts roughly 30,000 American service members and remains an important NATO and G7 partner.
  #### Military desegregation anniversary
  Admiral Kirby marks the 75th anniversary of President Truman’s desegregation of the U.S. military and uses the moment to emphasize the ongoing importance of diversity. He asserts that diversity strengthens the force, improves decision-making, and that continued work is necessary to break down barriers. The remarks connect historical courage to present-day efforts to ensure dignity and equal opportunity in the military.
- Mr. Kirby noted the 75th anniversary of President Truman’s decision to desegregate the U.S. military and honored generations of service by members of color.
- Mr. Kirby said diversity makes the U.S. military stronger and leads to better decision-making and operational performance.
- Mr. Kirby said more work remains to ensure all qualified service members receive equal treatment and opportunity.
  #### Niger political crisis
  Admiral Kirby addresses unfolding reports from Niger, condemning any detention or subversion of the democratically elected government and urging the presidential guard to release President Bazoum. He says the U.S. is closely monitoring developments and welcomes regional responses from ECOWAS and the African Union. Kirby advises Americans in Niger to exercise caution and says there are no evacuation plans at present.
- Mr. Kirby condemned efforts to detain or subvert Niger’s democratically elected government and urged the presidential guard to release President Bazoum.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States is closely monitoring the situation and welcomes strong statements and steps by ECOWAS and the African Union.
- Mr. Kirby advised Americans in Niger to be careful and said the U.S. has no evacuation plans at this time.
  #### Ukraine grain export impact
  Admiral Kirby warns that Russia’s withdrawal from the grain deal threatens supplies to developing countries — particularly in Africa — and has increased volatility in global food prices. He stresses that maritime Black Sea routes remain the most efficient way to move large quantities of grain and that the U.S. is working with EU and Ukrainian partners to identify overland alternatives, which are less efficient. The administration urges Russia to be honest with African leaders about consequences.
- Mr. Kirby said Russia’s decision to pull out of the grain deal risks food supplies for developing countries and has increased market volatility.
- Mr. Kirby said maritime Black Sea routes are the most efficient way to export Ukrainian grain and that overland options are being pursued with partners as a less efficient alternative.
- Mr. Kirby said he hopes Russian leaders will be candid with African counterparts about the consequences of their actions.
  #### F-16 training locations
  Kirby confirms plans to train Ukrainian pilots on F-16s in Romania and Denmark while noting that specific dates, syllabus length, and additional sites remain under negotiation. He adds that the United Kingdom is beginning English language training required for F-16 operations. The effort reflects coordinated allied work to accelerate Ukrainian pilot readiness.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States is pursuing F-16 training for Ukrainian pilots in Romania and Denmark while details such as timing and curriculum are still being worked out.
- Mr. Kirby said the United Kingdom is starting English-language training to prepare Ukrainian pilots for F-16 operations.
- Mr. Kirby said additional training sites remain under consideration as allies coordinate urgently.
  #### Unidentified aerial phenomena
  Admiral Kirby reiterates that the Department of Defense takes unidentified aerial phenomena (UAP) seriously, noting a Pentagon office collects and analyzes reports because some sightings have affected training and readiness. He stresses ongoing efforts to better understand the phenomena and supports transparency to the extent possible, while noting the government currently lacks definitive answers. Kirby declines to confirm specific claims about crash retrieval programs.
- Mr. Kirby said the Pentagon has an office stood up to collect and analyze UAP reports because some sightings have impacted training and readiness.
- Mr. Kirby said the administration supports transparency but currently does not have hard, definitive answers about the nature of these phenomena.
- Mr. Kirby declined to confirm allegations of a crash retrieval and reverse-engineering program raised in a Capitol Hill hearing.
  #### Americans fighting in Ukraine
  Kirby emphasizes the U.S. government actively discourages Americans from traveling to Ukraine to fight and says individuals who do so act on their own initiative. He notes that public messaging over the past 16 months has consistently urged Americans not to go and rejects any suggestion the U.S. encouraged such activity. Regarding Trevor Reed specifically, Kirby says he is aware of no U.S. communications with Russian officials about Reed since reports of his injury.
- Mr. Kirby said the U.S. actively and energetically urges Americans not to go fight in Ukraine and that those who go do so of their own accord.
- Mr. Kirby said he is aware of no communications with Russian officials about Trevor Reed since reports of his injury.
- Mr. Kirby said U.S. messaging has consistently and publicly discouraged Americans from participating in the conflict.
  #### Russia-North Korea outreach
  When asked about reports of Russian defense engagement with North Korea, Kirby says he cannot confirm specific reports but notes Russia is reaching out to other countries as it faces procurement and inventory challenges in Ukraine. He warns against any assistance that would enable Russia to continue its war and stresses that outside support for Russia would be concerning. Kirby frames such outreach as unsurprising given Russian needs but does not confirm concrete arms transfers.
- Mr. Kirby said he could not confirm reports of a direct arms deal between Russia and North Korea but noted Russian outreach to other countries for support.
- Mr. Kirby said no country should assist Russia in ways that enable more killing in Ukraine.
  #### U.S.-Mexico floating barrier
  In discussing DOJ language about floating barriers, Kirby says the recent U.S. delegation to Mexico focused on fentanyl and he is not certain whether the barriers were discussed in that trip. He adds that floating barriers are not helping U.S.-Mexico relations but that the bilateral relationship remains strong and cooperative. He refers detailed diplomatic concerns to the Department of Justice and appropriate diplomatic channels.
- Mr. Kirby said the U.S. delegation’s trip to Mexico was primarily focused on counternarcotics efforts, especially fentanyl.
- Mr. Kirby said he could not confirm whether Mexican officials raised the floating barrier issue during that trip and that the barriers are certainly not helping relations.
  #### Ukraine counteroffensive pace
  Kirby declines to second-guess Ukrainian operational tempo, noting President Zelenskyy has publicly said progress is slower than desired but that Ukrainian forces are advancing. He stresses the U.S. will not micromanage Ukrainian plans and says Washington has delivered the equipment Ukraine requested, with additional packages continuing. The U.S. frames its role as supplying tools and support rather than directing battlefield tactics.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States will not armchair-quarterback the Ukrainian military and that assessments of operational progress rest with Ukraine.
- Mr. Kirby said Ukraine has received equipment and assistance it requested and that the U.S. will continue providing capabilities to support their counteroffensive.
  #### Diplomacy and peace formula
  Kirby says the timing and decision to negotiate rests with President Zelenskyy and that the U.S. supports Ukraine’s prerogative on diplomacy. He notes the U.S. is working to help actualize Zelenskyy’s 10-point peace formula and that National Security Advisor Jake Sullivan recently discussed the topic with Ukrainian counterparts. Kirby frames this support as preparatory diplomacy tied to Ukraine’s vision for a just peace.
- Mr. Kirby said only President Zelenskyy can determine if and when he is ready to negotiate and that the U.S. respects that prerogative.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States is helping to actualize Zelenskyy’s 10-point peace proposal and that Jake Sullivan discussed the plan with Ukrainian officials.
  #### Military promotions hold
  Kirby and Ms. Jean-Pierre describe Senator Tuberville’s hold on more than 300 military nominations as causing concrete harm to families, disrupting moves, schooling, retirements, and readiness. Kirby details impacts on spouses, children, and retention, while reiterating the administration’s position that service members deserve access to health care, including travel to obtain reproductive care when local laws impede access. The White House calls the hold a political stunt and urges resolution.
- Mr. Kirby said Senator Tuberville’s hold on over 300 nominations is forcing families to delay moves, causing school disenrollments, and affecting retirements.
- Mr. Kirby said the Department of Defense policy allows travel support for service members who cannot access reproductive care at their duty station and that the President and Secretary Austin support that approach.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said Senator Tuberville’s tactic is a political stunt that endangers military families and readiness.
  #### White House Methane Summit
  Ms. Jean-Pierre summarizes the first-ever White House Methane Summit, which brought federal, state, and local leaders together to focus on methane emission reduction, detection technologies, public health protection, and union job creation. The summit spotlights how state and tribal governments respond to emissions events and ties methane action to the administration’s broader climate agenda. The President did not participate in the summit but will address extreme heat and climate actions the following day.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the White House hosted its first Methane Summit to spotlight methane detection technology and state and tribal responses to emissions events.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the summit aimed to reduce methane emissions, protect public health, and create thousands of good-paying union jobs.
  #### EV charging coalition
  Ms. Jean-Pierre frames the announcement of several major automakers forming an EV charging company as evidence of Bidenomics and the Investing in America agenda delivering results. She highlights the plan to install tens of thousands of high-powered chargers, create union jobs, and accelerate the transition to electric vehicles. The White House presents the move as advancing climate goals and economic opportunity.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said seven major automakers forming a new EV charging company reflects Bidenomics in action and will help install roughly 30,000 high-powered chargers.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the initiative will create union jobs for installation and maintenance and support climate objectives under the Investing in America agenda.
  #### Asylum rule litigation
  Ms. Jean-Pierre addresses a federal judge’s injunction blocking the administration’s new asylum rules, saying the Department of Justice has appealed and sought a stay and that operationally nothing has changed for now. She asserts the administration believes its approach is producing lower numbers of illegal migration and that the DOJ is pursuing legal remedies. The White House frames the rule as part of an effective border plan while awaiting appellate rulings.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said a federal judge blocked the administration’s asylum rule and that the Department of Justice has appealed and requested a stay.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the administration believes the President’s border plan is working and that, for now, operational practices remain in place pending appeals.
  #### Russia drone close pass
  Ms. Jean-Pierre responds to reports of a Russian fighter flying dangerously close to a U.S. drone over Syria, characterizing such approaches and use of flares as violations of established protocols and international norms. She reiterates the U.S. focus on defeating ISIS and notes the initial reports without offering further operational detail. The administration conveys concern and monitors the incident.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the reported close approach by a Russian fighter to a U.S. drone over Syria violates established protocols and international norms.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the United States remains focused on the mission to defeat ISIS while monitoring the incident.
  #### President schedule and outreach

She says the President intends to visit East Palestine but has no date to preview and refers inquiries about a major disaster declaration to FEMA. The White House reiterates continued engagement on mental health and domestic agenda items.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the President participated in an interview with Jay Shetty about mental health that will post Monday.
- Ms. Jean-Pierre said the President intends to visit East Palestine but that no date is scheduled yet and referred questions about disaster declarations to FEMA.
