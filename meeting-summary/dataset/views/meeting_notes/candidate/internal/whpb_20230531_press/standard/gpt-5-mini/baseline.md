### Overview
  This briefing covers a broad set of foreign-policy and domestic issues. MR. KIRBY opens with a new, 39th presidential drawdown to support Ukraine—providing air-defense munitions, Avenger and Stinger systems, and ammunition for HIMARS, artillery, and anti-armor—while describing intensified Russian strikes that underscore continued U.S. and allied assistance. He addresses a range of security concerns, calling a recent Chinese fighter intercept unsafe and urging restoration of military-to-military channels, declining to attribute responsibility for a Moscow drone strike while stressing that Ukrainian operational decisions are for Kyiv, and describing the JCPOA as not currently prioritized because Iran is not negotiating in good faith. Kirby also discusses DPRK launches and alliance coordination, U.S. reactions to escalating violence in Sudan and Kosovo, efforts on wrongfully detained Americans, and positions on business ties with China, cyber reports, and concerns about potential Russian information operations. MS. JEAN-PIERRE outlines domestic priorities focused on the bipartisan debt-limit agreement—highlighting the CBO’s deficit reduction estimate, protections for Medicaid and SNAP, targeted expansions for veterans and vulnerable groups, and extensive outreach to House Democrats—while noting reviews such as CFIUS on TikTok, plans to provide NSC gaggle transcripts, and that permitting reforms preserve environmental protections. Key outcomes include the announced Ukraine drawdown, reiterated U.S. policies against enabling strikes into Russia with U.S.-supplied equipment, continued alliance coordination on DPRK and other regional threats, condemnation of attacks on KFOR in Kosovo, and the White House’s expectation that the bipartisan debt-limit package will pass and be signed to avoid default.
