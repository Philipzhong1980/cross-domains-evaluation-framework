### Overview
In a background briefing, senior U.S. administration and military officials detailed a recent joint military strike conducted by the United States and United Kingdom, with support from Australia, Bahrain, Canada, and the Netherlands, targeting Houthi missile, radar, and UAV capabilities in Yemen. This action responds to escalating Iranian-backed Houthi attacks on commercial shipping in the Red Sea, which threaten global trade and freedom of navigation. The officials highlighted President Biden's active engagement in coordinating diplomatic and military responses, including the establishment of Operation Prosperity Guardian, a 22-country defensive coalition. The strikes aim to significantly degrade Houthi capabilities while minimizing collateral damage and avoiding escalation. Officials refuted Houthi claims linking their attacks to the Gaza conflict and emphasized the indiscriminate nature of the threats to vessels from over 50 countries. While no immediate retaliatory attacks have been observed, the U.S. remains prepared for further defense measures. Iran's role as the primary sponsor of the Houthis was affirmed, with ongoing pressure campaigns in place. Operational details remain classified, with further assessments forthcoming from Central Command. This coordinated response underscores the U.S. commitment to protecting vital international waterways and global commerce in partnership with allies.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Joint Military Strikes on Houthis
The United States and United Kingdom, supported by Australia, Bahrain, Canada, and the Netherlands, conducted joint strikes against Houthi-controlled areas in Yemen to disrupt missile, radar, and UAV capabilities threatening commercial shipping in the Red Sea. This action follows escalating Houthi attacks on commercial vessels, including a significant attack on January 9th targeting U.S. ships. The strikes aim to degrade Houthi capabilities without escalating the conflict.
- Senior administration official states the strikes targeted Houthi missile, radar, and UAV capabilities essential to attacks on commercial shipping.
- Senior military official confirms strikes were launched from air, surface, and subsurface platforms using precision-guided munitions to minimize collateral damage.
- Senior administration official notes the action was a collective response endorsed by multiple countries and aimed at protecting global trade routes.
#### Background of Houthi Attacks and International Response
Since November 19th, the Houthis, supported by Iran, have launched over 20 attacks on merchant vessels in the Red Sea, affecting global commerce and prompting international condemnation. The U.N. Security Council and a coalition of countries have condemned these attacks and supported defensive measures like Operation Prosperity Guardian to protect maritime navigation.
- Senior administration official details nearly 20 drones and multiple missiles launched against U.S. ships on January 9th, which were successfully defended.
- The U.N. Security Council issued a resolution condemning over two dozen attacks and those supplying arms to the Houthis, primarily Iran, as stated by the senior administration official.
- Operation Prosperity Guardian, a 22-country defensive coalition, was established to protect maritime traffic in the Red Sea, as announced by Secretary Austin and referenced by the senior administration official.
#### Presidential Engagement and Diplomatic Efforts
President Biden has been actively engaged in responding to the Houthi threat, directing diplomatic efforts and the formation of military options. Following attacks, the President convened his national security team to discuss responses, leading to the establishment of Operation Prosperity Guardian and the recent strikes.
- Senior administration official reports President Biden's near-daily involvement and direction to accelerate diplomatic efforts and develop military options.
- A multilateral statement on January 3rd warned the Houthis of full consequences for further attacks, as noted by the senior administration official.
- The President directed Secretary Austin to carry out the recent strikes following the January 9th attack, according to the senior administration official.
#### Clarification on Houthi Claims and Regional Context
The Houthis' claims linking their attacks to the Gaza conflict and targeting Israeli ships are refuted by U.S. officials, who emphasize the indiscriminate nature of the attacks on vessels from over 50 countries. The strikes are framed as protecting global commerce and freedom of navigation, separate from broader Middle East tensions.
- Senior administration official states Houthi claims of targeting Israeli ships are baseless and attacks are indiscriminate.
- The action aligns with U.S. National Security and Defense Strategies to protect global trade routes, as explained by the senior administration official.
- The strikes are not intended to escalate the situation but to degrade Houthi capabilities, according to the senior administration official.
#### Details of Military Operations and Coalition Support
The strikes involved U.S. Navy, Air Force, and UK manned aircraft using precision-guided munitions. The UK provided fighter aircraft for the strikes, while other coalition partners offered non-operational support. The strikes are distinct from Operation Prosperity Guardian, which remains active in defensive roles.
- Senior military official confirms use of air, surface, and subsurface platforms with precision-guided munitions to minimize collateral damage.
- The UK materially participated with fighter aircraft in the strikes, as stated by the senior military official.
- Other coalition partners' support levels are not detailed publicly, per senior administration official.
#### Assessment of Strike Effectiveness and Future Risks
While exact degradation levels of Houthi capabilities are undisclosed for operational security, officials characterize the impact as significant. No retaliatory attacks on U.S. or coalition forces have been observed yet, but further Houthi attacks are anticipated. The U.S. remains prepared to defend itself and continue protecting maritime traffic.
- Senior military official describes the degradation of Houthi capabilities as significant but does not provide exact figures.
- No direct retaliatory attacks on U.S. or coalition forces have been detected so far, according to the senior military official.
- President Biden has stated readiness to take further measures to protect personnel and commerce if necessary, as noted by the senior administration official.
#### Iran's Role and Potential Consequences
Iran is identified as the primary sponsor and enabler of the Houthis, providing intelligence and capabilities for attacks. The U.S. maintains a longstanding pressure campaign against Iran but does not disclose specific future actions. The broader regional conflict involving Iran-backed groups continues to pose risks.
- Senior administration official confirms Iran's operational involvement in supporting Houthi attacks.
- The U.S. holds Iran responsible and has communicated this to Tehran, as stated by the senior administration official.
- No specific new economic or diplomatic actions against Iran are announced, but existing pressure campaigns continue.
#### Operational Security and Targeting Details
Details on the number of targets, locations, and timing of target list development are withheld for security reasons. The strikes focused on minimizing collateral damage by targeting specific military capabilities rather than civilian areas. Further assessments and information will be released by Central Command.
- Senior military official states that target development is part of routine combatant commander responsibilities and specifics are classified.
- The strikes targeted specific capabilities in Houthi-controlled areas with precision munitions to avoid civilian harm, as emphasized by the senior military official.
- Further details on strike success and collateral damage will be provided by Central Command in due course.
