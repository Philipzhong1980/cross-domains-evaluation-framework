### Overview
  Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre and Admiral John Kirby brief reporters on a wide range of policy priorities and ongoing international engagements. Jean-Pierre opens by announcing the First Lady’s attendance at a Nashville vigil and renews the President’s call for an assault weapons ban while highlighting the FDA’s approval of over-the-counter Narcan and the administration’s opioid response funding proposals. She also updates Vice President Harris’s Africa trip and details immigration policy effects, including the parole program and CBP One app improvements. Admiral Kirby summarizes the Summit for Democracy announcements, including up to $690 million in new funding and technology initiatives, previews a bilateral with Argentina, and addresses Taiwan President Tsai’s routine transit while urging Beijing not to overreact. Reporters press on topics ranging from summit invitations and Israel’s judicial reform debate to TikTok security, potential Xi–Zelenskyy engagement, military promotion holds by Senator Tuberville, Ukraine aid oversight, Venezuela engagement, and the tragic migrant detention fire in Mexico; the administration responds by reaffirming diplomatic channels, legal and oversight referrals, and readiness to assist. The briefing closes with the White House urging congressional action on the debt limit and budget, defending extensive executive measures on guns and background checks while pressing Congress to pass an assault weapons ban, and expressing support for Senate action to repeal outdated AUMFs with a request for swift House follow-up.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Nashville vigil & guns
  Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre announces the First Lady will attend a Nashville candlelight vigil for Covenant School shooting victims and reiterates the administration's call for Congress to pass an assault weapons ban. She frames the request as a response to repeated mass shootings and emphasizes that the administration will continue executive and legislative efforts to reduce gun violence. The White House positions this as an urgent moral and policy priority while urging congressional action.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the First Lady will travel to Nashville to join a candlelight vigil for victims of the Covenant School shooting.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said President Biden continues to call on Congress to pass an assault weapons ban to make communities and children safer.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the administration will stay focused on actions that it believes will save lives while urging congressional courage.
  #### Narcan OTC approval
  Jean-Pierre announces that the FDA has approved over-the-counter use of Narcan (naloxone), calling it a lifesaving development that increases access to an opioid overdose reversal drug. She links the approval to broader administration efforts that the White House credits with flattening or decreasing overdose deaths for seven months. The announcement underscores ongoing budget and policy proposals to increase funding for opioid response and prevention.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the FDA approved Narcan for over-the-counter use and called the action life-saving.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said naloxone can reverse overdoses from opioids like fentanyl and has saved thousands of lives.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the administration’s actions have contributed to a decrease or flattening in overdose deaths for seven months and the President’s budget calls for more funding to combat trafficking and expand care.
  #### VP Harris Africa trip
  Jean-Pierre provides updates on Vice President Harris’s multi-country trip to Africa, noting recent stops in Ghana and upcoming visits to Tanzania and Zambia. She highlights meetings with President Akufo-Addo, a state banquet attended by African diaspora figures, and an announcement of nearly $1 billion to empower women entrepreneurs through digital access, capital, healthcare, and education. The Vice President also announced a Women in the Digital Economy Fund with the Gates Foundation and emphasized entrepreneurship and historical remembrance during public remarks.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said Vice President Harris completed a productive visit to Ghana and is en route to Tanzania with Zambia on the schedule.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said Vice President Harris announced nearly $1 billion in investments to support women entrepreneurs in Africa and a new Women in the Digital Economy Fund with the Gates Foundation.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the Vice President stressed innovation and entrepreneurship to 8,000 Ghanaians and spoke about the legacy of the transatlantic slave trade at Cape Coast Castle.
  #### Summit funding & agenda
  Admiral John Kirby summarizes the President’s remarks at the Summit for Democracy and announces up to $690 million in additional U.S. funding to support media freedom, anti-corruption, human rights defenders, election integrity, and technology that supports democracy. He says the U.S. will also spotlight technology-for-democracy initiatives and pass the summit baton to the Republic of Korea. The funding and agenda position the administration to advance democratic resilience globally.
- Admiral John Kirby said the President announced up to $690 million in additional funding for initiatives that support free media, combat corruption, bolster human rights activists, and defend elections.
- Admiral John Kirby said the United States will highlight how technology can work for, not against, democratic societies and will spotlight related events the following day.
- Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. is passing responsibility for the next summit to the Republic of Korea.
  #### Argentina bilat preview
  Kirby notes a bilateral meeting between President Biden and Argentina’s President Fernández scheduled that afternoon, framing Argentina as a key partner in the hemisphere. He says the leaders will emphasize upholding democratic values given regional events and promises a readout after the meeting. The bilat is positioned as part of broader Summit for Democracy engagement.
- Admiral John Kirby said President Biden will meet with President Fernández of Argentina to highlight the importance of upholding democratic values in the region.
- Admiral John Kirby said the White House will provide a readout after the bilateral meeting concludes.
  #### Taiwan transit & China
  Kirby addresses Taiwan President Tsai Ing-wen’s transit through the United States, reiterating that transits are routine, unofficial, and consistent with U.S. One China policy. He stresses that previous transits by Taiwan presidents, including Tsai, have occurred without incident and urges that China not use the transit as a pretext for aggressive activity. Kirby emphasizes continued communication with Beijing and discourages overreaction.
- Admiral John Kirby said Tsai Ing-wen’s transit is consistent with longstanding unofficial U.S. practice and with the One China policy.
- Admiral John Kirby said previous Taiwan presidential transits have occurred without incident and that there is no reason for Beijing to overreact.
- Admiral John Kirby said the United States is keeping lines of communication open with Beijing and will continue to do so.
  #### Diplomatic discussions transparency
  Reporters question why the administration did not publicly confirm a reported Sullivan phone call with Chinese counterparts about the Taiwan transit; Kirby declines to confirm press reports and says Sullivan routinely conducts such calls. He notes multiple diplomatic discussions at various levels with Beijing and that not every call is read out publicly. The exchange underscores the administration’s balance between diplomatic discretion and press transparency.
- A reporter asked why the Sullivan phone call with his Chinese counterpart was not publicly confirmed and Admiral John Kirby declined to confirm press reports.
- Admiral John Kirby said Mr. Sullivan routinely has discussions with counterparts and that multiple diplomatic discussions occurred with Beijing about the transit at different levels.
- Admiral John Kirby said the White House does not read out every single phone call that officials have with counterparts.
  #### Summit participation questions
  Reporters raise concerns about invite decisions to the Summit for Democracy, noting Iraq as the only Arab participant and questioning invitations to countries like India and exclusions like Hungary and Turkey. Kirby says invitations consider progress on human and civil rights, press freedom, and peaceful assembly, and that the summit is intended as a working forum rather than an endorsement. He frames the summit as an opportunity for multilateral dialogue to strengthen democratic practices.
- A reporter asked how the administration encourages Arab countries to be more transparent; Admiral John Kirby said the summit provides a multilateral platform to discuss transparency and accountability.
- Admiral John Kirby said invitation decisions are based on multiple factors including progress toward human and civil rights and not a simple stamp of approval.
- Admiral John Kirby said the summit is about doing the hard work of strengthening democracies rather than a popularity contest.
  #### Israel judicial reforms
  Reporters ask whether Prime Minister Netanyahu’s tone signals disrespect toward President Biden and whether personal ties affect Israel’s response to U.S. calls for compromise on judicial reform. Kirby emphasizes Netanyahu’s public statements on seeking compromise, affirms the long-standing U.S.-Israel relationship, and says Israel, as a sovereign democracy, should pursue changes with broad public support. He frames U.S. comments as encouragement for consensus-preserving outcomes.
- A reporter asked whether Netanyahu’s attitude toward President Biden affected diplomatic engagement and Admiral John Kirby noted Netanyahu’s statement references searching for compromise and respect for the U.S.-Israel relationship.
- Admiral John Kirby said President Biden shares respect for Prime Minister Netanyahu and that deep friendships allow for candid dialogue.
- Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. encourages decisions in Israel that are supported by the broadest possible public consensus to preserve democratic checks and balances.
  #### TikTok & app security
  Reporters ask about national security concerns involving TikTok and other ByteDance-owned apps; Kirby limits comments to reiterating the administration’s concern about TikTok on government devices. He notes the President has banned TikTok from government phones and emphasizes attention to data transfer, privacy, and secrecy risks for apps. The administration keeps broader app security considerations under ongoing review.
- A reporter asked whether national security concerns extend to other ByteDance apps and Admiral John Kirby said the administration assesses apps for data transfer and privacy concerns.
- Admiral John Kirby said the President has banned TikTok from government phones and the administration will continue to evaluate security risks.
  #### Zelenskyy–Xi dialogue
  When asked about President Zelenskyy’s call for a Xi-Zelenskyy conversation, Kirby says the U.S. supports, at minimum, a conversation between President Xi and President Zelenskyy and has advocated for it for weeks. He frames such dialogue as consistent with U.S. goals to reduce tensions and open communication channels. The comment reflects U.S. willingness to encourage high-level engagement on the Ukraine conflict.
- A reporter asked whether the White House agrees with Zelenskyy that Xi should engage with Ukraine and Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. supports at least a conversation between President Xi and President Zelenskyy.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration has been calling for such engagement for weeks.
  #### Senator Tuberville blockades
  Kirby explains that Senator Tommy Tuberville is holding up over 160 military promotions related to an abortion policy dispute, warning of downstream effects on military readiness and personnel movement. He cites Secretary Austin’s concerns about ripple effects and notes bipartisan Republican appeals for Tuberville to lift the blocks. The White House frames continued holds as potentially harmful to U.S. defense posture amid global challenges.
- Admiral John Kirby said Senator Tuberville is blocking more than 160 senior military promotions and nominees, which creates ripple effects that can harm military readiness.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration welcomes Senate Republicans who urged Tuberville to drop the blocks and agrees with their calls.
  #### Ukraine aid oversight
  Reporters ask about inspectors general receiving complaints about U.S. aid to Ukraine; Kirby says he is not aware of specific numbers referenced but notes there is no evidence of egregious misconduct so far. He emphasizes U.S. and Ukrainian commitments to transparency and notes the administration has stood up multiple inspectors general and welcomes congressional oversight. Kirby also stresses the realities of delivering materiel in an active war zone while maintaining accountability.
- A reporter asked about inspectors general complaints on Ukraine aid and Admiral John Kirby said he had not seen the specific reports and no evidence of egregious misconduct has been found.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration has stood up additional inspectors general at DOD and welcomes congressional oversight to ensure accountability.
  #### Venezuela engagement
  A reporter asks whether the White House is prepared to meet with Maduro’s government to address Venezuela’s political crisis; Kirby says the U.S. is interested in continued discussions but is not aware of any formal meetings. He states the U.S. remains willing to work with partners and continues security cooperation in the region. The response leaves the door open to further engagement while noting no concrete bilateral talks are scheduled.
- A reporter asked whether the White House is ready to sit down with Maduro’s government and Admiral John Kirby said the administration is interested in continued discussions but is not aware of any formal meeting.
- Admiral John Kirby said the U.S. will continue to work with partners on regional security concerns regardless of Venezuela’s participation decisions.
  #### Mexico migrant fire & CBP One
  Reporters question the White House response to a deadly fire at a Mexican detention center housing migrants and whether the U.S. can prevent similar tragedies by expanding humane immigration pathways. Kirby expresses condolences, says the U.S. has been in touch with Mexican authorities and stands ready to assist, and reiterates the administration’s push to open more legal pathways. Jean-Pierre addresses CBP One app glitches, says the parole policy is working based on reduced unlawful migration numbers for certain countries, and acknowledges efforts to improve the app.
- Admiral John Kirby expressed condolences for victims of the Mexican detention center fire and said U.S. officials have been in touch with Mexican authorities and stand ready to assist.
- Admiral John Kirby said the tragedy underscores the administration’s efforts to open more legal pathways for migration and urged Congress to act on immigration reforms.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the parole program and related policies are working and cited significant declines in unlawful migration from specific countries.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre acknowledged reported glitches in the CBP One app and said the administration is looking to improve the app while noting the overall policy impact.
  #### Fentanyl origins discussion
  Reporters press the administration on whether China is the primary source of fentanyl and whether the U.S. views the situation as akin to an Opium War; Kirby defers detailed attribution and emphasizes the President’s consistent messaging on the dangers of fentanyl. He reiterates that the administration seeks innovative, cross-cutting solutions and has been clear about the opioid crisis domestically. Kirby declines to characterize the issue as a state-waged war in the transcript and refers drug-specific enforcement questions to other parts of the government.
- A reporter asked whether the fentanyl crisis should be characterized as a war waged by China and Admiral John Kirby emphasized the President has been clear about the dangers of fentanyl and the need for cross-cutting solutions.
- Admiral John Kirby said the administration has not backed away from pursuing innovative approaches to address fentanyl and opioids domestically.
  #### Debt limit & budget
  Jean-Pierre addresses letters exchanged between President Biden and Speaker McCarthy on the debt limit and budget, reiterating the administration’s position that the debt limit should be addressed without conditions and that Republicans should present a budget. She notes the President has already submitted his budget outlining $3 trillion in deficit reduction over ten years and urges House Republicans to show transparency and propose their priorities. The White House separates debt limit mechanics from broader budget negotiations but insists on prompt action to avoid default risk.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the President expects Speaker McCarthy to move promptly to remove the threat of default and that the debt limit should be resolved without conditions.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the President has submitted a budget that outlines $3 trillion in deficit reduction over ten years and called on House Republicans to present their proposals.
  #### Gun policy specifics
  In a detailed exchange about gun policy, Jean-Pierre rejects arguments that an assault weapons ban is impractical due to widespread ownership, calls continued inaction unacceptable, and reiterates the President’s support for banning weapons of war from civilian circulation. She underscores extensive executive actions taken so far, including a recent executive order aimed at expanding background checks as far as legally possible, and urges Congress to pass legislation the President can sign. The White House frames these measures as morally necessary to protect children and communities.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said claims that nothing can be done because millions of assault weapons exist are unacceptable and called on Republicans to show courage.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the President believes AR-15s and similar assault weapons are weapons of war that should not be on U.S. streets and has taken more executive actions on gun violence than any prior president.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the President signed an executive order to expand background checks as far as possible absent new federal legislation and called on Congress to act.
  #### Classified documents oversight
  Reporters ask about classified documents and related oversight; Kirby defers some questions and Jean-Pierre later refers inquiries to the Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI). She also responds to Senator Warner’s criticism by acknowledging differences with Hill members but directing the matter to ODNI. The administration consistently routes sensitive classification and intelligence-access questions to the appropriate oversight bodies.
- A reporter asked about classified documents and Admiral John Kirby deferred specifics to other offices.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said questions about classified material and Senator Warner’s concerns belong in the purview of the ODNI and referred reporters there.
  #### AUMF repeal support
  Jean-Pierre states the administration supports the Senate’s repeal of the 2002 and 1991 Authorizations for Use of Military Force and urges the House to act quickly as well. She says repeal would not affect ongoing military or counterterrorism operations and aligns with the President’s commitment to replace outdated authorizations with a narrow, specific framework to protect Americans. The White House frames repeal as part of long-standing policy priorities.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre said the Biden administration supports the Senate’s repeal of the 2002 and 1991 AUMFs and applauds the Senate for taking action.
- Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre urged the House to move quickly and said repealing those AUMFs would not impact current military operations while enabling a more narrow legal framework going forward.
