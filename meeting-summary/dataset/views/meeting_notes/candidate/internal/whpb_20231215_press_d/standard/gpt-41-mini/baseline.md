### Overview
The meeting featured Mr. Kirby providing updates and responding to questions regarding the ongoing conflict in Gaza and related regional issues. Key topics included Israel's decision to open the Kerem Shalom crossing for humanitarian aid, discussions on post-conflict governance and security arrangements in Gaza involving the Palestinian Authority, and the U.S. engagement with Israel on transitioning military operations to a lower-intensity phase. The tragic killing of three hostages during an Israeli raid and the death of an Al Jazeera journalist were addressed with condolences and calls for careful investigation. Concerns about Houthi attacks disrupting Red Sea shipping and the U.S. efforts to enhance maritime security were discussed. The U.S. reiterated expectations for Israel's compliance with international humanitarian law in the use of military aid and highlighted ongoing concerns about violence by Israeli settlers in the West Bank, including measures taken to address it. Throughout, Mr. Kirby emphasized the complexity of the situation, the importance of precise military conduct, and the U.S. commitment to working with regional partners to support stability and humanitarian needs.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Kerem Shalom Crossing Opening
The meeting began with an update on the Kerem Shalom crossing, which Israel has decided to open for direct delivery of humanitarian aid to Gaza. This development is significant as it facilitates the entry of essential supplies such as food, water, medicine, and fuel to Palestinians in need. The U.S. expressed gratitude for Israel's decision, highlighting its potential positive impact on the humanitarian situation in Gaza.
- Mr. Kirby noted Jake Sullivan welcomed Prime Minister Netanyahu's decision to open Kerem Shalom for inspections and urged for direct truck entry.
- Mr. Kirby confirmed Israel's decision to allow direct delivery of humanitarian assistance through Kerem Shalom.
- Mr. Kirby emphasized the importance of this step for the lives and livelihoods of Palestinians in Gaza.
#### Post-Conflict Governance in Gaza
Discussions addressed the uncertain future governance and security arrangements in Gaza following the conflict. The U.S. is engaging with regional partners, including Israel and the Palestinian Authority, to explore governance possibilities. While no definitive plans exist, there is consensus that post-conflict Gaza must differ from the Hamas-controlled status quo and better reflect Palestinian aspirations for peace, stability, and security.
- Mr. Kirby acknowledged the complexity of post-conflict governance and security in Gaza and the lack of final answers.
- He stated Jake Sullivan discussed governance possibilities with Mahmoud Abbas, focusing on a revitalized Palestinian Authority.
- Mr. Kirby emphasized the need for Gaza's future governance to be more representative and secure than before October 6.
#### Palestinian Authority's Role in Gaza
The conversation included the Palestinian Authority's potential role in Gaza's future governance. While specific details of discussions between Jake Sullivan and President Abbas were not disclosed, the U.S. highlighted the importance of a credible, accountable Palestinian Authority managing Palestinian aspirations. The U.S. is encouraging efforts to strengthen the Authority's legitimacy and responsibility in the region.
- Mr. Kirby refrained from detailing conversations but confirmed broad discussions about the Palestinian Authority's role.
- He stressed the need for the Palestinian Authority to be seen as more credible, authentic, and accountable.
- Mr. Kirby indicated the U.S. supports a revitalized Palestinian Authority responsible for Palestinian governance.
#### Transition to Lower-Intensity Military Operations
The U.S. discussed with Israeli counterparts the importance of transitioning from high-intensity to lower-intensity military operations in Gaza. While the conflict may continue for months, there is agreement on the need for more targeted, precise, and surgical operations. The timing and specifics of this transition remain under Israeli control, with the U.S. encouraging forward planning and consideration of such a phase shift.
- Mr. Kirby conveyed Jake Sullivan's discussions with Israel about transitioning to lower-intensity operations.
- He noted the U.S. agrees with Israel that the conflict could last months but supports more precise military actions.
- Mr. Kirby declined to provide details on timelines or conditions for the transition, leaving decisions to Israeli authorities.
#### Hostage Casualty Incident
The U.S. responded to reports that Israeli forces mistakenly killed three hostages during a raid in Gaza. The incident is described as tragic and heartbreaking, with investigations expected to determine how the mistake occurred. The U.S. expressed condolences to the families and emphasized the need to understand the circumstances without making premature judgments about Israel's operational precision.
- Mr. Kirby called the killing of three hostages a heartbreaking and tragic mistake.
- He stated the U.S. lacks full details but expects Israeli forensics to investigate the incident.
- Mr. Kirby emphasized mourning with the families and refrained from broad judgments on Israel's military precision.
#### Impact of Houthi Attacks on Red Sea Shipping
Concerns were raised about commercial shipping companies, such as Maersk, halting travel through the Red Sea due to increased risks from Houthi attacks. The U.S. condemned these attacks and is working to strengthen combined maritime forces in the region to ensure safe and secure navigation. Further updates on the maritime task force are expected in the coming days.
- Mr. Kirby acknowledged increased dangers to commercial shipping in the Red Sea due to Houthi attacks.
- He stated the U.S. is working to bolster existing combined maritime forces to protect shipping lanes.
- Mr. Kirby condemned the Houthis' actions as unacceptable threats to international commerce and safety.
#### Journalist Safety and Targeting Concerns
The killing of an Al Jazeera photographer in Gaza was addressed with condolences extended to the journalist's family and colleagues. The U.S. maintains no evidence that Israeli forces deliberately target journalists, emphasizing the importance of press freedom in conflict zones. The U.S. continues to engage with Israeli counterparts to ensure military operations are conducted with precision and care to minimize civilian harm, including to journalists.
- Mr. Kirby expressed deepest condolences for the Al Jazeera photographer killed in Gaza.
- He stated the U.S. has no indications that Israel deliberately targets journalists.
- Mr. Kirby emphasized the need for precise and cautious military operations to protect civilians and journalists.
#### U.S.-Israel Coordination on Military Aid and Conduct
Questions about conditioning U.S. military aid to Israel based on compliance with international humanitarian law were addressed. The U.S. reiterated that all military assistance comes with expectations of lawful use. The administration believes its approach has influenced Israeli conduct positively and will continue to encourage steps to reduce civilian harm without imposing new conditions on aid.
- Mr. Kirby confirmed all U.S. military assistance requires compliance with international humanitarian law.
- He stated the administration sees results from its engagement with Israel on reducing civilian harm.
- Mr. Kirby declined to discuss any changes to conditions on military aid to Israel.
#### West Bank Violence and U.S. Response
The U.S. clarified its position on violence by Israeli settlers in the West Bank, emphasizing longstanding concerns and calls for cessation. While the U.S. did not join a recent EU statement, it has been clear publicly and privately about the unacceptability of settler violence. Measures such as visa bans on involved settlers have been implemented, and the U.S. continues to urge Israel to address the issue to prevent escalation.
- Mr. Kirby stated the U.S. has been clear and consistent about concerns over West Bank settler violence.
- He noted the President raised the issue publicly and the State Department imposed visa bans on violent settlers.
- Mr. Kirby emphasized the need to stop violence to avoid conflict escalation.
