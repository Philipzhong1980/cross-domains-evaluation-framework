### Overview
  Spokesperson John Kirby briefs reporters on a sequence of regional and operational issues following Jake Sullivan's trip to the region, noting that Israel has agreed to open the Kerem Shalom crossing for direct humanitarian deliveries — a step the U.S. welcomes as materially helpful to civilians in Gaza. Reporters and Kirby discuss post-conflict governance options, with Kirby saying the U.S. and partners, including the Palestinian Authority and Israel, are exploring possible PA roles but have no final answers and stress the need to restore PA credibility and ensure security and representation for Palestinians. Kirby says Sullivan urged Israeli planning for a transition from high-intensity to more targeted operations while acknowledging the timeline and conditions remain for Israeli decision-makers, and he condemns the tragic killing of three hostages in a raid, noting the U.S. lacks full visibility and expects Israeli forensics. The briefing also addresses wider regional impacts: Kirby condemns Houthi attacks that make Red Sea transit riskier and says the U.S. is strengthening combined maritime forces and will provide more details soon; he offers condolences after the death of an Al Jazeera photographer while saying the U.S. has not seen evidence that Israel is deliberately targeting journalists and would need detailed strike information to assess. On policy, Kirby reiterates that all U.S. military assistance carries expectations of compliance with international humanitarian law and that the administration believes its engagement has produced results, and he underscores U.S. concern about West Bank settler violence while noting measures such as visa bans and continued private and public pressure on Israeli counterparts.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Kerem Shalom opening
  Mr. Kirby reports that Jake Sullivan welcomed Israel's decision to open the Kerem Shalom crossing for inspections and direct delivery of humanitarian assistance. The move is significant because it enables direct entry of trucks carrying food, water, medicine, and fuel into Gaza. The U.S. expresses gratitude and frames this as a meaningful step to relieve civilian suffering while continuing to monitor implementation.
- Mr. Kirby said Jake Sullivan welcomed Prime Minister Netanyahu's decision to open Kerem Shalom for inspections and urged rapid availability for truck deliveries.
- Mr. Kirby said the Israeli government informed Jake Sullivan before he left that it had decided to allow direct humanitarian delivery through Kerem Shalom.
- Mr. Kirby said the administration is very grateful for the decision and expects it to make a big difference for people in need of food, water, medicine, and fuel.
  #### Post-conflict governance
  Reporters ask about post-conflict governance in Gaza and the possibility of reviving Palestinian security forces or a stronger Palestinian Authority role. Mr. Kirby emphasizes that the U.S. and partners, including Israel and the Palestinian Authority, are discussing options but do not yet have firm answers. The administration stresses that any future arrangement must be more representative and provide security and stability for Palestinians.
- Mr. Kirby said the U.S. is working with a range of regional partners, including Israel and the Palestinian Authority, to consider post-conflict governance in Gaza.
- Mr. Kirby said Jake Sullivan discussed governance possibilities with Palestinian Authority President Mahmoud Abbas during his visit.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States does not yet have final answers about what security services in Gaza will look like and is continuing to ask those questions with partners.
- Mr. Kirby said whatever post-conflict Gaza looks like, it must not resemble the situation on October 6 when Hamas was in control and must better reflect Palestinian aspirations for safety and stability.
  #### PA role and credibility
  The discussion covers whether the Palestinian Authority could assume responsibility in Gaza and what would be required for that to be credible. Mr. Kirby says conversations addressed a revitalized PA with responsibility and accountability for Palestinians' future. The U.S. highlights the need to make the PA more credible, authentic, and accountable before any expanded role is realistic.
- Mr. Kirby said talks included a broad discussion about the Palestinian Authority's potential role and what it should become.
- Mr. Kirby said the U.S. is focused on what needs to be done to make the Palestinian Authority seen as more credible and accountable.
- Mr. Kirby said the issue of PA authority ties directly into broader questions about how to ensure security and governance for the Palestinian people.
  #### Transition to lower-intensity operations
  Reporters ask about encouraging Israel to move from high-intensity to more targeted operations; Mr. Kirby says Jake Sullivan raised the importance of planning for such a transition. The U.S. urges a shift toward more precise, surgical operations when appropriate but does not dictate Israeli timelines or parameters. Officials acknowledge the conflict could continue for months and that how and when to transition is for Israeli counterparts to decide.
- Mr. Kirby said Jake Sullivan discussed the importance of transitioning from high-intensity to more targeted and surgical military operations with Israeli counterparts.
- Mr. Kirby said the idea of moving into different operational phases is common practice and was part of constructive conversations in Israel.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States is not dictating terms to Israel and that the calendar and conditions for any transition are for Israeli decision-makers.
- Mr. Kirby said the conflict could go on for months, which factors into planning about when to change operational intensity.
  #### Hostages killed in raid
  Mr. Kirby responds to reporting that the IDF mistakenly killed three hostages during a raid, calling the news heartbreaking and tragic. He stresses that the U.S. does not yet have full visibility into the specific circumstances and expects Israeli forensics and review. The President has been briefed by his national security team but has not yet spoken directly with Prime Minister Netanyahu about this incident.
- Mr. Kirby said the news that three hostages were killed in the conduct of a raid is heartbreaking and tragic.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States does not have perfect visibility on how the operation unfolded and will look to Israeli forensics to determine what happened.
- Mr. Kirby said Prime Minister Netanyahu expressed similar sentiments about the gravity of the loss.
- Mr. Kirby said the President has not yet spoken to Prime Minister Netanyahu about this specific incident but has been briefed by his national security team.
- Mr. Kirby said the U.S. is mourning with the families who received the worst possible news and is keeping them foremost in mind.
  #### Red Sea shipping threats
  Reporters ask about commercial carriers like Maersk suspending Red Sea transits amid Houthi attacks. Mr. Kirby says commercial operators must weigh risk and benefit but acknowledges transit is more dangerous now because of those attacks. He says the U.S. is working to bolster existing combined maritime forces and will have more details on a maritime task force in coming days, while strongly condemning Houthi actions that disrupt commerce and endanger crews.
- Mr. Kirby said he would let shipping companies speak for themselves about decisions to halt transit through the Red Sea.
- Mr. Kirby said commercial traffic in the Red Sea is more dangerous now because of Houthi attacks on commercial shipping.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States is working to bolster and strengthen existing combined maritime forces and will provide more specifics about a maritime task force in the coming days.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States condemns Houthi threats and actions that disrupt international commerce and endanger the safety of ships and crews.
  #### Journalist killed in Gaza
  Mr. Kirby offers condolences for the Al Jazeera photographer killed in Gaza and reiterates that deliberate targeting of journalists is never acceptable. He says the U.S. does not yet have sufficient details to determine whether the strike deliberately targeted journalists and reports no indications so far that Israel is deliberately targeting the press. He explains that evidence would require detailed knowledge of the strike's purpose, origin, targeting process, and weaponization decisions.
- Mr. Kirby offered the United States' deepest condolences to the family and colleagues of the Al Jazeera photographer and emphasized the vital importance of journalists' work.
- Mr. Kirby said he does not have all the details about the strike and therefore cannot characterize the operation as deliberate or not.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States has not seen indications that Israeli forces are deliberately targeting journalists as a matter of policy or doctrine.
- Mr. Kirby said determining deliberate targeting would require specific information about the strike's purpose, origin, targeting process, and weaponization decisions.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States will continue to press Israeli counterparts to be precise, cautious, and mindful of civilian harm, including the safety of journalists.
  #### Military aid expectations
  A question arises about conditioning U.S. military aid to Israel after concerns about civilian harm; Mr. Kirby reiterates that all foreign military assistance comes with the expectation of lawful use and compliance with international humanitarian law. He says the administration believes its current approach has produced results and that Israel is receptive to messages about reducing civilian harm, and he declines to add new information about conditioning assistance. The U.S. will continue to urge additional steps to minimize civilian casualties.
- Mr. Kirby said all U.S. foreign military assistance, including direct assistance and Foreign Military Sales, carries the expectation that recipients will use materials in compliance with international humanitarian law.
- Mr. Kirby said the President believes the administration's approach has produced results and that Israelis have been receptive to messages about reducing civilian harm.
- Mr. Kirby said he has no additional context to provide on the question of conditioning assistance and will continue to urge Israel to take steps to reduce civilian harm.
  #### West Bank settler violence
  Reporters ask why the United States did not join an EU statement urging Israel to act against settler violence in the West Bank; Mr. Kirby says the U.S. has been clear publicly and privately about its concerns and has taken measures. He highlights that the President raised West Bank violence publicly weeks earlier, that the U.S. views settler attacks as unacceptable, and that the State Department has imposed visa bans on settlers involved in violence. The U.S. warns that unchecked violence risks widening the conflict.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States has been crystal clear about concerns over West Bank violence and that such violence by settlers is unacceptable and must stop.
- Mr. Kirby said the President raised West Bank violence publicly in the Rose Garden many weeks ago.
- Mr. Kirby said the State Department has implemented visa bans on settlers involved in violence.
- Mr. Kirby said the United States has been speaking privately with Israeli counterparts, including during Jake Sullivan's trip, to press for action to calm the West Bank.
