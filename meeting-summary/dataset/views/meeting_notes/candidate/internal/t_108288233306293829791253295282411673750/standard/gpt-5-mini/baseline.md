#### Friendly Voices presentation
  Laura Steuer presents Friendly Voices, a Bay Area nonprofit that reduces senior isolation by matching trained volunteer callers with homebound seniors for weekly one-to-one phone conversations. She explains their volunteer-run operations, safety practices, partner referrals, and current service capacity and expansion plans. The presentation prompts questions about outreach, referrals, volunteer recruitment, and suitability for seniors with cognitive impairment.
- Laura Steuer explains that Friendly Voices matches seniors 1:1 with trained, background-checked, supervised volunteers who document every call to prioritize senior safety.
- Laura Steuer states the program is 100% volunteer run and that volunteers call from their personal phones rather than a central call center.
- Laura Steuer reports Friendly Voices currently serves 70 seniors, maintains a wait list, and expects a grant to triple capacity to about 250 seniors within two years.
- Laura Steuer requests referrals, volunteers, and offers her contact information and website as avenues for collaboration, as stated to the committee.
- Elna Tymes asks how Friendly Voices reaches seniors not connected to agencies and Laura Steuer acknowledges outreach to unconnected seniors is a key challenge and invites ideas.
- Elna Tymes recommends contacting the Alzheimer's Association 24/7 line for caregiver referrals and points out caregivers calling that line may also benefit from phone support.
- Laura Steuer clarifies that Friendly Voices is not equipped to serve people with advanced dementia by phone and that in-person visitors are preferable for those cases.
- Ish Sinha recommends developing a friendly visitors program and reports success with in-person visiting in Alameda County, advocating similar outreach in Mountain View.

#### Senior survey proposal
  Elna Tymes reports on a private survey firm (referred to as CASOA in the discussion) that conducts standardized senior surveys for multiple nearby cities and could do a Mountain View survey. She outlines the proposed approach (postcards plus online responses), an estimated cost of about $20,000 for Mountain View, and the need for City Council approval and formal budget routing. Members ask about data isolation, response rates, and privacy, and Elna confirms Mountain View–specific results are possible and that further documentation is forthcoming.
- Elna Tymes reports the vendor has completed or is working on senior surveys for Saratoga, Avenidas/Palo Alto, Cupertino, Los Gatos, Campbell, and Sunnyvale and uses standard statistical methods.
- Elna Tymes estimates the Mountain View portion of the survey would cost about $20,000 and says the project would require City Council approval and routing through city staff.
- Elna Tymes states the vendor can provide Mountain View–specific results and that aggregated regional data may be available for comparison if other cities allow sharing.
- Jim Nichols asks about response volumes and the ability to isolate Mountain View data, and Elna confirms those data can be provided and that more vendor detail is pending via email.
- Elna Tymes explains the SAC does not currently have a budget and that any funding request would be prepared and submitted through city channels by staff.
#### Data privacy and methodology
  Committee members discuss data privacy, sampling, and logistics related to the proposed survey. Elna explains public sources for address data, the methods for linking responses to geography while protecting personal identity, and the need to review vendor-provided documentation. Members acknowledge the sensitivity of address-linked data and request more vendor information to evaluate risk and methodology.
- Elna Tymes explains that address information is publicly accessible via voter registration and that survey returns can be linked to addresses while preserving respondent anonymity as designed by the vendor.
- Jim Nichols raises concerns about providing addresses and potential data misuse, and Elna Tymes confirms protections and that postcards/online returns would likely link only to address-level data without exposing identities.
- Elna Tymes commits to circulating the vendor's detailed materials and response-rate information to the committee when they arrive by email.
