### Detailed Summary
#### Approval of Previous Minutes
The meeting begins with a motion to approve the minutes from the previous meeting held on July 20th. The motion is seconded and approved, with one member abstaining due to absence from the last meeting.
- Elna Tymes moves to approve the minutes from July 20th.
- Ish Sinha abstains from voting as he was not present at the last meeting.

#### Introduction of Guest Speaker
Laura Steuer from Friendly Voices presents to the committee, discussing the impact of loneliness on seniors, particularly exacerbated by the pandemic. She highlights the importance of her organization in providing companionship through trained volunteers who connect with isolated seniors via phone calls.
- Laura Steuer emphasizes the devastating effects of loneliness on seniors, comparing it to smoking 15 cigarettes a day.
- Friendly Voices aims to create vital human connections through weekly phone conversations with trained volunteers.

#### Outreach Strategies for Seniors
Laura discusses how her organization reaches isolated seniors primarily through referrals from social workers and agencies. She acknowledges the challenge of connecting with seniors who are not already linked to these services and invites suggestions for outreach.
- Laura mentions that most seniors come to them through referrals from agencies like Community Services Agency.
- Elna Tymes suggests exploring connections with the Alzheimer's Association for outreach.

#### Volunteer Program Expansion
Laura shares that Friendly Voices currently serves 70 seniors and has a waitlist, with plans to triple their capacity to 250 seniors in the next two years due to a new grant. She discusses the need for more volunteers to support this expansion.
- Laura states they currently have a waitlist and aim to serve 250 seniors by 2025.
- The organization plans to triple the number of volunteers to meet the increased demand.

#### Senior Center Updates
Ashley Bittle provides updates on the Senior Center, including the introduction of a new recreation coordinator, Anthony Kukar. She also discusses upcoming workshops focused on transportation and the return of the holiday bazaar after a three-year hiatus.
- Ashley announces the new recreation coordinator, Anthony Kukar, replacing Alejandra.
- The holiday bazaar is set for November 19th, featuring 40 vendors.

#### Senior Survey Discussion
Elna Tymes updates the committee on a potential senior survey being conducted by an external organization, CASOA. She discusses the importance of gathering data specific to Mountain View seniors and the process for obtaining funding from the City Council.
- Elna explains that CASOA has successfully conducted surveys for several cities and is willing to work with Mountain View.
- The estimated cost for the survey is $20,000, which would require City Council approval.
