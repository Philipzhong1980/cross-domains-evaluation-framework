#### Presentation by Friendly Voices
Laura Steuer from Friendly Voices presented on their nonprofit's work addressing senior isolation through weekly phone calls by trained volunteers. The program matches seniors with volunteers for ongoing relationships to combat loneliness, which has significant health impacts. They currently serve 70 seniors with a waitlist and plan to expand with new funding. Challenges include reaching isolated seniors not connected to agencies and exploring partnerships with other organizations.
- Laura Steuer described the severe impact of senior isolation and loneliness, comparing it to smoking 15 cigarettes a day.
- Friendly Voices provides weekly phone calls by trained, background-checked volunteers to isolated seniors in the Bay Area.
- They serve 70 seniors currently and have a waitlist, aiming to triple capacity with a new grant.
- Most seniors are referred through social service agencies; reaching unconnected seniors remains a challenge.
- They do not operate a call center; volunteers call from their own homes.
- Discussion included potential outreach to Alzheimer's caregivers and suggestions for expanding community connections.

#### Senior Survey Committee Update
Elna Tymes provided an update on the senior survey efforts, discussing a private organization, CASOA, conducting senior surveys for multiple cities in Santa Clara County. The survey uses online and mailed questionnaires to gather data on seniors, which is valuable for community planning. Mountain View could participate for approximately $20,000, with data specific to the city. The process requires City Council approval and is expected to take place in the near future, not immediately.
- CASOA is conducting senior surveys for Saratoga, Palo Alto, Cupertino, Los Gatos, Sunnyvale, and others.
- The survey collects data via online and mailed questionnaires using standard statistical methods.
- Mountain View's participation would cost about $20,000 and yield city-specific data.
- The proposal requires vetting and approval by the City Council before implementation.
- The survey data can be isolated for Mountain View seniors and potentially compared with other cities' data.
- The Mountain View SAC currently has no budget and would need to request funds through city channels.
