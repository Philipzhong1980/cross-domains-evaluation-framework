#### Friendly Voices senior phone buddy program
Guest speaker Laura Steuer presents Friendly Voices, a Bay Area nonprofit that combats chronic loneliness among isolated seniors through weekly one-on-one phone calls with trained volunteers. She explains the severe health and community costs of social isolation and describes Friendly Voices’ model of carefully screened volunteers, one-to-one matches, and strong ties to local senior-serving agencies. The committee explores how seniors are reached, current capacity, and partnership opportunities, while also suggesting additional outreach channels and populations such as caregivers. The discussion establishes Friendly Voices as a potential resource and partner for Mountain View-area seniors and agencies.
- Guest Laura Steuer describes Friendly Voices as a volunteer-run nonprofit providing weekly phone calls from trained, background-checked volunteers to isolated seniors to create ongoing friendships.
- Laura explains that chronic loneliness among seniors has health effects comparable to smoking 15 cigarettes a day and significantly increases mental, emotional, and physical health costs to the community.
- Laura notes that Friendly Voices serves the entire Bay Area, is based locally, and already partners with Community Services Agency in Mountain View as well as Self Help for the Elderly, New Directions, and Life Elder Care.
- Laura emphasizes that each senior is matched one-to-one with a specific volunteer, calls are documented, and senior safety is the organization’s top priority.
- Laura clarifies that volunteers make calls from their own phones and locations, not from a call center, which keeps the experience personal and flexible for volunteers.
- Laura reports they are currently serving about 70 seniors and have a wait list but expect to triple their reach to roughly 250 seniors per year within two years thanks to a new grant from the Joseph and Vera Long Foundation.
- Laura explains that most senior participants are low-income, homebound, and come through referrals from social workers and case managers at partner agencies, though some more-resourced seniors self-refer online.
- In response to Elna Tymes, Laura acknowledges the major challenge of reaching isolated seniors who are not connected to any agency and notes that flyers and talks at housing sites such as Alta Housing have had mixed success.
- When asked by Vicki, Laura confirms that all calls are made by volunteers from their homes or personal phones rather than from a centralized call facility.
- Laura states that most volunteers carry a two-to-one caseload (two seniors per volunteer) because one-to-one scaling to 250 seniors would be difficult.
- In response to Elna, Laura clarifies that Friendly Voices generally does not serve seniors with advanced dementia or major cognitive impairment because their lay volunteers are not clinically trained and in-person friendly visitor programs are better suited for those seniors.
- Elna Tymes proposes that Friendly Voices contact the Alzheimer’s Association’s 24/7 helpline, noting that many callers are unpaid family caregivers who are themselves lonely and could benefit from the program.
- Laura welcomes Elna’s suggestion and commits to contacting the Alzheimer’s Association helpline to explore collaboration, recognizing she had overlooked the caregiver population.
- Member Irigon suggests Laura present the program at the Senior Center’s congregate nutrition program during lunchtime to reach more seniors directly in person.
- Laura agrees with Irigon’s idea, explaining that Friendly Voices launched during COVID and has been operating with a “Zoom mindset,” and she plans to follow up with Alicia at CSA about speaking at the Senior Center.
- Elna recommends additional outreach contacts including Tyler Taylor at Saratoga Area Senior Center, the San Jose senior council, the Los Altos Senior Center staff, and Cupertino’s senior organization contact Richard Adler.
- Laura notes that Friendly Voices is already connected with Saratoga (Tyler Taylor) and expects to appear in their newsletter in December, but she agrees it is time to do more in-person talks.
- Elna further suggests that Laura connect with Carmen Branmer, president of the Santa Clara County Senior Care Commission, as a potential audience for Friendly Voices presentations.
- Laura shares her email (laura@friendlyvoices.org) and website (www.friendlyvoices.org) and invites committee members to send ideas, refer potential volunteers, and refer seniors who might benefit from weekly calls.

#### Senior survey subcommittee and CASOA option
Elna Tymes provides a detailed update from the Senior Survey Subcommittee, which is exploring how to update Mountain View’s prior 2019 senior survey. She explains that she and another member have located the old survey and, in parallel, identified CASOA, a specialized external firm that conducts standardized senior surveys for multiple nearby cities. The committee discusses CASOA’s methodology, potential cost (~$20,000), data privacy, and the need to obtain City Council funding approval. Members support further research but recognize that a formal proposal and any survey implementation will take time and will not occur immediately.
- Elna Tymes reports that the subcommittee has retrieved the 2019 Mountain View senior survey and its questions and initially believes re-asking those questions would be valuable.
- Elna explains that the subcommittee has since identified CASOA, a private organization that specializes in senior surveys and is currently working with cities such as Saratoga, Palo Alto (via Avenidas), Cupertino, Los Gatos, Campbell, and Sunnyvale.
- Elna notes that CASOA uses standard statistical methods, primarily online surveys supplemented by mailed questionnaires to seniors, to gather robust data on community senior needs.
- Elna emphasizes that contracting with CASOA would give Mountain View specific, city-level senior data rather than only aggregated county data, allowing targeted planning.
- Elna estimates that a CASOA survey for Mountain View would cost about $20,000, based on her recent conversation with the firm, and she is awaiting more detailed information by email.
- Elna points out that because the Senior Advisory Committee has no independent budget, any funding request must go from SAC through staff (Ashley and Michelle Peterson) and then to City Council for approval.
- In response to Jim Nichols, Elna confirms that Mountain View would receive data isolated to its own seniors, and that any broader Santa Clara County data would be a separate matter that might be accessible through her contacts if not proprietary.
- Elna tells Jim that the methodology and response counts from other cities will be covered in the additional written materials she expects from CASOA, which did not arrive before this meeting.
- Jim raises concerns about potential skew if Mountain View data were merged with data from other cities, and Elna reassures him that the city-specific dataset is what Mountain View would be paying for.
- Jim also asks about address lists and privacy, and Elna responds that senior addresses can be derived from public sources such as voter registration and age-linked databases, and that individual identities would be protected through anonymous responses linked at most to addresses.
- Elna affirms that any list used for surveying would be treated as private, with access limited to those directly involved in the project, and that respondent postcards or online entries would not contain names.
- Jim comments that the topic of resident data is sensitive and that the City Council will likely require clear documentation of CASOA’s track record and protections, which Elna plans to include once materials arrive.
- Elna reiterates that she does not yet have enough hard information to draft a formal proposal to City Council and that no action is requested from the committee at this time.
- Member Irigon notes that, given the City’s approval process, a CASOA-backed survey is unlikely to occur quickly and may extend into next year.
- Vicki asks how this relates to the required “state of Mountain View seniors” report, and Elna clarifies that the CASOA survey is a separate project from that periodic report.
- Elna confirms that the meeting is recorded and that her update will be documented in the minutes for members, including Vicki, who experienced audio issues.
