### Overview
The Rental Housing Committee convened to address key operational and policy matters. A significant discussion focused on meeting formats amid ongoing state and city emergency declarations, resulting in a decision to continue virtual meetings while considering future hybrid options. Public health concerns were raised regarding in-person meeting safety. Staff presented the September 2022 monthly status report, highlighting public inquiries, mediation successes, and stable market conditions, with committee members anticipating enhanced future data. Amendments to hearing procedure regulations were adopted to clarify compliance processes post-hearing decisions. An extensive update on tenant relocation assistance and the Housing Crisis Act informed the committee about protections and benefits for displaced tenants amid redevelopment projects. The draft 2023 work plan was reviewed, with feedback emphasizing balanced scheduling and stakeholder engagement; staff committed to revising and recirculating the plan. Finally, staff outlined ongoing outreach efforts, and committee members shared recent recognitions, underscoring community connection and service.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Remote Meeting Authorization Discussion
The committee discussed whether to continue remote meetings or transition to a hybrid model. This is important due to ongoing state and city emergency declarations and technical readiness. The current state of emergency allows remote meetings, but some members prefer returning to in-person meetings with remote participation options.
- Committee member Pardo Dazella advocates for a hybrid meeting model allowing both in-person and remote participation.
- Staff member Anky van Deursen reports that the City Council is not yet ready to adopt hybrid meetings and is still addressing related technical challenges.
- Chair Nicole Haynes notes the state of emergency remains in effect until February 28, 2023, which authorizes remote meetings.
- Vice Chair Ramos moves to continue virtual meetings pursuant to Government Code Section 54953, which passes with a 4-1 vote.
#### Public Health and Meeting Safety
A public commenter emphasized ongoing COVID-19 risks and the importance of maintaining safety measures. This impacts decisions about meeting formats and public participation.
- Public commenter Steven highlights new COVID-19 variants and ongoing moderate to high wastewater virus levels in Santa Clara County.
- Steven notes the City Council room lacks adequate air circulation and viral contagion controls, suggesting caution in returning to in-person meetings.
#### Monthly Status Report for September 2022
Staff presented the monthly status report covering public inquiries, workshops, mediation outcomes, and market conditions. The report informs the committee about program activity and housing market trends, aiding oversight and policy decisions.
- Staff member Patricia Henslow reports nearly 600 public inquiries, with many related to eviction protections and rent increase petitions.
- Bilingual services remain significant, with 30% of inquiries in Spanish and recent support provided in Russian.
- Mediation program shows 47 mediations with 36 resolved, indicating effective dispute resolution.
- Vacancy rates stabilize around 4-6.6% depending on unit type, and market rents have recovered past pre-COVID levels.
- Committee members express enthusiasm for upcoming reports with additional data points, including mobile home specifics and registry compliance.
#### Amendments to Hearing Procedures Regulations
The committee reviewed proposed amendments to CSFRA and MHRSO hearing procedures to establish processes for determining compliance with final hearing decisions. This ensures clarity and enforcement mechanisms for landlords and tenants post-decision.
- Staff explains the amendments allow parties to request compliance hearings if disputes arise after final decisions.
- Landlords bear the burden of proving compliance during such hearings.
- A 30-day notice is required before rent restoration following compliance, consistent with state law.
- The committee unanimously adopts the amendments with one abstention.
#### Tenant Relocation Assistance and Housing Crisis Act Update
Staff provided an update on tenant relocation assistance under the City's ordinance and California's SB 330. The presentation covered eligibility criteria, benefits, and recent redevelopment projects affecting tenants. This informs the committee on tenant protections during redevelopment.
- Staff member Andrea details eligibility thresholds for relocation benefits under local and state laws, including income limits and special circumstances.
- Several redevelopment projects were reviewed, including those with demolition and new construction, highlighting tenant relocation benefits and first right of return provisions.
- Data on relocation assistance payments and tenant relocation destinations were shared, showing many tenants remain in Mountain View or the Bay Area.
- Committee members commend the clarity and comprehensiveness of the presentation.
#### Draft 2023 Rental Housing Committee Work Plan
Staff presented the draft work plan outlining agenda items for 2023, including regulations, reports, and outreach. The committee discussed scheduling, workload balance, and stakeholder engagement to optimize effectiveness and transparency.
- Staff member Anky van Deursen explains the work plan categories: monthly, quarterly, annual, and as-needed items.
- Committee members express concern about front-loading complex topics early in the year and suggest spreading them out.
- Vice Chair Ramos supports maintaining the schedule but proposes a mid-year work plan review to adjust as needed.
- Public commenter Joshua Howard from the California Apartment Association emphasizes stakeholder engagement and transparency in regulatory development.
- Committee members discuss the origin and prioritization of agenda items, including ratio utility billing and tenant subtenant master lease issues.
- Staff agrees to revise the work plan to move some items to later in the year and to recirculate it after adjustments.
#### Committee and Staff Announcements and Outreach Activities
Staff shared upcoming workshops, office hours, and community outreach events to support tenants and landlords. Committee members reported on recent recognitions and community engagement, fostering connection and awareness.
- Staff announced regular Housing and Eviction Help Center hours and upcoming workshops on habitability and maintenance.
- Outreach events include school and park visits and participation in community cultural events.
- Committee members Ramos and Almond shared experiences from a citywide committee recognition event and expressed appreciation for community involvement opportunities.
