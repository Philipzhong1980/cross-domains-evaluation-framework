### Overview
The Rental Housing Committee meeting on October 10, 2022, focused on several key topics including the approval of the consent calendar, discussions on teleconference meeting resolutions, and a presentation of the monthly status report by Patricia Henslow. The committee also reviewed updates on the Tenant Relocation Assistance Ordinance and the Housing Crisis Act of 2019, presented by Andrea, which highlighted the impact of these regulations on local housing projects. Additionally, Anky van Deursen presented the draft work plan for 2023, prompting discussions about balancing the agenda and ensuring community engagement. The meeting concluded with announcements and a reminder of the next meeting scheduled for November 14, 2022.

### Detailed Summary
#### Consent Calendar Approval
The committee reviewed the consent calendar, which included routine administrative items. Item 3.2 was pulled for further discussion, while the remaining items were approved unanimously. This process allows the committee to efficiently handle standard business without extensive debate.
- Nicole Haynes notes that the consent calendar is intended for quick approval unless items are pulled for discussion.
- Emily Ramos moves to approve the consent calendar excluding item 3.2, which was pulled by Pardo Dazella.
- The motion passes unanimously with all members voting in favor.

#### Teleconference Meetings Resolution
The committee discussed the resolution to authorize continued remote teleconference meetings under the Brown Act, as amended by Assembly Bill #361. Pardo Dazella advocated for a hybrid model, allowing both in-person and remote participation, citing the lack of a state of emergency as a reason to return to in-person meetings.
- Pardo Dazella emphasizes the need for a hybrid model to accommodate both in-person and remote participation.
- Anky van Deursen explains that the City Council is not yet ready to adopt a hybrid model for committees.
- Nicole Haynes clarifies that the state of emergency allows for remote meetings but does not mandate them.

#### Monthly Status Report Presentation
Patricia Henslow presented the monthly status report for September 2022, highlighting key metrics such as public inquiries, mediation efforts, and market conditions. The report indicated a high volume of inquiries related to eviction protections and rent increases, as well as a notable increase in mediation cases.
- Patricia Henslow reports nearly 600 public inquiries, with many related to eviction protections and rent increases.
- The mediation program has seen 47 mediations, with 36 resolved, indicating strong community engagement.
- The vacancy rate for fully covered units is reported at 4.4%, showing stabilization in the housing market.

#### Tenant Relocation Assistance Ordinance Update
Andrea provided an update on the Tenant Relocation Assistance Ordinance and the Housing Crisis Act of 2019 (SB 330). The presentation covered eligibility criteria for relocation assistance and the impact of these regulations on ongoing and completed redevelopment projects.
- Andrea explains the eligibility criteria for relocation assistance under the Tenant Relocation Assistance Ordinance.
- The presentation details the benefits available to tenants under SB 330, including rent differentials and relocation advisors.
- The committee discusses the implications of SB 330 on redevelopment projects and tenant rights.

#### Draft Work Plan for 2023
Anky van Deursen presented the draft work plan for the Rental Housing Committee for 2023, outlining key tasks and agenda items. The committee discussed the need to balance the workload and ensure adequate time for community input on significant issues.
- Anky van Deursen outlines the draft work plan, emphasizing the need for stakeholder engagement on new regulations.
- Emily Ramos suggests adding a mid-year review of the work plan to assess progress and adjust as needed.
- Committee members express concerns about front-loading the agenda and the potential for lengthy meetings.
