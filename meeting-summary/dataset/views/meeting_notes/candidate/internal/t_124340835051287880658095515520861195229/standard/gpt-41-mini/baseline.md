### Overview
The January 9th, 2023 Rental Housing Committee meeting, chaired by Haines-Livesay, focused primarily on procedural and administrative matters. The committee unanimously approved a resolution to continue virtual meetings under the amended Brown Act provisions, ensuring ongoing remote participation. No public comments were received during the oral communications segment. Chair Haines-Livesay announced her upcoming resignation due to residency requirements, prompting expressions of appreciation from committee members and staff. Additionally, the committee discussed potential scheduling conflicts for the next meeting, with staff committed to coordinating a suitable date. The meeting concluded with no further announcements, maintaining a professional and collaborative tone throughout.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### Virtual Meeting Continuation Resolution
The committee reviewed and approved a resolution to continue holding virtual meetings in accordance with the Brown Act provisions as amended by AB 361. This resolution allows the committee to conduct meetings remotely, ensuring accessibility and compliance with state regulations. The motion passed unanimously, reflecting consensus among committee members on maintaining virtual meeting protocols.
- Committee member Almond moved to adopt the resolution to continue virtual meetings pursuant to Brown Act provisions as amended by AB 361.
- Vice Chair Ramos seconded the motion to continue virtual meetings.
- Chair Haines-Livesay confirmed the motion passed unanimously with all present members voting aye.
#### Public Oral Communications
The committee opened the floor for public comments on any matters not listed on the agenda. No members of the public provided comments during this segment, indicating either no concerns or satisfaction with the current agenda and committee activities. This segment is important as it allows community members to voice issues or suggestions outside of scheduled topics.

#### Chair Haines-Livesay Resignation Announcement
Chair Haines-Livesay announced that the upcoming meeting at the end of January would be her last due to not meeting the residency requirement moving forward. She expressed gratitude and fondness for her time serving with the committee and conveyed that she will miss the members and the community. This announcement is significant as it signals an upcoming leadership transition within the committee.
- Chair Haines-Livesay informed the committee and public that she will step down after the January meeting due to residency requirements.
- Committee members Rosas, Almond, Ramos, and staff expressed their appreciation and sadness about her departure.
- Chair Haines-Livesay reflected on her positive experiences serving as chair and vice chair.
