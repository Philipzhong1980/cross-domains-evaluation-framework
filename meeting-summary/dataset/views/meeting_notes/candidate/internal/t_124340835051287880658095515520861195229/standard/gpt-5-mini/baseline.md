### Overview
  The Rental Housing Committee meets virtually with Chair Haines-Livesay, Vice Chair Ramos, committee members Almond and Rosas, and staff including Andrea Kennedy and Anky van Deursen. The committee unanimously adopts a consent-calendar resolution, moved by Almond and seconded by Ramos, to continue virtual meetings under AB 361. No public speakers address the committee during the oral communications period. Chair Haines-Livesay announces they will step down after the January meeting due to residency requirements, and committee members express appreciation. Staff notes a potential conflict with a City Council meeting on January 30 and commits to polling committee members to confirm or reschedule the next Rental Housing Committee meeting, leaving the next meeting date to be announced.
  
  ### Detailed Summary
  #### Consent Calendar Approval
  The committee considers the consent calendar and votes to continue holding meetings virtually under AB 361. The item is presented as the sole consent item and the committee discusses the procedural purpose of the consent calendar. The committee adopts the resolution to continue virtual meetings by a unanimous roll-call vote and the matter is closed.
- Chair Haines-Livesay explains the consent calendar is intended to efficiently consider routine or administrative items by a single motion.
- Committee member Almond moves to adopt a resolution to continue virtual meetings pursuant to AB 361.
- Vice Chair Ramos seconds the motion to continue virtual meetings under AB 361.
- Andrea Kennedy conducts the roll-call and states that the motion passes unanimously.
#### Public Oral Communications
  The committee opens the floor for public oral communications on non-agenda items but receives no public comments. Staff explains the process for signing up and the three-minute limit per speaker, and the committee notes it cannot act on non-agenda items under state law. With no speakers, the public comment period concludes without items to record.
- Chair Haines-Livesay opens the public comment period and invites members of the public to raise their hands to speak.
- Chair Haines-Livesay reminds attendees that state law prohibits the committee from acting on non-agenda items.
- Chair Haines-Livesay reports that no members of the public provide comments during the oral communications period.
#### Chair Resignation Announcement
  Chair Haines-Livesay announces that the January meeting will be their final meeting because they will not meet the residency requirement after January. The chair frames the departure as regretful and expresses appreciation for serving with the committee. Committee members respond with expressions of thanks and regret, acknowledging the upcoming leadership change.
- Chair Haines-Livesay announces they will step down after the end-of-January meeting because they will no longer meet the residency test.
- Chair Haines-Livesay states the upcoming meeting will be their last and thanks the committee and public for the opportunity to serve.
- Committee member Rosas expresses that the committee will miss the chair.
- Committee member Almond recalls enjoying serving with the chair and offers appreciation for their leadership.
#### Next Meeting Schedule Conflict
  Staff and committee members identify a potential scheduling conflict for the planned end-of-January meeting because the City Council has scheduled an extra meeting on January 30. Staff commits to polling committee members to determine whether the Rental Housing Committee meeting date should change. The meeting date remains to be announced pending that follow-up.
- Committee member Almond asks whether there is a new date for the end-of-January meeting.
- Staff member Anky van Deursen says she will poll all Rental Housing Committee members separately to determine if the meeting date needs to change.
- Chair Haines-Livesay notes the City Council planned an extra meeting for January 30 and that the Rental Housing Committee meeting on that date may be rescheduled.
- Chair Haines-Livesay indicates the next meeting date is to be announced and may change pending the staff poll.
