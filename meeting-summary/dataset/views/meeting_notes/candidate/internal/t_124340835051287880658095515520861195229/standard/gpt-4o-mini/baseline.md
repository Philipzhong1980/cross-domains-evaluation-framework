### Overview
The January 9th, 2023 Rental Housing Committee Meeting, led by Chair Haines-Livesay, focused on the approval of the consent calendar, which passed unanimously, and allowed for public communications, though no comments were received. Haines-Livesay announced her upcoming departure from the committee due to residency requirements, prompting emotional responses from members. The committee also discussed the scheduling of the next meeting, which may conflict with a City Council meeting, and Anky van Deursen will follow up with members to confirm a new date.

### Detailed Summary
#### Consent Calendar Approval
The committee discusses the consent calendar, which includes routine administrative items. The goal is to streamline the approval process without extensive discussion. The committee unanimously approves the resolution to continue virtual meetings under the Brown Act provisions.
- Haines-Livesay states that the consent calendar items will be approved by one motion unless pulled for discussion.
- Almond makes a motion to adopt the resolution to continue virtual meetings, which Ramos seconds.
- The motion passes unanimously with all committee members voting 'Aye'.

#### Public Communications
The meeting opens for public comments on non-agenda items, allowing community members to address the committee. However, no public comments are received during this time.
- Haines-Livesay invites public comments, stating that speakers can address any matter not on the agenda.
- No public comments are received, as noted by Haines-Livesay.

#### Chair Announcement
Chair Haines-Livesay announces that the upcoming meeting at the end of January will be her last due to residency requirements. This announcement elicits emotional responses from committee members, highlighting her contributions.
- Haines-Livesay expresses regret about stepping down and shares her fondness for the committee.
- Rosas and Almond express their sentiments, stating they will miss her leadership.

#### Next Meeting Date
The committee discusses the scheduling of the next meeting, noting a potential conflict with a City Council meeting. Anky van Deursen will coordinate with members to determine a new date.
- Anky van Deursen mentions the need to poll committee members regarding a new meeting date due to a scheduling conflict.
- Haines-Livesay confirms that the next meeting may need to be rescheduled.
