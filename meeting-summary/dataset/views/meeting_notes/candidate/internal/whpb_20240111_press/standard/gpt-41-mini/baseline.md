### Overview
The meeting featured National Economic Advisor Lael Brainard and Admiral John Kirby providing updates on key domestic and international issues. Brainard highlighted President Biden's upcoming visit to Allentown, Pennsylvania, showcasing economic recovery and a record surge in small-business applications, particularly among minority entrepreneurs. She reported encouraging inflation data with easing supply chain pressures and outlined the administration's focus on lowering costs for American families, including healthcare and housing affordability. Brainard also addressed concerns about rising car insurance costs, the health of the banking sector, and ongoing tax negotiations emphasizing the child tax credit. Admiral Kirby condemned Iran's seizure of a commercial tanker in the Gulf of Oman and reaffirmed U.S. support for Israel's right to self-defense amid legal and humanitarian questions regarding Gaza. He discussed U.S. preparedness to counter Houthi attacks in the Red Sea, congressional oversight of arms transfers to Israel, and challenges in tracking U.S. weapons provided to Ukraine. Kirby also addressed maritime security, NATO support, and humanitarian aid efforts in Gaza. Press Secretary Karine Jean-Pierre responded to questions on government funding negotiations, judicial nominations, immigration-related impeachment proceedings, President Biden's recent public engagements, and gun violence prevention initiatives. The session underscored the administration's commitment to economic growth, national security, and addressing domestic challenges while navigating complex international dynamics.
  
### Detailed Summary
#### President's Allentown Visit
National Economic Advisor Lael Brainard previews President Biden's upcoming trip to Allentown, Pennsylvania, highlighting the city's economic recovery and small-business growth under his administration. The visit aims to showcase the positive impact of Biden's policies on communities previously left behind, emphasizing job creation, infrastructure improvements, and increased internet access.
- Lael Brainard states President Biden will meet small-business owners in Allentown to discuss economic resurgence.
- Brainard notes Allentown's unemployment rate dropped from 6.4% to 3.9%, the lowest in 30 years.
- She highlights 4,000 new business applications in Allentown over three years as a sign of economic confidence.
#### Small-Business Boom and Inflation Data
Brainard presents data showing a record-breaking surge in small-business applications nationwide during the Biden administration, with significant growth among Black and Hispanic entrepreneurs. She also reports encouraging inflation trends, including reduced supply chain pressures and lower prices for key household items, while outlining the administration's ongoing efforts to lower costs for American families.
- Brainard reports 16 million business applications filed since Biden took office, the strongest three-year period in 20 years.
- She highlights inflation has decreased nearly two-thirds as supply chain issues ease.
- Brainard emphasizes the administration's focus on lowering prescription drug costs and urging corporations to reduce prices.
#### Addressing Rising Car Insurance Costs
In response to questions about rising car insurance rates contributing significantly to CPI inflation, Brainard explains the administration's use of legislative and regulatory tools to address elevated prices. She notes independent agencies are actively enforcing rules against unfair pricing practices and calls on businesses to pass on cost savings to consumers as supply chain pressures diminish.

- She mentions independent agencies have enforcement powers addressing unfair fees and pricing practices.
- Brainard calls on corporations to lower prices that were previously increased due to supply chain disruptions.
